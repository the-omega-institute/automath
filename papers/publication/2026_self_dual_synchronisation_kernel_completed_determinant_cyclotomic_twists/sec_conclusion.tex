\section{Further remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The weighted synchronisation kernel considered here is rigid enough to
produce a fully explicit completed determinant, but still complicated
enough for the completed curve to exhibit non-trivial geometry and
non-abelian Galois behaviour. The cyclotomic resultants show that the
unit-circle twists can be reduced to a one-parameter elimination
problem, and the resulting twisted Perron roots admit an effective
large-\(m\) expansion.

Two directions seem natural. The first is to identify structural
conditions on a general self-dual weighted kernel that force a
low-degree completed determinant or a controlled quotient geometry. The
second is to understand to what extent the large-\(m\) asymptotics of
\(\rho_m\) depend only on the local jet of \(\widehat\Delta\) at the
endpoint \(s=2\).
