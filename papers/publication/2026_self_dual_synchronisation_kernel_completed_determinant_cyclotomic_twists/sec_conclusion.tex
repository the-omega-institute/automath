\section{Further remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

This note isolates one weighted synchronisation kernel for which the
completed determinant, the quotient geometry, and the cyclotomic
asymptotics can all be written down explicitly. The point is not a new
general mechanism, but that even this small self-dual example already
exhibits non-trivial geometry and non-abelian Galois behaviour. The
cyclotomic resultants reduce the unit-circle twists to a one-parameter
elimination problem, and the resulting twisted Perron roots admit an
effective large-\(m\) expansion.

Two directions seem natural. The first is to identify structural
conditions on a general self-dual weighted kernel that force a
low-degree completed determinant or, more concretely, a quotient curve
of genus at most \(3\). The second is to understand to what extent the
large-\(m\) asymptotics of \(\rho_m\) depend only on the local jet of
\(\widehat\Delta\) at the endpoint \(s=2\).
