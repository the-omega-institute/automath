\title[A self-dual synchronisation kernel and cyclotomic twists]{A self-dual synchronisation kernel,\\
its completed determinant, and cyclotomic twists}

\author{Lexa From Work}
\email{1621420399@qq.com}

\date{}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
We study a weighted ten-state synchronisation kernel. Its weighted
adjacency matrix is \(B(u)=B_0+uB_1\), and we write
\(\Delta(z,u)=\det(I-zB(u))\) for the associated determinant. The
point of the paper is modest: rather than proposing a new general
method, we carry one explicit self-dual example through a complete
calculation. The kernel satisfies an explicit
self-duality relation
\(\Delta(z,u)=\Delta(uz,u^{-1})\), and after the completion
\(u=r^2\), \(s=r+r^{-1}\), \(w=zr\) this yields a polynomial
\(\widehat\Delta(w,s)\in\ZZ[s][w]\) of degree \(6\) in \(w\).
We determine \(\widehat\Delta\) explicitly, show that the normalisation
of the affine curve \(\widehat\Delta(w,s)=0\) has genus \(6\), that its
quotient by the involution \((w,s)\mapsto(-w,-s)\) is a smooth plane
quartic of genus \(3\), and that the generic Galois group over
\(\QQ(s)\) is \(S_6\).

For the cyclotomic resultants
\(
  R_m(w)=\Res_s(\widehat\Delta(w,s),\Phi_m^+(s))
\)
we prove a degree law and obtain a full asymptotic expansion of the
associated twisted Perron roots \(\rho_m\). We also record the
primitive-orbit asymptotics for the unweighted kernel and for its
cyclic lifts.
\end{abstract}
