\begin{abstract}
We study one explicit weighted ten-state self-dual kernel whose
self-duality compresses the full family of unit-circle twists to a
single completed determinant. Its weighted adjacency matrix is
\(B(u)=B_0+uB_1\), and we write \(\Delta(z,u)=\det(I-zB(u))\) for the
associated determinant. The kernel satisfies the explicit self-duality relation
\(\Delta(z,u)=\Delta(uz,u^{-1})\), and after the completion
\(u=r^2\), \(s=r+r^{-1}\), \(w=zr\) this yields a polynomial
\(\widehat\Delta(w,s)\in\ZZ[s][w]\) of degree \(6\) in \(w\). We
determine \(\widehat\Delta\) explicitly, show that the normalization
\(\overline{X}\) of the projective closure of \(\widehat\Delta(w,s)=0\)
has genus \(6\), that its quotient by the involution
\((w,s)\mapsto(-w,-s)\) is a smooth plane quartic of genus \(3\), and
that the generic Galois group over \(\QQ(s)\) is \(S_6\).

We also identify the correct cyclic skew-product lifts
\(\widetilde B_q=B_0\otimes I_q+B_1\otimes P_q\), whose Fourier
decomposition produces the unit-circle twists and their real
cyclotomic factors. The point is not a new general theory but that
this one degree-\(6\) completed determinant is small enough to support
a complete calculation of the quotient geometry, the generic Galois
group, and the cyclotomic twists in closed form.

For the cyclotomic resultants
\(
  R_m(w)=\Res_s(\widehat\Delta(w,s),\Phi_m^+(s))
\)
we prove a degree law and obtain an asymptotic expansion through order
\(m^{-12}\) for the associated twisted Perron roots \(\rho_m\).
\end{abstract}
