\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Let \(B(u)=B_0+uB_1\) be the weighted adjacency matrix of the
ten-state synchronisation kernel introduced in
\Cref{sec:preliminaries}. Here ``synchronisation'' refers to the
finite-state problem of comparing two labelled paths that emit the
same binary word and recording the residual mismatch until the paths
realign; for general background on this symbolic-dynamical viewpoint
see \cite{LindMarcus2021,Kitchens1998}. In the present binary model
those discrepancies form a ten-state comparison automaton, and the
strongly connected core carries an involution exchanging output-zero
and output-one edges. The kernel studied below is therefore a concrete
self-dual example arising from a standard synchronisation
construction. The purpose of this note is to make one such example
completely explicit. Even this small finite-state object already produces a completed
determinant whose spectral curve has genus \(6\), whose quotient by
the sign involution is a smooth quartic of genus \(3\), and whose
generic Galois group is the full symmetric group~\(S_6\).

For each fixed \(u\), the matrix \(B(u)\)
defines a finite-state dynamical \(\zeta\)-function through the
determinant
\[
  \Delta(z,u):=\det(I-zB(u)).
\]
The purpose of this note is to record the additional structure coming
from the explicit self-duality
\[
  \Delta(z,u)=\Delta(uz,u^{-1}),
\]
which compresses the unit-circle twists \(u=e^{i\theta}\) to a single
real parameter after the standard change of variables
\[
  u=r^2,\qquad s=r+r^{-1},\qquad w=zr.
\]
The resulting completed determinant \(\widehat\Delta(w,s)\) provides a
convenient algebraic model for the twisted spectrum.

This note has two strands. The first is geometric: the affine
curve \(\widehat\Delta(w,s)=0\) admits a natural involution, and its
normalisation and quotient can be described explicitly. The second is
arithmetic: eliminating the parameter \(s\) along cyclotomic slices
produces one-variable resultants whose smallest-modulus roots control
the twisted Perron radii. This yields explicit asymptotics for the
cyclotomic twists of the kernel.

\subsection{Main results}

Our first theorem records the explicit determinant and geometric
information.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:intro-kernel-geometry}
Let \(B(u)\) be the weighted synchronisation kernel of
\Cref{def:sync-kernel}. Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
  \item The determinant is
  \[
    \Delta(z,u)
    =1-(1+u)z-5uz^2+3u(1+u)z^3-u(u^2-3u+1)z^4
  \]
  \[
    \qquad
    +u(u^3-3u^2-3u+1)z^5+u^2(u^2+u+1)z^6.
  \]
  In particular,
  \[
    \Delta(z,1)=(z-1)(z+1)(3z-1)(z^3-z^2+z+1),
    \qquad
    C_B=\frac{243}{272}.
  \]

  \item The completed determinant
  \[
    \widehat\Delta(w,s):=\Delta(w/r,r^2)
    \qquad (u=r^2,\ s=r+r^{-1})
  \]
  is given by
  \[
    \widehat\Delta(w,s)
    =1-sw-5w^2+3sw^3+(5-s^2)w^4+(s^3-6s)w^5+(s^2-1)w^6.
  \]
  It satisfies
  \[
    \widehat\Delta(w,-s)=\widehat\Delta(-w,s).
  \]

  \item The normalisation of the affine curve
  \(\widehat\Delta(w,s)=0\) has genus \(6\). Its quotient by the
  involution \((w,s)\mapsto(-w,-s)\) is a smooth plane quartic of genus
  \(3\), and the generic Galois group over \(\QQ(s)\) is \(S_6\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

Our second theorem concerns cyclotomic twists.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:intro-kernel-cyclotomic}
For \(m\ge 2\), let \(s_m=2\cos(\pi/m)\), let \(\Phi_m^+\) be the
minimal polynomial of \(s_m\), and define
\[
  R_m(w):=\Res_s\bigl(\widehat\Delta(w,s),\Phi_m^+(s)\bigr).
\]
If
\[
  \rho_m:=\frac{1}{\min\{\abs{w}:R_m(w)=0\}},
\]
then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
  \item For every \(m\ge 4\),
  \[
    \deg_w R_m=3\varphi(2m).
  \]
  If \(m\) is even, then \(R_m(w)\) is an even polynomial.

  \item One has
  \[
    \rho_2=\sqrt{2+\sqrt3}.
  \]

  \item As \(m\to\infty\),
  \[
    \rho_m
    =3-\frac{11\pi^2}{17m^2}+O(m^{-4}).
  \]
  Moreover, \Cref{thm:rho-gap-m12} gives the full expansion through
  order \(m^{-12}\). In particular,
  \[
    3-\rho_m\sim \frac{11\pi^2}{17m^2}.
  \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

We also record the primitive-orbit asymptotics of the unweighted kernel
and of its cyclic lifts. These provide the dynamical interpretation of
the Perron root \(\rho_m\) as a twisted growth parameter.

\subsection{Previous work}

The rationality of dynamical \(\zeta\)-functions for shifts of finite
type goes back to Bowen--Lanford and Manning
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom}; general background on
symbolic dynamics may be found in
\cite{LindMarcus2021,Kitchens1998}. More recent treatments of weighted
or determinant-type zeta functions include Baladi's monograph on
dynamical determinants \cite{Baladi2018} and the finite-state
determinant formulas developed for weighted digraph zeta functions in
\cite{Morita2020,IdeIshikawaMoritaSatoSegawa2021}. The elimination step
used here is the classical Sylvester resultant construction
\cite{CoxLittleOShea2005}, while the geometric input comes from
 standard facts about smooth plane curves and Hurwitz's formula
\cite{Hartshorne1977}. For the Galois-group computation we follow the
usual specialisation philosophy together with standard
permutation-group criteria for transitivity and primitivity
\cite{Dixon1996,Serre2008Topics}. The present note is therefore a case
study. It does not introduce new general techniques; rather, it makes
one explicit self-dual kernel transparent enough to determine its
completed curve, its generic Galois group, and the cyclotomic
asymptotics in closed form.
