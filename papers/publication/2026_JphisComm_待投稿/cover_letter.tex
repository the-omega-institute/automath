Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, \emph{``Record-level thresholds of a phenomenological sampled Unruh--DeWitt counter model in static KMS spacetimes''}, for publication as a Research Paper in \emph{Journal of Physics Communications}.

The paper shows that, within a phenomenological sampled-counter model driven by the stationary Unruh--DeWitt excitation rate, the full click record carries geometric information that is invisible to the transition rate alone. Dead time and finite recovery are intrinsic features of any real counting detector, and exploiting their full stochastic output goes beyond the standard rate-thermometry approach. The cross-disciplinary nature of the work---drawing on quantum field theory in curved spacetime, symbolic dynamics, and renewal-process methods---aligns naturally with the broad scope of \emph{Journal of Physics Communications}.

We note that a shorter preliminary version was previously submitted to \emph{General Relativity and Gravitation}. The present manuscript is a substantially expanded and self-contained treatment, with new results on universal antibunching, soft dead time, robustness beyond the exponential-recovery model, and a complete Schwarzschild worked example.

The principal results are the following.
\begin{enumerate}
\item In the ideal dead-time model, the sampled record is a Markov chain on the golden-mean shift, and the Parry-law record benchmark is realized at a unique detector threshold.
\item In the finite-recovery model, the sampled record is a full-support renewal process with strictly increasing hazard, so the Parry-law benchmark is excluded. The relevant selector is instead the memory-transition shell at exact $1$-dependence.
\item Finite recovery approaches the ideal grammar only softly: consecutive-click violations are quadratically suppressed in the sampling interval, yet the hazard remains genuinely history dependent at every positive sampling interval.
\item The finite-recovery record is universally antibunched, with strictly increasing spectral density on $[0,\pi]$ and an asymptotic Fano factor below one across the whole parameter range.
\item In the homogeneous spectral-weight regime, the shell pair obeys an exact common-amplitude cancellation law. Near a nonextremal Hartle--Hawking horizon this yields a universal leading-order ratio law for shell distances.
\item Within the same homogeneous toy model, the shell pair determines the redshift scale and the Schwarzschild mass parameter, and two operating modes of the same hardware provide a local self-calibrating inverse principle on static spherical two-parameter families.
\end{enumerate}

Key proofs and derivations are included in the main-text appendices (Appendices~A--B). The enclosed Supplementary Material contains additional intermediate steps and extended calculations, so the central claims can be checked from the submission package itself.

The work is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission. We have no competing interests to declare.
