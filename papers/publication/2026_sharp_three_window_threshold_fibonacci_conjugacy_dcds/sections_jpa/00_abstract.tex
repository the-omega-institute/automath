\begin{abstract}
We study the canonical finite-window Fibonacci stabilization map
\[
\Fold_m:\{0,1\}^m\to X_m,
\]
which sends a binary word to the unique golden-mean admissible word with the same Fibonacci valuation modulo $F_{m+2}$.  Although this map is many-to-one on isolated windows, its overlapping sliding-block extension has a sharp injectivity threshold.  The main result proves that, for every $m\ge 3$, the sliding stabilized-window map has an explicit finite-memory inverse and identifies its image with a subshift of finite type conjugate to the full two-shift.  At $m=2$ the complete obstruction is a single branch orbit, which yields exact conditional-entropy and Kullback--Leibler defect formulas and a mod-zero inverse away from that orbit.  We also show that this two-window branch is specific to the golden case inside the metallic finite-window family: for every metallic parameter $A\ge2$, the corresponding two-window normalization is already a finite-memory conjugacy.  Above the Fibonacci threshold, the overlap presentation is the right Fischer cover of the stabilized image shift.  The auxiliary arithmetic of the finite fibers is recorded through a residue formula, a roots-of-unity expression, an affine transfer principle and a second-moment recurrence.  For the Sturmian rotation source law, standard discrepancy estimates give deterministic bounds for the resulting stabilized block laws and transported finite-range observables.
\end{abstract}
