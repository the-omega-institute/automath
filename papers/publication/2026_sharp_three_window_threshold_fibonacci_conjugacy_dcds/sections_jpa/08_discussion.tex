\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:discussion}

We have proved a sharp overlap threshold for a locally many-to-one Fibonacci normalization. The word map $\Fold_m$ collapses residue classes on isolated windows, but Theorems~\ref{thm:block-invertibility} and~\ref{thm:two-sided-conjugacy} show that overlapping windows restore injectivity for every $m\ge3$ and give a finite-memory conjugacy onto a finite-type image.

The failure at $m=2$ is equally rigid. Theorem~\ref{thm:m2-branch-locus} identifies the unique exceptional orbit pair, Corollary~\ref{cor:m2-defect-formulas} computes the entropy and Kullback--Leibler defect concentrated there, and Corollary~\ref{cor:m2-mod-zero} shows that the map is invertible modulo null sets away from the branch.

The metallic comparison in Theorem~\ref{thm:metallic-two-window-rigidity} shows that this branch is not a generic feature of finite-window metallic normalization. For every metallic parameter $A\ge2$, the corresponding two-window code has a local inverse. The golden case is therefore the unique member of this family in which two-window overlap still fails to separate the hidden carry.

The image shift also has an intrinsic finite-state description. Theorem~\ref{thm:fischer-cover-stabilized-image} proves that the overlap presentation is the right Fischer cover of $Y_m$ for every $m\ge3$. Thus the $2^{m-1}$ overlap states are forced by the follower structure of the stabilized image shift.

The fixed-window entropy decomposition and the path-space result should be read together. Section~\ref{sec:entropy-ledger} measures multiplicity inside a single fiber, while Theorem~\ref{thm:two-sided-conjugacy} proves that this multiplicity disappears after overlap above the threshold. The resulting transport of invariant measures is a standard consequence of the finite-memory conjugacy.

The residue formulas, Fourier representation, second-moment recurrence and discrepancy estimates give quantitative information about the same finite-window map. In the Sturmian rotation model they provide deterministic control of stabilized block laws and transported local observables. The quasicrystal readout motivation explains why the map is posed on the full cube and why such finite-sample estimates are relevant; the main theorem itself is symbolic-dynamical and arithmetic.
