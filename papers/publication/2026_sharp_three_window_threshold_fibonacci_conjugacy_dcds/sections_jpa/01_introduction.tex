\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

Finite-window recodings occur naturally in symbolic dynamics and in numeration. A local rule may identify several words when one window is read in isolation, while consecutive windows can contain enough overlap to recover the original sequence. We determine this effect for a cyclic Fibonacci normalization map.

For $m\ge1$ let $X_m$ be the set of binary words of length $m$ with no adjacent $1$'s, and let $F_1=F_2=1$, $F_{n+2}=F_{n+1}+F_n$. We consider the map
\[
\Fold_m:\{0,1\}^m\to X_m
\]
which sends a word to the unique element of $X_m$ having the same Fibonacci valuation modulo $F_{m+2}$. Thus $\Fold_m$ is a finite-window version of Zeckendorf normalization in which the right boundary is closed by reduction modulo the next Fibonacci number. It is onto, but it is not generally one-to-one on the full cube $\{0,1\}^m$.

The main result is that the local ambiguity is removed by overlap exactly from length three onward. If
\[
(\Phi_m(a))_t=\Fold_m(a_ta_{t+1}\cdots a_{t+m-1}),\qquad a\in\{0,1\}^{\mathbb Z},
\]
then the overlap graph gives a finite-state presentation of the image for every $m\ge2$. For $m\ge3$, the map $\Phi_m$ is a topological conjugacy from the full two-shift onto its image; its inverse is a sliding-block code with memory $0$ and anticipation $m-1$. At $m=2$ there is a single obstruction: the two constant raw sequences have the same stabilized image, and all other path-space fibers are singletons. These assertions are Theorems~\ref{thm:finite-state-lift}, \ref{thm:block-invertibility}, \ref{thm:m2-branch-locus} and~\ref{thm:two-sided-conjugacy}.

The threshold is arithmetic, not merely a formal consequence of sofic theory. We first build the de Bruijn-type overlap graph associated with $\Fold_m$ and observe that it is resolving in both time directions. This gives a uniform finite-to-one bound, but not injectivity. The decisive step is an explicit residue decoder at $m=3$; once the first overlap state is recovered, resolvingness propagates the reconstruction. For $m\ge4$ the same idea is implemented by congruences which separate the possible values of a boundary bit. The two-window case is small enough to classify directly.

The comparison with metallic means points in the same direction. In the metallic family with constant partial quotient $A\ge2$, the analogous two-window normalization is already invertible by a two-symbol local rule. Hence the branch at $m=2$ is special to the golden case, rather than a generic feature of metallic numeration. We also prove that, for $m\ge3$, the natural overlap presentation of the Fibonacci image is the right Fischer cover of the image shift; see Theorem~\ref{thm:fischer-cover-stabilized-image}.

We record the fixed-window arithmetic of the same map. The fibers of $\Fold_m$ are described by a residue formula and a roots-of-unity filter, and an affine transfer principle identifies the canonical valuation with a shifted-weight model up to a permutation of residues. The second moment
\[
\Sigma_m=\sum_{x\in X_m}|\Fold_m^{-1}(x)|^2
\]
satisfies a closed linear recurrence. These facts are independent of the overlap threshold, but they describe the local multiplicities which disappear on path space when $m\ge3$.

This should be distinguished from the general existence of normalization automata and resolving presentations. Fibonacci and Ostrowski numeration have well-developed finite-automaton normal forms, and symbolic dynamics supplies general criteria under which a degree-one resolving factor is a conjugacy. Those results do not determine the degree of the particular cyclic map $\Fold_m$ on the full binary cube. The point here is the exact degree computation under sliding overlap, including the sharp $m=3$ threshold, the complete $m=2$ branch locus and the Fischer-cover identification of the resulting image shift.

The source model plays a limited role. Sturmian words and Fibonacci numeration provide the admissible language and the normalization viewpoint \cite{MorseHedlund1940,Lothaire2002,Queffelec2010,Frougny1992,Berstel2001}. More general connections between numeration and symbolic dynamics are treated in \cite{BertheRigo2010,BertheSteinerThuswaldner2019}, and the metallic comparison uses the standard Ostrowski framework \cite{Ostrowski1922,Berthe2001Ostrowski,AlloucheShallit2003}. The broader numeration background includes the classical relation between $\beta$-expansions and shift spaces \cite{Parry1960}. Sofic shifts, sliding-block codes, resolving presentations, Fischer covers and finite-to-one factors are used in the sense of \cite{Hedlund1969,LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998,Nasu1995}; related resolving factor theory appears in \cite{Ashley1991,Boyle1983}.

Several auxiliary results are included to fix notation and to place the threshold in a broader symbolic-dynamical setting. Proposition~\ref{thm:noise-full-support} is the standard full-support consequence of a strictly positive finite channel \cite{CoverThomas2006}. Propositions~\ref{thm:fold-suite} and~\ref{thm:fiber-residue} are finite-window forms of Zeckendorf uniqueness and normalization \cite{Zeckendorf1972,Lothaire2002,Frougny1992,Berstel2001}. The Fourier expression for the fibers is the usual roots-of-unity filter \cite{Stanley2012}, and the affine transfer uses d'Ocagne's identity \cite{Koshy2001}. The entropy identities below are fixed-window or finite-to-one consequences used to distinguish local multiplicity from trajectory-level invertibility. The estimates in Section~\ref{sec:fp:tv} combine deterministic pushforward with classical discrepancy bounds for rotations \cite{KuipersNiederreiter1974,DrmotaTichy1997}.

The motivation from finite-resolution observation of quasiperiodic order \cite{Shechtman1984,Jagannathan2021,BaakeGrimm2013} enters only through the induced symbolic law. In a noiseless golden Sturmian model the words are already $11$-free, so stabilization acts trivially on each legal window. A strictly positive local readout, however, assigns positive probability to every binary word of a fixed length. The normalization must therefore be defined on the full cube, and the relevant question is whether overlap recovers the hidden raw word.

The paper is organized as follows. Section~\ref{sec:superspace} records the limited source-layer motivation. Section~\ref{sec:readout} fixes the symbolic source model and the full-support observation. Section~\ref{sec:stabilization} defines the finite-window Fibonacci normalization and studies its fibers. Section~\ref{sec:stab:conjugacy} proves the finite-state presentation, the three-window threshold and the two-window branch classification. Section~\ref{sec:metallic-cover} gives the metallic comparison theorem and the Fischer-cover identification. Section~\ref{sec:entropy-ledger} records the fixed-window entropy decomposition. Finally, Sections~\ref{sec:fiber-spectrum} and~\ref{sec:fp:tv} give the second-moment recurrence and the Sturmian discrepancy estimates.
