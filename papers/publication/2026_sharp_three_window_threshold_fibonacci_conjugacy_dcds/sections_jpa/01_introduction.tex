\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

Finite-window recodings occur throughout symbolic dynamics and numeration.  A local map may collapse many words at the level of a single block and nevertheless become invertible when it is applied to overlapping blocks along a trajectory.  The purpose of this paper is to determine exactly when this phenomenon occurs for the Fibonacci stabilization map.

For each $m\ge 1$ we consider
\[
\Fold_m:\{0,1\}^m\to X_m,
\]
where $X_m$ is the set of binary words with no adjacent $1$'s.  The word $\Fold_m(\omega)$ is the unique element of $X_m$ whose Fibonacci valuation is congruent to that of $\omega$ modulo $F_{m+2}$.  Thus the map is canonical from the point of view of finite Fibonacci numeration, but it is not injective on the full cube $\{0,1\}^m$.  We ask whether the corresponding sliding-block map is injective on path space.

The answer is sharp.  The main results may be summarized as follows.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item The overlap graph gives a finite-state presentation of the image and a uniform finite-to-one bound.  Lemma~\ref{lem:m3-decoder} supplies the explicit arithmetic decoder at $m=3$, and Theorem~\ref{thm:block-invertibility} proves injectivity for every $m\ge 3$.
  \item The case $m=2$ has one and only one obstruction.  Theorem~\ref{thm:m2-branch-locus} identifies the branch orbit, Corollary~\ref{cor:m2-defect-formulas} gives exact entropy and Kullback--Leibler defect formulas, and Corollary~\ref{cor:m2-mod-zero} gives a mod-zero inverse off the branch set.
  \item The exceptional nature of the golden branch is stable under comparison with metallic mean finite window normalizations.  Theorem~\ref{thm:metallic-two-window-rigidity} proves that for every metallic parameter $A\ge2$, the analogous two-window normalization is already a finite-memory conjugacy.
  \item For $m\ge 3$, the stabilized image is a subshift of finite type and the stabilized window map is a topological conjugacy from the full two-shift onto that image.  The inverse is a finite-memory sliding-block code; see Theorem~\ref{thm:two-sided-conjugacy}.  Theorem~\ref{thm:fischer-cover-stabilized-image} identifies the overlap presentation as the right Fischer cover of the image shift.
  \item Once the conjugacy is proved, the standard dynamical, statistical and thermodynamic quantities are transported by formal invariance under a finite-memory recoding.  These consequences are recorded explicitly to fix the precise lossless interpretation of the stabilization map.  In the Sturmian rotation model, standard discrepancy estimates then give explicit bounds for stabilized block laws and finite-range observables.
\end{enumerate}

The cut-and-project input appears only in the background source model.  Once the symbolic law and the golden-mean grammar have been fixed, all arguments are finite-state, symbolic and arithmetic.  Thus the new content is not a new quasicrystal theorem or a new thermodynamic formalism; it is the exact arithmetic threshold and the finite-state structure of the resulting symbolic recoding.

The paper is related to several standard lines of work.  Sturmian words and Fibonacci numeration provide the admissible language, normalization procedure and finite-automaton viewpoint \cite{MorseHedlund1940,Lothaire2002,Queffelec2010,Frougny1992,Berstel2001}; broader connections between numeration systems and symbolic dynamics are discussed in \cite{BertheRigo2010}, and $S$-adic generalizations in \cite{BertheSteinerThuswaldner2019}.  Ostrowski and metallic numeration supply the comparison family used in Section~\ref{sec:metallic-cover} \cite{Ostrowski1922,AlloucheShallit2003}.  Sofic shifts, labeled graph presentations, resolving maps and finite-to-one factors provide the ambient symbolic-dynamical language \cite{LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998,Nasu1995}.  Within that framework, resolving factor maps between shifts of finite type and the equal-entropy case have been studied in \cite{Ashley1991}, while lower-entropy sofic factors are treated in \cite{Boyle1983}.  The relation between $\beta$-expansions and shift spaces goes back to Parry \cite{Parry1960}, and the thermodynamic consequences used here are those of the usual variational formalism \cite{Walters1982}.

The point which is special to the present paper is the arithmetic threshold.  General sofic theory gives a natural setting for the labeled graph, but it does not determine that degree one begins exactly at $m=3$, nor does it identify the sole branch at $m=2$.  These conclusions come from the residue arithmetic of the Fibonacci weights.

\subsection{Relation with existing results}

Several statements in the sequel are included to fix notation.  Proposition~\ref{thm:noise-full-support} is the usual support consequence of a strictly positive finite channel \cite{CoverThomas2006}.  Propositions~\ref{thm:fold-suite} and~\ref{thm:fiber-residue} are finite-window forms of Zeckendorf uniqueness and normalization \cite{Zeckendorf1972,Lothaire2002,Frougny1992,Berstel2001}.  Proposition~\ref{thm:kl-ledger} is the entropy chain rule after grouping by fibers.

The finite-window arithmetic begins with Theorem~\ref{thm:fiber-dft}, where the roots-of-unity filter is applied to the modulo-$F_{m+2}$ Fibonacci fibers \cite{Stanley2012}.  Lemma~\ref{lem:cross-multiplication} is d'Ocagne's identity \cite{Koshy2001}; Theorem~\ref{thm:affine-transfer} uses it to give an explicit affine permutation between the canonical and shifted-weight fiber spectra.  Theorem~\ref{prop:second-moment-recurrence} gives a closed recurrence for the cyclic residue collision statistic of this stabilization map.

The main symbolic contribution starts with the overlap graph.  Hedlund's sliding-block formalism \cite{Hedlund1969} and the standard theory of labeled graphs explain the framework, but Theorem~\ref{thm:finite-state-lift} records the particular resolving presentation associated with $\Fold_m$.  Lemma~\ref{lem:m3-decoder} gives the three-window decoder, Theorem~\ref{thm:block-invertibility} proves the threshold $m=3$, and Theorem~\ref{thm:m2-branch-locus} proves the branch classification at $m=2$.  Theorem~\ref{thm:metallic-two-window-rigidity} proves that this two-window branch is a golden phenomenon rather than a generic metallic-mean finite-window obstruction, and Theorem~\ref{thm:fischer-cover-stabilized-image} identifies the minimal right-resolving presentation of the image shift.

Once the threshold theorem is established, Theorem~\ref{thm:two-sided-conjugacy} gives a finite-memory conjugacy and finite-type image.  Theorem~\ref{thm:exact-sufficiency} is the corresponding deterministic equivalence of block experiments in the sense of Blackwell comparison \cite{Blackwell1953}.  Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-potentials} is the usual transport of pressure and equilibrium states through a conjugacy \cite{Walters1982}.  The information-geometric corollaries use only the invariance of classical divergences under bijections \cite{AliSilvey1966}; they are included to delimit what the threshold theorem does and does not change.

The quantitative Sturmian estimates in Section~\ref{sec:fp:tv} combine the deterministic pushforward with standard discrepancy bounds for rotations \cite{KuipersNiederreiter1974,DrmotaTichy1997}.  They are included because the rotation model gives a natural source law for the finite-window distributions considered in the paper.

The distinction between a single window and a path is important throughout.  The entropy identity of Section~\ref{sec:entropy-ledger} measures the multiplicity inside isolated fibers.  For $m\ge 3$, however, overlap reconstructs the entire raw trajectory, so this local multiplicity does not become a loss of information on path space.  At $m=2$ the only remaining obstruction is the branch orbit classified in Theorem~\ref{thm:m2-branch-locus}.

The motivation from finite-resolution observation of quasiperiodic order \cite{Shechtman1984,Jagannathan2021,BaakeGrimm2013} enters in a limited way.  In the Sturmian model, strictly positive local detector noise gives positive probability to every binary word of length $m$, so the stabilization map has to be defined on the full cube rather than only on the admissible language.

The paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:readout} gives the symbolic setup, the Sturmian model and the full-support observation.  Section~\ref{sec:stabilization} defines the finite-window stabilization map and studies its fibers.  Section~\ref{sec:stab:conjugacy} proves the finite-state presentation, the sharp threshold and the two-window branch classification.  Section~\ref{sec:metallic-cover} gives the metallic comparison theorem and the Fischer-cover classification.  Section~\ref{sec:entropy-ledger} records the fixed-window entropy decomposition.  Section~\ref{sec:measurement} derives the block, path-space and thermodynamic consequences of the conjugacy.  Sections~\ref{sec:fiber-spectrum} and~\ref{sec:fp:tv} give, respectively, a second-moment recurrence and discrepancy estimates for the Sturmian model.  The appendix contains the short source-layer input and finite-range checks.
