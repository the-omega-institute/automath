\section{A motivating source law}\label{sec:superspace}

We begin with the only part of the cut-and-project formalism that is used later: it supplies a stationary symbolic source law. The stabilization theorem itself is a statement about finite words and subshifts.

Let $E=\RR^d$, $H=\RR^n$, and let $\Gamma\subset E\times H$ be a lattice such that $\pi_E|_{\Gamma}$ is injective and $\pi_H(\Gamma)$ is dense. Writing $\Gamma_E:=\pi_E(\Gamma)$ and $x^\star:=\pi_H(\gamma)$ for the unique $\gamma\in\Gamma$ with $\pi_E(\gamma)=x$, a compact window $W\subset H$ with nonempty interior and Haar-null boundary defines the regular model set
\[
\Lambda(W,u):=\{x\in\Gamma_E:\ x^\star\in W+u\}\subset E
\]
at phase $u\in H$. Varying $u$ moves along the hull, whose natural equicontinuous factor is $(E\times H)/\Gamma$. For regular model sets, unique ergodicity and uniform patch frequencies are standard. Thus a one-dimensional scan may be represented, at the symbolic level used here, by a torus translation followed by a finite observation map \cite{Moody2000,BaakeGrimm2013}.

No further cut-and-project facts enter the proof. Once the symbolic source law has been fixed, the argument reduces to the stabilization theory developed below.
