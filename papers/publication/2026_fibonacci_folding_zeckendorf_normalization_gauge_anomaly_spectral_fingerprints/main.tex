\title{Fibonacci Folding under Zeckendorf Normalization:\\
Multiscale Consistency, a Gauge-Anomaly Constant Law, and Generating-Function Spectral Fingerprints}
\author{}
\date{17 February 2026 (Singapore time)}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We introduce, on finite-window binary microstate spaces, a class of folding readouts induced by Fibonacci-weighted evaluation followed by Zeckendorf normalization. We develop its multiscale compatibility and quantify the resulting \emph{gauge anomaly}---the systematic discrepancy between naive truncation and a fold-aware restriction uniquely characterized by a commuting diagram. Our main results include: (i) at each window length the folding map is a well-defined, idempotent, surjective normalization onto the golden-mean language; (ii) compared with naive truncation, the gauge anomaly can be extensive in the worst case and cannot be uniformly corrected by any bounded tail patch; (iii) under the uniform microstate baseline the anomaly density converges almost surely to \(4/9\), satisfies a central limit theorem with variance constant \(118/243\), and admits closed-form autocovariances and a rational power spectral density; (iv) the fold is lifted to a sliding block code on the two-sided shift, whose image is sofic and (for window length \(m\ge 3\)) topologically conjugate to the full shift via an explicit inverse block code; (v) a weighted partition function over folded fibers has a fourth-order minimal recurrence, exhibits Hankel-rank collapse, and has a completely factorized discriminant yielding Lee--Yang type branch points; (vi) the squarefree part of the discriminant of a collision-kernel recurrence is promoted to an arithmetic fingerprint, with the \(q=2\) fingerprint equal to \(37\).
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Keywords.}
Zeckendorf normalization; Fibonacci numeration; sliding block codes; sofic shifts; gauge anomaly; Hankel rank; discriminant fingerprints; collision probability.
