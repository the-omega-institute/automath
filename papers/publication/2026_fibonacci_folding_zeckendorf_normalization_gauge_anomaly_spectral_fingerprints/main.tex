\begin{abstract}
We study the finite-window map obtained by evaluating a binary word in Fibonacci weights, replacing the resulting expansion by its Zeckendorf normal form, and restricting back to the original window. This fold is a projection onto the golden-mean language, but it does not commute with naive restriction across scales. We show that the compatible restriction is uniquely determined and that the resulting restriction discrepancy, which we call the gauge anomaly, can be extensive and cannot be removed by any bounded tail correction. In the stationary bulk limit for uniform microstates, the discrepancy is governed by an explicit four-state sofic pair process, from which we derive the exact discrepancy density \(4/9\), the variance \(118/243\), closed autocovariance formulas, and a rational power spectral density. We also prove that the induced sliding block code has sofic image and is a topological conjugacy for window size \(m\ge 3\), and we obtain a quartic transfer-matrix recurrence and a complete discriminant factorization for the associated weighted fiber partition function.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Keywords.}
Zeckendorf normalization; Fibonacci numeration; sofic shifts; sliding block codes; transfer matrices; thermodynamic formalism.
