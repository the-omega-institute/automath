\begin{abstract}
We study the finite-window map obtained by Fibonacci-weighted evaluation followed by Zeckendorf normalization and truncation. Building on the classical two-sided normalization picture, we keep the observation window finite and compare naive truncation with the uniquely determined fold-aware restriction that restores multiscale commutation. Their mismatch defines a restriction discrepancy, which we call the gauge anomaly. We prove that this discrepancy can be extensive, cannot be removed by any bounded tail patch, and, in the stationary bulk limit of uniform microstates, is governed by an explicit four-state sofic pair process with anomaly density \(4/9\), central-limit variance \(118/243\), closed-form autocovariances, and a rational power spectral density. We further lift the fold to a sliding block code on the full shift, prove that the image is sofic and topologically conjugate to the full shift for \(m\ge 3\), and record the quartic transfer-matrix recurrence and discriminant factorization controlling the weighted fold-fiber partition function.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Keywords.}
Zeckendorf normalization; Fibonacci numeration; sliding block codes; sofic shifts; restriction discrepancy; gauge anomaly; thermodynamic formalism; transfer matrix; discriminant factorization; Parry measure.
