\begin{abstract}
We study the finite-window map obtained by Fibonacci-weighted evaluation followed by Zeckendorf normalization and truncation. The resulting fold lands in the golden-mean language and admits a strict multiscale formalism: stable restriction commutes exactly once naive truncation is replaced by a uniquely determined fold-aware restriction. The discrepancy between naive and fold-aware restriction defines a gauge anomaly. We prove that this anomaly can be extensive, cannot be removed by any bounded tail patch, and, in the stationary bulk limit of uniform microstates, is governed by an explicit four-state sofic process with anomaly density \(4/9\), central-limit variance \(118/243\), closed-form autocovariances, and a rational power spectral density. We further lift the fold to a sliding block code on the full shift, prove that the image is sofic and topologically conjugate to the full shift for \(m\ge 3\), and show that the weighted fold-fiber partition function is controlled by a fourth-order transfer matrix whose discriminant factors completely.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Keywords.}
Zeckendorf normalization; Fibonacci numeration; sliding block codes; sofic shifts; gauge anomaly; thermodynamic formalism; transfer matrix; discriminant factorization; Parry measure.
