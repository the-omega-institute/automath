\begin{abstract}
We study the finite-window map obtained by evaluating a binary word in Fibonacci weights, replacing the resulting expansion by its Zeckendorf normal form, and restricting back to the original window. This fold is an idempotent projection onto the golden-mean language, but naive truncation does not commute with change of window size. We show that exact commutation determines a unique compatible restriction and that its discrepancy from naive truncation can be extensive and cannot be eliminated by any uniformly bounded tail correction. The structural input is an explicit four-state synchronized presentation for the stationary bulk pair process formed by a raw digit and its normalized counterpart. From that presentation we obtain the exact discrepancy density \(4/9\), variance \(118/243\), closed autocovariance formulas, a rational power spectral density, and a sharp sequence-level statement: the induced sliding block code is a topological conjugacy exactly for window size \(m\ge 3\). The same presentation also yields a transfer-matrix description of weighted fold fibers and, more importantly, a variational formula in which the free energy is the Legendre transform of a strictly concave microcanonical entropy profile on the pair shift.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Keywords.}
Zeckendorf normalization; Fibonacci numeration; sofic shifts; sliding block codes; transfer matrices; thermodynamic formalism.
