\begin{abstract}
We study the map obtained by evaluating a binary word in Fibonacci weights, replacing the resulting expansion by its Zeckendorf normal form, and then restricting again to the original window. This finite-window fold is an idempotent projection onto the golden-mean language, but it is not compatible with naive truncation when the window size changes. We show that there is a unique compatible restriction and that its discrepancy from naive truncation can be extensive: for every \(m\), the two restrictions may disagree at all \(m\) visible sites, and no uniformly bounded tail correction restores commutation. The bulk behavior is encoded by an explicit four-state synchronized presentation for the stationary process formed by a raw digit together with its normalized counterpart. From this presentation we derive the exact discrepancy density \(4/9\), the variance \(118/243\), closed autocovariance formulas, and a rational power spectral density. We also study the induced sliding block code on the full shift and prove that it is a topological conjugacy precisely for window size \(m\ge 3\). Finally, the same presentation yields a transfer-matrix description of weighted fibers and a variational formula identifying the free energy as the Legendre transform of a strictly concave entropy profile on the pair shift.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Keywords.}
Zeckendorf normalization; Fibonacci numeration; sofic shifts; sliding block codes; transfer matrices; thermodynamic formalism.
