\begin{abstract}
We study the map obtained by evaluating a binary word in Fibonacci weights, replacing the resulting expansion by its Zeckendorf normal form, and then restricting again to the original window. This finite-window fold is an idempotent projection onto the golden-mean language, but it is not compatible with naive truncation when the window size changes. We show that there is a unique restriction on microstates that commutes with folding, that its discrepancy from naive truncation can be extensive, and that no uniformly bounded tail correction restores commutation. The structural result is that the bulk raw/normalized pair process is presented by an explicit four-state synchronized graph. This identifies the finite-window obstruction with a canonical sofic model: exact discrepancy statistics, including density \(4/9\) and asymptotic variance \(118/243\), follow from its Parry measure, and the same presentation yields a local inverse for the induced sliding block code for every window size \(m\ge 3\). In the weighted setting the same graph gives a transfer-matrix formula for the fibers and a variational formula identifying the output free energy as the Legendre transform of a strictly concave entropy profile on the pair shift.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Keywords.}
Zeckendorf normalization; Fibonacci numeration; sofic shifts; sliding block codes; transfer matrices; thermodynamic formalism.
