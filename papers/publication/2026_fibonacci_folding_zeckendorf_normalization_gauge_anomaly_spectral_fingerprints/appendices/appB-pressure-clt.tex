\section{Appendix B: Transfer matrices, pressure, and the CLT constant}
\label{app:pressure}

This appendix makes explicit the finite-state transfer-matrix objects underlying Section~\ref{sec:gauge}.

\subsection{The four-state sofic presentation and the Parry measure}
The bulk pair process \((X_t,Y_t)\) is realized as the label process of a four-state edge shift with adjacency matrix
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}
1&1&0&1\\
0&0&1&0\\
1&2&0&0\\
1&0&0&0
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Its Perron eigenvalue is \(\lambda=2\). One choice of right/left Perron eigenvectors is
\[
r=(2,1,2,1)^{\mathsf T},
\qquad
\ell=(2,2,1,1)^{\mathsf T},
\qquad
\ell^{\mathsf T}r=9.
\]
The Parry transition matrix is
\[
P_{ij}=\frac{A_{ij}\,r_j}{\lambda r_i}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
\frac12&\frac14&0&\frac14\\
0&0&1&0\\
\frac12&\frac12&0&0\\
1&0&0&0
\end{pmatrix},
\]
and the stationary state distribution is \(\pi_i=\frac{\ell_i r_i}{\ell^{\mathsf T}r}=(4,2,2,1)/9\).

Splitting multiplicity-\(2\) transitions uniformly among the parallel edges \(2\to 1\) produces the exact symbol law in Theorem~\ref{thm:joint}.

\subsection{Tilted adjacency and the pressure function}
Let \(\Delta_t:=X_t\oplus Y_t\in\set{0,1}\) be the discrepancy indicator. On the sofic graph, \(\Delta_t=1\) exactly on the edges labeled \(01\) and \(10\). The corresponding tilted adjacency matrix is
\[
A_\theta=
\begin{pmatrix}
1& e^\theta &0&1\\
0&0& e^\theta &0\\
e^\theta &2&0&0\\
1&0&0&0
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Let \(\rho(\theta)=\rho(A_\theta)\) be its Perron root and set \(\Lambda(\theta)=\log\rho(\theta)-\log\rho(0)\) (so \(\rho(0)=2\)).
Then \(\Lambda\) is the scaled cumulant generating function for \(\sum_{t=1}^m \Delta_t\) under the Parry measure. Differentiating at \(0\) yields
\[
\Lambda'(0)=\frac{4}{9},
\qquad
\Lambda''(0)=\frac{118}{243}.
\]
The second derivative equals the Green--Kubo variance \(\Var(\Delta_0)+2\sum_{j\ge 1}\Cov(\Delta_0,\Delta_j)\), establishing the CLT variance constant in Theorem~\ref{thm:llnclt}.

\subsection{Closed-form autocovariances and spectrum}
The edge-chain transition matrix associated to \(P\) has eigenvalues \(\{1,\tfrac12,-\tfrac12,-\tfrac12,0,0,0\}\). As a consequence, the autocovariances \(c_j=\Cov(\Delta_0,\Delta_j)\) admit a two-mode closed form for \(j\ge 1\), with a single-step correction at \(j=0\) coming from the nilpotent sector. One convenient presentation is the one in Theorem~\ref{thm:spectrum}:
\[
c_0=\frac{20}{81},
\qquad
c_j = 2^{-j}\!\left(\frac{1}{8}+(-1)^j\!\left(\frac{7}{648}+\frac{j}{108}\right)\right)\quad (j\ge 1).
\]
Summing the corresponding cosine series gives the rational power spectral density
\[
S(\omega)
=
\frac{2\bigl(32\cos^3\omega+12\cos^2\omega-116\cos\omega-105\bigr)}
{9\,(4\cos\omega-5)\,(4\cos\omega+5)^2},
\]
and \(S(0)=118/243\) recovers \(\Lambda''(0)\).
