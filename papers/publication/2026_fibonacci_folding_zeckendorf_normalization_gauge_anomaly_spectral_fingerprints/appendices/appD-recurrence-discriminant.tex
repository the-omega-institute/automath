\section{Appendix D: The partition-function recurrence and discriminant computations}
\label{app:recurrence}

\subsection{From the denominator to the order-4 recurrence}
If \(Z(t;y)=\sum_{m\ge 0} Z_m(y)t^m\) has denominator \(D(t;y)\) as in \eqref{eq:Dty}, then
\[
D(t;y)\,Z(t;y)=\text{(a polynomial in \(t\) of degree at most \(3\))}.
\]
Equating coefficients of \(t^m\) for \(m\ge 4\) yields an order-4 linear recurrence for \(Z_m(y)\). Minimality (generic order \(4\)) can be certified by transfer-matrix dimension or by Hankel-rank arguments.

\subsection{Hankel rank}
Let \(H^{(n)}\) be the \(n\times n\) Hankel matrix with \((i,j)\)-entry \(Z_{i+j}(y)\). A rational generating function with denominator degree \(4\) implies \(\mathrm{rank}\,H^{(n)}\le 4\) for all \(n\), and generic equality holds unless the parameters lie on a proper algebraic subvariety.

\subsection{Discriminant factorization}
The factorization \eqref{eq:disc} follows by direct symbolic computation of \(\Disc_\lambda \Pi(\lambda,y)\) for \(\Pi\) in \eqref{eq:Pi}. The cubic factor \(256y^3+411y^2+165y+32\) has a unique negative real root \(y_{\mathrm{LY}}\approx -1.13445\), controlling the nearest negative real branch point of the free energy.
