\section{Appendix E: Discriminant squarefree parts and computational conventions}
\label{app:squarefree}

For a primitive polynomial \(P\in \ZZ[\lambda]\), we write \(\Disc(P)\) for its discriminant. The \emph{squarefree part} \(\Delta^{\mathrm{sf}}\) of a nonzero integer \(\Delta\) is obtained by dividing \(\Delta\) by the largest square dividing it:
\[
\Delta = s^2\,\Delta^{\mathrm{sf}},\qquad \Delta^{\mathrm{sf}}\ \text{squarefree}.
\]
In the \(q=2\) example of Proposition~\ref{prop:q2}, \(\Disc(P_2)=148=4\cdot 37\), so \(\Delta_2^{\mathrm{sf}}=37\) and the fingerprint field is \(\QQ(\sqrt{37})\).
