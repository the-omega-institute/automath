\section{Finite-window normalization}
\label{sec:preliminaries}

For \(m\ge 1\), let
\[
\Om_m:=\{0,1\}^m
\]
be the set of binary words of length \(m\), and let
\[
X_m:=\{x\in\{0,1\}^m:\text{$x$ contains no occurrence of \(11\)}\}
\]
be the corresponding length-\(m\) language of the golden-mean shift.

Throughout the paper we write words in low-to-high order,
\[
\omega=(\omega_1,\dots,\omega_m),
\]
so that coordinate \(k\) carries weight \(\Fib_{k+1}\). When it is convenient to scan from high weights to low weights we use the reversed word
\[
\omega^\sharp=(\omega_m,\dots,\omega_1).
\]
Appendix~\ref{app:rewriting} is written in this most-significant-digit-first convention; elsewhere we keep the low-to-high ordering. The reversal map
\[
\rev_m:\Om_m\to\Om_m,\qquad
\rev_m(\omega_1,\dots,\omega_m):=(\omega_m,\dots,\omega_1),
\]
passes between the two.

Let \((\Fib_k)_{k\ge 0}\) be the Fibonacci sequence with \(\Fib_0=0\), \(\Fib_1=1\), and \(\Fib_{k+2}=\Fib_{k+1}+\Fib_k\). For \(\omega=(\omega_1,\dots,\omega_m)\in\Om_m\), define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Nomega}
N(\omega):=\sum_{k=1}^m \omega_k\,\Fib_{k+1}.
\end{equation}
Thus the first two visible coordinates carry weights \(\Fib_2=1\) and \(\Fib_3=2\). This indexing differs slightly from some common numeration conventions \cite{Fraenkel1985,Frougny1988,Frougny1992,FrougnyConfluent1992,FrougnySolomyak1996,Berstel1985,AlloucheShallit2003}, but it is convenient for finite windows because coordinates are numbered from low weight to high weight.

Zeckendorf's theorem asserts that every \(N\in\NN\) admits a unique representation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:zeck}
N=\sum_{k\ge 1} z_k\Fib_{k+1},
\qquad
z_kz_{k+1}=0\quad\text{for all }k\ge 1,
\end{equation}
with \(z_k\in\{0,1\}\); see \cite{Lekkerkerker1952,Zeckendorf1972,Fraenkel1985}. We write \(Z(N)=z\) for this normalized digit sequence, and
\[
\proj_m:\{0,1\}^{\NN}\to\{0,1\}^m,\qquad
\proj_m(z)=(z_1,\dots,z_m)
\]
for truncation to the first \(m\) low-weight digits.

\begin{definition}\label{def:fold}
For \(m\ge 1\), define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fold}
\Fold_m(\omega):=\proj_m\!\left(Z\!\left(N(\omega)\right)\right)\in X_m,
\qquad \omega\in\Om_m.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

Thus \(\Fold_m\) normalizes the Fibonacci value represented by \(\omega\) and then discards the part of the normalized word lying beyond the visible window. In particular, if \(x\in X_m\), then \(x\) is already in Zeckendorf form and hence \(\Fold_m(x)=x\).
