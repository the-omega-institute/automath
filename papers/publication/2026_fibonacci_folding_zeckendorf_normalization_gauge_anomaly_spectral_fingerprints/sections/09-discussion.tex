\section{Discussion and outlook}
\label{sec:discussion}

We presented a multiscale-consistent folding readout induced by Fibonacci evaluation and Zeckendorf normalization, and quantified its multiscale incompatibility under naive truncation by a gauge anomaly. At the categorical level, the fold-aware restriction enforces strict commutation, converting multiscale consistency into an explicitly auditable algebraic diagram. At the statistical level, the anomaly not only persists but admits closed-form constants and spectral structure: the density converges to \(4/9\), fluctuations satisfy a CLT with variance \(118/243\), and second-order statistics are captured by an exact rational spectrum.

The weighted partition-function analysis shows that fold fibers exhibit an algebraic spectral structure: a fourth-order minimal recurrence with a completely factorized discriminant. The explicit numerator and denominator of the rational generating function identify the exceptional parameter values \(y=0\) and \(y=1\) at which the Hankel rank drops; for all other values, the rank is exactly \(4\).

\paragraph{Open directions.}
Several extensions appear natural.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2.2em]
\item \textbf{Beyond Fibonacci.} Replace Zeckendorf by more general Ostrowski or \(\beta\)-numeration normal forms \cite{FrougnySolomyak1996} and study the induced folds, anomalies, and transfer-matrix spectra.
\item \textbf{Refined thermodynamic invariants.} Extend the transfer-matrix analysis to higher-order observables, joint cumulants, and other weighted partition functions attached to the fold fibers.
\item \textbf{Higher-dimensional readouts.} Analyze analogous multiscale anomalies for projection schemes coming from other symbolic numeration systems or from higher-dimensional constraint languages.
\item \textbf{Connections to interval-based Zeckendorf statistics.} The fold-pushforward measure \(w_m\) and the interval-based Zeckendorf measure studied in \cite{KologluKoppMillerWang2011,MillerWang2012,BowerInsoftLiMillerTosteson2015} give distinct but related statistics. A systematic comparison of these two averaging schemes, and the transfer-matrix structures underlying each, remains to be carried out.
\end{itemize}
