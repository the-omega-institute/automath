\section{Discussion and outlook}
\label{sec:discussion}

We presented a multiscale-consistent folding readout induced by Fibonacci evaluation and Zeckendorf normalization, and quantified its multiscale incompatibility under naive truncation by a gauge anomaly. At the categorical level, the fold-aware restriction enforces strict commutation, converting multiscale consistency into an explicitly auditable algebraic diagram. At the statistical level, the anomaly not only persists but admits closed-form constants and spectral structure: the density converges to \(4/9\), fluctuations satisfy a CLT with variance \(118/243\), and second-order statistics are captured by an exact rational spectrum.

The weighted partition-function analysis shows that fold fibers exhibit an algebraic spectral collapse: a fourth-order minimal recurrence and Hankel rank \(4\) with a completely factorized discriminant. This yields a transparent description of the free-energy branch locus, including a Lee--Yang type critical point on the negative real axis. Finally, the discriminant squarefree fingerprint mechanism promotes an algebraic invariant of collision-kernel recurrences into an arithmetic audit interface, linking finite-length data to Frobenius parity via Kronecker symbols and, ultimately, to Chebotarev-type consistency.

\paragraph{Open directions.}
Several extensions appear natural.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2.2em]
\item \textbf{Beyond Fibonacci.} Replace Zeckendorf by more general Ostrowski or \(\beta\)-numeration normal forms and study the induced folds, anomalies, and transfer-matrix spectra.
\item \textbf{Higher-order collision spectra.} Develop a systematic theory of Galois groups and splitting-type statistics for higher \(q\), and connect the fingerprints \(\Delta_q^{\mathrm{sf}}\) to effective Chebotarev bounds.
\item \textbf{Fiber-volume laws.} Analyze the fiber-volume term \(\EE_{\pi}[\log d_m(X)]\) in the ledger identity under broader projection grammars and in higher-dimensional readout architectures.
\end{itemize}
