\section{Concluding remarks}
\label{sec:discussion}

The finite-window fold studied here sits at the intersection of two familiar constructions: Zeckendorf normalization on the one hand, and symbolic restriction maps on the other. What is specific to the finite-window setting is that these operations do not commute naively. The compatible restriction introduced in Section~\ref{sec:multiscale} isolates the obstruction in a canonical way, and the subsequent analysis shows that it is not a boundary fluctuation: it has an extensive worst case, a stationary bulk law, and a rigid finite-state description.

The same four-state presentation organizes the rest of the paper. It describes the bulk discrepancy process in Section~\ref{sec:gauge}, controls the ambiguity analysis behind the inverse sliding block code in Section~\ref{sec:sequence}, and supports the weighted transfer-matrix formalism in Section~\ref{sec:partition}. The later exact constants, covariance formulas, and algebraic identities are consequences of this common presentation.

The weighted theory adds a variational interpretation that does not reduce to recurrence algebra. Theorem~\ref{thm:microcanonical-output} identifies the free energy as the Legendre transform of a constrained entropy profile on the pair shift, so the weighted fibers are governed by the same symbolic object as the restriction discrepancy itself. In that sense the paper isolates a single finite-state mechanism underlying the finite-window fold, its multiscale obstruction, and the thermodynamic structure of the associated fibers.
