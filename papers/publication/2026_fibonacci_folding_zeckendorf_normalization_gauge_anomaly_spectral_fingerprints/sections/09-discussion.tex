\section{Concluding remarks}
\label{sec:discussion}

The finite-window fold studied here sits at the intersection of two familiar constructions: Zeckendorf normalization on the one hand, and symbolic restriction maps on the other. What is specific to the finite-window setting is that these operations do not commute naively. The compatible restriction introduced in Section~\ref{sec:multiscale} makes the obstruction visible, and the subsequent analysis shows that this obstruction is not merely local noise: it has an extensive worst case, an explicit bulk law, and a rigid four-state symbolic description.

The same four-state presentation organizes the rest of the paper. It yields the discrepancy statistics in Section~\ref{sec:gauge}, the decoder and sharp conjugacy threshold in Section~\ref{sec:sequence}, and the transfer-matrix formulas for weighted fibers in Section~\ref{sec:partition}. In that sense the paper identifies a single finite-state mechanism underlying the finite-window fold, its restriction discrepancy, and the associated weighted counting problem.
