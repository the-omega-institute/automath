\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

We presented a multiscale-consistent folding readout induced by Fibonacci evaluation and Zeckendorf normalization, and quantified its multiscale incompatibility under naive truncation by a gauge anomaly, or restriction discrepancy. The finite-state and Erd\H{o}s-measure background is classical; what this paper isolates is the finite-window pair/discrepancy observable generated by comparing naive and fold-aware restriction. At the categorical level, the fold-aware restriction enforces strict commutation, converting multiscale consistency into an explicitly auditable algebraic diagram. At the statistical level, the discrepancy persists and admits closed-form constants and spectral structure: the density converges to \(4/9\), fluctuations satisfy a CLT with variance \(118/243\), and second-order statistics are captured by an exact rational spectrum.

The weighted partition-function analysis shows that fold fibers exhibit an algebraic spectral structure: a fourth-order minimal recurrence with a completely factorized discriminant. The explicit numerator and denominator of the rational generating function identify the exceptional parameter values \(y=0\) and \(y=1\) at which the Hankel rank drops; for all other values, the rank is exactly \(4\).

The submission also contains short verification scripts for the state graph, worst-case anomaly family, covariance formulas, and discriminant factorization. They are not used in place of the proofs above, but they provide an independent finite-volume audit of the formulas recorded in Sections~\ref{sec:gauge} and \ref{sec:partition}.
