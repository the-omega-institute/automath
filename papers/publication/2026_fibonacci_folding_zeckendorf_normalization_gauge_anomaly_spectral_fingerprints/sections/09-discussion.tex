\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

We presented a multiscale-consistent folding readout induced by Fibonacci evaluation and Zeckendorf normalization, and quantified its incompatibility with naive truncation by a restriction discrepancy. The classical background enters through Zeckendorf normalization, sofic presentations, and Parry-measure techniques. The new contribution is the finite-window comparison between naive and fold-aware restriction, which yields an extensive discrepancy, an exact four-state bulk model, closed discrepancy statistics, and a sharp decoder threshold.

The same four-state presentation also controls the weighted fold-fiber partition function. This produces a quartic recurrence, an explicit rational generating function, a complete discriminant factorization, and the fold-pushforward LLN/CLT/LDP for output weight. Together these results place the finite-window fold, its discrepancy process, and its weighted fibers within a single symbolic and transfer-matrix framework.
