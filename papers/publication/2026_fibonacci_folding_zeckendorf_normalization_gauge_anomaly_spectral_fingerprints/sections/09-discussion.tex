\section{Concluding remarks}
\label{sec:discussion}

The finite-window fold isolates a canonical obstruction to naive restriction in Fibonacci numeration. The contribution of the paper is to identify that obstruction explicitly and to show that it is governed by a single four-state pair shift.

This scope should be read precisely. The paper does not propose a new abstract theory of numeration automata or sofic shifts; after the pair shift is identified, the later constants, spectra, and limit laws are standard finite-state consequences. Their role is to make the finite-window model fully explicit. Within that scope, however, the same object controls three genuinely linked features of the problem: the bulk discrepancy process, the local inverse for the induced sliding block code, and the weighted-fiber variational principle.
