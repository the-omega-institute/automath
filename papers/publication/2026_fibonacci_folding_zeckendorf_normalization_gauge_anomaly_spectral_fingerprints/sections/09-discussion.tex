\section{Concluding remarks}
\label{sec:discussion}

The finite-window model singles out a concrete obstruction to restricting Fibonacci expansions before normalization. What the paper shows is that this obstruction admits a completely explicit symbolic description: once raw and normalized digits are coupled in the pair shift, the discrepancy statistics, the inverse problem for the sliding block code, and the weighted fiber asymptotics are all governed by the same four-state presentation.

We have not tried to formulate this as a general theory of normalization automata. The point is more modest, and more specific: in this model the relevant sofic system can be written down completely, and once that is done the later formulas and limit laws fall into place in a uniform way.
