\section{Introduction}

In finite-window observations of binary words, a readout rule that fails to be \emph{multiscale consistent} can induce systematic biases: structures visible at one resolution need not be compatible with what is visible after refinement. Such biases can appear combinatorially (as an incompatibility of normal forms under restriction) and statistically (as non-vanishing anomaly densities).

This paper studies a readout scheme obtained by combining (i) Fibonacci-weighted evaluation of a microstate word and (ii) Zeckendorf normalization, followed by truncation back to the window. We call the resulting projection the \emph{Fibonacci fold}. On the stable side it lands in the golden-mean language (no adjacent ones), and it admits a strict multiscale framework via an inverse system of stable blocks. On the microstate side we show that the requirement of commutation with stable restriction uniquely determines a \emph{fold-aware restriction} operator; comparing this with naive truncation yields a natural gauge anomaly.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
Our main contributions can be summarized as follows.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2.2em]
\item We define a family of window-level folding maps \(\Fold_m:\Om_m\to X_m\), prove idempotence and surjectivity, and give a terminating and confluent rewriting realization.
\item We build a commuting multiscale diagram by introducing the fold-aware restriction \(\aware_{m+1\to m}\), characterized by \(\Fold_m\circ \aware_{m+1\to m}=\proj_{m+1\to m}\circ \Fold_{m+1}\), and show that it yields strict consistency between ``restrict-then-fold'' and ``fold-then-restrict''.
\item We define the gauge anomaly \(G_m\) as the Hamming distance between naive truncation and fold-aware restriction. We exhibit extensive worst-case deviations and prove that no bounded tail patch can uniformly correct them.
\item Under the uniform microstate baseline, we derive exact bulk statistics: a joint law for a naive bit and its fold-aware counterpart, a law of large numbers for anomaly density with limit \(4/9\), a central limit theorem with variance constant \(118/243\), and closed-form autocovariances and a rational power spectrum.
\item We lift folding to a sliding block code \(\Phi_m\) on the two-sided full shift, show that its image is sofic with an explicit labeled graph model, and prove a conjugacy threshold at \(m\ge 3\).
\item We study a weighted partition function over fold fibers, obtain a fourth-order minimal recurrence, identify Hankel-rank collapse, and factor the discriminant to locate Lee--Yang type branch points. We also propose a discriminant squarefree ``arithmetic fingerprint'' for collision-kernel recurrences.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Organization.}
Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries} introduces the window model, Fibonacci evaluation, and Zeckendorf normalization. Section~\ref{sec:multiscale} develops the folding projection, multiscale restriction, and the fold-aware restriction operator. Section~\ref{sec:gauge} defines the gauge anomaly and proves its worst-case behavior and limiting laws, including closed-form second-order statistics. Section~\ref{sec:sequence} upgrades folding to a sliding block code and establishes soficity and conjugacy. Sections~\ref{sec:partition}--\ref{sec:fingerprint} address weighted partition functions, Hankel rank, discriminants, and arithmetic fingerprints. Section~\ref{sec:ledger} presents an information-ledger identity and a dual-budget addressing capacity bound. Proof details and computational conventions are collected in the appendices.
