\section{Introduction}

Finite-window observations of binary words depend on the readout convention used to pass from microscopic configurations to stable observables. When that readout is not multiscale consistent, structures visible at one resolution need not agree with those seen after refinement. In the present setting this failure appears combinatorially, as a non-commutation of truncation with Zeckendorf normalization, and statistically, as a non-vanishing density of mismatched digits.

This paper studies a readout scheme obtained by Fibonacci-weighted evaluation of a microstate word, Zeckendorf normalization, and truncation back to the observation window. We call the resulting projection the \emph{Fibonacci fold}. On the stable side it lands in the golden-mean language, the basic symbolic model for Fibonacci numeration and Zeckendorf representations \cite{Lekkerkerker1952,Zeckendorf1972,Brown1964,Fraenkel1985,Frougny1988,Frougny1992,FrougnyConfluent1992,FrougnySolomyak1996,AlloucheShallit2003,ShallitShan2023}. On the microstate side, requiring compatibility with stable restriction singles out a unique \emph{fold-aware restriction}. Comparing it with naive truncation produces a visible mismatch. We call this mismatch the \emph{gauge anomaly}, and throughout we use the synonymous neutral phrase \emph{restriction discrepancy}: it is a symbolic-dynamics discrepancy created by truncation before versus after normalization, not a new physical effect.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
The paper has six main outputs.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2.2em]
\item We define the window-level folds \(\Fold_m:\Om_m\to X_m\), prove idempotence and surjectivity, and realize them by a terminating and confluent rewriting system.
\item We build a commuting multiscale diagram by introducing the fold-aware restriction \(\aware_{m+1\to m}\), characterized by \(\Fold_m\circ \aware_{m+1\to m}=\proj_{m+1\to m}\circ \Fold_{m+1}\).
\item We define the gauge anomaly (restriction discrepancy) \(G_m\) as the Hamming distance between naive truncation and fold-aware restriction, exhibit extensive worst-case deviations, and prove that no bounded tail patch can remove them uniformly.
\item Under the uniform microstate baseline, we prove that the bulk \((X_t,Y_t)\)-pair process is governed by an explicit synchronized four-state right-resolving presentation, and from that model derive the exact joint law, anomaly density \(4/9\), central-limit variance \(118/243\), closed-form autocovariances, and a rational power spectral density. The marginal density \(\PP(Y_0=1)=5/18\) is classical in the two-sided normalization literature, but the joint pair/discrepancy law is the new observable here.
\item We lift folding to a sliding block code \(\Phi_m\) on the full two-sided shift, prove that its image is sofic, and show that \(\Phi_m\) is topologically conjugate to its image precisely from window size \(m=3\) onward.
\item We record the weighted fold-fiber partition function in an explicit quartic transfer-matrix package, obtaining a fourth-order recurrence, an exceptional-parameter analysis, and a completely factored discriminant.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Related work and novelty positioning.}
Classical Zeckendorf theory goes back to Lekkerkerker and Zeckendorf \cite{Lekkerkerker1952,Zeckendorf1972}; broader numeration-theoretic context may be found in \cite{Brown1964,Fraenkel1985,Frougny1988,Frougny1992,FrougnyConfluent1992,FrougnySolomyak1996,FrougnySakarovitch1998,AlloucheShallit2003,ShallitShan2023,Berstel1985}. Finite-state normalization in Fibonacci and more general confluent numeration systems is standard \cite{Frougny1988,FrougnyConfluent1992,BerendFrougny1994,Frougny1992,FrougnySolomyak1996,FrougnySakarovitch1998,Frougny1999}, as are sofic shifts, right-resolving presentations, and sliding block codes \cite{Weiss1973,LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998,Blanchard1989}. Likewise, once a finite-state model has been identified, the Parry measure, Perron--Frobenius analysis, and Markov-chain LLN/CLT machinery used later are standard \cite{Parry1964,ParryPollicott1990,Walters1982,Billingsley1961,Gordin1969,Ruelle2004,Bowen2008}.

For the golden-ratio Erd\H{o}s measure itself, the hidden-Markov/sofic viewpoint is already part of the literature. Alexander--Zagier \cite{AlexanderZagier1991}, Sidorov--Vershik \cite{SidorovVershik1998}, and Kempton \cite{KemptonDigits2014} analyze the two-sided normalization picture and its digit statistics, including the classical density \(5/18\). Most relevant here, Bezhaeva and Oseledets \cite{BezhaevaOseledets2007} explicitly prove that Erd\H{o}s measures are sofic and represent them by a regular \(5\)-state Markov chain, while \cite{BezhaevaOseledets2010} studies the corresponding Erd\H{o}s--Vershik problem for the golden ratio; for related second-order Markov refinements see \cite{BezhaevaOseledets2Step}. These papers provide the background for the normalized-digit process itself.

The present manuscript therefore does \emph{not} claim the finite-state or hidden-Markov description of normalized Fibonacci digits as new. The paper-specific object is the finite-window comparison between two readouts of the same microstate: naive restriction and fold-aware restriction. The resulting pair observable \((X_t,Y_t)\), and the derived discrepancy process \(\Delta_t=X_t\oplus Y_t\), are different from the classical normalized-digit chain. The new contributions of the paper are the fold-aware restriction itself, the finite-window discrepancy it produces, the exact joint and discrepancy laws for that observable, the closed covariance and spectral formulas, the decoder threshold \(m\ge 3\), and the explicit transfer-matrix package for the fold-fiber partition function.

\subsection{Theorem-by-theorem comparison with prior work}
To make the novelty profile explicit, we separate the classical input from the paper-specific results. Definition~\ref{def:fold} introduces the finite-window fold, while Lemma~\ref{lem:convention} and Proposition~\ref{prop:idempotent} are the classical Zeckendorf-normalization input translated into the present window language; compare \cite[Proposition~2]{ShallitShan2023}. Definition~\ref{def:fold-aware} introduces the new restriction operator forced by the commuting square, and Lemma~\ref{lem:strict-commute} records its immediate commutation and uniqueness property.

Propositions~\ref{prop:worst} and \ref{prop:tail-patch} are the first genuinely finite-window obstruction results: they exhibit an extensive discrepancy family and rule out every bounded-width tail repair. Theorem~\ref{thm:pair-language} is the structural core of Section~\ref{sec:gauge}: earlier Erd\H{o}s-measure papers \cite{AlexanderZagier1991,SidorovVershik1998,BezhaevaOseledets2007,BezhaevaOseledets2010,KemptonDigits2014} treat the normalized-digit process, whereas Theorem~\ref{thm:pair-language} identifies the exact interior language of the pair observable. Theorem~\ref{thm:joint} upgrades the classical marginal frequency \(5/18\) from \cite[Theorem~1.2]{KemptonDigits2014} to the full joint law and the anomaly rate \(4/9\). Corollary~\ref{thm:llnclt} is the corresponding Markov-chain LLN/CLT for the discrepancy observable, with model-specific constants \(4/9\) and \(118/243\), and Theorem~\ref{thm:spectrum} adds the closed covariance formula and rational power spectrum.

In Section~\ref{sec:sequence}, Proposition~\ref{prop:sofic} is the standard sliding-block-code soficity statement \cite[Theorem~1]{Weiss1973}. The new sequence-level content lies in Theorem~\ref{thm:core-resolution}, which classifies the minimal ambiguities into two length-\(3\) cores and eight length-\(4\) cores and proves that a third label resolves every case, in Theorem~\ref{thm:conjugacy}, which turns that decoder into the sharp conjugacy threshold \(m\ge 3\), and in Proposition~\ref{thm:m2}, which isolates the unique non-injective fiber at \(m=2\).

Section~\ref{sec:partition} then extracts the model-specific transfer-matrix consequences of the same four-state presentation. Proposition~\ref{prop:recurrence} records the explicit quartic recurrence and rational generating function, Corollary~\ref{thm:hankel} identifies the only exceptional parameters \(y=0\) and \(y=1\), Proposition~\ref{prop:discriminant} factors the discriminant completely, and Corollary~\ref{thm:weight-stats} promotes the classical density \(5/18\) from \cite[Theorem~1.2]{KemptonDigits2014} to the fold-pushforward LLN, CLT, and LDP with explicit constants.

\paragraph{Proof architecture.}
Appendix~\ref{app:rewriting} proves the normalization and confluence statements used in Section~\ref{sec:multiscale}. Section~\ref{sec:gauge} contains the main proof that the interior reversed-pair language is exactly the label language of a four-state synchronized right-resolving presentation; Appendix~\ref{app:pressure} supplies the synchronized length-\(3\) and length-\(4\) classifications, the local weak-limit argument, and the transfer-matrix calculations behind the bulk constants. Section~\ref{sec:sequence} contains the main ambiguous-core argument proving the \(m\ge3\) decoder threshold; Appendix~\ref{app:sofic} records the complete \(m=3\) and \(m=4\) core tables and the corresponding fiber-product witnesses. Section~\ref{sec:partition} keeps only the structural recurrence and discriminant statements in the body, while Appendix~\ref{app:recurrence} contains the weighted-automaton linear representation and the remaining algebraic derivations.

\paragraph{Organization.}
Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries} introduces the window model, Fibonacci evaluation, Zeckendorf normalization, and a running example. Section~\ref{sec:multiscale} develops folding and fold-aware restriction. Section~\ref{sec:gauge} proves the bulk pair-language theorem and derives the exact discrepancy statistics. Section~\ref{sec:sequence} establishes the sequence-level sofic model and the conjugacy threshold. Section~\ref{sec:partition} records the weighted recurrence, discriminant, and output-weight statistics, and Section~\ref{sec:discussion} closes with the main consequences of these results.
