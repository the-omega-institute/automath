\section{Introduction}

Finite-window observations of binary words depend on the readout convention used to pass from microscopic configurations to stable observables. When that readout is not multiscale consistent, structures visible at one resolution need not agree with those seen after refinement. In the present setting this failure appears combinatorially, as a non-commutation of truncation with Zeckendorf normalization, and statistically, as a non-vanishing density of mismatched digits.

This paper studies a readout scheme obtained by Fibonacci-weighted evaluation of a microstate word, Zeckendorf normalization, and truncation back to the observation window. We call the resulting projection the \emph{Fibonacci fold}. On the stable side it lands in the golden-mean language, the basic symbolic model for Fibonacci numeration and Zeckendorf representations \cite{Lekkerkerker1952,Zeckendorf1972,Brown1964,Fraenkel1985,Frougny1988,Frougny1992,FrougnyConfluent1992,FrougnySolomyak1996,AlloucheShallit2003,ShallitShan2023}. On the microstate side, requiring compatibility with stable restriction singles out a unique \emph{fold-aware restriction}. Comparing it with naive truncation produces a visible mismatch. We call this mismatch the \emph{gauge anomaly}, and throughout we use the synonymous neutral phrase \emph{restriction discrepancy}: it is a symbolic-dynamics discrepancy created by truncation before versus after normalization, not a new physical effect.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
The paper has six main outputs.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2.2em]
\item We define the window-level folds \(\Fold_m:\Om_m\to X_m\), prove idempotence and surjectivity, and realize them by a terminating and confluent rewriting system.
\item We build a commuting multiscale diagram by introducing the fold-aware restriction \(\aware_{m+1\to m}\), characterized by \(\Fold_m\circ \aware_{m+1\to m}=\proj_{m+1\to m}\circ \Fold_{m+1}\).
\item We define the gauge anomaly (restriction discrepancy) \(G_m\) as the Hamming distance between naive truncation and fold-aware restriction, exhibit extensive worst-case deviations, and prove that no bounded tail patch can remove them uniformly.
\item Under the uniform microstate baseline, we prove that the bulk \((X_t,Y_t)\)-pair process is governed by an explicit synchronized four-state right Fischer cover, and from that model derive the exact joint law, anomaly density \(4/9\), central-limit variance \(118/243\), closed-form autocovariances, and a rational power spectral density. The marginal density \(\PP(Y_0=1)=5/18\) is classical in the two-sided normalization literature, but the joint pair/discrepancy law is the new observable here.
\item We lift folding to a sliding block code \(\Phi_m\) on the full two-sided shift, prove that its image is sofic, and show that \(\Phi_m\) is topologically conjugate to its image precisely from window size \(m=3\) onward.
\item We record the weighted fold-fiber partition function in an explicit quartic transfer-matrix package, obtaining a fourth-order recurrence, an exceptional-parameter analysis, and a completely factored discriminant.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Related work and novelty positioning.}
Classical Zeckendorf theory goes back to Lekkerkerker and Zeckendorf \cite{Lekkerkerker1952,Zeckendorf1972}; broader numeration-theoretic context may be found in \cite{Brown1964,Fraenkel1985,Frougny1988,Frougny1992,FrougnyConfluent1992,FrougnySolomyak1996,FrougnySakarovitch1998,AlloucheShallit2003,ShallitShan2023,Berstel1985}. Finite-state normalization in Fibonacci and more general confluent numeration systems is standard \cite{Frougny1988,FrougnyConfluent1992,BerendFrougny1994,Frougny1992,FrougnySolomyak1996,FrougnySakarovitch1998,Frougny1999}, as are sofic shifts, right-resolving presentations, and sliding block codes \cite{Weiss1973,LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998,Blanchard1989}. Likewise, once a finite-state model has been identified, the Parry measure, Perron--Frobenius analysis, and Markov-chain LLN/CLT machinery used later are standard \cite{Parry1964,ParryPollicott1990,Walters1982,Billingsley1961,Gordin1969,Ruelle2004,Bowen2008}.

For the golden-ratio Erd\H{o}s measure itself, the hidden-Markov/sofic viewpoint is already part of the literature. Alexander--Zagier \cite{AlexanderZagier1991}, Sidorov--Vershik \cite{SidorovVershik1998}, and Kempton \cite{KemptonDigits2014} analyze the two-sided normalization picture and its digit statistics, including the classical density \(5/18\). Most relevant here, Bezhaeva and Oseledets \cite{BezhaevaOseledets2007} explicitly prove that Erd\H{o}s measures are sofic and represent them by a regular \(5\)-state Markov chain, while \cite{BezhaevaOseledets2010} studies the corresponding Erd\H{o}s--Vershik problem for the golden ratio; for related second-order Markov refinements see \cite{BezhaevaOseledets2Step}. These papers are part of the present paper's background and should be read as prior art for the normalized-digit process itself.

The present manuscript therefore does \emph{not} claim the finite-state or hidden-Markov viewpoint as new. Its new object is the finite-window comparison between two readouts of the same microstate: naive restriction and fold-aware restriction. The resulting pair observable \((X_t,Y_t)\), and the derived discrepancy process \(\Delta_t=X_t\oplus Y_t\), are different from the classical normalized-digit chain. What is new here is the fold-aware restriction itself, the restriction discrepancy it produces, the exact joint and discrepancy laws for that observable, the closed-form covariance/spectral formulas, and the sharp decoder threshold \(m\ge 3\).

\subsection{Theorem-by-theorem comparison with prior work}
Definition~\ref{def:fold} appears to be a paper-specific finite-window construction: we did not find this exact evaluation-normalize-truncate map isolated in the classical Zeckendorf or automata-theoretic literature. Lemma~\ref{lem:convention} is classical and is essentially the standard \(011\to100\) normalization rule in MSD-first form, as in Shallit--Shan \cite[Proposition~2]{ShallitShan2023}. Proposition~\ref{prop:idempotent} is best read as the finite-window repackaging of that same normalization input and of classical Zeckendorf uniqueness, not as a separate new theorem. Definition~\ref{def:fold-aware} is new in that it introduces the restriction operator forced by the commuting square. Lemma~\ref{lem:strict-commute} is then a direct consequence of that definition, with the concrete gain being uniqueness of the commutative restriction map.

Proposition~\ref{prop:worst} appears new, and its concrete gain is an explicit family with discrepancy \(G_m=m\) at every visible site. Proposition~\ref{prop:tail-patch} also appears new, and its gain is an impossibility theorem for all bounded-width tail repairs, not merely a failure of one particular truncation rule. Theorem~\ref{thm:pair-language} is the main structural novelty in the gauge-anomaly section: earlier Erd\H{o}s-measure papers \cite{AlexanderZagier1991,SidorovVershik1998,BezhaevaOseledets2007,BezhaevaOseledets2010,KemptonDigits2014} treat the normalized-digit process, whereas this theorem identifies the exact interior language of the pair observable. Theorem~\ref{thm:joint} should therefore be positioned as an upgrade of the classical marginal frequency \(5/18\) from Kempton \cite[Theorem~1.2]{KemptonDigits2014} to the full joint law and anomaly rate \(4/9\). Corollary~\ref{thm:llnclt} is not a separate conceptual breakthrough but a standard Gordin-type consequence \cite[Theorem~1]{Gordin1969} once the four-state model is available, with the new content confined to the constants \(4/9\) and \(118/243\). Theorem~\ref{thm:spectrum} does add genuine new information, namely the closed covariance formula and rational power spectrum for the discrepancy process.

Proposition~\ref{prop:sofic} is classical symbolic dynamics in the sense of Weiss \cite[Theorem~1]{Weiss1973}; the novelty here is only the manuscript's concrete graph, not the soficity conclusion itself. Theorem~\ref{thm:core-resolution} appears new, and its concrete gain is the complete list of two length-\(3\) and eight length-\(4\) ambiguous cores together with the three-label decoder. Theorem~\ref{thm:conjugacy} should be positioned as new because the sharp threshold \(m\ge3\) and the explicit inverse code are not part of the standard finite-state-code literature. Proposition~\ref{thm:m2} is the complementary new edge case, isolating the unique non-injective fiber at \(m=2\).

Proposition~\ref{prop:recurrence} is best described as routine transfer-matrix algebra once the weighted automaton is fixed, and its concrete gain is the explicit quartic recurrence and generating function for this model. Corollary~\ref{thm:hankel} is a direct algebraic consequence of the same quartic package, with the useful new datum being that only \(y=0\) and \(y=1\) lower the effective order. Proposition~\ref{prop:discriminant} is likewise model-specific bookkeeping rather than a new general theorem, but the complete factorization does isolate the exact spectral-degeneracy locus. Corollary~\ref{thm:weight-stats} should be tied explicitly to the classical density \(5/18\) from Kempton \cite[Theorem~1.2]{KemptonDigits2014}; its genuine increment is the fold-pushforward CLT with variance \(67/972\) and the full large-deviation principle.

\paragraph{Proof architecture.}
Appendix~\ref{app:rewriting} proves the normalization and confluence statements used in Section~\ref{sec:multiscale}. Section~\ref{sec:gauge} contains the main proof that the interior reversed-pair language is exactly the label language of a four-state right Fischer cover; Appendix~\ref{app:pressure} supplies the synchronized length-\(3\) and length-\(4\) classifications, the adjacency matrix, and the transfer-matrix calculations behind the bulk constants. Section~\ref{sec:sequence} contains the main ambiguous-core argument proving the \(m\ge3\) decoder threshold; Appendix~\ref{app:sofic} records the complete \(m=3\) and \(m=4\) core tables and the corresponding fiber-product witnesses. Section~\ref{sec:partition} keeps only the structural recurrence and discriminant statements in the body, while Appendix~\ref{app:recurrence} contains the routine transfer-matrix derivations.

\paragraph{Organization.}
Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries} introduces the window model, Fibonacci evaluation, Zeckendorf normalization, and a running example. Section~\ref{sec:multiscale} develops folding and fold-aware restriction. Section~\ref{sec:gauge} proves the bulk pair-language theorem and derives the exact discrepancy statistics. Section~\ref{sec:sequence} establishes the sequence-level sofic model and the conjugacy threshold. Section~\ref{sec:partition} records the weighted recurrence, discriminant, and output-weight statistics, and Section~\ref{sec:discussion} closes with consequences and open directions.
