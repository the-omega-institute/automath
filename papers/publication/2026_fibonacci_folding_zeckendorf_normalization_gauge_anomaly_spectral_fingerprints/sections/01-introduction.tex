\section{Introduction}

Finite-window observations of binary words depend on the readout convention used to pass from microscopic configurations to stable observables. When that readout is not \emph{multiscale consistent}, structures visible at one resolution need not agree with those seen after refinement. Such failures appear combinatorially, as an incompatibility of normal forms under restriction, and statistically, as non-vanishing anomaly densities.

This paper studies a readout scheme obtained by combining Fibonacci-weighted evaluation of a microstate word with Zeckendorf normalization, followed by truncation back to the window. We call the resulting projection the \emph{Fibonacci fold}. On the stable side it lands in the golden-mean language, the basic symbolic model for Fibonacci numeration and Zeckendorf representations \cite{Lekkerkerker1952,Zeckendorf1972,Brown1964,Fraenkel1985,Frougny1992,AlloucheShallit2003,ShallitShan2023}. On the microstate side we show that the requirement of commutation with stable restriction uniquely determines a \emph{fold-aware restriction} operator; comparing this with naive truncation yields a gauge anomaly. The term is introduced here: it is not borrowed from the existing numeration or symbolic-dynamics literature.

Our viewpoint places a finite-window numeration problem inside the standard symbolic-dynamics and transfer-matrix framework of sofic shifts, finite-state codes, and thermodynamic formalism \cite{Weiss1973,LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998,ParryPollicott1990,Ruelle2004,Bowen2008}. What appears to be a local truncation issue is therefore encoded by a finite sofic model, and the exact constants in the anomaly law become accessible through Perron--Frobenius and Markov-chain methods \cite{Billingsley1961,Gordin1969}. To our knowledge, the exact anomaly density \(4/9\), the variance \(118/243\), and the closed-form spectral formulas of Section~\ref{sec:gauge} are new.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
Our main contributions can be summarized as follows.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2.2em]
\item We define a family of window-level folding maps \(\Fold_m:\Om_m\to X_m\), prove idempotence and surjectivity, and give a terminating and confluent rewriting realization.
\item We build a commuting multiscale diagram by introducing the fold-aware restriction \(\aware_{m+1\to m}\), characterized by \(\Fold_m\circ \aware_{m+1\to m}=\proj_{m+1\to m}\circ \Fold_{m+1}\), and show that it yields strict consistency between ``restrict-then-fold'' and ``fold-then-restrict''.
\item We define the gauge anomaly \(G_m\) as the Hamming distance between naive truncation and fold-aware restriction. We exhibit extensive worst-case deviations and prove that no bounded tail patch can uniformly correct them.
\item Under the uniform microstate baseline, we derive exact bulk statistics: a joint law for a naive bit and its fold-aware counterpart, a law of large numbers for anomaly density with limit \(4/9\), a central limit theorem with variance constant \(118/243\), and closed-form autocovariances and a rational power spectrum.
\item We lift folding to a sliding block code \(\Phi_m\) on the two-sided full shift, show that its image is sofic with an explicit labeled graph model, and prove a conjugacy threshold at \(m\ge 3\).
\item We study a weighted partition function over fold fibers, obtain a fourth-order minimal recurrence, identify Hankel-rank collapse, and factor the discriminant to locate Lee--Yang type branch points.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Related work.}
Classical Zeckendorf theory goes back to Lekkerkerker and Zeckendorf \cite{Lekkerkerker1952,Zeckendorf1972}; broader numeration-theoretic context may be found in Brown's early note, Fraenkel's survey, and in automata-oriented treatments by Frougny, Allouche, and Shallit \cite{Brown1964,Fraenkel1985,Frougny1992,AlloucheShallit2003,ShallitShan2023}. On the dynamical side, our sofic and conjugacy arguments use standard notions from symbolic dynamics and finite-state coding \cite{Weiss1973,LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998}. The transfer-matrix and pressure arguments in Sections~\ref{sec:gauge} and \ref{sec:partition} are modeled on the usual thermodynamic-formalism framework \cite{ParryPollicott1990,Ruelle2004,Bowen2008}, and the central-limit inputs are standard Markov-chain results \cite{Billingsley1961,Gordin1969}. The Lee--Yang terminology in Section~\ref{sec:partition} is used in the classical sense of singularities detected by discriminants of partition-function recurrences \cite{LeeYang1952}. What is specific to the present paper is the numeration-theoretic folding mechanism, the multiscale commuting diagram, and the exact anomaly law they produce.

\paragraph{Organization.}
Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries} introduces the window model, Fibonacci evaluation, and Zeckendorf normalization. Section~\ref{sec:multiscale} develops the folding projection, multiscale restriction, and the fold-aware restriction operator. Section~\ref{sec:gauge} defines the gauge anomaly and proves its worst-case behavior and limiting laws, including closed-form second-order statistics. Section~\ref{sec:sequence} upgrades folding to a sliding block code and establishes soficity and conjugacy. Section~\ref{sec:partition} studies weighted partition functions, Hankel rank, discriminants, and the resulting Lee--Yang branch point. Proof details are collected in the appendices.
