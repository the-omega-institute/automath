\section{Discriminant squarefree fingerprints: an arithmetic audit for collision-kernel recurrences}
\label{sec:fingerprint}

\subsection{Fingerprint definition}
Consider a collision recursion induced by a finite moment-kernel of order \(q\), with an associated minimal recurrence polynomial
\[
P_q(\lambda)\in \ZZ[\lambda].
\]
Let \(\Delta_q:=\Disc(P_q)\) be its discriminant and let \(\Delta_q^{\mathrm{sf}}\) denote the squarefree part of \(\Delta_q\).

\begin{definition}[Quadratic discriminant fingerprint]
Define the \emph{\(q\)-fingerprint} as the quadratic field
\[
K_q := \QQ\!\left(\sqrt{\Delta_q^{\mathrm{sf}}}\right).
\]
\end{definition}

\subsection{The q=2 fingerprint equals 37}
\begin{proposition}[A concrete fingerprint]\label{prop:q2}
For \(q=2\), one may take
\[
P_2(\lambda)=\lambda^3-2\lambda^2-2\lambda+2.
\]
Its discriminant is \(\Delta_2=148\), whose squarefree part is \(\Delta_2^{\mathrm{sf}}=37\). Hence
\[
K_2=\QQ(\sqrt{37}).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For a monic cubic \(x^3+ax^2+bx+c\), the discriminant is
\[
\Disc(x^3+ax^2+bx+c)=a^2b^2-4b^3-4a^3c-27c^2+18abc.
\]
Substituting \(a=-2\), \(b=-2\), and \(c=2\) gives
\[
\Delta_2
=
(-2)^2(-2)^2-4(-2)^3-4(-2)^3(2)-27(2)^2+18(-2)(-2)(2)
=
148.
\]
Since \(148=4\cdot 37\) and \(37\) is squarefree, the squarefree part is \(\Delta_2^{\mathrm{sf}}=37\). Therefore
\[
K_2=\QQ\!\left(\sqrt{\Delta_2^{\mathrm{sf}}}\right)=\QQ(\sqrt{37}).
\]
\end{proof}

\subsection{Frobenius parity and Kronecker symbols (audit use)}
Let \(f\in \ZZ[x]\) be an irreducible polynomial with discriminant \(\Delta\). Away from the finitely many ramified primes, the parity of the Frobenius permutation in the Galois group is governed by the Kronecker symbol \(\left(\frac{\Delta}{p}\right)\). Applied to \(P_q\), this yields a \emph{fingerprint--Chebotarev audit}: if empirical factorization types modulo primes \(p\) systematically deviate from the sign pattern dictated by \(\left(\frac{\Delta_q^{\mathrm{sf}}}{p}\right)\), then the proposed kernel model fails an arithmetic consistency check.
