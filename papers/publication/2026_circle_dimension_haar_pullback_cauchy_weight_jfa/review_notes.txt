JFA referee simulation for "Circle Dimension and Haar Pullback under the Cayley Transform"

Recommendation after revision:
Acceptable in principle, subject to final typographical verification by a clean LaTeX build.

Main weaknesses in the earlier draft:
1. The scope was too broad for Journal of Functional Analysis. Several excursions into side topics diluted the harmonic-analysis and Pontryagin-duality core.
2. The uniqueness statement for the Haar pullback needed a sharper formulation with explicit orientation and boundary normalization hypotheses.
3. The discussion of the precision potential previously risked overstating its convexity properties.
4. The introduction advertised material that was not essential to the main line of argument, which made the paper read more like a project digest than a journal article.

Fixes applied in the present revision:
1. The paper was reduced to a focused functional-analytic line: Cayley compactification, Haar pullback rigidity, circle dimension for finitely generated abelian groups, and Poisson--Cauchy analysis.
2. The Haar pullback theorem was rewritten as a precise rigidity statement for orientation-preserving C^1 homeomorphisms with the natural boundary normalization.
3. The precision-potential section now states the exact differential-entropy identity and the correct local convexity/concavity properties of log(1+x^2).
4. The introduction and conclusion were rewritten in a more efficient JFA style, with explicit main results and without project-language.
5. Source-level cross-reference and citation checks were carried out, and the bibliography was pruned to the entries actually used in the paper.

Residual point before submission:
Run a clean pdflatex/bibtex/pdflatex/pdflatex cycle in an environment with TeX binaries available, and check for any purely typographical warnings that cannot be detected by source inspection alone.
