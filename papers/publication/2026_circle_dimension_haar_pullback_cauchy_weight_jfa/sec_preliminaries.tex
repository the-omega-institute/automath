\section{Cayley coordinates and the Haar pullback}%
\label{sec:preliminaries}\label{sec:haar-pullback}

We fix the notation and normalizations used throughout the paper.  The
same section also records the only circle-theoretic input needed later:
the Cayley chart identifies normalized Haar measure on~$\TT$ with the
Cauchy weight on~$\RR$.

Write
\[
\TT:=\{z\in\CC:\abs{z}=1\},\qquad
\DD:=\{z\in\CC:\abs{z}<1\},\qquad
\UU:=\Bigl(-\tfrac12,\tfrac12\Bigr).
\]
The normalized Haar measure on~$\TT$ is
\[
\dd m_{\TT}(e^{\ii\theta})=\frac{1}{2\pi}\dd\theta,
\qquad
\theta\in(-\pi,\pi].
\]
We write
\[
\overline\RR:=\RR\cup\{\infty\}
\]
for the one-point compactification of~$\RR$.  For $f\in L^{1}(\RR)$ we
use the Fourier transform
\[
\widehat f(\xi):=\int_{\RR}f(x)e^{-\ii x\xi}\dd x,
\qquad \xi\in\RR,
\]
so that $\widehat{f*g}=\widehat f\,\widehat g$ whenever both sides are
defined.
For $f\in L^{1}(\RR)\cap L^{2}(\RR)$ we also use the inversion and
Plancherel formulas
\[
f(x)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{\RR}\widehat f(\xi)e^{\ii x\xi}\dd\xi,
\qquad
\|f\|_{L^2(\RR)}^{2}=\frac{1}{2\pi}\|\widehat f\|_{L^2(\RR)}^{2}
\]
\cite[Chapter~I]{Stein1971}.

If $X$ is a real-valued random variable with density $f_X$ with respect
to Lebesgue measure, its differential entropy is
\[
h_{\mathrm{diff}}(X):=-\int_{\RR}f_X(x)\log f_X(x)\dd x,
\]
whenever the integral is well defined.  If $f$ and $g$ are probability
density functions on~$\RR$ and $f\log(f/g)\in L^1(\RR)$, the
Kullback--Leibler divergence is
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(f\|g):=\int_{\RR}f(x)\log\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\dd x.
\]
We shall repeatedly use the elementary identity
\[
\int_{\RR}(f-g)\dd x=0
\]
whenever $f$ and $g$ are probability densities.
