\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Let
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2},
\qquad
P_t(x)=\frac{1}{\pi}\frac{t}{x^2+t^2}.
\]
The identity $K(a,b)=\pi P_2(a-b)$ places the paper simultaneously in
the language of Poisson smoothing and in that of
translation-invariant reproducing kernels.  If $\nu$ is a probability
measure on~$\RR$ with mean~$\bar\gamma$, we compare
\[
h_t:=P_t*\nu
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
g_t(x):=P_t(x-\bar\gamma).
\]
After the rescaling $x=\bar\gamma+ty$, the relevant quotient is
\[
\frac{P_t(x-\gamma)}{g_t(x)}
=R_{\Delta/t}(y),
\qquad
R_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{1+y^2}{1+(y-\varepsilon)^2},
\qquad
\Delta:=\gamma-\bar\gamma.
\]
The starting point of the paper is that, after the Cayley change of
variables $y=\tan\theta$, the kernel $R_\varepsilon$ becomes a finite
trigonometric polynomial.  The paper is concerned with the consequences
of this observation for one fixed kernel~$K$: the same secant family
controls the large-time entropy expansion and the concrete Hilbert-space
realization attached to~$K$.

The first part of the paper is asymptotic.  Theorem~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode}
identifies the Taylor coefficients of $R_\varepsilon$ with elementary
trigonometric modes.  The parity built into this representation implies
that the expansion of
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))
\]
contains only even powers of~$t^{-1}$; see
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders}.  Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}
then gives the coefficients through order~$t^{-8}$.  The subsequent
defect decomposition, stated in
Theorems~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder}
and~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification}, isolates the symmetric
two-point law as the extremal configuration and leads to the
quantitative $W_2$-stability estimate of
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability}.

The second part of the paper shows that the same secant family has a
precise Hilbert-space meaning.  The
secant family
\[
\Psi_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{R_\varepsilon(y)-1}{\varepsilon}
\]
has Gram kernel $K$, as shown in
Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-gram-kernel}.  Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-space-rkhs}
identifies the closed secant span with the zero-mean space
$L^2_0(\omega)$ and, via the associated unitary map, with the
reproducing-kernel Hilbert space~$\mathcal H_K$.  In
Theorem~\ref{thm:higher-secant-kernels} the quadratic Gram identity is
extended to explicit higher multilinear secant kernels, and the
coefficients in the expansion of
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))
\]
are recovered as Taylor coefficients of those kernels.  The quadratic
member of this hierarchy yields, through
Corollary~\ref{cor:quadratic-secant-energy}, a completely monotone
Hilbert energy represented as the Laplace transform of
$|\phi_{\nu_c}'|^2$.  In
Proposition~\ref{thm:gram-space-poisson-image} we realize
$\mathcal H_K$ as $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$, equivalently as a
Hardy space on the strip $\{z\in\CC:\ |\Im z|<1\}$, and
Proposition~\ref{prop:poisson-hardy-splitting} records the resulting
Hardy splitting.  This realization yields closed formulas for the
orthogonal projector onto $\mathcal H_K(h\ZZ)$, the periodized weight,
the sampling bounds on~$\ZZ$, and the cardinal interpolation norm; see
Theorems~\ref{thm:uniform-lattice-projection},
\ref{thm:poisson-lattice-sampling},
and~\ref{thm:poisson-cardinal-reconstruction}.

The general frameworks behind these statements are classical.  On the
analytic side we use standard facts on the Poisson semigroup and its
Fourier representation
\cite{Folland1995,Grafakos2014,Stein1971}, together with Laplace
uniqueness and characteristic-function inversion
\cite{Widder1941,Lukacs1970,Feller1971}.  On the entropy side the paper
should be viewed against the background of the entropic central limit
theorem and stable-law asymptotics
\cite{Barron1986,JohnsonBarron2004,ArtsteinBallBartheNaor2004,CarlenCarvalho1992,BobkovChistyakovGotzeEdgeworth2013,BobkovChistyakovGotzeBerryEsseen2014,BobkovChistyakovGotze2013,Toscani2015,Toscani2016}.
For the Hilbert-space and sampling aspects we rely on the theory of
translation-invariant kernels and principal shift-invariant spaces
\cite{Aronszajn1950,deBoorDeVoreRon1994,RonShen1995,Bownik2000,AldroubiGrochenig2001}
and on more recent sampling results
\cite{GrochenigRomeroStockler2018,GrochenigRomeroStockler2020,FuhrGrochenigHaimiKlotzRomero2018}.
Related explicit interpolation phenomena for Cauchy-type generators
appear in
\cite{KiselevMininNovikov2016,KiselevMininNovikov2019,BelovKulikovLyubarskii2022,BelovKulikovLyubarskii2023,UlanovskiiZlotnikov2025}.
The strip-space and lattice-sampling statements are included as
model-specific specializations of those theories.  For the kernel
$K(a,b)=2/(4+(a-b)^2)$, the same secant family that governs the entropy
expansion also supplies the Gram orbit of the RKHS, so the coefficient
extraction, the Hilbert realization, and the lattice formulas can all
be written in closed form within a single model.

The Cayley transform
\[
\iota(x)=\frac{1+\ii x}{1-\ii x}
\]
enters only as a normalization device.  It identifies the one-point
compactification~$\overline\RR$ with~$\TT$ and pulls normalized Haar
measure back to the Cauchy weight
\[
\frac{1}{\pi(1+x^2)}\,\dd x=P_1(x)\,\dd x.
\]
Section~\ref{sec:haar-pullback} records the corresponding rigidity
statement: in the normalized orientation-preserving $C^1$ class, the
pullback condition characterizes the standard Cayley chart.

The paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries}
fixes notation.  Section~\ref{sec:cayley-gate} introduces the Cayley
coordinates, and Section~\ref{sec:haar-pullback} proves the Haar
pullback rigidity statement.  Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential}
contains the mode calculus and the entropy asymptotics.  In
Section~\ref{sec:gram-space} we identify the associated reproducing
kernel space, compute the higher secant kernels, and recover the
centered law from Poisson data.  In
Section~\ref{sec:strip-lattice} we pass to the strip realization and
derive the lattice formulas.  The appendix contains the trigonometric
integral identities needed for the coefficient computation in
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}.
