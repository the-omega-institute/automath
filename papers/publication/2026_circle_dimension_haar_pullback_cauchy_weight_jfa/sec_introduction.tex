\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

\subsection{Setting and main results}

The Cayley transform
\[
\iota(x)=\frac{1+\ii x}{1-\ii x}
\]
identifies the one-point compactification $\overline\RR$ of the line with
the circle~$\TT$.  Under this identification the normalized Haar measure
on~$\TT$ pulls back to the Cauchy law
\[
\frac{1}{\pi(1+x^{2})}\dd x=P_1(x)\dd x.
\]
This places the Poisson semigroup on~$\RR$ in direct contact with the
compactification.  For a probability measure~$\nu$ on~$\RR$ with
mean~$\bar\gamma$, the central object of the paper is the comparison
kernel
\[
R_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{1+y^2}{1+(y-\varepsilon)^2},
\]
which measures the ratio between a translated Poisson profile and the
reference Cauchy density after the large-time rescaling
$x=\bar\gamma+ty$.  Passing to the Cayley angle variable
$y=\tan\theta$ reveals a finite Fourier structure governed by Chebyshev
polynomials of the second kind and yields the mode formulas of
Theorem~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode}.  The mode parity forces the
relative entropy
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ to have only even
powers of~$t^{-1}$ in its large-time expansion
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders}).

We highlight one central theorem and its main consequences.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \textbf{Eighth-order entropy expansion}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}).
An explicit formula expressing the $t^{-4}$, $t^{-6}$, and $t^{-8}$
coefficients of
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$
in terms of centred moments through order six.  The sixth-order term
already matches a Pearson-type skewness--kurtosis combination in these
coordinates, while the $t^{-8}$ coefficient is the genuinely new
coefficient calculation in the paper.

\item \textbf{Defect-ladder decomposition}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder}).
The sixth- and eighth-order entropy coefficients admit non-negative,
algebraically defined defect coordinates---obtained from canceling the
lower-order mode interactions rather than from orthogonal projections.
Its sixth-order identity is the Pearson relation rewritten in the
present variables, whereas the eighth-order layer is new.  The
coordinates are simultaneously minimized by the symmetric two-point
law $\frac12(\delta_{\bar\gamma+\sigma}+\delta_{\bar\gamma-\sigma})$.

\item \textbf{Defect amplification}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification}).
The eighth-order defect
$\Delta_8(\nu)$ is bounded below by
$\frac{13\sigma^2}{8}\Delta_6(\nu)$, with equality only at the
symmetric two-point extremizer.  Thus departures from the extremizer at
sixth order are strictly amplified at eighth order.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability} converts the sixth-order gap
into a quantitative $W_2$-stability statement: small defect forces the
centred law to lie close to the symmetric two-point extremizer at fixed
variance.  We treat this as a coarse corollary of the sixth-order
defect formula rather than as a separate flagship result.

The later appendices record auxiliary RKHS and lattice material
attached to the comparison kernel.  The secant family
$\Psi_\varepsilon(y):=(R_\varepsilon(y)-1)/\varepsilon$
has Gram kernel $K(a,b)=2/(4+(a-b)^2)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-gram-kernel}), and the resulting RKHS is
isometric to the Poisson image space $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$.
Lemma~\ref{lem:rkhs-delta} and
Corollary~\ref{cor:rkhs-strip-control} use these formulas to control
the fluctuation profile generated by Poisson smoothing.  Appendix
~\ref{app:strip-lattice} records the corresponding lattice symbol and
cardinal norm as a companion closed-form specialization.

\subsection{What is new, and what is recovered from known theory}%
\label{subsec:novelty-summary}

The paper contains one main calculation, several direct algebraic
consequences, and auxiliary appendices attached to the same kernel.  We
state that hierarchy explicitly here.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{New results.}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textit{Central theorem: explicit coefficient through order
$t^{-8}$.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth} is the main new theorem.  It gives
the full eighth-order expansion of
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ and identifies the
new moment combination appearing at order $t^{-8}$.

\item \textit{New eighth-order defect geometry.}
The second identity in Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder} and the
amplification inequality of
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification} are algebraic
consequences of Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}, but they produce a
new non-negative eighth-order defect coordinate and a sharp lower bound
$\Delta_8\ge(13\sigma^2/8)\Delta_6$.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Recovered, reformulated, or auxiliary material.}
\begin{itemize}
\item Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders} isolates a structural
parity mechanism: once the Cayley--Chebyshev mode formula is in place,
odd orders vanish automatically.

\item The first identity in
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder} recovers Pearson's classical
skewness--kurtosis inequality
\cite{Pearson1916,KlaassenVanEs2018,OgasawaraExtensions2017,SharmaBhandari2015}
in the present coordinates.  The novelty begins at the eighth-order
layer.

\item Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability} is a simple quantitative
corollary of the sixth-order defect formula.  We sharpen the constants
inside our proof, but we do not claim optimality.

\item The Cayley transform
(Sections~\ref{sec:cayley-gate}--\ref{sec:haar-pullback}), the entropy
change-of-variables identity (Proposition~\ref{thm:precision-ledger}),
and the RKHS/sampling appendices are classical or direct
specializations of standard analytic RKHS and shift-invariant-space
frameworks
\cite{SunZhou2008,TronarpKarvonen2024,deBoorDeVoreRon1994,RonShen1995,AldroubiGrochenig2001}.
Appendix~\ref{app:gram-space} feeds back into the main text through
Lemma~\ref{lem:rkhs-delta} and Corollary~\ref{cor:rkhs-strip-control};
Appendix~\ref{app:strip-lattice} is retained as an auxiliary closed-form
companion calculation for the same kernel.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Relation to entropy literature.}
Johnson \cite{Johnson2013} and Toscani
\cite{Toscani2015,Toscani2016,Toscani2017Score} analyze the
instantaneous entropy-production rate along stable semigroups via
de~Bruijn identities, fractional Fisher information, and stable
score-function formulas.
In particular, \cite{Toscani2017Score} develops the stable score and
generalized Fisher framework that we reference when comparing novelty in
Section~\ref{subsec:novelty-summary}.
Bobkov, Chistyakov, and G\"otze
\cite{BobkovChistyakovGotze2013,BobkovChistyakovGotzeFisher2014,%
BobkovChistyakovGotzeBerryEsseen2014,BobkovChistyakovGotzeEdgeworth2013,%
BobkovChistyakovGotzeRenyi2019}
study convergence to stable laws in relative entropy, Fisher
information, and R\'enyi divergence with the number of summands as the
large parameter.
The R\'enyi-divergence estimates of \cite{BobkovChistyakovGotzeRenyi2019}
provide the higher-order information-theoretic backdrop for our large-$t$
comparison.  Standard stable-law references such as
\cite{SamorodnitskyTaqqu1994,Zolotarev1986} provide the broader
background for the Cauchy and stable families considered here.
Classical entropic CLT works
\cite{Barron1986,ArtsteinBallBartheNaor2004,JohnsonBarron2004}
and their Edgeworth refinements
\cite{BobkovChistyakovGotzeEdgeworth2013,BobkovChistyakovGotzeBerryEsseen2014}
deal with Gaussian targets and cumulant expansions.
Our problem is different in two ways: the measure $\nu$ is fixed, and
the large parameter is the Poisson smoothing time $t$ rather than the
number of summands.  Direct divergence formulas for Cauchy location and
scale families
\cite{ChyzakNielsen2019,NielsenOkamura2023,Verdu2023}
therefore address a different question from the one studied here.
The same distinction separates our setting from i.i.d.\ stable-limit
theory.  Finally, weighted functional inequalities for Cauchy measures
\cite{BobkovLedoux2009} and broader information-theoretic work in
impulsive noise environments \cite{FahsAbouFaycal2018} provide useful
analytic context, but do not produce the large-$t$ coefficient
expansion or defect identities proved here.

\subsection{Logical structure of the main results}
\label{subsec:logical-structure}

The results in Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential} form a linear
dependency chain.  Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth} is the central
theorem; all subsequent results are consequences of it.

\[
\underbrace{\text{Thm.~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode}}}_{\text{mode formula}}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\underbrace{\text{Thm.~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders}}}_{\text{odd orders vanish}}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\underbrace{\text{Thm.~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}}}_{\text{eighth-order expansion}}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\begin{cases}
\text{Thm.~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder}} \\
\text{Thm.~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification}}
\end{cases}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\underbrace{\text{Thm.~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability}}}_{W_2\text{-stability}}
\]

More precisely:
\begin{itemize}
\item Theorem~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode} (Cayley--Chebyshev mode
formula, Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential}) establishes the
trigonometric expansion of the comparison kernel modes.
\item Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders} (odd orders vanish)
follows immediately via the parity of the modes, using
Lemma~\ref{lem:mode-parity-vanish} and
Lemma~\ref{lem:delta-expansion}.
\item Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth} (eighth-order expansion) is the
technically strongest statement in the paper.  It requires the explicit
mode integrals of Appendix~\ref{app:entropy-integrals} and the
coefficient assembly of Appendix~\ref{app:coefficient-assembly}.
\item Theorems~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder}
and~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification} are algebraic consequences of
the coefficients produced by Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}.
They require no additional moment hypotheses beyond those of
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}.
\item Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability} ($W_2$-stability) follows
from the sixth-order defect formula via an elementary coupling argument
that requires only finite fourth moment; it is a coarse corollary, and
we do not optimize its constants.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Organization}

Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation.
Section~\ref{sec:cayley-gate} records the Cayley formulas, and
Section~\ref{sec:haar-pullback} proves the Haar pullback rigidity.
Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential} is the technical core: it
develops the entropy identity, the mode calculus, the eighth-order
expansion, the defect amplification, and the $W_2$-stability theorem.
Appendix~\ref{app:entropy-integrals} records the weighted trigonometric
integrals, Appendix~\ref{app:coefficient-assembly} provides the
step-by-step coefficient verification for the eighth-order term,
Appendix~\ref{app:gram-space} computes the mode Gram kernel and its RKHS
used in Lemma~\ref{lem:rkhs-delta} and
Corollary~\ref{cor:rkhs-strip-control}, and
Appendix~\ref{app:strip-lattice} records the auxiliary lattice symbol
and cardinal norm identity for the integer secant orbit.
