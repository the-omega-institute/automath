\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

The paper is organized around a Poisson/Cauchy model with
translation-invariant kernel
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2}.
\]
The Cayley transform
\[
\iota(x)=\frac{1+\ii x}{1-\ii x}
\]
identifies the one-point compactification~$\overline\RR$ of the line
with the circle~$\TT$, and normalized Haar measure on~$\TT$ pulls back
to the Cauchy law
\[
\frac{1}{\pi(1+x^{2})}\dd x=P_1(x)\dd x.
\]
This classical identity is used as a normalization device.  It places
the problem directly in the Poisson semigroup on~$\RR$ and turns the
comparison kernel into a finite trigonometric object after the angular
substitution $y=\tan\theta$.  The rigidity statement of
Theorem~\ref{thm:haar-pullback-uniqueness} is therefore preliminary: in
the normalized orientation-preserving $C^1$ class, the pullback
condition already forces the chart to be the Cayley transform.

The first group of results concerns large-time entropy asymptotics for
Poisson averages.  Let $\nu$ be a probability measure on~$\RR$ with mean
$\bar\gamma$, and set $h_t:=P_t*\nu$ and
$g_t(x):=P_t(x-\bar\gamma)$.  After the rescaling $x=\bar\gamma+ty$, the
comparison between $h_t$ and~$g_t$ is governed by the kernel
\[
R_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{1+y^2}{1+(y-\varepsilon)^2}.
\]
Passing to the angular variable $y=\tan\theta$ turns this rational
function into a finite trigonometric object.  Theorem~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode}
records the resulting Cayley--Chebyshev mode calculus, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders} shows that the expansion of
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))
\]
contains only even powers of~$t^{-1}$.  The coefficient of
order~$t^{-8}$ is computed in Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}.  When
rewritten in defect variables adapted to the symmetric two-point law, it
leads to the amplification estimate of
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification} and the quantitative
$W_2$-stability statement of
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability}.

The second part of the paper is functional-analytic.  The secant family
\[
\Psi_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{R_\varepsilon(y)-1}{\varepsilon}
\]
has Gram kernel
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2},
\]
as shown in Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-gram-kernel}.  Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-space-rkhs}
identifies the closed span of the secants with the zero-mean space
$L^2_0(\omega)$ and with the reproducing-kernel
Hilbert space generated by~$K$.  In this framework the centered law can
be recovered from two central Poisson observation channels; see
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-central-two-channel}.  Proposition~\ref{thm:gram-space-poisson-image}
then realizes the same space as $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$, hence as
a translation-invariant strip space with holomorphic extension to
\[
\mathbb S=\{z\in\CC:\ |\Im z|<1\},
\]
and Proposition~\ref{prop:poisson-hardy-splitting} gives the associated
Hardy decomposition.  Theorem~\ref{thm:uniform-lattice-projection}
extends this strip model to every uniform lattice $h\ZZ$: the
lattice-generated subspace $\mathcal H_K(h\ZZ)$ is characterized by a
weighted periodicity condition on $e^{2|\xi|}\widehat f(\xi)$, the
orthogonal projector is given by an exact alias-averaging formula, and
the orthogonal complement consists precisely of those functions whose
lattice samples vanish.  Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-lattice-sampling} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-cardinal-reconstruction} then extract the
explicit $h=1$ Riesz bounds, lattice symbol, and cardinal interpolation
formula.

The ingredients are classical: the Poisson semigroup and its Fourier
representation \cite{Folland1995,Grafakos2014,Stein1971},
Laplace-transform uniqueness \cite{Widder1941},
characteristic-function inversion \cite{Lukacs1970,Feller1971},
reproducing kernels \cite{Aronszajn1950}, and Hardy-space boundary
theory \cite{Duren1970,Rudin1987}.  The special feature of the present
setting is that the Cayley normalization makes the comparison kernel
finite in the angular variable.  This is what allows the entropy
coefficients to be computed explicitly and the Hilbert-space model to be
written in closed form.

Relevant background on the entropy side includes the literature on the
entropic central limit theorem, entropy production, and stable-law
asymptotics
\cite{Barron1986,JohnsonBarron2004,ArtsteinBallBartheNaor2004,CarlenCarvalho1992}
and
\cite{BobkovChistyakovGotzeEdgeworth2013,BobkovChistyakovGotzeBerryEsseen2014,BobkovChistyakovGotze2013,Toscani2015,Toscani2016}.
The emphasis here is different.  The analysis is carried out in a rigid
Poisson/Cauchy model in which the parity of the modes forces the
disappearance of odd orders and the first nontrivial defect terms
already detect the symmetric two-point extremizer.

The sampling and RKHS part relies on the general theory of
finitely generated and principal shift-invariant spaces
\cite{deBoorDeVoreRon1994,RonShen1995,Bownik2000,AldroubiGrochenig2001},
together with later sampling results in reproducing-kernel and totally
positive settings
\cite{GrochenigRomeroStockler2018,GrochenigRomeroStockler2020,FuhrGrochenigHaimiKlotzRomero2018}.
Explicit interpolation formulae for Cauchy-type generators appear in
\cite{KiselevMininNovikov2016,KiselevMininNovikov2019}, and related
rational generators also occur in
\cite{BelovKulikovLyubarskii2022,BelovKulikovLyubarskii2023,UlanovskiiZlotnikov2025}.
The increment on this side consists of the exact Poisson-strip
realization for~$K$, the alias-projection theorem for every uniform
lattice, and the resulting closed formulas for the lattice symbol, the
cardinal kernel, and the interpolation norm on the integer orbit.

The paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries}
fixes notation.  Section~\ref{sec:cayley-gate} records the Cayley
parametrization, and Section~\ref{sec:haar-pullback} proves the Haar
pullback rigidity theorem.  Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential}
contains the entropy expansion and the defect analysis.
Section~\ref{sec:gram-space} develops the Gram-space and reconstruction
results.  Section~\ref{sec:strip-lattice} gives the strip realization,
the uniform-lattice projection theorem, and the explicit lattice
sampling theory.  The appendix collects the weighted trigonometric
integrals and the coefficient bookkeeping used in the proof of the
eighth-order expansion.
