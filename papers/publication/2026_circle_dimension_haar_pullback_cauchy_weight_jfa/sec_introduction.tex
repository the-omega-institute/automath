\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

\subsection{Setting and main results}

The Cayley transform
\[
\iota(x)=\frac{1+\ii x}{1-\ii x}
\]
identifies the one-point compactification $\overline\RR$ of the line with
the circle~$\TT$.  Under this identification the normalized Haar measure
on~$\TT$ pulls back to the Cauchy law
\[
\frac{1}{\pi(1+x^{2})}\dd x=P_1(x)\dd x.
\]
This places the Poisson semigroup on~$\RR$ in direct contact with the
compactification.  For a probability measure~$\nu$ on~$\RR$ with
mean~$\bar\gamma$, the central object of the paper is the comparison
kernel
\[
R_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{1+y^2}{1+(y-\varepsilon)^2},
\]
which measures the ratio between a translated Poisson profile and the
reference Cauchy density after the large-time rescaling
$x=\bar\gamma+ty$.  Passing to the Cayley angle variable
$y=\tan\theta$ reveals a finite Fourier structure governed by Chebyshev
polynomials of the second kind, giving the
\emph{Cayley--Chebyshev mode calculus}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode}).  The mode parity forces the
relative entropy
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ to have only even
powers of~$t^{-1}$ in its large-time expansion
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders}).

The paper's \textbf{main contributions} are:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \textbf{Eighth-order entropy expansion}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}).
An explicit formula expressing the $t^{-4}$, $t^{-6}$, and $t^{-8}$
coefficients of
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$
in terms of centred moments through order six.  The $t^{-8}$ coefficient
appears to be new.

\item \textbf{Defect-ladder decomposition}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder}).
The sixth- and eighth-order entropy coefficients admit non-negative
defect coordinates, minimized simultaneously by the symmetric two-point
law $\frac12(\delta_{\bar\gamma+\sigma}+\delta_{\bar\gamma-\sigma})$.

\item \textbf{Defect amplification}
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification}).
The eighth-order defect
$\Delta_8(\nu)$ is bounded below by
$\frac{13\sigma^2}{8}\Delta_6(\nu)$, with equality only at the
symmetric two-point extremizer.  This is the paper's strongest
structural inequality: departures from the extremizer at sixth order are
strictly amplified at eighth order.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability} converts the sixth-order gap
into a quantitative $W_2$-stability statement: small defect forces the
centred law to lie close to the symmetric two-point extremizer at fixed
variance.

The supporting sections develop the RKHS and lattice theory associated
with the comparison kernel.  The secant family
$\Psi_\varepsilon(y):=(R_\varepsilon(y)-1)/\varepsilon$
has Gram kernel $K(a,b)=2/(4+(a-b)^2)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-gram-kernel}), and the resulting RKHS is
isometric to the Poisson image space $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$,
with holomorphic extension to the strip $\{z\in\CC:|\Im z|<1\}$.  For
the integer secant orbit we specialize classical shift-invariant-space
theory to this kernel, recording closed-form formulas for the lattice
symbol, the cardinal kernel, and the exact interpolation norm.

\subsection{Relation to prior work and novelty map}%
\label{subsec:novelty-map}

To help the reader assess each result, we describe for each
section what is classical, what is adapted from existing literature, and
what is genuinely new.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Sections~\ref{sec:cayley-gate}--\ref{sec:haar-pullback}: Cayley transform
and Haar pullback.}
The Cayley transform and its identification of Haar measure with the
Cauchy law are classical.  The rigidity characterization
(Theorem~\ref{thm:haar-pullback-uniqueness}) is a clean reformulation
of the change-of-variables uniqueness.  We include this material for
self-containedness; the novelty is presentational, not mathematical.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential}: Entropy identity and mode calculus.}
The entropy identity (Proposition~\ref{thm:precision-ledger}) is a standard
change-of-variables formula.  The mode calculus
(Theorem~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode}) and the even-order rigidity
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders}) use the Chebyshev generating
function in a nonstandard way; the trigonometric mode formulas and parity
argument appear new in this specific comparison-kernel context.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential}: Entropy asymptotics and defect
structure (main results).}
The fourth-order term $\sigma^4/(8t^4)$ is essentially the Fisher
information rate and is not new.  Edgeworth-type expansions in the
entropic CLT literature
\cite{Barron1986,JohnsonBarron2004,ArtsteinBallBartheNaor2004,%
BobkovChistyakovGotzeEdgeworth2013,BobkovChistyakovGotzeBerryEsseen2014}
provide related asymptotic machinery, but
they concern normalized sums and Gaussian targets, not Poisson averages
relative to a translated Cauchy.  The information-theoretic properties
of Cauchy distributions in other settings are discussed in
\cite{Verdu2023}.
The genuinely new content is:
\begin{itemize}
\item the explicit $t^{-6}$ and $t^{-8}$ coefficients for the Poisson--Cauchy
comparison (Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth});
\item the defect-ladder decomposition
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder});
\item the defect-amplification inequality
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification}); and
\item the quantitative $W_2$-stability consequence
(Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability}).
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Section~\ref{sec:gram-space}: Mode Gram space and RKHS.}
The Gram kernel computation (Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-gram-kernel}) is a
short residue calculation that appears new in this setting.  The
density and RKHS identification (Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-space-rkhs})
follow a standard Poisson-injectivity argument once the kernel is
available.  The centered reconstruction
(Proposition~\ref{thm:poisson-central-two-channel}) is classical in spirit,
combining Laplace uniqueness with characteristic-function inversion; it
is included as a structural corollary rather than a headline result.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Section~\ref{sec:strip-lattice}: Strip RKHS and lattice sampling.}
The Riesz-basis and cardinal-reconstruction framework is a direct
application of the principal shift-invariant-space theory developed in
\cite{deBoorDeVoreRon1994,RonShen1995,Bownik2000,AldroubiGrochenig2001}.
Explicit Riesz constants and nod-function computations for
Cauchy/Lorentz integer translates appear in the prior literature
\cite{KiselevMininNovikSitnik2014,KiselevMininNovikov2016,%
KiselevMininNovikov2019}.
Hardy spaces on strips as reproducing-kernel Hilbert spaces via
Paley--Wiener methods are treated in
\cite{MaiQian2018}; the reader should compare
Propositions~\ref{thm:gram-space-poisson-image}
and~\ref{prop:poisson-hardy-splitting} against that reference.
For this paper, the genuinely new content in
Section~\ref{sec:strip-lattice} is limited to the
\emph{Poisson-strip reinterpretation} together with the closed-form
lattice symbol~\eqref{eq:lattice-symbol}, the cardinal Fourier
formula~\eqref{eq:cardinal-kernel-fourier}, and the exact norm
identity~\eqref{eq:cardinal-norm-formula}.

\subsection{Organization}

Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation.
Section~\ref{sec:cayley-gate} records the Cayley formulas, and
Section~\ref{sec:haar-pullback} proves the Haar pullback rigidity.
Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential} is the technical core: it
develops the entropy identity, the mode calculus, the eighth-order
expansion, the defect amplification, and the $W_2$-stability theorem.
Section~\ref{sec:gram-space} constructs the mode Gram kernel and RKHS,
and proves the centered reconstruction theorem.
Section~\ref{sec:strip-lattice} identifies the strip RKHS with a Poisson
image space and develops lattice sampling with explicit constants.
The appendix records the weighted trigonometric integrals and provides
a step-by-step coefficient verification for the eighth-order term.
