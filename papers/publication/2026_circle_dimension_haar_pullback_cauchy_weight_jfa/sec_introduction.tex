\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Let
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2},
\qquad
P_t(x)=\frac{1}{\pi}\frac{t}{x^2+t^2}.
\]
Thus $K(a,b)=\pi P_2(a-b)$, so the same object may be viewed either as a
translation-invariant reproducing kernel or as the Poisson kernel at
time~$2$.  Let $\nu$ be a probability measure on~$\RR$ with mean
$\bar\gamma$, and write
\[
h_t:=P_t*\nu
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
g_t(x):=P_t(x-\bar\gamma).
\]
After the rescaling $x=\bar\gamma+ty$, the comparison between $h_t$ and
$g_t$ is reduced to the quotient
\[
\frac{P_t(x-\gamma)}{g_t(x)}
=R_{\Delta/t}(y),
\qquad
R_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{1+y^2}{1+(y-\varepsilon)^2},
\qquad
\Delta:=\gamma-\bar\gamma.
\]
Our starting observation is that, under the Cayley parametrization
$y=\tan\theta$, the kernel $R_\varepsilon$ becomes a finite
trigonometric polynomial.  We exploit this fact for one specific
kernel~$K$ in two directions.  On the one hand, it leads to an
explicit large-time expansion of the relative entropy between $h_t$ and
$g_t$.  On the other hand, it produces a concrete description of the
reproducing-kernel Hilbert space generated by~$K$.

The asymptotic part begins with
Theorem~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode}, which identifies the Taylor
coefficients of $R_\varepsilon$ with explicit Cayley--Chebyshev modes.
The parity built into this representation forces the expansion of
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))
\]
to involve only even powers of $t^{-1}$; see
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders}.  In
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth} we compute the coefficients through
order $t^{-8}$.  The subsequent defect decomposition,
Theorems~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder}
and~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification}, isolates the symmetric
two-point law as the extremal configuration.  This in turn yields the
quantitative transport estimate of
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability}.

The same secant family has a precise Hilbert-space meaning.  If
\[
\Psi_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{R_\varepsilon(y)-1}{\varepsilon}
\]
then Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-gram-kernel} shows that its Gram kernel is
exactly~$K$.  Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-space-rkhs} identifies the closed
secant span with the zero-mean space $L^2_0(\omega)$ and, via the
associated unitary map, with the reproducing-kernel Hilbert
space~$\mathcal H_K$.  In Theorem~\ref{thm:higher-secant-kernels} we
extend the quadratic Gram identity to explicit higher multilinear
secant kernels, and the coefficients in the expansion of
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))
\]
are recovered as Taylor coefficients of these kernels.  The quadratic
member of the hierarchy yields, through
Corollary~\ref{cor:quadratic-secant-energy}, a completely monotone
energy represented as the Laplace transform of $|\phi_{\nu_c}'|^2$.
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-doob-phi-entropy} then shows that the quotient
flow $u_t=h_t/g_t$ is governed by a non-autonomous Doob transform of the
Cauchy generator, with an exact weighted Green identity and a full
$\Phi$-entropy dissipation formula.  In the quadratic case,
Corollary~\ref{cor:quadratic-energy-chi-square} identifies the secant
energy with the $\chi^2$-divergence of the Poisson channel and records
its nonlocal dissipation law.  Proposition~\ref{thm:gram-space-poisson-image}
realizes $\mathcal H_K$
as $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$, equivalently as a Hardy space on the
strip $\{z\in\CC:\ |\Im z|<1\}$, and
Proposition~\ref{prop:poisson-hardy-splitting} gives the corresponding
Hardy splitting.  From this strip model we obtain closed formulas for
the orthogonal projector onto $\mathcal H_K(h\ZZ)$, the periodized
weight, the sampling bounds on~$\ZZ$, and the cardinal interpolation
norm; see
Theorems~\ref{thm:uniform-lattice-projection},
\ref{thm:poisson-lattice-sampling},
and~\ref{thm:poisson-cardinal-reconstruction}.

The analytic ingredients are classical: the Poisson semigroup and its
Fourier and singular-integral representations
\cite{Folland1995,Grafakos2014,Stein1971,Kwasnicki2017}, together with Laplace
uniqueness and characteristic-function inversion
\cite{Widder1941,Lukacs1970,Feller1971}.  The entropy results belong to
the circle of ideas surrounding the entropic central limit theorem and
stable-law asymptotics
\cite{Barron1986,JohnsonBarron2004,ArtsteinBallBartheNaor2004,CarlenCarvalho1992,BobkovChistyakovGotzeEdgeworth2013,BobkovChistyakovGotzeBerryEsseen2014,BobkovChistyakovGotze2013,Toscani2015,Toscani2016}.
For the Hilbert-space and sampling aspects we rely on the theory of
translation-invariant kernels and principal shift-invariant spaces
\cite{Aronszajn1950,deBoorDeVoreRon1994,RonShen1995,Bownik2000,AldroubiGrochenig2001}
and on more recent sampling results
\cite{GrochenigRomeroStockler2018,GrochenigRomeroStockler2020,FuhrGrochenigHaimiKlotzRomero2018}.
Related explicit interpolation phenomena for Cauchy-type generators
appear in
\cite{KiselevMininNovikov2016,KiselevMininNovikov2019,BelovKulikovLyubarskii2022,BelovKulikovLyubarskii2023,UlanovskiiZlotnikov2025}.
What is special about the present kernel is that the same secant family
governs the entropy coefficients, the Gram orbit of the RKHS, and the
lattice formulas.  This makes it possible to write the asymptotics and
the functional-analytic structure in a single closed model.

The Cayley transform
\[
\iota(x)=\frac{1+\ii x}{1-\ii x}
\]
enters only through the normalization.  It identifies the one-point
compactification~$\overline\RR$ with~$\TT$ and pulls normalized Haar
measure back to the Cauchy weight
\[
\frac{1}{\pi(1+x^2)}\,\dd x=P_1(x)\,\dd x.
\]
Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries} records the corresponding rigidity
statement: in the normalized orientation-preserving $C^1$ class, the
Haar pullback condition characterizes the standard Cayley chart.

The paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries}
fixes the notation, introduces the Cayley chart, and proves the Haar
pullback rigidity statement.  Section~\ref{sec:entropy-asymptotics}
contains the mode calculus and the entropy asymptotics.
Section~\ref{sec:gram-space} identifies the associated reproducing
kernel space, computes the higher secant kernels, recovers the
centered law from Poisson data, and proves the quotient-flow
dissipation theorem.  Section~\ref{sec:strip-lattice}
develops the strip realization and the lattice formulas.  The appendix
contains the weighted trigonometric integrals used in the proof of
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}.
