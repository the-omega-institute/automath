\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

This paper studies a fixed Poisson/Cauchy model with
translation-invariant kernel
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2}.
\]
The Cayley transform
\[
\iota(x)=\frac{1+\ii x}{1-\ii x}
\]
identifies the one-point compactification~$\overline\RR$ of the line
with the circle~$\TT$, and normalized Haar measure on~$\TT$ pulls back
to the Cauchy law
\[
\frac{1}{\pi(1+x^{2})}\dd x=P_1(x)\dd x.
\]
This change of variables is used only to normalize the model and to pass
between line and circle coordinates.  The rigidity statement of
Theorem~\ref{thm:haar-pullback-uniqueness} is therefore preparatory: in
the normalized orientation-preserving $C^1$ class, the pullback
condition already forces the standard Cayley chart.

The main calculations concern the large-time comparison between
\[
h_t:=P_t*\nu
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
g_t(x):=P_t(x-\bar\gamma),
\]
where $\bar\gamma$ is the mean of~$\nu$.  After the rescaling
$x=\bar\gamma+ty$, the ratio $h_t/g_t$ is controlled by the kernel
\[
R_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{1+y^2}{1+(y-\varepsilon)^2}.
\]
Passing to the angular variable $y=\tan\theta$ turns $R_\varepsilon$
into a finite trigonometric expression.  Theorem~\ref{thm:cayley-chebyshev-mode}
records the resulting Cayley--Chebyshev mode calculus, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-even-orders} shows that the expansion of
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))
\]
contains only even powers of~$t^{-1}$.  Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}
computes the coefficients through order~$t^{-8}$, while
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability} rewrite these coefficients in
defect variables adapted to the symmetric two-point law and derive
amplification and quantitative $W_2$-stability estimates.  These
explicit entropy asymptotics are the primary model-specific contribution
of the paper.

The functional-analytic part serves to realize the same model in a
concrete Hilbert-space setting.  The secant family
\[
\Psi_\varepsilon(y):=\frac{R_\varepsilon(y)-1}{\varepsilon}
\]
has Gram kernel
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2},
\]
as shown in Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-gram-kernel}.  Theorem~\ref{thm:mode-space-rkhs}
identifies the closed secant span with the zero-mean space
$L^2_0(\omega)$ and with the reproducing-kernel Hilbert space generated
by~$K$, and Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-central-two-channel} recovers the
centered law from two central Poisson observation channels.
Proposition~\ref{thm:gram-space-poisson-image} then realizes the same
space as $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$, equivalently as a strip Hardy
space, and Proposition~\ref{prop:poisson-hardy-splitting} gives the
associated Hardy decomposition.

The lattice part of the paper should be read in the same spirit.
Theorem~\ref{thm:uniform-lattice-projection},
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-lattice-sampling}, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-cardinal-reconstruction} record the orthogonal
projection, Riesz bounds, and cardinal reconstruction formulas attached
to the specific kernel~$K$ on $h\ZZ$ and on~$\ZZ$.  These statements lie
within the classical theory of translation-invariant kernels and
principal shift-invariant spaces
\cite{deBoorDeVoreRon1994,RonShen1995,Bownik2000,AldroubiGrochenig2001}
and its later sampling developments
\cite{GrochenigRomeroStockler2018,GrochenigRomeroStockler2020,FuhrGrochenigHaimiKlotzRomero2018}.
Related explicit interpolation formulae for Cauchy-type generators
appear in
\cite{KiselevMininNovikov2016,KiselevMininNovikov2019,BelovKulikovLyubarskii2022,BelovKulikovLyubarskii2023,UlanovskiiZlotnikov2025}.
What is specific here is not the existence of the shift-invariant
machinery itself, but the closed formulas for the periodic weight, the
lattice symbol, the projector, and the interpolation norm in this
particular Poisson/Cauchy model.

The background ingredients are classical: the Poisson semigroup and its
Fourier representation \cite{Folland1995,Grafakos2014,Stein1971},
Laplace-transform uniqueness \cite{Widder1941},
characteristic-function inversion \cite{Lukacs1970,Feller1971},
reproducing kernels \cite{Aronszajn1950}, and Hardy-space boundary
theory \cite{Duren1970,Rudin1987}.  On the entropy side, the relevant
context includes the entropic central limit theorem, entropy production,
and stable-law asymptotics
\cite{Barron1986,JohnsonBarron2004,ArtsteinBallBartheNaor2004,CarlenCarvalho1992}
and
\cite{BobkovChistyakovGotzeEdgeworth2013,BobkovChistyakovGotzeBerryEsseen2014,BobkovChistyakovGotze2013,Toscani2015,Toscani2016}.
The present paper does not attempt to extract a general structural
entropy theorem from that literature.  Its contribution is instead to
exploit the finite mode structure of one rigid Poisson/Cauchy model in
order to obtain explicit coefficients, parity cancellations, and the
associated concrete kernel formulas.

The paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries}
fixes notation.  Section~\ref{sec:cayley-gate} records the Cayley
parametrization, and Section~\ref{sec:haar-pullback} proves the
preparatory Haar pullback rigidity theorem.  Section~\ref{sec:precision-potential}
contains the entropy expansion and the defect analysis.
Section~\ref{sec:gram-space} develops the mode-space and reconstruction
results.  Section~\ref{sec:strip-lattice} gives the strip realization
and the corresponding explicit lattice formulas.  The appendix collects
the weighted trigonometric integrals and the coefficient bookkeeping
used in the proof of the eighth-order expansion.
