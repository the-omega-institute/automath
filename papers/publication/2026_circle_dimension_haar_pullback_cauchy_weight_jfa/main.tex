\begin{abstract}
We study the Poisson/Cauchy model associated with the
translation-invariant kernel $K(a,b)=2(4+(a-b)^2)^{-1}$.
If $\nu$ is a probability measure with mean~$\bar\gamma$, then after the
rescaling $x=\bar\gamma+ty$ the comparison between $P_t*\nu$ and the
translated profile $P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma)$ is governed by the rational
kernel $R_\varepsilon(y)=(1+y^2)/(1+(y-\varepsilon)^2)$.  Under the
Cayley change of variables $y=\tan\theta$, this kernel becomes a finite
trigonometric polynomial.  Consequently, the relative entropy between
$P_t*\nu$ and $P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma)$ admits an asymptotic
expansion involving only even powers of~$t^{-1}$.  We compute the
coefficients through order~$t^{-8}$, rewrite them in defect variables
around the symmetric two-point law, and deduce a quantitative
$W_2$-stability estimate.  The same secant family generates the
reproducing-kernel Hilbert space of~$K$, identified explicitly with a
zero-mean $L^2$ space over the Cauchy weight, with
$\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$, and with a Hardy space on the strip
$|\Im z|<1$.  Within this model the
entropy coefficients are recovered from multilinear secant kernels, the
quadratic secant energy becomes a completely monotone Laplace transform
of $|\phi_{\nu_c}'|^2$, and the associated lattice projector,
periodized weight, sampling bounds, and cardinal interpolation norm
admit closed formulas.  The Cayley/Haar pullback is used only to fix the
normalization.
\end{abstract}
