\title{Cayley pullback, Poisson entropy asymptotics, and cardinal reconstruction in a Cauchy reproducing kernel space}
\author{}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The Cayley transform pulls normalized Haar measure on~$\TT$ back to the
Cauchy law on~$\RR$.  We show that, within the normalized
orientation-preserving $C^{1}$ class, this pullback property
characterizes the Cayley chart.  In the same coordinates we study the
comparison kernel
$R_\varepsilon(y)=(1+y^{2})/(1+(y-\varepsilon)^{2})$ and develop an
explicit Cayley--Chebyshev mode calculus for its Taylor coefficients.
This yields a large-time expansion for
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ in which all odd
powers of~$t^{-1}$ vanish, together with the coefficient formula
through order~$t^{-8}$, a defect amplification principle, and a
quantitative $W_2$-stability estimate around the symmetric two-point
law.  We also compute the Gram kernel of the associated secant family,
identify the resulting reproducing kernel space with
$\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^{2}(\RR)$, realize it as a Hardy-type strip
space, and obtain stable sampling and an explicit cardinal
interpolation formula on the integer orbit.
\end{abstract}
