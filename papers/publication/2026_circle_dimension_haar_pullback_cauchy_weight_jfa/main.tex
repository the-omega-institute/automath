\begin{abstract}
We study the large-time expansion of the relative entropy
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$,
where $P_t$ is the Poisson semigroup and $\nu$ is a probability
measure with mean $\bar\gamma$.
The Cayley transform identifies this problem with a mode calculus
governed by Chebyshev polynomials of the second kind, whose parity
forces all odd-order terms to vanish.
The paper's three main results concern the structure of the even-order
coefficients.
First, Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth} provides an explicit
eighth-order expansion whose $t^{-6}$ and $t^{-8}$ coefficients are
expressed in terms of centred moments through order six;
the $t^{-8}$ coefficient appears to be new.
Second, Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-ladder} shows that the
sixth- and eighth-order entropy gaps decompose into non-negative
defect coordinates that are simultaneously minimized by the symmetric
two-point law $\tfrac12(\delta_{\bar\gamma+\sigma}+\delta_{\bar\gamma-\sigma})$.
Third, Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-amplification} establishes a
defect-amplification inequality: the eighth-order gap is bounded below
by $(13\sigma^2/8)$ times the sixth-order gap, with equality only at
the symmetric two-point extremizer.
As a consequence (Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-defect-stability}),
small sixth-order defect forces the centred law to lie close to the
symmetric two-point law in $W_2$.
Appendix~A collects the weighted trigonometric integrals needed for
Theorem~\ref{thm:poisson-kl-eighth}, and Appendix~B records the
step-by-step coefficient assembly.
Appendix~C identifies the secant Gram kernel
$K(a,b)=2/(4+(a-b)^2)$ and its RKHS description, and Appendix~D
provides the lattice symbol and cardinal interpolation norm for the
integer secant orbit.
\end{abstract}
