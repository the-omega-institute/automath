\title{The Cayley Transform, Poisson Entropy Asymptotics, and Cardinal Reconstruction in a Cauchy Reproducing-Kernel Space}
\author{}

\begin{abstract}
The starting point of the paper is the classical fact that the Cayley
transform pulls normalized Haar measure on~$\TT$ back to the Cauchy law
on~$\RR$.  We first prove that, among normalized
orientation-preserving $C^{1}$-homeomorphisms
$\overline\RR\to\TT$, this pullback property characterizes the Cayley
chart.  We then develop a finite Cayley--Chebyshev mode calculus for the
comparison kernel
$R_\varepsilon(y)=(1+y^{2})/(1+(y-\varepsilon)^{2})$ and use it to study
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$: the large-time
expansion contains only even powers of~$t^{-1}$, and the coefficients
through order~$t^{-8}$ yield a defect amplification principle together
with a quantitative $W_2$-stability result for the symmetric two-point
law.  On the functional-analytic side, the secant family has Gram kernel
$K(a,b)=2/(4+(a-b)^{2})$, the corresponding reproducing-kernel space is
exactly $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^{2}(\RR)$, and this space admits a strip
realization with an orthogonal Hardy decomposition.  On the
lattice-generated subspace over~$\ZZ$ we obtain stable sampling and an
explicit cardinal interpolation formula.
\end{abstract}
