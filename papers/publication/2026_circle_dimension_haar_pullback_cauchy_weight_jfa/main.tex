\begin{abstract}
The Cayley parametrization identifies normalized Haar measure on~$\TT$
with the Cauchy law on~$\RR$; this change of variables is used only as a
normalization device for the Poisson/Cauchy model generated by
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2}.
\]
An explicit Cayley--Chebyshev mode calculus yields a large-time
expansion for
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ in which all odd
powers of~$t^{-1}$ vanish, together with coefficient formulas through
order~$t^{-8}$, a defect amplification theorem, and a quantitative
$W_2$-stability estimate around the symmetric two-point law.  The
associated secant family generates the reproducing-kernel space
$\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^{2}(\RR)$, equivalently the Hardy space of the
strip $\{z\in\CC:\ |\Im z|<1\}$.  For every lattice $h\ZZ$, an exact
alias-averaging formula is obtained for the orthogonal projection onto
the lattice-generated subspace, and the orthogonal complement is
characterized simultaneously by vanishing lattice samples and by
complete Fourier alias cancellation.  The uniform-lattice theorem yields
the integer-orbit sampling inequalities, the cardinal interpolation
formula, and the explicit lattice symbol as direct consequences.
\end{abstract}
