\begin{abstract}
We consider the translation-invariant kernel
$K(a,b)=2(4+(a-b)^2)^{-1}=\pi P_2(a-b)$ and the associated comparison
between $P_t*\nu$ and the translated Poisson profile
$P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma)$.  After the rescaling $x=\bar\gamma+ty$, the
relevant quotient is governed by
$R_\varepsilon(y)=(1+y^2)/(1+(y-\varepsilon)^2)$.  Under the Cayley
change of variables $y=\tan\theta$, this kernel becomes a finite
trigonometric polynomial.  It follows that
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ has a large-time
expansion involving only even powers of $t^{-1}$.  We compute this
expansion through order $t^{-8}$, rewrite the coefficients in defect
coordinates around the symmetric two-point law, and deduce a
quantitative $W_2$-stability estimate.  The quotient flow
$u_t=(P_t*\nu)/P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma)$ is shown to satisfy a
non-autonomous Doob-transform equation for the Cauchy generator, with an
exact $\Phi$-entropy dissipation formula and an integrated nonlocal
representation of the relative entropy.  The same secant family also
generates the reproducing-kernel Hilbert space of $K$.  We identify this
space with the zero-mean space $L^2_0(\omega)$ for the Cauchy weight,
with $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$, and with a Hardy space on the strip
$|\Im z|<1$, identify the quadratic secant energy with the
$\chi^2$-divergence of the Poisson channel, and derive explicit formulas
for the higher secant kernels, the lattice projector, the periodized
symbol, and the cardinal interpolation norm.
\end{abstract}
