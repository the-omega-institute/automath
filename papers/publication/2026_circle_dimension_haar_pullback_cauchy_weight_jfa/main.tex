\begin{abstract}
The paper studies the Poisson/Cauchy model generated by
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2}.
\]
After the Cayley normalization, the comparison kernel becomes a finite
trigonometric expression in the angular variable.  This yields an
explicit large-time expansion for
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ in which all odd
powers of~$t^{-1}$ vanish, together with coefficient formulas through
order~$t^{-8}$, a defect amplification theorem, and a quantitative
$W_2$-stability estimate around the symmetric two-point law.  The
associated secant family generates the reproducing-kernel space
$\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^{2}(\RR)$, equivalently a strip Hardy space on
$\{z\in\CC:\ |\Im z|<1\}$.  Within this classical
translation-invariant kernel framework, the paper records the
corresponding uniform-lattice projection, sampling, and cardinal
interpolation formulas for the specific kernel~$K$, including the
explicit periodic weight and interpolation norm.  The Haar/Cauchy
pullback under the Cayley map is used only to fix the normalization of
the model, and the associated rigidity statement is included as a
preparatory result.
\end{abstract}
