\begin{abstract}
We study the Poisson/Cauchy model associated with the
translation-invariant kernel
\[
K(a,b)=\frac{2}{4+(a-b)^2}.
\]
If $\nu$ is a probability measure with mean~$\bar\gamma$, then after the
rescaling $x=\bar\gamma+ty$ the comparison between $P_t*\nu$ and the
translated profile $P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma)$ is governed by the rational
kernel $R_\varepsilon(y)=(1+y^2)/(1+(y-\varepsilon)^2)$.  Under the
Cayley change of variables $y=\tan\theta$, this kernel becomes a finite
trigonometric polynomial.  As a consequence,
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ admits an asymptotic
expansion involving only even powers of~$t^{-1}$.  We compute the
coefficients through order~$t^{-8}$, rewrite them in defect variables
around the symmetric two-point law, and deduce a quantitative
$W_2$-stability estimate.  We also identify the secant orbit generated
by~$R_\varepsilon$ with the reproducing-kernel Hilbert space of~$K$,
equivalently with $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^2(\RR)$ and with a Hardy space
on the strip $\{z\in\CC:\ |\Im z|<1\}$.  In this realization we obtain
closed formulas for the lattice projector, the periodized weight, and
the cardinal interpolation norm.  The Haar pullback under the Cayley
map is used only to fix the natural normalization and the accompanying
rigidity statement.
\end{abstract}
