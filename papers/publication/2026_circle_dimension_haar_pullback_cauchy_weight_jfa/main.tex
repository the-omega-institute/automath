\begin{abstract}
We study the Poisson comparison between a smoothed probability measure
and the translated Cauchy profile with the same mean.  A Cayley
parametrization turns the comparison quotient into a finite
trigonometric object, which yields an even-power large-time expansion of
the relative entropy.  The coefficients are computed through order
$t^{-8}$, reorganized around the symmetric two-point law, and used to
prove a quantitative $W_2$-stability estimate.  The same secant family
generates the reproducing-kernel Hilbert space of
$K(a,b)=2/(4+(a-b)^2)$, which is identified with the zero-mean Cauchy
space, a Fourier multiplier image, and a Hardy space on a strip.  We also
derive a non-autonomous Doob transform for the quotient flow, an exact
$\Phi$-entropy dissipation formula, and explicit lattice and cardinal
interpolation formulas for the associated strip model.
\end{abstract}
