\title{Cayley--Chebyshev Mode Calculus, Poisson Entropy Asymptotics, and Cardinal Reconstruction in a Strip RKHS}
\author{}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The Cayley transform pulls back normalized Haar measure on the circle
to the Cauchy distribution on~$\RR$, and we prove that this property
characterizes the Cayley chart among orientation-preserving $C^{1}$
homeomorphisms.  A Cayley--Chebyshev mode calculus for the comparison
kernel $R_\varepsilon(y)=(1+y^{2})/(1+(y-\varepsilon)^{2})$ yields
finite trigonometric formulas for all Taylor modes and forces the
large-time relative entropy
$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P_t*\nu\|P_t(\cdot-\bar\gamma))$ to have only even
powers of~$t^{-1}$.  We derive a sharp eighth-order entropy expansion,
identify a defect ladder in which the eighth-order gap amplifies the
sixth-order gap, and obtain a quantitative $W_2$-stability theorem for
the symmetric two-point law.  The secant family of the comparison kernel
generates a reproducing-kernel Hilbert space with Gram kernel
$K(a,b)=2/(4+(a-b)^{2})$, isometrically equal to the Poisson image
space $\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^{2}(\RR)$, which extends holomorphically
to a strip and splits into two Hardy summands.  For the integer secant
orbit we specialize classical shift-invariant-space sampling theory to
this kernel, obtaining closed-form formulas for the lattice symbol, the
cardinal kernel, and the exact interpolation norm.
\end{abstract}
