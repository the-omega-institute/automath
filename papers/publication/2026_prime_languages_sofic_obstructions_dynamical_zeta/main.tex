\begin{abstract}
We prove two new finite-state obstructions for prime languages. First,
the density dichotomy for a fixed complete deterministic automaton over
the binary alphabet forces normalized word counts to be eventually
periodic up to exponentially small error; consequently, no fixed DFA
can approximate the binary prime language with recall and precision
both bounded away from zero along infinitely many lengths. Second, the
exponential-polynomial form of sofic word counts, combined with a
residue-class asymptotic lemma, excludes eventual sofic realization of
the prime Zeckendorf length slices.

As supporting context, we include a self-contained Perron--Frobenius
derivation of the known power-law growth constraint for regular
Zeckendorf sublanguages, and we record two classical analytic
observations: prime-supported Euler products convergent on the unit disc
have the unit circle as a natural boundary, while finite determinant
models become \(2\pi i\)-periodic after the change of variables
\(z=e^{-s}\).
\end{abstract}
