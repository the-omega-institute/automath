\begin{abstract}
This note places three elementary obstructions side by side for
prime-supported binary and Zeckendorf languages. For a fixed complete
deterministic automaton over the binary alphabet, the normalized word
counts are eventually periodic up to an exponentially small error.
Consequently no fixed DFA can approximate the binary prime language
with recall and precision both bounded away from zero along infinitely
many lengths.

For a regular sublanguage \(L\) of the Zeckendorf syntax, if \(\rho\)
is the maximal spectral radius of a strongly connected component in a
trim recognizing automaton, then
\(N_L(T)=T^{\alpha+o(1)}\) with
\(\alpha=\max(0,\log \rho/\log \varphi)\). This rules out exact
regular realization of the prime Zeckendorf language. A residue-class
asymptotic lemma for exponential-polynomial block counts then excludes
eventual sofic realization of the prime Zeckendorf length slices.
Finally, prime-supported Euler products convergent on the unit disc
have the unit circle as a natural boundary, while finite determinant
models become \(2\pi i\)-periodic after the change of variables
\(z=e^{-s}\).
\end{abstract}
