\begin{abstract}
We isolate from classical density theory for regular languages a form
suited to quantitative prime-approximation bounds. For a fixed complete
DFA on the full base-\(b\) shift, normalized length-\(m\) counts are
eventually periodic up to an exponentially small error. More generally,
for a trim deterministic presentation whose underlying graph is
strongly connected with Perron root \(\lambda>1\), intersections with
any fixed DFA satisfy the same relative-density dichotomy. Our new
contribution is to convert these background inputs into explicit
approximation obstructions for prime slices: no fixed DFA can keep both
recall and precision bounded below on infinitely many lengths, and the
symmetric difference from the base-\(b\) or Zeckendorf prime slice is
at least \(c_{\mathcal A,b}\,b^m/m\) or
\(c_{\mathcal A,\varphi}\,\varphi^m/m\) for all large \(m\). In the
Zeckendorf setting this yields the corresponding quantitative
corollaries, while eventual sofic realization of the prime slices is
ruled out by the usual regularity argument. A final section records two
classical analytic remarks concerning Euler products and determinant
models.
\end{abstract}
