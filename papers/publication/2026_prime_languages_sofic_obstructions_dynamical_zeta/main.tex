\begin{abstract}
We record several obstructions showing that languages supported on
primes do not fit naturally into finite-state models. For complete
deterministic automata over the binary alphabet, the accepted word
density at length \(m\) is eventually periodic up to an exponentially
small error. Consequently, no fixed automaton can approximate the
binary prime language with both recall and precision bounded away from
zero along infinitely many lengths.

For the Zeckendorf language, every regular sublanguage has counting
function \(N_L(T)=T^{\alpha+o(1)}\) for some \(\alpha\in[0,1]\). This
excludes the prime Zeckendorf language from the regular category and
excludes its length slices from the sofic category. Finally, we record
two analytic obstructions: prime-supported Euler products have the unit
circle as a natural boundary, and finite determinants become
\(2\pi i\)-periodic after the logarithmic change of variables.
\end{abstract}
