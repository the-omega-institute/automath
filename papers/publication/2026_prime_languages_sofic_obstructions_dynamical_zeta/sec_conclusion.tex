\section{Further remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper should be read as a short note about the quantitative prime
corollaries, not about new density theorems. The background input is
the classical periodic density law for regular languages and its trim
strongly connected relative-density analogue. The new output is that
these background facts already force fixed-DFA approximation of prime
slices to fail quantitatively: recall and precision cannot stay
simultaneously bounded below, and the symmetric difference from the
prime slice is always at least
\(c_{\mathcal A,\bullet}\,\lambda^m/m\), where \(\lambda=b\) in the
ordinary base-\(b\) setting and \(\lambda=\varphi\) in the Zeckendorf
setting. The Zeckendorf section then records the corresponding
non-regularity statement and the easy eventual-sofic corollary.

Several natural questions remain. The density dichotomy uses completeness
only as a normalization and uses determinism in an essential way, and
it would be interesting to know how far analogous lower bounds persist
for nondeterministic or weighted automata, where cancellation phenomena
become possible. It would also be natural to extend the fixed-DFA
obstruction further across Parry numeration systems, where the dominant
eigenvalue \(\lambda\) varies with the system, and to test other
arithmetic sets besides the primes, such as square-free numbers or
polynomial images. Finally, understanding whether bounded-ambiguity NFAs
or controlled families of automata can evade the dichotomy remains an
open direction.
