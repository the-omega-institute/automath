\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Finite-state symbolic models impose rigid constraints on counting
functions. In a deterministic automaton, the number of accepted words
of length \(m\) is governed by powers of a finite matrix. In a sofic
shift, the number of admissible words of a given length is likewise
controlled by a finite-state presentation. These mechanisms force
periodic density laws or exponential-polynomial growth, and that is
often enough to rule out arithmetic languages whose size is governed by
the prime number theorem.

This note contributes two new obstructions of this kind and collects
classical context around them. The first new result is a quantitative
approximation obstruction: we show that no fixed complete DFA can
simultaneously achieve bounded recall and precision for the binary
prime language. The second new result is a sofic-slice obstruction:
we show that the prime Zeckendorf length slices cannot be realized as
the admissible words of any sofic shift, even eventually.
As supporting context, we include a self-contained derivation of the
known growth law for regular Zeckendorf sublanguages, and we record
two classical analytic observations concerning Euler products and
finite determinant models.

Throughout, \(f(m)\asymp g(m)\) means that \(c_1g(m)\le f(m)\le c_2g(m)\)
for some positive constants \(c_1,c_2\) and all sufficiently large
\(m\). We write \(F_0=0\), \(F_1=1\) for the Fibonacci numbers,
\(\varphi=(1+\sqrt5)/2\), and for a Zeckendorf word
\(w=x_1\cdots x_m\) we set
\[
  \val(w):=\sum_{k=1}^m x_kF_{k+1}.
\]

\subsection{Main results}

Our first theorem is a density dichotomy for deterministic automata.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:intro-density}
Let \(\mathcal A\) be a complete deterministic finite automaton over
\(\Sigma=\{0,1\}\), and let
\[
  a_m(\mathcal A)
  :=\#\{w\in\Sigma^m:\mathcal A\text{ accepts }w\}.
\]
Then there exist an integer \(p\ge 1\), non-negative constants
\(c_0,\dots,c_{p-1}\), and \(\theta\in(0,1)\) such that
\[
  \frac{a_m(\mathcal A)}{2^m}=c_{m\bmod p}+O(\theta^m)
  \qquad (m\to\infty).
\]
Consequently exactly one of the following holds:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
  \item \(a_m(\mathcal A)/2^m\) decays exponentially.
  \item Along some residue class modulo \(p\), the ratio
  \(a_m(\mathcal A)/2^m\) is bounded below by a positive constant.
\end{enumerate}
In particular, no deterministic finite automaton can satisfy
\[
  a_m(\mathcal A)/2^m\asymp m^{-\alpha}
\]
for any \(\alpha>0\).
\end{theorem}

When this is applied to the binary prime language
\(\mathcal P_m^{(2)}\), whose size is \(\asymp 2^m/m\) by the prime
number theorem \cite{HardyWright2008}, one obtains a sharp
incompatibility with finite-state recognition.

\begin{corollary}[Symmetric-difference lower bound for binary primes]
  \label{cor:intro-binary-primes}
Let \(L(\mathcal A)\subseteq\{0,1\}^\ast\) be recognised by a fixed
deterministic finite automaton. Then there exists \(c>0\) such that
\[
  \abs{L(\mathcal A)\cap\{0,1\}^m\triangle\mathcal P_m^{(2)}}
  \ge c\,\frac{2^m}{m}
\]
for all sufficiently large \(m\). If \(L(\mathcal A)\) falls in the
positive-density alternative of \Cref{thm:intro-density}, then along an
infinite arithmetic progression of lengths one has the stronger bound
\[
  \abs{L(\mathcal A)\cap\{0,1\}^m\triangle\mathcal P_m^{(2)}}
  \gg 2^m.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[No simultaneous recall and precision for fixed DFA]
  \label{cor:intro-binary-precision}
  Let
  \[
    \mathrm{Rec}_m
    :=\frac{\abs{L(\mathcal A)\cap\mathcal P_m^{(2)}}}
            {\abs{\mathcal P_m^{(2)}}},
    \qquad
    \mathrm{Prec}_m
    :=\frac{\abs{L(\mathcal A)\cap\mathcal P_m^{(2)}}}
            {\abs{L(\mathcal A)\cap\{0,1\}^m}}
  \]
  whenever \(\abs{L(\mathcal A)\cap\{0,1\}^m}>0\). Then every fixed DFA
  satisfies one of the following:
  \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
    \item \(\mathrm{Rec}_m\to 0\) exponentially fast.
    \item \(\mathrm{Prec}_m\to 0\) along an infinite arithmetic
    progression of lengths.
  \end{enumerate}
  In particular, no fixed DFA has both recall and precision bounded
  away from zero for infinitely many lengths.
\end{corollary}

Our second theorem concerns the Zeckendorf language
\[
  \mathcal Z
  :=\{x_1\cdots x_m\in\{0,1\}^\ast:
      x_ix_{i+1}\neq 11,\ x_m=1\}.
\]

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:intro-zeckendorf}
Let \(L\subseteq\mathcal Z\) be regular. After deleting states that are
not reachable from the initial state or cannot reach an accepting
state, let \(\rho\) be the maximal spectral radius of a strongly
connected component in a DFA recognizing \(L\). Define
\[
  N_L(T):=\#\{w\in L:\val(w)\le T\},
\]
where \(\val(w)\) is the Zeckendorf value of \(w\). Then
\[
  N_L(T)=T^{\alpha+o(1)},
  \qquad
  \alpha=
  \begin{cases}
    0, & \rho\le 1,\\[0.3em]
    \dfrac{\log \rho}{\log \varphi}, & \rho>1.
  \end{cases}
  \qquad (T\to\infty).
\]
In particular, the language of Zeckendorf representations of prime
numbers is not regular. Combined with the residue-class asymptotics for
sofic word counts proved later
(\Cref{prop:sofic-counts-exponential-polynomial,lem:residue-class-asymptotics}),
the same comparison also shows that there is no sofic shift \(X\) over
\(\{0,1\}\) with \(\mathcal B_m(X)=\mathcal P_m^{(\mathrm Z)}\) for
all sufficiently large \(m\), where \(\mathcal B_m(X)\) denotes the
admissible words of length \(m\) in \(X\).
\end{theorem}

For completeness, we record two classical analytic obstructions.

\begin{theorem}[Background]\label{thm:intro-analytic}
The following statements hold.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
  \item If \((b_n)_{n\ge 1}\subseteq\ZZ_{\ge 0}\), if \(b_p>0\) for
  infinitely many primes \(p\), and if the Euler product
  \[
    Z_b(z):=\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-b_n}
  \]
  converges to a holomorphic function on \(\abs{z}<1\), then it has the
  unit circle as a natural boundary.

  \item For every finite matrix \(M\), the function
  \[
    F_M(s):=\det(I-e^{-s}M)^{-1}
  \]
  is \(2\pi i\)-periodic. Consequently, if \(F(s)=\sum_{n\ge 1}a_n n^{-s}\)
  is a Dirichlet series with at least one coefficient \(a_n\neq 0\) for
  some \(n\ge 2\), then \(F\) cannot agree with \(F_M\) on any non-empty
  open subset of a right half-plane. In particular, \(F_M\) cannot agree
  with the Riemann \(\zeta\)-function on any non-empty open subset of
  \(\Re(s)>1\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Relation to prior work}\label{subsec:prior-work}

We give a theorem-by-theorem account of what is known, what is a
specialized reproof, and what is new.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{\Cref{thm:intro-density} (density dichotomy):
  specialized reproof.} The fact that the normalized word counts of a
  regular language over a \(k\)-letter alphabet are eventually periodic
  up to exponential error is a classical consequence of
  Perron--Frobenius theory for the transfer matrix; the same conclusion
  appears in different guises in the rational-series literature
  \cite{SalomaaSoittola1978,BerstelReutenauer2011}. Our statement
  specializes this to the binary complete-DFA setting and repackages it
  as a density dichotomy.

  \item \textbf{\Cref{cor:intro-binary-primes} (symmetric-difference
  lower bound) and \Cref{cor:intro-binary-precision} (recall/precision
  obstruction): new.} These corollaries provide an explicit quantitative
  obstruction for approximating the binary prime language by a
  \emph{single fixed} DFA. The lower bound \(\Omega(2^m/m)\) on the
  symmetric difference and the incompatibility of simultaneous recall
  and precision do not appear in the general Cobham or Rigo
  non-recognizability framework
  \cite{Cobham1969,Cobham1972,Rigo2000,RigoMaes2002}. The
  approximation perspective connects to the study of regular
  approximations of non-regular languages initiated by Eisman and
  Ravikumar \cite{EismanRavikumar2003} and by Kappes and Kintala
  \cite{KappesKintala2006}, and to the asymptotic approximation results
  of Sin'ya \cite{Sinya2015}; our contribution is the specific
  recall/precision form for the prime language.

  \item \textbf{\Cref{thm:intro-zeckendorf} (regular Zeckendorf growth
  law): known, specialized derivation.} The growth of word-counting
  functions for regular and more general \(S\)-recognizable languages is
  classical; see Salomaa and Soittola \cite{SalomaaSoittola1978},
  Berstel and Reutenauer \cite{BerstelReutenauer2011}, and Charlier and
  Rampersad \cite{CharlierRampersad2011} for growth results in
  Zeckendorf-adjacent settings. The corollary that the prime Zeckendorf
  language is not regular is a special case of Rigo's theorem
  \cite{Rigo2000,RigoMaes2002}. We include a self-contained
  Perron--Frobenius proof because it directly supports the sofic
  obstruction below.

  \item \textbf{Sofic-slice obstruction
  (\Cref{thm:intro-zeckendorf}, second part): new in this form.} The
  comparison of the prime-number-theorem growth rate \(\varphi^m/m\)
  with the exponential-polynomial form forced by any sofic word count
  excludes eventual sofic realization of the prime Zeckendorf length
  slices. This specific combination appears to be new.

  \item \textbf{\Cref{thm:intro-analytic} (analytic obstructions):
  classical.} The natural-boundary result belongs to the P\'olya--Carlson--Estermann tradition
  \cite{Polya1923,Carlson1921,Estermann1928}, and the periodicity
  observation follows immediately from the Artin--Mazur determinant
  formula \cite{ArtinMazur1965}. These are included as background
  context, not as new contributions.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Summary.} The genuinely new content of this note
consists of two items: (1)~the fixed-DFA approximation obstruction with
its recall/precision analysis (\Cref{sec:automata}), and (2)~the
prime-Zeckendorf-slice sofic obstruction
(\Cref{thm:zeckendorf-primes-not-sofic}). All other results are
included as known context or specialized derivations.

\medskip
The impossibility of recognizing prime numbers by finite automata in
classical settings goes back to Hartmanis and Shank
\cite{HartmanisShank1968}. The interaction between primes and digital
constraints has a much deeper literature; in particular, the work of
Mauduit and Rivat \cite{MauduitRivat2010} and of Drmota, Mauduit and
Rivat \cite{DrmotaMauduitRivat2009} shows that prime inputs display
highly nontrivial equidistribution phenomena inside automatic and
digit-restricted settings. For Zeckendorf and more general Parry
numeration systems, the regularity of admissible expansions is standard;
see \cite{Frougny1992,BruyereHansel1997}. Our treatment uses only this
regular syntax together with the transfer-matrix viewpoint on word
counts, familiar from symbolic dynamics and analytic combinatorics
\cite{LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998,FlajoletSedgewick2009}.

\subsection{Organisation}

\Cref{sec:automata} proves the density dichotomy for deterministic
automata and derives the recall/precision obstruction for binary primes.
