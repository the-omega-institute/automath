\begin{theorem}[Three boundary sheet flips exclude compact connected simple groups by a central \(2\)-rank constraint]\label{thm:window6-center-2rank-excludes-simple-groups}
In the window-\(6\) dyadic uplift regime, the three fibers in the boundary sector each form a two-sheet cover.
Hence the transposition involution on each boundary fiber yields a central element, and together these three commuting involutions induce a canonical embedding
\[
(\ZZ_2)^3\hookrightarrow Z\!\left(\mathcal G_6^{\mathrm{bin}}\right)[2],
\qquad
\mathcal G_m^{\mathrm{bin}}:=\Aut(\Fold_m^{\mathrm{bin}}),
\]
where \(Z(\cdot)[2]\) denotes the \(2\)-torsion subgroup of the center.
\par
If \(H\) is a compact connected \emph{simple} Lie group, then there is no injective homomorphism
\(
(\ZZ_2)^3\hookrightarrow Z(H)[2].
\)
Therefore any attempt to retain all three boundary sheet flips as \emph{central superselection charges} inside a continuous simple-group envelope is incompatible with the classification of centers of compact connected simple Lie groups.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Theorem \ref{thm:window6-boundary-central-charge-unique-among-audited-lifts}, the boundary sector of \(\mathcal G_6^{\mathrm{bin}}\) contributes a central subgroup isomorphic to \((\ZZ_2)^3\), generated by the three transpositions on the audited two-point boundary fibers.
This gives the displayed embedding
\(
(\ZZ_2)^3\hookrightarrow Z(\mathcal G_6^{\mathrm{bin}})[2]
\).
\par
The classification of compact connected simple Lie groups shows that the center is always a finite abelian group whose elementary \(2\)-rank is at most \(2\):
\[
\mathrm{rank}_{\FF_2} Z(H)[2]\le 2.
\]
Indeed, for type \(A_{n-1}\), one has \(Z(SU(n))\cong \ZZ_n\), whose \(2\)-torsion is at most \(\ZZ_2\).
For types \(B_n\) and \(C_n\), the center is \(\ZZ_2\).
For type \(D_n\), the center is either \(\ZZ_4\) or \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\), so the \(2\)-rank is at most \(2\).
For the exceptional groups, the center is trivial or isomorphic to \(\ZZ_2\) or \(\ZZ_3\), and in particular cannot contain \((\ZZ_2)^3\).
Hence no injection \((\ZZ_2)^3\hookrightarrow Z(H)[2]\) can exist. \qedhere
\end{proof}
