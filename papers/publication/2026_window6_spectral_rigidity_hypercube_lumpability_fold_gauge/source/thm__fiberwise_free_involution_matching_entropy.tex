\begin{theorem}[Counting fiberwise free involutions and a lower bound on external information: matching entropy]\label{thm:fiberwise-free-involution-matching-entropy}
Let \(p:E\twoheadrightarrow B\) be a finite-to-one map, and write \(E_b:=p^{-1}(b)\) and \(d_b:=|E_b|\).
Call a map \(\tau:E\to E\) a \emph{fiberwise free involution} if
\[
\tau^2=\mathrm{id},\qquad p\circ\tau=p,\qquad \text{and}\qquad \tau|_{E_b}\ \text{has no fixed points}\ \ (\forall b\in B).
\]
Let \(\mathrm{Inv}_{\mathrm{free}}(p)\) denote the set of all such involutions.
Then:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{(Odd obstruction)} If \(d_b\) is odd for some \(b\in B\), then \(\mathrm{Inv}_{\mathrm{free}}(p)=\varnothing\).
  \item \textbf{(Exact count)} If every \(d_b\) is even, say \(d_b=2r_b\), then
  \[
  \#\mathrm{Inv}_{\mathrm{free}}(p)
  =\prod_{b\in B}\frac{(2r_b)!}{2^{r_b}\,r_b!}
  =\prod_{b\in B}(2r_b-1)!!.
  \]
  \item \textbf{(Lower bound on external information)} Any deterministic protocol that specifies a fiberwise free involution without changing \(p\) must use at least
  \[
  \mathsf I_{\min}\ \ge\ \log_2\#\mathrm{Inv}_{\mathrm{free}}(p)
  =\sum_{b\in B}\log_2\!\bigl((2r_b-1)!!\bigr)
  \]
  bits of externally variable information.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{proof}
For each \(b\in B\), the restriction \(\tau_b:=\tau|_{E_b}\) is a fixed-point-free involution on the finite set \(E_b\).
Equivalently, \(\tau_b\) is a partition of \(E_b\) into unordered pairs, namely a perfect matching.
\par
If \(|E_b|\) is odd, then no perfect matching exists on \(E_b\), so statement (1) follows.
\par
If \(|E_b|=2r_b\), then the number of perfect matchings is the classical count
\[
\frac{(2r_b)!}{2^{r_b}\,r_b!}=(2r_b-1)!!,
\]
obtained by ordering the \(2r_b\) elements and then dividing by the redundancy coming from swapping the two elements inside each pair and permuting the pairs themselves.
Since the fibers are independent, the total number of fiberwise free involutions is the product of these factors, which proves (2).
\par
For (3), suppose an external register or certificate can assume only \(\mathcal R\) distinct states across all admissible inputs.
Then it can distinguish at most \(|\mathcal R|\) different choices.
To specify deterministically one element among \(\#\mathrm{Inv}_{\mathrm{free}}(p)\) possible involutions, one must have \(|\mathcal R|\ge \#\mathrm{Inv}_{\mathrm{free}}(p)\).
Taking binary logarithms yields \(\mathsf I_{\min}\ge \log_2\#\mathrm{Inv}_{\mathrm{free}}(p)\). \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Binary jumps and \(2\)-torsion in the center of the gauge group]\label{cor:autp-center-z2-by-twofibers}
Let \(p:E\twoheadrightarrow B\) be as in Theorem \ref{thm:fiberwise-free-involution-matching-entropy}, and let
\[
\Aut(p):=\{g\in\Sym(E):\ p\circ g=p\}\ \cong\ \prod_{b\in B} S_{d_b}
\]
be its gauge group of fiberwise permutations.
Set \(N_2:=\#\{b\in B:\ d_b=2\}\).
Then
\[
Z(\Aut(p))\ \cong\ (\ZZ_2)^{N_2},
\]
generated by the transpositions on the two-point fibers.
More concretely, central involutions of \(\Aut(p)\) are in bijection with subsets of the two-point fibers: on each chosen two-point fiber one applies the unique transposition, and on every other fiber one acts trivially.
In particular, a central fiberwise free involution exists if and only if every fiber has cardinality \(2\); in that case it is unique.
\leanverified{paper\_autp\_center\_twofiber\_count\_six}
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
From the product decomposition \(\Aut(p)\cong \prod_b S_{d_b}\) we obtain
\(
Z(\Aut(p))\cong\prod_b Z(S_{d_b})
\).
Now \(Z(S_1)=\{e\}\), \(Z(S_2)=S_2\cong\ZZ_2\), and \(Z(S_d)=\{e\}\) for \(d\ge 3\).
Thus the center is contributed precisely by the fibers of size \(2\), proving the displayed isomorphism.
Therefore a central involution is specified by deciding, independently for each two-point fiber, whether to use the identity or the unique transposition on that fiber, while every other fiber contributes only the identity.
If \(\tau\) is moreover fiberwise free, then \(\tau_b\) must be fixed-point-free on every fiber.
The only central element of \(S_{d_b}\) with this property occurs when \(d_b=2\), where the unique nontrivial central element is the transposition.
Hence a central fiberwise free involution exists exactly when \(d_b=2\) for every \(b\in B\), and in that case the transposition on each fiber is forced, so the involution is unique. \qedhere
\end{proof}
