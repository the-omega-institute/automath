\begin{theorem}[Spectral rigidity of equitable lumpability: every equitable partition forces the discrete hypercube spectrum]\label{thm:foldbin-equitable-lumpability-spectral-rigidity}
Let \(m\ge 1\), and let \(Q_m\) be the \(m\)-dimensional hypercube with adjacency matrix \(A\).
For any surjection \(F:\{0,1\}^m\to X\), let \(M\) be the block-indicator matrix whose columns are indexed by \(X\) and whose \(x\)-th column is the indicator vector of \(F^{-1}(x)\).
If the partition is \emph{equitable}, that is, if there exists a matrix \(B\) satisfying
\[
A\,M=M\,B,
\]
then \(\mathrm{spec}(B)\subseteq \mathrm{spec}(A)\).
Accordingly, every eigenvalue of the quotient walk
\[
T:=\frac{1}{m}A,
\qquad
P:=\frac{1}{m}B
\]
must belong to the discrete set
\[
\left\{\,1-\frac{2k}{m}: k=0,1,\dots,m\,\right\}.
\]
Therefore, once one computes an eigenvalue of a proposed pushforward kernel that lies outside this set, strong lumpability is rigorously excluded in the sense of hidden-state-free equitable quotienting.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
If \(Bv=\lambda v\), then
\(
A(Mv)=M(Bv)=\lambda(Mv)
\).
Whenever \(Mv\neq 0\), the scalar \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\), so \(\mathrm{spec}(B)\subseteq \mathrm{spec}(A)\).
Since the adjacency spectrum of \(Q_m\) is \(\{m-2k:k=0,1,\dots,m\}\), we conclude that
\(
\mathrm{spec}(P)=\frac{1}{m}\mathrm{spec}(B)\subseteq \{1-2k/m\}
\).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Window-\(6\) is spectrally nonlumpable]\label{cor:terminal-window6-nonlumpable-by-spectrum}
For the window-\(6\) pushforward kernel \(P_6\), the audited spectral data give
\[
\lambda_2(P_6)\approx 0.4841207858,
\qquad
\lambda_\star(P_6)\approx 0.6030939754.
\]
If \(m=6\), the only spectral values compatible with an equitable quotient are
\[
\left\{\,1,\ \frac23,\ \frac13,\ 0,\ -\frac13,\ -\frac23,\ -1\,\right\}.
\]
Hence \(\mathrm{spec}(P_6)\) contains eigenvalues outside the allowed set, and the stable-type coarse-graining of window \(6\) cannot arise from a one-step equitable quotient chain on \(Q_6\).
Equivalently, strong lumpability is ruled out directly by the spectral certificate.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Compare \(\lambda_2(P_6)\) and \(\lambda_\star(P_6)\) with the set \(\{1-2k/6\}\), and then apply Theorem \ref{thm:foldbin-equitable-lumpability-spectral-rigidity}. \qedhere
\end{proof}
