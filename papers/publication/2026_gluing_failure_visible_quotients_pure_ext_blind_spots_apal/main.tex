\begin{abstract}
We study references evaluated at information states over a meet-semilattice site with Grothendieck pretopology. On the lower language, forcing at a state is exactly flat team semantics on a nonempty team of assignments. Once local-object predicates are added, one can distinguish local sectionability, compatible local sectionability, and global sectionability for an admitted reference. We show that compatible local existence is characterized by the sheafification of the local-object presheaf, that visible value classes are precisely the global sections of this sheafification, equivalently the global connected-component sections of a realization stack, and that the gluing predicates are not definable in the flat fragment.

The gerbe-theoretic and homological parts of the paper are formulated relative to a conditional datum $(r,p,\mathfrak P_r,\Band,v,\mathcal U,A,\omega)$ in the sense of \Cref{def:conditional-datum}. For such data, gluing failure occurs exactly when visible branches exist and every component gerbe is non-neutral. The corresponding branch obstruction determines a homological evaluation map $\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$ and hence a visible quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}(\mathcal U,A,\omega):=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}),
\]
which is independent of the chosen band trivialization and invariant under refinements inducing surjections on $H_2$. This quotient is exactly the part of the obstruction detected by the class-admissible characters, while the completely character-blind residue is precisely $\ker(\eta_A)$. In the support-presheaf specialization we recover, without any lift hypothesis, the Abramsky--Brandenburger compatible-family/no-global-section picture; the gerbe and $\ker(\eta_A)$ analysis requires the additional lift and finiteness assumptions encoded in the conditional datum.
\end{abstract}
