\author{Haobo Ma}
\address{CHRONOAI PTE.\ LTD., Singapore}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure for references evaluated at information states. On lower-layer formulas, state forcing coincides with a flat team-style semantics on nonempty teams of assignments. Compatible local presentations of a reference define visible branches, and we show that these are exactly the global connected components of a realization stack. Under a global conservativity hypothesis, gluing failure occurs precisely when visible branches exist but all component gerbes of those branches are non-neutral. For a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, the intrinsic visible quotient is $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$, where $\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$ is the homological evaluation map arising from the universal coefficient theorem. Equivalently, this quotient is detected exactly by the class-admissible character channels. In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting, the theory recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and identifies the cohomologically blind residue as the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part. Two worked finite examples isolate the visible and blind extremes.
\end{abstract}
