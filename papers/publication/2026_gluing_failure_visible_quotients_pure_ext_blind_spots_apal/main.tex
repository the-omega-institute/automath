\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure for references evaluated at information states, working over a meet-semilattice site with Grothendieck pretopology. On lower-layer formulas, state forcing coincides with a flat team-style semantics on nonempty teams of assignments. Compatible local presentations define visible branches, and we identify these with the global sections of the sheafification of the local-object presheaf, equivalently with the global connected-component sections of any realization stack. This yields a lower-language separation theorem showing that the gluing predicates are not definable in the flat fragment.

The gerbe-theoretic and homological claims are conditional in the precise sense of \Cref{def:conditional-datum}: once data $(r,p,\mathfrak P_r,\Band,v,\mathcal U,A,\omega)$ satisfying that definition are fixed, gluing failure occurs exactly when visible branches exist but every component gerbe is non-neutral. For a chosen adapted cover with finite \v{C}ech nerve and finite abelian trivialization, the visible quotient of a branch obstruction is the presentation-aware group $A_{\mathrm{vis}}(\mathcal U,A,\omega):=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$, where $\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$ is the homological evaluation map arising from the universal coefficient theorem. This quotient is independent of the chosen trivialization and is invariant under refinements whose induced map on $H_2$ is surjective (e.g., subdivision-type refinements); it is detected by the class-admissible character channels. In the support-presheaf specialization, the comparison with the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture is unconditional at the presheaf level, whereas the branch-gerbe and $\ker(\eta_A)$ analysis remain conditional on the additional lift and finite-presentation hypotheses. Three worked examples, including one end-to-end construction verifying the conditional hypotheses, isolate the visible and blind extremes.
\end{abstract}
