\section{Lower-Layer Semantics and Conservative Extension}\label{sec:preliminaries}

The later sections use only one abstract idea: a higher semantic layer may add coordinates or structure without changing the behavior of formulas already present below. The definitions in this section isolate that invariance principle and nothing more.

\begin{definition}[Logic layer]\label{def:logic-layer}
See \cite[Definition~2.1]{HomologicalVisibilityGluingObstructions2026}.
\end{definition}

The forcing relation is the primitive semantic notion. Consequence is derived from uniform preservation of forcing across all admissible models and states.

\begin{definition}[Conservative extension]\label{def:conservative-extension}
See \cite[Definition~2.2]{HomologicalVisibilityGluingObstructions2026}.
\end{definition}

Thus a higher layer may add expressive power, state coordinates, or geometric constraints, but it may not alter the forcing behavior of formulas that already belonged to a lower layer.

\begin{proposition}[Composition of conservative extensions]\label{prop:conservative-composition}
See \cite[Proposition~2.3]{HomologicalVisibilityGluingObstructions2026}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{corollary}[Finite-chain invariance]\label{cor:whole-chain-invariance}
See \cite[Corollary~2.4]{HomologicalVisibilityGluingObstructions2026}.
\end{corollary}

The constructions used later fit into the finite chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0
\preceq
\mathbb{L}_1
\preceq
\mathbb{L}_2
\preceq
\mathbb{L}_3
\preceq
\mathbb{L}_4.
\]
Here $\mathbb{L}_0$ is the underlying pointwise semantics, $\mathbb{L}_1$ is the information-state forcing layer, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local references and local objects, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds structural absence and, only when a suitable realization prestack has been separately supplied or constructed, gluing-obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ adds refinement dynamics. By \Cref{cor:whole-chain-invariance}, the passage up the chain is cumulative rather than revisionary: each later layer preserves the forcing behavior already fixed below.

The conditional clause in the description of $\mathbb{L}_3$ is part of the formal bookkeeping. An $\mathbb{L}_2$-model may contain a local-object presheaf and an intended abelian band without carrying any fibred groupoid of realizations. In that case the structural absence predicates attached to $F_{p,s}$ still make sense, but component gerbes, branch obstruction classes, and visible quotients do not. Those higher objects enter only after the additional stack-banded realization data required in \Cref{def:realization-prestack,def:gluing-sensitive-realization-input} have been supplied, or after the separated split-lift criterion of \Cref{prop:split-realization-prestack} has constructed them.

This five-layer chain is the complete formal setting of the present paper. Standard site-theoretic, descent-theoretic, and homological inputs are cited at the points where they are used.
