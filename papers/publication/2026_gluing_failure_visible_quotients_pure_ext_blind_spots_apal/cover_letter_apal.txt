Dear Editors,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript,

"Gluing Failure, Visible Quotients, and Pure-Ext Blind Spots,"

for publication in the Annals of Pure and Applied Logic.

This version is deliberately narrower than the longer manuscript from which it was extracted. It is organized around three main results. First, it proves a forcing-necessity theorem: compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable in the lower information-state forcing language, so the local-object layer contributes genuine logical expressivity rather than a change of presentation.

Second, it gives a branch-sensitive gerbe semantics for gluing failure. Visible branches are identified with the global connected components of a realization stack, and under global conservativity gluing failure is characterized exactly by the non-neutrality of all component gerbes.

Third, for finite abelian branch obstructions on finite Cech nerves, the universal coefficient theorem yields a homological evaluation map whose cokernel is the intrinsic visible quotient. This quotient captures exactly the H_2-visible part of the obstruction, while pure Ext-type residues are character-blind. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case, the paper recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and identifies the precise blind residual highlighted by Caru.

We believe the manuscript fits the journal's scope in mathematical logic and in applications of logic to mathematics and theoretical computer science. It also speaks directly to two active APAL-adjacent lines of work: information-state / team-style semantics and sheaf-theoretic contextuality. The present version omits the multi-branch budget calculus, refinement dynamics, and complexity appendix in order to keep the main theorem package focused.
