\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Truth at a fully specified point of evaluation is too coarse once one works under partial information. One may have ruled out many realizations without selecting a single assignment, or one may have fixed an admissible reference for which local realizations exist although no global object does. The purpose of this paper is to describe this local-to-global gap in forcing semantics and to isolate, when a finite abelian obstruction is present, the part of the obstruction that is semantically visible.

The lower semantic layer belongs to familiar territory. Kripke and Beth semantics replace absolute truth by forcing at stages of information and impose persistence under refinement \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,TroelstraVanDalen1988,Fitting1969}; team and inquisitive semantics evaluate formulas on information states or teams, and provide the natural comparison point for the flat fragment considered here \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007,CiardelliRoelofsen2011,CiardelliGroenendijkRoelofsen2015,CiardelliIemhoffYang2020,AlbertGradel2022,CiardelliGrilletti2022,Galliani2021}. On the geometric side we use standard sheaf, stack, and gerbe machinery \cite{MacLaneMoerdijk1994,Johnstone2002,Giraud1971,StacksProject}; for semantic applications of local-to-global methods see also \cite{AbramskySadrzadeh2014,LoSadrzadehMansfield2022}. The comparison with contextuality draws on the sheaf-theoretic framework of Abramsky and Brandenburger and its cohomological and homotopical refinements \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,AbramskyMansfieldBarbosa2015,Caru2017,Caru2018,OkayRaussendorf2020,OkaySheinbaum2021,AbramskyBarbosa2021,Aasnaess2022}.

What is specific to the present setting is the passage from the flat lower language to a layer with local-object predicates. On lower-layer formulas, forcing at an information state is exactly flat team semantics on a nonempty team of assignments. New behaviour appears only once one distinguishes local sectionability, compatible local sectionability, and global sectionability for an admitted reference. This enrichment is not merely expositional: \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization} identifies compatible local existence with nonemptiness of the sheafified fibre, while \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} shows that the predicates expressing local, compatible-local, and gluing-level phenomena are not definable in the lower information-state language.

The geometric part of the paper is organized so that the hypotheses are explicit. Visible value classes are identified with the global sections of the sheafification of the local-object presheaf, equivalently with the global connected-component sections of a realization stack (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}). When a gluing-sensitive realization prestack has been chosen, each visible branch determines a component gerbe (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}), and gluing failure is characterized by the coexistence of visible branches with non-neutrality of every component gerbe (\Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}). The necessary extra choices are packaged in the conditional datum of \Cref{def:conditional-datum}; no general existence theorem for gluing-sensitive lifts is proved here.

The homological analysis is likewise carried out relative to a finite presentation of a chosen branch obstruction. For a conditional datum
\[
(r,p,\mathfrak P_r,\Band,v,\mathcal U,A,\omega),
\]
the universal coefficient sequence yields the homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\longrightarrow A,
\]
and we define the visible quotient by
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}(\mathcal U,A,\omega):=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}).
\]
We show that this quotient is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible characters (\Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient}), that it is independent of the choice of band trivialization and invariant under refinements inducing surjections on $H_2$ (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-invariance}), and that the completely character-blind case is precisely the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ case $\omega\in\ker(\eta_A)$ (\Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}). In the support-presheaf specialization, the Abramsky--Brandenburger compatible-family/no-global-section picture is recovered without any lift hypothesis (\Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}); the gerbe and visible-quotient layers require the additional data encoded in \Cref{def:conditional-datum}.

Sections \ref{sec:preliminaries} and \ref{sec:information-states} are unconditional. Section \ref{sec:null-decomposition} works under the standing refinement-stability assumption of \Cref{def:refinement-stability}. Sections \ref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} and \ref{sec:homological-visibility} are conditional on a chosen datum in the sense of \Cref{def:conditional-datum}; the latter section further uses finite abelian trivializations and finite nerve presentations, and its invariance statements are restricted to refinements whose effect on $H_2$ is explicitly controlled.

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries} isolates the conservative-extension principle used throughout, and \Cref{sec:information-states} develops the information-state semantics and its flat team reading. In \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} we study local objects, visible branches, the trichotomy of structural absence, and the forcing-theoretic separation between the lower and enriched languages. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} develops the branchwise gerbe semantics of gluing failure, and \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} studies the visible quotient, its blindness kernel, the worked examples, and the comparison with sheaf-theoretic contextuality.
