\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Formal semantics is often presented as truth at a fully specified point of evaluation. That picture is exact when the relevant assignment or world has already been fixed. It is too coarse, however, when information is only partial. One may have ruled out some realizations without having selected a unique one; one may have an admissible reference before a global object has formed; or one may have compatible local data that fail to glue. In such situations the natural semantic notion is not pointwise truth but forcing at an information state.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Related work.}
Several familiar traditions isolate parts of this picture. Kripke and Beth semantics replace absolute truth by forcing at stages of information and impose persistence under refinement \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,TroelstraVanDalen1988}. Team and inquisitive semantics evaluate formulas on information states or teams and treat pointwise truth as the flat fragment of a richer state-based semantics \cite{Ronnholm2022,Puncochar2022,AlbertGradel2022,CiardelliGrilletti2022}. Sheaf-theoretic semantics separates local from global existence and treats gluing failure as a structural defect rather than as a mere lack of value \cite{MacLaneMoerdijk1994}. Related local-to-global obstruction viewpoints also appear in the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality and its cohomological refinements \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyMansfieldBarbosa2012,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,Caru2017}. Classical sheaf background and the site-theoretic formalism used below are standard \cite{Johnstone2002,Bredon1997,StacksProject}. The present paper combines these themes in order to address a single question: once a chosen realization of an admitted reference carries a finite abelian gluing obstruction, what part of that obstruction is semantically visible without enlarging the state space, and what part survives only as a blind residue?

The paper is deliberately narrow and is best read as a focused note. We retain only the lower-layer pointwise and information-state semantics, the local-object layer needed to express compatible local presence and gluing failure, and the sheaf/gerbe machinery used to interpret branch obstructions. We do not attempt here to derive the site-theoretic layer from an underlying measurable recursive-addressing hierarchy, and we do not claim that an arbitrary set-valued local object functor canonically determines either a realization prestack or a branch obstruction class. A realization prestack is separate input: the band fixes automorphism sheaves, but it does not provide the off-diagonal isomorphism torsors required to realize non-separated local object data. The formal split construction is therefore treated only as a separatedness criterion; outside the separated case, the existence of a realization prestack is an additional hypothesis or construction, not a consequence of having chosen a band. The unconditional split construction belongs to the sheafified local-object functor, where it realizes the visible sheafified classes and not, in general, the original presheaf. Accordingly, the phrase ``a stack-banded realization prestack with band $\Band$'' is used below only after such a prestack has been supplied or constructed under an explicit criterion. The dynamic refinement, support, complexity, symbolic-dynamical cylinder, and inverse-limit material of the longer manuscript is omitted here; standard background for those omitted directions may be found in \cite{LindMarcus1995,RibesZalesskii2010}.

Three dependency levels are kept separate throughout. The presheaf $F_{p,s}$ supplies local isomorphism classes and supports the sheafification result. A band $\Band$ supplies only the intended diagonal automorphism sheaves of a possible realization. A realization prestack supplies the missing groupoid data: objects, off-diagonal isomorphism sheaves, restriction functors, and composition. A stack-banded realization input then includes the induced identifications
\[
\beta^{\mathfrak L}_x:\Band|_U\xrightarrow{\sim}
\underline{\mathrm{Aut}}_{\mathfrak L_r}(x)
\qquad (x\in\mathfrak L_r(U)),
\]
which \Cref{prop:stackification-band-descends} constructs uniquely from the compatible prestack-level banding. The component-gerbe theorems use this stack-level banding, not merely the diagonal automorphism identifications inside $\mathfrak P_r$. The gerbe and obstruction theorems use only this realization level, together with the stated global-conservativity and finite-nerve hypotheses. This separation is used again in the examples: \Cref{prop:semantic-gerbe-realization,cor:good-cover-semantic-models} first construct an actual banded gerbe as the realization prestack and verify global conservativity, the visible branch, and the branch class; only then do the $S^2$-type and $\mathbb{R}P^2$-type cases apply the homological quotient theorem.

Against that background, the scope of the present paper is two-sided. On lower-layer formulas, the information-state semantics is just flat team semantics on nonempty sets of assignments. The phenomena studied here begin only when local-object predicates and a chosen realization-theoretic layer are added. At that point, the logic can express compatible local presence, global absence, and gluing failure, and these predicates are invisible to the lower flat fragment. This is why the paper is not merely a team-semantical definability note and also not merely a sheaf-theoretic repackaging of contextuality.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main result A: forcing necessity.}
After separating admissible reference, local sectionability, compatible local sectionability, and global sectionability, we show that compatible local existence is characterized by sheafification (\Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization}). The key logical separation theorem is an automorphism-invariant undefinability result: compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable by lower information-state formulas that omit the local-object predicates and do not name the distinguished reference constants (\Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}). This is the result that makes the enrichment logically necessary rather than merely convenient.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main result B: visible branches and gluing failure.}
After fixing an admitted reference, the paper works with a gluing-sensitive realization input in the sense of \Cref{def:gluing-sensitive-realization-input}: a chosen stack-banded realization prestack, a conjugation-compatible band, the explicit induced banding of all stackified automorphism sheaves, and global conservativity at the terminal fibre. This input is not obtained by merely naming a band. The split construction of \Cref{prop:split-realization-prestack} is included to show exactly where the easy construction works and where it fails: by \Cref{def:equality-sieve-pair,lem:split-isomorphism-presheaf}, the split isomorphism presheaf between $\sigma,\tau\in F_{p,s}(U)$ has value $\Band(V)$ exactly on those refinements $V\to U$ where the restrictions of $\sigma$ and $\tau$ agree, and is empty otherwise. This is a presheaf on the whole slice $\mathcal C_a/U$, not a constant presheaf decided in the single fibre over $U$. Thus a pair that is distinct over $U$ but equal after a cover gives local identity isomorphisms with no global split isomorphism. \Cref{lem:equality-loci-separated} identifies this restriction-dependent support with separatedness, while \Cref{prop:nonseparated-pairs-obstruct-split,ex:nonseparated-split-lift,cor:nonseparated-split-fails} record the resulting failure in the non-separated case. The alternative split construction after sheafification is stated separately in \Cref{cor:sheafified-split-lift}; it changes the object functor to the sheafified one and therefore cannot be used as an existence theorem for the original $F_{p,s}$ unless the unit is already an isomorphism on the slice. More strongly, \Cref{prop:realization-additional-data,cor:band-does-not-force-realization,prop:no-band-only-realization-principle} show that a non-separated pair requires genuine off-diagonal torsor data, and that if all $\Band$-torsors on the slice are trivial, then a non-separated local-object presheaf has no realization prestack with that band, split or nonsplit. \Cref{prop:realization-dependency-ledger,rem:realization-hypothesis-ledger} record the resulting dependency used by the rest of the paper. Once the gluing-sensitive realization input has been fixed, stackification recovers the sheafification of isomorphism classes (\Cref{prop:stackification-components}), visible value classes are the global connected components of the realization stack (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}), and stack-global objects can be compared back to the original local object functor. In that setting, each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe using the stack banding of \Cref{def:realization-prestack}, item~\textup{(ii)} (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition,cor:branch-obstruction-uses-stack-band}), and gluing failure occurs exactly when visible branches exist but every component gerbe is non-neutral (\Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}). Under branch constancy, the realization stack decomposes into those component gerbes (\Cref{cor:branch-decomposition}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main result C: intrinsic visible quotient and pure-Ext blindness.}
For a finite abelian branch obstruction attached to such a chosen visible branch on a finite \v{C}ech nerve presentation, the homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A
\]
defines the intrinsic visible quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}:=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}),
\]
and \Cref{thm:visible-quotient-presentation-invariance,thm:visible-quotient-transport-invariance} prove that this quotient is well defined on the allowed finite-presentation transport class: fixed-coefficient homological refinements preserve it on the nose, and coefficient retivializations on a common homological refinement transport it canonically. \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient} then proves that the same quotient is exactly the one detected by the class-admissible character channels. The blind part is equally sharp: \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions} identifies complete character-blindness with the case where the surviving obstruction class lies entirely in the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-summand of the universal coefficient sequence. In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting, \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison} recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and turns Car\`u-type blind cases into a theorem about pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residuals.

Standard inputs and new contributions should be kept separate. Sheafification, stackification, banded gerbes, and the universal coefficient sequence are not claimed as new, and the sheafification and component-identification results below are included as bookkeeping lemmas for the semantic application. The split-lift analysis is likewise a hypothesis-control result: it records the exact separated case in which the easy split prestack exists and the precise mixed-support obstruction that prevents using a band as a general realization theorem. Results whose content is the standard matching-family description of sheafification, the standard passage from a prestack to the sheaf of connected components, or the standard \v{C}ech presentation of a banded gerbe are used as infrastructure, not as novelty claims. The new contribution is their joint use in this narrowed setting: an automorphism-invariant forcing-necessity theorem for gluing predicates, a branch-sensitive gerbe semantics of gluing failure for chosen gluing-sensitive lifts, and the identification of the visible quotient with the $H_2$-detected part of an obstruction on its allowed finite-presentation transport class while the blind residue is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part. The finite $S^2$-type and $\mathbb{R}P^2$-type examples are realized by the verified construction of \Cref{prop:semantic-gerbe-realization,cor:good-cover-semantic-models}; their local-object functors and global conservativity hypotheses are checked before the homological calculations are applied, so the examples are not merely formal coefficient computations.

The main notation is as follows. The lower semantic layers are denoted by $\mathbb L_0$ and $\mathbb L_1$. For an admitted reference $r$ equipped with a chosen stack-banded realization prestack, the prestack is $\mathfrak P_r$, its stackification is $\mathfrak L_r$, the component gerbe of a visible branch $v$ is $\mathfrak G_v$, the branch obstruction class attached to a chosen branch of a chosen gluing-sensitive lift is $\omega_v$, the intrinsic hidden subgroup is $K_{\omega}$, and the intrinsic visible quotient is $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}$; these are intrinsic only on the branch obstruction's allowed finite-presentation transport class, not on arbitrary presentations or coefficient choices.

The paper is organized accordingly. \Cref{sec:preliminaries} and \Cref{sec:information-states} isolate the lower-layer semantics and its conservative-extension behavior. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} develops local objects, sheafification, the split-lift and realization-input audit of \Cref{subsec:realization-inputs}, visible value classes, and the forcing-necessity theorem. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} gives the branch-gerbe semantics of gluing failure. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} develops the intrinsic visible quotient, the pure-Ext blindness theorem, two worked finite examples, and the unique-branch support-presheaf comparison with contextuality.
