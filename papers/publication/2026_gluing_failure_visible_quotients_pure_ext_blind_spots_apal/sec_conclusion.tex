\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

Three claims have been established. First, the local-object layer is logically necessary: compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable in the lower information-state forcing language (\Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}). Second, once a conditional datum in the sense of \Cref{def:conditional-datum} has been fixed, visible branches are exactly the global connected-component sections of the realization stack, and gluing failure is the situation in which compatible local data exist but every component gerbe is non-neutral (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components,thm:gerbe-null-semantics}). Third, for a finite presentation of a branch obstruction, the visible quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}(\mathcal U,A,\omega)=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$ captures precisely the $H_2$-detected part of the obstruction: it is independent of band trivialization, stable under $H_2$-surjective refinements, and leaves as blind residue exactly the subgroup $\ker(\eta_A)$ (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-invariance,thm:character-blind-obstructions}).

The comparison with contextuality is correspondingly two-level. At the support-presheaf level, \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison} recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger compatible-family/no-global-section picture without any lift hypothesis. At the gerbe and homological levels, \Cref{thm:unique-branch-banded-gerbe} identifies the Car\`u-type blind cases as precisely the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ residuals of the obstruction class, under the additional gluing-sensitive lift and finite-presentation hypotheses. The worked examples separate the visible and blind extremes, while \Cref{ex:end-to-end} verifies the full conditional package in one finite model.

The scope of the paper is therefore exact: it isolates the branch-gerbe semantics of gluing failure and the visible/blind decomposition of finite abelian obstruction classes, while making the hypothesis profile of each layer explicit.
