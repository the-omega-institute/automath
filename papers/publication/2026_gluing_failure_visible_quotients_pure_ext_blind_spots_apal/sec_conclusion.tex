\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The present version is built around a narrower claim than the longer manuscript. Its first result is logical: compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable in the lower information-state forcing language (\Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}), so the local-object layer over a meet-semilattice site with Grothendieck pretopology is genuinely necessary. Its second result is conditional in the precise sense formalized in \Cref{def:conditional-datum}: once a gluing-sensitive realization prestack, a visible branch, and a gerbe-adapted cover have been chosen, visible branches are the global sections of the sheaf of connected components of the realization stack, and gluing failure is the situation in which compatible local data exist but every component gerbe is non-neutral. No general existence theorem for such lifts is proved here; the paper presents the gerbe semantics as a conditional framework, and the end-to-end example verifies the hypothesis in one concrete case. Its third result is homological: for a chosen finite presentation of a branch obstruction, the visible quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}(\mathcal U,A,\omega)=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$ is independent of band trivialization (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-invariance}(i)) and invariant under refinements whose induced map on $H_2$ is surjective (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-invariance}(ii), \Cref{cor:subdivision-visible-quotient}), so the visible part of an obstruction is its $H_2$-detected part, while the blind remainder is exactly the kernel of the universal coefficient map $\eta_A$. When $H_2$-surjectivity is not available, one retains only the canonical quotient maps of \Cref{rem:cover-dependence-visible-quotient}.

The external point of contact with established work is likewise separated into two parts. The support-presheaf part (\Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}) recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture unconditionally at the presheaf level. The banded-gerbe part (\Cref{thm:unique-branch-banded-gerbe}) characterizes the Car\`u-type cohomological blind cases as the $\ker(\eta_A)$-part of the obstruction class, under the additional gluing-sensitive lift and finite-presentation hypotheses. The contextuality dictionary in \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} makes explicit which ingredients are standard support-presheaf data and which belong to the extra branch-gerbe layer. The end-to-end worked example (\Cref{ex:end-to-end}) verifies all conditional hypotheses from the local reference frame through the visible quotient in a single concrete model, while the $S^2$-type and $\mathbb{R}P^2$-type examples isolate the visible and blind extremes of the homological theory.

What has been omitted is deliberate. The multi-branch budget calculus, refinement dynamics, and complexity bounds belong to a different narrative. The present paper is intended to stand or fall on the branch-gerbe semantics of gluing failure and on the identification of the visible quotient and its $\ker(\eta_A)$ blind residue.
