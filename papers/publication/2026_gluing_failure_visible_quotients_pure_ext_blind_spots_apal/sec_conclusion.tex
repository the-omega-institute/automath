\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The present version is built around a narrower claim than the longer manuscript. Its first result is logical: compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable in the lower information-state forcing language (\Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}), so the local-object layer over a meet-semilattice site with Grothendieck pretopology is genuinely necessary. Its second result is semantic: visible branches are the connected components of the realization stack, and gluing failure is the situation in which compatible local data exist but every component gerbe is non-neutral; this holds under the gluing-sensitive lift hypothesis (\Cref{def:gluing-sensitive}), which is explicitly verified in the discrete-stack case. Its third result is homological: the visible quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$ is independent of band trivialization (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-invariance}(i)) and independent of cover refinement when the refinement map on $H_2$ is surjective (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-invariance}(ii)), so the visible part of an obstruction is its $H_2$-detected part, while the blind remainder is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residual.

The external point of contact with established work is separated into two parts. The discrete-stack part (\Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}) recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture unconditionally in the unique-branch support-presheaf setting. The banded-gerbe part (\Cref{thm:unique-branch-banded-gerbe}) characterizes the Car\`u-type cohomological blind cases as the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part of the obstruction class, under the additional gluing-sensitive lift hypothesis. The end-to-end worked example (\Cref{ex:end-to-end}) verifies all hypotheses from the local reference frame through the visible quotient in a single concrete model, while the $S^2$-type and $\mathbb{R}P^2$-type examples isolate the visible and blind extremes of the homological theory.

What has been omitted is deliberate. The multi-branch budget calculus, refinement dynamics, and complexity bounds belong to a different narrative. The present paper is intended to stand or fall on the branch-gerbe semantics of gluing failure and on the identification of the visible quotient and its pure-Ext blind residue.
