\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The lower-language part of the paper shows that gluing phenomena enter only with the local-object layer: compatible local sectionability is controlled by sheafification, and the predicates expressing local, compatible-local, and gluing-level absence are not definable in the flat fragment (\Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization,thm:pointwise-irreducibility}). Once a conditional datum has been fixed, visible branches become global connected-component sections of a realization stack, and gluing failure is exactly the case in which such branches exist but every corresponding component gerbe is non-neutral (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components,thm:gerbe-null-semantics}).

For a finite presentation of a branch obstruction, the quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}(\mathcal U,A,\omega)=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})
\]
is the character-detectable part of the obstruction. It is independent of the chosen band trivialization, stable under $H_2$-surjective refinements, and leaves as blind residue precisely the subgroup $\ker(\eta_A)$ (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-invariance,thm:character-blind-obstructions}). In the support-presheaf specialization one recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger compatible-family/no-global-section picture without any lift hypothesis, while the gerbe and homological refinements require the additional data encoded in \Cref{def:conditional-datum}.
