4. Missing references

At minimum, the paper should cite the following bodies of work.

Mac Lane and Moerdijk, Sheaves in Geometry and Logic, for sites, sheaves, separatedness, and sheafification.

Johnstone, Stone Spaces or Sketches of an Elephant, for finitary coverages / Grothendieck topologies.

Stacks Project, for sheafification, descent on sites, and standard categorical facts if the manuscript wants modern references.

Bredon, Sheaf Theory, or Godement, for Čech cohomology and classical sheaf background.

SGA 4 or Vistoli’s notes on descent theory, if the paper wishes to frame descent more formally.

Lind and Marcus, An Introduction to Symbolic Dynamics and Coding, for cylinder geometry and prefix/cylinder constructions.

A standard reference on inverse limits of finite discrete spaces / profinite compactness, such as Bourbaki or Ribes–Zalesskii, if the inverse-limit language is to be retained.

If the authors keep the abelian comparison-torsor language, then a reference on torsors and banded descent data should be added.

5. Specific improvements needed to reach acceptance

The manuscript would need a fundamental redesign before it could be reconsidered.

First, it needs a real mathematical center. Right now the results are mostly standard repackagings. The paper needs either one genuinely nontrivial theorem or a much more modest note-like framing.

Second, the site-theoretic layer must actually be derived from the measurable recursive-addressing layer. At present that bridge is asserted rhetorically, not formalized.

Third, the obstruction theory must become intrinsic. The current 
𝐴
A-banded comparison datum is external to the base presheaf 
𝑅

Fourth, the inverse-limit section must either be strengthened substantially or demoted. In its current form it is not theorem-level material.

Fifth, the paper needs a full bibliography and all cross-references repaired.
