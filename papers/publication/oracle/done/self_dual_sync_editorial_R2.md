I reviewed the attached short manuscript, A Discrete Stokes Formula for Fold-Truncation Defects Across Resolutions. In this version, the theorem-labeled results are Theorem 4.1 and Theorem 7.1, with the rest of the named results given as propositions and corollaries. The paper has a viable deterministic core, but it is not submission-ready because the main telescoping identity is formally ill-typed as written, the bibliography is unfinished, and the statistical section is only a conditional criterion rather than a theorem actually established for the Fold defect process.

Major revision

I do not recommend rejection, because the central xor-telescoping idea is salvageable and could make a publishable short note. I also do not recommend minor revision, because the current version still has a theorem-level formal error and unfinished scholarly apparatus.

5. Specific improvements needed to reach acceptance

The first requirement is to repair the formal defect in the deterministic core. Until the main theorem is correctly typed, the paper cannot be accepted on mathematical grounds.

The second is to finish the bibliography and literature positioning. A paper with unresolved citation placeholders should not be sent to a journal.

The third is to decide what the manuscript is trying to be. In its current state, the strongest publishable object is a short deterministic note centered on the exact telescoping identity, its probability/Hamming consequences, and the cocycle law. Section 7 is worthwhile only if it is either proved for the actual Fold process or very clearly presented as an external conditional criterion, not as a parallel theorem of equal standing. 

main

The fourth is expository: Section 2 needs one explicit finite-window formula for Fold_m, so that the reader can verify examples and understand where the defects come from without relying on external project context.
