Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript "Finite-window rigidity in Fibonacci numeration" for consideration in The Fibonacci Quarterly, following the suggestion of Professor Curtis Cooper after our submission to the Journal of Number Theory (Ref. JNTH-D-26-00337).

The paper addresses a natural question about Zeckendorf normalization: given overlapping windows of fixed length m sliding along an arbitrary binary string, when do the local Zeckendorf normal forms determine the original string uniquely? We prove that m >= 3 is the sharp threshold for this finite-window rigidity. The main results are:

1. The fold map Fold_m is the normal-form map of a finite terminating confluent rewrite system, and the family (Fold_m) is compatible with restriction in m.

2. For m >= 3, the associated sliding block code gives an exact block bijection: every block of n >= m consecutive output symbols has a unique binary preimage of length n+m-1, with optimal inverse memory m-1.

3. Explicit formulas for Bernoulli measures pushed forward through the fold map, an exact m-step Markov law for the residue process, and the right Fischer cover of the image subshift.

All proofs start from Zeckendorf's theorem and use elementary Fibonacci arithmetic combined with techniques from symbolic dynamics. The paper is entirely self-contained.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by any other journal. All authors have approved the submission.
