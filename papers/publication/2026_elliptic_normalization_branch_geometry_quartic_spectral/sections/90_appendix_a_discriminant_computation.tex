\section{Discriminant computation}\label{app:disc}

Recall
\[
\Pi(\lambda,y)=\lambda^4-\lambda^3-(2y+1)\lambda^2+\lambda+y(y+1).
\]
Its derivative with respect to \(\lambda\) is
\[
\partial_\lambda \Pi(\lambda,y)=4\lambda^3-3\lambda^2-2(2y+1)\lambda+1.
\]

For a monic polynomial \(P(\lambda)\) of degree \(n\), the discriminant satisfies
\[
\Disc_\lambda P = (-1)^{n(n-1)/2}\Res_\lambda(P,P').
\]
Here \(n=4\) so \((-1)^{n(n-1)/2}=(-1)^6=1\), and thus
\[
\Disc_\lambda \Pi(\lambda,y)=\Res_\lambda\bigl(\Pi(\lambda,y),\partial_\lambda \Pi(\lambda,y)\bigr).
\]

The resultant can be computed as the determinant of the \(7\times 7\) Sylvester matrix formed from the coefficients of \(\Pi\) and \(\partial_\lambda\Pi\).
Expanding and factoring yields the complete factorization
\[
\Disc_{\lambda}\Pi(\lambda,y)=-y(y-1)\bigl(256y^3+411y^2+165y+32\bigr),
\]
which is the claim in Corollary~\ref{cor:disc-factor}.
