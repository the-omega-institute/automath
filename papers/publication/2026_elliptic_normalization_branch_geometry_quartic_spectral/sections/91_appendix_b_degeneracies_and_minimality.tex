\section{Degenerate parameters and resultant check}\label{app:degeneracies}

This appendix records the explicit cancellations at the exceptional parameter values \(y\in\{-1,0,1\}\) and the resultant identity used in Theorem~\ref{thm:minimal-order}.

\subsection{Resultant}

A direct computation gives
\[
\operatorname{Res}_t(N,D)=y^3(y-1)^3(y+1)^6.
\]
Hence \(N(t,y)\) and \(D(t,y)\) can have a common factor only when \(y=-1,0,1\).

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\(y=1\)}{y=1}}

At \(y=1\),
\[
N(t,1)=-(t-1)(t+1)^2,
\qquad
D(t,1)=(t-1)(t+1)^2(2t-1),
\]
so
\[
Z(t,1)=\frac{N(t,1)}{D(t,1)}=\frac{1}{1-2t},
\qquad
Z_m(1)=2^m.
\]

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\(y=0\)}{y=0}}

At \(y=0\),
\[
N(t,0)=-(t-1)(t+1),
\qquad
D(t,0)=(t-1)^2(t+1),
\]
so
\[
Z(t,0)=\frac{N(t,0)}{D(t,0)}=\frac{1}{1-t}.
\]
Thus the reduced denominator degree drops from \(4\) to \(1\), and the effective recurrence collapses to order \(1\) with \(Z_m(0)=1\) for all \(m\ge 0\).

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\(y=-1\)}{y=-1}}

At \(y=-1\), one has \(N(t,-1)=D(t,-1)\), hence \(Z(t,-1)\equiv 1\).

\subsection{Minimality for generic \texorpdfstring{\(y\)}{y}}

For \(y\notin\{-1,0,1\}\), the resultant is nonzero and none of the above common factors occur; equivalently, \(N(t,y)\) and \(D(t,y)\) are coprime.
Therefore the reduced denominator has degree \(4\) and the order-\(4\) recurrence \eqref{eq:recurrence} is minimal, as used in Theorem~\ref{thm:minimal-order}.
