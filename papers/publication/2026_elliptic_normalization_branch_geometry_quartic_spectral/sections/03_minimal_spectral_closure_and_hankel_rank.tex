\section{Minimal spectral closure and Hankel rank}\label{sec:hankel}

\subsection{Reduced rationality and minimal recurrence order}

Since $Z(t,y)=N(t,y)/D(t,y)$ with $\deg_t D=4$, the sequence $\{Z_m(y)\}$ satisfies a linear recurrence of order at most $4$.
Away from the standard degeneracy set in $y$, there is no numerator--denominator cancellation, so the order is exactly $4$; this is a standard property of reduced rational generating functions \cite{Stanley2012,FlajoletSedgewick2009,Partington1989}.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:minimal-order}
For \(y\notin\{-1,0,1\}\), the polynomials \(N(t,y)\) and \(D(t,y)\) are coprime in \(\CC[t]\).
Consequently, the minimal linear recurrence order of \(\{Z_m(y)\}_{m\ge 0}\) is \(4\), and its Hankel rank is \(4\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Appendix~\ref{app:degeneracies} records the resultant
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ND-resultant}
\operatorname{Res}_t(N,D)=y^3(y-1)^3(y+1)^6,
\end{equation}
showing that cancellations occur only at \(y\in\{-1,0,1\}\).
For all other $y$, the rational function $Z(t,y)$ is reduced with a denominator of degree $4$, so the recurrence order cannot drop below $4$.
Equation~\eqref{eq:recurrence} already shows that order-$4$ relations hold, proving minimality.

Let \(H=(Z_{i+j}(y))_{i,j\ge 0}\) be the infinite Hankel matrix.
The recurrence \eqref{eq:recurrence} forces every column with index \(j\ge 4\) to lie in the span of the preceding four columns, hence \(\operatorname{rank} H\le 4\).
If $\operatorname{rank}H<4$, the first four columns would be dependent, giving a recurrence of order at most $3$, which contradicts the minimality established above.
Therefore the Hankel rank is exactly $4$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Degenerate parameters}

At \(y=0\) and \(y=1\), the denominator \(D(t,y)\) has a reducible factor that cancels with \(N(t,y)\), lowering the effective recurrence order; at \(y=-1\), one has \(N(t,-1)=D(t,-1)\) and thus \(Z(t,-1)\equiv 1\).
These points form a finite degeneracy set and do not affect the generic quartic spectral closure discussed throughout the paper.
