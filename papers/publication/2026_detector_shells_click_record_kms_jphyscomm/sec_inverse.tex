\section{Shell-pair observables and ratio identifiability}\label{sec:inverse}

We now examine the joint observables carried by the shell pair and their local
identifiability content. The claims below are deliberately layered. A ratio of
two shells cancels a common response amplitude but still depends on detector
timing parameters. A single Schwarzschild shell pair gives explicit algebra only
after detector constants have been calibrated. A two-mode shell-pair experiment
gives a local inverse for two ratio values in a prescribed two-parameter
background family, subject to a Jacobian test and separate full-data
admissibility checks.
None of these statements is a detector-independent metric reconstruction
theorem.
Throughout this section the shell pair is therefore a conditional object:
the radius data are assumed to come from the response loci of
\Cref{thm:shells-general-main}. The stationary UDW response formula and static
KMS spectral relation supply the Planck denominator structure, while the
positive spectral weight remains a calibrated response-model factor. The
homogeneous results below use the additional
calibration $\mathcal S_x(\Omega)\equiv\mathcal S(\Omega)$ at the compared shell
points.

\subsection{Common-amplitude cancellation and spectral transport}

The shell-pair ratio used below is a quotient of the two Planck denominators,
not a quotient of the two target rates. Its orientation is fixed once and then
kept throughout the section. For shell radii $r_\varphi$ and
$r_{\mathrm{mem}}$ define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:denominator-convention-main}
D_\varphi=e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(r_\varphi)}-1,\qquad
D_{\mathrm{mem}}=e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(r_{\mathrm{mem}})}-1,\qquad
Q_{\varphi/\mathrm{mem}}=\frac{D_\varphi}{D_{\mathrm{mem}}}.
\end{equation}
Thus $Q_{\varphi/\mathrm{mem}}$ always means exact-clock denominator divided by
memory-transition denominator. The exact-clock condition contains the sampling
factor $\Delta\tau$, whereas the memory-transition condition is already a rate
condition. Keeping these two source equations in denominator form is what fixes
the quotient below.

The two members of the pair are obtained from the same field-facing detector
core: the gap, coupling scale, detector profile, and field state are fixed,
while the classical counter mode is changed from ideal one-step blanking to
exponential recovery. In the calibrated homogeneous spectral-weight regime,
this shared core contributes one common positive factor
	\[
	A=\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)
	\]
to both source shell equations.

Under the calibrated homogeneous spectral-weight regime and a common
field-facing detector core, the two source equations in the fixed denominator
notation are
\begin{equation}\label{eq:denominator-shells-main}
\frac{A\Delta\tau}{D_\varphi}=\log\varphi,
\qquad
\frac{A}{D_{\mathrm{mem}}}
=\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau),
\qquad
	A=\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)>0 .
\end{equation}
Here the exact-clock member uses ideal one-step blanking, while the
memory-transition member uses exponential recovery with the same sampling
interval and recovery rate. Since all factors are positive, these two source
equations are equivalently
\begin{equation}\label{eq:denominator-rearranged-main}
D_\varphi=\frac{A\Delta\tau}{\log\varphi},
\qquad
D_{\mathrm{mem}}=\frac{A}{\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)} .
\end{equation}
The factor $\Delta\tau$ appears only in the exact-clock source equation because
the ideal record threshold is $\Gamma_+\Delta\tau=\log\varphi$, whereas the
memory threshold is already a rate equation,
$\Gamma_+=\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)$.

\begin{corollary}[Conditional homogeneous denominator quotient]\label{thm:ratio-main}
Assume the calibrated homogeneous spectral-weight regime and let the exact-clock and
memory-transition shell radii be produced by one field-facing detector core
with fixed gap, coupling scale, detector profile, and field state. The
exact-clock member uses ideal one-step blanking with sampling interval
$\Delta\tau$, and the memory member uses exponential recovery with the same
sampling interval and recovery rate $\kappa_{\mathrm r}$. Using the fixed
denominator convention in \eqref{eq:denominator-convention-main}, the
shell-pair denominator quotient is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ratio-main}
Q_{\varphi/\mathrm{mem}}
=\frac{D_\varphi}{D_{\mathrm{mem}}}
=
\frac{e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(r_\varphi)}-1}
     {e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(r_{\mathrm{mem}})}-1}
=
\frac{\Delta\tau\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)}
{\log\varphi}.
\end{equation}
The reciprocal constant
\[
\frac{\log\varphi}
{\Delta\tau\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)}
\]
belongs to the reciprocal denominator quotient
$D_{\mathrm{mem}}/D_\varphi$ and not to
$Q_{\varphi/\mathrm{mem}}$. In particular, for the two record-selected target
rates
\[
\gamma_\varphi=\frac{\log\varphi}{\Delta\tau},
\qquad
\gamma_{\mathrm{mem}}=\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau),
\]
one has
\begin{equation}\label{eq:target-quotient-main}
\frac{\gamma_\varphi}{\gamma_{\mathrm{mem}}}
=
\frac{\log\varphi}
{\Delta\tau\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)}
=
\frac{D_{\mathrm{mem}}}{D_\varphi}
=Q_{\varphi/\mathrm{mem}}^{-1}.
\end{equation}
This is a cancellation identity inside the calibrated homogeneous response
model; it does not derive the common amplitude $A$ or the homogeneity of
$\mathcal S_x(\Omega)$ from the KMS condition.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The exact-clock and memory-transition thresholds are supplied by
Proposition~\ref{prop:ideal-main} and
\Cref{lem:one-dep-criterion-main}. In the calibrated homogeneous specialization of
\Cref{thm:shells-general-main,cor:homogeneous-main},
\[
\Gamma_+(r,\Omega)=\frac{A}{e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(r)}-1},
\qquad A=\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)>0 .
\]
Substitution gives the two source equations
\eqref{eq:denominator-shells-main}, hence the two positive denominator
identities
\[
D_\varphi=\frac{A\Delta\tau}{\log\varphi},
\qquad
D_{\mathrm{mem}}=\frac{A}{\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)},
\qquad
A=\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)>0.
\]
Dividing the first identity by the second cancels exactly the common response
amplitude $A$ and gives \eqref{eq:ratio-main}. Dividing in the opposite
orientation gives the reciprocal denominator quotient. Since the exact-clock
and memory target rates are
$\gamma_\varphi=\log\varphi/\Delta\tau$ and
$\gamma_{\mathrm{mem}}=\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)$, their quotient
is precisely the reciprocal denominator quotient, proving
\eqref{eq:target-quotient-main}. No further geometric hypothesis is used beyond
the stated response-model and common-core inputs.
\end{proof}

This cancellation law is used here with three hypotheses. First,
the two counter modes use the same field-facing core, so that $A$ is common.
Second, the spectral weight is homogeneous; otherwise the transport factor in
\cref{cor:spectral-transport-main} remains. Third, the sampling and recovery
parameters remain visible through the readout constant
\[
C_D=\frac{\Delta\tau\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)}{\log\varphi},
\]
while the detector gap remains in the response factors on the left. Thus the
ratio law is amplitude-free, not detector-free. What cancels is only the
unknown response amplitude common to the two counter modes. The reciprocal
denominator quotient, if explicitly needed, is
\[
Q_{\mathrm{mem}/\varphi}=\frac{1}{C_D}
=\frac{\log\varphi}{\Delta\tau\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)}.
\]
All ratio maps below use $Q_{\varphi/\mathrm{mem}}=C_D$ unless the reciprocal is
explicitly named.

The homogeneous ratio law is the simplest specialization of a more general identity.

\begin{corollary}[Spectral-transport identity]\label{cor:spectral-transport-main}
Assume the static response regularity and calibration of \Cref{ass:kms-response-main}. Let
$x_\varphi$ and $x_{\mathrm{mem}}$ be points on the exact-clock and
memory-transition loci for one common field-facing detector core with the same
gap, coupling scale, detector profile, and field state, but with the two
counter modes specified above. Thus
\[
\frac{\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S_{x_\varphi}(\Omega)\Delta\tau}
     {e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(x_\varphi)}-1}
=\log\varphi,
\qquad
\frac{\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S_{x_{\mathrm{mem}}}(\Omega)}
     {e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(x_{\mathrm{mem}})}-1}
=\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau).
\]
Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:spectral-transport-main}
\frac{e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(x_{\varphi})}-1}
     {e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(x_{\mathrm{mem}})}-1}
=
\frac{\Delta\tau\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)}
{\log\varphi}\,
\frac{\mathcal S_{x_{\varphi}}(\Omega)}
     {\mathcal S_{x_{\mathrm{mem}}}(\Omega)} .
\end{equation}
Thus every departure from the homogeneous denominator-ratio law is exactly the
displayed spectral-weight transport factor. If the lapse model is
independently known, the same identity can be read as a measurement of that
spectral-weight ratio.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Set
\[
D_\varphi(x_\varphi)=e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(x_\varphi)}-1,\qquad
D_{\mathrm{mem}}(x_{\mathrm{mem}})
=e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(x_{\mathrm{mem}})}-1 .
\]
The two displayed shell equations are positive and therefore equivalent to
\[
D_\varphi(x_\varphi)
=\frac{\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S_{x_\varphi}(\Omega)\Delta\tau}{\log\varphi},
\qquad
D_{\mathrm{mem}}(x_{\mathrm{mem}})
=
\frac{\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S_{x_{\mathrm{mem}}}(\Omega)}
{\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)} .
\]
Taking their quotient in the fixed orientation
$D_\varphi/D_{\mathrm{mem}}$ cancels the common coupling scale
$\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2$ and leaves exactly \eqref{eq:spectral-transport-main}. In the
homogeneous specialization
$\mathcal S_x(\Omega)\equiv\mathcal S(\Omega)$ the final factor is one, so
\Cref{thm:ratio-main} is recovered.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Spectral-weight stability]\label{prop:spectral-stability-main}
Assume the hypotheses of Corollary~\ref{cor:spectral-transport-main}. Suppose that, at the two shell points under comparison,
\[
\mathcal S_x(\Omega)=\mathcal S(\Omega)(1+\varepsilon(x)),
\qquad
|\varepsilon(x)|\le\varepsilon_0<1 .
\]
Let
\[
C_D=\frac{\Delta\tau\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)}{\log\varphi}
\]
and let
\[
R=
\frac{e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(x_{\varphi})}-1}
     {e^{\beta_\infty\Omega N(x_{\mathrm{mem}})}-1}.
\]
Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:spectral-stability-main}
C_D\,\frac{1-\varepsilon_0}{1+\varepsilon_0}
\le R\le
C_D\,\frac{1+\varepsilon_0}{1-\varepsilon_0},
\qquad
\left|\frac{R}{C_D}-1\right|
\le
\frac{2\varepsilon_0}{1-\varepsilon_0}.
\end{equation}
Thus the homogeneous ratio law is stable under uniformly small spectral-weight transport, with an explicit multiplicative error bound.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By the identity in \Cref{cor:spectral-transport-main},
\[
\frac{R}{C_D}
=
\frac{1+\varepsilon(x_\varphi)}{1+\varepsilon(x_{\mathrm{mem}})}.
\]
The two-sided estimate follows by maximizing and minimizing the numerator and denominator under $|\varepsilon|\le\varepsilon_0$. The absolute bound follows from
\[
\left|
\frac{1+\varepsilon_\varphi}{1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mem}}}-1
\right|
=
\left|\frac{\varepsilon_\varphi-\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mem}}}{1+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mem}}}\right|
\le
\frac{2\varepsilon_0}{1-\varepsilon_0}.
\]
\end{proof}

\subsection{Dimensionless small-shell near-horizon hierarchy}

The shell-pair ratio becomes especially sharp near a nonextremal Hartle--Hawking horizon, but only after two separate smallness conditions have been distinguished. The exact denominator ratio is global on the static region where the response model is defined. Passing from that denominator ratio to a proper-distance ratio uses the horizon expansion, so both shell points must enter a Gaussian normal collar of the horizon. Passing further to a linear Planck-denominator law requires the dimensionless product of the detector gap and the collar size to tend to zero. Without this last condition the near-horizon statement remains a nonlinear Planck-ratio identity and no universal proper-distance ratio follows.

\begin{assumption}[Small-shell horizon family]\label{ass:small-shell-main}
A family of calibrated homogeneous shell pairs is called \emph{small-shell} if it is indexed by a parameter $t$, has detector gap $\Omega(t)>0$, and has proper distances
\[
\rho_\varphi(t)>0,\qquad \rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)>0
\]
from the horizon, satisfies the exact homogeneous ratio law
\[
\frac{e^{\beta_\infty\Omega(t) N(\rho_\varphi(t))}-1}
     {e^{\beta_\infty\Omega(t) N(\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t))}-1}
=C_D(t),
\qquad
C_D(t)=
\frac{\Delta\tau(t)\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r}(t),\Delta\tau(t))}
{\log\varphi},
\]
and obeys
\[
\epsilon(t)=\max\{\rho_\varphi(t),\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)\}\downarrow0 .
\]
The detector parameters may be fixed or may vary with $t$. The hypothesis is part of the statement, not a consequence of the exact ratio law: outside such a family the horizon expansion below is not being invoked. This assumption by itself gives only an exact Planck-ratio relation in a normal collar. The linear proper-distance and area-excess laws below additionally assume the dimensionless small-gap condition $\Omega(t)\epsilon(t)\to0$.
\end{assumption}

\begin{lemma}[Near-horizon Planck denominator]\label{lem:varying-gap-denominator-main}
Assume the nonextremal horizon expansion
\[
N(\rho)=\kappa_{\mathrm H}\rho+O(\rho^3),
\qquad
\beta_\infty=\frac{2\pi}{\kappa_{\mathrm H}}.
\]
Let $\Omega(t)>0$, let $0<\rho(t)\le\epsilon(t)$, and suppose
$\epsilon(t)\downarrow0$. Then, uniformly for such $\rho(t)$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:exact-near-horizon-denominator-main}
e^{\beta_\infty\Omega(t)N(\rho(t))}-1
=
e^{2\pi\Omega(t)\rho(t)(1+O(\epsilon(t)^2))}-1 .
\end{equation}
If, in addition, $\Omega(t)\epsilon(t)\to0$, then the denominator linearizes
uniformly:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:varying-gap-denominator-main}
e^{\beta_\infty\Omega(t)N(\rho(t))}-1
=2\pi\Omega(t)\rho(t)
\Bigl(1+O(\Omega(t)\rho(t))+O(\rho(t)^2)\Bigr).
\end{equation}
In particular, if $\Omega(t)$ is bounded, the relative error is $O(\rho(t))$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The horizon expansion and the Hartle--Hawking relation give
\[
\beta_\infty\Omega(t)N(\rho)
=2\pi\Omega(t)\rho\bigl(1+O(\rho^2)\bigr),
\]
uniformly in a fixed normal collar. Since $\rho\le\epsilon(t)$ and
$\epsilon(t)\downarrow0$, this proves
\eqref{eq:exact-near-horizon-denominator-main}. If also
$\Omega(t)\epsilon(t)\to0$, the exponent is $o(1)$ uniformly along the family.
Using $e^y-1=y(1+O(y))$ for $y=o(1)$ then gives
\[
e^{\beta_\infty\Omega(t)N(\rho)}-1
=2\pi\Omega(t)\rho
\bigl(1+O(\rho^2)\bigr)
\bigl(1+O(\Omega(t)\rho)\bigr),
\]
which is \eqref{eq:varying-gap-denominator-main}. For bounded $\Omega(t)$ the
term $O(\Omega(t)\rho(t))$ is $O(\rho(t))$, while
$O(\rho(t)^2)$ is smaller.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Exact collar ratio before linearization]\label{lem:exact-collar-ratio-main}
Assume the small-shell horizon-family hypothesis in
\Cref{ass:small-shell-main} and the nonextremal Hartle--Hawking expansion
\[
N(\rho)=\kappa_{\mathrm H}\rho+O(\rho^3),
\qquad
\beta_\infty=\frac{2\pi}{\kappa_{\mathrm H}}.
\]
Then the exact homogeneous ratio law is equivalent to
\begin{equation}\label{eq:exact-collar-ratio-main}
C_D(t)=
\frac{
e^{2\pi\Omega(t)\rho_\varphi(t)(1+O(\epsilon(t)^2))}-1}
{
e^{2\pi\Omega(t)\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)(1+O(\epsilon(t)^2))}-1},
\end{equation}
with uniform $O(\epsilon(t)^2)$ terms along the family. This is the near-horizon
form that remains valid even when $\Omega(t)\epsilon(t)$ does not tend to zero.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Apply \Cref{lem:varying-gap-denominator-main} without the small-gap
linearization at $\rho_\varphi(t)$ and $\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)$, then substitute
the exact homogeneous ratio law in \Cref{ass:small-shell-main}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Small-shell horizon ratio]\label{thm:horizon-main}
Assume the small-shell horizon-family hypothesis in \Cref{ass:small-shell-main} and the dimensionless small-gap condition
\[
\eta(t):=\Omega(t)\epsilon(t)\longrightarrow0 .
\]
Suppose that the static region ends at a nonextremal Hartle--Hawking horizon, so that $\beta_\infty=2\pi/\kappa_{\mathrm H}$ and, in proper distance $\rho$ from the horizon,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:horizon-main}
N(\rho)=\kappa_{\mathrm H}\rho+O(\rho^3)
\qquad(\rho\downarrow0).
\end{equation}
Then, as $\epsilon(t)\downarrow0$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rho-ratio-main}
\frac{\rho_{\varphi}(t)}{\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)}
=
C_D(t)\Bigl(1+O(\eta(t))+O(\epsilon(t)^2)\Bigr).
\end{equation}
In Schwarzschild, if $A_H=16\pi M^2$ is the horizon area and $A_{\varphi}(t)$ and $A_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)$ are the areas of the shell spheres, then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:area-ratio-main}
\frac{A_{\varphi}(t)-A_H}{A_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)-A_H}
=
C_D(t)^2\Bigl(1+O(\eta(t))+O(\epsilon(t)^2)\Bigr).
\end{equation}
If the detector gap is fixed, or more generally uniformly bounded, these two
estimates reduce to the simpler $O(\epsilon(t))$ relative-error form.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
All estimates below are along the small-shell family, and the constants in the
$O(\cdot)$ terms are uniform after restricting to a sufficiently small normal
collar. Applying \Cref{lem:varying-gap-denominator-main} at
$\rho_\varphi(t)$ and $\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)$ gives
\[
\begin{aligned}
\frac{e^{\beta_\infty\Omega(t) N(\rho_\varphi(t))}-1}
     {e^{\beta_\infty\Omega(t) N(\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t))}-1}
&=
\frac{\rho_\varphi(t)}{\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)}
\frac{1+O(\Omega(t)\rho_\varphi(t))+O(\rho_\varphi(t)^2)}
     {1+O(\Omega(t)\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t))+O(\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)^2)}  \\
&=
\frac{\rho_\varphi(t)}{\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)}
\Bigl(1+O(\eta(t))+O(\epsilon(t)^2)\Bigr).
\end{aligned}
\]
The exact homogeneous ratio law says that the left-hand side is $C_D(t)$.
Hence
\[
C_D(t)=
\frac{\rho_\varphi(t)}{\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)}
\bigl(1+\delta(t)\bigr),
\qquad
\delta(t)=O(\eta(t))+O(\epsilon(t)^2).
\]
After restricting to $\epsilon(t)$ so small that $|\delta(t)|<1/2$,
$(1+\delta(t))^{-1}=1+O(\eta(t))+O(\epsilon(t)^2)$, which proves
\eqref{eq:rho-ratio-main}.

In Schwarzschild, $\rho$ is not the areal-radius increment. With $r_H=2M$,
\[
\rho=2\sqrt{r_H(r-r_H)}+O((r-r_H)^{3/2}),
\qquad
r-r_H=\frac{\rho^2}{4r_H}+O(\rho^4).
\]
Therefore
\[
A(r)-A_H=4\pi(r^2-r_H^2)=2\pi\rho^2+O(\rho^4)
=2\pi\rho^2\bigl(1+O(\rho^2)\bigr).
\]
Applying this at the two shell radii gives
\[
\frac{A_{\varphi}(t)-A_H}{A_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)-A_H}
=
\left(\frac{\rho_\varphi(t)}{\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)}\right)^2
\frac{1+O(\rho_\varphi(t)^2)}
     {1+O(\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)^2)}
=
\left(\frac{\rho_\varphi(t)}{\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)}\right)^2
\bigl(1+O(\epsilon(t)^2)\bigr).
\]
Combining this with~\eqref{eq:rho-ratio-main} gives
\eqref{eq:area-ratio-main}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[High-gap obstruction to the linear ratio]\label{lem:high-gap-obstruction-main}
The condition $\Omega(t)\epsilon(t)\to0$ in
\Cref{thm:horizon-main} cannot be dropped. Suppose, along a small-shell
family, that
\[
\Omega(t)\epsilon(t)\to\Lambda\in(0,\infty),\qquad
\frac{\rho_\varphi(t)}{\epsilon(t)}\to a,\qquad
\frac{\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}(t)}{\epsilon(t)}\to b
\]
with $a,b\in(0,1]$. Then the exact collar ratio gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:high-gap-limit-main}
C_D(t)\longrightarrow
\frac{e^{2\pi\Lambda a}-1}{e^{2\pi\Lambda b}-1},
\end{equation}
whereas the proper-distance ratio tends to $a/b$. These two limits are not equal
in general.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Under the displayed hypotheses the correction
$O(\epsilon(t)^2)$ in \eqref{eq:exact-collar-ratio-main} contributes
$o(1)$ to the exponent, because
$\Omega(t)\rho_\star(t)O(\epsilon(t)^2)=O(\epsilon(t)^2)$ for
$\star\in\{\varphi,\mathrm{mem}\}$. Taking the limit in
\eqref{eq:exact-collar-ratio-main} gives \eqref{eq:high-gap-limit-main}. The
last assertion follows, for example, by taking $a\ne b$ and any fixed
$\Lambda>0$: the function $(e^{2\pi\Lambda x}-1)/x$ is not constant on
$(0,\infty)$.
\end{proof}

\Cref{lem:exact-collar-ratio-main} is the valid near-horizon statement before
linearization. \Cref{thm:horizon-main} is the linear small-shell consequence
obtained only in the dimensionless small-gap regime
$\Omega(t)\epsilon(t)\to0$, and \Cref{lem:high-gap-obstruction-main} shows why
that hypothesis is essential. A fixed shell pair away from the horizon still
satisfies the exact ratio law proved in \Cref{thm:ratio-main}, but the
near-horizon expansion does not supply proper-distance or area-excess
asymptotics. Within the dimensionless small-gap small-shell regime, the leading
ratio is independent of the common response amplitude and depends on the
detector only through the known readout constant $C_D(t)$.

A particularly informative regime is the fast-sampling limit.
\begin{lemma}[Fast-sampling critical rate]\label{lem:fast-gammac-main}
For fixed $\kappa_{\mathrm r}>0$ and $\Delta\tau\downarrow0$, equivalently $s=\kappa_{\mathrm r}\Delta\tau\downarrow0$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fast-sampling-main}
\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)
=
\frac{L+\log L+o(1)}{\Delta\tau},
\qquad
L=\log\frac{1}{s}.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write $x=\Gamma_c/\kappa_{\mathrm r}$. The critical equation~\eqref{eq:gamma-c-main} is
\[
s=\frac{\log x}{x-1}.
\]
For $s\downarrow0$ the non-removable solution has $x\to\infty$. Put $x=(L+u)/s$, with $L=\log(1/s)$. Then
\[
\log x
=\log(L+u)-\log s
=L+\log(L+u),
\]
while
\[
s(x-1)=L+u-s.
\]
The equation $\log x=s(x-1)$ therefore becomes
\[
u=\log(L+u)+s .
\]
Since the right-hand side is $O(\log L)$, this identity first gives $u=O(\log L)$, and substituting back gives
$u=\log L+o(1)$. Hence
\[
\Gamma_c=\kappa_{\mathrm r}x
=\frac{s}{\Delta\tau}\frac{L+\log L+o(1)}{s}
=\frac{L+\log L+o(1)}{\Delta\tau}.
\]
\end{proof}

In the calibrated homogeneous near-horizon setting with fixed detector gap and response amplitude, both target rates diverge as $\Delta\tau\downarrow0$, so the corresponding lapse levels tend to zero. Thus the fast-sampling family is a concrete small-shell family satisfying the dimensionless small-gap condition in Corollary~\ref{thm:horizon-main}. Combining that corollary with Lemma~\ref{lem:fast-gammac-main} gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fast-ratio-main}
\frac{\rho_{\mathrm{mem}}}{\rho_{\varphi}}
\sim
\frac{\log\varphi}{L+\log L}
\longrightarrow0.
\end{equation}
Thus the memory-transition shell collapses toward the horizon parametrically faster than the exact-clock shell. Within this calibrated response model, the shell pair therefore resolves a genuine hierarchy of stretched-horizon scales rather than two nearby realizations of the same thermal profile.

\subsection{Static spherical ratio identifiability}

The shell pair also carries inverse information in static spherical settings, but
there are two distinct uses and they should not be merged. A single shell pair in
a fixed Schwarzschild response model gives explicit algebra once the detector
constants $\beta_\varphi(D)$ and $\beta_{\mathrm{mem}}(D)$ have been calibrated;
that calculation uses the absolute response scale. The two-mode construction
below is different: it first divides within each mode and solves only the
resulting ratio equations. The common response amplitude is absent from those
two equations, but it remains an admissibility variable that must be checked
after a candidate background parameter has been found.

Thus the main local theorem in this subsection uses the conservative inverse
that the ratio equations actually justify: it fixes the recorded radii and
inverts the two ratio values. A separate corollary records what happens if the
four radii are also allowed to vary as algebraic parameters in the residual
equations. That corollary gives an ambient implicit graph, not a neighbourhood
of physical shell records. The physical common-core shell data form a generally
smaller subset, cut out by the undivided shell equations, positivity of one
common hardware prefactor, and the exterior range of the chosen background
family. The distinction is part of the statement, not an interpretation added
after the proof.

For the Schwarzschild statement, ``model-admissible'' means that the displayed radii are generated by the calibrated homogeneous shell equations with a common positive response amplitude, with $r_\varphi>2M$, $r_{\mathrm{mem}}>2M$, and positive detector constants. Equivalently, the algebraic right-hand sides below must give $\beta_\infty^2>0$, $M>0$, and lapse values in $(0,1)$ at both radii. This condition is not cosmetic: arbitrary pairs of positive radii and detector constants need not describe two exterior Schwarzschild shell locations.

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:schw-main}
Assume the calibrated homogeneous spectral-weight regime and define the detector constants
\begin{equation}\label{eq:beta-constants-main}
\beta_{\varphi}(D)=
\frac{1}{\Omega}
\log\!\left(1+\frac{\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)\Delta\tau}{\log\varphi}\right),
\qquad
\beta_{\mathrm{mem}}(D)=
\frac{1}{\Omega}
\log\!\left(1+\frac{\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)}
{\Gamma_c(\kappa_{\mathrm r},\Delta\tau)}\right).
\end{equation}
If model-admissible shell data generated by the same response amplitude occur at distinct radii $r_{\varphi}\neq r_{\mathrm{mem}}$ in Schwarzschild, then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:beta-infty-main}
\beta_\infty^2=
\frac{\beta_{\varphi}(D)^2r_{\varphi}-\beta_{\mathrm{mem}}(D)^2r_{\mathrm{mem}}}
{r_{\varphi}-r_{\mathrm{mem}}},
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mass-main}
M=
\frac{r_{\varphi}r_{\mathrm{mem}}\bigl(\beta_{\varphi}(D)^2-\beta_{\mathrm{mem}}(D)^2\bigr)}
{2\bigl(\beta_{\varphi}(D)^2r_{\varphi}-\beta_{\mathrm{mem}}(D)^2r_{\mathrm{mem}}\bigr)}.
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
In Schwarzschild,
\[
N(r)^2=1-\frac{2M}{r}.
\]
The two homogeneous shell equations are
\[
\frac{\beta_{\varphi}(D)^2}{\beta_\infty^2}=1-\frac{2M}{r_\varphi},
\qquad
\frac{\beta_{\mathrm{mem}}(D)^2}{\beta_\infty^2}=1-\frac{2M}{r_{\mathrm{mem}}}.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
\beta_\infty^2-\frac{2M\beta_\infty^2}{r_\varphi}=\beta_{\varphi}(D)^2,
\qquad
\beta_\infty^2-\frac{2M\beta_\infty^2}{r_{\mathrm{mem}}}=\beta_{\mathrm{mem}}(D)^2.
\]
This is a nonsingular linear system for $\beta_\infty^2$ and $2M\beta_\infty^2$ when $r_\varphi\neq r_{\mathrm{mem}}$. Solving it gives~\eqref{eq:beta-infty-main} and~\eqref{eq:mass-main}. The model-admissibility hypothesis is what guarantees that the displayed right-hand sides are positive and correspond to a shell outside the horizon; arbitrary pairs of radii and detector constants need not do so.
\end{proof}

Proposition~\ref{prop:schw-main} is exact algebra within the response model. It is not an amplitude-free inversion: the detector constants $\beta_\varphi(D)$ and $\beta_{\mathrm{mem}}(D)$ contain the calibrated common response amplitude. Its significance is that, once those detector constants are fixed, two record-defined shell locations determine the global redshift scale and the Schwarzschild mass parameter.

A related construction extends beyond Schwarzschild in a local ratio form. Each
detector operating mode supplies one shell pair and hence one scalar ratio
equation. Two genuinely distinct modes can provide two equations for a
two-parameter background family. The data objects used in those equations are
defined explicitly as follows.

A two-mode radius record is written
\[
\eta=(\eta_{\varphi,1},\eta_{{\mathrm{mem}},1},
      \eta_{\varphi,2},\eta_{{\mathrm{mem}},2}).
\]
Here $J$ denotes the radial-coordinate interval for the chosen static spherical
coordinate convention. When radius records are varied only as ambient algebraic
variables, they are taken in an open set $\mathcal D\subset J^4$ on which the
response factors below are well defined, and the corresponding algebraic ratio
data lie in an open set $\mathcal E\subset\mathcal D\times(0,\infty)^2$.
For each $m$, the exact-clock radius is obtained from ideal one-step blanking with sampling interval $\Delta\tau_m$, while the memory radius is obtained from exponential recovery with the same sampling interval and recovery rate $\kappa_{{\mathrm r},m}$. The detector settings supply the known physical ratio targets
\begin{equation}\label{eq:two-mode-ratio-constants-main}
C_m=\frac{\Delta\tau_m\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{{\mathrm r},m},\Delta\tau_m)}{\log\varphi},
\qquad m=1,2.
\end{equation}

For a candidate background parameter $\theta$, define the two radius-dependent ratio observables
\begin{equation}\label{eq:two-mode-ratio-functions-main}
R_m(\theta;\eta)=
\frac{e^{\beta_\infty(\theta)\Omega N(\eta_{\varphi,m};\theta)}-1}
     {e^{\beta_\infty(\theta)\Omega N(\eta_{{\mathrm{mem}},m};\theta)}-1},
\qquad
\mathcal R_\eta(\theta)=(R_1(\theta;\eta),R_2(\theta;\eta)).
\end{equation}
The physical residuals are
\[
H_m(\theta,\eta)=R_m(\theta;\eta)-C_m,\qquad
H(\theta,\eta)=\mathcal R_\eta(\theta)-C,
\]
where $C=(C_1,C_2)$.

For the later ambient residual graph, introduce the algebraic ratio datum
\[
\mathsf e=(\eta,c),\qquad c=(c_1,c_2),
\]
and set
\begin{equation}\label{eq:two-mode-phi-main}
\Phi_m(\theta,\mathsf e)=R_m(\theta;\eta)-c_m,\qquad
\Phi=(\Phi_1,\Phi_2).
\end{equation}
For the physical detector settings, $c=C$ and $\Phi(\theta,(\eta,C))=H(\theta,\eta)$. Exact generated data at the true parameter are denoted
\[
\eta_*=(r_{\varphi,1},r_{{\mathrm{mem}},1},
        r_{\varphi,2},r_{{\mathrm{mem}},2}),
\qquad
\mathsf e_*=(\eta_*,C).
\]
For fixed detector settings we also write
\[
\Phi_C(\theta,\eta)=\Phi(\theta,(\eta,C))=H(\theta,\eta).
\]

The full shell bookkeeping is separate from the two ratio equations. Let
\[
\mathcal A=\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)
\]
denote the unknown homogeneous hardware prefactor. For a candidate background parameter $\theta$, radius record $\eta$, and mode $m$, the undivided shell equations infer
\[
\mathcal A_{\varphi,m}(\theta;\eta)=
\frac{\log\varphi}{\Delta\tau_m}
\bigl(e^{\beta_\infty(\theta)\Omega N(\eta_{\varphi,m};\theta)}-1\bigr),
\]
and
\[
\mathcal A_{{\mathrm{mem}},m}(\theta;\eta)=
\Gamma_c(\kappa_{{\mathrm r},m},\Delta\tau_m)
\bigl(e^{\beta_\infty(\theta)\Omega N(\eta_{{\mathrm{mem}},m};\theta)}-1\bigr).
\]

\begin{lemma}[Ratio residuals and full common-core data]\label{lem:ratio-admissibility-main}
For a fixed physical mode $m$, candidate parameter $\theta$, and radius record
$\eta$, the equation $H_m(\theta,\eta)=0$ is equivalent to the within-mode
amplitude equality
\[
\mathcal A_{\varphi,m}(\theta;\eta)=\mathcal A_{{\mathrm{mem}},m}(\theta;\eta).
\]
The pair of ratio equations $H(\theta,\eta)=0$ contains no unknown
$\mathcal A$. A radius record is an exact full common-core shell record at the
same $\theta$ only if, in addition, the four estimates
\[
\mathcal A_{\varphi,1},\quad \mathcal A_{{\mathrm{mem}},1},\quad
\mathcal A_{\varphi,2},\quad \mathcal A_{{\mathrm{mem}},2}
\]
are all positive and equal to one common value $\mathcal A>0$, and the four
radii lie in the exterior coordinate ranges of the background family. These
extra conditions are not consequences of the ratio equations.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By the definitions above,
\[
\frac{\mathcal A_{\varphi,m}(\theta;\eta)}
{\mathcal A_{{\mathrm{mem}},m}(\theta;\eta)}
=
\frac{e^{\beta_\infty(\theta)\Omega N(\eta_{\varphi,m};\theta)}-1}
     {e^{\beta_\infty(\theta)\Omega N(\eta_{{\mathrm{mem}},m};\theta)}-1}
\frac{\log\varphi}
{\Delta\tau_m\,\Gamma_c(\kappa_{{\mathrm r},m},\Delta\tau_m)}
=\frac{H_m(\theta,\eta)+C_m}{C_m}.
\]
Thus $H_m(\theta,\eta)=0$ is exactly the equality of the two within-mode
amplitude estimates. The formula for $H_m$ contains only the radius record, the
background family, the gap, and the readout constants; the common prefactor
$\mathcal A$ has cancelled. Equality across the two modes, positivity, and the
exterior-range restrictions are separate requirements. They cannot be inferred
from $H(\theta,\eta)=0$, because the ratio operation has deliberately discarded
the absolute scale and does not encode the allowed radial range.
\end{proof}

\begin{unnumberedlemma}[Data-dependent residual graph]
Let $U_0\subset\RR^2$ and $W_0\subset\RR^q$ be open sets and let
$G:U_0\times W_0\to\RR^2$ be $C^1$. If $G(\theta_0,w_0)=0$ and
$D_\theta G(\theta_0,w_0)$ is nonsingular, then there are neighbourhoods
$U\subset U_0$ of $\theta_0$ and $W\subset W_0$ of $w_0$ and a unique
$C^1$ map $\Psi:W\to U$ such that $\Psi(w_0)=\theta_0$ and
\[
G(\Psi(w),w)=0\qquad(w\in W).
\]
After shrinking $U$ and $W$ if necessary, every solution of
$G(\theta,w)=0$ in $U\times W$ lies on this graph. Freezing the data first
would give only local uniqueness of a zero of $\theta\mapsto G(\theta,w_0)$;
the data-to-parameter map is the graph obtained with $w$ retained as the
parameter.
\end{unnumberedlemma}

\begin{proof}
This is the parameter-dependent implicit function theorem \cite{KrantzParks2002},
applied with $\theta$ as the unknown and $w$ as the parameter.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Fixed-record local ratio inversion]\label{thm:two-mode-main}
Let $\Theta\subset\RR^2$ be open, let $J$ be the radial interval used by a fixed
static spherical coordinate chart, and suppose
\[
N:J\times\Theta\to(0,\infty),
\qquad
\beta_\infty:\Theta\to(0,\infty)
\]
are $C^1$. Choose an exact radius quadruple
\[
\eta_*=(r_{\varphi,1},r_{{\mathrm{mem}},1},
        r_{\varphi,2},r_{{\mathrm{mem}},2})\in J^4
\]
on which the ratio functions in \eqref{eq:two-mode-ratio-functions-main} are
defined. The detector settings are two readout settings of one field-facing
core at gap $\Omega$, with a positive homogeneous prefactor
	$\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)$ shared across both settings but not assumed
known.

Let $C=(C_1,C_2)$ be the ratio targets from
\eqref{eq:two-mode-ratio-constants-main}, and set
\[
\mathcal R_*=\mathcal R_{\eta_*}.
\]
Assume that the exact radius record is produced at
$\theta_*\in\Theta$ by admissible common-core shell data, so that
\[
\mathcal R_*(\theta_*)=C.
\]
If
\begin{equation}\label{eq:two-mode-ratio-jacobian-main}
\det D_\theta\mathcal R_{\eta_*}(\theta_*)\neq0,
\end{equation}
then there are neighbourhoods $U\subset\Theta$ of $\theta_*$ and
$Y\subset(0,\infty)^2$ of $C$, together with a unique $C^1$ branch
\[
\mathcal I_{\eta_*}:Y\to U,\qquad \mathcal I_{\eta_*}(C)=\theta_*,
\]
such that
\[
\mathcal R_*(\mathcal I_{\eta_*}(y))=y\qquad(y\in Y).
\]
Moreover, if $\theta\in U$ and $\mathcal R_*(\theta)=y\in Y$, then
$\theta=\mathcal I_{\eta_*}(y)$. The concrete increment here is a local inverse
from nearby ratio values to background parameters with the radius record
$\eta_*$ held fixed; it is not an inverse for arbitrary nearby four-radius
records, and it does not determine the shared response amplitude.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
With $\eta_*$ fixed, the stated regularity makes
$\mathcal R_*:\Theta\to\RR^2$ a $C^1$ map. Exact model-admissible shell data
satisfy the two homogeneous ratio equations by Corollary~\ref{thm:ratio-main},
hence $\mathcal R_*(\theta_*)=C$.

To make the independence condition explicit, write
\[
B_{\varphi,m}(\theta)=\beta_\infty(\theta)\Omega N(r_{\varphi,m};\theta),
\qquad
B_{\mathrm{mem},m}(\theta)=\beta_\infty(\theta)\Omega N(r_{{\mathrm{mem}},m};\theta).
\]
Then
\[
d_\theta R_m=
\frac{
e^{B_{\varphi,m}}\,d_\theta B_{\varphi,m}\,(e^{B_{\mathrm{mem},m}}-1)
-(e^{B_{\varphi,m}}-1)e^{B_{\mathrm{mem},m}}\,d_\theta B_{\mathrm{mem},m}
}
{(e^{B_{\mathrm{mem},m}}-1)^2}.
\]
Thus the determinant condition is the concrete requirement that the two row
covectors supplied by the two fixed-radius ratio equations are not collinear at
$\theta_*$. It can fail because the detector modes are redundant, because the
two shell pairs sample the background family redundantly, or both.

Apply the ordinary inverse function theorem to
$\mathcal R_*:\Theta\to\RR^2$ at $\theta_*$. The determinant in
\eqref{eq:two-mode-ratio-jacobian-main} is exactly the Jacobian determinant of
this fixed-radius map. The inverse function theorem gives the inverse branch
$\mathcal I_{\eta_*}$ and the stated uniqueness.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Ambient residual graph, not a shell-data inverse]\label{cor:ambient-ratio-graph-main}
In the setting of Corollary~\ref{thm:two-mode-main}, keep the algebraic datum
$\mathsf e=(\eta,c)$ as an explicit parameter in
\eqref{eq:two-mode-phi-main}. Under the Jacobian condition
\eqref{eq:two-mode-ratio-jacobian-main}, there are neighbourhoods
$U\subset\Theta$ of $\theta_*$ and $W\subset\mathcal E$ of $\mathsf e_*$ and a
unique $C^1$ map
\[
\Psi:W\to U,\qquad \Psi(\mathsf e_*)=\theta_*,
\]
such that
\[
\Phi(\Psi(\mathsf e),\mathsf e)=0\qquad(\mathsf e\in W).
\]
After shrinking $U$ and $W$, every solution of
$\Phi(\theta,\mathsf e)=0$ in $U\times W$ lies on this graph. On the
fixed-detector slice
\[
W_C=\{\eta\in\mathcal D:(\eta,C)\in W\},
\qquad
\Psi_C(\eta)=\Psi((\eta,C)),
\]
one has
\[
\Phi_C(\Psi_C(\eta),\eta)=0\qquad(\eta\in W_C),
\]
and if $\theta\in U$ and $\Phi_C(\theta,\eta)=0$ for some $\eta\in W_C$, then
$\theta=\Psi_C(\eta)$. This is only an ambient residual graph for the two ratio
equations. It does not assert that the radius quadruples in $W_C$ are physical
common-core shell data.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply Corollary~\ref{cor:ambient-ratio-graph-app-main}. The full ambient
argument is in the appendix; the slice statement follows by evaluation at
$\mathsf e=(\eta,C)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Fixed-record local full-shell subset]\label{thm:full-shell-subset-main}
In the setting of \Cref{cor:ambient-ratio-graph-main}, shrink $W_C$ if
necessary so that the four radii remain in the coordinate range where the
background family and the response factors are defined. For $\eta\in W_C$ set
\[
\theta(\eta)=\Psi_C(\eta),
\]
and define the two mode amplitudes along the ratio graph by
\[
\mathcal A_m(\eta)=
\mathcal A_{\varphi,m}(\theta(\eta);\eta)
=
\mathcal A_{{\mathrm{mem}},m}(\theta(\eta);\eta),
\qquad m=1,2,
\]
where the equality is the within-mode equality forced by
$\Phi_C(\theta(\eta),\eta)=0$. Let
\[
E_C(\eta)=\mathcal A_1(\eta)-\mathcal A_2(\eta).
\]
Then the nearby full common-core shell records in the fixed-detector slice are
exactly
\begin{equation}\label{eq:full-shell-subset-main}
\mathcal P_C
=
\{\eta\in W_C:E_C(\eta)=0,\ \mathcal A_1(\eta)>0,
\text{ and the four radii are exterior-admissible}\}.
\end{equation}
The function $E_C$ is $C^1$. In particular, if the exact datum $\eta_*$ is
exterior-admissible and
\[
dE_C(\eta_*)\neq0
\]
as a covector on the ambient radius-record space $W_C$, then
$\mathcal P_C$ is, near $\eta_*$, a nonempty $C^1$ codimension-one submanifold
of $W_C$. If this transversality fails, the theorem still gives the exact local
zero-set characterization \eqref{eq:full-shell-subset-main}, but no manifold
structure is asserted.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
For every $\eta\in W_C$, the defining property of the ambient graph gives
\[
\Phi_C(\theta(\eta),\eta)=0,
\]
which is the pair of physical ratio equations $H_m(\theta(\eta),\eta)=0$.
\Cref{lem:ratio-admissibility-main} says that each of these two ratio equations
is equivalent to equality of the exact-clock and memory amplitude estimates
within the same mode. Hence the displayed definitions of
$\mathcal A_m(\eta)$ are unambiguous along the graph.

A full common-core shell record requires exactly one positive homogeneous
hardware prefactor for all four undivided shell equations, together with the
exterior-range restrictions. Along the ratio graph the two estimates within
each mode are already equal, so the only remaining cross-mode amplitude
condition is
\[
\mathcal A_1(\eta)=\mathcal A_2(\eta),
\]
which is $E_C(\eta)=0$. Positivity of the common amplitude and exterior
admissibility are the other two conditions in
\Cref{lem:ratio-admissibility-main}. This proves the set identity
\eqref{eq:full-shell-subset-main}.

The maps $\Psi_C$, $N$, and $\beta_\infty$ are $C^1$ on the neighbourhoods in
question, and the amplitude estimates are compositions of $C^1$ functions with
the smooth exponential function. Therefore $\mathcal A_m$ and $E_C$ are $C^1$.
At exact generated data, the common-core hypothesis gives
$E_C(\eta_*)=0$, so $\eta_*\in\mathcal P_C$ after shrinking to preserve
exterior admissibility and positivity. If $dE_C(\eta_*)\neq0$, the regular
level-set theorem makes $E_C^{-1}(0)$ a $C^1$ codimension-one submanifold of
$W_C$ near $\eta_*$. Positivity and exterior admissibility are open conditions,
so intersecting with them leaves the same local submanifold structure. Without
the nonvanishing derivative, only the proved zero-set characterization remains.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Restriction to full common-core shell records]\label{cor:full-shell-restriction-main}
In the setting of \Cref{thm:full-shell-subset-main}, let
$\mathcal P_C\subset W_C$ be the full common-core subset characterized in
\eqref{eq:full-shell-subset-main}.
For every $\eta\in\mathcal P_C$, the parameter $\Psi_C(\eta)$ is the unique
local background parameter in $U$ compatible with the corresponding full
common-core shell data, and the common amplitude is obtained by evaluating any
one of the four equal undivided amplitude estimates. Conversely, if
$\eta\in W_C$ is compatible with full common-core shell equations at some
$\theta\in U$, then $\theta=\Psi_C(\eta)$ and $\eta\in\mathcal P_C$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If $\eta\in\mathcal P_C$, then by definition the ratio equations hold at
$\Psi_C(\eta)$ and the undivided amplitude, positivity, and exterior-range
checks also hold there. Hence the full shell equations are satisfied with one
positive common amplitude. If another $\theta\in U$ satisfied the same full
shell equations, division within each mode would give
$\Phi_C(\theta,\eta)=0$, so the uniqueness part of
\Cref{cor:ambient-ratio-graph-main} gives $\theta=\Psi_C(\eta)$. The converse is
the same argument in reverse: full common-core shell equations imply the ratio
equations and the admissibility checks, hence the local root is $\Psi_C(\eta)$
and $\eta$ belongs to $\mathcal P_C$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Ratio admissibility and amplitude consistency]\label{rem:amplitude-consistency-main}
Corollary~\ref{thm:two-mode-main}, \Cref{cor:ambient-ratio-graph-main}, and
\Cref{lem:ratio-admissibility-main} separate checks that are sometimes
conflated. The fixed-radius map $\mathcal R_{\eta_*}$ contains the two
ratio-level observables and no unknown common amplitude; the theorem is the
local inverse for those two values. If the radius record is also varied, the
only general statement is the ambient residual graph in
\Cref{cor:ambient-ratio-graph-main}. \Cref{cor:full-shell-restriction-main} is
the step that restricts that graph to full shell data. A candidate ratio-level
solution is a full common-core solution only if the four undivided amplitude estimates
\[
\mathcal A_{\varphi,1}(\theta;\eta),\quad
\mathcal A_{{\mathrm{mem}},1}(\theta;\eta),\quad
\mathcal A_{\varphi,2}(\theta;\eta),\quad
\mathcal A_{{\mathrm{mem}},2}(\theta;\eta)
\]
are positive and equal to one common value in the exact model. Thus amplitude-free ratio inversion means that the amplitude is not needed to solve the two ratio equations; it does not mean that arbitrary radius records automatically come from one physical detector core.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:jacobian-main}
The Jacobian condition~\eqref{eq:two-mode-ratio-jacobian-main} is a local identifiability condition on both the detector modes and the background family, evaluated at the exact radius record and physical ratio target. It can fail because the two modes produce redundant ratio functions, or because the chosen background parametrization affects the two recorded radii in linearly dependent ways. Equality $C_1=C_2$ is therefore not a complete degeneracy criterion; it is a warning sign that the two modes may not provide independent constraints. Choosing distinct sampling and recovery parameters is necessary in practice, but the determinant in~\eqref{eq:two-mode-ratio-jacobian-main} is the actual test.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Schwarzschild worked example}\label{subsec:worked-example}

As a concrete verification of the preceding formulas, \cref{tab:worked} presents a worked example in the Schwarzschild background with $r_{\mathrm s}=2M=1$ (so $\beta_\infty=4\pi$), $\Omega=1$, and $\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}}^2\mathcal S(\Omega)=2$. Two detector modes $D_1=(\Omega,\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}},0.5,\,8.0)$ and $D_2=(\Omega,\lambda_{\mathrm{sw}},1.2,\,3.0)$ are tabulated; these modes have distinct ratio constants $C_1=\Delta\tau_1\Gamma_{c,1}/\log\varphi=0.164812$ and $C_2=\Delta\tau_2\Gamma_{c,2}/\log\varphi=0.228130$, but the actual local identifiability test is the Jacobian condition in \cref{thm:two-mode-main}. For each mode, the critical rate $\Gamma_c$ is obtained from~\eqref{eq:gamma-c-main} and the shell radii from the lapse conditions in \cref{cor:homogeneous-main}. The mode-specific ratio $C_m$ is verified to agree with the denominator quotient in~\eqref{eq:ratio-main}. The inverse formulas of \cref{prop:schw-main} are then applied to the $D_1$ shell pair alone to recover $\beta_\infty$ and $M$ to full numerical precision in the same normalization.

For the two-mode ratio inverse, the nonsingularity check in \cref{thm:two-mode-main} is first evaluated at the fixed radius record $\eta_*$ in the ambient coordinate chart $\theta=(\beta_\infty,M)$, giving
\[
\det D_\theta\mathcal R_{\eta_*}(\theta_*)\approx-2.97\times10^{-1}.
\]
This confirms a local inverse for nearby ratio values at fixed $\eta_*$. For physical Schwarzschild records the parameters are constrained by the Hartle--Hawking relation $\beta_\infty=8\pi M$ (so the family is one-dimensional), so the independent test is the corresponding constrained one-parameter sensitivity check along that curve rather than a two-parameter ambient Jacobian.
