\section{Zero accumulation and the spectral geometry of the boundary}\label{sec:zeros}

\subsection{A Beraha--Kahane--Weiss-type framework}

For each fixed \(y\), the sequence \(\{Z_m(y)\}\) satisfies a constant-coefficient linear recurrence, hence admits a representation
\[
Z_m(y)=\sum_{i=1}^4 a_i(y)\lambda_i(y)^m,
\]
with \(\lambda_i(y)\) the roots of \(\Pi(\lambda,y)\) and coefficient functions \(a_i(y)\) determined by the initial values.
When \(y\) varies, the limiting geometry of the zero sets \(\{y: Z_m(y)=0\}\) is typically controlled by \emph{equimodular competition} among the dominant roots.

\begin{theorem}[Beraha--Kahane--Weiss-type zero accumulation]\label{thm:bkw}
Assume \(y\in\CC\setminus\Branch\) and that the roots \(\lambda_i(y)\) of \(\Pi(\cdot,y)\) are simple.
If there exist indices \(i\neq j\) such that
\[
\abs{\lambda_i(y)}=\abs{\lambda_j(y)}=\max_k \abs{\lambda_k(y)},
\]
and the corresponding coefficient functions do not vanish simultaneously, then \(y\) belongs to the limiting accumulation set of zeros of \(\{Z_m\}\) as \(m\to\infty\).
Moreover, points in the branch locus \(\Branch\) may arise as endpoints of such accumulation sets.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
Theorem~\ref{thm:bkw} is a specialization of the general Beraha--Kahane--Weiss theory for recursively defined families of polynomials; see \cite{BerahaKahaneWeiss1975} for a classical reference.
\end{remark}

In the present model, Theorem~\ref{thm:disc-factor} gives a complete algebraic description of \(\Branch\).
On the real axis, the only nontrivial branch point is \(\yLY<0\), so \(\yLY\) is a natural candidate for an ``edge endpoint'' of the zero accumulation set along real-axis slices.
Numerically, one may trace equimodular curves where two dominant spectral branches satisfy \(\abs{\lambda_i(y)}=\abs{\lambda_j(y)}\) and observe the convergence of zero clouds toward such endpoints; the theoretical mechanism is precisely the combination of equimodular competition and branch-point geometry.
