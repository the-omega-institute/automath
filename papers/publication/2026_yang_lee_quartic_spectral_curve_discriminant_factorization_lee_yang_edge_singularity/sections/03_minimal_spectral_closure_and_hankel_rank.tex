\section{Minimal spectral closure and Hankel rank}\label{sec:hankel}

\subsection{Reduced rationality and minimal recurrence order}

The rational form of \(Z(t,y)\) implies that \(\{Z_m(y)\}\) satisfies a linear recurrence whose order is at most \(\deg_t D=4\).
The key point is that, away from a finite degeneracy set in \(y\), there is no cancellation between numerator and denominator, so the order-\(4\) recurrence is minimal.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:minimal-order}
For \(y\notin\{-1,0,1\}\), the polynomials \(N(t,y)\) and \(D(t,y)\) are coprime in \(\CC[t]\).
Consequently, the minimal linear recurrence order of \(\{Z_m(y)\}_{m\ge 0}\) is \(4\), and its Hankel rank is \(4\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The numerator and denominator are coprime if and only if they have no common root in \(t\), equivalently if and only if their resultant is nonzero.
A direct computation gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ND-resultant}
\operatorname{Res}_t(N,D)=y^3(y-1)^3(y+1)^6.
\end{equation}
Hence \(N(t,y)\) and \(D(t,y)\) can have a common factor only for \(y\in\{-1,0,1\}\).
For \(y\notin\{-1,0,1\}\), the rational function \(Z(t,y)=N(t,y)/D(t,y)\) is already reduced and its denominator has degree \(4\).
Therefore \(\{Z_m(y)\}\) satisfies no linear recurrence of order \(<4\), while \eqref{eq:recurrence} shows that an order-\(4\) recurrence does hold.
The minimal recurrence order is thus \(4\).

For a reduced rational generating function, the minimal recurrence order equals the rank of the infinite Hankel matrix \(\bigl(Z_{i+j}(y)\bigr)_{i,j\ge 0}\); therefore the Hankel rank is also \(4\).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Degenerate parameters}

At \(y=0\) and \(y=1\), the denominator \(D(t,y)\) has a reducible factor that cancels with \(N(t,y)\), lowering the effective recurrence order.
At \(y=-1\), one has \(N(t,-1)=D(t,-1)\) and thus \(Z(t,-1)\equiv 1\).
These points form a finite degeneracy set and do not affect the generic quartic spectral closure.
