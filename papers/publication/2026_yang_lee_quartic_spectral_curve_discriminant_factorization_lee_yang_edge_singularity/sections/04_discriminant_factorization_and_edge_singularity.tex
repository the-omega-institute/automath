\section{Discriminant factorization and a Lee--Yang-type edge singularity}\label{sec:disc}

\subsection{Complete description of the branch locus}

View \(\Pi(\lambda,y)\) in \eqref{eq:spectral-poly} as a degree-\(4\) polynomial in \(\lambda\).
Spectral branching occurs precisely when two (or more) roots in \(\lambda\) collide, which is controlled by the discriminant.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:disc-factor}
The discriminant of \(\Pi(\lambda,y)\) with respect to \(\lambda\) admits the complete factorization
\begin{equation}\label{eq:disc-factor}
\Disc_{\lambda}\Pi(\lambda,y)=-y(y-1)\bigl(256y^3+411y^2+165y+32\bigr).
\end{equation}
Hence the parameter values where the root configuration of \(\Pi(\cdot,y)\) is non-simple are contained in
\[
\Branch=\{y=0\}\cup\{y=1\}\cup\{256y^3+411y^2+165y+32=0\}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The discriminant of a quartic polynomial can be written as a resultant:
\(\Disc_{\lambda}\Pi=\Res_{\lambda}\bigl(\Pi,\partial_{\lambda}\Pi\bigr)\).
For the explicit \(\Pi\) in \eqref{eq:spectral-poly}, the Sylvester determinant can be computed and completely factored, yielding \eqref{eq:disc-factor}.
Details are given in Appendix~\ref{app:disc}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{A nontrivial real-axis edge singularity}

Let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cubic}
c(y)=256y^3+411y^2+165y+32.
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:unique-real-root}
The cubic \(c(y)\) has exactly one real root, denoted \(\yLY\), and it satisfies
\[
\yLY\in(-1.13446,-1.13445).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The discriminant of the cubic \(c\) is negative, hence \(c\) has a unique real root.
Moreover, direct evaluation gives \(c(-1.13446)<0<c(-1.13445)\), which certifies the stated interval containment.
\end{proof}

Except for the endpoint degeneracies \(y\in\{0,1\}\), the real axis therefore contains a unique nontrivial branch point \(\yLY<0\).
We refer to \(\yLY\) as the \emph{Lee--Yang-type real-axis edge singularity} of the present quartic spectral-closure model: it is an algebraic, certificate-friendly anchor point of the critical boundary.
