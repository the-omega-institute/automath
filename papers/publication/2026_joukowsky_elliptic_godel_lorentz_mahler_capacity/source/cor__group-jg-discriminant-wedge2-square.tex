\begin{corollary}[Wedge-square factorization of the discriminant]\label{cor:group-jg-discriminant-wedge2-square}
In the setting of Theorem \ref{thm:group-jg-discriminant-factorization}, assume that \(P\) is monic and separable. Let \(z_1,\dots,z_n\) be the roots of \(P\), and define
\[
P^{\wedge 2}(u):=\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}\bigl(u-z_i z_j\bigr).
\]
Then \(P^{\wedge 2}(u)\in K[u]\), and
\[
\operatorname{Disc}_{w}(Q_r)
=r^{n(n-1)}\,\operatorname{Disc}_{z}(P)\,\Bigl(P^{\wedge 2}(r^{-2})\Bigr)^2.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Because \(P\) is monic, Theorem \ref{thm:group-jg-discriminant-factorization} gives
\[
\operatorname{Disc}_{w}(Q_r)
=\operatorname{Disc}_{z}(P)\,
r^{-n(n-1)}
\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(r^2 z_i z_j-1)^2.
\]
For each pair \(i<j\),
\[
r^2 z_i z_j-1=r^2\bigl(z_i z_j-r^{-2}\bigr),
\]
so
\[
\prod_{i<j}(r^2 z_i z_j-1)^2
=r^{2n(n-1)}\prod_{i<j}\bigl(z_i z_j-r^{-2}\bigr)^2
=r^{2n(n-1)}\Bigl(P^{\wedge 2}(r^{-2})\Bigr)^2.
\]
The last identity uses only the fact that squaring removes the sign discrepancy between \(z_i z_j-r^{-2}\) and \(r^{-2}-z_i z_j\). Substituting this into the discriminant formula yields the stated factorization.

Finally, the coefficients of \(P^{\wedge 2}\) are symmetric polynomials in the roots \(z_1,\dots,z_n\), so they belong to \(K\).
\end{proof}
