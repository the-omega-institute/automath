\begin{abstract}
We study two families of Joukowsky-type maps that send the unit circle to confocal ellipses and extract from them arithmetic, algebraic, and potential-theoretic invariants. First, for the elliptic projection gates \(J_{c,m}(e^{i\theta})=e^{im\theta}+c e^{-im\theta}\), we prove a sharp Fourier support theorem for the logarithmic speed, identify its \(p\)-adic slope invariants, and show that the full spectral data uniquely reconstruct the rational parameter \(c\) and the covering multiplicity \(m\). Second, we algebraize prime-register data by a homomorphism into \(\mathrm{SO}^+(1,1)\), obtaining Lorentz-type composition laws, condition-number identities, and primorial extremality statements for associated ellipses and ellipsoids. Third, for the two-scale transport polynomial generated by \(J_r(z)=rz+r^{-1}z^{-1}\), we establish pullback and discriminant factorizations, derive Lee--Yang elliptic Mahler/free-energy identities, and prove holographic reconstruction results from a single transport scale.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}
The classical Joukowsky map provides one of the simplest conformal mechanisms by which circles generate ellipses and, more generally, confocal families in the plane. It therefore sits naturally at the intersection of conformal mapping, algebraic elimination, and logarithmic capacity theory \citep{Pommerenke1992,Tsuji1959,Ransford1995,GKZ1994}. The purpose of the present paper is to show that once the Joukowsky correspondence is equipped with arithmetic parameters, it also supports a rigid \(p\)-adic spectral calculus and a Lorentz-type algebraization of prime data.

Our first object is the \(m\)-fold elliptic projection gate
\[
J_{c,m}(e^{i\theta})=e^{im\theta}+c\,e^{-im\theta},
\qquad c\in(0,1)\cap\QQ,\ \ m\in\NN,
\]
whose image is an ellipse traversed \(m\) times. The logarithmic speed defect of this parametrization has an exceptionally sparse Fourier expansion, and that sparsity converts arithmetic information about \(c\) into linear asymptotics of \(p\)-adic valuations. This places the problem in the general circle of ideas surrounding local valuations and spectral rigidity in arithmetic analysis \citep{Gouvea1997,Koblitz1984,Serre1973}. We show that the full Fourier family determines \(m\) and all prime valuations of \(c\), while finite congruence audits necessarily miss sufficiently deep \(p\)-adic perturbations.

Our second theme concerns the multiplicative encoding of prime data by finitely supported prime registers. After passing to logarithmic coordinates, the Joukowsky ellipse family becomes a concrete model for rapidity addition in \(\mathrm{SO}^+(1,1)\), with the norm of a register playing the role of a Lorentz scale parameter. This produces an exact bridge between Gödel-type multiplicative coding, condition numbers, Einstein addition, and confocal ellipse geometry \citep{Ungar2008}. The resulting formalism also yields primorial minimization principles: among decomposable codebooks with pairwise coprime images, the smallest total cost is attained by the first \(n\) primes, and the same extremizer minimizes the associated ellipsoid-volume and ellipse-area products.

The third theme is potential-theoretic. For a polynomial \(P\) with nonzero constant term, the two-scale Joukowsky transform \(J_r(z)=rz+r^{-1}z^{-1}\) generates a transport polynomial \(Q_r\) whose pullback along the circle factors exactly as a product of \(P\) and its reciprocal polynomial. This identity leads to Jensen-type formulas on the ellipse, a closed Bessel representation for the exponential moment generating function of the pushed-forward Haar measure, and a Lee--Yang free-energy law when the zeros of \(P\) lie on the unit circle \citep{LeeYang1952I,LeeYang1952II,Mahler1962,Smyth1981,Boyd1981,EverestWard1999}. At the level of elimination theory, the same factorization yields explicit discriminant formulas and a one-scale holographic reconstruction procedure \citep{GKZ1994}.

The paper is organized as follows. Section \ref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation and records the pullback/discriminant factorizations that drive the later arguments. Section \ref{sec:elliptic-gates} develops the Fourier and \(p\)-adic rigidity theory for elliptic projection gates. Section \ref{sec:godel-lorentz} constructs the Gödel--Lorentz dictionary and its extremal consequences. Section \ref{sec:mahler-free-energy} establishes the Mahler/free-energy identities and the associated ellipse dynamics. Section \ref{sec:discriminant} derives discriminant and resultant corollaries, and Section \ref{sec:discussion} closes with consequences for capacity theory, \(p\)-adic asymptotics, and several open problems.

\section{Preliminaries}\label{sec:preliminaries}
We write \(\mathbb P\) for the set of prime numbers. For \(x\in\QQ^\times\) and \(p\in\mathbb P\), \(\nu_p(x)\) denotes the normalized \(p\)-adic valuation, characterized by \(\nu_p(p)=1\) and additivity under multiplication; we also set \(\nu_p(0)=+\infty\). The prime-register monoid is
\[
\mathsf P:=\bigl\{\,r:\mathbb P\to\mathbb Z_{\ge 0}\;:\;r(p)=0\text{ for all but finitely many }p\,\bigr\},
\]
endowed with pointwise addition. Its associated norm is
\[
N(r):=\prod_{p\in\mathbb P} p^{r(p)}\in\NN_{>0}.
\]
We further write \(p_n\#:=\prod_{i=1}^{n}p_i\) for the \(n\)th primorial, \(I:=[0,1]\), \(B_d\subset\RR^d\) for the Euclidean unit ball, and \(\NN_{\mathrm{sf},>1}\) for the square-free integers greater than \(1\).

For \(c\in(0,1)\cap\QQ\) and \(m\in\NN\), the elliptic projection gate is
\[
J_{c,m}(e^{i\theta})=e^{im\theta}+c\,e^{-im\theta}\qquad (\theta\in\RR/2\pi\ZZ),
\]
and its image ellipse is
\[
E_c=\bigl\{(1+c)\cos t+i(1-c)\sin t:\ t\in\RR/2\pi\ZZ\bigr\}.
\]
For \(r>0\), we also use the two-scale Joukowsky map
\[
J_r(z):=rz+r^{-1}z^{-1},
\qquad
E_r:=J_r(\TT),
\qquad
\TT:=\{e^{i\theta}:\theta\in\RR/2\pi\ZZ\}.
\]
If \(f\in L^1(\TT)\), its Fourier coefficients are
\[
\widehat f(k):=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{0}^{2\pi} f(\theta)e^{-ik\theta}\,\dd\theta
\qquad (k\in\ZZ).
\]

Let \(K\) be a field and let
\[
P(u)=a_n\prod_{j=1}^{n}(u-z_j)\in K[u],
\qquad
a_0=P(0)\neq 0,
\]
with roots taken in a fixed algebraic closure of \(K\). We use the reciprocal polynomial
\[
P^{\vee}(u):=u^nP(1/u),
\]
the zero set notation \(\mathrm{Zero}(F)\) for a polynomial \(F\), and the standard resultant/discriminant notation \(\operatorname{Res}\) and \(\operatorname{Disc}\) \citep{GKZ1994}. For \(\rho>0\), we define the logarithmic circular mean
\[
\mathcal M_\rho(P):=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{0}^{2\pi}\log|P(\rho e^{i\theta})|\,\dd\theta,
\]
so that \(\mathcal M_1(P)\) is the logarithmic Mahler measure of \(P\) \citep{Mahler1962,EverestWard1999}.

The next two identities are the algebraic input for the Mahler, discriminant, and holographic statements in Sections \ref{sec:mahler-free-energy} and \ref{sec:discriminant}.

\begin{theorem}[Pullback factorization]\label{thm:group-jg-pullback-factorization}
Let \(P(u)=a_n\prod_{j=1}^{n}(u-z_j)\in K[u]\) with \(a_0\neq 0\), and let \(r\) be an indeterminate or an element of \(K^\times\). Define
\[
Q_r(w):=a_0\prod_{j=1}^{n}\Bigl(w-r z_j-r^{-1}z_j^{-1}\Bigr).
\]
Then \(Q_r(w)\in K(r)[w]\), and for every \(z\in\CC^\times\) one has
\[
\boxed{\;
r^n z^n\,Q_r\!\bigl(J_r(z)\bigr)=P(z)\,P^\vee(r^2 z).
\;}
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The coefficients of \(Q_r\) are elementary symmetric polynomials in the Galois-stable family \(\{r z_j+r^{-1}z_j^{-1}\}_{j=1}^{n}\), hence lie in \(K(r)\). For each \(j\),
\[
J_r(z)-\bigl(r z_j+r^{-1}z_j^{-1}\bigr)
=r(z-z_j)+r^{-1}(z^{-1}-z_j^{-1})
=\frac{(z-z_j)(r^2 z z_j-1)}{r z z_j}.
\]
Multiplying over \(j\) yields
\[
Q_r\!\bigl(J_r(z)\bigr)
=a_0\prod_{j=1}^{n}\frac{(z-z_j)(r^2 z z_j-1)}{r z z_j}
=r^{-n}z^{-n}\Bigl(a_0\prod_{j=1}^{n}z_j^{-1}\Bigr)\prod_{j=1}^{n}(z-z_j)\prod_{j=1}^{n}(r^2 z z_j-1).
\]
Since \(a_0=(-1)^n a_n\prod_{j=1}^{n} z_j\), we have \(a_0\prod_j z_j^{-1}=(-1)^n a_n\). Therefore
\[
Q_r\!\bigl(J_r(z)\bigr)
=r^{-n}z^{-n}\,a_n\prod_{j=1}^{n}(z-z_j)\cdot (-1)^n\prod_{j=1}^{n}(r^2 z z_j-1).
\]
The first factor is \(P(z)\), while
\[
P^\vee(r^2 z)=a_n\prod_{j=1}^{n}(1-r^2 z z_j)=a_n(-1)^n\prod_{j=1}^{n}(r^2 z z_j-1).
\]
Combining the two identities gives the stated factorization.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Discriminant factorization]\label{thm:group-jg-discriminant-factorization}
In the setting of Theorem \ref{thm:group-jg-pullback-factorization},
\[
\boxed{\;
\operatorname{Disc}_{w}(Q_r)
=\operatorname{Disc}_{z}(P)\,
r^{-n(n-1)}
\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(r^2 z_i z_j-1)^2.
\;}
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Write \(w_j:=r z_j+r^{-1}z_j^{-1}\). Then
\[
\operatorname{Disc}_{w}(Q_r)=a_0^{2n-2}\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(w_i-w_j)^2.
\]
Using
\[
w_i-w_j
=r(z_i-z_j)+r^{-1}(z_i^{-1}-z_j^{-1})
=\frac{(z_i-z_j)(r^2 z_i z_j-1)}{r z_i z_j},
\]
we obtain
\[
\operatorname{Disc}_{w}(Q_r)
=a_0^{2n-2}r^{-n(n-1)}
\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(z_i-z_j)^2
\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(r^2 z_i z_j-1)^2
\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(z_i z_j)^{-2}.
\]
Now
\[
\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(z_i z_j)^{-2}
=\Bigl(\prod_{i=1}^{n} z_i\Bigr)^{-2(n-1)},
\]
and \(a_0=(-1)^n a_n\prod_i z_i\) implies
\[
a_0^{2n-2}\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(z_i z_j)^{-2}=a_n^{2n-2}.
\]
Since
\[
\operatorname{Disc}_{z}(P)=a_n^{2n-2}\prod_{1\le i<j\le n}(z_i-z_j)^2,
\]
the desired identity follows.
\end{proof}

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}
Several themes emerging from the preceding sections deserve emphasis. First, the Joukowsky ellipse is not merely a convenient parametrized curve: it is a conformal boundary object whose logarithmic capacity, annular modulus, and Mahler averages are all controlled by the same scale parameter. In this sense, the transport identity of Theorem \ref{thm:group-jg-pullback-factorization} should be viewed as a potential-theoretic bridge between circle data and ellipse data, parallel to the classical role of conformal maps in logarithmic potential theory \citep{Tsuji1959,Ransford1995,Pommerenke1992}. The discriminant and resultant formulas show that the same bridge also preserves delicate elimination-theoretic information \citep{GKZ1994}.

Second, the \(p\)-adic rigidity statements suggest a broader arithmetic theory. For rational \(c\), the slopes of \(\nu_p(\widehat u_{c,m}(2mn))\) recover the full local data of \(c\), while Theorem \ref{thm:group-jg-elliptic-gate-padics-blindspot} shows that any finite congruence audit leaves a systematic uncertainty window. It would be natural to extend this analysis from rational parameters to algebraic numbers, to study whether the resulting local slope packages satisfy compatibility relations across extensions, and to compare the finite-audit blindspot with asymptotic phenomena from Iwasawa-theoretic growth or \(p\)-adic dynamical interpolation \citep{Koblitz1984,Serre1973}.

Third, the Gödel--Lorentz dictionary gives a geometric interpretation of multiplicative coding: additive rapidities in \(\mathrm{SO}^+(1,1)\) correspond to multiplicative norms, while the normalized ellipse parameter obeys Einstein addition. This perspective may be useful beyond the present setting, for example in higher-rank analogues where one replaces \(\mathrm{SO}^+(1,1)\) by more general diagonal or Cartan subgroups and asks for analogous capacity/volume formulas. A parallel question is whether the primorial extremizers obtained here remain optimal under weaker combinatorial constraints than pairwise coprimality.

Finally, the Lee--Yang and holographic results leave several concrete open problems. One would like to extend the exact Mahler/free-energy identity to multivariable polynomials and higher-genus or higher-dimensional transport geometries; to strengthen the single-scale reconstruction theorem to noisy or partially observed data; and to mechanize the full resultant/discriminant infrastructure underlying the present arguments. These problems lie at the interface of complex analysis, arithmetic geometry, and formalized algebra, and they seem well suited for further development.
