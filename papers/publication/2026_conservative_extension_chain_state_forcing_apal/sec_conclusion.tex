\section{Concluding Remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper separates three questions that are often run together in local-to-global semantics. The first is logical: whether compatible local sectionability can already be detected in the lower pointwise fragment. The answer given by \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} is negative in the automorphism-invariant constant-free one-variable setting considered here. The second is geometric: how visible value classes and gluing failure should be organized once a gluing-sensitive realization lift has been fixed. At that level the relevant objects are the component gerbes attached to visible branches. The third is homological: which part of a finite abelian obstruction class is intrinsically visible without enlarging the state space. That part is measured by the image of the homological evaluation map, and the remaining character-blind component is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime. Under the additional hypothesis $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$, the converse realization theorem \Cref{thm:prescribed-branch-realization} shows that the branchwise hidden subgroups are not merely computable invariants of existing models: every homologically admissible finite family occurs on a fixed connected finite nerve.

This gives a more finely graded picture than the bare no-global-section criterion. In the unique-branch case the comparison with the support-presheaf approach to contextuality is direct, while in the multibranch case the gap between branch-sensitive and branch-uniform visibility is controlled by an explicit short exact sequence and is now accompanied by a realization theorem for the full range of homologically admissible aggregate quotients. The GHZ/Mermin discussion has a correspondingly limited role: it shows where additional amplitude data enter, but it does not provide a canonical computation of the intrinsic hidden subgroup.

Conceptually, the framework remains close to forcing and sheaf semantics in the sense of \cite{Tierney1972,Johnstone2002,MacLaneMoerdijk1994}. What is specific to the present setting is the explicit stratification into semantic layers and the extraction of a visibility quotient that is invariant under the appropriate homological changes of presentation.
