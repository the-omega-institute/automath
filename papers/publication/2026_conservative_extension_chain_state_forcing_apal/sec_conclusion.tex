\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper separates three stages of the local-to-global problem. At the level of information states, \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} shows that compatible local sectionability and gluing-level nullity are invisible in the automorphism-invariant, constant-free, one-variable fragment considered here. This is not intended as a general expressivity theorem, but it does isolate the first place at which the local-object layer goes beyond the lower forcing language.

At the passage from set-valued to groupoid-valued data, \Cref{thm:canonical-lift-rigidity} shows that a representative-rigid lift can be globally conservative only when the sheafification unit at the terminal fibre is surjective; under $H^1$-vanishing and separatedness, this is equivalent to sheafiness of the local object functor at that fibre. Thus the gerbe-theoretic part of the theory is necessarily relative to additional lift data. \Cref{thm:intrinsic-branch-obstruction-extension} identifies the maximal canonicity available without an existence theorem: once the sheafified local object functor has been lifted to a banded realization stack, the branch obstruction class is intrinsic up to equivalence of such extensions. Visible value classes are then governed by the sheaf of connected components and gluing failure is carried by the component gerbes of the visible branches.

For a finite abelian branch obstruction class, the intrinsic subgroup $K_\omega=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$ and quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega$ capture the homological information that survives the semantics. The branchwise comparison sequence and the realization theorems show how these quotients behave when several visible branches are present, and the bouquet family supplies a natural class of examples in which the resulting two-branch resolution kernels can be described completely. The appendix contains only supplementary material: the refinement-dynamic extension, finite-state complexity bounds, and the GHZ/Mermin lift used as an illustrative chosen datum.
