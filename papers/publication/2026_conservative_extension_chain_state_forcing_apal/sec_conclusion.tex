\section{Concluding Remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper separates three levels of the local-to-global problem. At the logical level, \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} isolates a genuine gap between pointwise semantics and compatible local sectionability. At the geometric level, visible value classes are organized by the sheaf of connected components, and gluing failure is carried by the component gerbes attached to visible branches once a gluing-sensitive realization lift has been fixed. At the homological level, a finite abelian obstruction class yields the subgroup $K_\omega=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$ and the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega$, thereby separating the $H_2$-visible part of the obstruction from the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ residual.

This sharpens the usual no-global-section picture in two directions. In the unique-branch case, the comparison with the support-presheaf approach to contextuality shows that a nontrivial obstruction is invisible to every admissible character precisely when the surviving class lies in the $\operatorname{Ext}$-summand of the universal coefficient sequence. In the multibranch case, the branch-sensitive and branch-uniform quotients are related by an explicit short exact sequence measuring the loss of information when the branch label is forgotten. Under the additional hypothesis $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$, the mixed realization theorem shows that these hidden subgroups and aggregate quotients are not merely derived invariants of previously chosen models: on a fixed connected finite nerve, every homologically admissible finite family occurs, and the neutral branches are exactly the zero homomorphisms. When $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, the realizable hidden subgroups are precisely the subgroups of the band generated by at most $\beta$ elements, so maximal collapse is possible exactly at the ranks permitted by the second homology.

The appendices record two supplementary directions that are not used in the core proofs. One is a refinement-dynamic extension of the semantics together with the associated finite-state complexity bounds. The other is a GHZ/Mermin amplitude lift showing where additional realization data enter the gerbe layer.
