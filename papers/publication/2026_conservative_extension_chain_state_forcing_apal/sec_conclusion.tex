\section{Concluding Remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The results isolate three layers of the local-to-global problem in this semantics. At the lower level, \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} gives a restricted separation result: compatible local sectionability and gluing-level nullity are not visible in the automorphism-invariant constant-free one-variable fragment of information-state forcing considered here. This is a deliberate lower-bound statement for the chosen comparison fragment, not a general expressivity theorem.

At the interface between the set-valued and groupoid-valued layers, \Cref{thm:canonical-lift-rigidity} is an obstruction theorem. A representative-rigid lift can be globally conservative only when the sheafification unit at the terminal fibre is already surjective, and under $H^1$-vanishing plus separatedness this is equivalent to the local object functor already being a sheaf there. The paper does not prove that gluing-sensitive lifts always exist; rather, it identifies what follows once such a datum has been fixed. Under that explicit hypothesis, visible value classes are governed by the sheaf of connected components and gluing failure is carried by the component gerbes attached to the visible branches.

At the homological level, a finite abelian branch obstruction class yields the subgroup $K_\omega=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$ and the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega$. Once the branch class, finite nerve, and band are fixed, the surrounding homological algebra is classical; the point of the paper is to extract the canonical image and quotient that survive the semantics, to control their invariance under the allowed changes of presentation, and to compare their branchwise aggregates. In the multibranch setting, the branch-sensitive and branch-uniform quotients are related by a short exact sequence, and under the additional hypothesis $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$ the realization theorems show that prescribed branchwise homomorphisms can be produced within the fixed finite-nerve framework. When $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, the realizable hidden subgroups are exactly the subgroups of the band generated by at most $\beta$ elements.

The bouquet theorem singles out a natural family in which the acyclic-cover hypothesis is automatic. For the canonical good-cover family of $\bigvee_{j=1}^{\beta}S^2$, the pure two-branch resolution kernels are exactly the finite abelian groups $G$ with $d(G)\le 2\beta$ that are not cyclic $p$-groups. The appendix records the refinement-dynamic extension, its finite-state complexity bounds, and the GHZ/Mermin lift referred to in the introduction.
