\section{Concluding Remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The results isolate three layers of the local-to-global problem in this semantics. At the lower level, \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} shows that compatible local sectionability and gluing-level nullity are not visible in the one-variable constant-free fragment of information-state forcing considered here. After a gluing-sensitive realization prestack has been fixed, visible value classes are governed by the sheaf of connected components, and gluing failure is carried by the component gerbes attached to the visible branches.

At the homological level, a finite abelian branch obstruction class yields the subgroup $K_\omega=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$ and the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega$. This separates the $H_2$-visible part of the obstruction from the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ contribution. In the multibranch setting, the branch-sensitive and branch-uniform quotients are related by a short exact sequence, and under the additional hypothesis $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$ the realization theorems show that prescribed branchwise homomorphisms can be produced within the fixed finite-nerve framework. When $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, the realizable hidden subgroups are exactly the subgroups of the band generated by at most $\beta$ elements.

The bouquet theorem singles out a natural family in which the acyclic-cover hypothesis is automatic. For the canonical good-cover family of $\bigvee_{j=1}^{\beta}S^2$, the pure two-branch resolution kernels are exactly the finite abelian groups $G$ with $d(G)\le 2\beta$ that are not cyclic $p$-groups. The appendix records the refinement-dynamic extension, its finite-state complexity bounds, and the GHZ/Mermin lift referred to in the introduction.
