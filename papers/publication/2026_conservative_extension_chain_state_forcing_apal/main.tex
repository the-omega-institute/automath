\begin{abstract}
Pointwise semantics in the automorphism-invariant constant-free lower reduct does not perceive the local-to-global gap between compatible families and global sections. We study that gap inside a four-layer chain that passes from pointwise states to information-state forcing, local objects, and branched obstruction data. The first unconditional result is a restricted lower-fragment undefinability theorem: compatible local sectionability is not definable by automorphism-invariant $\Sigma_1$-formulas with one free reference variable in the $\mathbb L_1$-reduct. The second, and main, result isolates the homological evaluation map and the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$; this quotient is invariant under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and band re-trivialization, and the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime is exactly the character-blind case. The gerbe layer is presented only as a conditional semantics relative to a chosen gluing-sensitive realization lift. For Abramsky--Brandenburger support presheaves, we make explicit that an amplitude lift is extra data not carried by the support presheaf itself; the GHZ/Mermin model is included only as an illustration at that conditional level, not as a canonical computation of $K_\omega$. The sheafification, gerbe, and universal-coefficient tools themselves are classical; the new content lies in the precise lower-fragment separation, the homology-equivalent visibility invariant, and the clean isolation of the blind pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ regime.
\end{abstract}
