\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure in a forcing semantics whose states are sets of realizations. For an admitted reference, compatible local sectionability is equivalent to nonemptiness of the sheafification of the associated local-object functor. We also prove a lower-fragment separation theorem: in the automorphism-invariant, constant-free, one-variable fragment of the lower reduct, the predicates for compatible local sections, global sections, and gluing-level nullity are undefinable. At the groupoid-valued level, a representative-rigid realization prestack that is globally conservative at the terminal fibre forces surjectivity of the sheafification unit there; under separatedness and $H^1$-vanishing, this is equivalent to the local object functor already being a sheaf at that fibre, so gluing-level nullity cannot be recovered from a strict functorial choice of local representatives. For a banded realization-stack extension of the sheafified local object functor, each visible branch determines a canonical Giraud class in $H^2$, recovering the derived branch obstruction class when gluing-sensitive data are chosen. For finite abelian bands, this obstruction yields a homological evaluation map whose image controls a visible-quotient invariant, and the character-blind case is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ contribution. If $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, then the realizable hidden subgroups are exactly the subgroups generated by at most $\beta$ elements.
\end{abstract}
