\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure in a state-forcing semantics whose states are sets of realizations. Compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification. In the automorphism-invariant constant-free one-variable fragment of the lower reduct, the predicates expressing compatible local sections, global sections, and gluing-level nullity are undefinable; no broader lower-fragment inexpressibility claim is made. Conditional on a chosen gluing-sensitive realization datum, visible value classes identify with sections of the sheaf of connected components, and each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe. For a fixed finite nerve presentation with finite abelian band, the resulting obstruction class $\omega$ defines a homological evaluation map on $H_2$; its image $K_{\omega}$ is the hidden subgroup and the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}$ is the corresponding visible quotient. These groups are invariant under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and re-trivialization of the band, and the character-blind case is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime. When $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$, the paper gives converse constructions inside the same fixed finite-nerve framework: every finite family of homomorphisms $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$ is realizable, with the neutral branches exactly the zero homomorphisms. If moreover $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, then the realizable hidden subgroups are exactly the subgroups of $A$ generated by at most $\beta$ elements. For the canonical good-cover family of a bouquet $\bigvee_{j=1}^{\beta}S^2$, the pure two-branch resolution kernels are exactly the finite abelian groups $G$ with $d(G)\le 2\beta$ that are not cyclic $p$-groups. The contribution is therefore organizational rather than a new gerbe- or homology-theoretic existence theorem: it isolates what follows from classical sheaf, gerbe, and universal-coefficient tools once the state-forcing and comparison data are fixed explicitly.
\end{abstract}
