\begin{abstract}
Pointwise semantics in the automorphism-invariant constant-free lower reduct does not perceive the local-to-global gap between compatible families and global sections. We study that gap inside a four-layer chain that passes from pointwise states to information-state forcing, local objects, and branched obstruction data. Three repairs drive the new presentation. First, we cleanly fix the comparison class and show that compatible local sectionability is not definable by automorphism-invariant $\Sigma_1$-formulas with one free reference variable in the $\mathbb L_1$-reduct. Second, we isolate the part of the obstruction determined by the homological evaluation map and prove that $K_\omega$ and $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega$ are independent of the chosen cover, trivialization, or cocycle whenever the comparisons are homology equivalences. Third, we spell out the gluing-sensitive realization data for the Abramsky--Brandenburger support presheaves of the GHZ/Mermin model, verify global conservativity, and compute the obstruction on the flag nerve that models the measurement overlaps. The gerbe arguments remain conditional on the stated hypotheses, but the refined invariant and the explicit GHZ verification turn the conditional statements into concrete consequences. The sheafification, gerbe, and universal-coefficient tools themselves are classical; the contribution lies in the precise logical separation, the intrinsic visibility quotient, and the verified semantic packaging for a standard empirical model.
\end{abstract}
