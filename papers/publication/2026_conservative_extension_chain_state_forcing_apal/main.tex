\begin{abstract}
Pointwise semantics cannot express the local-to-global gap that drives gluing failure in sheaf theory. We introduce a four-layer conservative extension chain, from pointwise semantics through information-state forcing to local objects and refinement dynamics. The central question is: given an admitted reference carrying a finite abelian gluing obstruction, what part of that obstruction is semantically visible without enlarging the state space? Three results answer this question under the explicit hypotheses stated in the text. \emph{Theorem~A} (forcing necessity at the pointwise boundary): compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification, and the gluing predicates are not definable by automorphism-invariant pointwise formulas of the lower-layer reduct, so the local-object enrichment is logically necessary in that precise sense. \emph{Theorem~B} (branched gerbe semantics): under gluing-sensitive realization and global-conservativity hypotheses, each visible value class determines a banded component gerbe, and gluing-level absence is equivalent to the existence of visible branches together with non-neutrality of every component gerbe. \emph{Theorem~C} (homological visibility): under finite-nerve realizability, the image of the homological evaluation map is the intrinsic hidden subgroup and the quotient by this image is the intrinsic visible quotient; when the homological image vanishes, any remaining nonzero obstruction is a pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-type blind class. Under the corresponding unique-branch support-presheaf assumptions, this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and identifies the precise mechanism behind cohomological blind cases.
\end{abstract}
