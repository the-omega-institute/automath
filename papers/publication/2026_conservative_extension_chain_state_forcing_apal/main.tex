\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure in a forcing semantics whose states are sets of realizations. We show that compatible local sectionability is equivalent to nonemptiness of the associated sheafification, and we prove that, in the automorphism-invariant constant-free one-variable fragment of the lower reduct, the predicates for compatible local sections, global sections, and gluing-level nullity are undefinable. A rigidity theorem is also proved for the higher lift: any representative-rigid realization prestack that is globally conservative at the terminal fibre forces surjectivity of the sheafification unit there; under $H^1$-vanishing and separatedness, this is equivalent to the local object functor already being a sheaf at the terminal fibre. Hence genuine gluing-level nullity cannot be produced by a canonical objectwise lift. After fixing a gluing-sensitive realization prestack, visible value classes are identified with global sections of the sheaf of connected components, and each visible branch gives rise to a banded component gerbe. For a fixed finite nerve presentation with finite abelian band $A$, the branch obstruction class $\omega$ induces a homological evaluation map $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$; its image $K_\omega$ and quotient $A^\omega_{\mathrm{vis}}=A/K_\omega$ are invariant under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and re-trivialization of the band, and the character-blind case is precisely the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ contribution. Assuming $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$, we also obtain realization theorems for prescribed finite families of homomorphisms $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$, with neutral branches corresponding exactly to the zero maps. If, moreover, $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, then the realizable hidden subgroups are exactly the subgroups of $A$ generated by at most $\beta$ elements. For the canonical good-cover family of a bouquet $\bigvee_{j=1}^{\beta}S^2$, the pure two-branch resolution kernels are exactly the finite abelian groups $G$ with $d(G)\le 2\beta$ that are not cyclic $p$-groups.
\end{abstract}
