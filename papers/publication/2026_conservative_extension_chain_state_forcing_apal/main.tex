\begin{abstract}
We study the local-to-global gap between compatible local families and global sections in a stratified state-forcing semantics. At the lower end of the chain we prove that compatible local sectionability is not definable, within the automorphism-invariant constant-free one-variable $\Sigma_1$-fragment of the $\mathbb L_1$-reduct, by pointwise formulas alone. At the local-object level, visible value classes are identified with sections of the sheafification, and, under a chosen gluing-sensitive realization lift, each visible branch carries a banded component gerbe. For a finite abelian band on a finite nerve presentation we define the homological evaluation map $\mathrm{ev}_\omega:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$, the intrinsic hidden subgroup $K_\omega=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)$, and the intrinsic visible quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega$. These are invariant under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and band re-trivialization, and the character-blind case is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime. The GHZ/Mermin example is used only to exhibit the additional amplitude data required by the conditional gerbe layer.
\end{abstract}
