\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Sheaf-theoretic semantics gives a natural language for the passage from local data to global objects. In the settings that arise in logic, and in particular in the sheaf-theoretic study of contextuality \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyMansfieldSoaresBarbosa2012,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,Caru2017,Caru2018,Montanhano2021}, the basic distinction is between the existence of compatible local families and the existence of a global section. The failure of the latter leads, in a classical way, to obstruction classes, gerbes, and cohomological invariants \cite{Giraud1971,Breen2006,Hatcher2002}. The aim of the present paper is to examine this local-to-global gap in a state-based forcing semantics and to determine which parts of it are already visible at the lower forcing level.

The semantics we consider is formulated on sets of realizations rather than on single assignments. It is therefore related, at least in spirit, to semantics of imperfect information and to team semantics \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007,AbramskyPuljujarviVaananen2025}. At the same time, the organization of the paper is closer to the forcing and sheaf-theoretic tradition initiated by Beth, Kripke, Fitting, and Tierney, and later developed in topos-theoretic form \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,Fitting1969,Tierney1972,Goldblatt2006,Johnstone2002}. A basic feature of our treatment is that the successive enrichments of the semantics are arranged in an explicit extension chain, so that one can see precisely where the geometric and homological data begin to matter.

More concretely, we work with the chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0 \xhookrightarrow{\iota} \mathbb{L}_1 \preceq \mathbb{L}_2 \preceq \mathbb{L}_3 \preceq \mathbb{L}_4,
\]
where $\mathbb{L}_0$ is the pointwise layer, $\mathbb{L}_1$ is the information-state forcing layer, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local objects and site structure, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds branchwise obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ adds refinement dynamics. The map $\mathbb{L}_0\hookrightarrow\mathbb{L}_1$ is a singleton semantic embedding, whereas the later steps are conservative extensions in the sense of \Cref{def:conservative-extension}. Thus the geometric and homological material introduced later does not alter the forcing behaviour of formulas already present in the lower layers.

The first group of results concerns the passage from pointwise semantics to local objects. In \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization} we identify compatible local sectionability with the existence of a section of the sheafification. We then prove in \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} that the predicates $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}_s$ are not definable by automorphism-invariant constant-free formulas of the $\mathbb{L}_1$-reduct with one free reference variable. This is the lower-fragment separation result that motivates the move from the forcing layer to the geometric layer.

At the next stage we fix a gluing-sensitive realization prestack whose stackification recovers the sheafification of isomorphism classes. Under this additional hypothesis, visible value classes are exactly the global sections of the sheaf of connected components (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}), and each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}). The corresponding branch obstruction classes control gluing-level nullity: \Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics} shows that gluing-level absence is equivalent to the existence of visible branches together with non-neutrality of all associated component gerbes. We emphasize that the gerbe-theoretic analysis depends on the choice of gluing-sensitive lift; it is not asserted for an arbitrary local-object functor. The GHZ/Mermin construction in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-ghz} is included only as an example of such additional realization data.

The homological part of the paper begins with a finite nerve presentation of a branch obstruction class. For a finite abelian band, the universal coefficient sequence yields a homological evaluation map $\mathrm{ev}_\omega:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$. Its image $K_\omega$ is the hidden subgroup, and the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega$ is the visible quotient attached to the class. We show that these groups are invariant under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and re-trivialization of the band (\Cref{prop:invariance-visible-quotients}), and that the admissible characters are exactly the characters annihilating $K_\omega$ (\Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient}). The character-blind case is treated in \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}, where it is identified with the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime.

When more than one visible branch is present, two aggregate quotients arise naturally, according as the branch label is retained or forgotten. Their relation is described by the short exact sequence of \Cref{thm:branch-comparison-exact-sequence}. Assuming in addition that $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$, we prove realization theorems for prescribed branchwise data: \Cref{thm:prescribed-branch-realization} treats non-neutral branches, and \Cref{thm:mixed-branch-realization} extends the construction to arbitrary finite families of homomorphisms $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$, with the neutral branches characterized by the zero homomorphisms. If $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, \Cref{cor:torsion-free-h2-classification} yields the corresponding subgroup classification. The natural-family theorem \Cref{thm:bouquet-two-branch-resolution} then replaces the abstract finite nerve by the canonical good-cover family of a bouquet $\bigvee_{j=1}^{\beta}S^2$: in that setting the acyclic-cover hypothesis is automatic, and the pure two-branch resolution kernels are exactly the finite abelian groups $G$ with $d(G)\le 2\beta$ that are not cyclic $p$-groups. In the unique-branch case this gives a comparison with the usual no-global-section criterion in the support-presheaf approach to contextuality; see \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}.

The background from sheafification, stackification, gerbes, and the universal coefficient sequence is classical. What is specific to the present paper is the way these ingredients are combined with the forcing semantics: the extension chain makes the passage between semantic layers explicit, the lower-fragment undefinability result justifies the local-object predicates, the homological evaluation map extracts a canonical quotient from the obstruction class within a controlled comparison class of presentations, and the bouquet good-cover theorem isolates a natural semantic family in which multibranch resolution is classified rather than freely engineered.

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries} sets up the semantic framework and the relevant notions of conservativity. \Cref{sec:information-states} develops forcing over information states. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} treats local objects, sheafification, and the lower-fragment undefinability theorem. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} introduces the branch gerbes and their obstruction classes. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} and \Cref{sec:branch-aggregation} contain the intrinsic visibility theory and the branchwise comparison and realization results, including the natural bouquet-good-cover theorem. \Cref{sec:conclusion} records the final scope and consequences of the strengthened results. The appendix collects the refinement-dynamic material and the GHZ/Mermin example.
