\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Sheaf-theoretic semantics offers a natural language for the passage from local data to global objects. In logical applications, and in particular in sheaf-theoretic analyses of contextuality \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyMansfieldSoaresBarbosa2012,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,Caru2017,Caru2018,Montanhano2021}, the central question is whether compatible local families glue to global sections. The classical tools for measuring the failure of gluing are gerbes, obstruction classes, and cohomological invariants \cite{Giraud1971,Breen2006,Hatcher2002}. In this paper we study that question in a forcing semantics whose states are sets of realizations, with the aim of separating the successive layers at which genuinely new local-to-global structure appears.

Our starting point is the extension chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0 \xhookrightarrow{\iota} \mathbb{L}_1 \preceq \mathbb{L}_2 \preceq \mathbb{L}_3 \preceq \mathbb{L}_4,
\]
where $\mathbb{L}_0$ is the pointwise layer, $\mathbb{L}_1$ is the information-state forcing layer, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local objects and site structure, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds branchwise obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ adds refinement dynamics. The map $\mathbb{L}_0\hookrightarrow\mathbb{L}_1$ is a singleton semantic embedding, whereas the later steps are conservative extensions in the sense of \Cref{def:conservative-extension}. Thus the geometric and homological material introduced later does not alter the forcing behaviour of formulas already present in the lower layers.
The semantics is thus related both to team and imperfect-information semantics \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007,AbramskyPuljujarviVaananen2025} and to the forcing and sheaf-theoretic tradition stemming from Beth, Kripke, Fitting, and Tierney and later developed in topos-theoretic form \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,Fitting1969,Tierney1972,Goldblatt2006,Johnstone2002}.

The first part of the paper concerns the passage from information states to local objects. \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization} shows that compatible local sectionability is equivalent to nonemptiness of the associated sheafification. We then prove in \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} that the predicates $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}_s$ are not definable by automorphism-invariant, constant-free formulas of the $\mathbb{L}_1$-reduct with one free reference variable. This is only a lower-bound statement for the comparison fragment under consideration, but it isolates a genuine change of behaviour between $\mathbb L_1$ and $\mathbb L_2$.

The second part begins once the set-valued local-object functor is enriched by groupoid-valued data. \Cref{thm:canonical-lift-rigidity} gives a rigidity statement for representative-rigid lifts: if a realization prestack admits a strict functorial choice of representatives and is globally conservative at the terminal fibre, then the sheafification unit is surjective there; under $H^1$-vanishing and separatedness, this is equivalent to the local object functor already being a sheaf at the terminal fibre. Thus gluing-level nullity is necessarily tied to genuinely gluing-sensitive lift data. \Cref{thm:intrinsic-branch-obstruction-extension} identifies the canonicity that survives at this level: once the sheafified local object functor has been lifted to a banded realization stack, the branch obstruction map to derived $H^2$ is intrinsic up to equivalence of banded extensions. For such an extension, visible value classes are the global sections of the sheaf of connected components (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}), each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}), and \Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics} identifies gluing-level absence with the existence of visible branches together with non-neutrality of the corresponding component gerbes. No general existence theorem for gluing-sensitive lifts is proved; Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-ghz} gives an explicit GHZ/Mermin example of chosen lift data.

The homological part of the paper starts only after one visible branch, one finite nerve presentation, and one constant finite abelian band have been fixed. From that point the tools are classical: the universal coefficient sequence, finite-abelian duality, and standard quotient arguments. What matters for the semantics is the canonical image
\[
K_\omega:=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)
\]
and quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega$ attached to the branch obstruction class. \Cref{prop:invariance-visible-quotients} establishes invariance under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and re-trivialization of the band, \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient} gives the corresponding character-theoretic description, and \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions} identifies the character-blind case with the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ contribution.

When several visible branches are present, one obtains branch-sensitive and branch-uniform quotients, related by the short exact sequence of \Cref{thm:branch-comparison-exact-sequence}. Under the additional assumption $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\allowbreak=0$, we prove realization theorems for prescribed branchwise homomorphisms $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\allowbreak\to A$. \Cref{thm:prescribed-branch-realization} treats non-neutral branches, while \Cref{thm:mixed-branch-realization} allows arbitrary finite families and identifies the neutral branches with the zero maps.

If $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, \Cref{cor:torsion-free-h2-classification} shows that the realizable hidden subgroups are exactly the subgroups of $A$ generated by at most $\beta$ elements. The bouquet theorem \Cref{thm:bouquet-two-branch-resolution} then supplies a natural family of examples, namely the canonical good covers of $\bigvee_{j=1}^{\beta}S^2$, for which the acyclic-cover hypothesis is automatic and the pure two-branch resolution kernels can be classified explicitly. In the unique-branch case this yields a direct comparison with the usual no-global-section picture from sheaf-theoretic contextuality; see \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}.

The sheaf-theoretic, gerbe-theoretic, and homological tools used here are classical, and several of the later comparison results are formal once the branchwise intrinsic data have been fixed. The contribution of the paper is accordingly structural: the extension chain separates the semantic layers, the dependence on extra realization data is made explicit, and the obstruction class gives rise to canonical quotients inside a controlled finite-nerve comparison class.

\subsection*{Theorem-by-theorem comparison with prior work}

The preliminary and information-state statements use standard material from forcing, team semantics, and conservative-extension bookkeeping \cite{Kripke1965,Fitting1969,Hodges1997,Vaananen2007}. The formal composition and monotonicity statements have no increment beyond those classical inputs; their role is to make the later layer projections explicit. The first paper-specific increment is \Cref{prop:chain-instantiation}: it fixes the five-layer chain and records that the first step is only a singleton semantic embedding, while the later steps are conservative extensions.

The local-object layer is built from the usual theory of sites, sheafification, separated presheaves, stacks, and stackification \cite{MacLaneMoerdijk1994,Johnstone2002,StacksProject}. The increment of \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization} is the exact identification of compatible local sectionability at an admitted reference with nonemptiness of the sheafified local-object functor at the associated reference class. The increment of \Cref{thm:canonical-lift-rigidity} is the terminal-fibre criterion: representative-rigid globally conservative lifts force surjectivity of the sheafification unit, and under the stated $H^1$-vanishing and separatedness hypotheses this becomes a bijectivity criterion. The increment of \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} is the four-reference separation showing that the local, compatible, global, and gluing-null predicates are invisible in the specified automorphism-invariant lower fragment.

The gerbe-theoretic rows use Giraud's classification of banded gerbes, the standard local-trivialization cocycle construction, and the corresponding stack language \cite{Giraud1971,Breen2006,Murray1996,Brylinski1993,StacksProject}. The increment of \Cref{thm:intrinsic-branch-obstruction-extension} is a canonical map from visible branches to Giraud $H^2$-classes that is invariant under equivalence of banded realization-stack extensions. The increment of \Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics} is the semantic criterion that gluing-level absence is exactly the presence of visible branches whose component gerbes are all non-neutral, with the derived $H^2$ formulation available only under the stated \v{C}ech-to-derived comparison hypothesis.

The homological visibility rows use the universal coefficient theorem, injectivity of divisible groups, and finite-abelian character duality \cite{Hatcher2002,Weibel1994,Terras1999,DummitFoote2004}. The increment of \Cref{prop:invariance-visible-quotients} is invariance of the subgroup $K_\omega$ and quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega$ under the three specified presentation changes. The increment of \Cref{prop:strict-vs-intrinsic-hidden-subgroups} and \Cref{prop:chain-vs-homological-visibility} is the distinction between chain-level presentation visibility and cycle-level class visibility. The increment of \Cref{thm:intrinsic-pure-ext-initiality} and \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions} is the identification of the remaining blind regime with the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ part of the universal coefficient sequence in the paper's finite-nerve semantics.

The branch-aggregation rows are mostly formal finite-abelian-group algebra once the branchwise subgroups $K_v$ have been fixed \cite{Terras1999,DummitFoote2004}. The increment of \Cref{thm:prescribed-branch-realization} is realization of any finite family of nonzero $H_2$-evaluation images as branch hidden subgroups on a connected $H_1=0$ finite nerve. The increment of \Cref{thm:mixed-branch-realization} is the inclusion of zero branches and the resulting equivalence between global sectionability and the existence of a zero branch. The increment of \Cref{cor:torsion-free-h2-classification} is the generator-rank classification for torsion-free $H_2$, while the increment of \Cref{thm:bouquet-two-branch-resolution} is the natural good-cover family whose two-branch kernels are classified by $d(G)\le 2\beta$ together with the non-cyclic-$p$-group condition. The contextuality comparison in \Cref{prop:ab-compatibility-translation,thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison} is conditional on identifying the local-object functor with an Abramsky--Brandenburger support presheaf, and its increment is the unique-branch translation of no-global-section contextuality into the visible-quotient and pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ terminology used here \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyMansfieldSoaresBarbosa2012,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,Caru2017,Caru2018,Montanhano2021}.

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries} fixes the extension chain and the relevant notions of conservativity. \Cref{sec:information-states} recalls forcing over information states. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} studies local objects, sheafification, and lower-fragment undefinability. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} introduces component gerbes and branch obstruction classes. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} and \Cref{sec:branch-aggregation} contain the homological visibility theory together with the branchwise comparison and realization results. The appendix collects the supplementary material on refinement dynamics and the GHZ/Mermin example.
