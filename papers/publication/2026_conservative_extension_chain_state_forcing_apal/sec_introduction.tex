\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Sheaf-theoretic semantics provides a natural setting for the passage from local data to global objects. In logical applications, and especially in the sheaf-theoretic study of contextuality \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyMansfieldSoaresBarbosa2012,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,Caru2017,Caru2018,Montanhano2021}, the basic distinction is between the existence of compatible local families and the existence of a global section. The gap between the two is measured classically by obstruction classes, gerbes, and cohomological invariants \cite{Giraud1971,Breen2006,Hatcher2002}. The purpose of the present paper is to analyse that gap in a forcing semantics whose states are sets of realizations, and to isolate where the geometric and homological data first become relevant.

Our starting point is the extension chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0 \xhookrightarrow{\iota} \mathbb{L}_1 \preceq \mathbb{L}_2 \preceq \mathbb{L}_3 \preceq \mathbb{L}_4,
\]
where $\mathbb{L}_0$ is the pointwise layer, $\mathbb{L}_1$ is the information-state forcing layer, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local objects and site structure, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds branchwise obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ adds refinement dynamics. The map $\mathbb{L}_0\hookrightarrow\mathbb{L}_1$ is a singleton semantic embedding, whereas the later steps are conservative extensions in the sense of \Cref{def:conservative-extension}. Thus the geometric and homological material introduced later does not alter the forcing behaviour of formulas already present in the lower layers.
The semantics is therefore close in spirit both to team semantics and imperfect-information semantics \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007,AbramskyPuljujarviVaananen2025}, and to the forcing and sheaf-theoretic tradition originating with Beth, Kripke, Fitting, and Tierney and later developed in topos-theoretic form \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,Fitting1969,Tierney1972,Goldblatt2006,Johnstone2002}.

The paper has two intentionally limited aims. The first is to identify a precise semantic threshold between the lower forcing layer and the local-object layer. In \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization} we show that compatible local sectionability is equivalent to the existence of a section of the sheafification. We then prove in \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} that the predicates $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}_s$ are not definable by automorphism-invariant constant-free formulas of the $\mathbb{L}_1$-reduct with one free reference variable. This is a lower-bound statement for that specific comparison fragment only: it witnesses a genuine semantic gain from $\mathbb L_1$ to $\mathbb L_2$, but it is not intended as a full expressivity classification of richer lower languages.

The second aim is to isolate what the higher sheaf-theoretic and gerbe-theoretic layer contributes once additional groupoid-valued data have been fixed. A structural restriction on any putatively canonical lift is established in \Cref{thm:canonical-lift-rigidity}: if a realization prestack admits a strict functorial choice of representatives of the local-object classes, then global conservativity at the terminal fibre forces surjectivity of the sheafification unit, and under $H^1$-vanishing plus separatedness this is equivalent to the local object functor already being a sheaf at the terminal fibre. The role of this theorem is negative rather than existential: it shows that genuine gluing-null semantics cannot come from a representative-rigid lift. The later gerbe semantics are therefore conditional on a chosen gluing-sensitive realization datum. Under such a datum, visible value classes coincide with the global sections of the sheaf of connected components (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}), each visible branch yields a banded component gerbe (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}), and \Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics} identifies gluing-level absence with the existence of visible branches together with non-neutrality of the associated component gerbes. No unrestricted existence or canonicity theorem for gluing-sensitive lifts is proved for arbitrary local-object functors; Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-ghz} gives only an explicit GHZ/Mermin example of chosen lift data.

The homological part of the paper begins only after one visible branch, one finite nerve presentation, and one constant finite abelian band have been fixed. From that point the underlying tools are classical: the universal coefficient sequence, finite-abelian duality, and standard quotient arguments. The point here is not to reprove those tools, but to isolate the canonical image
\[
K_\omega:=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)
\]
and quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega$ actually attached to the branch obstruction class inside the forcing semantics, and to make precise the comparison class under which they are invariant. \Cref{prop:invariance-visible-quotients} shows invariance under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and re-trivialization of the band, \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient} identifies the admissible characters as those annihilating $K_\omega$, and \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions} identifies the character-blind case with the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime.

When more than one visible branch is present, two aggregate quotients arise naturally, according as the branch label is retained or forgotten. Their relation is described by the short exact sequence of \Cref{thm:branch-comparison-exact-sequence}. Assuming in addition that $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$, we prove realization theorems for prescribed branchwise data: \Cref{thm:prescribed-branch-realization} treats non-neutral branches, and \Cref{thm:mixed-branch-realization} extends the construction to arbitrary finite families of homomorphisms $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$, with the neutral branches characterized by the zero homomorphisms. If $H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\cong \mathbb Z^\beta$, \Cref{cor:torsion-free-h2-classification} identifies the realizable hidden subgroups as exactly the subgroups of $A$ generated by at most $\beta$ elements. The bouquet theorem \Cref{thm:bouquet-two-branch-resolution} then provides a natural topological family, namely the canonical good covers of $\bigvee_{j=1}^{\beta}S^2$, in which the acyclic-cover hypothesis is automatic and the pure two-branch resolution kernels can be classified explicitly. In the unique-branch case this gives a comparison with the usual no-global-section criterion in the support-presheaf approach to contextuality; see \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}.

The sheaf-theoretic, gerbe-theoretic, and homological tools used here are classical, and several later comparison results are formal once the branchwise intrinsic data have been fixed. The contribution of the paper is accordingly structural: the extension chain separates the semantic layers, theorems are stated so that the dependence on extra realization data is explicit, and the obstruction class yields canonical quotients inside a controlled finite-nerve comparison class.

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries} introduces the semantic framework and the relevant notions of conservativity. \Cref{sec:information-states} develops forcing over information states. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} treats local objects, sheafification, and lower-fragment undefinability. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} introduces component gerbes and branch obstruction classes. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} and \Cref{sec:branch-aggregation} contain the intrinsic visibility theory together with the branchwise comparison and realization results. The appendix collects the refinement-dynamic material and the GHZ/Mermin example.
