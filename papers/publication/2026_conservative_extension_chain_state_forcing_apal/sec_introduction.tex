\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Sheaf-theoretic semantics provides a natural language for the passage from local data to global objects. For presheaves arising in logic and in the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyMansfieldSoaresBarbosa2012,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,Caru2017,Caru2018,Montanhano2021}, the basic distinction is between the existence of compatible local families and the existence of a global section. When the latter fails, one is led to obstruction classes, gerbes, and related cohomological invariants. What is less clear at the outset is how much of this local-to-global behaviour is already visible at the level of pointwise states, and how much genuinely appears only after one passes to information states, local objects, and obstruction data.

The present paper addresses that question in a forcing framework built from sets of realizations rather than single assignments. In this respect it is close in spirit to the semantics of imperfect information and to team semantics \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007,AbramskyPuljujarviVaananen2025}, although the formal setting is different. It also belongs to the forcing and sheaf-theoretic tradition stemming from Beth, Kripke, Fitting, Tierney, and the later topos-theoretic expositions \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,Fitting1969,Tierney1972,Goldblatt2006,Johnstone2002}. The organizing principle is that each passage to a richer semantic layer should come with an explicit conservativity statement for the lower language.

We therefore work with the chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0 \xhookrightarrow{\iota} \mathbb{L}_1 \preceq \mathbb{L}_2 \preceq \mathbb{L}_3 \preceq \mathbb{L}_4,
\]
where $\mathbb{L}_0$ is the pointwise layer, $\mathbb{L}_1$ is the information-state forcing layer, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local objects and site structure, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds branchwise obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ records refinement dynamics. The passage $\mathbb{L}_0\hookrightarrow\mathbb{L}_1$ is a singleton semantic embedding, while the higher boundaries are conservative extensions in the sense of \Cref{def:conservative-extension}. This gives a precise way of saying that later layers add geometric or homological structure without altering the behaviour of formulas already present below.

The first group of results belongs to the boundary between pointwise semantics and local objects. Compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification in \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization}. More significantly, \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} shows that the predicates $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}_s$ are not definable by automorphism-invariant constant-free formulas of the $\mathbb{L}_1$-reduct with one free reference variable. Thus the passage from pointwise truth to local objecthood is not merely a change of presentation: even in the restricted lower fragment considered here, it reveals distinctions that are invisible at the pointwise level.

The second group of results concerns the obstruction layer. Once one chooses a gluing-sensitive realization prestack whose stackification recovers the sheafification of isomorphism classes and satisfies the comparison hypotheses stated later, visible value classes become global sections of the sheaf of connected components of the realization stack; see \Cref{thm:visible-value-components}. Each visible branch $v$ then determines a banded component gerbe $\mathfrak G_v$ (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}), and gluing-level absence is characterized by the existence of visible branches together with non-neutrality of all component gerbes (\Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}). The GHZ/Mermin discussion in \Cref{thm:ghz-realization} serves only to exhibit the extra amplitude data needed to place a support presheaf inside this conditional framework.

The main quantitative results appear at the homological level. For a finite abelian band and a fixed finite nerve presentation, the universal coefficient theorem yields the homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_\omega:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A.
\]
Its image
\[
K_\omega=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)
\]
is the intrinsic hidden subgroup, and the quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega
\]
is the intrinsic visible quotient. \Cref{prop:invariance-visible-quotients} shows that these groups are unchanged under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and band re-trivialization. \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient} identifies the class-admissible characters with the annihilator of $K_\omega$, while \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions} shows that the genuinely character-blind case is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime. When several visible branches are present, the branch-sensitive and branch-uniform quotients are related by the exact sequence of \Cref{thm:branch-comparison-exact-sequence}, and in the unique-branch case this yields a direct comparison with the no-global-section criterion from the support-presheaf approach to contextuality; see \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}.

The ingredients drawn from sheafification, stackification, gerbe theory, and the universal coefficient sequence are classical. The novelty lies in the way these constructions are assembled into one semantic chain, in the lower-fragment separation theorem, and in the extraction of the intrinsic visibility quotient from the homological evaluation map. The standing hypotheses are correspondingly stratified: site-theoretic assumptions suffice for the lower-fragment results, the gerbe layer requires a chosen gluing-sensitive realization lift, and the finite-group visibility statements are formulated for covers on which the band has been trivialized.

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} introduces the semantic layers and the relevant conservativity notions. \Cref{sec:information-states} develops the forcing semantics for information states. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} studies local objects, sheafification, and the pointwise undefinability result. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} treats component gerbes and branched gluing obstructions. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} and \Cref{sec:branch-aggregation} establish the intrinsic visibility quotients and their behaviour across branches. \Cref{sec:refinement-dynamics-overview} briefly indicates the supplementary refinement-dynamic material, whose formal statements and proofs are collected in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-dynamics} together with the finite-state complexity bounds.
