\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Standard sheaf-theoretic semantics captures local-to-global passage: compatible local sections glue when the underlying presheaf is a sheaf, and fail to glue when it is not. The obstruction to gluing is recorded by cohomology classes, and the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011}, together with its cohomological refinements \cite{AbramskyMansfieldSoaresBarbosa2012,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,Caru2017} and topological perspectives \cite{OkayRobertsBartlettRaussendorf2017,OkayTyhurstRaussendorf2018}, treats the absence of a global section as the central semantic defect. The pointwise comparison considered in this paper is narrower: within the automorphism-invariant pointwise fragment of the lower-layer reduct, the distinction between compatible local sectionability and global sectionability is invisible. A formula evaluated at a single realization can register only pointwise value data; under the syntactic restrictions made precise later, it does not detect that compatible local data exist but fail to glue.

The present work sits in the forcing and sheaf-theoretic tradition in logic, building on the Kripke semantics for intuitionistic logic \cite{Kripke1965}, Beth's semantic tableaux constructions \cite{Beth1956}, Fitting's model-theoretic forcing \cite{Fitting1969}, Tierney's early topos-theoretic forcing perspective \cite{Tierney1972}, and the later expositions of Goldblatt and Johnstone \cite{Goldblatt2006,Johnstone2002}. What distinguishes our setting is the explicit stratification of the semantics into conservative extension layers, so that each enrichment---from pointwise truth to information-state forcing, from forcing to local objects, from local objects to gluing obstructions---is accompanied by a precise invariance guarantee.

This gap motivates the present paper. We construct a four-layer chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0 \xhookrightarrow{\iota} \mathbb{L}_1 \preceq \mathbb{L}_2 \preceq \mathbb{L}_3 \preceq \mathbb{L}_4,
\]
where $\mathbb{L}_0$ is pointwise semantics, $\mathbb{L}_1$ adds information-state forcing, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local references and local objects on an explicit site, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds structural absence and gluing-obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ adds refinement dynamics. The base boundary $\mathbb{L}_0\hookrightarrow\mathbb{L}_1$ is a singleton semantic embedding: each pointwise state maps faithfully into the information-state layer, but the inverse projection from arbitrary multi-realization information states to single pointwise assignments does not exist in general. The three higher boundaries are conservative extensions in the sense of \Cref{def:conservative-extension}, with explicit forgetful functors and state projections spelled out in \Cref{prop:chain-instantiation}. Repeated composition along the conservative-extension segment gives the layer-preservation statement recorded in \Cref{cor:whole-chain-invariance}. The chain is the minimal framework in which the restricted logical separation theorem, the conditional gerbe semantics, and the homological visibility quotient can all be stated together.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Research question.}
Once an admitted reference carries a finite abelian gluing obstruction, what part of that obstruction is semantically visible without enlarging the state space, and how much additional information is required to recover the hidden part?

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main results.}
The answer is delivered by three groups of results, each located at a specific boundary of the extension chain. The homological result is the main mathematical payoff; the forcing layer isolates the logical comparison class, and the gerbe layer is retained as a conditional semantic framework making the later hypotheses explicit.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~A} (restricted logical separation; $\mathbb{L}_1$--$\mathbb{L}_2$ boundary).
Compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification (\Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization}), and the predicates $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}_s$ are not definable by automorphism-invariant pointwise formulas of the $\mathbb{L}_1$-reduct within the syntactic comparison class fixed in \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}. This separation result is relative to that specific comparison class: under the explicit syntactic and invariance hypotheses of \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}, the gluing predicates that drive the obstruction theory are not reducible to automorphism-invariant pointwise formulas.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~B} (conditional branched gerbe semantics; $\mathbb{L}_2$--$\mathbb{L}_3$ boundary).
The gerbe layer is conditional on a gluing-sensitive realization prestack whose stackification recovers the sheafification of isomorphism classes, is globally conservative at the terminal fibre, and is equipped with a cofinal \v{C}ech-to-derived comparison family of covers. Under those hypotheses, visible value classes are the global sections of the sheaf of connected components of the realization stack (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}), each visible branch $v$ determines a banded component gerbe $\mathfrak{G}_v$ (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}), and gluing-level absence holds exactly when visible branches exist but every component gerbe is non-neutral (\Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~C} (homological visibility; at $\mathbb{L}_3$).
Under explicit finite-nerve and realizability hypotheses, and after restricting to covers on which the finite abelian band is trivialized, the universal coefficient theorem yields a homological evaluation map $\mathrm{ev}_\omega : H_2(N(\mathcal{U}),\mathbb{Z}) \to A$ whose image is the intrinsic hidden subgroup $K_\omega$ and whose cokernel is the intrinsic visible quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega$. The preparatory algebra is standard; the substantive point is the semantic interpretation: the class-admissible characters are exactly the annihilator of that image, and the blind residual---the part of the obstruction invisible to every admissible character---is precisely the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-type regime singled out in \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}. Under assumptions (i)--(iv) of \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}, including the unique-branch condition and the realization hypotheses, the theory yields a formal comparison with the no-global-section criterion in the support-presheaf setting of \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011}.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Secondary consequences.}
The multi-branch theory yields an exact comparison between branch-sensitive and branch-uniform visibility: the canonical surjection $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\mathrm{br}}(r) \twoheadrightarrow A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\mathrm{unif}}(r)$ has kernel $(\sum_v K_v)/(\bigcap_v K_v)$, so the hidden budget behind uniform visibility factors into a branch-resolution term and a within-branch residual (\Cref{thm:branch-comparison-exact-sequence,thm:branch-resolution-budget,cor:uniform-hidden-budget-factorization}). Worked finite examples (\Cref{ex:one-bit-hidden-obstruction,ex:rp2-character-blind,ex:s2-single-branch-maximal-collapse,ex:two-branch-sphere-separation}) separate the visible, blind, and multi-branch regimes.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Hypothesis stratification.}
The hypotheses used in these results are stratified rather than cumulative. The site-theoretic assumptions enter already at the local-object layer: the reference preorder is promoted to a thin category equipped with a Grothendieck pretopology and the pullbacks needed for matching families. Branch constancy enters only for the decomposition corollary (\Cref{cor:branch-decomposition}), not for the component-gerbe theorem itself. Global conservativity is used only when stack-global objects are compared back to the original local object functor. Finite nerve presentations appear only in the finite abelian visibility theorems, and those theorems are explicitly restricted to covers on which the band is trivialized; nontrivial monodromy would require local coefficient systems. The logical separation result is an automorphism-invariant pointwise non-definability theorem, the gerbe semantics is conditional on gluing-sensitive realization data together with a \v{C}ech-to-derived comparison hypothesis, and the cohomological statements are conditional on finite-nerve realizability together with a fixed cohomological model.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Plan of the paper.}
\Cref{sec:preliminaries} defines logic layers and conservative extensions and introduces the four-layer chain, together with its concrete adjacent interfaces. \Cref{sec:information-states} defines information states, forcing, and lifted pointwise soundness. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} upgrades the local-reference geometry to an explicit site, proves the sheafification characterization, states the restricted forcing-necessity theorem (Theorem~A), and isolates the additional gluing-sensitive realization hypotheses needed later. The gerbe-obstruction subsection establishes the conditional branched gerbe semantics (Theorem~B) under explicit \v{C}ech-comparison hypotheses. The homological-visibility subsection fixes the cohomological conventions for finite nerve presentations and then establishes the intrinsic visible quotient and the character-blind obstruction theorem (Theorem~C), together with the multi-branch comparison sequence and a formal contextuality dictionary. \Cref{sec:multiaxis-refinement} records supplementary refinement monotonicity results at $\mathbb{L}_4$, and a short appendix records finite-state upper bounds.
