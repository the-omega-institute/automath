\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Sheaf-theoretic semantics makes precise the passage from local data to global objects. For presheaves arising in logic and in the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyMansfieldSoaresBarbosa2012,AbramskyBarbosaKishidaLalMansfield2015,Caru2017,Caru2018,Montanhano2021}, the basic distinction is between the existence of a compatible local family and the existence of a global section. When the latter fails, one is naturally led to obstruction classes, gerbes, and related cohomological invariants \cite{Giraud1971,Breen2006,Hatcher2002}. The question studied here is how much of this local-to-global behaviour is already visible at the level of pointwise states, and how much only appears after one passes to richer semantic structure.

We consider this question in a forcing semantics whose states are sets of realizations rather than single assignments. The setting is related in spirit to semantics of imperfect information and team semantics \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007,AbramskyPuljujarviVaananen2025}, but the organization is closer to the forcing and sheaf-theoretic tradition stemming from Beth, Kripke, Fitting, Tierney, and later topos-theoretic expositions \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,Fitting1969,Tierney1972,Goldblatt2006,Johnstone2002}. Our basic requirement is that each passage to a richer semantic layer should come with an explicit comparison to the preceding one.

Accordingly, we work with the chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0 \xhookrightarrow{\iota} \mathbb{L}_1 \preceq \mathbb{L}_2 \preceq \mathbb{L}_3 \preceq \mathbb{L}_4,
\]
where $\mathbb{L}_0$ is the pointwise layer, $\mathbb{L}_1$ is the information-state forcing layer, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local objects and site structure, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds branchwise obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ adds refinement dynamics. The map $\mathbb{L}_0\hookrightarrow\mathbb{L}_1$ is a singleton semantic embedding, whereas the later steps are conservative extensions in the sense of \Cref{def:conservative-extension}. Thus the geometric and homological material introduced later does not alter the forcing behaviour of formulas already present in the lower layers.

The first main result concerns the passage from pointwise semantics to local objects. \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization} identifies compatible local sectionability with the existence of a section of the sheafification. \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} then shows that the predicates $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}_s$ are not definable by automorphism-invariant constant-free formulas of the $\mathbb{L}_1$-reduct with one free reference variable. This is the only lower-fragment separation theorem proved in the paper, and it is stated exactly at that level of generality.

At the obstruction layer we fix a gluing-sensitive realization prestack whose stackification recovers the sheafification of isomorphism classes and satisfies the comparison hypotheses recorded later. Under that hypothesis, visible value classes are exactly the global sections of the sheaf of connected components; see \Cref{thm:visible-value-components}. Each visible branch $v$ then determines a banded component gerbe \Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}, and gluing-level absence is equivalent to the presence of visible branches together with non-neutrality of all component gerbes \Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}. The paper does not claim that every bare local object functor admits such a lift; rather, it isolates the additional realization data on which the gerbe-theoretic part of the semantics depends. A GHZ/Mermin amplitude lift is recorded separately in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-ghz} only as an illustration of that extra datum.

The main quantitative invariants appear at the homological level. For a finite abelian band on a fixed finite nerve presentation, the universal coefficient sequence yields the homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_\omega:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A.
\]
Its image
\[
K_\omega=\operatorname{Im}(\mathrm{ev}_\omega)
\]
is the intrinsic hidden subgroup, and the quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega=A/K_\omega
\]
is the intrinsic visible quotient. \Cref{prop:invariance-visible-quotients} shows that these groups are unchanged under homology-equivalent refinements, cocycle replacement, and band re-trivialization. \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient} identifies the class-admissible characters with the annihilator of $K_\omega$, while \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions} shows that the character-blind case is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime.

When several visible branches occur, one obtains two natural aggregate quotients, according as the branch label is or is not available. These are compared by the short exact sequence of \Cref{thm:branch-comparison-exact-sequence}. Under the additional hypothesis $H_1(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)=0$, \Cref{thm:prescribed-branch-realization} gives a converse existence result: every finite family of homomorphisms
\[
H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A
\]
is realized by one admitted reference on the fixed nerve, and hence every corresponding pair of branch-sensitive and branch-uniform visible quotients occurs in the semantics. In the unique-branch case this yields a direct comparison with the no-global-section criterion in the support-presheaf approach to contextuality; see \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}.

The ingredients taken from sheafification, stackification, gerbe theory, and the universal coefficient theorem are classical. The contribution of the paper lies in the way these ingredients are assembled: a conservative-extension chain that isolates where the classical machinery enters, a precise lower-fragment separation theorem justifying the local-object predicates, and a canonical visibility quotient extracted from the homological evaluation map within an explicit comparison class.

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} introduces the semantic layers and the relevant conservativity notions. \Cref{sec:information-states} develops forcing over information states. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} treats local objects, sheafification, and the lower-fragment separation theorem. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} develops component gerbes and presentation-level visibility. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} and \Cref{sec:branch-aggregation} contain the intrinsic visibility theory and the branchwise comparison results. Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-complexity} and Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-dynamics} collect the supplementary refinement-dynamic material, and Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-ghz} records the GHZ/Mermin amplitude lift.
