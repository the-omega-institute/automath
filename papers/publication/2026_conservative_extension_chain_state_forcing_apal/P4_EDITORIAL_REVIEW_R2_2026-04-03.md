**Theorem 4.37 (thm:cech-bridge-compatible-realizations).** The four-part Cech obstruction proof:

**(i)** Existence of $\phi_{ij}$: The isomorphism presheaf $\mathrm{Isom}(x_i|_{U_{ij}}, x_j|_{U_{ij}})$ is a $\Band|_{U_{ij}}$-torsor (because the gerbe is banded). Vanishing $H^1(U_{ij}, \Band) = 0$ forces triviality, giving global sections. Correct. The citation to Mac Lane-Moerdijk III and Stacks Project Sec. 11 is appropriate.

**(ii)** Cocycle condition: The composite $\phi_{ik}^{-1} \circ \phi_{jk} \circ \phi_{ij}$ on $U_{ijk}$ is an automorphism of $x_i|_{U_{ijk}}$, hence a section of $\Band(U_{ijk})$. The standard calculation on quadruple overlaps gives the Cech cocycle identity. Correct. Citation to Giraud Chap. IV is appropriate.

**Technical note on the cocycle formula:** The paper writes $g_{ijk} = \phi_{ik}^{-1}|_{U_{ijk}} \circ \phi_{jk}|_{U_{ijk}} \circ \phi_{ij}|_{U_{ijk}}$. This is the standard Giraud convention. One might expect the alternating form $\phi_{jk} \circ \phi_{ij}^{-1} \circ \phi_{ik}$ or similar, depending on the chosen cocycle normalization. However, since the banding identifies automorphisms with the abelian group $\Band$, the additive cocycle condition is $g_{ijk} - g_{ijl} + g_{ikl} - g_{jkl} = 0$ on quadruple overlaps, which is satisfied by the given formula. **Correct under the stated convention.**

**(iii)** Independence of choices:
- Changing $\phi_{ij}$: $\phi'_{ij} = \phi_{ij} \circ h_{ij}^{-1}$ gives $g' = (\delta h) g$. For an abelian band this is the standard coboundary calculation. Correct.
- Changing $x_i$: The isomorphism $\psi_i : x_i \to x'_i$ exists because $\mathrm{Isom}(x_i, x'_i)$ is a trivial torsor (by $H^1(U_i, \Band) = 0$). Transporting gives a coboundary. Correct.
- Passage to refinement: Standard pullback stability. Correct.

**(iv)** Neutrality iff $[g] = 0$:
- Forward: Global object gives isomorphisms $\psi_i : x|_{U_i} \to x_i$, setting $\phi_{ij} = \psi_j \circ \psi_i^{-1}$ gives $g \equiv 0$. Correct.
- Backward: $[g] = 0$ gives a 1-cochain $h$ with $g = \delta h$. Modified isomorphisms $\phi'_{ij} = \phi_{ij} \circ h_{ij}^{-1}$ satisfy the exact cocycle condition, hence form an effective descent datum (stack axiom). Correct.

**SOUND.**
