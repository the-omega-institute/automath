\section{Refinement Dynamics and Support}\label{sec:refinement-dynamics-overview}

The obstruction-theoretic arguments of the paper are carried out at fixed states, but the same framework admits a natural refinement dynamics. The formal development is collected in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-dynamics}; we record here only the points needed to orient the reader.

Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-dynamics} introduces multi-axis dynamic structures and shows that axis-wise refinements preserve forcing while allowing delayed decidability. It also defines support, indispensability, and minimal support for refinement axes, together with the corresponding finite-state complexity bounds. Finally, it proves that refinement can only decrease the intrinsic hidden subgroup of a visible branch and that value-preserving rewrites do not create new readout facts. None of these results is used in the core proofs above, but they indicate how the visibility theory behaves once one equips the state space with additional dynamic bookkeeping.
