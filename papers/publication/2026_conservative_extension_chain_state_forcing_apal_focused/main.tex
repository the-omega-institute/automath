\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure for references evaluated at information states \emph{relative} to a chosen gluing-sensitive realization prestack with fixed abelian band. No canonical obstruction theory on a bare set-valued presheaf is claimed; every theorem below assumes the explicit hypotheses listed in the new scope section. First, on lower-layer formulas state forcing coincides with flat team semantics on nonempty teams of assignments, and \textit{only} in the constant-free equality-only lower reduct evaluated on singleton parameter states of parameter-indexed local-object expansions do we prove that compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable without the local-object enrichment. Second, once a gluing-sensitive realization prestack with fixed band and global conservativity has been chosen, compatible local presentations of a reference define visible branches that agree with the global connected components of the realization stack; gluing failure then occurs precisely when visible branches exist but all component gerbes are non-neutral. Third, for a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ arising from such a chosen realization prestack on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, the visible quotient relative to that presentation is the cokernel of the homological evaluation map $\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A$, equivalently the quotient detected by the class-admissible character channels. The quotient is well defined up to the explicit presentation morphisms (equivalences of lifts, changes of band trivialization, and $H_2$-surjective refinements). When the support presheaf is separated, a compatible realization prestack exists, and the unique visible branch hypothesis holds, the theory recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and identifies the cohomologically blind residue as the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part. Canonical contextuality and Kripke-style semantic examples are worked out in detail to demonstrate the scope and limitations of the formalism.
\end{abstract}
