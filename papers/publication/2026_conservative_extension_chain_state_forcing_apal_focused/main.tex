\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure for references evaluated at information states, relative to a gluing-sensitive realization prestack with fixed abelian band. On the lower language, state forcing is just flat team semantics on nonempty teams, but in the constant-free equality fragment on singleton parameter states compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable without the local-object predicates. For a chosen realization prestack, visible branches coincide with the connected components of the realization stack, and gluing failure occurs exactly when visible branches exist but all corresponding component gerbes are non-neutral. For a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, we identify the presentation-relative visible quotient with $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$, equivalently with the quotient detected by the class-admissible character channels, and prove invariance under equivalence of lifts, change of band trivialization, and $H_2$-surjective refinement. In the unique-branch separated case this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture, while the character-blind residue is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part; the torus-type, $S^2$-type, $\mathbb{R}P^2$-type, and Kripke-style examples illustrate the range of the formalism.
\end{abstract}
