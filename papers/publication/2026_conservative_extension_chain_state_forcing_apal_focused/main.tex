\begin{abstract}
We study local-to-global failure for references interpreted at information states. The lower layer is flat team semantics on nonempty teams, while admissibility, compatible local sectionability, and gluing belong to an enriched layer. We show that compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification and that, in the constant-free equality fragment, the predicates $\CompSec$, $\Sec$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}$ are not definable in the lower forcing language over the natural class of parameter-indexed local-object expansions.

Fix a realization prestack with abelian band. Then visible value classes are the connected components of the associated realization stack. Under global conservativity at the terminal fibre, gluing failure is equivalent to the existence of visible branches with non-neutral component gerbes. Under branch constancy this is equivalent to the existence of a branch selector, and also to surjectivity of the terminal sheafification unit. For a finite abelian branch obstruction presented on a finite \v{C}ech nerve we define the presentation-relative visible quotient $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$ and show that it is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible characters. This quotient is invariant under equivalence of chosen lifts, change of band trivialization, and $H_2$-surjective refinement. A Mayer--Vietoris theorem shows that the hidden subgroup is additive across decompositions with $H_1$-acyclic overlap; consequently, filtrations by $H_2$-trivial pieces can contribute at most a pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ residue, and contribute no branch obstruction at all when the terminal $H_1$ is free. If every chosen branch presentation has vanishing $H^2$, then compatible local sectionability coincides with global sectionability. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger criterion, and the remaining blind part is the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residue.
\end{abstract}
