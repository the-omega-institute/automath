\begin{abstract}
We study local-to-global failure for references interpreted at information states. The basic forcing semantics is flat team semantics on nonempty teams; admissibility, local sectionability, and gluing appear only after passing to an enriched local-object layer. We prove that compatible local sectionability is equivalent to nonemptiness of the sheafification and that, over singleton states and within the comparison class of arbitrary parameter-indexed local-object expansions, the predicates $\CompSec$, $\Sec$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}$ are not definable in the lower forcing language.

We then fix a realization prestack with abelian band. Visible value classes are the connected components of the associated realization stack. Assuming global conservativity at the terminal fibre, gluing failure is equivalent to the existence of a visible branch with non-neutral component gerbe. Under branch constancy, branch selectors are equivalent to neutrality of all visible branches. For a finite abelian branch obstruction presented on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, we define the presentation-relative visible quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}:=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$ and show that it is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible characters. This quotient is invariant under equivalence of chosen lifts, change of band trivialization, and $H_2$-surjective refinement. In the free-$H_1$ regime it becomes the initial coefficient quotient that neutralizes the corresponding branch gerbe, and gluing failure is equivalent to strict collapse of the visible quotient on every visible branch. A Mayer--Vietoris argument identifies the hidden subgroup and shows that filtrations by $H_2$-trivial pieces leave at most a pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ residue. If every chosen branch presentation has vanishing $H^2$, then compatible local sectionability implies global sectionability. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case, gluing failure is therefore equivalent to $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}\neq A$, giving a converse-complete visible-quotient criterion under the chosen lift hypotheses.
\end{abstract}
