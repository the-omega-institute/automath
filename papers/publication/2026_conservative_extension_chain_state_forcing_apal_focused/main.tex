\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure for references evaluated at information states, relative to a chosen realization prestack with fixed abelian band. At the lower layer, state forcing is flat team semantics on nonempty teams; nevertheless, in the constant-free equality fragment over singleton parameter states, the predicates expressing compatible local sectionability and gluing-level failure are not definable in the lower forcing language. For a fixed realization prestack, visible branches are exactly the connected components of the associated realization stack. Under global conservativity at the terminal fibre, gluing failure occurs precisely when visible branches exist and all component gerbes are non-neutral; under branch constancy, a section of the component projection exists exactly when all visible branches are neutral, equivalently, under global conservativity, when the terminal sheafification unit is surjective. For a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, we define the presentation-relative visible quotient as $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$, show that it is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible character channels, and prove invariance under equivalence of lifts, change of band trivialization, and $H_2$-surjective refinement. Under the support-presheaf identification and a chosen globally conservative realization prestack, the one-branch case recovers the compatible-family/global-section criterion of Abramsky--Brandenburger, while the character-blind residue is precisely the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part.
\end{abstract}
