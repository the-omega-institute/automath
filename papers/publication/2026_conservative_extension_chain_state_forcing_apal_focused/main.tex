\begin{abstract}
We study gluing failure for references evaluated at information states. The lower layer is flat team semantics on nonempty teams, but the predicates for admissibility, compatible local sectionability, and gluing belong to a genuine enrichment. Compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification, and in the constant-free equality fragment over singleton parameter states the predicates $\CompSec$, $\Sec$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}$ are not definable in the lower forcing language.

Fix a realization prestack with abelian band. Visible value classes are then the connected components of the realization stack. Under global conservativity at the terminal fibre, gluing failure is equivalent to the presence of visible branches whose component gerbes are non-neutral; under branch constancy this is equivalent to the existence of a branch selector, or, equivalently, to surjectivity of the terminal sheafification unit. For a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ on a finite \v{C}ech nerve we define the presentation-relative visible quotient $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$ and show that it is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible character channels. This quotient is invariant under equivalence of lifts, change of band trivialization, and $H_2$-surjective refinement. If every chosen branch presentation has vanishing $H^2$, then every component gerbe is neutral, so compatible local sectionability collapses to global sectionability. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger criterion, and the remaining blind part is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residue.
\end{abstract}
