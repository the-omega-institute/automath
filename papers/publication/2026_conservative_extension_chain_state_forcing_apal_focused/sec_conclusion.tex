\section{Final Remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The lower forcing layer considered here is flat, but the local-object enrichment is not: within the constant-free equality fragment on singleton parameter states it is needed to express compatible local existence and genuine gluing failure. Once a gluing-sensitive realization prestack is fixed, visible branches become the connected components of the realization stack, gluing failure becomes branchwise non-neutrality of the corresponding component gerbes, and the homological evaluation map isolates the quotient seen by the semantics.

In the unique-branch separated case this brings the theory back to the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture, while identifying the remaining blind part exactly with the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residue. The torus-type, $S^2$-, $\mathbb{R}P^2$-, and Kripke-style examples show that both visible and completely character-blind behavior occur naturally within the same framework.
