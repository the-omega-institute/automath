\section{Final Remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The lower forcing layer considered here is flat, but the local-object enrichment is not: within the singleton-parameter comparison class of \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility} it is needed to express compatible local existence and genuine gluing failure. Once a chosen realization prestack is fixed, visible branches become the connected components of the realization stack. Under terminal-fibre global conservativity, gluing failure becomes branchwise non-neutrality of the corresponding component gerbes. Under branch constancy, canonical branch selection is available exactly when those component gerbes are all neutral, equivalently, under global conservativity, when the terminal sheafification unit is surjective.

The homological quotient is likewise presentation-relative: for a fixed finite nerve presentation it records the $H_2$-visible part of the chosen branch obstruction, and the blind residue is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part. Under the support-presheaf identification and a chosen globally conservative realization prestack, the unique-branch case recovers the compatible-family/global-section criterion of Abramsky--Brandenburger. The torus-type construction should be read as an explicit existence proof for the conditional hypothesis package, while the $S^2$-, $\mathbb{R}P^2$-, and Kripke-style examples isolate the visible and blind extremes once such data are fixed.
