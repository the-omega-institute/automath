\section{Further remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The lower forcing layer is flat, but the passage to local objects introduces genuinely new semantic structure: admissibility, compatible local sectionability, and gluing are not recoverable from the lower language in the comparison class studied here. Once a realization prestack has been fixed, this additional structure admits a branchwise description. Visible value classes are the connected components of the realization stack, and gluing failure is governed by the non-neutrality of the corresponding component gerbes.

In the finite-presentation setting, the obstruction attached to a visible branch separates into an $H_2$-visible part and a residual pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ part. The presentation-relative visible quotient $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$ captures exactly the part seen by the admissible character channels and is stable under the natural changes of presentation considered above. In the free-$H_1$ regime, \Cref{thm:free-h1-quotient-completeness} shows that this quotient is also the minimal coefficient quotient that neutralizes the chosen branch gerbe, and that gluing-nullity is equivalent to strict collapse of the visible quotient on every visible branch. The Mayer--Vietoris theorem of \Cref{thm:mv-hidden-subgroup} shows that this visible part is additive across decompositions with $H_1$-acyclic overlap, so filtrations by $H_2$-trivial pieces can leave only a pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ residue, and no obstruction at all once the terminal $H_1$ is free. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case this yields a converse-complete visible-quotient reformulation of the Abramsky--Brandenburger criterion. The statements proved here remain conditional on the chosen realization prestack and chosen finite presentation; they identify the invariants that persist within that comparison class, not a canonical obstruction extracted from the lower semantics alone.
