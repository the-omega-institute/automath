\section{Final Remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The lower forcing layer considered here is flat, but the local-object enrichment is not: within the constant-free equality fragment on singleton parameter states it is needed to express compatible local existence and genuine gluing failure. Once a gluing-sensitive realization prestack is fixed, visible branches become the connected components of the realization stack, gluing failure becomes branchwise non-neutrality of the corresponding component gerbes, and canonical branch selection is possible exactly when those component gerbes are all neutral. Under global conservativity at the terminal fibre, this is equivalent to surjectivity of the sheafification unit on global objects. The homological evaluation map then isolates the quotient seen by the semantics.

In the unique-branch separated case this brings the theory back to the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture, while identifying the remaining blind part exactly with the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residue. The torus-type, $S^2$-, $\mathbb{R}P^2$-, and Kripke-style examples show that both visible and completely character-blind behavior occur naturally within the same framework.
