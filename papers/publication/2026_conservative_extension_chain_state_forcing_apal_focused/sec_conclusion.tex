\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The lower forcing layer considered here is flat, but the passage to local objects introduces new semantic structure: admissibility, compatible local sectionability, and gluing are not recoverable from the lower language alone within the comparison class studied here. Once a realization prestack has been fixed, this extra structure admits a branchwise description. Visible value classes are the connected components of the realization stack, and gluing failure is governed by the non-neutrality of the corresponding component gerbes.

In the finite-presentation setting, the obstruction class attached to a visible branch separates into a part detected by $H_2$ and a residual pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ part. The presentation-relative visible quotient $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$ captures the portion seen by the admissible character channels and is stable under the natural changes of presentation considered above. The Mayer--Vietoris theorem of \Cref{thm:mv-hidden-subgroup} shows that this visible part is additive across decompositions with $H_1$-acyclic overlap, so filtrations by $H_2$-trivial pieces can create only pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ residue, and no obstruction at all once the terminal $H_1$ is free. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger criterion, while the remaining blind cases are exactly the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ ones. These branch-gerbe and visible-quotient statements remain conditional on the chosen realization prestack and chosen finite presentation; the paper identifies the invariants that persist within that comparison class, not a canonical obstruction attached to the lower semantics alone.
