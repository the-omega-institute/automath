\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The lower forcing layer considered here is flat, but the enrichment by local-object predicates is not. Already over singleton parameter states, the predicates expressing compatible local existence and genuine gluing failure cannot be reduced to the lower language. Once a realization prestack has been chosen, visible branches become the connected components of the realization stack, and gluing failure is read branchwise in terms of non-neutral component gerbes. Under branch constancy, a branch selector exists exactly when those gerbes are neutral.

The homological analysis is necessarily presentation-relative. For a fixed finite nerve presentation, the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}$ records the $H_2$-visible part of the branch obstruction, while the blind residue is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part. In the one-branch case, and under the support-presheaf identification together with a chosen globally conservative lift, this recovers the familiar compatible-family/global-section criterion of Abramsky--Brandenburger and sharpens it by isolating the precise source of character-blindness.
