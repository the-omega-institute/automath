\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The main point of the paper is that gluing failure over information states has two distinct aspects. On the logical side, the lower forcing layer remains flat, but the predicates governing admissibility, compatible local existence, and gluing are not eliminable in the comparison class of parameter-indexed local-object expansions treated here. On the geometric side, once a realization prestack has been chosen, the failure of global realization is branchwise: visible value classes are the connected components of the chosen realization stack, and gluing failure is measured by the non-neutrality of the corresponding component gerbes.

The site-theoretic and gerbe-theoretic ingredients used to reach this point are standard; the contribution of the paper is to place them under explicit semantic hypotheses and to identify, for a chosen finite presentation, the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}$ with the part of a branch obstruction detected by the class-admissible character channels. The remaining blind part is the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residue. The two-dimensionality theorem shows that genuine gluing-nullity cannot occur in the absence of degree-two overlap geometry. In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting this recovers the familiar compatible-family/global-section criterion of Abramsky--Brandenburger and identifies where character-blindness remains.
