\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The lower forcing layer considered here is flat, but the enrichment by local-object predicates is not. Already over singleton parameter states, the predicates expressing compatible local existence and genuine gluing failure cannot be reduced to the lower language. No existence or uniqueness theorem for realization prestacks is claimed; the paper instead isolates what follows once a chosen globally conservative lift and, where needed, chosen finite branch presentations are available. Within that conditional framework, visible branches become the connected components of the realization stack, and gluing failure is read branchwise in terms of non-neutral component gerbes. Under branch constancy, a branch selector exists exactly when those gerbes are neutral.

The homological analysis is necessarily presentation-relative. For a fixed finite nerve presentation, the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}$ records the $H_2$-visible part of the branch obstruction, while the blind residue is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part. The conditional two-dimensionality theorem shows that the gluing-null regime requires genuinely degree-two overlap geometry: vanishing $H^2$ on every chosen branch presentation forces all component gerbes to be neutral, collapses compatible local sectionability to global sectionability, and excludes graph-type unique-branch support models from the chosen-lift regime. In the one-branch case, and under the support-presheaf identification together with a chosen globally conservative lift, this recovers the familiar compatible-family/global-section criterion of Abramsky--Brandenburger and sharpens it by isolating the precise source of character-blindness. The space-based examples are intended only as calibration checks for the visible and blind extremes and for consistency of the standing hypotheses.
