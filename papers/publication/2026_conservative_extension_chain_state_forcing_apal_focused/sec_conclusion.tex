\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The main point of the paper is that gluing failure over information states has two distinct aspects. On the logical side, the lower forcing layer remains flat, but the predicates governing admissibility, compatible local existence, and gluing are not reducible to that lower language. On the geometric side, once a realization prestack has been chosen, the failure of global realization is branchwise: visible value classes are the connected components of the realization stack, and gluing failure is measured by the non-neutrality of the corresponding component gerbes.

The homological analysis refines this picture by isolating the part of a branch obstruction detected by $H_2$. For a fixed finite presentation, the quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}$ records exactly that visible part, while the remaining blind part is the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residue. The two-dimensionality theorem shows that genuine gluing-nullity cannot occur in the absence of degree-two overlap geometry. In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting this recovers the familiar compatible-family/global-section criterion of Abramsky--Brandenburger and identifies precisely where character-blindness remains.
