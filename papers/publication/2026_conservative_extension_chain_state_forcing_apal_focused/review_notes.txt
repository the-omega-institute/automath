Target journal: APAL (Annals of Pure and Applied Logic)
Previously: NDJFL (changed after internal review)

Revision summary (2026-03-23):

1. Fixed correctness issue in Theorem 4.26 (component gerbe decomposition):
   The original version claimed the full stack decomposition unconditionally.
   The proof only showed each visible branch gives a gerbe, not that every
   local branch comes from a global one. Fix: the gerbe property is now the
   theorem; the full decomposition is a corollary under an explicit branch
   constancy hypothesis.

2. Fixed correctness issue in Theorem 4.28 (gerbe-null-semantics):
   The equivalence Sec_s(r) <=> L_r(a) nonempty used the direction from
   L_r(a) to Sec_s(r) without justification. Fix: added Definition
   (global conservativity at the terminal fibre) requiring essential
   surjectivity of the stackification morphism on the terminal fibre,
   and Proposition (no new global objects at a) deriving the equivalence.

3. Added forcing necessity theorem (pointwise irreducibility of gluing
   predicates): Two admitted references are constructed that are
   indistinguishable at L_1 but one has CompSec /\ Sec while the other
   has CompSec /\ not Sec. This proves that the local-object enrichment
   is not merely a convenience but introduces genuinely new predicates.

4. Added connection to sheaf-theoretic contextuality: In the unique-branch
   case, the branched gerbe semantics reduces to the standard no-global-
   section picture of Abramsky--Brandenburger. The Ext-residual provides
   a precise mechanism for the incomplete cohomological detection
   identified by Caru.

5. Removed all references to "larger architecture", "broader research
   program", "eleven-layer architecture". The paper is now presented as
   a self-contained four-layer fragment.

6. Moved finite-state complexity upper bounds from main text to appendix.

7. Split sec_null_decomposition.tex (1689 lines) into three files under
   800 lines each.

8. Updated abstract, introduction, preliminaries, and conclusion to
   reflect all changes.

Residual limitations:
1. The paper gives upper bounds for the support problems but not matching
   lower bounds. This is now stated as an open problem.
2. The realization layer remains abstract.
