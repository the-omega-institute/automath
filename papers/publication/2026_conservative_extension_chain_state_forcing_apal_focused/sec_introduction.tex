\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Formal semantics is often presented as truth at a fully specified point of evaluation. That picture is exact when the relevant assignment or world has already been fixed. It is too coarse, however, when information is only partial. One may have ruled out some realizations without having selected a unique one; one may have an admissible reference before a global object has formed; or one may have compatible local data that fail to glue. In such situations the natural semantic notion is not pointwise truth but forcing at an information state.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Related work.}
Several familiar traditions isolate parts of this picture. Kripke and Beth semantics replace absolute truth by forcing at stages of information and impose persistence under refinement \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,TroelstraVanDalen1988}. Team and inquisitive semantics evaluate formulas on information states or teams and treat pointwise truth as the flat fragment of a richer state-based semantics \cite{AlbertGradel2022,CiardelliGrilletti2022}. Sheaf-theoretic semantics separates local from global existence and treats gluing failure as a structural defect rather than as a mere lack of value \cite{MacLaneMoerdijk1994}. Related local-to-global obstruction viewpoints also appear in the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality and its cohomological refinements \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,Caru2017}. The present paper combines these themes in order to address a single question: once an admitted reference carries a finite abelian gluing obstruction, what part of that obstruction is semantically visible without enlarging the state space, and what part survives only as a blind residue?

The paper is deliberately narrow. We retain only the lower-layer pointwise and information-state semantics, the local-object layer needed to express compatible local presence and gluing failure, and the sheaf/gerbe machinery used to interpret branch obstructions. The dynamic refinement, support, and complexity material of the longer manuscript is omitted here.

Against that background, the novelty of the present paper is two-sided. On lower-layer formulas, the information-state semantics is just flat team semantics on nonempty sets of assignments. The new phenomena begin only when local-object predicates are added. At that point, the logic can express compatible local presence, global absence, and gluing failure, and these predicates are invisible to the lower flat fragment. This is why the paper is not merely a team-semantical definability note and also not merely a sheaf-theoretic repackaging of contextuality.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main result A: forcing necessity.}
After separating admissible reference, local sectionability, compatible local sectionability, and global sectionability, we show that compatible local existence is characterized by sheafification (\Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization}). The key logical separation theorem is that compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable by any formula of the lower information-state forcing language without the local-object layer (\Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}). This is the result that makes the enrichment logically necessary rather than merely convenient.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main result B: visible branches and gluing failure.}
Once an abelian band $\Band$ has been fixed on the slice site, every local object functor admits a canonical split realization prestack (\Cref{prop:split-realization-prestack}). The substantive hypotheses are semantic: we work with gluing-sensitive lifts whose stackifications recover the sheafification of isomorphism classes (\Cref{prop:stackification-components}), identify visible value classes with the global connected components of the realization stack (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}), and are globally conservative at the terminal fibre when stack-global objects are compared back to the original local object functor. In that setting, each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}), and gluing failure occurs exactly when visible branches exist but every component gerbe is non-neutral (\Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}). Under branch constancy, the realization stack decomposes into those component gerbes (\Cref{cor:branch-decomposition}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main result C: intrinsic visible quotient and pure-Ext blindness.}
For a finite abelian branch obstruction on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, the homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A
\]
defines the intrinsic visible quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}:=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}),
\]
and \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient} proves that this quotient is exactly the one detected by the class-admissible character channels. The blind part is equally sharp: \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions} identifies complete character-blindness with the case where the surviving obstruction class lies entirely in the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-summand of the universal coefficient sequence. In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting, \Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison} recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and turns Car\`u-type blind cases into a theorem about pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residuals.

Standard inputs and new contributions should be kept separate. Sheafification, stackification, banded gerbes, and the universal coefficient sequence are not claimed as new. The new contribution is their joint semantic use: a forcing-necessity theorem for gluing predicates, a branch-sensitive gerbe semantics of gluing failure, and the identification of the visible quotient with the $H_2$-detected part of an obstruction while the blind residue is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part.

The main notation is as follows. The lower semantic layers are denoted by $\mathbb L_0$ and $\mathbb L_1$. For an admitted reference $r$, the realization prestack is $\mathfrak P_r$, its stackification is $\mathfrak L_r$, the component gerbe of a visible branch $v$ is $\mathfrak G_v$, the branch obstruction class is $\omega_v$, the intrinsic hidden subgroup is $K_{\omega}$, and the intrinsic visible quotient is $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}$.

The paper is organized accordingly. \Cref{sec:preliminaries} and \Cref{sec:information-states} isolate the lower-layer semantics and its conservative-extension behavior. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} develops local objects, sheafification, visible value classes, and the forcing-necessity theorem. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} gives the branch-gerbe semantics of gluing failure. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} develops the intrinsic visible quotient, the pure-Ext blindness theorem, two worked finite examples, and the unique-branch support-presheaf comparison with contextuality.
