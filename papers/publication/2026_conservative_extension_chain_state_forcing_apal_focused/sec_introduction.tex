\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Passing from single valuations to information states brings a local-to-global problem into the forcing semantics. An admitted reference may admit local realizations on a cover, or even compatible local realizations, without admitting a global realization at the root. The purpose of this paper is to analyze that failure of descent and to isolate the part of the resulting obstruction that is visible through character channels.

At the lower layer the semantics is entirely familiar. Information states are nonempty sets of assignments, and first-order formulas are evaluated by flat team semantics in the sense of Hodges and V\"a\"an\"anen \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007}; see also the forcing semantics of Beth and Kripke \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,TroelstraVanDalen1988}. Information states likewise play a central role in inquisitive and dependence-based semantics \cite{CiardelliGroenendijsenRoelofsen2019,CiardelliGrilletti2022,AlbertGradel2022}. The additional structure enters only after adjoining predicates for admissibility, local sectionability, and gluing. This enriched layer separates three distinct kinds of failure: absence of local realizations, incompatibility of local realizations, and failure of compatible local realizations to glue.

The resulting picture is close in spirit to the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality initiated by Abramsky and Brandenburger and pursued in \cite{AbramskyMansfield2012,AbramskyEtAl2015,MansfieldBarbosa2013,BeerOsborne2018,BudroniCabelloGuhneKleinmannLarsson2022}. The gerbe-theoretic tools used below are standard \cite{Giraud1971,Johnstone2002,Bredon1997,StacksProject}. What is specific here is their combination with state forcing. Once a realization prestack with fixed abelian band has been chosen, the obstruction theory becomes branchwise: visible value classes are the connected components of the associated realization stack, and each branch carries its own gerbe and degree-two obstruction class.

Our results are relative to chosen lift data. We do not attempt to construct canonical realization prestacks, nor do we address uniqueness. Instead, we study what follows from a chosen lift that is globally conservative at the terminal fibre and, in the homological part of the argument, from a chosen finite presentation of the relevant branch obstruction.

The first group of results is logical. Compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification, and in the constant-free equality fragment over singleton parameter states none of the predicates $\CompSec$, $\Sec$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}$ is definable in the lower forcing language over the comparison class of arbitrary parameter-indexed local-object expansions fixed in \Cref{def:lower-enriched-languages}; see \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization,thm:pointwise-irreducibility}. This is a lower-bound statement for that comparison class, not a claim about canonical realization data. It nevertheless shows that the local-object layer cannot in general be eliminated in favour of the lower forcing language.

The second group of results concerns a chosen realization prestack. Visible value classes are precisely the global connected components of the stackification, each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe, and, under global conservativity at the terminal fibre, gluing failure is equivalent to the existence of visible branches whose component gerbes are non-neutral; see \Cref{thm:visible-value-components,thm:component-gerbe-decomposition,thm:gerbe-null-semantics}. Under branch constancy, the component projection admits a section exactly when every visible branch is neutral; with global conservativity this is further equivalent to surjectivity of the terminal sheafification unit; see \Cref{thm:branch-selector-neutrality}.

The third part of the paper is homological. For a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ presented on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, the universal coefficient sequence yields a homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A.
\]
We define the presentation-relative visible quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}:=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})
\]
and show that it is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible characters; see \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient}. Within the chosen presentation groupoid, this quotient is stable under equivalence of lifts, change of band trivialization, and common refinement with surjective $H_2$-transition maps; see \Cref{prop:visible-quotient-trivialization-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-lift-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-refinement-invariance,prop:presentation-groupoid}. The completely character-blind case is the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$ regime of the universal coefficient sequence; see \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}.

The homological part also yields a Mayer--Vietoris generation theorem for the hidden subgroup. Whenever a chosen finite nerve presentation splits as
\[
N=N_1\cup N_2
\]
with $H_1(N_1\cap N_2,\mathbb Z)=0$, the $H_2$-visible subgroup of the global obstruction is the sum of the visible subgroups of the two restricted classes; see \Cref{thm:mv-hidden-subgroup}. Iterating this observation shows that filtrations by $H_2$-trivial pieces can leave only a pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ residue, and no branch obstruction at all once the terminal $H_1$ is free; see \Cref{cor:piecewise-h2-trivial-residue,cor:piecewise-h2-trivial-no-glue}.

The paper closes with a dimensional constraint on gluing-nullity. If every visible branch admits a finite presentation with vanishing degree-two cohomology, then every component gerbe is neutral; under global conservativity, compatible local sectionability therefore collapses to global sectionability. With branch constancy one also obtains a branch selector and surjectivity of the terminal sheafification unit; see \Cref{thm:two-dimensionality-gluing-null}. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger criterion, and the remaining blind part of the obstruction is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residue.

The sheaf-theoretic, gerbe-theoretic, and homological ingredients used below are classical, and several of the comparison results are formal once the chosen lift and finite presentation have been fixed. The contribution of the paper is therefore structural: it isolates the lower-fragment non-eliminability of the enriched predicates, makes the dependence on chosen realization data explicit, and extracts from a chosen branch obstruction the canonical subgroup $K_{\omega}$ and quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}$ that survive the allowed changes of presentation.

The standing assumptions used in the branchwise and homological analysis are collected in \S\ref{subsec:scope-chosen-lift}. The $S^2$- and $\mathbb{R}P^2$-type examples calibrate the visible and completely character-blind regimes. The torus-type example is included only as an explicit witness that the standing hypotheses can occur simultaneously; no canonical origin from the lower semantics is claimed for these examples.

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries,sec:information-states} fix the semantic framework. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} introduces the local-object layer, relates compatible local sectionability to sheafification, and identifies visible value classes with connected components. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} develops the branchwise gerbe semantics of gluing failure. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} defines the presentation-relative visible quotient, proves its invariance properties, isolates the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ blind case, and compares the resulting picture with sheaf-theoretic contextuality. Appendix~\ref{app:deferred-proofs} contains a few deferred routine proofs.
