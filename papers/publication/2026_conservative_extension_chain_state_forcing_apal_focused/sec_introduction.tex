\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

The passage from single valuations to information states introduces a local-to-global phenomenon that is invisible at the pointwise level. An admitted reference may admit local realizations on a cover, or even compatible local realizations, and nevertheless fail to admit a global realization at the root. The first aim of this paper is to separate these failures. The second is to identify the part of a gluing obstruction that remains visible to character-theoretic tests.

At the lower layer nothing essentially new happens semantically. Information states are nonempty sets of assignments, and first-order formulas are evaluated by flat team semantics in the sense of Hodges and V\"a\"an\"anen \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007}; compare also forcing semantics in the tradition of Beth and Kripke \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,TroelstraVanDalen1988}. Information states likewise play a central role in inquisitive and dependence-based semantics \cite{CiardelliGroenendijsenRoelofsen2019,CiardelliGrilletti2022,AlbertGradel2022}. The additional structure appears only after adjoining predicates for admissibility, local sectionability, and gluing. This enriched layer distinguishes absence of local realizations, failure of compatibility, and failure of descent.

The point of view is close in spirit to the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality initiated by Abramsky and Brandenburger and developed in \cite{AbramskyMansfield2012,AbramskyEtAl2015,MansfieldBarbosa2013,BeerOsborne2018,BudroniCabelloGuhneKleinmannLarsson2022}, but the basic objects here are information states rather than measurement scenarios. Once a realization prestack with fixed abelian band has been chosen, the obstruction theory becomes branchwise: visible value classes are the connected components of the associated realization stack, and each visible branch carries its own component gerbe and degree-two obstruction class.

All gerbe-theoretic and homological statements below are relative to chosen data. We fix a realization prestack, and in the homological part we also fix a finite presentation of the relevant branch obstruction. We do not address existence or uniqueness of such data. The point is rather to determine what follows, and what remains invariant, once these choices have been made.

The first group of results concerns the relation between the lower forcing language and the enriched layer. Compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification, and over singleton parameter states the predicates $\CompSec$, $\Sec$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}$ are not definable in the lower forcing language within the comparison class of arbitrary parameter-indexed local-object expansions fixed in \Cref{def:lower-enriched-languages}; see \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization,thm:pointwise-irreducibility}. In particular, the enriched predicates cannot, in general, be eliminated in favour of the lower forcing language.

The second group of results gives a branchwise account of gluing failure for a chosen realization prestack. Visible value classes are precisely the global connected components of the stackification, each visible branch determines a component gerbe, and, assuming global conservativity at the terminal fibre, gluing failure is equivalent to the existence of a visible branch with non-neutral component gerbe; see \Cref{thm:visible-value-components,thm:component-gerbe-decomposition,thm:gerbe-null-semantics}. Under branch constancy, the component projection admits a section exactly when every visible branch is neutral; with global conservativity this is further equivalent to surjectivity of the terminal sheafification unit; see \Cref{thm:branch-selector-neutrality}.

The third part of the paper is homological. For a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ presented on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, the universal coefficient sequence produces a homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A.
\]
We define the presentation-relative visible quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}:=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})
\]
and show that it is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible characters; see \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient}. Within the chosen presentation groupoid, this quotient is stable under equivalence of lifts, change of band trivialization, and common refinement with surjective $H_2$-transition maps; see \Cref{prop:visible-quotient-trivialization-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-lift-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-refinement-invariance,prop:presentation-groupoid}. The completely character-blind case is precisely the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ regime of the universal coefficient sequence; see \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}.

When the chosen branch presentation has free first homology, the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ ambiguity disappears. In that regime the visible quotient is the initial coefficient quotient that neutralizes the corresponding component gerbe, and gluing failure is equivalent to strict collapse of the visible quotient on every visible branch; see \Cref{thm:free-h1-quotient-completeness}. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case this yields a converse-complete visible-quotient criterion for the no-global-section phenomenon.

A complementary Mayer--Vietoris argument identifies how visible obstruction can be created globally. Whenever a chosen finite nerve presentation decomposes as
\[
N=N_1\cup N_2
\]
with $H_1(N_1\cap N_2,\mathbb Z)=0$, the hidden subgroup of the global obstruction is the sum of the hidden subgroups of the restricted classes; see \Cref{thm:mv-hidden-subgroup}. Iterating this observation shows that filtrations by $H_2$-trivial pieces leave at most a pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ residue, and no branch obstruction at all once the terminal $H_1$ is free; see \Cref{cor:piecewise-h2-trivial-residue,cor:piecewise-h2-trivial-no-glue}.

Finally, if every visible branch admits a finite presentation with vanishing degree-two cohomology, then every component gerbe is neutral. Under global conservativity, compatible local sectionability therefore collapses to global sectionability. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section criterion, while the remaining blind part is exactly the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ residue; see \Cref{thm:two-dimensionality-gluing-null}.

The novelty is structural rather than foundational. The sheaf-theoretic, gerbe-theoretic, and homological ingredients are standard, but their combination with state forcing yields a precise separation between lower-language expressibility, branchwise gluing obstructions, and the visible quotient extracted from a chosen finite presentation.

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries,sec:information-states} fix the semantic framework. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} introduces the local-object layer, relates compatible local sectionability to sheafification, and identifies visible value classes with connected components. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} develops the branchwise gerbe semantics of gluing failure. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} introduces the presentation-relative visible quotient, proves its invariance properties, establishes the free-$H_1$ completeness theorem for visible quotients, and isolates the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ blind case. Appendix~\ref{app:deferred-proofs} contains a few auxiliary proofs that are routine in the present setting.
