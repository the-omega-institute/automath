\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Local-to-global questions arise naturally once formulas are evaluated at information states rather than at single assignments. An admitted reference need not determine a global object. One may have local realizations on a cover, or even compatible local realizations, without obtaining a section at the root. The purpose of this paper is to analyze this phenomenon inside a forcing semantics and to isolate the part of the resulting obstruction that is semantically visible.

At the lower level, the forcing relation is entirely familiar. Information states are nonempty sets of assignments, and forcing for first-order formulas is just flat team semantics in the sense of Hodges and V\"a\"an\"anen \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007}; see also Beth and Kripke forcing \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,TroelstraVanDalen1988}. The novelty enters only after adjoining predicates for admissibility, local sectionability, and gluing. In that enriched language one can ask whether failure comes from the absence of local witnesses, from incompatibility of local witnesses, or from a genuine failure of descent.

This brings the discussion close to the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality initiated by Abramsky and Brandenburger and developed further in \cite{AbramskyMansfield2012,AbramskyEtAl2015,MansfieldBarbosa2013,BeerOsborne2018,BudroniCabelloGuhneKleinmannLarsson2022}. The gerbe-theoretic language used below is standard \cite{Giraud1971,Johnstone2002,Bredon1997,StacksProject}, but it is organized here around visible branches of a realization stack. Car\`u's examples of cohomological incompleteness \cite{Caru2017,Caru2018} indicate that one should distinguish the part of an obstruction seen by character channels from a residual part that remains invisible at that level.

The paper is structural in scope. We do not attempt to construct a canonical realization prestack from an arbitrary local object functor. Instead, the obstruction theory is developed relative to a chosen realization prestack equipped with an abelian band. Once such data are fixed, two questions become natural. First, which predicates introduced at the enriched level are genuinely new from the point of view of forcing? Second, for a finite branch obstruction, which quotient of the coefficient group is detected by the semantics?

Our first result answers the logical question. Sheafification characterizes compatible local sectionability, and in the constant-free equality fragment over singleton parameter states the predicates $\CompSec$, $\Sec$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}$ are not definable in the lower forcing language without the local-object predicates (\Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization,thm:pointwise-irreducibility}). Thus the enrichment is not merely a change of notation; it adds expressive strength already in the weakest comparison class considered here.

The second group of results concerns the chosen realization prestack. Visible value classes are identified with the global connected components of the realization stack (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}). Each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}). Under global conservativity at the terminal fibre, gluing failure is equivalent to the existence of visible branches whose component gerbes are all non-neutral (\Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}). If branch constancy is assumed, the component projection admits a section exactly when all visible branches are neutral (\Cref{thm:branch-selector-neutrality}); under global conservativity this is further equivalent to surjectivity of the terminal sheafification unit.

The third group of results is homological. For a fixed finite nerve presentation of a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$, the universal coefficient sequence yields the homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A.
\]
We define the presentation-relative visible quotient by
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}:=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}),
\]
and show that this is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible characters (\Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient}). The quotient is invariant under equivalence of chosen lifts, change of band trivialization, and common refinements whose induced maps on $H_2$ are surjective (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-trivialization-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-lift-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-refinement-invariance,prop:presentation-groupoid}). Complete character-blindness is equivalent to the obstruction class lying in the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part of the universal coefficient sequence (\Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}).

Under the support-presheaf identification and a chosen globally conservative realization prestack, the case of a single visible branch recovers the familiar compatible-family/global-section criterion of Abramsky and Brandenburger (\Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}). In that situation the remaining blind residue is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part. The $S^2$-, $\mathbb{R}P^2$-, and torus-type examples illustrate, respectively, a nontrivial visible quotient, a completely character-blind obstruction, and an end-to-end realization of the full hypothesis package.

For ease of reference, we isolate the standing hypotheses used at various points:
\begin{description}[labelwidth=2.6em]
\item[A1] (\emph{Chosen lift}) Visible branches are interpreted via a chosen realization prestack with fixed band (\S\ref{sec:null-decomposition}).
\item[A2] (\emph{Global conservativity}) Comparisons between stack-global objects and the original local-object functor assume global conservativity at the terminal fibre.
\item[A3] (\emph{Finite presentation}) The homological results of \S\ref{sec:homological-visibility} are stated for finite nerve presentations with compatible band trivializations.
\item[A4] (\emph{Branch constancy}) The decomposition and branch-selector results use the branch-constancy condition of \Cref{def:branch-constancy}.
\end{description}

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries,sec:information-states} fix the semantic framework and the conservative-extension formalism. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} introduces local objects, sheafification, visible value classes, and the logical separation between the lower and enriched languages. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} interprets visible branches by means of component gerbes and branch obstruction classes. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} develops the presentation-relative visible quotient, its invariance properties, the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ blind case, and the comparison with sheaf-theoretic contextuality. Routine deferred proofs are collected in Appendix~\ref{app:deferred-proofs}.
