\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

The passage from single assignments to information states raises a local-to-global question. An admitted reference may admit local realizations on a cover, or even compatible local realizations, without admitting a global realization at the root. The aim of this paper is to analyse that failure of descent inside a forcing semantics and to isolate the part of the resulting obstruction that is visible at the level of character channels.

At the lower layer, the forcing relation is entirely familiar. Information states are nonempty sets of assignments, and forcing for first-order formulas is flat team semantics in the sense of Hodges and V\"a\"an\"anen \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007}; compare also Beth and Kripke forcing \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,TroelstraVanDalen1988}. Information states also play a central role in inquisitive and dependence-based semantics; see, for example, \cite{CiardelliGroenendijsenRoelofsen2019,CiardelliGrilletti2022,AlbertGradel2022}. The genuinely new features appear only after adjoining predicates for admissibility, local sectionability, and gluing. At that point one can distinguish three different reasons for failure: there may be no local realizations at all, local realizations may exist but be incompatible, or compatible local realizations may fail to glue.

This places the discussion close to the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality initiated by Abramsky and Brandenburger and developed further in \cite{AbramskyMansfield2012,AbramskyEtAl2015,MansfieldBarbosa2013,BeerOsborne2018,BudroniCabelloGuhneKleinmannLarsson2022}. The gerbe-theoretic language used below is standard \cite{Giraud1971,Johnstone2002,Bredon1997,StacksProject}; what matters here is that, once a realization prestack with fixed abelian band has been chosen, the obstruction theory becomes branchwise. Visible value classes correspond to connected components of the chosen realization stack, and each such component carries its own gerbe and its own degree-two obstruction class.

The paper is deliberately conditional in scope. We do not prove that an arbitrary local object functor admits a canonical lift to a realization prestack, nor do we claim uniqueness of such lifts. Instead, we ask what follows from a chosen lift once it has been verified to be globally conservative at the terminal fibre and, in the homological part of the argument, once a finite presentation has been fixed. This is also the point at which the local-object layer becomes genuinely stronger than the lower forcing language.

At the technical level, the site-theoretic and gerbe-theoretic inputs are standard. The paper does not claim new sheafification theorems, new classification results for gerbes, or an intrinsic obstruction theory attached to every local object functor. The contribution is instead to isolate the semantic hypotheses under which those standard tools apply in the present forcing setting and to identify, for a chosen finite presentation, the quotient $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$ as the exact part of a branch obstruction seen by the admissible character channels.

Our first main result is logical. Compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification, and in the constant-free equality fragment over singleton parameter states none of the predicates $\CompSec$, $\Sec$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}$ is definable by a lower-language formula over the comparison class of parameter-indexed local-object expansions fixed in \Cref{def:lower-enriched-languages}; see \Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization,thm:pointwise-irreducibility}. In particular, the enrichment by local-object predicates is not eliminable in that comparison class.

The second group of results concerns the chosen realization prestack. Visible value classes are precisely the global connected components of the stackification, each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe, and, under global conservativity at the terminal fibre, gluing failure is equivalent to the existence of visible branches all of whose component gerbes are non-neutral; see \Cref{thm:visible-value-components,thm:component-gerbe-decomposition,thm:gerbe-null-semantics}. If branch constancy is added, the component projection admits a section exactly when every visible branch is neutral, equivalently, under global conservativity, when the terminal sheafification unit is surjective; see \Cref{thm:branch-selector-neutrality}.

The third part of the paper is homological. For a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ presented on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, the universal coefficient sequence yields a map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A.
\]
We define the presentation-relative visible quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}:=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})
\]
and identify it with the quotient detected by the class-admissible characters; see \Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient}. Within the chosen presentation groupoid, this quotient is stable under equivalence of chosen lifts, change of band trivialization, and common refinement with surjective $H_2$-transition maps; see \Cref{prop:visible-quotient-trivialization-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-lift-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-refinement-invariance,prop:presentation-groupoid}. The completely character-blind case is the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ regime of the universal coefficient sequence; see \Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}.

A final theorem shows that, in the chosen finite-presentation setting, gluing-nullity is a genuinely two-dimensional phenomenon. If every visible branch admits a finite presentation with vanishing degree-two cohomology, then every component gerbe is neutral. Under global conservativity, compatible local sectionability then collapses to global sectionability. If branch constancy is added, one also obtains a branch selector and surjectivity of the terminal sheafification unit; see \Cref{thm:two-dimensionality-gluing-null}. In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger criterion, and the remaining blind part of the obstruction is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residue.

The standing assumptions used in the branchwise and homological analysis are collected in \S\ref{subsec:scope-chosen-lift}. The $S^2$- and $\mathbb{R}P^2$-type examples illustrate, respectively, a visible and a completely character-blind obstruction at the level of finite nerve presentations, while the torus-type construction is synthetic and is included only as a consistency witness for the chosen-lift formalism.

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries,sec:information-states} fix the semantic framework and the conservative-extension formalism. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} introduces local objects, sheafification, visible value classes, and the logical separation between the lower and enriched languages. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} develops the branchwise gerbe semantics of gluing failure. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} defines the presentation-relative visible quotient, proves its invariance properties, isolates the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ blind case, and compares the resulting picture with sheaf-theoretic contextuality. Routine deferred proofs are collected in Appendix~\ref{app:deferred-proofs}.
