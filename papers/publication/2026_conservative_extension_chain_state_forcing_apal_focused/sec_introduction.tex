\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Information-state semantics becomes relevant as soon as one has to work with partial realizations. A reference may be admissible without yet naming a global object; local witnesses may exist and agree on overlaps without extending globally; and the failure of extension may itself carry nontrivial obstruction data. The purpose of this paper is to treat these phenomena at the level of forcing over information states and to isolate, under explicit realization hypotheses, the part of a gluing obstruction that is semantically visible.

At the lower level the forcing semantics is familiar. Information states are nonempty sets of assignments, and forcing is the usual flat team semantics of first-order formulas in the sense of Hodges and V\"a\"an\"anen \cite{Hodges1997,Vaananen2007}; compare also Beth and Kripke forcing \cite{Beth1956,Kripke1965,TroelstraVanDalen1988}. For recent APAL work around team and inquisitive semantics see \cite{AlbertGradel2022,CiardelliGrilletti2022}. What matters here is the passage from this flat layer to a local-object language capable of expressing admissibility, compatible local sectionability, and genuine failure of gluing.

This brings the paper close to the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality. The distinction between compatible families and global sections, introduced by Abramsky and Brandenburger and sharpened by later cohomological refinements, provides the relevant local-to-global background \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011,AbramskyMansfield2012,AbramskyEtAl2015}. Related extendability and geometric viewpoints may be found in \cite{MansfieldBarbosa2013,BeerOsborne2018,BudroniCabelloGuhneKleinmannLarsson2022}. The gerbe-theoretic language used below is standard \cite{Giraud1971,Johnstone2002,Bredon1997,StacksProject}; the point is to organize it around visible branches of a realization stack. Car\`u's examples of cohomological incompleteness \cite{Caru2017,Caru2018} then become the natural testing ground for the blind part of the theory.

The obstruction-theoretic statements are made relative to a gluing-sensitive realization prestack with fixed abelian band. This hypothesis is part of the data. We do not derive such a lift from an arbitrary set-valued presheaf. Once it is in place, however, one may ask a concrete question: given a finite abelian branch obstruction, which quotient of the coefficient group is already detected by the semantics, and which part survives only as a blind residue?

The first main point is logical. Sheafification characterizes compatible local sectionability, and in the constant-free equality fragment on singleton parameter states the predicates $\CompSec$, $\Sec$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}$ are not definable in the lower forcing language without the local-object predicates (\Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization,thm:pointwise-irreducibility}). Thus the enrichment is not merely notational: within the stated comparison class it adds genuinely new expressive power.

The second main point is semantic. Visible value classes are precisely the global connected components of the realization stack (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}). Each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}), and gluing failure amounts to the existence of visible branches none of whose component gerbes is neutral (\Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}). Under branch constancy the realization stack splits as the coproduct of its component gerbes (\Cref{cor:branch-decomposition}), and the component projection admits a section exactly when all visible branches are neutral (\Cref{thm:branch-selector-neutrality}). With global conservativity at the terminal fibre, this is equivalent to surjectivity of the sheafification unit on global objects, so a gluing-null state cannot support a canonical branch selector.

The third main point is homological. For a finite abelian branch obstruction $\omega$ on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, the homological evaluation map
\[
\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}:H_2(N(\mathcal U),\mathbb Z)\to A
\]
determines the presentation-relative visible quotient
\[
A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\omega}:=\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}),
\]
and this is exactly the quotient detected by the class-admissible character channels (\Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient}). The quotient is invariant under equivalence of lifts, change of band trivialization, and common refinements with surjective maps on $H_2$ (\Cref{prop:visible-quotient-trivialization-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-lift-invariance,prop:visible-quotient-refinement-invariance,prop:presentation-groupoid}). Complete character-blindness is equivalent to the obstruction class lying entirely in the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part of the universal coefficient sequence (\Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}).

In the unique-branch separated case, this recovers the familiar no-global-section picture. Under the additional hypothesis of a compatible realization prestack, the present semantics agrees with the Abramsky--Brandenburger account, while the remaining blind residue is identified exactly with the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-part (\Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}). The torus-type construction of \Cref{ex:end-to-end}, together with the $S^2$-, $\mathbb{R}P^2$-, and Kripke-style examples, shows both the visible and the blind behavior.

The standing assumptions used later are the following.
\begin{description}[labelwidth=2.6em]
\item[A1] (\emph{Chosen lift}) Visible branches are interpreted using a gluing-sensitive realization prestack with fixed band (\S\ref{sec:null-decomposition}).
\item[A2] (\emph{Global conservativity}) Comparisons between stack-global objects and the original local-object functor assume global conservativity at the terminal fibre.
\item[A3] (\emph{Finite presentation}) The homological results of \S\ref{sec:homological-visibility} are stated for finite nerve presentations with compatible band trivializations.
\item[A4] (\emph{Branch constancy}) The decomposition result \Cref{cor:branch-decomposition} assumes the branch-constancy condition of \Cref{def:branch-constancy}.
\end{description}

The paper is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:preliminaries,sec:information-states} fix the lower forcing layer and the conservative-extension framework. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} introduces local objects, visible classes, and the logical separation between the lower and enriched languages. \Cref{sec:gerbe-obstruction} interprets visible branches in terms of component gerbes and branch obstruction classes. \Cref{sec:homological-visibility} develops the presentation-relative visible quotient, its invariance properties, the pure-$\operatorname{Ext}$ blind cases, and the comparison with sheaf-theoretic contextuality. Routine proofs needed later are collected in Appendix~\ref{app:deferred-proofs}.
