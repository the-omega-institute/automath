\section{Conservative extensions}\label{sec:preliminaries}

We begin with the abstract notion of conservative extension used throughout the paper. Its role is to separate the lower forcing language from the enriched language of local objects without changing the meaning of formulas already present at the lower level.

\begin{definition}[Logic layer]\label{def:logic-layer}
A \emph{logic layer} is a tuple
\[
\mathbb{L}=(\Sigma,\mathrm{Form},\Mod,\St,\Vdash,\models),
\]
where $\Sigma$ is a signature, $\mathrm{Form}$ is the class of well-formed $\Sigma$-formulas, $\Mod$ is a class of admissible models, $\St$ assigns to each $M\in\Mod$ a nonempty set $\St(M)$ of states, $\Vdash$ is a forcing relation on model-state pairs, and $\models$ is the induced consequence relation
\[
\Gamma\models\varphi
\quad:\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\forall M\in\Mod\;\forall p\in\St(M)\,
\bigl(M,p\Vdash\Gamma \Rightarrow M,p\Vdash\varphi\bigr).
\]
\end{definition}

Thus forcing is taken as primitive, while consequence is induced by quantifying over models and states.

\begin{definition}[Conservative extension]\label{def:conservative-extension}
Let
\[
\mathbb{L}_m=(\Sigma_m,\mathrm{Form}_m,\Mod_m,\St_m,\Vdash_m,\models_m)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathbb{L}_n=(\Sigma_n,\mathrm{Form}_n,\Mod_n,\St_n,\Vdash_n,\models_n)
\]
with $m<n$. We say that $\mathbb{L}_n$ is a \emph{conservative extension} of $\mathbb{L}_m$, written $\mathbb{L}_m\preceq\mathbb{L}_n$, if there exist
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
\item a language embedding $e_{m,n}:\Sigma_m\hookrightarrow\Sigma_n$,
\item a forgetful functor $U_{n,m}:\Mod_n\to\Mod_m$,
\item for every $M\in\Mod_n$, a state projection
\[
\pi^M_{n,m}:\St_n(M)\to \St_m\bigl(U_{n,m}(M)\bigr),
\]
\end{enumerate}
such that for every $\varphi\in \mathrm{Form}_m$, every $M\in\Mod_n$, and every $p\in\St_n(M)$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:conservative-extension}
M,p\Vdash_n \varphi
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
U_{n,m}(M),\pi^M_{n,m}(p)\Vdash_m \varphi.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

In particular, a conservative extension may enlarge both the language and the state space, but formulas from the lower layer retain their original forcing behaviour after the additional structure is forgotten.

\begin{proposition}[Composition of conservative extensions]\label{prop:conservative-composition}
If $\mathbb{L}_\ell\preceq\mathbb{L}_m$ and $\mathbb{L}_m\preceq\mathbb{L}_n$, then $\mathbb{L}_\ell\preceq\mathbb{L}_n$.
\leanverified{paper_conservative_extension_composition_seeds}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
See Appendix~\ref{app:deferred-proofs}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Finite-chain invariance]\label{cor:whole-chain-invariance}
If
\[
\mathbb{L}_0\preceq\mathbb{L}_1\preceq\cdots\preceq\mathbb{L}_n,
\]
then every formula belonging to a lower layer has the same forcing behaviour in every later layer after projection.
\leanverified{paper_conservative_extension_whole_chain_invariance_seeds}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Iterate \Cref{prop:conservative-composition} along the chain.
\end{proof}

In the present setting, the singleton embedding $i_M:\Val_M(\Gamma)\to \St(M)$ from \Cref{def:singleton-embedding} identifies ordinary satisfaction with forcing at singleton states by \Cref{prop:singleton-conservativity}. This passage is not itself conservative in the above sense, since an arbitrary information state does not project to a single valuation. The genuinely conservative step is the passage from the lower forcing language $\mathbb{L}_1$ to the enriched local-object language $\mathbb{L}_2$. By \Cref{cor:whole-chain-invariance}, the higher layer adds expressive resources without altering the forcing behaviour of lower-layer formulas.
