Dear Editors,

We submit for your consideration the manuscript "Homological Visibility
of Gluing Obstructions in a State-Forcing Semantics."  The paper
addresses the following question: given a presheaf carrying a finite
abelian gluing obstruction, what part of that obstruction is
semantically visible -- that is, detectable by admissible characters --
without enlarging the state space?

The answer is developed within a four-layer conservative extension
chain that stratifies the passage from pointwise semantics through
information-state forcing to local objects and refinement dynamics.
Each layer strictly extends the one below and preserves the forcing
behavior of all formulas already expressible at lower layers.  The
first main result (Theorem A) is a forcing-necessity separation
theorem: compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are
undefinable in the information-state forcing language, so the
local-object enrichment is logically necessary.  The second result
(Theorem B) establishes a branched gerbe semantics in which each
visible value class determines a banded component gerbe, and
gluing-level absence holds exactly when every component gerbe is
non-neutral.  The third result (Theorem C) applies the universal
coefficient theorem to define the intrinsic visible quotient as the
cokernel of a homological evaluation map; the blind residual -- the
part of the obstruction invisible to every admissible character -- is a
pure Ext-type class.  In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting,
this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and
identifies the precise mechanism behind cohomological blind cases.

APAL is our preferred venue because the paper draws on several
traditions that the journal has helped develop.  The conservative
extension chain builds on Kripke's semantics for intuitionistic logic,
Beth's semantic tableaux, and Fitting's model-theoretic forcing.  The
gerbe-theoretic layer applies the Giraud classification in a
site-specific way that connects to the topos-theoretic forcing of
Tierney and Johnstone; we discuss the relationship explicitly in the
conclusion, including the open question of whether the four-layer chain
admits a topos-internal reformulation via Lawvere--Tierney topologies.
The combination of Heyting-valued forcing separation with homological
algebra over finite Cech nerves lies squarely within the journal's
scope in categorical logic and sheaf-theoretic methods.

The manuscript is 37 pages in amsart format, contains
15 references, and is self-contained: all definitions, hypotheses, and
proofs are given in full.  No external data or computational
verification is required.  Results on multi-observer extensions are
deferred to a sequel and are not needed for the present paper.

Thank you for considering this submission.

Sincerely,
The Authors
