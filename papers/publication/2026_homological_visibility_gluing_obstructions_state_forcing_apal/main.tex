\title[Homological Visibility of Gluing Obstructions]{Homological Visibility of Gluing Obstructions in a State-Forcing Semantics}
\author{}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
Within the typed reference-aware lower comparison language, singleton-realization evaluation does not detect the local-to-global gap between compatible local families and global sections. We study that gap in a five-layer, four-extension-step conservative chain, from pointwise semantics through reference-aware information-state forcing to local objects, branched obstruction data, and refinement dynamics, formulated over explicit sites on already admitted reference classes. The lower comparison language is already reference-aware: the sort $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and predicate $\Adm_s$ belong to $\Sigma_1$, while the local-object predicates belong only to $\Sigma_2$. The first main result is a restricted logical separation theorem: compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification, but the predicates governing gluing failure are not definable by parameter-free formulas of the $\mathbb L_1$ fragment $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$ over local-object functor expansions with fixed lower reduct, state, admitted reference, site, and covers. The gerbe layer is presented conditionally rather than as an unconditional realization theorem: assuming a gluing-sensitive realization prestack, global conservativity at the terminal fibre, and a \v{C}ech-to-derived comparison family of covers, each visible value class determines a banded component gerbe and gluing-level absence amounts to a nonempty set of visible branches together with non-neutrality of every component gerbe. The main quantitative contribution is the semantic interpretation of the homological evaluation map: for a finite-nerve presentation with trivialized finite abelian band, the semantically detectable quotient is $\operatorname{coker}(\mathrm{ev}_{\omega})$, while a nonzero class with $\mathrm{ev}_{\omega}=0$ is exactly a pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-type blind obstruction. Under the same explicit realization hypotheses, the unique-branch support-presheaf setting yields a formal comparison with the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture.
\end{abstract}
