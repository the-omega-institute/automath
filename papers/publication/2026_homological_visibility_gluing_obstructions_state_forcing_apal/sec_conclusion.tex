\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

\subsection*{Summary of results}

Under the stated hypotheses, this paper addresses the following question: given an admitted reference carrying a finite abelian gluing obstruction, what part of that obstruction is semantically visible without enlarging the state space?

Theorem~A shows that, under its explicit syntactic hypotheses, compatible local sectionability and the gluing predicates are not definable by parameter-free formulas of the typed reference-aware fragment $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$. This fragment already contains reference variables and admissibility, but not local-object predicates. The separating models keep the lower reduct and admitted reference fixed and vary only the local-object expansion, so the obstruction is located exactly at the $\mathbb L_1$--$\mathbb L_2$ boundary. The local-object enrichment is therefore logically necessary within that precise comparison class rather than a notational convenience.

Theorem~B provides a conditional gerbe-theoretic framework rather than an unconditional realization theorem. Under gluing-sensitive realization, global conservativity, and \v{C}ech-to-derived comparison hypotheses, visible value classes are the global connected components of the realization stack; each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe; and gluing-level absence is characterized by the existence of visible branches together with the non-neutrality of all component gerbes. The conditional package is not left abstract: \Cref{thm:good-cover-gerbe-realization} verifies it for banded gerbes on paracompact good-cover sites and produces a unique-branch support-presheaf class inside the paper.

Theorem~C is the main quantitative result under finite-nerve hypotheses with trivialized finite abelian band. The image of the homological evaluation map is the intrinsic hidden subgroup, the quotient by that image is the intrinsic visible quotient, and the blind residual---the part invisible to every admissible character---is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-type regime. In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting, this yields a formal comparison with the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and identifies the precise mechanism behind the cohomological blind cases highlighted by Car\`u \cite{Caru2017}, again only under the same explicit realizability assumptions.

\subsection*{External significance}

The connection to contextuality gives the results an anchor outside pure sheaf theory. The forcing-necessity theorem shows that the passage from reference-aware state forcing to local objects is a logical enrichment in the precise sense that gluing predicates are invisible to the typed lower fragment fixed in Theorem~A. The branched gerbe semantics replaces the binary local-to-global defect of standard contextuality analysis with a graded, branch-indexed account, but only after the additional realization hypotheses are verified. The homological visibility theorem then turns the universal coefficient sequence into a tool for describing the boundary between what is semantically accessible and what remains hidden.

\subsection*{Relation to topos-theoretic forcing}

The five-layer chain developed here shares its conceptual ancestry with topos-theoretic forcing as developed by Tierney, Johnstone, and Mac Lane--Moerdijk \cite{Tierney1972,Johnstone2002,MacLaneMoerdijk1994}. In the topos-theoretic tradition, forcing is modeled by the internal logic of a sheaf topos, and conservativity results take the form of geometric morphism adjunctions that preserve logical structure. The present framework is narrower in one respect---it works with a fixed site rather than varying the ambient topos---but broader in another: the explicit stratification into numbered layers with explicit forgetful functors and state projections (\Cref{def:conservative-extension}) makes the conservativity claims syntactically verifiable at each boundary, rather than relying on the general preservation properties of geometric morphisms. The gerbe-theoretic layer, in particular, uses the Giraud classification in a site-specific rather than topos-internal way, which permits the finite combinatorial analysis of visibility that drives Theorem~C. Whether the full five-layer chain admits a natural topos-internal reformulation---with each layer corresponding to a subterminal object or a Lawvere--Tierney topology on a single ambient topos---remains an open question that would connect the present results to the broader categorical semantics programme.

\subsection*{Open problems}

Two directions remain open. First, the finite-state complexity upper bounds in the appendix have no matching lower bounds; determining the exact complexity of the support and minimal-support problems remains a supplementary combinatorial question rather than part of the main proof chain. Second, the present paper treats a single admitted reference at one state and restricts the homological analysis to trivialized bands on finite covers. Extending the theory to local coefficient systems, and verifying the realization hypotheses in broader semantic model classes such as empirical support presheaves, remains future work. The multi-observer extension---indexed families of references with coupled states and observer symmetry---is likewise deferred to a sequel.
