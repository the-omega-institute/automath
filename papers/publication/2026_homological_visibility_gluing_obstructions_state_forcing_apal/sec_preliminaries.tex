\section{Preliminaries: Conservative Extensions}\label{sec:preliminaries}

The later sections use only one abstract idea: a higher semantic layer may add coordinates or structure without changing the behavior of formulas already present below. The definitions in this section isolate that invariance principle and nothing more.

\subsection*{Standing notation}
States are denoted by $p,q,u$, and admitted references by $r:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ with associated class $a=[r]_p$. The site on admitted reference classes at a state and sort is $\mathcal C_{p,s}$, the local object functor is $F_{p,s}$, the visible value classes of a reference are $\Vis_{p,s}(r)$, and branch obstruction classes are denoted by $\omega$ or $\omega_v$. Subscripts such as $\mathrm{vis}$, $\mathrm{br}$, and $\mathrm{unif}$ consistently indicate visible, branch-sensitive, and branch-uniform quotients.

\begin{definition}[Logic layer]\label{def:logic-layer}
A \emph{logic layer} is a tuple
\[
\mathbb{L}=(\Sigma,\mathrm{Form},\Mod,\St,\Vdash,\models),
\]
where $\Sigma$ is a signature, $\mathrm{Form}$ is the class of well-formed $\Sigma$-formulas, $\Mod$ is a class of admissible models, $\St$ assigns to every $M\in\Mod$ a nonempty set $\St(M)$ of states, $\Vdash$ is a forcing relation on model-state pairs, and $\models$ is the induced consequence relation defined by
\[
\Gamma\models\varphi
\quad:\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\forall M\in\Mod\;\forall p\in\St(M)\,
\bigl(M,p\Vdash\Gamma \Rightarrow M,p\Vdash\varphi\bigr).
\]
\end{definition}

The forcing relation is the primitive semantic notion. Consequence is derived from uniform preservation of forcing across all admissible models and states.

\begin{definition}[Conservative extension]\label{def:conservative-extension}
Let
\[
\mathbb{L}_m=(\Sigma_m,\mathrm{Form}_m,\Mod_m,\St_m,\Vdash_m,\models_m)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathbb{L}_n=(\Sigma_n,\mathrm{Form}_n,\Mod_n,\St_n,\Vdash_n,\models_n)
\]
with $m<n$. We say that $\mathbb{L}_n$ is a \emph{conservative extension} of $\mathbb{L}_m$, written $\mathbb{L}_m\preceq\mathbb{L}_n$, if there exist
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
\item a language embedding $e_{m,n}:\Sigma_m\hookrightarrow\Sigma_n$,
\item a forgetful functor $U_{n,m}:\Mod_n\to\Mod_m$,
\item for every $M\in\Mod_n$, a state projection
\[
\pi^M_{n,m}:\St_n(M)\to \St_m\bigl(U_{n,m}(M)\bigr),
\]
\end{enumerate}
such that for every $\varphi\in \mathrm{Form}_m$, every $M\in\Mod_n$, and every $p\in\St_n(M)$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:conservative-extension}
M,p\Vdash_n \varphi
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
U_{n,m}(M),\pi^M_{n,m}(p)\Vdash_m \varphi.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

Thus a higher layer may add expressive power, state coordinates, or geometric constraints, but it may not alter the forcing behavior of formulas that already belonged to a lower layer.

\begin{proposition}[Composition of conservative extensions]\label{prop:conservative-composition}
If $\mathbb{L}_\ell\preceq\mathbb{L}_m$ and $\mathbb{L}_m\preceq\mathbb{L}_n$, then $\mathbb{L}_\ell\preceq\mathbb{L}_n$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Compose the language embeddings and forgetful functors:
\[
e_{\ell,n}:=e_{m,n}\circ e_{\ell,m},
\qquad
U_{n,\ell}:=U_{m,\ell}\circ U_{n,m}.
\]
For every $M\in\Mod_n$, define
\[
\pi^M_{n,\ell}:=
\pi^{\,U_{n,m}(M)}_{m,\ell}\circ \pi^M_{n,m}.
\]
If $\varphi\in\mathrm{Form}_\ell$ and $p\in\St_n(M)$, then
\[
M,p\Vdash_n\varphi
\Longleftrightarrow
U_{n,m}(M),\pi^M_{n,m}(p)\Vdash_m\varphi
\Longleftrightarrow
U_{n,\ell}(M),\pi^M_{n,\ell}(p)\Vdash_\ell\varphi,
\]
by two applications of \Cref{def:conservative-extension}. This is exactly the required conservativity condition.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Finite-chain invariance]\label{cor:whole-chain-invariance}
If
\[
\mathbb{L}_0\preceq\mathbb{L}_1\preceq\cdots\preceq\mathbb{L}_n,
\]
then every formula belonging to any lower layer keeps the same forcing behavior when interpreted in any later layer and projected back down.
\leanverified{paper_conservative_extension_whole_chain_invariance_apal}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Apply \Cref{prop:conservative-composition} repeatedly along the chain.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The reference-aware local-object boundary}\label{subsec:reference-aware-boundary}

The comparison used in Theorem~A is not the comparison between the original pointwise language and a later language containing references. It is the comparison between an already reference-aware information-state language and its local-object enrichment. The following ledger is fixed before any site is chosen:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.14\linewidth}p{0.38\linewidth}p{0.34\linewidth}}
\hline
layer & symbols present at that layer & symbols not yet present\\
\hline
$\Sigma_0$ & underlying pointwise symbols & $\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s$ and all local-object symbols\\
$\Sigma_1$ & information-state forcing symbols together with $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$ & site, cover, functor, and sectionability symbols\\
$\Sigma_2$ & the whole $\Sigma_1$ signature plus sites on already admitted reference classes, covers, local object functors, and sectionability predicates & branch gerbes, obstruction classes, and refinement dynamics\\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Thus the phrase ``site on admitted reference classes'' is a semantic construction over the $\Sigma_1$ set of admitted classes; it is not a delayed introduction of the reference sort or of the admissibility predicate. This ledger is the typing convention used by all later references to $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$.
In particular, $\mathrm{Form}_1$ is not the reference-free pointwise formula class. It is the formula class generated by the reference-aware signature $\Sigma_1$, so formulas with displayed context
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s
\]
are ordinary $\mathrm{Form}_1$ formulas whenever their nonlogical symbols lie in $\Sigma_1$. The smaller notation $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$ is used later only to impose the additional restrictions relevant to Theorem~A: no parameters and no local-object symbols.

The constructions used later fit into the finite chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0
\preceq
\mathbb{L}_1
\preceq
\mathbb{L}_2
\preceq
\mathbb{L}_3
\preceq
\mathbb{L}_4.
\]
Here $\mathbb{L}_0$ is the underlying pointwise semantics, $\mathbb{L}_1$ is the reference-aware information-state forcing layer, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local-object geometry over classes that have already been cut out by the $\mathbb L_1$ reference domain and the $\mathbb L_1$ admissibility predicate, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds structural absence and gluing-obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ adds refinement dynamics. The phrase ``already admitted'' is literal: reference variables and admissibility tests are fixed before the local-object layer is introduced. Thus the boundary relevant to Theorem~A is
\[
\text{reference-aware }\mathbb L_1\quad\leadsto\quad\text{local-object-aware }\mathbb L_2.
\]
The type judgments used later are therefore already lower-layer judgments:
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash \Adm_s(x)\in\mathrm{Form}_1,
\]
whereas the local-object atoms
\[
\LocSec_s(x),\qquad \CompSec_s(x),\qquad \Sec_s(x)
\]
first become formulas only after the $\Sigma_2$ increment has been added. This is the formal boundary used in Theorem~A.
By \Cref{cor:whole-chain-invariance}, the passage up the chain is cumulative rather than revisionary: each later layer preserves the forcing behavior already fixed below.

The next proposition is the numbered ledger used by Theorem~A. Its first item places the reference sort and admissibility predicate in the lower signature; its second item is the complete $\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1$ increment. Later phrases such as ``admitted reference class'' always refer back to the first item before the local-object increment is added.

\begin{proposition}[Theorem A boundary typing: references are lower-layer data]\label{prop:theorem-a-boundary-typing}
Fix the family $S_{\mathrm{ref}}$ of referenceable sorts used in the local-object construction.
At the comparison boundary used in Theorem~A, the passage
\[
\mathbb L_1\leadsto\mathbb L_2
\]
has the following typed signature form. This proposition is the authoritative boundary contract for the paper's lower-fragment comparison: it is not inferred from the later site construction, and the site construction is not allowed to move any reference symbol across the boundary. In particular, the phrase ``site on admitted reference classes'' always means a site placed on classes already formed inside the $\Sigma_1$ reduct; it never means that references or admissibility are added at $\Sigma_2$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=3em]
\item The lower signature is explicitly
\[
\Sigma_1
=
\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{st}}
\cup
\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\},
\]
where $\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{st}}$ denotes the remaining information-state forcing symbols. Hence the reference sort and admissibility predicate are lower-layer symbols:
\[
\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\in\Sigma_1
\quad\text{for every }s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}.
\]
Thus
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash \Adm_s(x)\in\mathrm{Form}_1
\]
is already a judgment of the reference-aware information-state language. More generally,
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash\varphi(x)\in\mathrm{Form}_1
\]
is well typed exactly when $\varphi$ is a formula over this $\Sigma_1$ signature. No local-object symbol is needed to type the displayed reference variable or the atom $\Adm_s(x)$.
\item The later local-object signature is
\[
\Sigma_2=\Sigma_1\cup\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}},
\qquad
\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}}
=
\{\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},
\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s
\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}.
\]
Therefore the actual $\Sigma_2$ increment contains only local-object data:
\[
\Delta_{1,2}:=\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1
=
\{\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},
\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s
\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}.
\]
This item is the complete list of new symbols at the $\mathbb L_1\leadsto\mathbb L_2$ boundary. The full signature $\Sigma_2$ of course still contains $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$, but only because $\Sigma_1\subseteq\Sigma_2$; those symbols are inherited lower symbols, not members of the increment $\Delta_{1,2}$.
The words ``site on admitted reference classes'' in this display mean a site whose objects have already been selected by the $\Sigma_1$ predicate $\Adm_s$ from the $\Sigma_1$ domain $\mathsf{Ref}_s$. They do not introduce a new sort, a new admissibility predicate, or a new interpretation of either one.
In particular,
\[
\Delta_{1,2}\cap
\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}
=\varnothing .
\]
This is an equality of increments, not merely a list of examples: every symbol of $\Sigma_2$ used to define local sectionability is either already in $\Sigma_1$ or appears in the displayed local-object list. Thus there is no reading under which $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ or $\Adm_s$ belongs to $\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1$.
Consequently a formula or theorem that uses a variable $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and the atom $\Adm_s(x)$ is using retained $\Sigma_1$ data; it is not quantifying over, or appealing to, any sort or predicate introduced by the local-object increment.
\item Equivalently, the boundary has the following no-shift table:
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
\text{symbol or judgment} & \text{belongs to }\Sigma_1 & \text{new in }\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1\\ \hline
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s & \text{yes} & \text{no}\\
\Adm_s(x) & \text{yes} & \text{no}\\
\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s} & \text{no} & \text{yes}\\
\LocSec_s(x),\CompSec_s(x),\Sec_s(x) & \text{no} & \text{yes.}
\end{array}
\]
\item The lower comparison fragment is therefore the typed reference-aware fragment
\[
\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)\subseteq\mathrm{Form}_1
\]
whose displayed free variable may have sort $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and whose atoms may include $\Adm_s(x)$, but which contains none of
\[
\LocSec_s,\qquad \CompSec_s,\qquad \Sec_s,
\qquad \Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}.
\]
\end{enumerate}
Consequently every formula quantified over in Theorem~A is well typed before local-object geometry is added, and every predicate separated by that theorem belongs to the later local-object side of the boundary. The comparison is therefore between a $\Sigma_1$ formula evaluated at a retained semantic reference element and a $\Sigma_2$ target predicate evaluated in a local-object expansion of the same $\Sigma_1$ reduct.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Clause \textup{(i)} defines $\Sigma_1$ as the information-state signature together with the displayed reference sorts and admissibility predicates. Therefore the formula class $\mathrm{Form}_1$ is already generated over a signature containing $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$, and the judgment $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash\Adm_s(x)\in\mathrm{Form}_1$ is immediate. Clause \textup{(ii)} defines $\Sigma_2$ by adjoining the local-object part $\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}}$ to this already reference-aware signature; subtracting $\Sigma_1$ leaves only sites, covers, functors, and sectionability predicates. Hence neither $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ nor $\Adm_s$ is introduced by the $\mathbb L_1\leadsto\mathbb L_2$ step. The word ``admitted'' in the description of the objects of $\mathcal C_{p,s}$ refers back to the truth of the already available $\Sigma_1$ formula $\Adm_s(x)$, so it cannot add an admissibility symbol at $\Sigma_2$. Clause \textup{(iii)} is just this separation written as a typing table. Clause \textup{(iv)} follows immediately: a formula over the lower signature may use the symbols of $\Sigma_1$, including $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$, but cannot contain symbols that occur only in $\Delta_{1,2}$. Since $\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}(x)$ abbreviates $\Adm_s(x)\land\CompSec_s(x)\land\neg\Sec_s(x)$, it is also outside the lower fragment.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Lower reference data and admitted classes]\label{def:lower-reference-data}
Fix a referenceable sort $s$. The reference-aware layer $\mathbb L_1$ contains the sort $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and the unary predicate $\Adm_s$ before any site, cover, local object functor, or sectionability predicate is present. For a $\Sigma_1$ model $N$ and a state $p\in\St_1(N)$, define
\[
d\equiv_p e
\quad:\Longleftrightarrow\quad
N,p[d/x,e/y]\Vdash_1 x=y
\]
for semantic elements $d,e\in D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^N$, where $x,y:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ are fresh variables. The lower-layer admitted reference classes are
\[
\mathcal R_s^1(N,p):=
\{[d]_p\mid d\in D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^N
\text{ and }N,p\Vdash_1\Adm_s(d)\}.
\]
This construction uses only $\Sigma_1$ data: the reference domain, equality, the information state, and the admissibility predicate. Later phrases such as ``site on admitted reference classes'' always mean a site placed on the already formed set $\mathcal R_s^1(N,p)$. They do not introduce $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ or $\Adm_s$ at $\Sigma_2$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[No-reference-increment signature boundary]\label{prop:no-reference-increment}
Fix a family $S_{\mathrm{ref}}$ of referenceable sorts. At the boundary used in Theorem~A the passage
\[
\mathbb L_1\leadsto\mathbb L_2
\]
is a local-object increment over an already reference-aware lower language. More precisely, the signature placement is part of the data of the chain and is as follows.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=3em]
\item The lower signature already contains the reference sorts and admissibility predicates:
\[
\Sigma_1
=
\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{st}}
\cup
\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\},
\]
where $\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{st}}$ denotes the remaining information-state forcing symbols. Hence the symbols
\[
\mathsf{Ref}_s,\qquad \Adm_s
\]
already belong to $\Sigma_1$ for every $s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}$, and
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash \Adm_s(x)\in\mathrm{Form}_1
\]
is a lower-layer judgment.
\item The local-object signature is obtained by adjoining only site, cover, functor, and sectionability symbols:
\[
\Sigma_2=\Sigma_1\cup\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}},
\]
where the new local-object part is
\[
\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}}
:=
\{\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},
\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s
\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}.
\]
Thus the full $\Sigma_2$ signature contains $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$ only by inheritance from $\Sigma_1$; they are not newly added at this step.
\item The actual increment is therefore the identity
\[
\Delta_{1,2}:=\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1
=
\{\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},
\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s
\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\};
\]
no symbol in this display is a reference sort or an admissibility predicate. In particular,
\[
\Delta_{1,2}\cap
\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}
=\varnothing .
\]
No later reference to a ``site on admitted reference classes'' changes this disjointness. By \Cref{def:lower-reference-data}, those classes are already cut out by the $\Sigma_1$ reference domain, equality, state, and $\Sigma_1$ admissibility predicate before any local-object symbol is present.
\item The reduct $U_{2,1}$ forgets precisely $\Delta_{1,2}$ and preserves the reference domains and admissibility predicates:
\[
D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{\,U_{2,1}(M)}=D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{\,M},
\qquad
\Adm_s^{\,U_{2,1}(M)}=\Adm_s^{\,M}.
\]
\item Consequently the boundary typing table is
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
\text{expression} & \text{layer where it is well formed} & \text{retained by }U_{2,1}\\ \hline
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s & \mathrm{Form}_1\text{ contexts} & \text{yes}\\
\Adm_s(x) & \mathrm{Form}_1 & \text{yes}\\
\LocSec_s(x),\ \CompSec_s(x),\ \Sec_s(x) & \mathrm{Form}_2\text{ only} & \text{no.}
\end{array}
\]
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the lower comparison language at this boundary is reference-aware but not local-object-aware: variables $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and atoms $\Adm_s(x)$ are $\Sigma_1$ data, while $\LocSec_s$, $\CompSec_s$, and $\Sec_s$ are $\Sigma_2$ data not present in $\Sigma_1$. Clause \textup{(iii)} is the complete list of symbols added after references and admissibility are already present.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Clause \textup{(i)} fixes the reference-aware information-state language. Clause \textup{(ii)} defines the local-object part of the next signature, and subtracting $\Sigma_1$ from the displayed union gives the increment in \textup{(iii)}. Since that increment is displayed explicitly and contains only local-object symbols, it contains no reference sort and no admissibility predicate. The objects of the later sites are equivalence classes cut out from the retained $\Sigma_1$ reference domain by the retained $\Sigma_1$ admissibility predicate. The reduct along the inclusion $\Sigma_1\hookrightarrow\Sigma_2$ forgets precisely the symbols in $\Delta_{1,2}$ and therefore retains all $\Sigma_1$ domains and predicates, including $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$. This gives clause \textup{(iv)}, and the typing table in \textup{(v)} follows immediately.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Reference-aware boundary contract]\label{def:reference-aware-boundary}
With $S_{\mathrm{ref}}$ as in \Cref{prop:no-reference-increment}, the part of the signature chain used in Theorem~A is scheduled as follows:
\[
\Sigma_1
=
\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{st}}
\cup
\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\},
\]
where $\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{st}}$ denotes the remaining information-state forcing symbols, and
\[
\Sigma_2
=
\Sigma_1
\cup
\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}},
\]
where
\[
\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}}
:=
\{\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},
\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s
\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}.
\]
Thus
\[
\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\in\Sigma_1
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
(\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1)\cap\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}=\varnothing .
\]
The $\mathbb L_1$--$\mathbb L_2$ boundary is therefore governed by the following typing table:
\[
\begin{array}{c|l|l}
\text{layer} & \text{reference-aware symbols} & \text{local-object symbols}\\ \hline
\Sigma_1 & \mathsf{Ref}_s,\ \Adm_s & \text{none of }\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s\\
\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1 & \text{none} &
\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s .
\end{array}
\]
Consequently a variable declaration $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and the atom $\Adm_s(x)$ are already well formed in $\mathrm{Form}_1$. The local-object atoms
\[
\LocSec_s(x),\qquad \CompSec_s(x),\qquad \Sec_s(x)
\]
are well formed only after passing to $\mathrm{Form}_2$.

The lower reference-aware signature over $s$ is the sub-signature
\[
\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)\subseteq\Sigma_1
\]
containing the displayed symbols $\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s$ together with the other $\Sigma_1$ symbols used by information-state forcing, and containing no symbol of $\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}}$. The formula fragment later denoted $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$ consists of formulas over this signature. It is a genuine part of $\mathbb L_1$, not the reference-free pointwise language $\mathbb L_0$, and not a fragment that obtains its reference sort by forgetting local-object data from $\mathbb L_2$.
Equivalently, the fragment used in Theorem~A is the following set of type judgments:
\[
\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)
=
\{\varphi(x)\mid
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash \varphi(x)\in\mathrm{Form}_1
\text{ over }\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)\text{, with no parameters}\},
\]
where
\[
\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\in\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s),
\qquad
\{\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s\}\cap\Sigma_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)=\varnothing.
\]
Thus $\Adm_s(x)$ is available to the lower comparison language, while the local-object predicates being separated from it are not.

The phrase ``site on admitted reference classes'' used below is semantic shorthand for a site whose objects are the already formed lower-layer classes
\[
\mathcal R_s(p)=\{[d]_p\mid d\in D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^M\text{ and }M,p\Vdash\Adm_s(d)\}.
\]
It does not introduce a new sort or a new admissibility predicate. Passing from $\mathbb L_1$ to $\mathbb L_2$ equips those existing classes with a site, covers, a local object functor, and sectionability predicates; it does not create or reinterpret $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ or $\Adm_s$.

This is the standing type discipline for \Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}. The phrase ``lower formula'' in that theorem always means a parameter-free formula of the typed subfragment $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)\subseteq\mathrm{Form}_1$; it never means a formula of the original pointwise language $\mathrm{Form}_0$ and never allows any of the newly adjoined $\Sigma_2$ local-object predicates.
\end{definition}

Thus the contract is part of the definition of the comparison problem, not a conclusion proved later from local-object data.

Equivalently, the type judgments at the boundary are
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash x=x\in\mathrm{Form}_1,
\qquad
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash \Adm_s(x)\in\mathrm{Form}_1,
\]
whereas
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\vdash \LocSec_s(x),\ \CompSec_s(x),\ \Sec_s(x)
\quad\text{are judgments only after passing to }\mathrm{Form}_2 .
\]
Reference typing and admissibility are lower-layer matters, settled in $\Sigma_1$; compatible local families and global sectionability are local-object matters, added at $\Sigma_2$. The separation theorem later compares these two questions over a fixed $\Sigma_1$ reduct.

\begin{proposition}[Layer typing at the comparison boundary]\label{prop:layer-typing-comparison-boundary}
For every referenceable sort $s$, the following are part of the signature data at the $\mathbb L_1$--$\mathbb L_2$ boundary.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=3em]
\item The reference context and admissibility atom are already lower-layer judgments:
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s\in \mathrm{Form}_1\text{ contexts},
\qquad
\Adm_s(x)\in \mathrm{Form}_1.
\]
\item The local-object step adjoins only the site, cover, functor, and sectionability symbols:
\[
\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1
=
\{\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s
\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}.
\]
In particular, $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$ are not members of
$\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1$.
\item The reduct $U_{2,1}$ forgets the local-object symbols in \textup{(ii)} and retains the reference domain and admissibility predicate:
\[
D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{\,U_{2,1}(M)}=D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{\,M},
\qquad
\Adm_s^{\,U_{2,1}(M)}=\Adm_s^{\,M}.
\]
\item Consequently the boundary typing table is
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
\text{expression} & \text{layer where it is well formed} & \text{retained by }U_{2,1}\\ \hline
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s & \mathrm{Form}_1\text{ contexts} & \text{yes}\\
\Adm_s(x) & \mathrm{Form}_1 & \text{yes}\\
\LocSec_s(x),\ \CompSec_s(x),\ \Sec_s(x) & \mathrm{Form}_2\text{ only} & \text{no}\\
\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}(x) & \mathrm{Form}_2\text{ only} & \text{no.}
\end{array}
\]
\end{enumerate}
Consequently a formula of $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$ may have the displayed free variable $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and may contain $\Adm_s(x)$, but no formula of $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$ contains $\LocSec_s$, $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, or $\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Clause \textup{(i)} is exactly the placement $\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\in\Sigma_1$ in \Cref{prop:no-reference-increment,def:reference-aware-boundary}. Clause \textup{(ii)} is the displayed definition of the local-object increment $\Delta_{1,2}=\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{loc}}$ in the same boundary contract. Hence
\[
(\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1)\cap\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\}=\varnothing
\]
and the passage from $\mathbb L_1$ to $\mathbb L_2$ neither creates nor reinterprets references or admissibility. Clause \textup{(iii)} is the concrete reduct clause of \Cref{prop:chain-instantiation}(ii): $U_{2,1}$ forgets the newly adjoined site, cover, functor, and sectionability symbols while retaining the whole $\Sigma_1$ reduct. The table in \textup{(iv)} now follows from \textup{(i)}--\textup{(iii)}. Finally,
\[
\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}(x)=\Adm_s(x)\land \CompSec_s(x)\land \neg\Sec_s(x),
\]
so it uses $\Sigma_2$ sectionability symbols and is not in $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Concrete instantiation of the finite chain]\label{prop:chain-instantiation}
The four adjacent interfaces of
\[
\mathbb L_0\preceq \mathbb L_1\preceq \mathbb L_2\preceq \mathbb L_3\preceq \mathbb L_4
\]
are instantiated as follows. The reference placement at the first nontrivial boundary is the one fixed in \Cref{prop:no-reference-increment}. Thus, for every referenceable sort $s$, the assertion includes:
\[
\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\}\subseteq \Sigma_1,
\qquad
(\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1)\cap\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\}=\varnothing,
\]
and
\[
\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1
=
\{\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s
\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\},
\]
on the part of the signature used in Theorem~A. Thus item \textup{(ii)} below is a literal statement about the new $\Sigma_2$ symbols: it does not move the reference sort or the admissibility predicate out of $\Sigma_1$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=3em]
\item \textbf{$\mathbb L_0\leadsto\mathbb L_1$ (pointwise states to reference-aware information states).}
The language embedding
\[
e_{0,1}:\Sigma_0\hookrightarrow \Sigma_1
\]
is the inclusion of the underlying many-sorted symbols into the reference-aware state-forcing language. As required by \Cref{def:reference-aware-boundary}, for every referenceable sort $s$, $\Sigma_1$ also contains the reference sort $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and the admissibility predicate $\Adm_s$; these are ordinary lower-layer symbols at this interface and carry no site-on-reference-classes or local-object structure. The forgetful functor
\[
U_{1,0}:\Mod_1\to\Mod_0
\]
forgets the information-state presentation and the added reference-aware symbols, and remembers only the underlying many-sorted structure. A pointwise state $\rho$ in $\mathbb L_0$ is represented inside $\mathbb L_1$ by the singleton information state
\[
p_\rho:=(\Gamma,\{\rho\}).
\]
By \Cref{prop:singleton-conservativity},
\[
M,p_\rho\Vdash_1\varphi
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
U_{1,0}(M),\rho\Vdash_0\varphi
\]
for every $\mathbb L_0$-formula $\varphi$. Thus the $\mathbb L_0$--$\mathbb L_1$ boundary is conservative on the canonical singleton image that realizes pointwise semantics inside information-state semantics.

\item \textbf{$\mathbb L_1\leadsto\mathbb L_2$ (reference-aware information states to sites on admitted reference classes).}
The embedding
\[
e_{1,2}:\Sigma_1\hookrightarrow \Sigma_2
\]
uses, for each $\Sigma_1$ model $N$ and state $p$, the lower-layer classes
\[
\mathcal R_s^1(N,p)=
\{[d]_p\mid d\in D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{N}
\text{ and }N,p\Vdash_1\Adm_s(d)\}
\]
as the object candidates for the sites, and adjoins only the sites on those already admitted classes, the covers, the local object functors, the predicates $\LocSec_s$, $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, and the associated geometric notation. In particular, this interface preserves the reference domains and the interpretation of $\Adm_s$ fixed at $\Sigma_1$; it does not introduce a new reference sort, a new admissibility predicate, or a new interpretation of either. Equivalently,
\[
\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1
=
\{\mathcal C_{p,s},\Cov_{p,s},F_{p,s},\LocSec_s,\CompSec_s,\Sec_s
\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\},
\]
and hence
\[
(\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1)\cap
\{\mathsf{Ref}_s,\Adm_s\mid s\in S_{\mathrm{ref}}\}
=\varnothing .
\]
This displayed difference is meant literally: the words ``admitted reference classes'' refer to the lower-layer equivalence classes already cut out by $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$ in $\Sigma_1$. They are not a shorthand for adding a new reference sort or a new admissibility predicate at $\Sigma_2$. Consequently a $\Sigma_1$ formula with a free variable $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ remains well typed before any local-object predicate is available, and the atom $\Adm_s(x)$ is a lower-layer atom rather than a local-object atom. The forgetful functor
\[
U_{2,1}:\Mod_2\to\Mod_1
\]
forgets the sites on admitted reference classes, covers, local object functors, and sectionability predicates while retaining the reference domains and admissibility interpretation. Explicitly, for every $\Sigma_2$ expansion $M$,
\[
D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{\,U_{2,1}(M)}=D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{\,M},
\qquad
\Adm_s^{\,U_{2,1}(M)}=\Adm_s^{\,M},
\]
while $\mathcal C_{p,s}$, $\Cov_{p,s}$, $F_{p,s}$, $\LocSec_s$, $\CompSec_s$, and $\Sec_s$ are omitted from the reduct. The state projection
\[
\pi^M_{2,1}:\St_2(M)\to \St_1(U_{2,1}(M))
\]
is the identity on the underlying information state $(\Gamma_p,R_p)$. Since the forcing clauses for formulas already in $\mathrm{Form}_1$ never inspect the added local-object data, one has
\[
M,p\Vdash_2\varphi
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
U_{2,1}(M),\pi^M_{2,1}(p)\Vdash_1\varphi
\]
for every $\varphi\in\mathrm{Form}_1$.

\item \textbf{$\mathbb L_2\leadsto\mathbb L_3$ (local objects to obstruction data).}
The embedding
\[
e_{2,3}:\Sigma_2\hookrightarrow \Sigma_3
\]
adjoins the predicates and symbols used to talk about structural absence, visible branches, component gerbes, and obstruction classes. The forgetful functor
\[
U_{3,2}:\Mod_3\to\Mod_2
\]
drops the realization prestack, band, component-gerbe, and obstruction data. The state projection
\[
\pi^M_{3,2}:\St_3(M)\to \St_2(U_{3,2}(M))
\]
again leaves the underlying state unchanged. Formulas from $\mathrm{Form}_2$ are evaluated exactly as before, so
\[
M,p\Vdash_3\varphi
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
U_{3,2}(M),\pi^M_{3,2}(p)\Vdash_2\varphi
\]
for every $\varphi\in\mathrm{Form}_2$.

\item \textbf{$\mathbb L_3\leadsto\mathbb L_4$ (obstruction data to refinement dynamics).}
The embedding
\[
e_{3,4}:\Sigma_3\hookrightarrow \Sigma_4
\]
adjoins the update relations, axis indices, support notions, and rewrite language of the refinement layer. The forgetful functor
\[
U_{4,3}:\Mod_4\to\Mod_3
\]
forgets the dynamic axes, update structure, and rewrite operations. The state projection
\[
\pi^M_{4,3}:\St_4(M)\to \St_3(U_{4,3}(M))
\]
forgets only the new dynamic bookkeeping. Because the forcing clauses for $\mathrm{Form}_3$ do not depend on that added bookkeeping, one has
\[
M,p\Vdash_4\varphi
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
U_{4,3}(M),\pi^M_{4,3}(p)\Vdash_3\varphi
\]
for every $\varphi\in\mathrm{Form}_3$.
\end{enumerate}
Hence every adjacent boundary is conservative in the precise sense actually used later: the base boundary is mediated by the canonical singleton realization of pointwise states, while the higher boundaries use literal forgetful functors and state projections.
\end{proposition}

\begin{corollary}[Boundary-correct lower reference formulas]\label{cor:boundary-correct-reference-formulas}
At the $\mathbb L_1$--$\mathbb L_2$ interface, formulas with displayed variable
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s
\]
are lower formulas exactly when all of their nonlogical symbols lie in $\Sigma_1$. In particular, $\Adm_s(x)$ may occur in such a formula, while $\LocSec_s(x)$, $\CompSec_s(x)$, $\Sec_s(x)$, and $\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}(x)$ may not. For every local-object expansion $M$, the reduct $U_{2,1}(M)$ retains the domain of $x$ and the interpretation of $\Adm_s$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
This is the boundary contract of \Cref{def:reference-aware-boundary} instantiated by \Cref{prop:chain-instantiation}(i)--(ii). Item \textup{(i)} places $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$ in $\Sigma_1$. Item \textup{(ii)} says that the only new symbols at $\Sigma_2$ are the site, cover, local-object, and sectionability symbols, and that $U_{2,1}$ forgets those symbols while retaining the reference domains and admissibility interpretation. Since $\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}(x)$ contains $\CompSec_s(x)$ and $\Sec_s(x)$, it is also outside the lower fragment.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Reference data survive the $\mathbb L_2\to\mathbb L_1$ reduct]\label{lem:reference-data-survive-reduct}
Let $M$ be a $\Sigma_2$ local-object model, let $p$ be a state, and let $r\in D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{M}$. Then
\[
D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{\,U_{2,1}(M)}=D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^{\,M},
\qquad
\Adm_s^{\,U_{2,1}(M)}=\Adm_s^{\,M},
\]
and therefore
\[
M,p\Vdash\Adm_s(r)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
U_{2,1}(M),\pi^M_{2,1}(p)\Vdash\Adm_s(r).
\]
Moreover $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$ is a genuine subfragment of $\mathrm{Form}_1$: its displayed variable has sort $\mathsf{Ref}_s$, its admissibility atom is $\Adm_s(x)$, and neither construction uses any symbol of $\Sigma_2\setminus\Sigma_1$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The domain equality and the equality of $\Adm_s$ are part of \Cref{prop:chain-instantiation}(ii). Since $\Adm_s$ is a $\Sigma_1$ predicate and the state projection $\pi^M_{2,1}$ is the identity on the underlying information state, the forcing equivalence for $\Adm_s(r)$ is exactly the conservativity clause at the $\mathbb L_1\leadsto\mathbb L_2$ boundary. The final assertion follows from the definition of $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$ and the boundary contract in \Cref{def:reference-aware-boundary}.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Pointed reference evaluation]\label{def:pointed-reference-evaluation}
Let
\[
p=(\Gamma_p,R_p)
\]
be a $\Sigma_1$ information state, let $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ be a displayed variable not already in $\Gamma_p$, and let
\[
r\in D_{\mathsf{Ref}_s}^M.
\]
Define the pointed state
\[
p[r/x]:=
\bigl((\Gamma_p,x:\mathsf{Ref}_s),\{\rho[x\mapsto r]\mid \rho\in R_p\}\bigr).
\]
For a formula $\chi(x)$ whose displayed free variable is $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$, write
\[
M,p\Vdash \chi[r]
\quad:\Longleftrightarrow\quad
M,p[r/x]\Vdash \chi(x).
\]
The same semantic convention is used for $\Sigma_2$ target predicates $P_s(x)$ such as $\CompSec_s(x)$ or $\Sec_s(x)$. This does not add a constant symbol for $r$ to the lower language: the formula remains parameter-free, and the reference element is supplied only by the pointed model-state evaluation.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Reference-aware lower comparison fragment]\label{def:reference-aware-comparison-fragment}
For a referenceable sort $s$, let
\[
\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)
\]
denote the class of parameter-free $\Sigma_1$-formulas whose only displayed free variable is
\[
x:\mathsf{Ref}_s
\]
and whose bound variables range only over $\Sigma_1$ sorts. These formulas are evaluated on realizations in the sense of \Cref{def:state-forcing}. This is a reference-aware subfragment of $\mathrm{Form}_1$, not a fragment of the original pointwise language $\mathrm{Form}_0$ and not a reduct in which references have been forgotten. Because $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$ belong to $\Sigma_1$ by \Cref{def:reference-aware-boundary}, formulas such as $\Adm_s(x)$ are well formed in this fragment. Evaluation at a particular admitted reference is a semantic pointed-state convention, introduced in \Cref{def:pointed-reference-evaluation}; it is not the addition of a new constant symbol. No parameters naming local-object data are allowed. The fragment contains no symbols adjoined at the $\mathbb L_1\leadsto\mathbb L_2$ interface: in particular it contains neither the sites on admitted reference classes, nor covers, nor the local object functors, nor the predicates
\[
\LocSec_s,\qquad \CompSec_s,\qquad \Sec_s.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Typed comparison boundary]\label{lem:typed-comparison-boundary}
For every referenceable sort $s$, the variable declaration $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and the formula $\Adm_s(x)$ are well formed in $\mathrm{Form}_1$. The predicates $\LocSec_s$, $\CompSec_s$, and $\Sec_s$ are not symbols of $\Sigma_1$, and the derived predicate $\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}$ is therefore not a formula of $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The first assertion is exactly the placement of $\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and $\Adm_s$ at the $\mathbb L_0\leadsto\mathbb L_1$ interface in \Cref{prop:chain-instantiation}(i). At the next interface, \Cref{prop:chain-instantiation}(ii) adjoins only the sites on admitted reference classes, covers, local object functors, and sectionability predicates; it explicitly retains, rather than introduces, the reference domains and admissibility interpretation. Hence the lower comparison language is a $\Sigma_1$ language with reference variables, while the local-object predicates belong to $\Sigma_2$. The predicates $\LocSec_s$, $\CompSec_s$, and $\Sec_s$ first occur at $\Sigma_2$. Finally,
\[
\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}(x)
:=
\Adm_s(x)\land \CompSec_s(x)\land \neg\Sec_s(x),
\]
so it uses symbols absent from $\Sigma_1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Layer-correct comparison at the gluing boundary]\label{cor:layer-correct-gluing-boundary}
The lower side of Theorem~A is the reference-aware reduct
\[
U_{2,1}(M)
\]
of a local-object model $M$, not the original pointwise language and not a reference-free language. Thus a lower formula may have the displayed free variable $x:\mathsf{Ref}_s$ and may contain the lower atom $\Adm_s(x)$. What it may not contain are the $\Sigma_2$ predicates
\[
\LocSec_s,\qquad \CompSec_s,\qquad \Sec_s
\]
or the derived gluing predicate $\Null_s^{\mathrm{glue}}$. Consequently the non-definability theorem compares well-typed $\Sigma_1$ formulas with $\Sigma_2$ target predicates over local-object expansions of the same $\Sigma_1$ reduct.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
This is just \Cref{lem:typed-comparison-boundary,lem:reference-data-survive-reduct} together with the concrete reduct description in \Cref{prop:chain-instantiation}(ii). The reduct $U_{2,1}$ keeps the reference domains and $\Adm_s$ fixed and forgets exactly the site-on-reference-classes and local-object symbols. Hence reference variables and admissibility tests are available to lower formulas, while sectionability and gluing predicates remain outside $\Frm_1^{\mathrm{ref}}(s)$.
\end{proof}

Thus the lower comparison language used at the $\mathbb L_1$--$\mathbb L_2$ boundary is reference-aware but not local-object-aware: it can talk about admitted references, but it cannot inspect compatible local families or global sectionability.

This five-layer chain is the complete setting of the present paper. It is self-contained and does not presuppose any external framework.
