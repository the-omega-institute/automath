ETDS referee simulation
Date: 2026-03-19

Recommendation after revision: acceptable, subject to normal editorial copy-editing.

Principal weaknesses found in the pre-revision draft:
1. The main structural result was fragmented across several propositions and did not appear early enough in a recognizably ETDS-style theorem form.
Fix applied: the introduction now states a single Main Structural Theorem, and Section 4 proves it by a clear sliding-block/factor argument.

2. The manuscript mixed measure-theoretic arguments with nonstandard semantic language that obscured the mathematical content.
Fix applied: the paper now uses standard symbolic-dynamics and factor-map terminology throughout; the nonessential semantic framework material was removed from the main line.

3. The quantitative section stated an exact boundary exponent but did not yet supply a finite-state mechanism for reading that exponent from regular boundary data.
Fix applied: a finite-state counting proposition was added to the scan-factor section, and the clarity section now contains a regular-boundary proposition and a finite-state exponent corollary.

4. The capacity section was not yet aligned with the introduction and did not isolate the clean register lower bound that follows from multiplicity alone.
Fix applied: the section was rewritten around a double-budget theorem and a separate auxiliary-register lower bound, with the introduction adjusted to match.

5. The earlier draft still carried companion-paper style references and placeholders that would weaken an ETDS submission.
Fix applied: the bibliography and narrative now rely only on standard, relevant references used directly in the paper.
