\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The central finding is that finite observation of first-entry residue
events for a Markov hole in a mixing shift of finite type recovers
three layers of open-system information: the escape rate
\(\gamma_H(\varphi)=P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(\varphi)\), the
residue-dependent quasistationary ambiguity amplitude
\(\xi_H^{\mathsf T}b_r\), and the cyclotomic lift of the killed
spectrum visible through the error resolvent.  In the one-step Markov
setting, these objects are completely explicit: the killed-matrix formula
(Theorem~\ref{thm:first-entry-escape-rate}) gives exact Bayes error
asymptotics, and the doubling-hole example yields the closed-form
constant \(2/5\).

The survivor R\'enyi pressure spectrum
\(s\mapsto sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi)\) packages the
collision behaviour of surviving samples into open-system thermodynamic
formalism.  At \(s=2\) it determines the birthday scale of the
conditioned survivor law, with the ambient two-sample exponent splitting
as \(\chi_H = 2\gamma_H(\varphi)+h_{2,H}\).  The Poisson threshold
is therefore a consequence of the pressure spectrum rather than
a separate phenomenon.

Two directions remain natural.  First, the H\"older extension
sketched in Theorem~\ref{thm:holder-open-resonance} requires a
complete operator-theoretic verification of the spectral-gap and
regularity hypotheses; establishing this in full generality would extend
the reach of the Bayes-error observable beyond the finite-state setting.
Second, it would be interesting to understand whether finer residue
observables or multidimensional holes detect larger pieces of the open
resonance set than the cyclotomic lifts considered here.
