\title{Finite Observation of Open Symbolic Dynamics:\\
Escape Rates, Cyclotomic Resonances, and Survivor R\'enyi Spectra}
\author{}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Let $(X,\mathcal{B},\mu,T)$ be a measure-preserving system with a binary
readout set $W\in\mathcal{B}$, and let $H$ be a Markov hole in the
resulting symbolic factor.  We ask what the depth-$m$ Bayes error
$\varepsilon_m$ for first-entry residue events of $H$ detects about the
open dynamics.  We prove that $\varepsilon_m$ decays at rate equal to the
escape rate $\gamma_H(\varphi)=P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(\varphi)$, that its
ratio to survivor mass converges to a residue-dependent quasistationary
amplitude determined by the Perron eigenvector of the killed chain, and
that its generating function is a residue-twisted killed resolvent whose
poles lie in the cyclotomic lift of the open spectrum.  Under the
standard spectral-gap hypothesis this resolvent picture extends from the
finite-state setting to open Ruelle operators for H\"older potentials.
As a thermodynamic consequence, we establish the survivor R\'enyi
pressure spectrum and derive Poisson birthday thresholds for surviving
samples at the scale $\binom{N_m}{2}e^{-mh_{2,H}}\asymp 1$.
\end{abstract}
