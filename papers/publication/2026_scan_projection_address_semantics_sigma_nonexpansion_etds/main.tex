\begin{abstract}
Let \(Y\) be a topologically mixing one-step subshift of finite type,
\(\nu_\varphi\) the equilibrium Markov measure of a one-step potential,
and \(H\) a Markov hole with mixing survivor subshift \(Y_H\).  We
introduce the depth-\(m\) Bayes error of first-entry residue events into
\(H\) as a new observable for open symbolic dynamics and show that it
admits an exact killed-matrix path sum: it is asymptotic to survivor mass
up to a residue-dependent quasistationary amplitude, and its exponential
rate recovers the escape rate
\(\gamma_H(\varphi)=P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(\varphi)\).  The paper is
deliberately finite-state: the escape-rate formula,
Perron--Frobenius asymptotics for the killed matrix, and Chen--Stein
Poisson approximation are standard inputs, whereas the Bayes-error
observable and its residue-dependent ambiguity amplitude are the new
features.  As a refinement, we identify the error generating function as
a scalar resolvent coefficient whose poles are contained in the
reciprocal \(q\)-th cyclotomic lift of the killed spectrum and whose
dominant real pole \(e^{\gamma_H(\varphi)}\) is genuine; no claim is made
about detecting every eigenvalue.  A broader H\"older/cylinder-hole
extension is mentioned only briefly as future work.  As corollaries of
the same path-sum calculation we recover the survivor R\'enyi pressure
spectrum \(sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi)\) together with the
resulting Poisson collision thresholds.
\end{abstract}
