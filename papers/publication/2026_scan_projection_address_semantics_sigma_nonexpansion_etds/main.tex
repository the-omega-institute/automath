\begin{abstract}
Let \(Y\) be a topologically mixing one-step subshift of finite type,
\(\nu_\varphi\) the equilibrium Markov measure of a one-step potential,
and \(H\) a Markov hole with mixing survivor subshift \(Y_H\).  We prove
that the depth-\(m\) Bayes error of first-entry residue events into
\(H\) satisfies an exact killed-matrix formula: it is asymptotic to
survivor mass up to an explicit residue-dependent quasistationary
amplitude, and its exponential rate recovers the escape rate
\(\gamma_H(\varphi)=P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(\varphi)\).  This is the main
theorem.  As a refinement, the error generating function is a
residue-twisted killed resolvent whose poles lie in the cyclotomic lift
of the killed spectrum, with dominant real pole
\(e^{\gamma_H(\varphi)}>1\).  As secondary consequences of the same
path-sum calculation, we derive the survivor R\'enyi pressure spectrum
\(sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi)\) and the resulting Poisson
collision thresholds.
\end{abstract}
