\begin{abstract}
Let \(Y\) be a mixing subshift of finite type, \(\nu_\varphi\) an
equilibrium Markov measure, and \(H\) a Markov hole.  For first-entry
residue events into \(H\), we show that the depth-\(m\) Bayes error of
finite observation has the same exponential decay as survivor mass and
therefore recovers the escape rate
\(P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(\varphi)\).  More precisely, the Bayes error
divided by survivor mass converges to a residue-dependent
quasistationary amplitude.  We then derive the survivor R\'enyi pressure
spectrum \(sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi)\), so the conditioned
survivor law has R\'enyi-\(2\) entropy rate \(h_{2,H}\) and Poisson
collision thresholds on the scale
\(\binom{N_m}{2}e^{-mh_{2,H}}\asymp 1\).  A supplementary resolvent
reformulation identifies the error generating function with a
cyclotomic lift of the killed spectrum and extends, under the usual
spectral-gap hypothesis, to H\"older potentials.
\end{abstract}
