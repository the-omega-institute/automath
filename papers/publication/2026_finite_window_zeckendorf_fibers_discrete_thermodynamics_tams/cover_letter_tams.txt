Dear Editors,

We submit for your consideration the manuscript "Finite-Window Zeckendorf
Fibers and the Discrete Thermodynamics of Fibonacci Partition Differences."

The paper addresses the following question: what structure does the fiber
spectrum of finite-window Zeckendorf stabilization carry, and how does it
connect to the classical theory of Fibonacci partitions?  We show that an
explicit affine permutation of residues converts each fiber multiplicity
into a Fibonacci-lag discrete derivative of the indexed partition function.
This single identity opens an escalation that proceeds through six main
theorems:

  (A) Partition-difference formula for individual fibers.
  (B) Moment transfer: collision moments satisfy S_q(m) ~ lambda_q^m,
      with lambda_q the partition constant of Sanna.
  (C) Finite-state kernel realization identifying each lambda_q (q >= 2)
      as the Perron root of a nonnegative integer matrix of polynomial
      size, proving algebraicity; convexity of the pressure sequence.
  (D) Gibbs selection: canonical exponential concentration on adjacent
      pressure-slope bands.
  (E) Microcanonical band bounds with two-sided exponential estimates.
  (F) Zero-temperature concentration recovering D_m^{1/m} -> sqrt(phi).

Beyond the thermodynamic formalism, the same partition constants carry
arithmetic content.  In the window q = 9, ..., 17, the minimal recurrence
polynomials are irreducible over Q with full symmetric Galois groups; for
q in {12, 13, 14, 15}, squareclass independence of discriminants certifies
linear disjointness of the splitting fields and yields product Chebotarev
densities.

We believe the Transactions is a natural venue for this work.  The paper
sits at the intersection of combinatorial number theory (Zeckendorf
representations, Fibonacci partition functions), algebraic integers and
Galois theory (Perron roots, irreducibility, symmetric groups, Chebotarev
density), and symbolic dynamics (finite-state realizations, pressure
functions, Gibbs measures) -- each a tradition well represented in the
Transactions.  The escalation from a single combinatorial identity to a
complete thermodynamic formalism with certified arithmetic content may be
of interest to readers across these areas.

The manuscript is approximately 40 pages in amsart format, contains 13
references, and is self-contained: all proofs are given in full, with
computational certificates recorded in an appendix.

Thank you for considering this submission.
