\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

An explicit affine permutation converts each finite-window Zeckendorf fiber
multiplicity into a Fibonacci-lag difference of the indexed partition function.
This identification transfers the full pressure sequence $(P_q)_{q\ge 0}$ to
the collision moments, realizes each partition constant $\lambda_q$ ($q\ge 2$)
as the Perron root of a polynomial-size nonnegative integer matrix, and
assembles a closed canonical/microcanonical formalism: the adjacent pressure
slopes $\Delta_q$ select the canonical thickness bands, the zero-temperature
limit recovers $D_m^{1/m}\to\sqrt{\varphi}$, and the R\'enyi entropy-rate
spectrum of the visible law is the $q=1$ projection of the same pressure
geometry.

In the arithmetic window $q=9,\dots,17$, the displayed recurrence polynomials are
used through the exact certificate records of
Theorem~\ref{thm:arithmetic-window-certificates}.  Their recurrence part is certified
inside the exact histogram kernels by the residual identities
\(R_qP_q(\widetilde A_q)\widetilde v_q=0\).  These identities give
\(N_q=\dim\widetilde A_q\) consecutive exact residual zeros after \(P_q\) has
been substituted into the kernel; the a priori certification theorem then uses
Cayley--Hamilton to propagate that block to the whole recurrence tail.  The
additional Krylov and observability ranks identify the observable quotient, but
no finite scalar moment check is used as a recurrence proof.  Using these exact
records, the resulting recurrence polynomials of \(S_q(m)\) are
irreducible with full symmetric Galois groups.  For the four-element block
$q\in\{12,13,14,15\}$, squareclass independence of discriminants certifies
linear disjointness of the splitting fields and yields product Chebotarev
densities.  Extension of these arithmetic certificates beyond $q=17$ and
symbolic root-isolation for the secondary spectral pattern remain open.
