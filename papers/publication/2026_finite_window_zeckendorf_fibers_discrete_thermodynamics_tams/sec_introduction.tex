\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Every positive integer has a unique representation as a sum of
non-consecutive Fibonacci numbers \cite{Lekkerkerker1952,Zeckendorf1972}.
An arbitrary binary word $\omega\in\{0,1\}^m$ determines a Fibonacci-weighted
integer $N_m(\omega)=\sum_{j=1}^m\omega_jF_{j+1}$ that generally violates the
adjacency constraint; reducing modulo $F_{m+2}$ and inverting the standard
bijection $V_m\colon X_m\to\{0,\dots,F_{m+2}-1\}$ yields the finite-window
fold
\[
  \Fold_m(\omega):=V_m^{-1}\bigl(N_m(\omega)\bmod F_{m+2}\bigr).
\]
The fiber multiplicity $d_m(x):=|\Fold_m^{-1}(x)|$ records how many raw words
stabilize to a given visible word~$x$.

A natural question arises: what structure does this fiber spectrum carry, and
how does it connect to the classical theory of Fibonacci partitions?  We show
that the answer is highly structured.  After an explicit affine permutation
of residues, each fiber multiplicity equals a Fibonacci-lag discrete
derivative of the classical partition function.  This single identity converts
the fiber spectrum into a discrete thermodynamic system whose pressures are the
Fibonacci partition constants of Sanna~\cite{Sanna2025}, and whose
kernel-certified recurrence polynomials carry arithmetic content in a
computable window.

The paper makes three contributions.  First, it identifies the finite-window
fiber multiplicities with an explicit affine reindexing of Fibonacci partition
differences, so single fibers inherit the exact formulas of Chow and
Slattery~\cite{ChowSlattery2021}.  Second, it transfers the full moment
sequence to Sanna's partition constants and realizes those constants as Perron
roots of finite-state kernels, yielding algebraicity, convex pressure,
canonical concentration, and zero-temperature asymptotics.  Third, in the
arithmetic window \(q=9,\dots,17\), exact finite certificate records, isolated
as a separate certificate theorem, imply
irreducibility and full symmetric Galois groups for recurrence polynomials
derived from the exact finite-state kernels by residual certificates with an a
priori order bound, together with linear disjointness and product Chebotarev
densities for the block \(q\in\{12,13,14,15\}\).

\subsection{Main results}

Let $R(n)$ be the number of partitions of~$n$ into distinct Fibonacci numbers
(with $F_1=F_2=1$ not distinguished), set $R^\dagger(n):=R(n)+R(n-1)$, and
write $T_q^\dagger(N):=\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}R^\dagger(n)^q$.  For integers
$q\ge 1$, set $S_q(m):=\sum_{x\in X_m}d_m(x)^q$.

\begin{theoremA}
For every pair of integers $m\ge 1$ and $n$ with $0\le n<F_{m+2}$,
\[
  d_m^\#(\pi_m(n))
  =
  R^\dagger(n)-R^\dagger(n-F_{m+1}).
\]
\end{theoremA}

\noindent
The fiber spectrum is the Fibonacci-lag discrete derivative of the indexed
partition function.  Exact Zeckendorf formulas of Chow and
Slattery~\cite{ChowSlattery2021} therefore apply directly to individual fibers.

\begin{theoremB}
For every pair of integers $m\ge 1$ and $q\ge 1$,
\[
  T_q^\dagger(F_{m+1})\le S_q(m)\le T_q^\dagger(F_{m+2}),
\]
and consequently $S_q(m)\asymp_q \lambda_q^m$, where $\lambda_q$ is the
partition constant of Sanna~\cite[Thm.~1.1]{Sanna2025}.
\end{theoremB}

\begin{theoremC}
For $q\ge 2$, the Perron root of the finite-state collision kernel of
Section~\ref{sec:moment-kernel} equals $\lambda_q$.  More precisely,
$\lambda_q$ is the spectral radius of a nonnegative integer matrix of
dimension polynomial in~$q$; in particular, $\lambda_q$ is an algebraic
integer.  The pressure sequence $P_0:=\log\varphi$,
$P_q:=\log\lambda_q$ is convex, and its discrete slopes
$\Delta_q:=P_q-P_{q-1}$ satisfy $\Delta_q\nearrow\frac12\log\varphi$.
\end{theoremC}

\begin{theoremD}
For each fixed integer $q\ge 1$ and every $\varepsilon>0$, the $q$-canonical law
$\mu_{m,q}\propto d_m(x)^q$ is exponentially concentrated on the adjacent
pressure-slope band $[\Delta_q,\Delta_{q+1}]$:
\[
  \mu_{m,q}\bigl\{x:m^{-1}\log d_m(x)\notin
  [\Delta_q-\varepsilon,\,\Delta_{q+1}+\varepsilon]\bigr\}
  \le \exp\bigl(-(\varepsilon+o(1))m\bigr).
\]
\end{theoremD}

\begin{theoremE}
For each fixed integer $q\ge 1$ and every $\varepsilon>0$, the canonical band
$B_{m,q}(\varepsilon)$ of Theorem~D satisfies two-sided
exponential estimates for both its cardinality $|B_{m,q}(\varepsilon)|$ and its
visible mass $p_m(B_{m,q}(\varepsilon))$, with exponents determined by
$P_q,\Delta_q,\Delta_{q+1}$.
\end{theoremE}

\begin{theoremF}
Every integer sequence of diverging tilts $q(m)\to\infty$ selects the
zero-temperature thickness \(\frac12\log\varphi\):
\[
  D_m^{1/m}\to\sqrt{\varphi},
  \qquad
  \log S_{q(m)}(m)
  =
  \bigl(\tfrac12\log\varphi+o(1)\bigr)m\,q(m).
\]
\end{theoremF}

\medskip\noindent
The escalation summarized by Theorems~A--F proceeds as follows.
Theorem~A identifies single fibers with partition differences.
Theorem~B transfers the moment growth to Fibonacci windows of Sanna's
power sums.  Theorem~C realizes the partition constants as finite-state
Perron roots and extracts a convex pressure function.  Theorems~D and~E
use the pressure geometry for canonical concentration and microcanonical
band bounds.  Theorem~F passes to the zero-temperature limit.  These six
results do not use the arithmetic-window certificates of
Section~\ref{sec:chebotarev}; the latter enter only the Galois and
Chebotarev statements, where the recurrences are certified from exact kernel
matrices in Appendix~\ref{app:certification}.

The hypotheses in this chain are only the displayed finite-window and moment
parameters plus the standard deterministic Fibonacci normalizer used to build
the kernels: Theorem~A is pointwise in \(m\ge1\) and
\(0\le n<F_{m+2}\); Theorem~B is pointwise in \(m\ge1\) and \(q\ge1\);
Theorem~C uses the collision kernel obtained from the deterministic
subsequential Fibonacci normalizer for \(q\ge2\); and Theorems~D--F use the
pressure sequence already identified in Theorems~B--C.  No arithmetic-window
recurrence, Galois, or Chebotarev certificate is used in the proofs of
Theorems~A--F.
For clarity, the only exact certificate records in the paper enter after this
main theorem chain: Section~\ref{sec:chebotarev} uses the certificate records of
Theorem~\ref{thm:arithmetic-window-certificates}, the
recurrence soundness theorem \Cref{prop:kernel-derived-recurrences}, the Smith-form
certificates of Table~\ref{tab:appendix-principalization-q9-17}, and the finite-field
factorization and discriminant certificates displayed in its tables.  The
recurrence certificate consists of the exact residual identity defining
\(\mathsf W_q(P_q)=\mathrm{accept}\) in the finite certificate-record format of
Appendix~\ref{app:certification}.  The
coefficient row and this residual certificate are inseparable: the coefficient
row states the polynomial, while the residual certificate is the proof that the
polynomial annihilates the tail.  It is a kernel identity in the exact
histogram matrix \(\widetilde A_q\): with
\[
  R_q=
  \begin{bmatrix}
  \widetilde u_q^{\mathsf T}\widetilde A_q^2\\
  \widetilde u_q^{\mathsf T}\widetilde A_q^3\\
  \vdots\\
  \widetilde u_q^{\mathsf T}\widetilde A_q^{N_q+1}
  \end{bmatrix},
  \qquad N_q=\dim\widetilde A_q,
\]
the residual certificate asserts that the displayed row polynomial satisfies
\[
  R_qP_q(\widetilde A_q)\widetilde v_q=0.
\]
This is a block of \(N_q\) exact residual zeros after \(P_q\) has been
substituted into the kernel.  Because the residual sequence is a scalar
coefficient of the same \(N_q\times N_q\) matrix, Cayley--Hamilton propagates
that block to the whole recurrence tail.  The appendix also records additional
rank identities for the observable quotient, but those ranks are not required
to prove the all-tail recurrence.  No finite window of scalar moment values is
used as a stand-alone recurrence proof, and a row of the coefficient table is
not cited anywhere below without this residual-verifier theorem.

All entries in the first five rows of
Table~\ref{tab:intro-dependency-map} are proved in the body of the paper from the
displayed hypotheses and the cited standard inputs.  The last row is the only
certificate-record row: its recurrence input is the exact residual certificate
of Theorem~\ref{thm:arithmetic-window-certificates}, where the relevant
residual sequence has the a priori order bound \(N_q=\dim\widetilde A_q\), and
its Galois inputs are finite algebra certificates over \(\ZZ\) and finite
fields.  Thus the
arithmetic window has a separate, explicit certificate layer rather than an
implicit assumption in Theorems~A--F.  Within that layer, the recurrence rows
are used only after the exact kernel residual verifier has accepted and the
a priori certification theorem has converted the finite residual block into an
all-tail recurrence.

Thus the arithmetic statements in Section~\ref{sec:chebotarev} are proved from
the finite exact certificate records stated in
Theorem~\ref{thm:arithmetic-window-certificates}; they do not rest on a finite numerical
fit.  Their recurrence input is the exact
residual-verifier certificate in the kernel, and the remaining computational
inputs are finite-field and Smith-form certificates displayed in
Section~\ref{sec:chebotarev} and Appendix~\ref{app:certification}.  Theorems~A--F
and the entropy ledger are independent of those arithmetic certificates.

Beyond the thermodynamic formalism, the same partition constants carry
arithmetic content.  The quadratic case $q=2$ yields the cubic
$\lambda^3-2\lambda^2-2\lambda+2$, in agreement with Chow and
Jones~\cite{ChowJones2024}.  In the arithmetic window $q=9,\dots,17$, the
kernel-certified recurrence polynomials of $S_q(m)$ are, using the exact
certificate records of Theorem~\ref{thm:arithmetic-window-certificates},
irreducible over~$\QQ$ with full symmetric Galois groups $S_{d_q}$
(Section~\ref{sec:chebotarev}); irreducibility then identifies them with the
minimal recurrence polynomials.  For $q\in\{12,13,14,15\}$ the splitting
fields are linearly disjoint, yielding product Chebotarev densities.

\subsection{Related work}

Zeckendorf~\cite{Zeckendorf1972} and Lekkerkerker~\cite{Lekkerkerker1952}
established the uniqueness of Fibonacci representations.  Earlier analyses of
the Fibonacci partition function and its extremal structure include
Robbins~\cite{Robbins1996}, Weinstein~\cite{Weinstein2016}, and
Kempton~\cite{Kempton2023}; Kempton's dynamical viewpoint is especially close
to the partition-function side of Sections~\ref{sec:residue-affine}--
\ref{sec:moment-kernel}.  Chow and Slattery~\cite{ChowSlattery2021} gave exact
formulas for $R(n)$ via the Zeckendorf expansion; our Theorem~A shows that
finite-window fiber
multiplicities are, after an explicit affine permutation, the Fibonacci-lag
discrete derivatives of these same partition counts.  Chow and
Jones~\cite{ChowJones2024} identified the cubic governing the variance of
$R(n)$; our cubic recurrence for $S_2(m)$ gives a finite-window explanation.
Sanna~\cite[Thm.~1.1]{Sanna2025} proved that $\sum_{n<N}R(n)^q\asymp_q
N^{(\log\lambda_q)/\log\varphi}$; Theorem~B transfers these constants to the
collision moments.  The finite-state realization of
Section~\ref{sec:moment-kernel} draws on classical Perron--Frobenius theory
\cite{Seneta2006,LindMarcus1995} and a bounded-delay transducer model for
Fibonacci normalization
\cite{Frougny1992NumbersAutomata,Frougny1999OnlineAddition}.  Its
automata-theoretic certification viewpoint is close in spirit to the general
Fibonacci-representation framework of Shallit and
Shan~\cite{ShallitShan2023}, which provides a direct methodological comparator
for the transducer and certificate constructions in the appendices.

\subsection{Organization}

Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries} defines the finite-window fold and records
its basic properties.  Section~\ref{sec:residue-affine} proves the affine
transfer principle and the partition-difference formula.
Section~\ref{sec:second-collision} establishes the Fibonacci-window moment
transfer and treats the explicit quadratic case.
Section~\ref{sec:moment-kernel} develops the finite-state realization, convex
pressure geometry, canonical selection, and zero-temperature concentration.
Section~\ref{sec:ledger} deduces the R\'enyi entropy-rate spectrum of the
visible law.  Section~\ref{sec:chebotarev} proves, from the exact certificate
records, irreducibility, full symmetric Galois groups, and Chebotarev density
results for the recurrence polynomials in the arithmetic window
$q=9,\dots,17$.
Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} summarizes.  Appendix~\ref{app:transducer}
gives the transducer construction for the collision kernels, and
Appendix~\ref{app:certification} records the exact recurrence, Smith-form, and
finite-field certificates.
