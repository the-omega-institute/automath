\begin{abstract}
We prove that the fiber multiplicities of finite-window Zeckendorf
stabilization are Fibonacci-lag discrete derivatives of the classical
Fibonacci partition function.  The collision moments
$S_q(m)=\sum_{x}d_m(x)^q$ are thereby squeezed between adjacent
Fibonacci windows of Sanna's partition power sums, yielding
$S_q(m)\asymp_q\lambda_q^m$ for every $q\ge 1$.  A finite-state
kernel realization identifies each $\lambda_q$ ($q\ge 2$) as the
Perron root of a nonnegative integer matrix of polynomial size,
proving that $\lambda_q$ is an algebraic integer.  The sequence
$P_q=\log\lambda_q$ (with $P_0=\log\varphi$) is convex, and its
discrete slopes $\Delta_q=P_q-P_{q-1}$ select the canonical
thickness bands of the model: the $q$-canonical law is exponentially
concentrated on $[\Delta_q,\Delta_{q+1}]$, with two-sided exponential
estimates for band cardinality and visible mass.  Diverging tilts
select the zero-temperature thickness $\frac12\log\varphi$, recovering
$D_m^{1/m}\to\sqrt{\varphi}$ and the diagonal high-moment asymptotic.
In the arithmetic window $q=9,\dots,17$, explicit exact-kernel residual
certificates give recurrence polynomials that are irreducible over
$\QQ$ with full symmetric Galois groups $S_{d_q}$; for
\(q\in\{12,13,14,15\}\) the
splitting fields are linearly disjoint, giving the product Chebotarev density
$1/20449$ for simultaneous irreducibility.
\end{abstract}
