\section{Further remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper proves one structural theorem and records two classical
connective tools around it. The Fredholm--Witt bridge places traces, Witt
coordinates, and determinants in one common language. The cyclic-block
theorem is the main new input: the Euler data admit a trace-class normal
realization exactly at the summability threshold
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\),
and in that case the canonical cyclic-block model already fixes the
closed non-zero spectral support and the exact trace budget. Spectral
rigidity then shows that once the determinant germ is fixed, the non-zero
spectrum is fixed as well. In infinite rank the algebraic range may still
be a proper dense nonclosed subspace of that closed support.

Application-level packages built from secondary determinants, twisted
observables, and limit laws are intentionally left out of this version.
They depend on additional finite-dimensional perturbative hypotheses and
are better treated as separate determinant-side papers rather than as part
of the JST core theorem chain.

Natural continuation directions are therefore twofold. One is to extend
the cyclic-block realization question beyond normal trace-class operators
or beyond Hilbert-space trace-class settings, where a replacement for the
canonical zero divisor and determinant rigidity would be needed. The other
is to develop the omitted finite-state application packages on their own
terms.
