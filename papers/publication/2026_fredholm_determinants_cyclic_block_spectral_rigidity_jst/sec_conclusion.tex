\section{Further remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper proves a two-step structural theorem chain and records two
classical connective tools around it. The Fredholm--Witt bridge places
traces, Witt coordinates, and determinants in one common language. The
first structural step is the cyclic-block realization/classification
theorem: the Euler data admit a trace-class normal realization exactly at
the summability threshold
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\),
and in that case the canonical cyclic-block model already fixes the
closed non-zero spectral support and the exact trace budget. The second
structural step is the determinant-classification theorem: once the
determinant germ is fixed, the only invariant that remains is the
collapsed non-zero eigenvalue multiset, equivalently the multiplicity
function
\[
  \lambda\mapsto \sum_{\{j:\lambda^{n_j}=\beta_j\}} m_j.
\]
The classical rigidity input is what turns determinant equality into this
multiset statement. In infinite rank the algebraic range may still be a
proper dense nonclosed subspace of the resulting closed support.

Further determinant-side perturbative developments are intentionally left
out of this version. They depend on additional hypotheses and are better
treated as separate papers than as part of the JST core theorem chain.

Natural continuation directions are therefore twofold. One is to extend
the cyclic-block realization question beyond normal trace-class operators
or beyond Hilbert-space trace-class settings, where a replacement for the
canonical zero divisor and determinant rigidity would be needed. The other
is to develop those omitted determinant-side perturbative results on their
own terms.
