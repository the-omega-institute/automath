\title[Cyclic-Block Trace-Class Realisations]{Cyclic-block trace-class realisations\\
for symbolic \(\zeta\)-functions}

\author{Haobo Ma}
\address{CHRONOAI}

\date{}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
We place symbolic dynamical \(\zeta\)-functions in a trace-class
Fredholm framework. The Fredholm--Witt identity and the associated
spectral rigidity are recorded as connective background. The main
structural result is a cyclic-block characterization for data satisfying
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\). The positive-exponent product
\[
  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{m_j}
\]
is the Fredholm determinant of a trace-class normal cyclic-block direct
sum, and only on zero-free neighbourhoods is its reciprocal denoted by the
negative-exponent Euler product. Thus realising the reciprocal Euler
product is always a determinant-side statement for the positive-exponent
Fredholm determinant. Conversely, any trace-class normal operator with
this Fredholm determinant has non-zero part, up to unitary equivalence and
an arbitrary zero summand, equal to the direct sum of cyclic blocks
\(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\) with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\). In
particular, the total non-zero block size is forced; for a single factor
\((1-\beta z^n)^{-m}\), the closed span of the non-zero spectral subspaces
has dimension exactly \(mn\). The same cyclic zero divisor forces the exact
trace budget
\(
  \sum_j m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}
\)
in the normal trace-class category. In infinite cyclic sums this is a
closed-support statement; the algebraic range can be proper and nonclosed.

We then record determinant-level applications in symbolic dynamics.
For analytic families of primitive matrices with expanding Perron branch
we record explicit
group-inverse and Fredholm formulas for the variation of the finite-part
constants and of the Abel renormalization, together with a rapidly
convergent truncation scheme. For locally constant observables, twisted
Fredholm determinants determine periodic-orbit moment data and
asymptotic cumulants; on compact frequency sets away from the arithmetic
lattice obstruction, they also yield uniform off-origin decay of
characteristic functions. These applications are finite-dimensional
consequences of the usual finite-dimensional spectral-gap formalism,
with an additional explicit expansion/nonresonance hypothesis whenever
secondary determinants are used. Explicit computations are given for the
golden-mean family and for the full two-shift. The secondary determinant
formulas require the explicit secondary-admissibility hypothesis: a uniform
complementary relative gap,
\[
  \sup_{\theta\in U}
  \frac{\rho(A_\theta|_{(I-P_\theta)\CC^d})}
       {\abs{\lambda(\theta)}}<1,
\]
together with absolute expansion,
\[
  \inf_{\theta\in U}\abs{\lambda(\theta)}>1,
\]
which is equivalently the subunit secondary-radius condition
\[
  \sup_{\theta\in U}\sup_{k\ge2}
  \rho(\lambda(\theta)^{-k}A_\theta)<1 .
\]
A branch-isolating relative gap alone is not used to infer invertibility of
\(I-\lambda^{-k}A\).
\end{abstract}
