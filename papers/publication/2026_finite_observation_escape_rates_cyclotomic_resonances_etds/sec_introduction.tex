\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

\subsection*{Problem}

Let \((X,\mathcal{B},\mu,T)\) be a measure-preserving system and let
\(W\in\mathcal{B}\) be a binary readout set.  The coding map
\(\omega_\infty(x)=(\mathbf{1}_W(T^t x))_{t\geq 0}\) defines a
measurable symbolic factor.  For a visible event \(\mathsf P\), the
depth-\(m\) Bayes error
\[
  \varepsilon_m(\mathsf P;\mu)
\]
is the smallest classification error achievable from the first \(m\)
visible symbols.  This paper studies the sequence
\(m\mapsto \varepsilon_m(\mathsf P;\mu)\) when \(\mathsf P\) is defined
by first entry into a symbolic hole, and shows that it encodes the
escape rate, quasistationary amplitudes, and cyclotomic resonance
structure of the associated open system.

\subsection*{Setting}

Fix a topologically mixing one-step subshift of finite type \(Y\), a
one-step potential \(\varphi\), and the equilibrium Markov measure
\(\nu_\varphi\).  Let \(H\) be a Markov hole with mixing survivor
subshift \(Y_H\), and write
\[
  \gamma_H(\varphi):=P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(\varphi)
\]
for the escape rate.  For a non-empty proper subset
\(R\subsetneq \ZZ/q\ZZ\), let \(\mathsf P_R\) be the event that the
first entry time into \(H\) is finite and belongs to \(R\) modulo
\(q\).

\subsection*{Main results}

The main result is the escape-rate and quasistationary-amplitude theorem.
The survivor R\'enyi and resonance statements are consequences of the
same killed path-sum formalism.  For clarity, the concrete contributions
are:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item an exact killed-matrix formula for the Bayes error of first-entry
    residue events of Markov holes, identifying its exponential decay
    with the escape rate and refining the leading term by
    residue-dependent quasistationary ambiguity amplitudes;
  \item a supplementary finite-dimensional resolvent reformulation that
    places the Markov error generating function in the reciprocal
    \(q\)-th cyclotomic lift of the killed matrix spectrum;
  \item a survivor R\'enyi pressure formalism for the conditioned
    survivor law, yielding the R\'enyi-\(2\) entropy rate and the
    resulting Poisson birthday thresholds, with the required
    third-moment condition stated below.
\end{enumerate}
For ETDS purposes, the center of gravity is the escape-rate theorem
together with its quasistationary amplitude refinement; the resonance
and survivor-R\'enyi statements are recorded as subsequent consequences
of the same killed-operator formalism.
The finite-state assumptions used for the main theorems are finite graph
or matrix hypotheses: one-step Markov presentation, mixing survivor
subshift, and the two-terminal residue reachability condition.  They are
not spectral assumptions for an open Ruelle operator, and the main
theorems do not contain a H\"older open-operator extension.  The only
cyclotomic resolvent theorem in the paper is the finite-state theorem
below.

\paragraph{Logical scope of the hypotheses.}
The hypotheses are used in three separate layers.
\begin{description}[leftmargin=2.8cm,style=nextline]
  \item[Escape and amplitudes.]
  Theorems~\ref{thm:main-amplitude} and
  \ref{thm:first-entry-escape-rate} assume a finite Markov presentation,
  a mixing survivor subshift, and the two-terminal residue reachability
  condition in a finite graph.  These are checkable graph or matrix
  hypotheses; they do not assert a spectral gap beyond the
  Perron--Frobenius theorem for a finite primitive matrix.
  \item[Resonance lift.]
  Theorem~\ref{thm:main-resonance} is only the rational resolvent of the
  finite lifted matrix \(\widehat B_H\).  Its poles are finite-matrix
  poles, not Banach-space Ruelle resonances.
  \item[H\"older appendix.]
  Theorem~\ref{thm:holder-open-transfer} is a standalone
  \(C^\alpha\)-space statement for finite-cylinder holes after block
  recoding: it defines the open Ruelle operator, proves the isolated
  leading eigenvalue and adjoint eigenfunctional, and identifies the
  survivor pressure from standard Ruelle--Perron--Frobenius theory plus a
  kernel estimate.  Theorem~\ref{thm:holder-finite-cylinder-escape}
  then proves the finite-prefix Bayes-error exponent from Gibbs
  distortion and the finite reachability condition.  Neither appendix
  theorem supplies a hypothesis to the finite-state main theorem.
\end{description}

\begin{theorem}[Quasistationary ambiguity amplitudes]
\label{thm:main-amplitude}
Assume the setting above and the residue non-degeneracy hypothesis from
Section~\ref{sec:open-system}.  Let \(B_H\) be the killed transition
matrix on the safe alphabet, and let \(\ell_H\) be a non-negative left
eigenvector of \(B_H\) for \(\rho_H:=\rho(B_H)\), normalized by
\[
  \xi_H:=\frac{\ell_H}{\ell_H^{\mathsf T}\mathbf 1}.
\]
For \(r\in \ZZ/q\ZZ\), let \(b_r\) be the residue ambiguity vector from
Theorem~\ref{thm:first-entry-escape-rate}.  Then for every residue class
\(r\),
\[
  \lim_{n\to\infty}
  \frac{\varepsilon_{nq+r+1}(\mathsf P_R;\nu_\varphi)}
       {\nu_\varphi(\tau_H\ge nq+r+1)}
  = \xi_H^{\mathsf T}b_r.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
  \varepsilon_m(\mathsf P_R;\nu_\varphi)
  = \bigl(\xi_H^{\mathsf T}b_{m-1\bmod q}+o(1)\bigr)\,
    \nu_\varphi(\tau_H\ge m).
\]
In particular, finite observation records not only the escape exponent
\(\gamma_H(\varphi)\) but also a residue-dependent quasistationary
ambiguity amplitude.
\leanverified{paper_scan_projection_address_main_amplitude}
\end{theorem}

\noindent
Theorem~\ref{thm:main-renyi} is a thermodynamic consequence of the same
path-sum calculation, now applied to power weights on survivor words.

\begin{theorem}[Survivor R\'enyi pressure spectrum]
\label{thm:main-renyi}
Assume again that \(Y\) is a mixing one-step subshift of finite type,
\(\nu_\varphi\) is the equilibrium Markov measure of a one-step
potential \(\varphi\), and \(H\) is a Markov hole with mixing survivor
subshift \(Y_H\).  For \(m\geq 1\), let \(\mathcal L_m^H\) be the set of
admissible length-\(m\) words that avoid \(H\) for the first \(m\)
steps, and for \(s>0\) define
\[
  \mathcal Z_{H,m}(s)
  := \sum_{a\in \mathcal L_m^H} \nu_\varphi([a])^s.
\]
Then
\[
  \lim_{m\to\infty}
  -\frac1m\log \mathcal Z_{H,m}(s)
  = sP_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi).
\]

If \(\lambda_m^H\) denotes the law of the survivor word conditioned on
survival to depth \(m\),
\[
  \lambda_m^H(a)
  := \frac{\nu_\varphi([a])}{\nu_\varphi(\tau_H\ge m)},
  \qquad a\in\mathcal L_m^H,
\]
then
\[
  \lim_{m\to\infty}
  -\frac1m\log
  \sum_{a\in\mathcal L_m^H} \lambda_m^H(a)^s
  = sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi).
\]

In particular, at \(s=2\) the conditioned survivor law has R\'enyi-\(2\)
entropy rate
\[
  h_{2,H}:=2P_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(2\varphi),
\]
while the ambient two-sample exponent for simultaneous survival and
visible collision is
\[
  \chi_H:=2P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(2\varphi)
        = 2\gamma_H(\varphi)+h_{2,H}.
\]
\leanverified{paper_scan_projection_address_main_renyi}
\end{theorem}

This theorem is the survivor analogue of the standard \(L^s\)-spectrum
or R\'enyi pressure identity for Gibbs weights; compare
\citet{Renyi1961,HentschelProcaccia1983,Pesin1997}.  Its role here is
not to introduce a new pressure formalism, but to record the exact
pressure corollary needed for the collision estimates after conditioning
on survival.

The identity \(\chi_H = 2\gamma_H(\varphi)+h_{2,H}\) places the
collision story inside open-system thermodynamic formalism.  Combined
with a Chen--Stein argument (Section~\ref{sec:double-budget}), it yields
Poisson birthday thresholds for surviving samples on the scale
\(\binom{N_m}{2}e^{-m h_{2,H}}\asymp 1\), provided
\[
  N_m^3S_3^H(m)\to0,
  \qquad
  S_3^H(m)=e^{-mh_{3,H}+o(m)},
  \qquad
  h_{3,H}:=3P_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(3\varphi).
\]
Thus at the pure exponential threshold
\(N_m=e^{(h_{2,H}/2+o(1))m}\), Poisson convergence requires
\[
  h_{3,H}>\frac32h_{2,H}.
\]

\noindent
The next theorem is the finite-state resolvent reformulation of the
preceding asymptotics.  It has only the finite Markov hypotheses stated in
Theorem~\ref{thm:first-entry-escape-rate}; the H\"older block-recoding
comparison in Appendix~\ref{app:holder-finite-cylinder} is a separate
finite-prefix exponent statement.

\begin{theorem}[Finite-state cyclotomic resolvent]
\label{thm:main-resonance}
For first-entry residue events of one-step Markov holes under the
finite-state hypotheses of
Theorem~\ref{thm:first-entry-escape-rate}, the Bayes error generating
function is the finite-dimensional residue-twisted killed resolvent
\[
  \mathcal E_{\mathsf P_R}(z)
  = z\,\widehat u^{\mathsf T}(I-z\widehat B_H)^{-1}\widehat b,
\]
and every pole belongs to the \(q\)-th cyclotomic lift of the killed
spectrum after taking reciprocals.  The positive real pole of smallest
modulus is
\[
  z_H=e^{\gamma_H(\varphi)}
      =e^{P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(\varphi)},
\]
which is \(\rho_H^{-1}\), the reciprocal of the leading killed
eigenvalue \(\rho_H=e^{-\gamma_H(\varphi)}\).
This theorem is only a statement about the finite matrix
\(\widehat B_H\).
\leanverified{paper_scan_projection_address_main_resonance}
\end{theorem}

The H\"older material appears in
Appendix~\ref{app:holder-finite-cylinder} as two separate block-recoding
statements for finite unions of cylinders and H\"older potentials.  The
open-transfer theorem is formulated on the explicit Banach space
\(C^\alpha\) and proves its leading spectral data; the Bayes-error
theorem uses only Gibbs distortion, survivor pressure, and finite
reachability to obtain the finite-prefix escape exponent.  Both appendix
statements are independent of the finite-state resolvent theorem.

\subsection*{Context and comparison}

Thermodynamic formalism for symbolic systems and equilibrium states is
classical; we use standard material from
\citet{Bowen2008,Ruelle1978,Walters1975GMeasures,WaltersErgodicTheory}.
The open-system viewpoint---escape through holes, killed transfer
operators, and quasistationary behaviour---goes back to
\citet{PianigianiYorke1979} and was developed for symbolic systems by
\citet{ColletMartinezSchmitt1997}, \citet{DemersYoung2006},
\citet{KellerLiverani2009}, \citet{BunimovichYurchenko2011},
\citet{DemersWrightYoung2012}, and
\citet{FergusonPollicott2012}.  For closely related work on symbolic
holes, Markov holes, and the dependence of escape rates on hole
placement, see
\citet{BruinDemersTodd2018,CheriyathAgarwal2023},
\citet{BonannoCristadoroLenci2022}, and
\citet{AgarwalCheriyathTikekar2024}; see also
\citet{DemersTodd2017} for pressure and equilibrium states in the
presence of holes for multimodal maps and
\citet{BruinDemers2022} for recent extensions to dispersing billiards.
Two recent ETDS benchmarks are close in open-system theme but different
in observable: \citet{DemersTodd2021} prove asymptotic escape rates and
limiting conditionally invariant distributions for multimodal maps with
small holes and H\"older/geometric potentials, while
\citet{AtnipFroylandGonzalezTokmanVaienti2023} develop equilibrium
states for random open and closed interval systems without transitivity
assumptions and relate Lyapunov exponents to escape through random
holes.  The present paper stays in the finite-observation symbolic
setting and asks which open-system invariants are visible from finite
Bayes errors and collision statistics.
The Poisson approximation input is the Chen--Stein method of
\citet{ArratiaGoldsteinGordon1989}.  What is specific here is the finite
observation question: the visible symbolic coding recovers the
escape-rate pressure gap and the residue-dependent quasistationary
amplitudes, while the cyclotomic resolvent and survivor R\'enyi spectrum
are downstream spectral and pressure corollaries.

\subsection*{ETDS focus and hierarchy}

Prepared for \emph{Ergodic Theory and Dynamical Systems}, the manuscript
is organized around one dynamical theme: finite observation detects the
open-system pressure gap and its quasistationary refinement.
Section~\ref{sec:open-system} is therefore the center of gravity.  Its
first subsection proves the escape-rate theorem and the amplitude
refinement.  The resolvent discussion in the remainder of
Section~\ref{sec:open-system} is supplementary to that main theorem,
packaging the same killed-operator data in spectral language.
Section~\ref{sec:double-budget} records secondary thermodynamic
consequences of the same calculation.  The recursive visible-refinement
material is independent of the escape-rate proof and is collected in
Appendix~\ref{sec:recursive-addressing}.
