\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The central finding is that finite observation of a binary coding
detects more than just the escape exponent of a symbolic hole.  For
first-entry residue events, the depth-\(m\) Bayes error profile carries
three layers of open-system information: the escape rate
\(\gamma_H(\varphi)\), the residue-dependent quasistationary ambiguity
amplitude \(\xi_H^{\mathsf T}b_r\), and the cyclotomic lift of the
killed spectrum visible through the error resolvent.  In the one-step
Markov setting these objects are completely explicit, and the
doubling-hole example yields the closed-form constant \(2/5\).

The survivor R\'enyi pressure spectrum
\(s\mapsto sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi)\) packages the
collision behaviour of surviving samples into open-system thermodynamic
formalism.  At \(s=2\) it determines the birthday scale of the
conditioned survivor law, with the ambient two-sample exponent splitting
as \(\chi_H = 2\gamma_H(\varphi)+h_{2,H}\).  The Poisson limit also
requires the third-moment smallness \(N_m^3S_3^H(m)\to0\), equivalently
at the exponential birthday scale \(h_{3,H}>\frac32h_{2,H}\).  With this
condition included, the threshold theorem is a consequence of the
pressure spectrum rather than a separate phenomenon.

Two directions remain natural.  One is to extend the boundary-transfer
and survivor-pressure formalism beyond finite-state and standard Gibbs
classes while retaining a usable transfer-operator description.  The
other is to understand whether finer residue observables or
multidimensional holes detect larger pieces of the open resonance set
than the cyclotomic lifts considered here.
