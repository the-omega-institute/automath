\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The central finding is that finite observation of a finite symbolic coding
detects more than just the escape exponent of a symbolic hole.  For
first-entry residue events, the depth-\(m\) Bayes error profile is an
observable with its own terminal ambiguity weights.  It recovers the
escape rate \(\gamma_H(\varphi)\) and the residue-dependent
quasistationary ambiguity amplitude \(\xi_H^{\mathsf T}b_r\), while the
degenerate-residue dichotomy identifies the subsequences on which finite
observation eventually decides the event.  As a finite-dimensional
corollary, the same killed-matrix formula can be repackaged as an error
resolvent whose possible scalar poles lie in the reciprocal cyclotomic
lift of the killed matrix spectrum, with actual visibility characterized
by a spectral-projection non-cancellation criterion.  In the one-step
Markov setting these objects are completely explicit, and the
doubling-hole example yields the closed-form constant \(2/5\).

The standard survivor R\'enyi pressure identity
\(s\mapsto sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi)\) packages the
iid collision behaviour of surviving samples into open-system thermodynamic
formalism.  At \(s=2\) it determines the birthday scale of the
conditioned survivor law under independent sampling, with the ambient
two-sample exponent splitting
as \(\chi_H = 2\gamma_H(\varphi)+h_{2,H}\).  The Poisson limit also
requires the third-moment smallness \(N_m^3S_3^H(m)\to0\), equivalently
at the exponential birthday scale \(h_{3,H}>\frac32h_{2,H}\).  With this
condition included, the threshold criterion is a consequence of the
pressure spectrum rather than a separate phenomenon.

Two directions remain natural.  One is to develop an operator-theoretic
version of the finite-observation problem in a setting where the relevant
functional-analytic hypotheses are proved directly.  The other is to
understand whether finer residue observables or multidimensional holes
make larger pieces of the finite-state open spectrum visible than the
scalar reciprocal cyclotomic resolvents considered here.
