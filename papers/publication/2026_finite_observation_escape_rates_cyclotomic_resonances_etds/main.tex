\begin{abstract}
Let \(Y\) be a topologically mixing one-step subshift of finite type,
\(\nu_\varphi\) the equilibrium Markov measure of a one-step potential,
and \(H\) a Markov hole.  For first-entry residue events into \(H\), we
prove an exact finite-observation formula and a degenerate-residue
dichotomy: each residue subsequence is either eventually decided by the
finite observation, or is asymptotic to survivor mass with an explicit
residue-dependent quasistationary factor.  In the two-terminal
non-degenerate case all residue subsequences are of the second kind, and
the Bayes error recovers the escape rate
\(P_Y(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(\varphi)\).  We then derive the
survivor R\'enyi pressure spectrum
\(sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi)\), so the conditioned survivor law
has R\'enyi-\(2\) entropy rate \(h_{2,H}\) and iid Poisson collision
thresholds on the scale
\(\binom{N_m}{2}e^{-mh_{2,H}}\asymp 1\), provided the third-moment
condition \(N_m^3S_3^H(m)\to0\) holds; at the pure exponential scale this
is \(h_{3,H}>\frac32h_{2,H}\).  As a supplementary
finite-dimensional corollary of the killed-matrix formula, a resolvent
reformulation places all possible scalar poles of the Markov error
generating function in a reciprocal cyclotomic lift of the killed matrix
spectrum and gives a spectral-projection non-cancellation criterion for
which lifted eigenvalues are actually visible.  The H\"older
material is kept outside the main theorems: an appendix gives an
explicit \(C^\alpha\) open-transfer statement for finite-cylinder holes
after block recoding and separately proves the finite-prefix Gibbs
comparison used for escape exponents.  Neither appendix result is used in
the finite-state cyclotomic resolvent theorem.
\end{abstract}
