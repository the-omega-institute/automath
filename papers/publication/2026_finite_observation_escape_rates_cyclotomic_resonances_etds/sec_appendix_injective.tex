\appendix
\section{Injective refinements and hidden conditional entropy}
\label{sec:injective-appendix}

If a visible quotient collapses the survivor collision spectrum, then any
injective refinement that restores the microscopic R\'enyi profile must
pay exactly the hidden conditional entropy.

\begin{theorem}[Entropy ledger for finite refinements]
\label{thm:entropy-ledger}
Let \(f_m:\Sigma^m\to \mathcal{Y}_m\) be a map into a finite set and
\(U_m\) a \(\Sigma^m\)-valued random variable with law \(u_m\).  Set
\(Y_m:=f_m(U_m)\) and \(d_m(y):=\card{f_m^{-1}(y)}\).  Then
\[
  H(U_m\mid Y_m)
  = \EE[\log d_m(Y_m)]-D(u_m\Vert \widetilde u_m)
  \leq \EE[\log d_m(Y_m)],
\]
where \(\widetilde u_m(\omega):=\PP(Y_m=f_m(\omega))/d_m(f_m(\omega))\).
If \(r_m:\Sigma^m\to R_m\) makes \(\omega\mapsto (f_m(\omega),r_m(\omega))\)
injective, then \(H(r_m(U_m)\mid Y_m)=H(U_m\mid Y_m)\leq \log\card{R_m}\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Expanding relative entropy and rearranging yields the identity.  The
bound follows from non-negativity of relative entropy.  Injectivity of
the joint map forces \(H(r_m(U_m)\mid Y_m)=H(U_m\mid Y_m)\).
\end{proof}
