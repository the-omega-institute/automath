The Editors
Ergodic Theory and Dynamical Systems


Dear Editors,

We submit for your consideration the manuscript "Finite Observation of
Open Symbolic Dynamics: Escape Rates, Cyclotomic Resonances, and
Survivor Renyi Spectra."  The paper asks a single question: what does
the depth-m Bayes error of a binary coding detect about the open
dynamics of a symbolic hole?  The answer is that finite observation
recovers considerably more than the escape exponent alone.

The paper proves three main theorems in the setting of topologically
mixing subshifts of finite type with equilibrium Markov measures and
Markov holes.  First, the Bayes error for first-entry residue events
decays at the escape rate gamma_H(phi) = P_Y(phi) - P_{Y_H}(phi), and
its ratio to the survivor mass converges to a residue-dependent
quasistationary amplitude determined by the Perron eigenvector of the
killed chain (Theorem 1.1).  Second, the survivor Renyi pressure
spectrum s -> sP_{Y_H}(phi) - P_{Y_H}(s phi) governs the collision
behaviour of surviving samples, yielding Poisson birthday thresholds at
the scale binom(N_m,2) exp(-m h_{2,H}) ~ 1 via a Chen-Stein argument
(Theorem 1.2).  Third, the error generating function is a
residue-twisted killed resolvent whose poles belong to the q-th
cyclotomic lift of the open spectrum; under the standard spectral-gap
hypothesis, this resolvent picture extends from the finite-state setting
to open Ruelle operators for Holder potentials (Theorem 1.3).

These results connect the finite-observation theory of binary codings to
the spectral theory of open transfer operators, a subject to which ETDS
has made central contributions through the work of Demers, Young,
Ferguson, Pollicott, and Keller-Liverani, among others.  The paper
builds directly on the open-system thermodynamic formalism developed in
this journal and applies it to a new class of observables---Bayes error
profiles of first-entry residue events---that have not previously been
studied in this context.  We believe ETDS is the natural venue for this
work.

The manuscript is approximately 35 pages, has no figures, and contains
17 references.  All results are self-contained proofs; no numerical
computations or external data are required.

Sincerely,

The Authors
