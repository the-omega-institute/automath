\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The main point of the paper is that primitive orbit data for finite-group
extensions are naturally encoded by Adams--M\"obius inversion on class
functions. This yields determinant formulas for the primitive
Peter--Weyl channels and, under the hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\),
classwise Mertens asymptotics together with Fourier reconstruction at
the Perron point. The borderline case \(\rho(A_\varrho)=\lambda\) is
governed by an exact rigidity theorem: equality occurs precisely when
the twisted channel carries a cocycle-equivariant line field, and this
forces a one-dimensional quotient on the closed-path label group.

The finite-part formula and the cyclic-lift reconstruction provide the
finite-state model for these constructions, while the quotient formalism
and the \(S_3\)-example show that periodic and primitive data can
separate in non-trivial channels. In particular, if the closed-path
labels generate a perfect subgroup, the twisted spectral gap
\(\eta<\lambda\) is automatic in every non-trivial channel.
