\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper has a single finite-state message. At the scalar level,
ordinary M\"obius inversion yields the finite part at the Perron pole,
and in cyclic lifts the same passage becomes Fourier inversion on roots
of unity. For finite-group extensions, the genuinely new ingredient is
that primitive extraction takes place through Adams--M\"obius inversion
on class functions, which carries the periodic determinant data to the
primitive Peter--Weyl channels.

The unconditional content lies in the primitive formulas inside the disc
\(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\), while the Perron-point consequences require
\(\eta<\lambda\). Under that hypothesis one obtains classwise Mertens
constants and their non-abelian Fourier reconstruction; when
\(\max\{\Lambda(A),\eta\}>\lambda^{1/2}\), the same formalism identifies
the first spectral-edge correction. The rigidity criterion describes
precisely when Perron-point evaluation fails, and the quotient
formalism together with the \(S_3\)-example shows that cyclic or
abelian data need not detect the genuinely non-abelian primitive
contribution.

The final subsection shows that the same inversion package is not tied
to finite matrices. For real H\"older potentials on a mixing shift of
finite type, Adams--M\"obius inversion again governs the weighted
primitive class sums at the pressure point, and the same Fourier
reconstruction of the class constants persists under the twisted gap
\(\eta_\varphi<e^{P(\varphi)}\). Equality at the pressure boundary is
again equivalent to the existence of a cocycle-equivariant line field,
now in H\"older regularity.
