\section{Discussion and open problems}\label{sec:conclusion}

\subsection{Summary}

The main contribution of the paper is the Adams--M\"obius primitive
inversion mechanism for finite-group extensions. At the formal level,
it expresses each primitive Peter--Weyl channel directly in terms of
twisted determinant logarithms and Adams operations on class functions
(\Cref{cor:class-function-adams-mobius,%
cor:primitive-peter-weyl-determinant}). Under the additional hypothesis
\(\eta<\lambda\), this yields explicit class Mertens constants and a
non-abelian Fourier reconstruction at the Perron point
(\Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit,thm:class-mertens-fourier}).

The finite-part identity and cyclic-lift reconstruction of
\Cref{sec:finite-part} are included as supporting finite-state
coordinates rather than as separate main theorem lines. Likewise,
\Cref{sec:shadows} records the quotient interpretation needed to locate
the cyclically visible part of the primitive profile and closes with the
\(S_3\)-model, which shows that vanishing periodic traces in a
non-trivial channel do not force vanishing primitive bias.

\subsection{Open problems}

We close with two questions that remain outside the scope of the
present paper.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Criteria for the twisted spectral gap.} The condition
  \(\eta=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda\) is the
  essential extra input for the Perron-point formulas. It is not
  automatic. For canonical cyclic lifts,
  \Cref{rem:abelian-toy-model-cyclic} gives
  \(A_{\chi_j}=\omega_q^jA\), so every non-trivial cyclic character has
  spectral radius \(\lambda\). More generally,
  \Cref{prop:eta-gap-coboundary-obstruction} shows that a
  one-dimensional coboundary forces equality \(\rho(A_\chi)=\lambda\).
  By contrast, \Cref{rem:c2-example-explicit-gap} gives an explicit
  strict example with \(\eta=\sqrt2<2\). It would be useful to identify
  checkable hypotheses on the cocycle \(\tau\) that separate these
  equality mechanisms from genuine spectral gap. For structural
  background, compare Boyle--Schmieding
  \cite{BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions}, Sharp
  \cite{Sharp2020AmenableGroupExtensions}, and Stadlbauer
  \cite{Stadlbauer2013KestenAmenability,Stadlbauer2019ConformalMeasures}.

\item \emph{Transfer-operator extensions of the primitive calculus.}
  The present paper works entirely with finite matrices. For
  countable-state shifts or for Ruelle transfer operators on spaces of
  H\"older functions, one expects Fredholm-determinant analogues of the
  Adams--M\"obius formulas. Relating the finite-group primitive
  calculus developed here to that broader transfer-operator setting
  would connect the current results more directly with the functional
  framework of Baladi
  \cite{Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}, the
  regularity results of J\'ez\'equel
  \cite{Jezequel2019ParameterRegularity}, and the wider theory of
  dynamical zeta functions for group extensions.
\end{enumerate}
