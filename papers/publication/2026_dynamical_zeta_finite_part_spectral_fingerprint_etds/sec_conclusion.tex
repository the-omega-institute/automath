\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The results above show that the passage from determinant data to
primitive orbit data is more delicate for finite-group extensions than
it is at the periodic level. For a single primitive matrix this passage
leads to an explicit finite-part formula at the Perron pole, and for
cyclic lifts it recovers the reduced spectral polynomial from the
residue constants. In the finite-group setting, the corresponding
transform is given by M\"obius inversion together with the Adams
operations on class functions.

Under the hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\), these primitive formulas yield
classwise Mertens asymptotics and a Fourier inversion formula for the
associated constants. Above the square-root barrier
\(\max\{\Lambda(A),\eta\}>\lambda^{1/2}\), the classwise primitive
remainders carry an explicit secondary term governed by the spectral
edge of the twisted transition matrices, and the family of conjugacy
class counts detects that dominant modulus. The rigidity theorem
describes precisely when the condition \(\eta<\lambda\) can fail, while
the quotient formalism and the \(S_3\)-example show that cyclic data do
not in general exhaust the primitive non-abelian information.
