\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper isolates a primitive-level finite-state formalism. At the
matrix level, the finite part at the Perron pole and the residue
constants of cyclic lifts give reduced-determinant coordinates for the
same non-Perron spectrum. For finite-group extensions, the corresponding
primitive channels are recovered from the periodic determinant
logarithms by M\"obius inversion twisted by the Adams operations on
class functions.

Under the hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\), this primitive formalism yields
classwise Mertens constants and non-abelian Fourier reconstruction at
the Perron point. The rigidity theorem identifies exactly when such
evaluation can fail, and in particular forces a strict twisted gap when
the closed-path labels generate a perfect subgroup. The quotient
formalism and the \(S_3\)-example show that cyclic or periodic data need
not capture the full primitive non-abelian contribution.
