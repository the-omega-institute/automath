\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper separates two levels of orbit data for finite-group
extensions of mixing shifts of finite type. At the finite-state level,
\Cref{sec:finite-part} rewrites the classical Perron-pole constant in
reduced-determinant coordinates and shows that cyclic-lift residue
constants recover the reduced spectral polynomial. At the
finite-group level, \Cref{sec:chebotarev} introduces the
Adams--M\"obius inversion that extracts primitive Peter--Weyl channels
from twisted determinant logarithms, something not available from the
classical periodic determinant formalism alone.

Under the explicit twisted spectral-gap hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\),
this primitive calculus yields classwise Mertens constants and
non-abelian Fourier reconstruction at the Perron point. The quotient
compatibility of \Cref{sec:shadows} and the explicit \(S_3\)-example
show, in particular, that periodic vanishing in a non-trivial channel
does not preclude a non-zero primitive contribution. This identifies
the precise role of the Adams operations in the finite-group orbit
calculus developed here.
