\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper isolates one finite-state mechanism: once group labels are
present, determinant data do not descend to primitive orbit data by
M\"obius inversion alone, but by M\"obius inversion composed with the
Adams operations on class functions. The scalar finite-part identity
and the cyclic-lift spectral fingerprint are the one-channel and
abelian shadows of this mechanism, while the finite-group results
describe its full Peter--Weyl form.

Within the same symbolic framework, the rigidity theorem identifies
exactly when Perron-point evaluation can fail. Under the hypothesis
\(\eta<\lambda\), the primitive channels become evaluable at
\(z=\lambda^{-1}\), which yields explicit classwise Mertens constants
and a non-abelian Fourier reconstruction formula. The quotient
formalism then shows precisely which part of the primitive class
profile is visible in cyclic data, and the \(S_3\)-example exhibits a
genuinely non-abelian primitive bias that is invisible at the periodic
trace level.
