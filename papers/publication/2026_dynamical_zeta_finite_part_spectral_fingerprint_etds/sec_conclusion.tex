\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The results obtained above show that the finite part at the Perron pole
and the residue constants of cyclic lifts provide reduced-determinant
coordinates for the non-Perron spectrum of a primitive matrix. For
finite-group extensions, Adams--M\"obius inversion converts the twisted
determinant logarithms into primitive Peter--Weyl series.

Under the hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\), this leads to classwise Mertens
constants and a non-abelian Fourier inversion formula at the Perron
point. The rigidity theorem identifies precisely when the boundary case
\(\rho(A_\varrho)=\lambda\) can occur, while the quotient formalism and
the \(S_3\)-example show that cyclic or periodic data do not in general
determine the full primitive non-abelian contribution.
