\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper stays entirely in the finite-state matrix setting, and its
results are best read as different expressions of one
primitive-extraction principle. For a single primitive matrix, ordinary
M\"obius inversion yields the Perron-point finite part; for cyclic lifts,
the same mechanism becomes Fourier inversion on roots of unity and
recovers the reduced spectral polynomial; for finite-group extensions,
Adams--M\"obius inversion lifts this passage to the full Peter--Weyl
decomposition.

The unconditional content lies in the primitive formulas inside the disc
\(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\), while the Perron-point consequences require
\(\eta<\lambda\). Under that hypothesis one obtains classwise Mertens
constants, their non-abelian Fourier reconstruction, and, above the
square-root barrier, the first spectral-edge correction. The rigidity
criterion explains precisely when the Perron-point evaluation can fail,
and the quotient formalism together with the \(S_3\)-example shows that
cyclic or abelian data need not capture the genuinely non-abelian
primitive contribution.
