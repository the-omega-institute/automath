\section{Concluding remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The finite-state setting considered here isolates a single inversion
principle in several guises. For a primitive matrix, ordinary
M\"obius inversion yields the finite part at the Perron pole; for cyclic
lifts, the same mechanism becomes Fourier inversion on roots of unity
and recovers the reduced spectral polynomial; for finite-group
extensions, Adams--M\"obius inversion carries the passage to the full
Peter--Weyl decomposition.

The unconditional content lies in the primitive formulas inside the disc
\(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\), while the Perron-point consequences require
\(\eta<\lambda\). Under that hypothesis one obtains classwise Mertens
constants and their non-abelian Fourier reconstruction; when
\(\max\{\Lambda(A),\eta\}>\lambda^{1/2}\), the same formalism identifies
the first spectral-edge correction. The rigidity criterion describes
precisely when Perron-point evaluation fails, and the quotient
formalism together with the \(S_3\)-example shows that cyclic or
abelian data need not detect the genuinely non-abelian primitive
contribution.
