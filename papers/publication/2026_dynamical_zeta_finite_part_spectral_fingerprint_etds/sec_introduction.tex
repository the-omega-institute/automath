\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Let \((X_A^+,\sigma)\) be a mixing subshift of finite type with
primitive adjacency matrix \(A\). Its dynamical \(\zeta\)-function is
\[
  \zeta_A(z)
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\det(I-zA)^{-1}.
\]
Since the work of Artin--Mazur \cite{ArtinMazur1965PeriodicPoints},
Bowen--Lanford \cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta}, and Manning
\cite{Manning1971Axiom}, this function has provided a convenient bridge
between spectral data and orbit counting in symbolic and hyperbolic
dynamics; see also Ruelle
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic},
Parry--Pollicott \cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}, and Baladi
\cite{Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}. For a
primitive matrix the determinant formula describes the periodic data
completely. Primitive orbit data require a further inversion step, and
the behaviour at the Perron point is correspondingly subtler. The
purpose of the paper is to make this passage explicit, first in the
scalar finite-state setting and then for finite group extensions.

Write \(\lambda=\rho(A)\) for the Perron root. We consider the reduced
determinant
\[
  C(A):=\det\nolimits'(I-A/\lambda)^{-1}
\]
and the reduced spectral polynomial
\[
  F_A(t):=\frac{\det(I-tA/\lambda)}{1-t}
  =\prod_{j=2}^d\left(1-\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}t\right),
\]
where \(\mu_2,\dots,\mu_d\) are the non-Perron eigenvalues of \(A\). If
\(p_n(A)\) denotes the number of primitive orbits of length \(n\), we
also introduce the Abel-regularized primitive series
\[
  P_A(r):=\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)\lambda^{-n}r^n,
  \qquad
  \FP(A):=\lim_{r\uparrow 1}\bigl(P_A(r)+\log(1-r)\bigr).
\]
Our first aim is to express \(\FP(A)\) and related residue constants in
terms of reduced determinants. The second is to show that, for finite
group extensions, the same question leads naturally to a
representation-theoretic primitive inversion.

The basic mechanism is simple to state. In the scalar case, ordinary
M\"obius inversion removes repetitions of orbit length. For a cocycle
with values in a finite group \(G\), one must in addition account for
the effect of taking powers on the group label. This is encoded by the
Adams operations \(g\mapsto g^m\) on class functions. The correct
primitive transform is therefore an Adams--M\"obius transform. It
produces determinant formulas for the primitive Peter--Weyl channels and
the associated class constants.

Periodic determinant factorizations and leading Chebotarev asymptotics
for finite extensions are classical. What matters here is
the primitive-orbit refinement: we isolate the primitive Peter--Weyl
channels from the twisted determinant logarithms and use them to
describe the constant term at the Perron point. Accordingly, we
separate the identities that hold in the disc \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\) from
the Perron-point statements that require the twisted spectral gap
\(\eta<\lambda\).

\subsection{Main results}

We begin with the scalar finite-part formula at the Perron pole.

\begin{proposition}[Finite part at the Perron pole]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-part}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative matrix with Perron root
  \(\lambda=\rho(A)>1\), reduced determinant \(C(A)\), and primitive
  orbit counts \(p_n(A)\). Then
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{k\ge 2}\frac{\mu(k)}{k}
        \log\det(I-\lambda^{-k}A)^{-1}.
  \]
  Equivalently,
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)
        \bigl(\lambda^{-n}+\log(1-\lambda^{-n})\bigr).
  \]
  In particular,
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_n(A)}{\lambda^n}
    =\log N+\mathcal M(A)+o(1),
    \qquad
    \mathcal M(A):=\gamma+\FP(A).
  \]
\end{proposition}

The proof is given in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form,cor:finite-part-mertens-asymptotic}.
The asymptotic itself is classical; the point here is the explicit
description of the constant term in reduced-determinant coordinates.

The next result concerns cyclic lifts. For
\(A^{\langle q\rangle}=A\otimes P_q\), set
\[
  \Psi_q(A):=\log q+\log C(A^{\langle q\rangle}).
\]

\begin{proposition}[Cyclic reconstruction from cyclic lifts]
  \label{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix. Then the
  dimension \(d\) together with the sequence
  \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}_{q\ge 1}\) determines \(F_A\), and hence the
  non-Perron spectrum of \(A\) with algebraic multiplicity.
\end{proposition}

This is proved in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-spectrum-identifiability}. In the
cyclic situation, one-dimensional characters already recover reduced
spectral data from residue constants, and this serves as the abelian
model for the non-abelian Fourier inversion developed later.

We now turn to finite-group extensions. Let \(G\) be a finite group and
\(\tau:E_A\to G\) an edge cocycle. The associated skew product gives
twisted transition matrices \(A_\varrho\), indexed by
\(\varrho\in\Irr(G)\). For a class function \(\phi:G\to\CC\), write
\[
  \mathcal L_\phi(z)
  :=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{T_{\phi,n}}{n}z^n,
  \qquad
  \Pi_\phi(z):=\sum_{\gamma\in\cP}\phi(\tau(\gamma))z^{|\gamma|}.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Adams--M\"obius inversion and class constants]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-group}
  Let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle into a finite group, let
  \(A_\varrho\) be the twisted transition matrices, and let
  \(\phi:G\to\CC\) be a class function. Then:
  \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
    \item For \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\),
      \[
        \Pi_\phi(z)
        =\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}\,
          \mathcal L_{\psi^m\phi}(z^m).
      \]
    \item Assume that
      \[
        \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda.
      \]
      If \(C\subset G\) is a conjugacy class and \(p_{n,C}\) denotes
      the number of primitive orbits of length \(n\) with label in
      \(C\), then there exists a constant \(\mathcal M_C(A)\) such that
      \[
        p_{n,C}\sim \frac{|C|}{|G|}\frac{\lambda^n}{n},
      \]
      and
      \[
        \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_{n,C}}{\lambda^n}
        =\frac{|C|}{|G|}\log N+\mathcal M_C(A)+O(N^{-1}).
      \]
      Moreover, the deviations
      \[
        \Delta_C(A):=\mathcal M_C(A)-\frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)
      \]
      determine \(\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})\) for every non-trivial
      \(\varrho\) by non-abelian Fourier inversion.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

Part~\textup{(i)} is established in
\Cref{cor:class-function-adams-mobius} and requires no spectral-gap
hypothesis. Part~\textup{(ii)} is proved in
\Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit,thm:class-mertens-fourier}; here the
gap \(\eta<\lambda\) enters only in evaluating the non-trivial channels
at \(z=\lambda^{-1}\).

There is also a finite-state secondary-term refinement. If
\[
  R:=\max\{\Lambda(A),\eta\}>\lambda^{1/2},
\]
then the class-counting functions \(p_{n,C}\) admit an explicit first
correction term obtained from the spectral contributions with
\(|\alpha|=R\) in the trivial and non-trivial Peter--Weyl channels; see
\Cref{thm:class-mertens-secondary-term,cor:class-secondary-term-detects-spectrum}.
The same mechanism persists in the H\"older setting: every spectral
window above the larger of the common essential radius and \(e^{P/2}\)
produces an explicit weighted correction term built from the twisted
Ruelle projectors; see \Cref{thm:holder-transfer-spectral-window}.

We also analyse the hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\) itself. In
\Cref{subsec:eta-gap} we show that \(\rho(A_\varrho)=\lambda\) holds if
and only if the \(\varrho\)-channel carries a cocycle-equivariant line
field. In particular, the strict inequality follows whenever the
subgroup generated by closed-path labels is perfect. In the
one-dimensional case, the same condition reduces to a scalar
cohomological equation with constant phase.

Finally, the finite-state formalism extends to the H\"older
transfer-operator setting. For a real H\"older potential \(\varphi\) on
a mixing shift of finite type, with pressure \(P(\varphi)\), and a
locally constant finite-group cocycle, the same Adams--M\"obius
inversion yields weighted primitive class sums, pressure-point Mertens
constants, and explicit spectral-window expansions above the square-root
barrier; see
\Cref{thm:holder-transfer-perron-point,thm:holder-transfer-spectral-window,thm:holder-transfer-boundary-rigidity}.

\subsection{Background}

The prime orbit theorem for shifts of finite type goes back to Parry
\cite{Parry1983PrimeOrbit}. Mertens-type asymptotics for closed orbits
were established by Sharp \cite{Sharp1991Mertens}, with later
refinements in
\cite{Sharp1993PrimeOrbitAnnals,PollicottSharp1998Mertens}. In the
graph-theoretic setting one has closely related formulas for the Ihara
zeta function; see
\cite{StarkTerras1996ZetaGraphCoverings,StarkTerras2000ZetaGraphCoveringsII,%
TerrasStark2007ZetaGraphCoveringsIII,HasegawaSaito2016GraphMertens,%
HasegawaSaitoSato2015AbelianCovering,HasegawaSaitoSato2018GaloisCovering}.
Our scalar results should be viewed as finite-state prototypes, written
in reduced-determinant coordinates, for the finite-group statements that
follow.

For finite-group extensions, twisted Perron--Frobenius theory and
dynamical \(L\)-functions go back to Adachi--Sunada
\cite{AdachiSunada1987TwistedPerronFrobenius} and Sunada
\cite{Sunada1989DynamicalLFunctions}. Periodic determinant
factorizations and Chebotarev-type counting for finite homogeneous
extensions of shifts were established by Parry--Pollicott
\cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev} and Noorani--Parry
\cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts}; see also
\cite{PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev,Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian,%
BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions}. The present paper does not
revisit that periodic theory. Instead, it gives a primitive-orbit
version of it in the finite homogeneous SFT setting: the
Adams--M\"obius transform isolates the primitive Peter--Weyl channels,
produces explicit Perron-point constants under \(\eta<\lambda\), and
above the square-root barrier identifies the first spectral-edge
correction.

For related spectral and growth questions on covers and group
extensions, see Dougall--Sharp \cite{DougallSharp2021AnosovCovers},
Sharp \cite{Sharp2020AmenableGroupExtensions}, Stadlbauer
\cite{Stadlbauer2013KestenAmenability,Stadlbauer2019ConformalMeasures},
and Pollicott--Zhang \cite{PollicottZhang2025RapidMixing}.

\subsection{Structure of the paper}

The paper is organised as follows. In \S\ref{sec:preliminaries} we fix
notation for primitive orbit counts, reduced determinants, cyclic lifts,
and finite-group extensions. Section~\ref{sec:finite-part} treats the
scalar case, establishing the finite-part formula at the Perron point
and the cyclic reconstruction theorem. In
\S\ref{sec:chebotarev} we pass to finite-group extensions: first we
derive the primitive determinant formulas in the open disc, then the
Perron-point consequences under \(\eta<\lambda\), the spectral-edge
refinement above the square-root barrier, and finally a H\"older
transfer-operator extension with a projector-level spectral-window
expansion. Section~\ref{sec:shadows} records the
behaviour under quotients and gives an explicit \(S_3\)-example showing
that cyclic information does not in general detect the genuinely
non-abelian primitive contribution. The appendix contains the auxiliary
cyclic formulas and the orthogonal decomposition used in the discussion
of cyclic detection.
