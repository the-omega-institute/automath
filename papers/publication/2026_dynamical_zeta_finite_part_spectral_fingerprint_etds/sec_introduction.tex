\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Let \((X_A^+,\sigma)\) be a mixing subshift of finite type with
primitive adjacency matrix \(A\). Its dynamical \(\zeta\)-function is
\[
  \zeta_A(z)
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\det(I-zA)^{-1}.
\]
Since the work of Artin--Mazur \cite{ArtinMazur1965PeriodicPoints},
Bowen--Lanford \cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta}, and Manning
\cite{Manning1971Axiom}, the analytic behaviour of \(\zeta_A\) has been
closely linked with orbit counting in symbolic and hyperbolic dynamics;
see also Ruelle
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic},
Parry--Pollicott \cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}, and Baladi
\cite{Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}. If
\(\lambda=\rho(A)\) denotes the Perron root, then \(z=\lambda^{-1}\) is
a simple pole of \(\zeta_A\). At the periodic level the determinant
formula gives a complete finite-state description. For primitive orbit
data one must additionally disentangle repetitions, and the structure
at the Perron point is correspondingly less immediate. The aim of the
present paper is to make this passage explicit, first for a single
primitive matrix and then for finite group extensions of shifts of
finite type.

For a primitive matrix \(A\), write
\[
  C(A):=\det\nolimits'(I-A/\lambda)^{-1}
\]
for the reduced determinant at the Perron eigenvalue, and
\[
  F_A(t):=\frac{\det(I-tA/\lambda)}{1-t}
  =\prod_{j=2}^d\left(1-\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}t\right)
\]
for the reduced spectral polynomial, where
\(\mu_2,\dots,\mu_d\) are the non-Perron eigenvalues of \(A\). If
\(p_n(A)\) denotes the number of primitive orbits of length \(n\), we
also consider the Abel-regularised primitive series
\[
  P_A(r):=\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)\lambda^{-n}r^n,
  \qquad
  \FP(A):=\lim_{r\uparrow 1}\bigl(P_A(r)+\log(1-r)\bigr).
\]
The first part of the paper gives explicit formulas for \(\FP(A)\) and
for the residue constants of the cyclic lifts \(A^{\langle q\rangle}\).
These scalar formulas reappear later as the trivial and
one-dimensional models for the finite-group statements proved in
\Cref{sec:chebotarev,sec:shadows}.

The mechanism is straightforward to describe. In the scalar case, the
passage from periodic data to primitive data is governed by ordinary
M\"obius inversion. For finite group extensions one must also keep track
of the effect of taking powers on the group labels, and this brings in
the Adams operations \(g\mapsto g^m\) on class functions. The resulting
Adams--M\"obius transform is the basic device throughout the paper. It
produces primitive determinant formulas, classwise Mertens constants,
and a non-abelian Fourier reconstruction of those constants.

\subsection{Main results}

We begin with the scalar statement at the Perron pole.

\begin{proposition}[Finite part at the Perron pole]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-part}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative matrix with Perron root
  \(\lambda=\rho(A)>1\), reduced determinant \(C(A)\), and primitive
  orbit counts \(p_n(A)\). Then
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{k\ge 2}\frac{\mu(k)}{k}
        \log\det(I-\lambda^{-k}A)^{-1}.
  \]
  Equivalently,
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)
        \bigl(\lambda^{-n}+\log(1-\lambda^{-n})\bigr).
  \]
  In particular,
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_n(A)}{\lambda^n}
    =\log N+\mathcal M(A)+o(1),
    \qquad
    \mathcal M(A):=\gamma+\FP(A).
  \]
\end{proposition}

The proof is given in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form,cor:finite-part-mertens-asymptotic}.
The asymptotic itself is classical; the point here is the explicit
expression for the constant in reduced-determinant coordinates.

The second result concerns cyclic lifts. For
\(A^{\langle q\rangle}=A\otimes P_q\), set
\[
  \Psi_q(A):=\log q+\log C(A^{\langle q\rangle}).
\]

\begin{proposition}[Cyclic reconstruction from cyclic lifts]
  \label{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix. Then the
  dimension \(d\) together with the sequence
  \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}_{q\ge 1}\) determines \(F_A\), and hence the
  non-Perron spectrum of \(A\) with algebraic multiplicity.
\end{proposition}

This is proved in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-spectrum-identifiability}. A
single-root-of-unity refinement is deferred to the appendix. This
cyclic reconstruction theorem is the abelian prototype for the
Peter--Weyl Fourier inversion developed later: in the cyclic case,
one-dimensional characters already recover reduced spectral data from
residue constants.

We now pass to finite-group extensions. Let \(G\) be a finite group and
\(\tau:E_A\to G\) an edge cocycle. The associated skew product carries
twisted transition matrices \(A_\varrho\), indexed by
\(\varrho\in\Irr(G)\). For a class function \(\phi:G\to\CC\), write
\[
  \mathcal L_\phi(z)
  :=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{T_{\phi,n}}{n}z^n,
  \qquad
  \Pi_\phi(z):=\sum_{\gamma\in\cP}\phi(\tau(\gamma))z^{|\gamma|}.
\]
The next theorem identifies the primitive transform and records its
first Perron-point consequences.

\begin{theorem}[Adams--M\"obius inversion and class constants]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-group}
  Let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle into a finite group, let
  \(A_\varrho\) be the twisted transition matrices, and let
  \(\phi:G\to\CC\) be a class function. Then:
  \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
    \item For \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\),
      \[
        \Pi_\phi(z)
        =\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}\,
          \mathcal L_{\psi^m\phi}(z^m).
      \]
    \item Assume that
      \[
        \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda.
      \]
      If \(C\subset G\) is a conjugacy class and \(p_{n,C}\) denotes
      the number of primitive orbits of length \(n\) with label in
      \(C\), then there exists a constant \(\mathcal M_C(A)\) such that
      \[
        p_{n,C}\sim \frac{|C|}{|G|}\frac{\lambda^n}{n},
      \]
      and
      \[
        \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_{n,C}}{\lambda^n}
        =\frac{|C|}{|G|}\log N+\mathcal M_C(A)+O(N^{-1}).
      \]
      Moreover, the deviations
      \[
        \Delta_C(A):=\mathcal M_C(A)-\frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)
      \]
      determine \(\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})\) for every non-trivial
      \(\varrho\) by non-abelian Fourier inversion.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

The theorem separates the unconditional primitive transform from its
conditional Perron-point continuation. Part~\textup{(i)} is proved in
\Cref{cor:class-function-adams-mobius} and holds throughout the open
disc \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\) without any spectral-gap assumption.
Part~\textup{(ii)} is obtained in
\Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit,thm:class-mertens-fourier} by
combining the primitive channel formulas with the scalar finite-part
analysis; the hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\) enters only when one
evaluates the non-trivial channels at \(z=\lambda^{-1}\).

A further finite-state refinement isolates the first subleading term. If
\[
  R:=\max\{\Lambda(A),\eta\}>\lambda^{1/2},
\]
then each class-counting function \(p_{n,C}\) admits an explicit
secondary term obtained by summing the spectral-edge contributions
\(|\alpha|=R\) from the trivial and non-trivial Peter--Weyl channels.
After subtraction of the universal harmonic term
\((|C|/|G|)\sum_{n\le N}1/n\), the same spectral edge produces the
first correction to the classwise Mertens sums, and the family of
classwise primitive remainders detects \(R\) itself; see
\Cref{thm:class-mertens-secondary-term,cor:class-secondary-term-detects-spectrum}.

We also examine the condition \(\eta<\lambda\) itself. In
\Cref{subsec:eta-gap} we show that \(\rho(A_\varrho)=\lambda\) holds if
and only if the \(\varrho\)-channel carries a cocycle-equivariant line
field. In particular, the strict inequality holds automatically
whenever the subgroup generated by closed-path labels is perfect. In
the one-dimensional case the equality mechanism reduces to a scalar
cohomological equation with constant phase.

All arguments are carried out within the finite-dimensional
twisted-matrix formalism. We do not attempt a transfer-operator
generalisation beyond the finite-state setting. Rather, the purpose is
to isolate, in a concrete symbolic model, the primitive inversion
mechanism that lies behind the familiar periodic determinant identities.

\subsection{Background and related work}

The prime orbit theorem for shifts of finite type is classical; see
Parry \cite{Parry1983PrimeOrbit}. Mertens-type asymptotics for closed
orbits were established by Sharp \cite{Sharp1991Mertens}, with further
refinements in \cite{Sharp1993PrimeOrbitAnnals,PollicottSharp1998Mertens}.
In the graph-theoretic setting one has closely related formulas for the
Ihara zeta function; see
\cite{StarkTerras1996ZetaGraphCoverings,StarkTerras2000ZetaGraphCoveringsII,%
TerrasStark2007ZetaGraphCoveringsIII,HasegawaSaito2016GraphMertens,%
HasegawaSaitoSato2015AbelianCovering,HasegawaSaitoSato2018GaloisCovering}.
Those papers already contain determinant factorisations and Mertens
constants for Ihara and Artin--Ihara zeta functions of finite graph
coverings. The role of \Cref{thm:intro-finite-part} and
\Cref{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction} is correspondingly narrower:
they provide the scalar and cyclic finite-state prototypes, in
reduced-determinant coordinates, for the finite-group formulas proved
later.

For finite-group extensions, twisted Perron--Frobenius theory and
dynamical \(L\)-functions go back to Adachi--Sunada
\cite{AdachiSunada1987TwistedPerronFrobenius} and Sunada
\cite{Sunada1989DynamicalLFunctions}. The determinant factorisation and
Chebotarev-type orbit counting for finite homogeneous extensions of
shifts were established by Parry--Pollicott
\cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev} and Noorani--Parry
\cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts}; see also
\cite{PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev,Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian,%
BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions}. These works provide the
periodic determinant identities and the leading asymptotic
\(p_{n,C}\sim (|C|/|G|)\lambda^n/n\). The present paper does not claim a
new leading-density theorem or a new covering determinant factorisation.
Rather, the contribution is at the primitive level: the
Adams--M\"obius inversion isolates the primitive Peter--Weyl channels
from the periodic determinant logarithms, yields explicit Perron-point
constants under \(\eta<\lambda\), and, above the square-root barrier
\(R>\lambda^{1/2}\), identifies the first spectral-edge correction in
the finite homogeneous SFT setting.

For related spectral and growth questions on covers and group
extensions, compare Dougall--Sharp \cite{DougallSharp2021AnosovCovers},
Sharp \cite{Sharp2020AmenableGroupExtensions}, Stadlbauer
\cite{Stadlbauer2013KestenAmenability,Stadlbauer2019ConformalMeasures},
and Pollicott--Zhang \cite{PollicottZhang2025RapidMixing}.

\subsection{Organisation of the paper}

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation for primitive orbit counts,
reduced determinants, cyclic lifts, and finite-group extensions.
\Cref{sec:finite-part} treats the scalar case: the finite part at the
Perron pole and the cyclic reconstruction theorem. In
\Cref{sec:chebotarev} we pass to finite-group extensions, separating the
unconditional identities in the open disc from the Perron-point
consequences under \(\eta<\lambda\), and derive the secondary-term
refinement above the square-root barrier. \Cref{sec:shadows} discusses
quotients and an explicit \(S_3\)-example showing where cyclic data no
longer detect the genuinely non-abelian primitive contribution. The
appendix collects the auxiliary cyclic formulas and the orthogonal
decomposition used in the discussion of cyclic detection.
