\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Let \((X_A^+,\sigma)\) be a mixing subshift of finite type with
primitive adjacency matrix \(A\). Its dynamical \(\zeta\)-function is
given by
\[
  \zeta_A(z)
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\det(I-zA)^{-1}.
\]
Since the work of Artin--Mazur \cite{ArtinMazur1965PeriodicPoints},
Bowen--Lanford \cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta}, and
Manning \cite{Manning1971Axiom}, the analytic behaviour of
\(\zeta_A\) has been closely tied to orbit counting in symbolic and
hyperbolic dynamics; see also Ruelle
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic},
Parry--Pollicott \cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}, and Baladi
\cite{Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}. If
\(\lambda=\rho(A)\) is the
Perron root, then \(z_\ast=\lambda^{-1}\) is a simple pole of
\(\zeta_A\). In this paper we study how primitive orbit data at the
Perron point may be recovered from determinant data, first for a single
primitive matrix and then for finite-group extensions of shifts of
finite type.

Write
\[
  C(A):=\det\nolimits'(I-A/\lambda)^{-1}
\]
for the reduced determinant at the Perron eigenvalue, and
\[
  F_A(t):=\frac{\det(I-tA/\lambda)}{1-t}
  =\prod_{j=2}^d\left(1-\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}t\right)
\]
for the reduced spectral polynomial, where
\(\mu_2,\dots,\mu_d\) are the non-Perron eigenvalues of \(A\). If
\(p_n(A)\) denotes the number of primitive orbits of length \(n\), we
also consider the Abel-regularised primitive series
\[
  P_A(r):=\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)\lambda^{-n}r^n,
  \qquad
  \FP(A):=\lim_{r\uparrow 1}\bigl(P_A(r)+\log(1-r)\bigr).
\]
The results of \Cref{sec:finite-part} furnish the scalar and abelian
models for the finite-group statements proved later. They show how the
finite part at the Perron pole can be written in reduced-determinant
coordinates and how cyclic lifts encode the reduced spectral
polynomial.

The paper has a single conceptual theme. In the scalar case, one asks
how the primitive series at the Perron point is recovered from
determinant data. In the cyclic case, the same question becomes Fourier
analysis on roots of unity. In the finite-group case, it becomes
Peter--Weyl analysis on class functions. The genuinely new point is
that, in the non-abelian setting, primitive extraction is governed not
by bare M\"obius inversion but by M\"obius inversion intertwined with
the Adams operations. The scalar finite-part formula and the cyclic
reconstruction theorem are included as the one-channel and abelian
prototypes of that mechanism.

\subsection{Main results}

We therefore state the scalar and abelian prototypes first and then the
main finite-group theorem. Our first result identifies the finite part
at the Perron pole in reduced-determinant coordinates.

\begin{proposition}[Finite part at the Perron pole]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-part}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative matrix with Perron root
  \(\lambda=\rho(A)>1\), reduced determinant \(C(A)\), and primitive
  orbit counts \(p_n(A)\). Then
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{k\ge 2}\frac{\mu(k)}{k}
        \log\det(I-\lambda^{-k}A)^{-1}.
  \]
  Equivalently,
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)
        \bigl(\lambda^{-n}+\log(1-\lambda^{-n})\bigr).
  \]
  In particular,
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_n(A)}{\lambda^n}
    =\log N+\mathcal M(A)+o(1),
    \qquad
    \mathcal M(A):=\gamma+\FP(A).
  \]
\end{proposition}

This is proved in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form,cor:finite-part-mertens-asymptotic}.
The asymptotic itself is classical; the point here is the explicit
formula for the constant, which reappears in the twisted setting.

We next consider cyclic lifts. For \(A^{\langle q\rangle}=A\otimes P_q\),
set
\[
  \Psi_q(A):=\log q+\log C(A^{\langle q\rangle}).
\]

\begin{proposition}[Cyclic reconstruction from cyclic lifts]
  \label{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix. Then the
  dimension \(d\) together with the sequence
  \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}_{q\ge 1}\) determines \(F_A\), and hence the
  non-Perron spectrum of \(A\) with algebraic multiplicity.
\end{proposition}

The proof is given in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-spectrum-identifiability}; a
single-root-of-unity refinement is recorded in the appendix.

Now let \(G\) be a finite group and \(\tau:E_A\to G\) an edge cocycle.
The associated skew-product carries twisted transition matrices
\(A_\varrho\), indexed by
\(\varrho\in\Irr(G)\). For a class function \(\phi:G\to\CC\), write
\[
  \mathcal L_\phi(z)
  :=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{T_{\phi,n}}{n}z^n,
  \qquad
  \Pi_\phi(z):=\sum_{\gamma\in\cP}\phi(\tau(\gamma))z^{|\gamma|}.
\]
The main observation is that the passage from periodic data to primitive
data is governed not by M\"obius inversion alone, but by M\"obius
inversion intertwined with the Adams operations \(g\mapsto g^m\).

\begin{theorem}[Adams--M\"obius inversion and Perron-point consequences]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-group}
  Let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle into a finite group, let
  \(A_\varrho\) be the twisted transition matrices, and let
  \(\phi:G\to\CC\) be a class function. Then:
  \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
    \item For \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\),
      \[
        \Pi_\phi(z)
        =\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}\,
          \mathcal L_{\psi^m\phi}(z^m).
      \]
    \item Assume that
      \[
        \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda.
      \]
      If \(C\subset G\) is a conjugacy class and \(p_{n,C}\) denotes
      the number of primitive orbits of length \(n\) with label in
      \(C\), then there exists a constant \(\mathcal M_C(A)\) such that
      \[
        p_{n,C}\sim \frac{|C|}{|G|}\frac{\lambda^n}{n},
      \]
      and
      \[
        \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_{n,C}}{\lambda^n}
        =\frac{|C|}{|G|}\log N+\mathcal M_C(A)+O(N^{-1}).
      \]
      Moreover, the deviations
      \[
        \Delta_C(A):=\mathcal M_C(A)-\frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)
      \]
      determine \(\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})\) for every non-trivial
      \(\varrho\) by non-abelian Fourier inversion.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

Part~\textup{(i)} is proved in
\Cref{cor:class-function-adams-mobius}; the Perron-point statements in
part~\textup{(ii)} are obtained in
\Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit,thm:class-mertens-fourier}. The
hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\) is needed only to evaluate the non-trivial
channels at \(z=\lambda^{-1}\); the identities in the open disc
\(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\) are unconditional.

Part~\textup{(i)} is the central structural statement of the paper.
Part~\textup{(ii)} is its Perron-point package: once the non-trivial
primitive channels can be evaluated at \(z=\lambda^{-1}\), the
classwise Mertens constants and their Fourier reconstruction follow by
combining Peter--Weyl inversion with the scalar finite-part analysis.

The discussion of \(\eta<\lambda\) is sharpened in
\Cref{subsec:eta-gap}: an exact rigidity theorem shows that
\(\rho(A_\varrho)=\lambda\) holds precisely when the
\(\varrho\)-channel carries a cocycle-equivariant line field.
Consequently the strict gap is automatic whenever the closed-path
labels generate a perfect subgroup of \(G\), and the one-dimensional
borderline case is completely classified by a scalar cohomological
equation with a constant phase. Thus the paper does not produce a
general criterion for \(\eta<\lambda\), but it does identify the
equality mechanism exactly and extracts a usable unconditional
sufficient condition.

All arguments remain within the finite-dimensional twisted-matrix
formalism. We do not attempt a transfer-operator extension beyond the
finite-state setting, nor do we claim a new Axiom~A theorem. The point
is instead to make the primitive-level transform behind the classical
determinant identities explicit and to trace its scalar, abelian, and
non-abelian manifestations within one unified symbolic framework.

\subsection{Background and related work}

The prime orbit theorem for shifts of finite type is classical; see
Parry \cite{Parry1983PrimeOrbit}. Mertens-type asymptotics for closed
orbits were established by Sharp \cite{Sharp1991Mertens}, with further
refinements by Sharp \cite{Sharp1993PrimeOrbitAnnals} and
Pollicott--Sharp \cite{PollicottSharp1998Mertens}. In the graph-theoretic
setting one has analogous formulas for the Ihara zeta function; see
Stark--Terras
\cite{StarkTerras1996ZetaGraphCoverings,StarkTerras2000ZetaGraphCoveringsII,%
TerrasStark2007ZetaGraphCoveringsIII} and Hasegawa--Saito
\cite{HasegawaSaito2016GraphMertens}. \Cref{thm:intro-finite-part}
places the finite-state constant in reduced-determinant form, while
\Cref{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction} shows that the residue constants
of cyclic lifts determine the reduced spectrum.

For finite-group extensions, twisted Perron--Frobenius theory and
dynamical \(L\)-functions go back to Adachi--Sunada
\cite{AdachiSunada1987TwistedPerronFrobenius} and Sunada
\cite{Sunada1989DynamicalLFunctions}. The determinant factorisation and
Chebotarev-type orbit counting for finite homogeneous extensions of
shifts were established by Parry--Pollicott
\cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev} and Noorani--Parry
\cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts}; see also
\cite{PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev,Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian,
BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions}.

These works provide the periodic determinant identities, the twisted
\(L\)-function formalism, and the leading asymptotic
\(p_{n,C}\sim (|C|/|G|)\lambda^n/n\). The present paper does not seek to
improve that periodic-level theory. Its contribution is to isolate,
within the same finite-state formalism, the additional operation needed
to descend from periodic data to primitive data in a Peter--Weyl
channel. A direct M\"obius transform of a single twisted logarithm
still retains the terms \(\chi_\varrho(g^r)\) coming from repeated
orbit labels, and the Adams operations remove exactly this obstruction.

The broader context includes growth and spectral questions for covers
and group extensions; see, for instance, Dougall--Sharp
\cite{DougallSharp2021AnosovCovers}, Sharp
\cite{Sharp2020AmenableGroupExtensions}, Stadlbauer
\cite{Stadlbauer2013KestenAmenability,Stadlbauer2019ConformalMeasures},
and Pollicott--Zhang \cite{PollicottZhang2025RapidMixing}. The results
proved here concern the primitive orbit series attached to the twisted
channels, the rigidity of the Perron boundary \(\rho(A_\varrho)=\lambda\),
and the resulting Perron-point constants.

\subsection{Organisation of the paper}

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation for primitive orbit counts,
reduced determinants, cyclic lifts, and finite-group extensions.
\Cref{sec:finite-part} proves the finite-part formula at the Perron
pole and the cyclic-lift reconstruction of the reduced spectrum.
\Cref{sec:chebotarev} develops the Adams--M\"obius formalism for
finite-group extensions, proves the determinant formulas for primitive
Peter--Weyl channels, and derives classwise Mertens asymptotics under
the hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\). \Cref{sec:shadows} records the
behaviour under quotients, identifies the cyclic shadow of the
primitive class profile, and studies an explicit \(S_3\)-example. The
appendix contains auxiliary cyclic-lift identities and the orthogonal
decomposition underlying cyclic detection.
