\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

The dynamical \(\zeta\)-function of a mixing subshift of finite type
\((X_A^+,\sigma)\) with primitive adjacency matrix \(A\) is the
rational function
\[
  \zeta_A(z)
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\det(I-zA)^{-1}.
\]
Since Bowen--Lanford \cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta} and Manning
\cite{Manning1971Axiom}, the analytic properties of \(\zeta_A\) have
been central to the orbit-counting theory of symbolic dynamics, with
further development through the thermodynamic formalism of Ruelle
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic} and the
transfer-operator programme of Parry--Pollicott
\cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}. The spectral theory of the associated
Ruelle operator underlies both the prime orbit theorem and equidistribution
results for periodic orbits in hyperbolic dynamics
\cite{Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,ParryPollicott1990Zeta}.

The Perron root \(\lambda=\rho(A)\) gives \(\zeta_A\) a simple pole at
\(z_\ast=\lambda^{-1}\). This paper studies the finite part at that
pole and shows that the resulting invariant organises both cyclic
reconstruction and finite-group extensions of shifts into a single
primitive-orbit determinant calculus.

After removing the Perron factor one obtains the reduced determinant
\[
  C(A):=\det\nolimits'(I-A/\lambda)^{-1}
\]
and the reduced spectral polynomial
\[
  F_A(t):=\frac{\det(I-tA/\lambda)}{1-t}
  =\prod_{j=2}^d\left(1-\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}t\right),
\]
where \(\mu_2,\dots,\mu_d\) are the non-Perron eigenvalues. The
Abel-regularised finite-part constant
\[
  \FP(A):=\lim_{r\uparrow 1}\bigl(P_A(r)+\log(1-r)\bigr),
  \qquad
  P_A(r):=\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)\lambda^{-n}r^n,
\]
measures the deviation of the primitive orbit sum from the harmonic
series. Our first main result gives a closed formula for this quantity.

\begin{theorem}[Theorem A: Finite-part formula at the Perron pole]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-part}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative matrix with Perron root
  \(\lambda=\rho(A)>1\), reduced determinant \(C(A)\), and primitive
  orbit counts \(p_n(A)\). Then
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{k\ge 2}\frac{\mu(k)}{k}
        \log\det(I-\lambda^{-k}A)^{-1}.
  \]
  Equivalently,
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)
        \bigl(\lambda^{-n}+\log(1-\lambda^{-n})\bigr).
  \]
  In particular, the orbit-Mertens constant
  \(\mathcal M(A)=\gamma+\FP(A)\) satisfies
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_n(A)}{\lambda^n}
    =\log N+\mathcal M(A)+o(1).
  \]
\end{theorem}

This is the SFT analogue of Mertens' theorem for primes.
\Cref{thm:intro-finite-part} is proved in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form,cor:finite-part-mertens-asymptotic}.

The second result addresses spectral reconstruction from
root-of-unity data, a theme connected to inverse spectral problems for
SFTs \cite{LindMarcus1995}.

\begin{theorem}[Theorem B: Cyclic reconstruction of the reduced spectrum]
  \label{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix, and set
  \[
    \Psi_q(A):=\log q+\log C(A^{\langle q\rangle}),
  \]
  where \(A^{\langle q\rangle}=A\otimes P_q\) is the cyclic lift.
  Then:
  \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
    \item the sequence \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}_{q\ge 1}\) determines \(F_A\),
      and hence the non-Perron spectrum of \(A\) with algebraic
      multiplicity;
    \item if \(q\ge d\), then the single root-of-unity layer
      \(\{F_A(\omega_q^j)\}_{0\le j\le q-1}\) already determines every
      coefficient of \(F_A\);
    \item the exponential fingerprint satisfies
      \[
        \limsup_{q\to\infty}\abs{\Psi_q(A)}^{1/q}
        =
        \Lambda(A)/\lambda.
      \]
  \end{enumerate}
\leanverified{paper_etds_intro_cyclic_reconstruction_seeds}
\end{theorem}

The proof combines M\"obius inversion on multiples with Newton's
identities; see
\Cref{thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-spectrum-identifiability,%
thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-single-q-torsion-reconstruction,%
cor:finite-part-cyclic-lift-zeta-torsion-determines-spectrum}.
The terminology of cyclic, torsion, and exponential spectral
fingerprints is fixed in \Cref{def:spectral-fingerprints}.

For finite-group extensions the situation is genuinely different. In
the Chebotarev-type theory initiated by Parry--Pollicott
\cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev} and, in the finite homogeneous SFT
setting, carried out by Noorani--Parry
\cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts}, and further developed in
\cite{PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev,%
BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions,%
Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian},
the Peter--Weyl decomposition of
the extension cocycle leads to twisted transition matrices
\(A_\varrho\) and their determinants; for the representation-ring
background behind the Adams operations, compare
\cite{Serre1977LinearRepresentations}. We show that passing from
periodic data to primitive data requires not M\"obius inversion alone,
but M\"obius inversion intertwined with the Adams operations
\(g\mapsto g^m\) on the class functions of~\(G\).

\begin{theorem}[Theorem C: Primitive determinant calculus for
  finite-group extensions]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-group}
  Let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle into a finite group, let
  \(A_\varrho\) be the twisted transition matrices, and for a class
  function \(\phi:G\to\CC\) write
  \[
    \mathcal L_\phi(z)
    :=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{T_{\phi,n}}{n}z^n,
    \qquad
    \Pi_\phi(z):=\sum_{\gamma\in\cP}\phi(\tau(\gamma))z^{|\gamma|}.
  \]
  Then:
  \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
    \item \textup{(Adams--M\"obius inversion.)} Class-function
      logarithms linearise the twisted determinant data, and
      Adams--M\"obius inversion expresses each primitive Peter--Weyl
      series directly in terms of the twisted determinants:
      \[
        \Pi_\phi(z)
        =\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}\,
          \mathcal L_{\psi^m\phi}(z^m).
      \]
    \item \textup{(Chebotarev density and class Mertens constants.)}
      Assume
      \[
        \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda.
      \]
      Then for every conjugacy class \(C\subset G\) the primitive
      counts satisfy the Chebotarev density law
      \[
        p_{n,C}\sim \frac{|C|}{|G|}\frac{\lambda^n}{n},
      \]
      and the partial sums satisfy
      \[
        \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_{n,C}}{\lambda^n}
        =\frac{|C|}{|G|}\log N+\mathcal M_C(A)+O(N^{-1}),
      \]
      and the deviations
      \(\Delta_C(A):=\mathcal M_C(A)-\frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)\)
      reconstruct \(\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})\) for every non-trivial
      \(\varrho\) by non-abelian Fourier inversion.
    \item \textup{(Quotient functoriality.)} Quotient maps
      \(G\twoheadrightarrow Q\) intertwine primitive class profiles.
      Cyclic quotient observables span exactly the one-dimensional
      Peter--Weyl sector, exhausting the abelian shadow and remaining
      orthogonal to the genuine non-abelian defect.
  \end{enumerate}
  An explicit \(S_3\)-extension shows that vanishing periodic traces
  does not force vanishing primitive bias: the sign-character periodic
  channel vanishes identically, yet the corresponding primitive channel
  is non-zero.
\end{theorem}

The detailed statements underlying Theorem~C are proved in
\Cref{thm:class-function-linearisation,%
cor:class-function-adams-mobius,%
cor:primitive-peter-weyl-determinant,%
thm:class-mertens-explicit,%
thm:class-mertens-fourier}
and in
\Cref{thm:quotient-functoriality,%
thm:abelian-shadow-defect,%
thm:cyclic-detection-boundary,%
cor:s3-example-profile}.
The determinant identities and primitive inversion of Theorem~C\textup{(i)}
are unconditional inside the open Perron disc. The Perron-point class
constants and their Fourier reconstruction in Theorem~C\textup{(ii)}
use the explicit hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\), which is assumed rather
than derived in the present paper.

\subsection{Relation to existing work}

The dynamical \(\zeta\)-function for SFTs goes back to
Artin--Mazur \cite{ArtinMazur1965PeriodicPoints} and Bowen--Lanford
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom}. The prime-orbit
asymptotic for shifts of finite type is classical: Parry
\cite{Parry1983PrimeOrbit} proved the analogue of the prime number
theorem for closed orbits of SFTs and their suspensions. The existence
of Mertens-type asymptotics for Axiom~A flows was established by Sharp
\cite{Sharp1991Mertens}. The present paper does not claim a new
prime-orbit theorem; its main contributions are instead the explicit
finite-part identity at the Perron pole (the constant in the Mertens
asymptotic as an explicit determinant expression) and the Adams--M\"obius
reconstruction of primitive Peter--Weyl channels from twisted determinant
data.

The transfer-operator approach is developed systematically in
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic,%
ParryPollicott1990Zeta,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer}.
Twisted determinant
factorisations for finite-group extensions appear in
\cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev,%
NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts,%
PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev,%
BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions,%
Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian}; Tauberian refinements and closed-orbit
asymptotics are treated in \cite{Pollicott1992AgmonTauberian} and the
monograph \cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}. For the functorial
orbit-counting perspective underlying Section~\ref{sec:shadows} one
should also compare \cite{PakapongpunWard2009FunctorialOrbit}.
For graph coverings one should compare the Ihara--Bass type
determinantal identities and their weighted variants
\cite{Bass1992Ihara,%
ChoeKwakParkSato2007Bartholdi,%
Sato2008WeightedBartholdiCoverings}.
For more recent perspectives on dynamical zeta functions and dynamical
determinants beyond the classical symbolic-dynamics sources, compare
Baladi's modern monograph \cite{Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}, the
parameter-regularity results of J\'ez\'equel
\cite{Jezequel2019ParameterRegularity}, and Shen's work on twisted
Ruelle zeta functions on locally symmetric spaces
\cite{Shen2023ComplexValuedAnalyticTorsion}. Our results stay in the
finite-state symbolic setting but fit naturally into this broader
determinant-based programme.

The relation between the present results and their closest antecedents
may be organised along three threads. The first thread is classical
\(\zeta\)-rationality and prime-orbit counting for shifts of finite
type, from which we extract the finite-part formula at the Perron pole.
The second is root-of-unity sampling for cyclic covers, which becomes a
reconstruction mechanism for the reduced spectrum. The third is the
Chebotarev theory of finite-group extensions, where the new ingredient
is the Adams--M\"obius passage from twisted determinant data to
primitive Peter--Weyl series. The appendices record secondary
consequences of these three main threads rather than additional primary
theorem chains.

\subsection{Plan of the paper}

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation for primitive orbit counts,
reduced determinants, cyclic lifts, and finite-group extensions.
\Cref{sec:finite-part} proves Theorem~A and Theorem~B:
the finite-part formula and the cyclic reconstruction of the reduced
spectrum. \Cref{sec:chebotarev} develops the
Adams--M\"obius mechanism for finite-group extensions, derives the
primitive Peter--Weyl series, and establishes the class Mertens theorem
under a twisted spectral gap. \Cref{sec:shadows} proves quotient
functoriality, identifies the abelian shadow and the exact boundary of
cyclic detection, and closes with the \(S_3\)-model demonstrating the
Adams obstruction. The appendices collect secondary consequences:
holomorphic variation of the finite part (\Cref{app:variation}),
arithmetic root-of-unity corollaries including a Dirichlet-character
inversion (\Cref{app:arithmetic}), and rigidity statements for growing
families (\Cref{app:rigidity}).
