\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

The dynamical \(\zeta\)-function of a mixing subshift of finite type
\((X_A^+,\sigma)\) with primitive adjacency matrix \(A\) is the
rational function
\[
  \zeta_A(z)
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\det(I-zA)^{-1}.
\]
Since Bowen--Lanford \cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta} and Manning
\cite{Manning1971Axiom}, the analytic properties of \(\zeta_A\) have
been central to the orbit-counting theory of symbolic dynamics, with
further development through the thermodynamic formalism of Ruelle
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic} and the
transfer-operator programme of Parry--Pollicott
\cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}. The spectral theory of the associated
Ruelle operator underlies both the prime orbit theorem and equidistribution
results for periodic orbits in hyperbolic dynamics
\cite{Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,ParryPollicott1990Zeta}.

The Perron root \(\lambda=\rho(A)\) gives \(\zeta_A\) a simple pole at
\(z_\ast=\lambda^{-1}\). The existence of Mertens-type asymptotics at
this pole is classical; an explicit Mertens constant in the
Axiom~A flow setting was established by Sharp
\cite{Sharp1991Mertens}, and the same structural form appears in the
orbit-counting framework of Pollicott--Sharp
\cite{PollicottSharp1998Mertens} and in the graph-theoretic context of
Stark--Terras \cite{StarkTerras1996ZetaGraphCoverings}.

The main contribution of this paper is an Adams--M\"obius primitive
inversion mechanism for finite-group extensions, expressed in
Peter--Weyl coordinates, that turns twisted determinant data into
explicit primitive-channel series. The cyclic reconstruction and
finite-part formulas of \Cref{sec:finite-part} provide the supporting
abelian infrastructure; they specialise the classical dynamical Mertens
asymptotic to the reduced-determinant language of finite matrices
rather than claiming a new prime-orbit theorem.

After removing the Perron factor one obtains the reduced determinant
\[
  C(A):=\det\nolimits'(I-A/\lambda)^{-1}
\]
and the reduced spectral polynomial
\[
  F_A(t):=\frac{\det(I-tA/\lambda)}{1-t}
  =\prod_{j=2}^d\left(1-\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}t\right),
\]
where \(\mu_2,\dots,\mu_d\) are the non-Perron eigenvalues. The
Abel-regularised finite-part constant
\[
  \FP(A):=\lim_{r\uparrow 1}\bigl(P_A(r)+\log(1-r)\bigr),
  \qquad
  P_A(r):=\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)\lambda^{-n}r^n,
\]
measures the deviation of the primitive orbit sum from the harmonic
series. The following result records a closed determinant formula for
this constant. Its existence follows from classical Mertens-type
asymptotics \cite{Sharp1991Mertens,PollicottSharp1998Mertens}; the
contribution here is the explicit reduced-determinant expression in the
finite-state setting.

\begin{theorem}[Theorem A: Finite-part formula at the Perron pole]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-part}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative matrix with Perron root
  \(\lambda=\rho(A)>1\), reduced determinant \(C(A)\), and primitive
  orbit counts \(p_n(A)\). Then
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{k\ge 2}\frac{\mu(k)}{k}
        \log\det(I-\lambda^{-k}A)^{-1}.
  \]
  Equivalently,
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)
        \bigl(\lambda^{-n}+\log(1-\lambda^{-n})\bigr).
  \]
  In particular, the orbit-Mertens constant
  \(\mathcal M(A)=\gamma+\FP(A)\) satisfies
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_n(A)}{\lambda^n}
    =\log N+\mathcal M(A)+o(1).
  \]
\end{theorem}

\Cref{thm:intro-finite-part} is proved in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form,cor:finite-part-mertens-asymptotic}.

The second result addresses spectral reconstruction from
root-of-unity data, a theme connected to inverse spectral problems for
SFTs \cite{LindMarcus1995}.

\begin{theorem}[Theorem B: Cyclic reconstruction of the reduced spectrum]
  \label{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix, and set
  \[
    \Psi_q(A):=\log q+\log C(A^{\langle q\rangle}),
  \]
  where \(A^{\langle q\rangle}=A\otimes P_q\) is the cyclic lift.
  Then:
  \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
    \item the dimension \(d\) together with the sequence
      \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}_{q\ge 1}\) determines \(F_A\), and hence the
      non-Perron spectrum of \(A\) with algebraic multiplicity;
    \item if \(q\ge d\), then the single root-of-unity layer
      \(\{F_A(\omega_q^j)\}_{0\le j\le q-1}\) already determines every
      coefficient of \(F_A\);
    \item the exponential fingerprint satisfies
      \[
        \limsup_{q\to\infty}\abs{\Psi_q(A)}^{1/q}
        =
        \Lambda(A)/\lambda.
      \]
  \end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

The proof combines M\"obius inversion on multiples with Newton's
identities; see
\Cref{thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-spectrum-identifiability,%
thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-single-q-torsion-reconstruction,%
cor:finite-part-cyclic-lift-zeta-torsion-determines-spectrum}.
The terminology of cyclic, torsion, and exponential spectral
fingerprints is fixed in \Cref{def:spectral-fingerprints}.

For finite-group extensions the situation is genuinely different. In
the Chebotarev-type theory initiated by Parry--Pollicott
\cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev} and, in the finite homogeneous SFT
setting, carried out by Noorani--Parry
\cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts}, and further developed in
\cite{PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev,%
BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions,%
Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian},
the Peter--Weyl decomposition of
the extension cocycle leads to twisted transition matrices
\(A_\varrho\) and their determinants; for the representation-ring
background behind the Adams operations, compare
\cite{Serre1977LinearRepresentations}. We show that passing from
periodic data to primitive data requires not M\"obius inversion alone,
but M\"obius inversion intertwined with the Adams operations
\(g\mapsto g^m\) on the class functions of~\(G\).

\begin{theorem}[Theorem C: Primitive determinant calculus for
  finite-group extensions]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-group}
  Let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle into a finite group, let
  \(A_\varrho\) be the twisted transition matrices, and for a class
  function \(\phi:G\to\CC\) write
  \[
    \mathcal L_\phi(z)
    :=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{T_{\phi,n}}{n}z^n,
    \qquad
    \Pi_\phi(z):=\sum_{\gamma\in\cP}\phi(\tau(\gamma))z^{|\gamma|}.
  \]
  Then:
  \begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
    \item \textup{(Adams--M\"obius inversion.)} Class-function
      logarithms linearise the twisted determinant data, and
      Adams--M\"obius inversion expresses each primitive Peter--Weyl
      series directly in terms of the twisted determinants:
      \[
        \Pi_\phi(z)
        =\sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}\,
          \mathcal L_{\psi^m\phi}(z^m).
      \]
    \item \textup{(Chebotarev density and class Mertens constants.)}
      Assume
      \[
        \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda.
      \]
      Then for every conjugacy class \(C\subset G\) the primitive
      counts satisfy the Chebotarev density law
      \[
        p_{n,C}\sim \frac{|C|}{|G|}\frac{\lambda^n}{n},
      \]
      and the partial sums satisfy
      \[
        \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_{n,C}}{\lambda^n}
        =\frac{|C|}{|G|}\log N+\mathcal M_C(A)+O(N^{-1}),
      \]
      and the deviations
      \(\Delta_C(A):=\mathcal M_C(A)-\frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)\)
      reconstruct \(\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})\) for every non-trivial
      \(\varrho\) by non-abelian Fourier inversion.
    \item \textup{(Quotient functoriality.)} Quotient maps
      \(G\twoheadrightarrow Q\) intertwine primitive class profiles.
      Cyclic quotient observables span exactly the one-dimensional
      Peter--Weyl sector, exhausting the abelian shadow and remaining
      orthogonal to the genuine non-abelian defect.
  \end{enumerate}
  An explicit \(S_3\)-extension shows that vanishing periodic traces
  does not force vanishing primitive bias: the sign-character periodic
  channel vanishes identically, yet the corresponding primitive channel
  is non-zero.
\end{theorem}

The detailed statements underlying Theorem~C are proved in
\Cref{thm:class-function-linearisation,%
cor:class-function-adams-mobius,%
cor:primitive-peter-weyl-determinant,%
thm:class-mertens-explicit,%
thm:class-mertens-fourier}
and in
\Cref{thm:quotient-functoriality,%
thm:abelian-shadow-defect,%
thm:cyclic-detection-boundary,%
cor:s3-example-profile}.
The determinant identities and primitive inversion of Theorem~C\textup{(i)}
are unconditional inside the open Perron disc. The Perron-point class
constants and their Fourier reconstruction in Theorem~C\textup{(ii)}
rely on the explicit hypothesis \(\eta<\lambda\), which is assumed
throughout.  The present paper does not derive this strict inequality
from separate dynamical criteria; for a discussion of conditions
guaranteeing it, see
\cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev,BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions}.

\subsection{Relation to existing work}\label{subsec:prior-work}

\subsubsection*{Dynamical Mertens constants and Theorem~A}
The dynamical \(\zeta\)-function for SFTs goes back to
Artin--Mazur \cite{ArtinMazur1965PeriodicPoints} and Bowen--Lanford
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom}. The prime-orbit
asymptotic for shifts of finite type is classical: Parry
\cite{Parry1983PrimeOrbit} proved the analogue of the prime number
theorem for closed orbits of SFTs and their suspensions. The existence
of Mertens-type asymptotics for Axiom~A flows was established by Sharp
\cite{Sharp1991Mertens}; see also \cite{Sharp1993PrimeOrbitAnnals}
for multi-dimensional refinements. Pollicott--Sharp
\cite{PollicottSharp1998Mertens} give explicit Mertens-constant
formulas in homology classes, and graph-theoretic analogues appear in
the covering-zeta literature of Stark--Terras
\cite{StarkTerras1996ZetaGraphCoverings}. \emph{Theorem~A does not
claim a new prime-orbit theorem.} Its contribution is the explicit
expression of the classical Mertens constant as a reduced-determinant
identity in the primitive-matrix language, which serves as the abelian
infrastructure for the Adams--M\"obius mechanism. The structural form
of the constant is close to those in \cite{Sharp1991Mertens,%
PollicottSharp1998Mertens}; the increment is the translation into
finite-state determinant coordinates and the resulting cyclic-lift
reconstruction.

\subsubsection*{Twisted \(L\)-functions and Theorem~C}
The twisted Perron--Frobenius theorem and the associated dynamical
\(L\)-functions were introduced by Adachi--Sunada
\cite{AdachiSunada1987TwistedPerronFrobenius} and Sunada
\cite{Sunada1989DynamicalLFunctions}. The Peter--Weyl determinant
factorisation of the extension zeta function and Chebotarev-type
equidistribution for finite-group extensions of Axiom~A systems are
due to Parry--Pollicott \cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev}; the
finite homogeneous SFT case was carried out by Noorani--Parry
\cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts}. Further developments include
the Chebotarev results of Pollicott--Sharp
\cite{PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev} in homology classes, Parry's
non-abelian Liv\v{s}ic theorem \cite{Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian}, and
the \(K\)-theory approach of Boyle--Schmieding
\cite{BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions}.

In this context, the \emph{leading Chebotarev asymptotic}
\(p_{n,C}\sim (|C|/|G|)\lambda^n/n\) in
\Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit} is known from
\cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts,%
ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev}. The new content of Theorem~C is:
\begin{itemize}
  \item the Adams--M\"obius primitive inversion formula
    (\Cref{cor:class-function-adams-mobius,%
    cor:primitive-peter-weyl-determinant}), which expresses the
    primitive Peter--Weyl series directly in terms of twisted
    determinants and the Adams operations on the character ring;
  \item the explicit class Mertens constant-term packaging
    (\Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit,%
    thm:class-mertens-fourier}), conditional on the twisted spectral
    gap \(\eta<\lambda\), and the non-abelian Fourier reconstruction
    of these constants;
  \item the identification of the abelian shadow and the genuine
    non-abelian defect via quotient functoriality
    (\Cref{thm:abelian-shadow-defect,thm:cyclic-detection-boundary}).
\end{itemize}
All three items are stated and proved in the determinant language of
finite-state symbolic dynamics. The first item is not a consequence of
the existing literature on twisted \(L\)-functions, though it is
conceptually close: the standard Adachi--Sunada
\cite{AdachiSunada1987TwistedPerronFrobenius} and Parry--Pollicott
\cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev} frameworks provide determinant
factorisations at the periodic level, but do not explicitly perform the
Adams--M\"obius passage to primitive data.

\subsubsection*{Covering-zeta and graph-theoretic context}
The Ihara--Bass determinantal identities and their weighted variants
\cite{Bass1992Ihara,ChoeKwakParkSato2007Bartholdi,%
Sato2008WeightedBartholdiCoverings,%
StarkTerras1996ZetaGraphCoverings}
provide the graph-theoretic counterpart of the covering-space
factorisations used here. For more recent perspectives on dynamical
zeta functions and dynamical determinants beyond the classical
symbolic-dynamics sources, compare Baladi's monograph
\cite{Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}, the parameter-regularity results of
J\'ez\'equel \cite{Jezequel2019ParameterRegularity}, and Shen's work
on twisted Ruelle zeta functions on locally symmetric spaces
\cite{Shen2023ComplexValuedAnalyticTorsion}. The transfer-operator
approach is developed systematically in
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic,%
ParryPollicott1990Zeta,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer};
Tauberian refinements and closed-orbit asymptotics are treated in
\cite{Pollicott1992AgmonTauberian}. For the functorial orbit-counting
perspective underlying Section~\ref{sec:shadows} one should also
compare \cite{PakapongpunWard2009FunctorialOrbit}.

\subsubsection*{Structure of the paper's contribution}
The appendices record secondary consequences of the main threads rather
than additional primary theorem chains.

\subsection{Plan of the paper}

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation for primitive orbit counts,
reduced determinants, cyclic lifts, and finite-group extensions.
\Cref{sec:finite-part} proves Theorem~A and Theorem~B:
the finite-part formula (a reduced-determinant specialisation of the
classical Mertens asymptotic) and the cyclic reconstruction of the
reduced spectrum. \Cref{sec:chebotarev}, which forms the core of the
paper, develops the Adams--M\"obius mechanism for finite-group
extensions, derives the primitive Peter--Weyl series, and establishes
the class Mertens theorem under a twisted spectral gap; a dictionary
with the classical twisted \(L\)-function framework is included.
\Cref{sec:shadows} identifies the abelian shadow and the exact
boundary of cyclic detection, and closes with the \(S_3\)-model
demonstrating the Adams obstruction. The appendices collect secondary
consequences: holomorphic variation of the finite part
(\Cref{app:variation}), arithmetic root-of-unity corollaries
(\Cref{app:arithmetic}), and rigidity statements for growing families
(\Cref{app:rigidity}). All appendix results are routine consequences
of the main-text formulas and are included for completeness.
