\title[Finite parts of dynamical $\zeta$-functions]{Finite parts of dynamical $\zeta$-functions for shifts of finite type}

\author{The Omega Project}
\address{The Omega Institute for Mathematical Research}

\date{}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
For a mixing subshift of finite type with primitive adjacency matrix
\(A\) and Perron root \(\lambda\), we study the finite part of the
dynamical \(\zeta\)-function \(\zeta_A(z)=\det(I-zA)^{-1}\) at the
Perron pole \(z=\lambda^{-1}\). We prove a closed primitive-orbit
formula for the finite-part constant \(\FP(A)\) and show that the
cyclic-lift constants \(C(A^{\langle q\rangle})\) reconstruct the
non-Perron spectrum with multiplicity; a single root-of-unity layer of
order \(q\ge d\) already suffices. For finite-group extensions we
derive an Adams--M\"obius primitive inversion expressing each
irreducible Peter--Weyl channel in terms of twisted determinants.
Under a twisted spectral gap this yields explicit class Mertens
constants and a non-abelian Fourier reconstruction at the Perron
point. Quotient functoriality identifies cyclic data as the exact
abelian shadow of the full primitive class profile; the orthogonal
complement is a genuine non-abelian defect. An \(S_3\)-example
exhibits non-vanishing primitive bias despite identically vanishing
periodic traces.
\end{abstract}
