\begin{abstract}
For a mixing subshift of finite type with primitive adjacency matrix
\(A\) and Perron root \(\lambda\), we study finite-group extensions
through the twisted transition matrices \(A_\varrho\) and the associated
primitive orbit series. We prove that the primitive Peter--Weyl series
are recovered from the logarithmic periodic data by M\"obius inversion
twisted by the Adams operations \(g\mapsto g^m\). This gives determinant
formulas for the primitive irreducible channels. Under the spectral-gap
hypothesis
\(\eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf{1}}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda\), we deduce
classwise Mertens asymptotics and a non-abelian Fourier inversion
formula for the corresponding Mertens constants at the Perron pole. An
exact rigidity theorem characterises the borderline case
\(\rho(A_\varrho)=\lambda\) by the existence of a cocycle-equivariant
line field in the \(\varrho\)-channel; in particular, the strict
inequality \(\eta<\lambda\) is automatic when the closed-path labels
generate a perfect subgroup. We also give a reduced-determinant
expression for the finite part of a
primitive matrix at its Perron pole and show that the residue constants
of cyclic lifts determine the reduced spectral polynomial. An explicit
\(S_3\)-example shows that vanishing twisted periodic traces in a
non-trivial channel does not force vanishing primitive bias.
\end{abstract}
