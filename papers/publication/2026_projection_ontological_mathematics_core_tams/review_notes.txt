Referee simulation for the pre-revision draft

Recommendation on the original draft: reject in current form.

Major weaknesses identified

1. The theorem chain was materially weaker than the available source material. The introduction advertised only a small fraction of the strongest results.
2. The information section stopped at the basic ledger identity and omitted the operational side-information theorem and the rigidity characterization of the uniform lift.
3. The rewriting section proved only confluence of a small fragment and did not exploit the fiber statistics already present upstream, especially the root-of-unity filter and exponential modular uniformization.
4. The moment section lacked the strongest recoverability statements. In particular, the capacity/Stieltjes inversion and finite-Hankel recovery theorems were missing.
5. The arithmetic section overclaimed. In the earlier draft, full symmetric Galois groups were asserted on cases where the displayed certificates did not by themselves justify that conclusion.
6. The paper did not read like a Transactions of the AMS submission: the front matter was light, the main results were not stated sharply enough in the introduction, and several sections were written as compressed summaries rather than as complete arguments.

Fixes applied in the revision

1. Rebuilt the introduction around four precise main theorems in formal journal style.
2. Added KL-isometry/uniqueness of the uniform lift and the prefix side-information lower bound with proofs.
3. Added the root-of-unity filter and exponential modular uniformization theorem, together with the parity split corollary.
4. Added the capacity curve theorem, determinant/Stieltjes rigidity, and finite-Hankel spectral-gap recovery theorem.
5. Reworked the arithmetic section so that irreducibility is proved on the full resonance window, while full symmetric Galois groups are claimed only on the audited set {9,10,11,13,16,17}. The unsupported claim for q=12,14,15 was removed and explicitly flagged as unresolved.
6. Rewrote the abstract, front matter, and conclusion to match a research-paper register rather than a project summary.

Post-revision assessment

The revised manuscript is substantially stronger and much closer to journal quality. The main remaining mathematical gap is the absence of a decisive additional cycle certificate for q=12,14,15; the current version handles this correctly by not overclaiming. Subject to clean compilation and bibliography checks, the revised paper is plausible as a serious submission.
