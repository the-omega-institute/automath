\begin{abstract}
We prove that the fiber multiplicities of finite-window Zeckendorf
stabilization are Fibonacci-lag discrete derivatives of the classical
Fibonacci partition function.  The collision moments
$S_q(m)=\sum_{x}d_m(x)^q$ are thereby squeezed between adjacent
Fibonacci windows of Sanna's partition power sums, yielding
$S_q(m)\asymp_q\lambda_q^m$ for every $q\ge 1$.  A finite-state
kernel realization identifies each $\lambda_q$ ($q\ge 2$) as the
Perron root of a nonnegative integer matrix of polynomial size and
hence proves that $\lambda_q$ is an algebraic integer.  The pressure
sequence $p_q=\log\lambda_q$ (with $p_0=\log\varphi$) is convex, and
its discrete slopes $\Delta_q=p_q-p_{q-1}$ select the canonical
thickness bands of the model: the $q$-canonical law is exponentially
concentrated on $[\Delta_q,\Delta_{q+1}]$, with two-sided exponential
estimates for band cardinality and visible mass.  Diverging tilts
select the zero-temperature thickness $\frac12\log\varphi$, so the
largest fiber size $D_m$ satisfies $D_m^{1/m}\to\sqrt{\varphi}$ and
the diagonal high-moment asymptotic follows.  We also record an
audited finite arithmetic window $q=9,\dots,17$: the recurrence
polynomials $\Pi_q$ extracted from the exact tables in
Appendix~B are computationally certified to be irreducible with full
symmetric Galois groups throughout that window, and for
$q\in\{12,13,14,15\}$ the same audit indicates linear disjointness and
the product Chebotarev density $1/20449$ for simultaneous
irreducibility.
\end{abstract}
