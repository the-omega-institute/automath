\section{Reproducible experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

The experiments are not used as proofs of the main theorems.  They serve as audit interfaces for the finite objects constructed above.  Each experiment reports a fixed input protocol, a deterministic output object, and a comparison statistic.

\subsection{Folded histogram audit}
The first experiment samples or enumerates words \(\omega\in\Om_m\), applies the folding map, and records
\[
H_m(x)=|\{\omega\in\Om_m:\Fold_m(\omega)=x\}|,
\qquad x\in X_m.
\]
For exhaustive enumeration, the empirical folded distribution is
\[
\widehat\nu_m(x)=\frac{H_m(x)}{2^m}.
\]
One may compare \(\widehat\nu_m\) with a specified baseline \(\nu_m\), such as the uniform distribution on \(X_m\) or the Parry/Perron--Frobenius baseline of a graph presentation, using
\[
\TV(\widehat\nu_m,\nu_m).
\]

\subsection{Cross-resolution residual audit}
The second experiment compares folded representatives across adjacent resolutions.  For the fold-aware version, define
\[
\Delta_m(\omega)=
d\!\left(
r_{m+1,m}\Fold_{m+1}(\omega),
\Fold_m\rho^{\Aware}_{m+1,m}(\omega)
\right).
\]
The certificate target is
\[
\Delta_m(\omega)=0
\]
for all typed inputs.  The naive truncation residual is
\[
\Delta_m^{\rm naive}(\omega)=
d\!\left(
\Trunc_{m+1,m}\Fold_{m+1}(\omega),
\Fold_m\Trunc_{m+1,m}(\omega)
\right),
\]
and is reported as a finite-resolution normalization defect rather than as a failure of the fold-aware construction.

\subsection{Clarity profile audit}
The third experiment fixes an event \(P\in\mathcal F_\infty\) and computes
\[
\varepsilon_m(P;\mu)=
\sum_{C\in\mathcal C_m}
\min\{\mu(P\cap C),\mu(C\setminus P)\}.
\]
The output is the curve
\[
m\longmapsto \varepsilon_m(P;\mu),
\]
together with the finite-depth slope
\[
-\frac1m\log \varepsilon_m(P;\mu)
\]
when the error is nonzero.  The audit compares this decay with boundary-cylinder counts \(N_m(\partial P)\).

\subsection{Reproducibility package}
The publication package should include
\[
(\text{code},\text{data},\text{configuration},\text{random seed},\text{environment},\text{output hashes}).
\]
Statistical claims should record \(m\), \(N\), \(N_{\mathrm{eff}}\), the distance used, the uncertainty radius, the model tolerance, and the decision rule.  A figure without the data and script needed to reproduce it is not an audit certificate.
