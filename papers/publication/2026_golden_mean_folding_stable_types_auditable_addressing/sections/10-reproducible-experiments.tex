\section{Reproducible experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

The experiments are not used as proofs of the main theorems.  They serve as audit interfaces for the finite objects constructed above.  Each experiment reports a fixed input protocol, a deterministic output object, and a comparison statistic.

\subsection{Folded histogram audit}
For a binary input stream \(x=(x_t)\), the fold-histogram experiment forms
\[
Z_t^{(m)}=\Fold_m(x_tx_{t+1}\cdots x_{t+m-1})
\]
and records the empirical law \(\widehat\pi_{m,N}\) on \(X_m\).  Appendix~\ref{app:statistical-audit} gives the full statistical protocol.  The empirical law is compared with two exact baselines: the IID-uniform fiber law
\[
p_m^{\mathrm{fib}}(z)=\frac{|\Fold_m^{-1}(z)|}{2^m}
\]
and the golden-mean Parry cylinder law \(q_m\) from \Cref{app:golden-parry-baseline}.  The Parry law is a maximum-entropy reference, not an asserted limit law for arbitrary sources.  Reported values of
\[
\TV(\widehat\pi_{m,N},q_m)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\KL(\widehat\pi_{m,N}\mid q_m)
\]
are interpreted using the sampling/model decomposition of \Cref{thm:statistical-audit-decomposition}.

\subsection{Cross-resolution residual audit}
The second experiment compares adjacent empirical folded laws under stable prefix restriction.  It reports
\[
E_{m,N}:=
\TV\!\left(
\widehat\pi_{m,N},
(\pi_{m+1\to m})_\#\widehat\pi_{m+1,N}
\right).
\]
If TV confidence radii \(\tau_m\) and \(\tau_{m+1}\) are declared, then \(E_{m,N}>\tau_m+\tau_{m+1}\) gives a finite-sample certificate of population-level cross-resolution inconsistency for the selected restriction protocol.

\subsection{Clarity profile audit}
The third experiment fixes an event \(P\in\mathcal F_\infty\) and computes
\[
\varepsilon_m(P;\mu)=
\sum_{C\in\mathcal C_m}
\min\{\mu(P\cap C),\mu(C\setminus P)\}.
\]
The output is the curve
\[
m\longmapsto \varepsilon_m(P;\mu),
\]
together with the finite-depth slope
\[
-\frac1m\log \varepsilon_m(P;\mu)
\]
when the error is nonzero.  The audit compares this decay with boundary-cylinder counts \(N_m(\partial P)\).

\subsection{Exact deterministic tables}
Every fold-histogram run separates deterministic tables from random empirical histograms.  The deterministic tables include
\[
\Fold_m,\qquad
d_m(z)=|\Fold_m^{-1}(z)|,\qquad
p_m^{\mathrm{fib}},\qquad
q_m.
\]
Appendix~\ref{app:fiber-ledger} defines the deterministic fiber ledger, including \(D_m\), \(\kappa_m\), collision moments, and the exact IID-uniform fiber law.  Hashes of these exact tables are reported with the empirical output.  This prevents changes in the fold table or baseline construction from being mistaken for statistical variation.

\subsection{Reproducibility package}
The publication package specified in Appendix~\ref{app:reproducibility} should include
\[
(\text{code},\text{data},\text{configuration},\text{random seed},\text{environment},\text{output hashes}).
\]
Its deterministic core consists of \texttt{fold\_table.json}, \texttt{fiber\_ledger.csv}, \texttt{parry\_baseline.csv}, \texttt{moment\_spectrum.csv}, \texttt{cross\_resolution\_fib.csv}, and canonical SHA256 hashes.  Its empirical layer stores sampled windows, folded histograms, TV/KL comparisons, and cross-resolution residuals.  Statistical claims should record \(m\), \(N\), \(N_{\mathrm{eff}}\), the distance used, the uncertainty radius, the model tolerance, and the decision rule.  A figure without the saved data and script needed to reproduce it is not an audit certificate.
