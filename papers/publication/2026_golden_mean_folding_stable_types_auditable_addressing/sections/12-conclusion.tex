\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper constructs a golden-mean folding mechanism for binary scan--projection systems.  Given a singly generated action, a binary window readout, and a finite microstate space
\[
\Om_m=\{0,1\}^m,
\]
the folding map
\[
\Fold_m:\Om_m\to X_m
\]
produces unique Zeckendorf-legal stable normal forms.

We prove that the folding system is terminating, confluent, idempotent, and order independent.  We also prove that stable types admit cross-resolution comparison through fold-aware restriction, while naive truncation generally fails to commute with folding.  Recursive addressing is shown to reorganize previous visible layers without enlarging the visible sigma-algebra.

The scan error profile
\[
\varepsilon_m(P;\mu)
\]
turns finite-resolution clarity into an auditable quantity.  Its cylinder decomposition, Bayes-optimal prefix classifier, and boundary-dimension bound provide a direct quantitative chain from finite readouts to statistical stability.

The conclusions are restricted to stable type generation, addressing, and clarity certification.  Arithmetic, spectral, geometric, Hilbert-space, quantum, and spacetime bridges are possible downstream extensions, but they require additional conditions and are not conclusions of this paper.
