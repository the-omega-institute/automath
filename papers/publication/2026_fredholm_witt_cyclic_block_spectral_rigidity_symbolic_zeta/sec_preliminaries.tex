\section{Finite-State \texorpdfstring{$\zeta$}{zeta}-Functions in One Proposition}
\label{sec:preliminaries}

This section only records the standard finite-state motivation for the
operator-theoretic discussion in \Cref{sec:fredholm}. Let \(A\) be a
complex \(d\times d\) matrix and set \(a_n(A):=\Tr(A^n)\). Define the
M\"obius-inverted sequence
\[
  p_n(A):=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{d\mid n}\mu(d)\,a_{n/d}(A)
  \qquad (n\ge 1).
\]

\begin{proposition}[Finite matrices, Fredholm zeta, and Euler products]
  \label{prop:prelim-trace-primitive}
  For every \(d\times d\) complex matrix \(A\),
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:zeta-finite-matrix}
    \det(I-zA)^{-1}
    =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
    =\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-p_n(A)}
  \end{equation}
  as an identity of holomorphic germs at \(z=0\) (or of formal series).
  When \(A\) is a primitive \(\{0,1\}\)-matrix, the coefficients
  \(p_n(A)\) are the non-negative counts of primitive orbits of length
  \(n\) for the corresponding subshift \cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
  The determinant identity is classical and follows from the Newton
  identities for finite matrices. The relation
  \(
    a_n(A)=\sum_{d\mid n}d\,p_d(A)
  \)
  is the inverse M\"obius transform of the definition of \(p_n(A)\),
  so
  \[
    \sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{a_n(A)}{n}z^n
    =-\sum_{m\ge 1}p_m(A)\log(1-z^m).
  \]
  Exponentiating yields the Euler product and is justified for formal
  power series or for holomorphic germs with \(\abs{z}<\min(1,\norm{A}^{-1})\).
\end{proof}
