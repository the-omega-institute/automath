\section{Further remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The three operator-theoretic statements proved here have different
logical status. The Fredholm--Witt bridge is formal once trace-class
determinants are available. The cyclic-block theorem is an existence
result showing that Euler products admit far more realisations than the
finite-state category would suggest. Spectral rigidity then shows that this
additional freedom is geometric rather than spectral: once the determinant
is fixed, the non-zero spectrum is fixed as well.

For symbolic dynamics, the practical consequence is that several
standard perturbative arguments may be organised entirely at the level of
reduced determinants and resolvents. This is the reason that the
finite-part variation formula and the spectral-gap central limit theorem
fit naturally into the same short note.

Several natural extensions merit further investigation. First, the cyclic-block
construction is expected to adapt to Banach-space nuclear settings, but
such extensions would require a concrete operator class and a
non-hilbertian trace-class framework; these assertions are conjectural
in this level of generality. Second, one expects analogous statements for
Axiom~A flows via symbolic suspension models, but a full treatment of the
flow setting (especially in mixed regularity and trace-class realisability)
remains open and is not part of this paper.
Third, quantitative refinements of the CLT---such as Berry--Esseen
rates or moderate-deviation principles---could be obtained by combining
the spectral-gap mechanism of
\Cref{thm:clt-spectral} with higher-order perturbation expansions of
the dominant eigenvalue.
