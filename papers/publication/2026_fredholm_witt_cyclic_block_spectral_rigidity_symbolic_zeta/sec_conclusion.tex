\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The note isolates an operator-theoretic realisation problem for Euler
products rather than a new symbolic-dynamical theorem. The main result,
\Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation}, shows that the summability
condition
\[
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\]
is exactly the criterion for the existence of a normal trace-class
re\-alisation of the prescribed Fredholm zeta function, and that every
such realisation is, up to a zero summand and unitary equivalence, the
corresponding cyclic-block model. The determinant-intrinsic counting
invariant is \(N(T)\), the total algebraic multiplicity of the non-zero
eigenvalues; for compact normal operators this equals
\(\operatorname{rank}\mathbf 1_{(0,\infty)}(|T|)\)
and \(\dim\overline{\mathrm{ran}(T)}\).

Once one normal model is available,
\Cref{prop:fredholm-factorisation-rigidity,thm:general-support-optimality}
propagate the same non-zero eigenvalue multiset and Witt data to every
trace-class realisation. The new trace-norm minimality principle in
\Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality} shows that the cyclic-block model
already minimises the trace norm among all such realisations, so normal
form and determinant rigidity line up exactly. Through
\Cref{cor:symbolic-trace-norm} and the finite-state bridge in
\Cref{prop:prelim-trace-primitive}, this minimality propagates to the
dynamical \(\zeta\)-functions of primitive subshifts.

The manuscript is therefore presented as a streamlined note centred on
Section~\ref{sec:fredholm}; classical symbolic-dynamical motivation has
been reduced to the single proposition in
\Cref{sec:preliminaries}, and no appendices remain. Questions about
non-normal Jordan geometry, weaker hypotheses outside the normal
category, or extensions to regularised determinants lie beyond the
scope of the present note.
