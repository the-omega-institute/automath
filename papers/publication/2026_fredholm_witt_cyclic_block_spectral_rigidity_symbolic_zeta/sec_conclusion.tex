\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The paper treats a determinant-constrained realization problem for Euler
products inside the Hilbert trace-class setting. The main result,
\Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation}, shows that the summability
condition
\[
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\]
is exactly the criterion for the existence of a normal trace-class
realization of the prescribed Fredholm zeta function, and that every
such realization is, up to a zero summand and unitary equivalence, the
corresponding cyclic-block model. The determinant-intrinsic counting
invariant is \(N(T)\), the total algebraic multiplicity of the non-zero
eigenvalues; for compact normal operators this equals
\(\operatorname{rank}\mathbf 1_{(0,\infty)}(|T|)\)
and \(\dim\overline{\mathrm{ran}(T)}\).

Once one normal model is available,
\Cref{prop:fredholm-factorisation-rigidity,thm:general-support-optimality}
propagate the same non-zero eigenvalue multiset and Witt data to every
trace-class realization. The trace-norm minimality principle in
\Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality} shows that the cyclic-block model
already attains the minimal trace norm among all such realizations, and
that every minimizer is block-cyclic up to unitary equivalence and a
zero summand. Moreover,
\Cref{thm:nonnormal-jordan-classification} shows that the same determinant data
also admit a complete non-zero Jordan classification in the full
trace-class category: every partition of each determinant-forced
non-zero algebraic multiplicity is realized by some trace-class
operator, and the trace-norm infimum within a fixed Jordan profile is
still the normal value, attained only in the semisimple profile. In
contrast, \Cref{rem:zero-spectrum-freedom} shows that the quasinilpotent
sector at the spectral point \(0\) remains unrestricted.

The symbolic-dynamical input is intentionally auxiliary and enters only
through \Cref{prop:prelim-trace-primitive}. The paper does not attempt
extensions to Banach-space dynamical determinants, regularized
determinants outside the trace class, or inverse spectral problems
beyond the determinant-constrained questions settled here.
