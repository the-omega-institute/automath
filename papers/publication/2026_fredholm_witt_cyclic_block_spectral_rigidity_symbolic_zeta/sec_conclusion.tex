\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The classical input from Fredholm and regularized determinant theory is
the recovery of the non-zero spectral divisor. The additional structure
isolated here is more rigid. First, the admissible Euler product has a
canonical normal realization by cyclic blocks. Second,
\Cref{thm:kyfan-dominance} shows that this normal model is the minimal
point of the full singular-value order: every determinant-constrained
realization dominates it in all Ky Fan norms and therefore in every
unitarily invariant norm. Third,
\Cref{thm:line-rigidity-trace,thm:line-rigidity-schatten} identify a
natural class in which the determinant becomes complete: once the
spectrum is constrained to a line \(e^{i\vartheta}\RR\), no
quasinilpotent freedom survives and the determinant determines the
operator up to a zero summand.

Outside the line-spectrum subclasses, the inverse problem remains
sharply delimited by \Cref{rem:zero-spectrum-freedom}: the determinant
controls the entire non-zero Jordan geometry but leaves the
quasinilpotent sector free. The Schatten theory supplements this with
the coercive estimate of \Cref{thm:schatten-quantitative-stability},
which promotes the norm inequality to quantitative proximity to the
canonical normal orbit.

This scope remains narrower than the inverse spectral theories surveyed
in \cite{PoschelTrubowitz1987,Marchenko2011} and narrower than the
Banach-space dynamical determinant framework of
\cite{Ruelle1990Extension,Pollicott1986Meromorphic,Baladi2018DynZeta}.
Within the present Hilbert-space trace-ideal setting, however, the
determinant recovers all non-zero spectral data, the full associated
extremal geometry, and, on every fixed spectral line, the operator
itself up to the unavoidable zero summand.
