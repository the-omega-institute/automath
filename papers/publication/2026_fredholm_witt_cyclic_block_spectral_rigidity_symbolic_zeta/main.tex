\begin{abstract}
	We characterize those Euler products
	\[
	  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
	\]
	that occur as Fredholm zeta functions
	\(
	  Z_T(z)=\det(I-zT)^{-1}
	\)
	of normal trace-class operators on a Hilbert space. The criterion is
	exactly
	\(
	  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
	\).
	When this condition holds, the corresponding realization is, up to a
	zero summand and unitary equivalence, the direct sum of cyclic blocks
	\(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\) with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\).
	We further show that this cyclic-block model minimizes the trace norm
	among all trace-class realizations with the same Fredholm zeta
	function, with equality precisely in the normal case. Finally, away
	from the spectral point \(0\), the Fredholm determinant imposes no
	additional restriction on Jordan structure beyond the forced algebraic
	multiplicities.
\end{abstract}
