\begin{abstract}
	We study determinant-constrained realizations of countable Euler
	products in the Hilbert trace and Schatten classes. If
	\[
	  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty,
	\]
	then
	\[
	  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
	\]
	is the Fredholm zeta germ of an explicit block-cyclic normal operator
	\(
	  \bigoplus_j (C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j})
	\)
	with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\), and every normal realization is
	unitarily equivalent to this model up to a zero summand. The same
	determinant data force the non-zero eigenvalue multiset of every
	trace-class realization, yield the sharp trace-norm lower bound
	\[
	  \|T\|_1\ge \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
	\]
	and allow arbitrary Jordan profiles away from \(0\) subject only to
	the forced algebraic multiplicities; within each compatible Jordan
	class the infimum is the normal value and is attained exactly in the
	semisimple case.

	For each integer \(p\ge 2\), the same cyclic-block model realizes the
	corresponding regularized Euler product in \(\mathcal S_p\). In that
	setting the regularized determinant again fixes the non-zero spectrum,
	yields the sharp Schatten \(p\)-norm bound, allows the same
	Jordan-profile flexibility, and determines the spectral \(p\)-mass by
	a Jensen--Mellin formula.
\end{abstract}
