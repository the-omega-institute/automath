\begin{abstract}
	We consider countable Euler products
	\[
	  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
	\]
	and ask when they arise from Fredholm or regularized determinants of
	compact operators on a Hilbert space. In trace class, the summability
	condition
	\[
	  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
	\]
	is exactly the criterion for realization by an explicit block-cyclic
	normal operator
	\(
	  \bigoplus_j (C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j})
	\)
	with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\), and every normal realization is
	unitarily equivalent to this model up to a zero summand. The Fredholm
	determinant then fixes the non-zero spectrum of every trace-class
	realization, yields the sharp lower bound
	\[
	  \|T\|_1\ge \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
	\]
	and allows arbitrary Jordan structure away from \(0\) subject only to
	the forced algebraic multiplicities; within each compatible Jordan
	class the infimum is the normal value and is attained precisely in the
	semisimple case. For each integer \(p\ge 2\), the same picture holds
	for the regularized determinant on \(\mathcal S_p\): the block-cyclic
	model gives the normal realization, the regularized determinant yields
	the sharp Schatten \(p\)-norm bound, and the spectral \(p\)-mass is
	recovered by a Jensen--Mellin formula. The same determinant data also
	make the minimization problem coercive in Schatten geometry: the
	\(p\)-norm defect above the block-cyclic minimum controls the
	\(\mathcal S_p\)-distance to the normal block-cyclic orbit, and for
	\(p=2\) this defect is exactly the squared Hilbert--Schmidt distance.
\end{abstract}
