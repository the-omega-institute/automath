\begin{abstract}
We study an operator-theoretic framework for symbolic dynamical
\(\zeta\)-functions. For a trace-class operator \(T\), the trace
sequence, the Witt coordinates, and the Fredholm determinant are shown
to fit into a single Euler-product identity. Conversely, every Euler
product of the form
\(
  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\)
under a natural trace-class summability condition admits an explicit
realisation by a direct sum of cyclic blocks. We then prove a spectral
rigidity theorem: equality of Fredholm determinants forces equality of
the non-zero spectra with algebraic multiplicity.

As applications, we recover two perturbative statements for mixing
subshifts of finite type. First, the finite-part constants at the
Perron pole vary holomorphically in analytic matrix families, with an
explicit group-inverse gradient formula. Second, locally constant
observables satisfy a spectral-gap central limit theorem together with
uniform exponential decay of characteristic functions away from the
origin.
\end{abstract}
