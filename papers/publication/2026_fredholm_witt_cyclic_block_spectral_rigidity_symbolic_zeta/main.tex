\begin{abstract}
  Countable Euler products
  \[
    \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
  \]
  are studied as Fredholm determinant data for compact operators on a
  Hilbert space.
  In trace class, the summability condition
  \[
    \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
  \]
  is equivalent to the existence of a normal realization, and every such
  realization is unitarily equivalent, up to a zero summand, to an
  explicit direct sum of cyclic blocks
  \(
    C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}
  \)
  with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\). For arbitrary trace-class
  realizations with the same determinant germ, the non-zero spectrum is
  forced, the block-cyclic model simultaneously minimizes every Ky Fan
  norm and every unitarily invariant norm, and in particular the trace
  norm satisfies the sharp lower bound
  \[
    \|T\|_1\ge \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
  \]
  while the Jordan structure away from \(0\) is constrained only by the
  corresponding algebraic multiplicities. The unrestricted
  quasinilpotent sector disappears on every rotated-selfadjoint spectral
  line \(e^{i\vartheta}\RR\): in that class the determinant gives a
  complete unitary classification up to a zero summand. For each
  integer \(p\ge 2\), the analogous realization and line-spectrum
  rigidity statements hold for the regularized determinant on
  \(\mathcal S_p\); moreover the spectral \(p\)-mass is recovered by a
  Jensen--Mellin formula, and the \(p\)-norm defect controls the
  distance to the normal block-cyclic orbit. For \(p=2\), this defect
  is exactly the squared Hilbert--Schmidt distance.
\end{abstract}
