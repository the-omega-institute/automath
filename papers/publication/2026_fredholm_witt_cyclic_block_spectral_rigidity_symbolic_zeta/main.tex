\begin{abstract}
  We study countable Euler products
  \[
    \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
  \]
  as Fredholm determinant data for compact operators on a Hilbert space.
  In trace class, the summability condition
  \[
    \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
  \]
  is equivalent to the existence of a normal realization, and every such
  realization is unitarily equivalent, up to a zero summand, to an
  explicit direct sum of cyclic blocks
  \(
    C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}
  \)
  with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\). For arbitrary trace-class
  realizations with the same determinant germ, the non-zero spectrum is
  forced, the trace norm satisfies the sharp lower bound
  \[
    \|T\|_1\ge \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
  \]
  and the Jordan structure away from \(0\) is constrained only by the
  corresponding algebraic multiplicities. For each integer \(p\ge 2\),
  we prove the analogous realization and minimality statements for the
  regularized determinant on \(\mathcal S_p\), recover the spectral
  \(p\)-mass by a Jensen--Mellin formula, and show that the
  \(p\)-norm defect controls the distance to the normal block-cyclic
  orbit; for \(p=2\) this defect is exactly the squared
  Hilbert--Schmidt distance.
\end{abstract}
