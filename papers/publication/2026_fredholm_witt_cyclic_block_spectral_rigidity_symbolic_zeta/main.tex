\title[Normal Trace-Class Realisations of Symbolic Euler Products]{Normal trace-class realisations of Euler products\\
arising from symbolic \(\zeta\)-functions}

\author{Haobo Ma}
\address{CHRONOAI}

\date{}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
The main result of this note is a cyclic-block characterization theorem
for normal trace-class realisations of Euler products arising from
symbolic \(\zeta\)-functions. Specifically, an Euler product
\[
  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\]
with
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\)
admits a normal trace-class realisation if and only if, up to an
arbitrary zero summand and unitary equivalence, its non-zero part is a
direct sum of cyclic blocks \(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\) with
\(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\). In particular, the total non-zero block
size is forced and each factor requires exactly \(m_jn_j\) non-zero
dimensions; the summability condition is both necessary and sufficient
within the normal class. The paper does not address non-normal
trace-class realisability. The Fredholm--Witt bridge and spectral
rigidity are recorded as classical connective background. An
applications section illustrates how the determinant viewpoint organizes
standard perturbative and spectral-gap consequences---finite-part
constants, twisted-determinant moment recovery, and limit-law
data---within one framework; these applications are not claimed as new
results.
\end{abstract}
