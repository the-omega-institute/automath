\begin{abstract}
This note concerns normal trace-class realisations of Fredholm zeta
functions \(Z_T(z):=\det(I-zT)^{-1}\) attached to Euler products
\[
  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\]
under the summability condition
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\).
For a trace-class operator \(T\), let \(N(T)\) denote the total
algebraic multiplicity of its non-zero eigenvalues; for compact normal
operators,
\(
  N(L)=\operatorname{rank}\mathbf 1_{(0,\infty)}(|L|)
  =\dim\overline{\mathrm{ran}(L)}
\).
We show that the Euler product above is the Fredholm zeta function of a
normal trace-class operator if and only if, up to an arbitrary zero
summand and unitary equivalence, the operator is a direct sum of cyclic
blocks \(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\) with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\).
Consequently every normal realisation satisfies
\[
  N(L)=\sum_j m_jn_j,
  \qquad
  \|L\|_1=\sum_j m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
\]
and these quantities depend only on the Fredholm zeta germ, not on a
chosen Euler factorization. A strengthened rigidity theorem further
shows that among all trace-class realisations with the same Fredholm
zeta function, the cyclic-block normal model attains the minimal trace
norm, and every minimiser is unitarily equivalent to that model up to
an arbitrary zero summand. Symbolic dynamics now enters only as
motivation through one finite-state proposition.
\end{abstract}
