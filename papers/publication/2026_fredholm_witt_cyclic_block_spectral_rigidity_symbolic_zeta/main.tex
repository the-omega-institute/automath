\begin{abstract}
	We consider countable Euler products
	\[
	  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
	\]
	as determinant data for compact operators on a Hilbert space. In trace
	class, the summability
	condition
	\[
	  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
	\]
	is exactly the criterion for the existence of a normal realization by
	an explicit block-cyclic operator
	\(
	  \bigoplus_j (C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j})
	\)
	with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\); every normal realization is
	unitarily equivalent to this model up to a zero summand. Once this
	normal model exists, the Fredholm determinant fixes the non-zero
	spectrum of every trace-class realization with the same germ and
	yields the sharp lower bound
	\[
	  \|T\|_1\ge \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
	\]
	and allows arbitrary Jordan structure away from \(0\) subject only to
	the forced algebraic multiplicities; within each compatible Jordan
	class the infimum is the normal value and is attained precisely in the
	semisimple case. For each integer \(p\ge 2\), the regularized
	determinant on \(\mathcal S_p\) admits the analogous normal
	realization criterion and Schatten \(p\)-norm lower bound, and the
	spectral \(p\)-mass is recovered by a Jensen--Mellin formula. The new
	quantitative point in Schatten geometry is that the same determinant
	data make the minimization problem coercive: the
	\(p\)-norm defect above the block-cyclic minimum controls the
	\(\mathcal S_p\)-distance to the normal block-cyclic orbit, and for
	\(p=2\) this defect is exactly the squared Hilbert--Schmidt distance.
\end{abstract}
