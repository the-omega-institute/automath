\begin{abstract}
	For countable Euler data
	\(
	  \{(n_j,\beta_j,m_j)\}_{j\in J}
	\)
	satisfying
	\(
	  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
	\),
	the corresponding Euler product
	\(
	  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
	\)
	is realized by a normal trace-class operator on a Hilbert space.
	Every normal realization is, up to a zero summand and unitary
	equivalence, the cyclic-block direct sum
	\(
	  \bigoplus_j (C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j})
	\),
	with \(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\). This model minimizes the trace norm
	among all trace-class realizations with the same Fredholm zeta germ,
	with equality precisely in the normal class. Away from the spectral
	point \(0\), the determinant imposes no additional restriction on
	Jordan structure beyond the forced algebraic multiplicities.

	For every integer \(p\ge 2\), the same framework admits a regularized
	determinant extension in the Schatten class \(\mathcal S_p\). If
	\(
	  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{p/n_j}<\infty
	\),
	then the same cyclic-block model yields the unique normal
	\(\mathcal S_p\)-realization of the prescribed regularized Euler
	product, gives the sharp lower bound for the Schatten \(p\)-norm among
	all \(\mathcal S_p\)-realizations, and realizes every compatible
	non-zero Jordan profile with the same optimal infimum. The
	regularized determinant also determines the spectral \(p\)-mass by a
	Jensen--Mellin formula.
\end{abstract}
