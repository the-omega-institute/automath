\begin{abstract}
In the Hilbert trace-class setting we consider Euler products
\[
  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\]
and ask when they arise as Fredholm zeta functions
\(
  Z_T(z)=\det(I-zT)^{-1}
\)
of normal trace-class operators. We prove that this happens precisely
under the summability condition
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\).
In that case there is a canonical model, namely a direct sum of cyclic
blocks \(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\) with
\(\alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j\), and every normal realization is unitarily
equivalent to this model up to an arbitrary zero summand. It follows
that the total multiplicity of the non-zero spectrum and the trace norm
are determined by the Fredholm zeta germ within the normal class. We
also show that the cyclic-block model minimizes the trace norm among
all trace-class realizations with the same Fredholm zeta function, and
that equality characterizes the normal model. Beyond the normal class,
the Fredholm zeta germ imposes no further obstruction on the non-zero
Jordan geometry: every family of Jordan partitions compatible with the
determinant-forced non-zero multiplicities is realized by a trace-class
operator, and the corresponding trace-norm infimum remains the normal
value, attained only in the semisimple case. A short finite-dimensional
proposition is included only as motivation from symbolic dynamics.
\end{abstract}
