\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

For a primitive finite matrix \(A\), the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function
\[
  \zeta_A(z)=\det(I-zA)^{-1}
\]
packages periodic-orbit data, transfer-operator traces, and non-Perron
spectral data into one holomorphic object. In symbolic dynamics this
identity is classical and can be viewed as the finite-state shadow of a
broader Fredholm determinant identity valid for general trace-class
operators.

This paper separates three layers that are often used together. The
first layer is connective and classical: the Fredholm determinant
recovers both the trace sequence and the associated Witt coordinates.
The second layer is the new structural contribution of the paper: we
identify exactly when an abstract Euler product admits a trace-class
normal realisation by cyclic blocks, and we show that in the normal
class this cyclic-block geometry is forced and support-minimal. The
third layer returns to symbolic dynamics: once the determinant is taken
as the primary object, finite-part constants, Abel renormalisations,
orbit-weight moments, and limit-law data can all be handled within one
operator-theoretic package.

The novelty is concentrated in the strengthened cyclic-block theorem.
The Fredholm--Witt bridge and the spectral rigidity statement are
included because they are the two classical links that make this
characterisation useful in the symbolic applications: the former turns
periodic data into determinants, and the latter turns determinant
identities back into spectral information.

\subsection{Main results}

We begin by recording the classical bridge in the form used throughout
the paper.
\begin{proposition}[Connecting Fredholm--Witt identity]
  \label{prop:intro-fredholm-witt}
  Let \(T\in \sone(H)\) be a trace-class operator on a complex Hilbert
  space. Define
  \[
    a_n(T):=\Tr(T^n),
    \qquad
    p_n(T):=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{d\mid n}\mu(d)\,a_{n/d}(T)
    \qquad (n\ge 1).
  \]
  Then for every \(\abs{z}<\norm{T}^{-1}\),
  \[
    \det(I-zT)^{-1}
    =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{a_n(T)}{n}z^n\right)
    =\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-p_n(T)}.
  \]
\end{proposition}

The main new theorem strengthens the converse realization statement by
adding necessity and optimality in the normal trace-class category.
\begin{theorem}[Cyclic-block characterization and optimality]
  \label{thm:intro-cyclic-characterization}
  Let \(\{(n_j,\beta_j,m_j)\}_{j\in J}\) be a countable family with
  \(n_j,m_j\in\NN\), \(\beta_j\in\CC\setminus\{0\}\), and
  \[
    \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty.
  \]
  Then the Euler product
  \[
    \prod_{j\in J}(1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
  \]
  is the Fredholm determinant of a trace-class normal operator if and
  only if, after adjoining an arbitrary zero summand, that operator is
  unitarily equivalent to
  \[
    \bigoplus_{j\in J}\bigl(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\bigr),
    \qquad \alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j.
  \]
  Consequently every trace-class normal realization has non-zero
  spectral multiplicity
  \(
    \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j
  \)
  and trace norm
  \(
    \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}
  \).
  In particular, each factor \((1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}\) requires
  exactly \(m_j n_j\) non-zero dimensions.
\end{theorem}

Once the determinant is fixed, the non-zero spectrum is fixed as well.
The next statement is classical but important because it is the
mechanism that turns determinant identities into rigidity.
\begin{proposition}[Spectral rigidity]
  \label{prop:intro-spectral-rigidity}
  Let \(T_1,T_2\in \sone\). If
  \[
    \det(I-zT_1)=\det(I-zT_2)
  \]
  in a neighbourhood of \(z=0\), then \(T_1\) and \(T_2\) have the same
  non-zero eigenvalues with algebraic multiplicity.
\end{proposition}

Section~4 applies this perspective in two symbolic-dynamical
directions. The first concerns finite-part constants and their Abel
renormalisations. The second concerns twisted determinants attached to
locally constant observables, which simultaneously encode
periodic-orbit moments and limit-law data.
\begin{theorem}\label{thm:intro-perturbative-applications}
Let \(\theta\mapsto A_\theta\) be a holomorphic family of primitive
matrices, and let \(\lambda(\theta)=\rho(A_\theta)\) be the simple
Perron root. Then \(\log C(A_\theta)\) and \(\log\MM(A_\theta)\) vary
holomorphically, admit explicit group-inverse/Fredholm derivative
formulas, and can be computed from finitely many secondary Fredholm
factors with exponentially small error. If \(f\) is a locally constant
observable on a mixing subshift of finite type, then the corresponding
twisted Fredholm determinant determines every periodic-orbit moment sum
and all asymptotic cumulants of the centered Birkhoff sums. In
particular one recovers the usual spectral-gap central limit theorem
together with uniform exponential decay of characteristic functions
away from the origin.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Previous work}

The rationality of symbolic dynamical \(\zeta\)-functions goes back to
Bowen--Lanford and Manning
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom}; see also
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic,ParryPollicott1990Zeta,LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998}.
The theory of Fredholm determinants for trace-class operators, and more
generally for operators in trace ideals, originates with Grothendieck
\cite{Grothendieck1956}, is extended to the nuclear setting by Ruelle
\cite{Ruelle1990Extension}, and is developed systematically in Simon
\cite{Simon2005TraceIdeals}. The product representation for trace-class
Fredholm determinants that underlies the spectral rigidity discussion is
\cite[Theorem~3.7]{Simon2005TraceIdeals}.

The operator-theoretic approach to dynamical zeta-functions via
Fredholm determinants of transfer operators was developed by Pollicott
\cite{Pollicott1986Meromorphic} in the Axiom~A setting. The perturbative
arguments in the symbolic applications draw on transfer-operator
spectral theory in the sense of Ruelle and Baladi
\cite{Ruelle1978Thermodynamic,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer}. For finite-state
spectral-gap CLT and perturbative regularity of eigenvalues, we also
follow the framework of
\cite{Ruelle1969,KellerLiverani1999,HennionHerve2001}. The spectral-gap
approach to limit theorems for Markov chains was initiated by Nagaev
\cite{Nagaev1957} and extended to the dynamical setting by Guivarc'h and
Hardy \cite{GuivarchHardy1988}. For group inverses and reduced
resolvents we use standard matrix-analytic material from
\cite{CampbellMeyer1979}, while the analytic perturbation theory for
isolated eigenvalues is taken from Kato
\cite{Kato1995Perturbation}. Liv\v{s}ic's coboundary criterion is used
in the finite-state setting as in \cite{Livsic1971}.

The present note does not claim a new symbolic model or a new Fredholm
identity. The new point is the strengthened cyclic-block theorem of
\Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation}: sufficiency, necessity in the
normal trace-class class, and optimality of the resulting non-zero block
size. The remaining operator statements are classical connective input,
included because they place the symbolic applications in the right
determinant-theoretic framework.

\subsection{Organisation}

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} recalls the finite-state \(\zeta\)-function,
primitive orbit counts, and the finite-part constants used later.
\Cref{sec:fredholm} contains the Fredholm--Witt bridge, the strengthened
cyclic-block characterization theorem, and the determinant rigidity
statement. \Cref{sec:perturbation} develops the symbolic applications:
holomorphic variation of the finite-part quantities, rigidity of twisted
determinants for locally constant observables, explicit computational
examples, and a comparison with classical finite-dimensional
perturbation theory. \Cref{sec:conclusion} closes with the logical
status of the main ingredients and the scope of the operator-theoretic
package.
