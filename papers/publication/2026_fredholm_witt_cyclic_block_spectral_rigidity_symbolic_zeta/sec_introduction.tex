\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

For a primitive finite matrix \(A\), the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function
\[
  \zeta_A(z)=\det(I-zA)^{-1}
\]
packages periodic orbit data, transfer-operator traces, and the
non-Perron spectrum into a single holomorphic object. In the symbolic
setting this identity is classical, but it is useful to separate two
distinct layers that are often merged in practice. The first layer is
purely operator-theoretic: trace sequences, Witt coordinates, Euler
products, and Fredholm determinants make sense for every trace-class
operator. The second layer is genuinely finite-state: non-negativity,
integrality, and the combinatorial interpretation of primitive orbit
counts depend on the existence of an underlying shift of finite type.

The purpose of this paper is to isolate the operator-theoretic core and
to identify its exact rigidity. On the one hand, the Fredholm
determinant admits a very flexible converse realisation by cyclic
blocks. On the other hand, once the determinant is fixed, the
non-zero spectrum is fixed as well. This produces a clean
existence-versus-rigidity picture. We then return to the symbolic
category and use the same perturbative viewpoint to record two standard
but useful consequences: holomorphic variation of finite-part
constants and spectral-gap limit laws for locally constant
observables.

\subsection{Main results}

Our first theorem collects the operator-theoretic core of the paper.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:intro-fredholm-rigidity}
Let \(T\in \sone(H)\) be a trace-class operator on a complex Hilbert
space. Define
\[
  a_n(T):=\Tr(T^n),
  \qquad
  p_n(T):=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{d\mid n}\mu(d)\,a_{n/d}(T)
  \qquad (n\ge 1).
\]
Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
  \item For every \(\abs{z}<\norm{T}^{-1}\),
  \[
    \det(I-zT)^{-1}
    =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{a_n(T)}{n}z^n\right)
    =\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-p_n(T)}.
  \]

  \item Let \(\{(n_j,\beta_j,m_j)\}_{j\in J}\) be a countable family
  with \(n_j,m_j\in\NN\), \(\beta_j\in\CC\), and
  \[
    \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j \abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty.
  \]
  Then the Euler product
  \[
    \prod_{j\in J}(1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
  \]
  admits a trace-class Fredholm realisation by an explicit direct sum
  of cyclic blocks.

  \item If \(T_1,T_2\in \sone\) satisfy
  \[
    \det(I-zT_1)=\det(I-zT_2)
  \]
  in a neighbourhood of \(z=0\), then \(T_1\) and \(T_2\) have the
  same non-zero eigenvalues with algebraic multiplicity.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

Part \textup{(ii)} shows that Fredholm determinants are considerably
more flexible than finite-state dynamical models. Part \textup{(iii)}
shows that this flexibility stops at the level of the non-zero
spectrum.

Our second theorem records two perturbative consequences in the
finite-state setting.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:intro-perturbative-applications}
Let \(\theta\mapsto A_\theta\) be a holomorphic family of primitive
matrices, and let \(\lambda(\theta)=\rho(A_\theta)\) be the simple
Perron root. Then:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
  \item The finite-part quantities \(C(A_\theta)\) and
  \(\log\MM(A_\theta)\) vary holomorphically. If
  \(R_\theta=(I-A_\theta/\lambda(\theta))^{\#}\) denotes the group
  inverse on the reduced spectral complement, then
  \[
    \partial_\theta\log C(A_\theta)
    =\Tr\!\left(
      R_\theta\left(
        \frac{\partial_\theta A_\theta}{\lambda(\theta)}
        -\frac{\partial_\theta\lambda(\theta)}{\lambda(\theta)^2}
         A_\theta\right)\right),
  \]
  and \(\partial_\theta\log\MM(A_\theta)\) is obtained by adding the
  absolutely convergent higher-order Fredholm corrections.

  \item Let \((X_A^+,\sigma)\) be the mixing subshift associated with a
  primitive matrix \(A\), let \(\mu\) be the Parry measure, and let
  \(f:X_A^+\to\RR\) be locally constant. If \(f\) is not a coboundary
  modulo constants, then there exists \(\sigma_f^2>0\) such that
  \[
    \frac{\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}f\circ\sigma^k
      -n\int f\,\dd\mu}{\sqrt n}
    \Longrightarrow \mathcal N(0,\sigma_f^2),
  \]
  and the corresponding characteristic functions decay exponentially
  fast away from the origin.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Previous work}

The rationality of symbolic dynamical \(\zeta\)-functions goes back to
Bowen--Lanford and Manning
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom}; see also
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic,ParryPollicott1990Zeta,LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998}.
The operator-theoretic side of the paper is closer in spirit to the
Fredholm determinant formalism for trace-class operators
\cite{Simon2005TraceIdeals}, while the perturbative arguments for the
symbolic applications are standard consequences of transfer-operator
spectral theory in the sense of Ruelle and Baladi
\cite{Ruelle1978Thermodynamic,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer}.

The novelty of the present note is not a new symbolic model. Rather, it
is the systematic separation of three statements that are often used
implicitly together but are logically distinct: the Fredholm--Witt
bridge, the cyclic-block converse realisation, and the spectral
rigidity of the resulting determinant.
