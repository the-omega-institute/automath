\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

For a trace-class operator \(T\) on a complex Hilbert space, the
Fredholm determinant
\[
  Z_T(z):=\det(I-zT)^{-1}
\]
is a holomorphic germ at \(z=0\) and satisfies Grothendieck's trace
formula
\[
  Z_T(z)=\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(T^n)}{n}z^n\right).
\]
In finite dimension this reduces to the familiar identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-finite-zeta}
  \det(I-zA)^{-1}
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-p_n(A)}.
\end{equation}
In symbolic dynamics, \eqref{eq:intro-finite-zeta} is one of the standard
forms of the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function; see
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom,Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,ParryPollicott1990Zeta,LindMarcus1995}.
Motivated by this Euler-product form, we study the following narrow
realization problem in the Hilbert trace-class category. Given countable
Euler data
\[
  \mathcal D=\{(n_j,\beta_j,m_j)\}_{j\in J},
  \qquad
  n_j,m_j\in\NN,\ \beta_j\in\CC\setminus\{0\},
\]
we ask when the germ
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-euler-product}
  \prod_{j\in J}(1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\end{equation}
can occur as the Fredholm zeta function of a trace-class operator, and
what spectral information is then forced by the determinant.

Fredholm determinants for trace and nuclear operators, together with their
factorization properties and the description of their zeros, belong to the
classical theory; see
\cite{Grothendieck1956,GohbergKrein1969,Simon2005TraceIdeals,GohbergGoldbergKrupnik2000}.
In dynamics, related determinant formalisms on Banach spaces are used to
study transfer operators, meromorphic continuation, and spectral
variation; see, for instance,
\cite{Ruelle1990Extension,Pollicott1986Meromorphic,Keller1989Fredholm,KellerLiverani1999,BaladiTsujii2007,Baladi2018DynZeta}.
We do not address those more flexible settings here. Rather, we stay in
the Hilbert trace and Schatten categories, where the relevant
ingredients are already classical: Grothendieck's formula, Simon's
description of the zero divisor of Fredholm and regularized
determinants, the spectral theorem for compact normal operators, and
classical triangularization and eigenvalue--singular-value inequalities
for compact operators. The point of the paper is not a new determinant
formalism, nor an extension of dynamical determinants beyond this
elementary Hilbert-space setting. It is rather to isolate what these
classical tools imply, for prescribed Euler products, in one concrete
normal-form package: explicit cyclic-block realizations, sharp norm
minimality, and a complete description of the non-zero Jordan freedom.

The first part of that package concerns normal trace-class realizations.
For \(n\ge 1\), let \(\Pi_n\) be the cyclic permutation matrix on
\(\CC^n\), and set \(C(n,\alpha)=\alpha\Pi_n\).
\Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation} shows that every data set
satisfying
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-summability}
  \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\end{equation}
gives a normal trace-class model operator
\[
  L_{\mathcal D}
  =\bigoplus_{j\in J}\bigl(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j,
\]
and every normal realization is unitarily equivalent to
\(0\oplus L_{\mathcal D}\). Thus the Fredholm zeta germ determines both
the total algebraic multiplicity of the non-zero spectrum and the trace
norm in the normal class. If
\[
  N(T):=\sum_{\lambda\in\spec(T)\setminus\{0\}}m_a(\lambda),
\]
then every normal realization \(L\) satisfies
\[
  N(L)=\sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j,
  \qquad
  \|L\|_1=\sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}.
\]
Thus the existence statement is exact in the normal class. The later
trace-class results do not enlarge that realization theorem; instead
they describe what any trace-class operator with the same determinant
must look like away from the spectral point \(0\).

The determinant also controls the ambient trace-class problem.
\Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality} shows that the same quantity is the least
possible trace norm among all trace-class realizations of
\eqref{eq:intro-euler-product}, and that equality characterizes the normal
model. Thus the cyclic-block realization is singled out variationally by
the determinant. On the other hand, once the non-zero eigenvalue multiset is
fixed, the determinant places no further restriction on Jordan structure
away from the spectral point \(0\). \Cref{thm:nonnormal-jordan-classification}
shows that every family of Jordan partitions compatible with the forced
algebraic multiplicities is realized by a trace-class operator. The
infimum of the trace norm over such a Jordan class is still the normal
value, and it is attained only in the semisimple case.

The Schatten-class variant uses regularized determinants, which suppress
the first \(p-1\) trace coordinates while preserving the same non-zero
zero divisor. For every integer \(p\ge 2\),
\Cref{thm:schatten-regularized,cor:schatten-mass,thm:schatten-jordan}
shows that the same cyclic-block model remains the normal realization of
the renormalized Euler factors
\[
  (1-\beta z^n)^{-1}
  \exp\!\left(
    -\sum_{\ell=1}^{\lfloor (p-1)/n\rfloor}\frac{\beta^\ell z^{n\ell}}{\ell}
  \right),
\]
provided that
\[
  \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{p/n_j}<\infty.
\]
The resulting spectral quantity
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{p/n_j}
\)
is determined directly by the regularized determinant through a
Jensen--Mellin formula, gives the sharp lower bound for the Schatten
\(p\)-norm among all realizations with the prescribed regularized
determinant, and remains the optimal infimum in every compatible
non-zero Jordan class. Again, the exact realization statement is made in
the normal class, while the non-normal results are minimality and
Jordan-profile statements for operators with the same regularized
determinant.

The finite-dimensional proposition in \Cref{sec:preliminaries} is
included only to keep in view the symbolic-dynamical source of the Euler
products; all later proofs are operator-theoretic.

The paper is organized as follows. In \Cref{sec:preliminaries} we record
the determinant identities and conventions used later. Section
\Cref{sec:fredholm} proves the normal trace-class realization theorem.
Section \Cref{sec:rigidity} studies the determinant constraints that
persist in the full trace-class category, including trace-norm
minimality and the possible Jordan geometry away from \(0\). Section
\Cref{sec:schatten} gives the regularized-determinant analogue in every
Schatten class \(\mathcal S_p\), including the Jensen--Mellin mass
formula, sharp \(\mathcal S_p\)-minimality, and the corresponding
Jordan-profile classification.
