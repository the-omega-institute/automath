\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

For a trace-class operator \(T\) on a complex Hilbert space, the
Fredholm determinant
\[
  Z_T(z):=\det(I-zT)^{-1}
\]
is holomorphic near \(z=0\) and satisfies Grothendieck's trace formula
\[
  Z_T(z)=\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(T^n)}{n}z^n\right).
\]
In finite dimension one recovers the familiar identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-finite-zeta}
  \det(I-zA)^{-1}
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-p_n(A)}.
\end{equation}
For shifts of finite type, \eqref{eq:intro-finite-zeta} is one of the
standard forms of the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function; see
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom,Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,ParryPollicott1990Zeta,LindMarcus1995}.
This leads naturally to the following realization problem. Given
countable data
\[
  \mathcal D=\{(n_j,\beta_j,m_j)\}_{j\in J},
  \qquad
  n_j,m_j\in\NN,\ \beta_j\in\CC\setminus\{0\},
\]
when does the germ
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-euler-product}
  \prod_{j\in J}(1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\end{equation}
occur as the Fredholm zeta function of a trace-class operator, and how
much of the operator is then determined by the determinant alone?

Fredholm determinants for trace and nuclear operators, together with the
description of their zeros, belong to the classical theory; see
\cite{Grothendieck1956,GohbergKrein1969,Simon2005TraceIdeals,GohbergGoldbergKrupnik2000}.
In dynamics, determinant formalisms on Banach spaces are used to study
transfer operators, meromorphic continuation, and spectral variation; see
for instance
\cite{Ruelle1990Extension,Pollicott1986Meromorphic,Keller1989Fredholm,KellerLiverani1999,BaladiTsujii2007,Baladi2018DynZeta}.
We do not enter that more flexible framework here. The point is rather
to isolate, in a purely Hilbert-space setting, what the classical
determinant theory already implies for prescribed Euler products.

A precise division between classical input and new results is useful.
The realization theorem and the identification of the non-zero spectrum
rest on Grothendieck--Simon determinant theory, the spectral theorem for
normal compact operators, and Ringrose triangularization. The new
contribution begins when the determinant-constrained inverse problem is
recast as a minimization and classification problem: the Jordan
classification theorems
\Cref{thm:nonnormal-jordan-classification,thm:schatten-jordan} and the
quantitative stability theorem \Cref{thm:schatten-quantitative-stability}
show that the determinant not only fixes the non-zero spectral multiset
but also makes the normal block-cyclic orbit coercive in Schatten
geometry.

\subsection{Trace-class results}

For \(n\ge 1\), let \(\Pi_n\) be the cyclic permutation matrix on
\(\CC^n\), and set \(C(n,\alpha):=\alpha\Pi_n\). Our first result,
\Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation}, shows that the summability
condition
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-summability}
  \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\end{equation}
is exactly the criterion for a normal trace-class realization of
\eqref{eq:intro-euler-product}. Such a realization is given by the
explicit block-cyclic operator
\[
  L_{\mathcal D}
  =\bigoplus_{j\in J}\bigl(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j,
\]
and every normal realization is unitarily equivalent to
\(0\oplus L_{\mathcal D}\). In particular, if
\[
  N(T):=\sum_{\lambda\in\spec(T)\setminus\{0\}}m_a(\lambda),
\]
then every normal realization \(L\) satisfies
\[
  N(L)=\sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j,
  \qquad
  \|L\|_1=\sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}.
\]

Once one admissible block-cyclic model exists, the determinant retains
more information in the full trace-class category than only the trace
sequence. By
\Cref{prop:fredholm-factorisation-rigidity,thm:general-support-optimality},
every trace-class realization with determinant
\eqref{eq:intro-euler-product} has the same non-zero eigenvalue multiset,
counted with algebraic multiplicity. This yields the sharp lower bound
\[
  \|T\|_1\ge \sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
\]
proved in \Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality}; equality holds precisely for
the normal block-cyclic model. On the other hand,
\Cref{thm:nonnormal-jordan-classification} shows that the determinant
imposes no further restriction on the Jordan structure away from
\(0\): every compatible family of Jordan partitions occurs, and within a
fixed Jordan class the infimum of the trace norm is again the normal
value, attained only in the semisimple case.

\subsection{Regularized determinants}

For each integer \(p\ge 2\), the same discussion admits a regularized
analogue in the Schatten class \(\mathcal S_p\). The relevant Euler
factors are
\[
  (1-\beta z^n)^{-1}
  \exp\!\left(
    -\sum_{\ell=1}^{\lfloor (p-1)/n\rfloor}\frac{\beta^\ell z^{n\ell}}{\ell}
  \right),
\]
and \Cref{thm:schatten-regularized} shows that the same block-cyclic
model realizes the corresponding product whenever
\[
  \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{p/n_j}<\infty.
\]
The regularized determinant again fixes the non-zero spectrum, gives the
sharp lower bound for the Schatten \(p\)-norm, and singles out the
normal realization as the equality case. In addition,
\Cref{thm:schatten-quantitative-stability} strengthens that equality
statement to a coercive estimate: the Schatten \(p\)-norm defect above
the determinant-forced minimum controls the \(\mathcal S_p\)-distance to
the normal block-cyclic orbit, and for \(p=2\) the defect is exactly
the squared Hilbert--Schmidt distance to the diagonal normal part in a
triangularizing basis. Moreover,
\Cref{cor:schatten-mass} recovers the spectral \(p\)-mass
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{p/n_j}
\)
from the regularized determinant by a Jensen--Mellin formula, while
\Cref{thm:schatten-jordan} gives the corresponding Jordan
classification.

The finite-dimensional proposition in \Cref{sec:preliminaries} is
included only to keep the symbolic-dynamical origin of the Euler
products visible; all later arguments are operator-theoretic.

\subsection{Organization of the paper}

In \Cref{sec:preliminaries} we record the determinant identities used
throughout. Section \Cref{sec:fredholm} treats normal trace-class
realizations. Section \Cref{sec:rigidity} turns to arbitrary
trace-class operators and proves the rigidity, minimality, and Jordan
classification statements. Section \Cref{sec:schatten} contains the
regularized-determinant analogue in the Schatten classes. A short
conclusion closes the paper.
