\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

For a finite matrix \(A\), one has the familiar identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-finite-zeta}
  \det(I-zA)^{-1}
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-p_n(A)}.
\end{equation}
In symbolic dynamics, \eqref{eq:intro-finite-zeta} is one of the standard
forms of the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function; see
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom,Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,ParryPollicott1990Zeta,LindMarcus1995}.
The purpose of this paper is to study the corresponding inverse problem in
the trace-class setting. Given countable Euler data
\[
  \mathcal D=\{(n_j,\beta_j,m_j)\}_{j\in J},
  \qquad
  n_j,m_j\in\NN,\ \beta_j\in\CC\setminus\{0\},
\]
we ask when the germ
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-euler-product}
  \prod_{j\in J}(1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\end{equation}
can be realized as the Fredholm zeta function
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-fredholm-zeta}
  Z_T(z):=\det(I-zT)^{-1}
\end{equation}
of a normal trace-class operator.

Fredholm determinants for trace and nuclear operators, together with their
factorization properties and the description of their zeros, belong to the
classical theory; see
\cite{Grothendieck1956,GohbergKrein1969,Simon2005TraceIdeals,GohbergGoldbergKrupnik2000}.
In dynamics, related determinant formalisms on Banach spaces are used to
study transfer operators, meromorphic continuation, and spectral
variation; see, for instance,
\cite{Ruelle1990Extension,Keller1989Fredholm,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,Baladi2018DynZeta}.
We do not address those more flexible settings here. Rather, we stay in
the Hilbert trace-class category and ask a narrower question: for a
prescribed Euler product, when does a realization exist, and to what
extent is it determined by the determinant?

The answer is rigid. For \(n\ge 1\), let \(\Pi_n\) be the cyclic
permutation matrix on \(\CC^n\), and set \(C(n,\alpha)=\alpha\Pi_n\).
\Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation} shows that the condition
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-summability}
  \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\end{equation}
is necessary and sufficient for normal trace-class realizability. Whenever
\eqref{eq:intro-summability} holds, one obtains a model operator
\[
  L_{\mathcal D}
  =\bigoplus_{j\in J}\bigl(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j,
\]
and every normal realization is unitarily equivalent to
\(0\oplus L_{\mathcal D}\). Thus the Fredholm zeta germ determines both
the total algebraic multiplicity of the non-zero spectrum and the trace
norm in the normal class. If
\[
  N(T):=\sum_{\lambda\in\spec(T)\setminus\{0\}}m_a(\lambda),
\]
then every normal realization \(L\) satisfies
\[
  N(L)=\sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j,
  \qquad
  \|L\|_1=\sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}.
\]

The determinant also controls the ambient trace-class problem.
\Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality} shows that the same quantity is the least
possible trace norm among all trace-class realizations of
\eqref{eq:intro-euler-product}, and that equality characterizes the normal
model. In this sense the cyclic-block realization is not merely one
realization among many; it is singled out variationally by the Fredholm
determinant. On the other hand, once the non-zero eigenvalue multiset is
fixed, the determinant places no further restriction on Jordan structure
away from the spectral point \(0\). \Cref{thm:nonnormal-jordan-classification}
shows that every family of Jordan partitions compatible with the forced
algebraic multiplicities is realized by a trace-class operator. The
infimum of the trace norm over such a Jordan class is still the normal
value, and it is attained only in the semisimple case.

The proofs use only standard ingredients: Grothendieck's formula for the
Fredholm determinant, Simon's description of the zeros of
\(\det(I-zT)\), the spectral theorem for compact normal operators, and
classical triangularization results for compact operators. The point is
that, in this inverse problem, these ingredients combine to give a sharp
realization theorem, a unitary classification in the normal class, and a
complete description of the freedom that remains outside it. The
finite-dimensional proposition in \Cref{sec:preliminaries} is included
only to keep in view the symbolic-dynamical source of the Euler products;
the proofs of the main results are operator-theoretic throughout.

The paper is organized as follows. In \Cref{sec:preliminaries} we record
the determinant identities used later. Section \Cref{sec:fredholm}
contains the normal realization theorem and the cyclic-block model. In
\Cref{sec:rigidity} we study the determinant rigidity that persists in the
full trace-class class, establish the trace-norm minimality statement, and
describe the possible Jordan geometry away from \(0\).
