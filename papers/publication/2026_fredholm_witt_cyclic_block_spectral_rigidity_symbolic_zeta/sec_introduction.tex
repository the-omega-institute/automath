\section{Introduction and main results}\label{sec:introduction}

Let \(T\) be a trace-class operator on a complex Hilbert space. Its
Fredholm determinant
\[
  Z_T(z):=\det(I-zT)^{-1}
\]
is holomorphic near \(z=0\) and satisfies Grothendieck's trace formula
\[
  Z_T(z)=\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(T^n)}{n}z^n\right).
\]
In finite dimension one also has the familiar Euler product
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-finite-zeta}
  \det(I-zA)^{-1}
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-p_n(A)}.
\end{equation}
For shifts of finite type, \eqref{eq:intro-finite-zeta} is one of the
standard expressions for the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function; see
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom,Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,ParryPollicott1990Zeta,LindMarcus1995}.

The starting point of the paper is the following realization problem.
Given countable data
\[
  \mathcal D=\{(n_j,\beta_j,m_j)\}_{j\in J},
  \qquad
  n_j,m_j\in\NN,\ \beta_j\in\CC\setminus\{0\},
\]
when does the germ
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-euler-product}
  \prod_{j\in J}(1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\end{equation}
occur as the Fredholm determinant of a trace-class operator, and to what
extent does this determinant determine the operator?

The general theory of Fredholm and regularized determinants already
shows that the zero set of \(\det(I-zT)\) recovers the non-zero
eigenvalues of \(T\), counted with algebraic multiplicity; see
\cite{Grothendieck1956,GohbergKrein1969,Simon2005TraceIdeals}. Our aim
is to make this principle explicit for Euler products of the form
\eqref{eq:intro-euler-product}, and to understand the resulting
extremal and rigidity phenomena in trace ideals. The symbolic-dynamical
language provides the motivation for the product expansion, but the
arguments themselves are operator-theoretic. We do not work in the more
flexible Banach-space setting of dynamical determinants; for that circle
of ideas we refer, for instance, to
\cite{Fried1986,Ruelle1990Extension,Pollicott1986Meromorphic,Keller1989Fredholm,Baladi2018DynZeta}.

\subsection{Trace class}

For \(n\ge 1\), let \(\Pi_n\) denote the cyclic permutation matrix on
\(\CC^n\), and write \(C(n,\alpha):=\alpha\Pi_n\). The first main result
identifies the normal trace-class realizations of
\eqref{eq:intro-euler-product}. More precisely,
\Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation} shows that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:intro-summability}
  \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\end{equation}
is necessary and sufficient for the existence of a normal realization.
Whenever \eqref{eq:intro-summability} holds, one obtains an explicit
model
\[
  L_{\mathcal D}
  =\bigoplus_{j\in J}\bigl(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j,
\]
and every normal realization is unitarily equivalent to
\(0\oplus L_{\mathcal D}\). In particular, the determinant fixes both
the non-zero spectrum and the trace norm inside the normal class.

The more interesting point is that, once the normal model exists, the
same determinant data constrain arbitrary trace-class realizations much
more strongly than one might expect from the trace sequence alone.
Indeed, \Cref{prop:fredholm-factorisation-rigidity,thm:general-support-optimality}
show that every trace-class operator with determinant germ
\eqref{eq:intro-euler-product} has the same non-zero eigenvalue multiset
as \(L_{\mathcal D}\). From this one obtains the sharp estimate
\[
  \|T\|_1\ge \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
\]
with equality precisely in the normal case; see
\Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality}. On the other hand,
\Cref{thm:nonnormal-jordan-classification} shows that away from the
origin the determinant imposes no restriction on the Jordan structure
beyond the forced algebraic multiplicities: every compatible family of
Jordan partitions occurs. Within a fixed Jordan class, the infimum of
the trace norm is still the normal value, and it is attained exactly in
the semisimple case.

\subsection{Regularized determinants}

For each integer \(p\ge 2\), one can make the same analysis in the
Schatten class \(\mathcal S_p\), using the regularized determinant
\(\det_p(I-zT)\). The corresponding Euler factors are
\[
  (1-\beta z^n)^{-1}
  \exp\!\left(
    -\sum_{\ell=1}^{\lfloor (p-1)/n\rfloor}\frac{\beta^\ell z^{n\ell}}{\ell}
  \right),
\]
and \Cref{thm:schatten-regularized} proves that the same block-cyclic
operator \(L_{\mathcal D}\) is a normal realization precisely when
\[
  \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{p/n_j}<\infty.
\]
As in trace class, the determinant then determines the non-zero
spectrum, yields the sharp lower bound for the Schatten \(p\)-norm, and
characterizes the normal case as the equality case.

What is specific to the Schatten setting is the quantitative stability
statement in \Cref{thm:schatten-quantitative-stability}: the defect of
\(\|T\|_p^p\) above the determinant-forced minimum controls the
\(\mathcal S_p\)-distance to the normal block-cyclic orbit, and for
\(p=2\) this defect is exactly the squared Hilbert--Schmidt distance.
We also recover the spectral \(p\)-mass from the regularized determinant
by a Jensen--Mellin formula; see \Cref{cor:schatten-mass}. Finally,
\Cref{thm:schatten-jordan} gives the corresponding classification of
Jordan structures away from \(0\).

\Cref{sec:rigidity} treat the trace-class realization and rigidity
