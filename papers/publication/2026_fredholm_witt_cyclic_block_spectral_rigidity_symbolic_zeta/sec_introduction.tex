\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

For a finite matrix \(A\), the identity
\[
  \det(I-zA)^{-1}
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\prod_{n\ge 1}(1-z^n)^{-p_n(A)}
\]
is one of the standard forms of the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function in
symbolic dynamics; see
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom,Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,ParryPollicott1990Zeta,LindMarcus1995}.
The present paper is concerned with the corresponding converse problem
at the Fredholm level. Given Euler data
\[
  \mathcal D=\{(n_j,\beta_j,m_j)\}_{j\in J},
  \qquad
  n_j,m_j\in\NN,\ \beta_j\in\CC\setminus\{0\},
\]
we ask when the germ
\[
  \prod_{j\in J}(1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}
\]
can be realized as the Fredholm zeta function
\(
  Z_T(z)=\det(I-zT)^{-1}
\)
of a normal trace-class operator.

This question sits inside a much larger literature. Fredholm
determinants for trace and nuclear operators, together with their zero
sets and factorization properties, are classical; see
\cite{Grothendieck1956,GohbergKrein1969,Simon2005TraceIdeals,GohbergGoldbergKrupnik2000}.
In dynamical systems, related determinant formalisms are used on Banach
spaces tailored to transfer operators for meromorphic continuation,
spectral variation, or linear response; see, for example,
\cite{Ruelle1990Extension,Keller1989Fredholm,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,Baladi2018DynZeta}.
The present paper does not enter those non-Hilbertian settings and does
not address inverse spectral problems in comparable generality.
Instead, it asks for exact realizability and rigidity questions inside
the trace-class Hilbert-space category.

The answer is particularly rigid. Let \(C(n,\alpha)=\alpha\Pi_n\),
where \(\Pi_n\) is the cyclic permutation matrix on \(\CC^n\). The main
result, \Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation}, shows that the
summability condition
\[
  \sum_{j\in J}m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\]
is necessary and sufficient for normal trace-class realizability. When
it holds, the direct sum of cyclic blocks
\[
  \bigoplus_{j\in J}\bigl(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\bigr),
  \qquad
  \alpha_j^{n_j}=\beta_j,
\]
is trace class and has the prescribed Fredholm zeta function; conversely,
every normal realization is unitarily equivalent to this model up to an
arbitrary zero summand. If
\[
  N(T):=\sum_{\lambda\in\spec(T)\setminus\{0\}}m_a(\lambda)
\]
denotes the total algebraic multiplicity of the non-zero spectrum, then
every normal realization \(L\) of the given germ satisfies
\[
  N(L)=\sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j,
  \qquad
  \|L\|_1=\sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}.
\]
For compact normal operators, \(N(L)\) is the rank of the support
projection \(\mathbf 1_{(0,\infty)}(|L|)\), equivalently
\(\dim\overline{\operatorname{ran}(L)}\).

Our second main result, \Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality}, places the
same block-cyclic model in the full trace-class category. If
\(T\in \sone(H)\) has the prescribed Fredholm zeta function, then
\[
  \|T\|_1\ge \sum_{j\in J}m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j},
\]
and equality holds if and only if \(T\) is normal, or equivalently, if
and only if \(T\) is block-cyclic up to unitary equivalence and an
arbitrary zero summand. In this sense the Fredholm zeta germ fixes not
only the non-zero eigenvalue multiset, but also the minimal trace norm
among all trace-class realizations.

The non-normal category admits a stronger structural description.
Classical compact-operator theory provides triangular and root-space
decompositions for isolated eigenvalues
\cite{GohbergKrein1969,Ringrose1971}, but those results do not identify
which Jordan profiles remain compatible with a fixed Fredholm
determinant. \Cref{thm:nonnormal-jordan-classification} shows that, once
the determinant-forced non-zero eigenvalue multiset is fixed, there is
no further finite-dimensional obstruction: every partition of each
non-zero algebraic multiplicity is realized by a trace-class operator
with the prescribed Fredholm zeta germ. The same theorem shows that the
trace-norm infimum over any fixed non-zero Jordan profile is the normal
value
\(
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}
\),
and that this infimum is attained only in the semisimple profile.

All determinant identities used below are classical. For the Fredholm
determinant we rely on Grothendieck's formula and Simon's description of
the zeros of \(\det(I-zT)\)
\cite{Grothendieck1956,Simon2005TraceIdeals}; for the normal converse
statement we use in addition the spectral theorem for compact normal
operators \cite{Conway1990Course}. What seems not to be recorded in the
cited literature is the exact package obtained here in the Hilbert
trace-class setting: a necessary and sufficient normal realizability
criterion for arbitrary summable Euler data, a unitary classification of
all normal realizations, a determinant-constrained trace-norm
minimization theorem in the full trace-class class, and a complete
description of the admissible non-zero Jordan profiles. The paper should
therefore be read as a precise synthesis in a deliberately restricted
setting, rather than as a new Fredholm-determinant formalism or a new
dynamical-determinant theory. The symbolic-dynamical input is kept to a
minimum: \Cref{prop:prelim-trace-primitive} is included only to record
the finite-dimensional source of the Euler products, while the proofs of
the main results are operator-theoretic.

The paper is organized as follows. In \Cref{sec:preliminaries} we record
the finite-dimensional and Fredholm determinant identities used later.
\Cref{sec:fredholm} contains the cyclic-block classification of normal
realizations. In \Cref{sec:rigidity} we establish the determinant-induced
spectral rigidity statements, the trace-norm minimality theorem, the
non-normal Jordan classification, and the residual freedom at the
spectral point \(0\). \Cref{sec:conclusion} summarizes the resulting
normal and non-normal realizability picture.
