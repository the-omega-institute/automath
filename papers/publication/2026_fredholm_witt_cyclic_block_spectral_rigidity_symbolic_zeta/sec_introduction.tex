\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

For a finite matrix \(A\), the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function
\(
  z\mapsto \det(I-zA)^{-1}
\)
simultaneously packages periodic-orbit counts, transfer-operator traces,
and non-Perron spectral data; see
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom,Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,ParryPollicott1990Zeta,LindMarcus1995,Kitchens1998}.
In symbolic dynamics this classical identity is the finite-state shadow
of the Fredholm zeta formulas available for trace-class operators.

This note studies the converse normal realisation problem for Euler
products of the form
\[
  \prod_j (1-\beta_j z^{n_j})^{-m_j}.
\]
Throughout, for a trace-class operator \(T\) we write
\[
  N(T):=\sum_{\lambda\in\spec(T)\setminus\{0\}} m_a(\lambda),
\]
where \(m_a(\lambda)\) is the algebraic multiplicity. When \(L\) is
compact and normal,
\[
  N(L)=\operatorname{rank}\mathbf 1_{(0,\infty)}(|L|)
  =\dim\overline{\mathrm{ran}(L)},
\]
so the support projection, rather than the possibly non-closed
range, carries the determinant-intrinsic invariant.

The main theorem, \Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation}, shows that the
summability condition
\[
  \sum_j m_j n_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}<\infty
\]
is exactly the criterion for a normal trace-class realisation of the
prescribed Euler data: the Fredholm zeta function is realised by a
direct sum of cyclic blocks \(C(n_j,\alpha_j)\otimes I_{m_j}\), and every
normal realisation is unitarily equivalent to that model after adjoining
a zero summand. As a consequence,
\[
  N(L)=\sum_j m_jn_j,
  \qquad
  \|L\|_1=\sum_j m_jn_j\abs{\beta_j}^{1/n_j}
\]
for every normal realisation, and
\Cref{cor:determinant-intrinsic-normal-data} shows that these quantities
depend only on the Fredholm zeta germ. Simon's zero-description theorem
propagates the same non-zero eigenvalue multiset to every trace-class
re\-alisation. A new rigidity statement,
\Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality}, strengthens this picture by proving
that the cyclic-block normal model uniquely minimises the trace norm
among all trace-class operators with that Fredholm zeta function, with
equality forcing the block-cyclic form. The symbolic-dynamical
specialisation in \Cref{cor:symbolic-trace-norm} applies this
minimality to the dynamical \(\zeta\)-functions of primitive subshifts
via \Cref{prop:prelim-trace-primitive}.

Canonical products, zero distributions, and Fredholm determinant
terminology follow standard references: Levin
\cite{LevinZeros} and Boas \cite{BoasEntireFunctions} for entire
functions, Grothendieck \cite{Grothendieck1956}, Simon
\cite{Simon2005TraceIdeals}, and Gohberg--Goldberg--Krupnik
\cite{GohbergGoldbergKrupnik2000,GohbergGoldbergKrupnik1996} for trace
and determinant theory, and Conway \cite{Conway1990Course} for the
spectral theorem. The necklace and Witt terminology attached to the
Euler factors is classical; see
\cite{MetropolisRota1983,DressSiebeneicher1988,DressSiebeneicher1989}.
Beyond the finite-state setting, background on dynamical determinants
and transfer operators is provided by
\cite{Pollicott1986Meromorphic,Keller1989Fredholm,BaladiTsujii2007,Baladi2018DynZeta,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,Kato1995Perturbation,PollicottVytnova2016,Jezequel2019Parameter}.

\subsection*{What is classical and what is new}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \Cref{thm:fredholm-witt-bridge} (Fredholm zeta versus Euler
  product) is a direct transcription of Grothendieck's theory.
  \item The consolidated rigidity proposition
  (\Cref{prop:fredholm-factorisation-rigidity}, merging former
  Lemma~3.9 and Proposition~3.14) is standard once Simon's
  zero-description theorem and the compact-normal spectral theorem are
  invoked.
  \item The determinant-imposed eigenvalue statement
  (\Cref{thm:general-support-optimality}) is an immediate consequence of
  \Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation} together with Simon's theorem.
  \item The new contributions are the cyclic-block synthesis in
  \Cref{thm:cyclic-fredholm-realisation}, the trace-norm minimality
  principle in \Cref{thm:trace-norm-minimality}, and the
  symbolic-dynamical trace-norm corollary
  (\Cref{cor:symbolic-trace-norm}).
\end{itemize}

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} compresses the finite-state motivation to a
single proposition. \Cref{sec:fredholm} contains the Fredholm
zeta/Euler-product identity, the cyclic-block classification, the
factorization-intrinsic rigidity statements, and the
trace-norm results together with their symbolic corollary.
\Cref{sec:conclusion} records the scope and open directions.
