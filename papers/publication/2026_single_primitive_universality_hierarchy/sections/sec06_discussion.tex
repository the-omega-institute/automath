\section{Discussion and Outlook}\label{sec:discussion}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

% ── 6.1  The structural motif ────────────────────────────
\subsection{The single-primitive motif}

The history of foundations is, in part, a history of discovering
that apparent diversity hides a single generator:
NAND for Boolean functions, $\lambda$ for computable functions,
$S$--$K$ for combinatory logic.
Odrzywołek's EML result extends this motif to continuous
elementary functions, demonstrating that the 36-key scientific
calculator is structurally redundant.

Our hierarchy reveals that this motif has \emph{depth}.
Collapsing a function library to a single operator
(expression universality) is qualitatively weaker than collapsing
the \emph{operations} on that library to a single protocol
(protocol universality), which is in turn weaker than equipping
the protocol with an intrinsic positive-definite audit kernel
(certificate universality).

The analogy in computer science would be:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item L0: a universal instruction set (e.g., a single-instruction
  computer);
\item L1: a compiler that produces canonical object code with
  decidable equivalence;
\item L2: a compiler that additionally emits a proof certificate
  (cf.\ proof-carrying code~\cite{Necula1997}).
\end{itemize}

% ── 6.2  Implications for symbolic regression ────────────
\subsection{Implications for symbolic regression}

Odrzywołek applies EML universality to symbolic regression:
the uniform tree grammar $S \to 1 \mid \eml(S,S)$ flattens the
search space, eliminating the combinatorial explosion of choosing
among dozens of operators at each node.

The protocol and certificate levels suggest a stronger approach.
At L1, the search space is not merely flattened---it is
\emph{canonicalized}.  Two candidate expressions that compute the
same function map to the same Zeckendorf normal form, eliminating
semantic duplicates that L0 search must handle post hoc.
At L2, the Hankel spectral fingerprint provides a
\emph{resolution-parameterized similarity kernel}
(\Cref{thm:cross-kernel}): candidates can
be compared not just by output agreement but by structural
similarity of their fiber spectra, enabling kernel-based search
methods (Gaussian processes, kernel PCA) over the space of
symbolic expressions.

The derived consequences of \Cref{sec:consequences}
further strengthen this picture.  The escort concentration
theorem (\Cref{thm:escort-ldp}) shows that the collision
directing measure freezes onto the maximum-fiber locus at
rate $\Delta_{m}$, while the collision zeta function
(\Cref{thm:zeta}) reveals that the full collision theory
at fixed resolution is a finite Euler product over
fiber-size alphabet shifts with necklace-polynomial
primitive orbits.  The Gibbs variational principle on
fibers (\Cref{thm:gibbs}) completes the thermodynamic
picture: the termination potential governs the rewriting
trajectory, while the free energy $\psi_{x}$ governs
the residual entropy within each fiber.

% ── 6.3  What the hierarchy does not claim ───────────────
\subsection{Scope and limitations}

Several caveats deserve emphasis:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item The hierarchy compares \emph{structural depth of
  universality}, not computational power.  The distinctions lie
  in canonicalization, decidability, and audit structure,
  not in the class of computable functions that can be
  represented.

\item The L0 $\not\Rightarrow$ L1 separation
  (\Cref{thm:separation}) relies on the undecidability of
  expression equivalence over~$\RR$.  For restricted function
  classes (e.g., polynomials, rational functions) where
  equivalence is decidable, the gap narrows.  The hierarchy
  is sharpest for the full elementary-function setting that
  EML targets.

\item The concrete realization of L1 and L2 in this paper
  uses Fibonacci--Zeckendorf numeration.  Whether other
  numeration systems (Ostrowski representations, balanced
  ternary, etc.) support analogous protocol and certificate
  structures is an open question.

\item The present note does not address the complexity of
  fold normalization or Prony--Hankel reconstruction.
  Polynomial-time bounds are established
  in~\cite{OmegaSPG2026}; we defer to that reference for
  details.
\end{enumerate}

% ── 6.4  Future directions ───────────────────────────────
\subsection{Future directions}

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*)]
\item \textbf{Continuous extension.}
  Can the protocol layer be extended from $\NN$-arithmetic to
  continuous function spaces, providing a protocol-universal
  system that directly subsumes EML?

\item \textbf{Proof-carrying symbolic regression.}
  Combining L0 search (EML grammar) with L2 certificates
  (Hankel fingerprints as convergence witnesses) could yield
  symbolic regression algorithms that not only find formulas
  but certify their structural identity.

\item \textbf{Higher levels.}
  The hierarchy L0--L1--L2 may extend further.
  A natural L3 candidate is \emph{capacity universality}:
  the Hankel kernel should satisfy quantitative capacity
  bounds (Chebotarev-type density laws) that constrain not
  just the spectrum but its asymptotic distribution across
  resolutions.  This direction is developed
  in~\cite{OmegaSPG2026}.
\end{enumerate}
