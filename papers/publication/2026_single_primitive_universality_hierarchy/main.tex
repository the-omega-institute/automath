\begin{abstract}
We distinguish three kinds of data that are often conflated in
single-primitive representation statements.  Expression
universality asks only for terms over one binary operation;
Odrzywo{\l}ek's operation \(e^x-\operatorname{Log}y\) supplies such
terms for a standard elementary library.  Protocol universality
adds computable normal forms and readout maps.  Using Zeckendorf
normal forms and the classical identities for Church iteration, we
record a protocol for \(+\) and \(\times\) on \(\NN\), and prove
that multiplication cannot be read on a single free monogenic orbit
by the ambient product, even after injective recoding.  Finite
fibers automatically give Hankel-positive collision moments and
finite Prony reconstruction; the finite-state transfer property is
an additional requirement.  This requirement is not forced by the
finite-fiber data: computable finite-fiber covers can preserve the
same normal forms and readouts while having second moments that
grow faster than any exponential.  Richardson's theorem separately
rules out computable canonical forms for the transported real
semantics of the Richardson fragment represented by EML terms.
\end{abstract}
