\begin{abstract}
We separate three kinds of structure that are easily conflated in
single-primitive representation results.  Expression universality
requires only terms over one binary operation; Odrzywo{\l}ek's
operation \(e^x-\operatorname{Log}y\) gives such terms for a
standard elementary library.  Protocol universality adds computable
normal forms and readout maps.  With Zeckendorf normal forms and the
classical Church-iteration identities we obtain a protocol for
\(+\) and \(\times\) on \(\NN\), and we show that multiplication
cannot be read on one free monogenic orbit by the ambient product,
even after an injective recoding.  Finite fibers give
Hankel-positive collision moments and finite Prony reconstruction,
but they do not force a finite-state transfer law: computable
finite-fiber covers may preserve the normal forms and readouts while
having second moments of superexponential growth.  Richardson's
theorem gives the corresponding obstruction to computable canonical
forms for the EML translation of a real elementary fragment.
\end{abstract}
