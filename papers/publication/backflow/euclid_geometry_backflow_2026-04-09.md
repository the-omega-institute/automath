## Main Lessons from Euclid

The strongest backflow points are these:

- Primitive objects are not enough; admissible operations must be stated as part of the theory.
- Construction theorems and rigidity theorems should not be presented as the same kind of result.
- Global coherence claims need explicit threshold assumptions; they cannot ride on local pictures.
- Limit/control arguments read better when presented as finite-stage disciplined approximation rather than as a black-box continuum jump.
- Obstruction classes should be classified, not treated as annoying leftovers.
