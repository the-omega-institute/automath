\begin{abstract}
Assuming a Chebotarev-type spectral expansion for primitive counts and the corresponding Artin factorization, we analyze the passage from a finite label group to a quotient. For quotients of finite abelian groups we prove an exact chain rule for relative entropy with respect to the uniform measure and an orthogonal decomposition of the associated \(L^2\)-defect into Fourier modes that factor through the quotient and those that do not. Under a spectrally isolated dominant nontrivial channel we derive a second-order asymptotic for subset counts, identify the precise pole order contributed by the dominant Artin factor, and deduce exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to the uniform law. When \(\lambda_1(u)>1\), the same argument yields a sharp obstruction to square-root total-variation decay once a detected dominant modulus exceeds \(\lambda_1(u)^{1/2}\). We then consider the fold-groupoid algebra attached to a fold map. In that setting we prove rigidity of the scan-reversal fingerprint under the compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms considered here, realize the induced \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-decomposition by explicit central idempotents, and show that signed Watatani-index moments recover the corresponding sectorwise spectral data. In window \(6\) this leads to an explicit reconstruction theorem and to a quantitative lower bound for the dimension of the central quotient not detected by the basic central observables.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}
The Chebotarev density theorem and its refinements govern the distribution of arithmetic labels attached to primitive objects \cite{Tschebotareff1926,NeukirchSchmidtWingberg2008,IwaniecKowalski2004,Serre1981}. In many applications, however, only a quotient of the full label group is observed, or only a restricted family of subset statistics is accessible. Once a Chebotarev-type expansion is available, one is therefore led to a structural question: which quantitative features of the fine distribution remain visible after passage to a quotient, and how is the first secondary term reflected in the coarser statistics?

The first part of the paper answers this question in an abstract setting. We do not derive a new Chebotarev theorem; instead, we start from an assumed spectral expansion for primitive counts together with the corresponding Artin factorization and extract several consequences in a unified form. The relative-entropy chain rule itself is classical \cite{CoverThomas2006,CsiszarKorner2011,DemboZeitouni1998}, as is finite Fourier analysis on finite abelian groups \cite{Diaconis1988,Terras1999}. The contribution here is to combine these classical ingredients with the Chebotarev input so that the quotient-visible and quotient-invisible parts of the primitive distribution are identified exactly and in a form directly compatible with the spectral data.

More precisely, the arithmetic contribution has three components. First, for a quotient \(\pi:G_2\twoheadrightarrow G_1\) of finite abelian groups, Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-quotient-relative-entropy-chain} gives an exact decomposition of the relative entropy of the fine distribution into the entropy of the quotient distribution and the average entropy remaining along the fibers, while Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-visible-hidden-character-energy} identifies the corresponding \(L^2\)-loss with the Fourier mass carried by characters that do not factor through \(\pi\). Second, when a nontrivial spectral channel is isolated, Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-second-main-term-witness} extracts the dominant oscillatory term in subset counts and Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-artin-dominant-channel-pole-order} translates the same spectral isolation into an exact pole-order statement for the associated Artin factor. Third, Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-statistical-convergence} converts the spectral gap into exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to the uniform measure and proves that, when \(\lambda_1(u)>1\), square-root total-variation decay is impossible once a detected dominant modulus exceeds \(\lambda_1(u)^{1/2}\).

The second part of the paper concerns the fold-groupoid algebra attached to a fold map. The operator-algebraic ingredients are standard finite-dimensional consequences of equivalence-relation groupoids and conditional expectations \cite{Renault1980,Paterson1999,Watatani1990,BrownOzawa2008,Jones1983}; the point here is again the synthesis. We isolate the scan-reversal fingerprint as the relevant coarse observable on simple blocks, prove its rigidity under the compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms considered in this paper, realize the induced \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-sector decomposition by explicit central idempotents, and show that signed Watatani-index moments recover the sectorwise multiplicity data. In window \(6\) this yields an explicit reconstruction theorem together with a quantitative lower bound for the dimension of the undetected central quotient.

The paper is organized as follows. Section \ref{sec:entropy-chain} records the arithmetic setup and proves the exact quotient identities. Section \ref{sec:artin-pole} isolates the dominant channel and derives the analytic and probabilistic consequences. Section \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} introduces the fold-groupoid algebra and proves rigidity of the fingerprint. Section \ref{sec:central-idempotents} studies the central observables, the associated moment problem, and the window-\(6\) reconstruction results.

\section{Chebotarev quotients}\label{sec:entropy-chain}
We begin by fixing the notation used throughout this and the next section. The arguments below are formal consequences of the abstract Chebotarev input recorded here.

\subsection{Finite groups, characters, and divergences}
If \(G\) is a finite group, we write \(\widehat G\) for a complete set of irreducible complex representations of \(G\); when \(G\) is abelian, this is identified with its Pontryagin dual of characters. We write \(\mathrm{unif}_G\) for the uniform probability measure on \(G\). For a surjective homomorphism \(\pi:G_2\twoheadrightarrow G_1\) with kernel \(K\), we set
\[
K^\perp:=\{\chi\in\widehat G_2:\chi|_K\equiv 1\}\cong \widehat G_1.
\]
When \(G\) is abelian and \(f:G\to \CC\), its Fourier transform is
\[
\widehat f(\chi):=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G} f(c)\,\overline{\chi(c)}.
\]
With respect to the normalized inner product
\[
\langle f,g\rangle_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}:=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G}f(c)\,\overline{g(c)},
\]
Parseval's identity takes the form
\[
\|f\|_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}^2=\sum_{\chi\in \widehat G}\abs{\widehat f(\chi)}^2.
\]

For probability measures \(\mu,\nu\) on a finite set \(X\) with \(\mu\ll\nu\), we use
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\mu(x)\log\frac{\mu(x)}{\nu(x)},
\]
\[
\chi^2(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\frac{(\mu(x)-\nu(x))^2}{\nu(x)},
\qquad
\|\mu-\nu\|_{\mathrm{TV}}:=\frac12\sum_{x\in X}\abs{\mu(x)-\nu(x)}.
\]
When \(\nu=\mathrm{unif}_G\) on a finite group \(G\) and \(r:=|G|\mu\) denotes the density of \(\mu\) relative to \(\mathrm{unif}_G\), one has
\[
\chi^2(\mu\Vert \mathrm{unif}_G)=\|r-1\|_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}^2.
\]
When \(X\) is partitioned into fibers of a map \(\pi:X\to Y\), the relative-entropy chain rule reads
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu)=D(\pi_\ast\mu\Vert \pi_\ast\nu)+\sum_{y\in Y}(\pi_\ast\mu)(y)\,D(\mu(\cdot\mid y)\Vert \nu(\cdot\mid y)),
\]
whenever the conditional distributions are defined.

\subsection{Abstract Chebotarev input}
Fix a finite group \(G\), a temperature parameter \(u\in(0,1]\), and nonnegative primitive Chebotarev counts \(B_{n,c}(u)\) indexed by \(c\in G\). We write
\[
B_n(\ind;u):=\sum_{c\in G}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
and, when \(G\) is abelian,
\[
B_n(\chi;u):=\sum_{c\in G}\chi(c)\,B_{n,c}(u)
\quad(\chi\in \widehat G),
\]
and for each irreducible channel \(\rho\) we let \(M_{K,\rho}(u)\) denote the corresponding transfer matrix. The trivial-channel Perron root is
\[
\lambda_1(u):=\spr(M_{K,\ind}(u)),
\]
the nontrivial spectral radius is
\[
\eta(u):=\max_{\rho\neq \ind}\spr(M_{K,\rho}(u)),
\]
and we set
\[
\Lambda(u):=\max\{|\lambda_2(u)|,\eta(u)\},
\]
where \(\lambda_2(u)\) denotes the second-largest eigenvalue modulus in the trivial channel.

The results of Sections \ref{sec:entropy-chain} and \ref{sec:artin-pole} depend only on the following hypotheses.

\begin{assumption}[Chebotarev spectral expansion]\label{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp}
Assume \(\Lambda(u)<\lambda_1(u)\). Then, uniformly in \(c\in G\),
\[
B_{n,c}(u)
=
\frac{1}{|G|}\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right),
\qquad
B_n(\ind;u)
=
\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right).
\]
Moreover, for every conjugacy-invariant subset \(A\subseteq G\), if we set
\[
B_{n,A}(u):=\sum_{c\in A}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
then there exist linear functionals \(L_{A,\rho}\) on the corresponding channel spaces such that
\[
B_{n,A}(u)
=
\mathrm{unif}_G(A)\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+\frac{1}{n}\sum_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{A,\rho}\!\bigl(M_{K,\rho}(u)^n\bigr).
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Artin factorization]\label{prop:kernel-artin-factorization}
For a finite group \(G\), define
\[
L_{K,\rho}(z,u):=\det(I-zM_{K,\rho}(u))^{-1}.
\]
Then the associated zeta function satisfies
\[
\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)=\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho},
\]
and hence
\[
\frac{\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)}{L_{K,\ind}(z,u)}
=
\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho}.
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{definition}[Square-root spectral gap]\label{def:kernel-rh}
We write
\[
\textsf{GRH}_{K,G}(u):\qquad \Lambda(u)\le \lambda_1(u)^{1/2}
\]
for the square-root spectral gap condition that plays the role of a generalized Riemann-hypothesis bound in the present transfer-matrix setting.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
In Sections \ref{sec:entropy-chain} and \ref{sec:artin-pole} we use Assumptions \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp} and \ref{prop:kernel-artin-factorization} only through the statements displayed above. In particular, the arguments there require no additional arithmetic input.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}
The condition in Definition \ref{def:kernel-rh} yields a genuine square-root \emph{decay} statement only when \(\lambda_1(u)>1\). This is the regime relevant for Section \ref{sec:artin-pole}. If \(\lambda_1(u)\le 1\), the same inequalities remain formally valid but should not be interpreted as decay estimates.
\end{remark}

\section{Fold groupoids and fingerprint rigidity}\label{sec:groupoid-rigidity}
We now turn to the finite-dimensional companion problem. This section introduces the fold-groupoid algebra and the compatible automorphisms under which the scan-reversal fingerprint is rigid.

\subsection{The fold-groupoid algebra}
\begin{definition}[Fold groupoid algebra]\label{def:fold-equivalence-groupoid-algebra}
For each resolution \(m\), let \(\Fold_m:\Omega_m\to X_m\) be the fold map from microstates to stable macrostates, where \(\Omega_m\) is a binary microstate set of cardinality \(2^m\). The associated equivalence-relation groupoid is
\[
\mathcal R_m:=\Omega_m\times_{X_m}\Omega_m
=
\{(\omega,\omega')\in \Omega_m\times\Omega_m:\Fold_m(\omega)=\Fold_m(\omega')\},
\]
and we write
\[
A_{m,1}:=\CC[\mathcal R_m]
\]
for its complex groupoid algebra. The fiber multiplicity at \(x\in X_m\) is
\[
d_m(x):=\abs{\Fold_m^{-1}(x)},
\qquad
D_m:=\max_{x\in X_m}d_m(x),
\qquad
\mathcal M_m:=\{x\in X_m:d_m(x)=D_m\}.
\]
For each \(x\in X_m\), we further assume that the fold model provides distinguished permutations
\[
c_x,\iota_x\in \mathrm{Sym}\bigl(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\bigr),
\qquad
\iota_x^2=\mathrm{id},
\]
called respectively the scan permutation and the reversal permutation of the fiber. Only their signatures and their behavior under the compatible automorphisms introduced below will be used in the sequel.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Wedderburn decomposition]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-wedderburn}
There is a canonical \(\ast\)-isomorphism
\[
A_{m,1}\cong \bigoplus_{x\in X_m}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
For each \(x\in X_m\), we denote by \(\widetilde P_x\in Z(A_{m,1})\) the minimal central idempotent projecting onto the block \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\) determines a full equivalence-relation subgroupoid whose algebra is canonically \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\); see, for example, \cite[Chapter 1]{Renault1980} or \cite[Section 4.1]{Paterson1999}. Taking the direct sum over all fibers gives the stated decomposition.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[\(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-sector decomposition]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-z2x2-sector-by-orientation}
For each \(x\in X_m\), let \(c_x\) and \(\iota_x\) be the scan and reversal permutations of the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Define the fingerprint
\[
\chi(x):=\bigl(\operatorname{sgn}(c_x),\operatorname{sgn}(\iota_x)\bigr)\in\{\pm1\}^2.
\]
For \((\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2\), set
\[
X_m^{\alpha,\beta}:=\{x\in X_m:\chi(x)=(\alpha,\beta)\},
\qquad
\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}:=\bigoplus_{x\in X_m^{\alpha,\beta}}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
Then
\[
A_{m,1}=\bigoplus_{(\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2}\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}
\]
is the sector decomposition determined by the scan-reversal fingerprint.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}
Only two features of the distinguished permutations \(c_x\) and \(\iota_x\) will be used below: their signatures and their behavior under the compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms considered in this section. No additional combinatorics of the fold map enters the proofs.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Compatible automorphisms}
\begin{theorem}[Automorphisms of the fold groupoid algebra]\label{thm:fold-groupoid-aut-semidirect-pi0}
Let
\[
\Pi_0:=\{\sigma\in \mathrm{Sym}(X_m):d_m(\sigma(x))=d_m(x)\ \forall x\in X_m\}.
\]
Then the \(\ast\)-automorphism group of \(A_{m,1}\) is naturally isomorphic to
\[
\Aut^\ast(A_{m,1})
\cong
\Bigl(\prod_{x\in X_m}\mathrm{PU}(d_m(x))\Bigr)\rtimes \Pi_0.
\]
Equivalently, a \(\ast\)-automorphism acts by blockwise projective-unitary conjugation together with a permutation of blocks of equal size.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By finite-dimensional \(C^\ast\)-algebra structure theory, every \(\ast\)-automorphism of \(\bigoplus_x M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\) permutes the minimal central projections; see \cite[Section 2.1]{BrownOzawa2008}. Compatibility with matrix size forces the induced permutation of blocks to lie in \(\Pi_0\). After that permutation is fixed, the restriction to each simple summand \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\) is a \(\ast\)-automorphism of a full matrix algebra, hence inner, and is therefore represented by conjugation with a unitary unique modulo scalars. This yields the factor \(\prod_x\mathrm{PU}(d_m(x))\), while \(\Pi_0\) acts by reindexing the blocks.
\end{proof}

\section{Central observables and reconstruction}\label{sec:central-idempotents}
Having established rigidity of the fingerprint, we study the center of the fold-groupoid algebra and the information carried by the natural central observables. The arguments in this section depend only on the fingerprint partition and the fiber-multiplicity data recorded below; no further combinatorial properties of the fold map are used.

\subsection{Auxiliary input}
\begin{assumption}[Maximal-fiber fingerprint]\label{thm:pom-maxfiber-orientation-charge-periodic}
For every resolution \(m\), there exists a fingerprint
\[
\chi_m=(\alpha_m,\beta_m)\in\{\pm1\}^2
\]
such that
\[
\chi(x)=\chi_m
\qquad\text{for all }x\in \mathcal M_m.
\]
In the odd-\(m\) stable periodic regime, one furthermore has \(\beta_m=+1\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{proposition}[Entropy loss under fiber averaging]\label{prop:fold-condexp-index-maxfiber}
Let \(E_m\) be the conditional expectation induced by \(\Fold_m\), that is, averaging uniformly on the fibers of \(\Fold_m\). For a probability distribution \(\rho\) on \(\Omega_m\), write \(\rho_X:=(\Fold_m)_\ast\rho\) and define the fiberwise-uniform distribution
\[
\rho^\flat(\omega):=\frac{\rho_X(\Fold_m(\omega))}{d_m(\Fold_m(\omega))}
\qquad(\omega\in \Omega_m).
\]
Equivalently, \(\rho^\flat\) has the same pushforward to \(X_m\) as \(\rho\) and is uniform on each fiber of \(\Fold_m\). Then
\[
\sup_{\rho}D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m,
\]
where \(\rho\) ranges over probability distributions on \(\Omega_m\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For each \(x\in X_m\), let \(u_x\) denote the uniform distribution on the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Since \(\rho^\flat\) has the same macroscopic marginal as \(\rho\) and fiberwise conditionals \(u_x\), the relative-entropy chain rule gives
\[
D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}\rho_X(x)\,
D\!\bigl(\rho(\cdot\mid x)\Vert u_x\bigr).
\]
For each \(x\), the divergence to the uniform measure on a fiber of size \(d_m(x)\) is at most \(\log d_m(x)\le \log D_m\). Hence \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)\le \log D_m\) for every \(\rho\). Conversely, if \(x_\star\in X_m\) satisfies \(d_m(x_\star)=D_m\), choose \(\rho\) to be a Dirac mass on a point of \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x_\star)\). Then \(\rho^\flat\) is the uniform distribution on that same fiber, and therefore \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Block-depolarizing expectation and Watatani index]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-block-expectation}
Under the Wedderburn identification
\[
A_{m,1}\cong \bigoplus_{x\in X_m}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC),
\]
define
\[
E_{m,1}\bigl((A_x)_{x\in X_m}\bigr)
:=
\bigoplus_{x\in X_m}\frac{\Tr(A_x)}{d_m(x)}\,I_{d_m(x)}.
\]
Then \(E_{m,1}:A_{m,1}\to Z(A_{m,1})\) is a faithful conditional expectation. In the sense of \cite{Watatani1990}, its Watatani index element is
\[
\mathrm{IndW}(E_{m,1})=\sum_{x\in X_m}d_m(x)\,\widetilde P_x.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For each block \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\), fix matrix units \(\{e_{ij}^{(x)}\}_{1\le i,j\le d_m(x)}\). The displayed formula is the normalized trace on each simple summand, so it defines a faithful conditional expectation onto the direct sum of scalar matrices, which is exactly \(Z(A_{m,1})\).

Moreover,
\[
E_{m,1}(e_{ij}^{(x)})=\delta_{ij}\,d_m(x)^{-1}\widetilde P_x.
\]
A direct computation shows that
\[
\bigl\{(e_{ij}^{(x)},e_{ji}^{(x)}): x\in X_m,\ 1\le i,j\le d_m(x)\bigr\}
\]
is a quasi-basis for \(E_{m,1}\). Hence \(E_{m,1}\) has finite Watatani index and
\[
\mathrm{IndW}(E_{m,1})
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}\sum_{i,j=1}^{d_m(x)}e_{ij}^{(x)}e_{ji}^{(x)}
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}d_m(x)\,\widetilde P_x.
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Window-\texorpdfstring{$6$}{6} data]\label{cor:fold-bin-m6-fiber-hist}
For the window-\(6\) results, we assume the following fiber-multiplicity data:

\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=3\}&=4,\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=4\}&=9.
\end{aligned}
\]
and therefore

\abs{X_6}=21.
\]
\end{assumption}
