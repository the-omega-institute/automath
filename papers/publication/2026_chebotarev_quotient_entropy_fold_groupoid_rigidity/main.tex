\begin{abstract}
Assume a Chebotarev-type spectral expansion for primitive counts together with an Artin factorization of the associated zeta function. For quotients of finite abelian label groups we prove an exact chain rule for relative entropy with respect to the uniform measure, as well as an orthogonal decomposition of the corresponding \(L^2\)-defect into quotient-visible and quotient-hidden Fourier modes. Under a simple dominant-channel hypothesis we then obtain a second-term formula for subset counts, determine the order of the first nontrivial Artin pole, and deduce exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to the uniform measure. When \(\lambda_1(u)>1\), we also show that square-root decay need not persist once the dominant nontrivial modulus exceeds the square-root threshold. For the fold-groupoid algebra attached to a fold map, we prove rigidity of the scan-reversal fingerprint under compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms, construct the resulting \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-central idempotents, and recover sectorwise spectral information from signed Watatani-index moments. In window \(6\) this yields an explicit reconstruction theorem and a bound for the part of the center not detected by the basic central observables.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}
The Chebotarev density theorem governs the distribution of Frobenius classes in finite Galois extensions and lies behind a large class of refined counting questions in number theory \cite{Tschebotareff1926,NeukirchSchmidtWingberg2008,IwaniecKowalski2004,Serre1981}. In many situations, however, one does not observe the full labeling group. One passes instead to a quotient, keeps only a prescribed family of channels, or records some other coarse statistic. Once a Chebotarev-type spectral expansion is available, it is then natural to ask which part of the information survives exactly under this passage and how the first secondary term is reflected in the resulting coarse distribution.

The first purpose of the present paper is to isolate several consequences of this kind from a common abstract hypothesis package. We do not prove a new Chebotarev theorem. Rather, starting from the spectral expansion and the Artin factorization stated in Section \ref{sec:preliminaries}, we derive exact entropy identities, Fourier-energy decompositions, second-term asymptotics, information on the first nontrivial Artin pole, and convergence estimates for primitive label distributions.

The second part of the paper is finite-dimensional and concerns the fold-groupoid algebra attached to a fold map. It should be viewed as a companion model for the same coarse-graining principle. There the relevant observable is the scan-reversal fingerprint on the simple blocks, and the basic question is which central data can be recovered from natural operators attached to that fingerprint.

\paragraph{Arithmetic results.}
Let \(\pi:G_2\twoheadrightarrow G_1\) be a quotient of finite abelian groups. Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-quotient-relative-entropy-chain} gives an exact decomposition of the relative entropy of the fine distribution into a quotient term and an averaged fiber term. Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-visible-hidden-character-energy} provides the corresponding \(L^2\)-identity and shows that the loss under passage to the quotient is measured precisely by the Fourier mass carried by characters that do not factor through \(\pi\). The entropy identity itself is classical \cite{CoverThomas2006,CsiszarKorner2011,DemboZeitouni1998}; the point here is that, in the present Chebotarev setting, both pieces admit a direct interpretation in terms of primitive label counts and their finite Fourier transforms \cite{Diaconis1988,Terras1999}.

We then turn to the contribution of a dominant nontrivial channel. Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-second-main-term-witness} yields a second-term formula for subset counts. Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-artin-dominant-channel-pole-order} determines the order of the first nontrivial Artin pole, and Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-statistical-convergence} gives exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to the uniform measure. Under the additional condition \(\lambda_1(u)>1\), the same theorem also shows that square-root decay in total variation may fail once the dominant nontrivial modulus exceeds \(\lambda_1(u)^{1/2}\).

\paragraph{Fold-groupoid results.}
For the fold-groupoid algebra, Theorem \ref{thm:fold-groupoid-chi-rigidity} proves that the fingerprint \(\chi\in\{\pm1\}^2\) is preserved by the compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms considered here, and Proposition \ref{prop:fold-groupoid-aut-chi-semidirect-classification} describes the resulting automorphism group. Theorem \ref{thm:fold-groupoid-z2x2-central-idempotents} realizes the \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-sector decomposition by central idempotents, while Theorem \ref{thm:fold-groupoid-z2x2-joint-spectral-measure} and the subsequent results recover sectorwise information from signed Watatani-index moments \cite{Renault1980,Paterson1999,Watatani1990,BrownOzawa2008,Jones1983}. In window \(6\), Theorem \ref{thm:fold-window6-chi-layered-spectrum-reconstruction-by-12-moments} gives an explicit reconstruction theorem, and Theorem \ref{thm:fold-window6-center-three-observables-dimension-defect} bounds the part of the center that is not detected by the basic central observables.

\paragraph{Organization.}
Section \ref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation and records the standing hypotheses. Section \ref{sec:entropy-chain} treats the entropy and \(L^2\)-decompositions attached to quotient maps. Section \ref{sec:artin-pole} studies dominant channels, the first secondary term, Artin poles, and convergence to the uniform measure. Sections \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} and \ref{sec:central-idempotents} contain the fold-groupoid results.

\section{Notation and hypotheses}\label{sec:preliminaries}
\subsection{Finite groups, characters, and divergences}
If \(G\) is a finite group, we write \(\widehat G\) for a complete set of irreducible complex representations of \(G\); when \(G\) is abelian, this is identified with its Pontryagin dual of characters. We write \(\mathrm{unif}_G\) for the uniform probability measure on \(G\). For a surjective homomorphism \(\pi:G_2\twoheadrightarrow G_1\) with kernel \(K\), we set
\[
K^\perp:=\{\chi\in\widehat G_2:\chi|_K\equiv 1\}\cong \widehat G_1.
\]
When \(G\) is abelian and \(f:G\to \CC\), its Fourier transform is
\[
\widehat f(\chi):=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G} f(c)\,\overline{\chi(c)}.
\]
With respect to the normalized inner product
\[
\langle f,g\rangle_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}:=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G}f(c)\,\overline{g(c)},
\]
Parseval's identity takes the form
\[
\|f\|_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}^2=\sum_{\chi\in \widehat G}\abs{\widehat f(\chi)}^2.
\]

For probability measures \(\mu,\nu\) on a finite set \(X\) with \(\mu\ll\nu\), we use
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\mu(x)\log\frac{\mu(x)}{\nu(x)},
\]
\[
\chi^2(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\frac{(\mu(x)-\nu(x))^2}{\nu(x)},
\qquad
\|\mu-\nu\|_{\mathrm{TV}}:=\frac12\sum_{x\in X}\abs{\mu(x)-\nu(x)}.
\]
When \(X\) is partitioned into fibers of a map \(\pi:X\to Y\), the relative-entropy chain rule reads
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu)=D(\pi_\ast\mu\Vert \pi_\ast\nu)+\sum_{y\in Y}(\pi_\ast\mu)(y)\,D(\mu(\cdot\mid y)\Vert \nu(\cdot\mid y)),
\]
whenever the conditional distributions are defined.

\subsection{Chebotarev input}
Fix a finite group \(G\), a temperature parameter \(u\in(0,1]\), and primitive Chebotarev counts \(B_{n,c}(u)\) indexed by \(c\in G\). We write
\[
B_n(\ind;u):=\sum_{c\in G}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
and, when \(G\) is abelian,
\[
B_n(\chi;u):=\sum_{c\in G}\chi(c)\,B_{n,c}(u)
\quad(\chi\in \widehat G),
\]
and for each irreducible channel \(\rho\) we let \(M_{K,\rho}(u)\) denote the corresponding transfer matrix. The trivial-channel Perron root is
\[
\lambda_1(u):=\spr(M_{K,\ind}(u)),
\]
the nontrivial spectral radius is
\[
\eta(u):=\max_{\rho\neq \ind}\spr(M_{K,\rho}(u)),
\]
and we set
\[
\Lambda(u):=\max\{|\lambda_2(u)|,\eta(u)\},
\]
where \(\lambda_2(u)\) denotes the second-largest eigenvalue modulus in the trivial channel.

The arithmetic results of Sections \ref{sec:entropy-chain} and \ref{sec:artin-pole} depend only on the following abstract input.

\begin{assumption}[Spectral expansion for primitive counts]\label{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp}
Assume \(\Lambda(u)<\lambda_1(u)\). Then, uniformly in \(c\in G\),
\[
B_{n,c}(u)
=
\frac{1}{|G|}\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right),
\qquad
B_n(\ind;u)
=
\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right).
\]
Moreover, for every conjugacy-invariant subset \(A\subseteq G\), if we set
\[
B_{n,A}(u):=\sum_{c\in A}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
then there exist linear functionals \(L_{A,\rho}\) on the corresponding channel spaces such that
\[
B_{n,A}(u)
=
\mathrm{unif}_G(A)\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+\frac{1}{n}\sum_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{A,\rho}\!\bigl(M_{K,\rho}(u)^n\bigr).
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Artin factorization]\label{prop:kernel-artin-factorization}
For a finite group \(G\), define
\[
L_{K,\rho}(z,u):=\det(I-zM_{K,\rho}(u))^{-1}.
\]
Then the associated zeta function satisfies
\[
\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)=\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho},
\]
and hence
\[
\frac{\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)}{L_{K,\ind}(z,u)}
=
\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho}.
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{definition}[Square-root spectral gap]\label{def:kernel-rh}
We write
\[
\textsf{GRH}_{K,G}(u):\qquad \Lambda(u)\le \lambda_1(u)^{1/2}
\]
for the square-root spectral gap condition that plays the role of a generalized Riemann-hypothesis bound in the present transfer-matrix setting.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
In Sections \ref{sec:entropy-chain} and \ref{sec:artin-pole}, Assumptions \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp} and \ref{prop:kernel-artin-factorization} are used only through the statements recorded above. Thus the results there are formal consequences of the asymptotic expansion, the channel decomposition of subset counts, and the factorization identity.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}
The condition in Definition \ref{def:kernel-rh} yields a genuine square-root \emph{decay} statement only when \(\lambda_1(u)>1\). This is the regime relevant for Section \ref{sec:artin-pole}. If \(\lambda_1(u)\le 1\), the same inequalities remain formally valid but should not be interpreted as decay estimates.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Fold-groupoid input}
We now record the finite-dimensional framework used in Sections \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} and \ref{sec:central-idempotents}.

\begin{definition}[Fold groupoid algebra]\label{def:fold-equivalence-groupoid-algebra}
For each resolution \(m\), let \(\Fold_m:\Omega_m\to X_m\) be the fold map from microstates to stable macrostates, where \(\Omega_m\) is a binary microstate set of cardinality \(2^m\). The associated equivalence-relation groupoid is
\[
\mathcal R_m:=\Omega_m\times_{X_m}\Omega_m
=
\{(\omega,\omega')\in \Omega_m\times\Omega_m:\Fold_m(\omega)=\Fold_m(\omega')\},
\]
and we write
\[
A_{m,1}:=\CC[\mathcal R_m]
\]
for its complex groupoid algebra. The fiber multiplicity at \(x\in X_m\) is
\[
d_m(x):=\abs{\Fold_m^{-1}(x)},
\qquad
D_m:=\max_{x\in X_m}d_m(x),
\qquad
\mathcal M_m:=\{x\in X_m:d_m(x)=D_m\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Wedderburn decomposition]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-wedderburn}
There is a canonical \(\ast\)-isomorphism
\[
A_{m,1}\cong \bigoplus_{x\in X_m}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
For each \(x\in X_m\), we denote by \(\widetilde P_x\in Z(A_{m,1})\) the minimal central idempotent projecting onto the block \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\) determines a full equivalence-relation subgroupoid whose algebra is canonically \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\); see, for example, \cite[Chapter 1]{Renault1980} or \cite[Section 4.1]{Paterson1999}. Summing over all fibers gives the direct-sum decomposition.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[\(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-sector decomposition]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-z2x2-sector-by-orientation}
For each \(x\in X_m\), let \(c_x\) and \(\iota_x\) be the scan and reversal permutations of the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Define the fingerprint
\[
\chi(x):=\bigl(\operatorname{sgn}(c_x),\operatorname{sgn}(\iota_x)\bigr)\in\{\pm1\}^2.
\]
For \((\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2\), set
\[
X_m^{\alpha,\beta}:=\{x\in X_m:\chi(x)=(\alpha,\beta)\},
\qquad
\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}:=\bigoplus_{x\in X_m^{\alpha,\beta}}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
Then
\[
A_{m,1}=\bigoplus_{(\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2}\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}
\]
is the sector decomposition determined by the scan-reversal fingerprint.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}
Only two features of the distinguished fiber permutations \(c_x\) and \(\iota_x\) are used below: their signatures and their behavior under the compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms introduced in Section \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity}. No further combinatorial property of the fold map enters the proofs.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Automorphisms of the fold groupoid algebra]\label{thm:fold-groupoid-aut-semidirect-pi0}
Let
\[
\Pi_0:=\{\sigma\in \mathrm{Sym}(X_m):d_m(\sigma(x))=d_m(x)\ \forall x\in X_m\}.
\]
Then the \(\ast\)-automorphism group of \(A_{m,1}\) is naturally isomorphic to
\[
\Aut^\ast(A_{m,1})
\cong
\Bigl(\prod_{x\in X_m}\mathrm{PU}(d_m(x))\Bigr)\rtimes \Pi_0.
\]
Equivalently, a \(\ast\)-automorphism acts by blockwise projective-unitary conjugation together with a permutation of blocks of equal size.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By finite-dimensional \(C^\ast\)-algebra structure theory, every \(\ast\)-automorphism of \(\bigoplus_x M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\) permutes the minimal central projections and is inner on each simple summand; see \cite[Section 2.1]{BrownOzawa2008}. Compatibility with matrix size forces the induced permutation of blocks to lie in \(\Pi_0\), and the inner part is recorded blockwise in the projective unitary groups.
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Maximal-fiber fingerprint]\label{thm:pom-maxfiber-orientation-charge-periodic}
For every resolution \(m\), there exists a fingerprint
\[
\chi_m=(\alpha_m,\beta_m)\in\{\pm1\}^2
\]
such that
\[
\chi(x)=\chi_m
\qquad\text{for all }x\in \mathcal M_m.
\]
In the odd-\(m\) stable periodic regime, one furthermore has \(\beta_m=+1\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{proposition}[Entropy loss under fiber averaging]\label{prop:fold-condexp-index-maxfiber}
Let \(E_m\) be the conditional expectation induced by \(\Fold_m\), that is, averaging uniformly on the fibers of \(\Fold_m\). For a probability distribution \(\rho\) on \(\Omega_m\), write \(\rho_X:=(\Fold_m)_\ast\rho\) and define the fiberwise-uniform distribution
\[
\rho^\flat(\omega):=\frac{\rho_X(\Fold_m(\omega))}{d_m(\Fold_m(\omega))}
\qquad(\omega\in \Omega_m).
\]
Equivalently, \(\rho^\flat\) has the same pushforward to \(X_m\) as \(\rho\) and is uniform on each fiber of \(\Fold_m\). Then
\[
\sup_{\rho}D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m,
\]
where \(\rho\) ranges over probability distributions on \(\Omega_m\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For each \(x\in X_m\), let \(u_x\) denote the uniform distribution on the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Since \(\rho^\flat\) has the same macroscopic marginal as \(\rho\) and fiberwise conditionals \(u_x\), the relative-entropy chain rule gives
\[
D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}\rho_X(x)\,
D\!\bigl(\rho(\cdot\mid x)\Vert u_x\bigr).
\]
For each \(x\), the divergence to the uniform measure on a fiber of size \(d_m(x)\) is at most \(\log d_m(x)\le \log D_m\). Hence \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)\le \log D_m\) for every \(\rho\). Conversely, if \(x_\star\in X_m\) satisfies \(d_m(x_\star)=D_m\), choose \(\rho\) to be a Dirac mass on a point of \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x_\star)\). Then \(\rho^\flat\) is the uniform distribution on that same fiber, and therefore \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Block-depolarizing expectation and Watatani index]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-block-expectation}
Under the Wedderburn identification
\[
A_{m,1}\cong \bigoplus_{x\in X_m}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC),
\]
define
\[
E_{m,1}\bigl((A_x)_{x\in X_m}\bigr)
:=
\bigoplus_{x\in X_m}\frac{\Tr(A_x)}{d_m(x)}\,I_{d_m(x)}.
\]
Then \(E_{m,1}:A_{m,1}\to Z(A_{m,1})\) is a faithful conditional expectation. In the sense of \cite{Watatani1990}, its Watatani index element is
\[
\mathrm{IndW}(E_{m,1})=\sum_{x\in X_m}d_m(x)\,\widetilde P_x.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For each block \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\), fix matrix units \(\{e_{ij}^{(x)}\}_{1\le i,j\le d_m(x)}\). The formula for \(E_{m,1}\) is the normalized trace on each simple summand, hence defines a faithful conditional expectation onto the direct sum of scalar matrices, which is exactly \(Z(A_{m,1})\).

Moreover,
\[
E_{m,1}(e_{ij}^{(x)})=\delta_{ij}\,d_m(x)^{-1}\widetilde P_x.
\]
A direct computation shows that the family
\[
\bigl\{(e_{ij}^{(x)},e_{ji}^{(x)}): x\in X_m,\ 1\le i,j\le d_m(x)\bigr\}
\]
is a quasi-basis for \(E_{m,1}\). Therefore \(E_{m,1}\) has finite Watatani index and
\[
\mathrm{IndW}(E_{m,1})
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}\sum_{i,j=1}^{d_m(x)}e_{ij}^{(x)}e_{ji}^{(x)}
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}d_m(x)\,\widetilde P_x.
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Window-\texorpdfstring{$6$}{6} enumerative input]\label{cor:fold-bin-m6-fiber-hist}
For the window-\(6\) results, we assume the following fiber-multiplicity data:
\[

d_6(x)&\in\{2,3,4\},\\

\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=3\}&=4,\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=4\}&=9.
\end{aligned}
\]
and therefore
\[
\abs{X_6}=21.
\]
\end{assumption}
