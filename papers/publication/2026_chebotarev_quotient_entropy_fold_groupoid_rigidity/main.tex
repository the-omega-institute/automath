\begin{abstract}
We study consequences of a Chebotarev-type spectral expansion and Artin factorization through the joint lenses of entropy, Fourier analysis, and finite quotient maps. For surjective homomorphisms of finite abelian label groups, we prove that the relative entropy with respect to Haar measure decomposes exactly into a quotient contribution and an averaged fiber contribution, and we identify the corresponding \(L^2\)-defect as the sum of visible and hidden character energies. Under a dominant-channel hypothesis, we then relate nontrivial Artin factors to exact pole orders and explicit second main terms, obtaining exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to uniformity together with witness sets that obstruct square-root decay when the spectral gap is too small. On the fold-groupoid side, we prove \(\chi\)-rigidity for automorphisms, construct the \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\) central idempotents explicitly, and derive reconstruction and resolution results for the resulting central spectral data.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}
The Chebotarev density theorem describes the asymptotic distribution of Frobenius classes in finite Galois extensions and underlies many refined counting problems in analytic number theory \cite{Tschebotareff1926,NeukirchSchmidtWingberg2008,IwaniecKowalski2004,Serre1981}. In such problems one often observes only a quotient label, a restricted family of channels, or a coarse statistic extracted from the full arithmetic datum. The first aim of this paper is to isolate, in a precise quantitative form, what information survives under that passage to a quotient.

The Chebotarev part of the paper is conditional on the transfer-matrix input recorded in Section \ref{sec:preliminaries}. Once those hypotheses are available, the arguments are self-contained and exact. We prove that for a surjective homomorphism of finite abelian label groups the relative entropy with respect to the uniform measure satisfies an exact chain rule, and that the corresponding \(L^2\)-defect splits orthogonally into visible Fourier modes, which factor through the quotient, and hidden modes, which are nontrivial on the kernel. The point is not another derivation of Chebotarev density itself, but rather a precise information-theoretic description of quotient loss in terms of character energy \cite{CoverThomas2006,CsiszarKorner2011,DemboZeitouni1998,Diaconis1988,Terras1999}.

The same abstract spectral input yields analytic consequences. Under a simple dominant-channel hypothesis we extract an explicit second main term for subset counts, identify the exact pole order contributed by the dominant nontrivial Artin factor, and deduce exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to the uniform distribution. The witness construction shows that the square-root threshold is sharp within this framework: once the dominant nontrivial modulus exceeds \(\lambda_1(u)^{1/2}\), one obtains subsets whose fluctuations preclude square-root decay in total variation.

The fold-groupoid part serves as a finite-dimensional algebraic companion to the same coarse-graining theme. Here the relevant observable is the scan-reversal fingerprint \(\chi\in \{\pm1\}^2\) attached to each simple block of the fold groupoid algebra. We prove that this fingerprint is rigid under scan-reversal compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms, realize the four sector projectors internally as central idempotents, and show how signed Watatani-index moments recover sectorwise spectral data \cite{Renault1980,Paterson1999,Watatani1990,BrownOzawa2008,Jones1983}. In particular, the window-\(6\) calculation gives a concrete finite model in which the range and limitations of a small family of central observables can be described exactly.

The paper is organized as follows. Section \ref{sec:preliminaries} records notation and the standing inputs used later. Section \ref{sec:entropy-chain} proves the entropy and Fourier decompositions under quotient maps. Section \ref{sec:artin-pole} derives the second main term, pole-order statement, and statistical consequences of the dominant spectral channel. Sections \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} and \ref{sec:central-idempotents} treat the rigidity and central structure of the fold-groupoid algebra. Section \ref{sec:discussion} concludes with a concise summary of the two coarse-graining mechanisms studied here.

\section{Preliminaries}\label{sec:preliminaries}
\subsection{Finite groups, characters, and divergences}
If \(G\) is a finite group, we write \(\widehat G\) for a complete set of irreducible complex representations of \(G\); when \(G\) is abelian, this is identified with its Pontryagin dual of characters. We write \(\mathrm{unif}_G\) for the uniform probability measure on \(G\). For a surjective homomorphism \(\pi:G_2\twoheadrightarrow G_1\) with kernel \(K\), we set
\[
K^\perp:=\{\chi\in\widehat G_2:\chi|_K\equiv 1\}\cong \widehat G_1.
\]
When \(G\) is abelian and \(f:G\to \CC\), its Fourier transform is
\[
\widehat f(\chi):=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G} f(c)\,\overline{\chi(c)}.
\]
With respect to the normalized inner product
\[
\langle f,g\rangle_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}:=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G}f(c)\,\overline{g(c)},
\]
Parseval's identity takes the form
\[
\|f\|_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}^2=\sum_{\chi\in \widehat G}\abs{\widehat f(\chi)}^2.
\]

For probability measures \(\mu,\nu\) on a finite set \(X\) with \(\mu\ll\nu\), we use
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\mu(x)\log\frac{\mu(x)}{\nu(x)},
\]
\[
\chi^2(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\frac{(\mu(x)-\nu(x))^2}{\nu(x)},
\qquad
\|\mu-\nu\|_{\mathrm{TV}}:=\frac12\sum_{x\in X}\abs{\mu(x)-\nu(x)}.
\]
When \(X\) is partitioned into fibers of a map \(\pi:X\to Y\), the relative-entropy chain rule reads
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu)=D(\pi_\ast\mu\Vert \pi_\ast\nu)+\sum_{y\in Y}(\pi_\ast\mu)(y)\,D(\mu(\cdot\mid y)\Vert \nu(\cdot\mid y)),
\]
whenever the conditional distributions are defined.

\subsection{Standing input for the Chebotarev sector}
Fix a finite group \(G\), a temperature parameter \(u\in(0,1]\), and primitive Chebotarev counts \(B_{n,c}(u)\) indexed by \(c\in G\). We write
\[
B_n(\ind;u):=\sum_{c\in G}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
and, when \(G\) is abelian,
\[
B_n(\chi;u):=\sum_{c\in G}\chi(c)\,B_{n,c}(u)
\quad(\chi\in \widehat G),
\]
and for each irreducible channel \(\rho\) we let \(M_{K,\rho}(u)\) denote the corresponding transfer matrix. The trivial-channel Perron root is
\[
\lambda_1(u):=\spr(M_{K,\ind}(u)),
\]
the nontrivial spectral radius is
\[
\eta(u):=\max_{\rho\neq \ind}\spr(M_{K,\rho}(u)),
\]
and we set
\[
\Lambda(u):=\max\{|\lambda_2(u)|,\eta(u)\},
\]
where \(\lambda_2(u)\) denotes the second-largest eigenvalue modulus in the trivial channel.

Sections \ref{sec:entropy-chain} and \ref{sec:artin-pole} use only the following standing input.

\begin{assumption}[Chebotarev-type spectral expansion for primitive counts]\label{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp}
Assume \(\Lambda(u)<\lambda_1(u)\). Then, uniformly in \(c\in G\),
\[
B_{n,c}(u)
=
\frac{1}{|G|}\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right),
\qquad
B_n(\ind;u)
=
\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right).
\]
Moreover, for every conjugacy-invariant subset \(A\subseteq G\), if we set
\[
B_{n,A}(u):=\sum_{c\in A}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
then there exist linear functionals \(L_{A,\rho}\) on the corresponding channel spaces such that
\[
B_{n,A}(u)
=
\mathrm{unif}_G(A)\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+\frac{1}{n}\sum_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{A,\rho}\!\bigl(M_{K,\rho}(u)^n\bigr).
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Artin factorization]\label{prop:kernel-artin-factorization}
For a finite group \(G\), define
\[
L_{K,\rho}(z,u):=\det(I-zM_{K,\rho}(u))^{-1}.
\]
Then the associated zeta function satisfies
\[
\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)=\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho},
\]
and hence
\[
\frac{\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)}{L_{K,\ind}(z,u)}
=
\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho}.
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{definition}[Square-root spectral gap]\label{def:kernel-rh}
We write
\[
\textsf{GRH}_{K,G}(u):\qquad \Lambda(u)\le \lambda_1(u)^{1/2}
\]
for the square-root spectral gap condition that plays the role of a generalized Riemann-hypothesis bound in the present transfer-matrix setting.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
Sections \ref{sec:entropy-chain} and \ref{sec:artin-pole} depend only on Assumptions \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp} and \ref{prop:kernel-artin-factorization}. In particular, the results proved below are exact consequences of the asymptotic expansion, the channel decomposition of subset counts, and the factorization statement recorded here.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Fold groupoids and central observables}
We now record the finite-dimensional groupoid-algebraic framework used in Sections \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} and \ref{sec:central-idempotents}.

\begin{definition}[Fold equivalence groupoid algebra]\label{def:fold-equivalence-groupoid-algebra}
For each resolution \(m\), let \(\Fold_m:\Omega_m\to X_m\) be the fold map from microstates to stable macrostates, where \(\Omega_m\) is a binary microstate set of cardinality \(2^m\). The associated equivalence-relation groupoid is
\[
\mathcal R_m:=\Omega_m\times_{X_m}\Omega_m
=
\{(\omega,\omega')\in \Omega_m\times\Omega_m:\Fold_m(\omega)=\Fold_m(\omega')\},
\]
and we write
\[
A_{m,1}:=\CC[\mathcal R_m]
\]
for its complex groupoid algebra. The fiber multiplicity at \(x\in X_m\) is
\[
d_m(x):=\abs{\Fold_m^{-1}(x)},
\qquad
D_m:=\max_{x\in X_m}d_m(x),
\qquad
\mathcal M_m:=\{x\in X_m:d_m(x)=D_m\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Wedderburn decomposition of the fold groupoid algebra]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-wedderburn}
There is a canonical \(\ast\)-isomorphism
\[
A_{m,1}\cong \bigoplus_{x\in X_m}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
For each \(x\in X_m\), we denote by \(\widetilde P_x\in Z(A_{m,1})\) the minimal central idempotent projecting onto the block \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\) determines a full equivalence-relation subgroupoid whose algebra is canonically \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\); summing over all fibers gives the direct-sum decomposition.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[\(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-sector decomposition by orientation]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-z2x2-sector-by-orientation}
For each \(x\in X_m\), let \(c_x\) and \(\iota_x\) be the scan and reversal permutations of the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Define the fingerprint
\[
\chi(x):=\bigl(\operatorname{sgn}(c_x),\operatorname{sgn}(\iota_x)\bigr)\in\{\pm1\}^2.
\]
For \((\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2\), set
\[
X_m^{\alpha,\beta}:=\{x\in X_m:\chi(x)=(\alpha,\beta)\},
\qquad
\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}:=\bigoplus_{x\in X_m^{\alpha,\beta}}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
Then
\[
A_{m,1}=\bigoplus_{(\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2}\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}
\]
is the sector decomposition determined by the scan-reversal fingerprint.
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Automorphisms of the fold groupoid algebra]\label{thm:fold-groupoid-aut-semidirect-pi0}
Let
\[
\Pi_0:=\{\sigma\in \mathrm{Sym}(X_m):d_m(\sigma(x))=d_m(x)\ \forall x\in X_m\}.
\]
Then the \(\ast\)-automorphism group of \(A_{m,1}\) is naturally isomorphic to
\[
\Aut^\ast(A_{m,1})
\cong
\Bigl(\prod_{x\in X_m}\mathrm{PU}(d_m(x))\Bigr)\rtimes \Pi_0.
\]
Equivalently, a \(\ast\)-automorphism acts by blockwise projective-unitary conjugation together with a permutation of blocks of equal size.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By finite-dimensional \(C^\ast\)-algebra structure theory, every \(\ast\)-automorphism of \(\bigoplus_x M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\) permutes simple summands and is inner on each summand. Compatibility with matrix size forces the permutation to lie in \(\Pi_0\).
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Maximal-fiber orientation charge]\label{thm:pom-maxfiber-orientation-charge-periodic}
For every resolution \(m\), there exists a fingerprint
\[
\chi_m=(\alpha_m,\beta_m)\in\{\pm1\}^2
\]
such that
\[
\chi(x)=\chi_m
\qquad\text{for all }x\in \mathcal M_m.
\]
In the odd-\(m\) stable periodic regime, one furthermore has \(\beta_m=+1\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{proposition}[Maximal entropy loss to fiber uniformization]\label{prop:fold-condexp-index-maxfiber}
Let \(E_m\) be the conditional expectation induced by \(\Fold_m\), that is, averaging uniformly on the fibers of \(\Fold_m\). For a probability distribution \(\rho\) on \(\Omega_m\), write \(\rho^\flat:=\rho\circ E_m\); equivalently, \(\rho^\flat\) has the same pushforward to \(X_m\) as \(\rho\) and is uniform on each fiber of \(\Fold_m\). Then
\[
\sup_{\rho}D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m,
\]
where \(\rho\) ranges over probability distributions on \(\Omega_m\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\rho_X:=(\Fold_m)_\ast\rho\), and for each \(x\in X_m\) let \(u_x\) denote the uniform distribution on the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Since \(\rho^\flat\) has the same macroscopic marginal as \(\rho\) and fiberwise conditionals \(u_x\), the relative-entropy chain rule gives
\[
D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}\rho_X(x)\,
D\!\bigl(\rho(\cdot\mid x)\Vert u_x\bigr).
\]
For each \(x\), the divergence to the uniform measure on a fiber of size \(d_m(x)\) is at most \(\log d_m(x)\le \log D_m\). Hence \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)\le \log D_m\) for every \(\rho\). Conversely, if \(x_\star\in X_m\) satisfies \(d_m(x_\star)=D_m\), choose \(\rho\) to be a Dirac mass on a point of \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x_\star)\). Then \(\rho^\flat\) is the uniform distribution on that same fiber, and therefore \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m\).
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Window-\texorpdfstring{$6$}{6} fiber histogram]\label{cor:fold-bin-m6-fiber-hist}
For \(m=6\), direct enumeration yields
\[
\begin{aligned}
d_6(x)&\in\{2,3,4\},\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=2\}&=8,\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=3\}&=4,\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=4\}&=9.
\end{aligned}
\]
and therefore
\[
\abs{X_6}=21.
\]
\end{assumption}

\end{remark}

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}
The Chebotarev part of the paper isolates an exact quotient mechanism. Once the spectral expansion and factorization input are assumed, quotienting a finite abelian label group produces no approximation at the level of information: relative entropy splits exactly into a quotient term and an averaged fiber term, while the \(L^2\)-defect splits exactly into visible and hidden character energy. The subsequent analytic consequences show that the same dominant nontrivial spectral channel simultaneously controls the second main term, the first nontrivial pole of the Artin factor, and the rate of convergence of the primitive label distribution to uniformity.

The fold-groupoid part gives a finite-dimensional companion model for the same coarse-graining principle. The scan-reversal fingerprint is rigid under the natural symmetry class considered here, the corresponding sector projections are realized internally by central idempotents, and the signed Watatani-index moments recover the sectorwise fiber spectrum. In window \(6\) the three central observables \(\mathrm{IndW}(E_{m,1})\), \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{scan}}\), and \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{rev}}\) determine the sectorwise multiplicity data but still fail to generate the entire center, so the precise extent of the residual ambiguity can be quantified. Taken together, the two parts of the paper identify exact algebraic and information-theoretic invariants that survive finite coarse-graining.
