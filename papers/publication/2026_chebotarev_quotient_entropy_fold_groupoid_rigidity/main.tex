\begin{abstract}
Assume a Chebotarev-type spectral expansion for primitive counts and an Artin factorization for the associated zeta function. For a quotient of finite abelian label groups, we prove an exact chain rule for relative entropy with respect to the uniform measure and an orthogonal decomposition of the corresponding \(L^2\)-defect into quotient-visible and kernel-hidden Fourier modes. Under a dominant nontrivial channel hypothesis, we obtain an explicit second term for subset counts, determine the order of the first nontrivial Artin pole, and deduce exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to Haar measure, together with a witness showing that square-root decay need not hold beyond the square-root spectral threshold. For the fold-groupoid algebra attached to a fold map, we prove rigidity of the scan-reversal fingerprint under compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms, construct the associated \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-central idempotents, and recover sectorwise spectral data from signed Watatani-index moments. In window \(6\) this yields an explicit reconstruction result and a quantitative bound for the part of the center not generated by the basic central observables.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}
The Chebotarev density theorem describes the distribution of Frobenius classes in finite Galois extensions and is the starting point for many refined counting problems in number theory \cite{Tschebotareff1926,NeukirchSchmidtWingberg2008,IwaniecKowalski2004,Serre1981}. In several situations one does not work with the full label group, but only with a quotient, a restricted family of channels, or a coarse observable. The first purpose of this paper is to measure exactly what is lost under such a passage to a quotient when one assumes a Chebotarev-type spectral expansion for primitive counts.

The paper has two largely independent parts. The first is analytic and information-theoretic. It does not aim at a new Chebotarev theorem; rather, it extracts consequences of the spectral input recorded in Section \ref{sec:preliminaries}. The second part is finite-dimensional and concerns the fold-groupoid algebra attached to a fold map. In both settings the emphasis is on invariants that survive coarse-graining.

\subsection*{Main results}
Let \(\pi:G_2\twoheadrightarrow G_1\) be a quotient of finite abelian groups. Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-quotient-relative-entropy-chain} proves that the relative entropy of the fine-layer distribution with respect to the uniform measure splits exactly into a quotient term and an averaged fiber term. Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-visible-hidden-character-energy} gives the corresponding \(L^2\)-identity and identifies the part lost under passage to the quotient with the Fourier energy carried by characters that are nontrivial on the kernel. This places the quotient operation in a precise information-theoretic framework; see \cite{CoverThomas2006,CsiszarKorner2011,DemboZeitouni1998,Diaconis1988,Terras1999}.

The next results concern the effect of a dominant nontrivial spectral channel. Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-second-main-term-witness} yields an explicit second term for subset counts. Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-artin-dominant-channel-pole-order} identifies the order of the first nontrivial Artin pole, and Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-statistical-convergence} gives exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to the uniform measure. The same theorem also shows that, once the dominant modulus exceeds \(\lambda_1(u)^{1/2}\), one can find witness sets for which square-root decay in total variation fails.

The second part of the paper studies the scan-reversal fingerprint \(\chi\in\{\pm1\}^2\) on the simple blocks of the fold-groupoid algebra. Theorem \ref{thm:fold-groupoid-chi-rigidity} shows that \(\chi\) is rigid under the compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms considered here, and Proposition \ref{prop:fold-groupoid-aut-chi-semidirect-classification} describes the resulting automorphism group. Theorem \ref{thm:fold-groupoid-z2x2-central-idempotents} realizes the \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-sector decomposition by central idempotents, while Theorem \ref{thm:fold-groupoid-z2x2-joint-spectral-measure} and its consequences recover sectorwise information from signed Watatani-index moments \cite{Renault1980,Paterson1999,Watatani1990,BrownOzawa2008,Jones1983}. In window \(6\), Theorem \ref{thm:fold-window6-chi-layered-spectrum-reconstruction-by-12-moments} gives an explicit reconstruction theorem, and Theorem \ref{thm:fold-window6-center-three-observables-dimension-defect} bounds the part of the center not seen by the basic central observables.

\subsection*{Organization}
Section \ref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation and states the standing hypotheses. Section \ref{sec:entropy-chain} treats the entropy and Fourier decompositions under quotient maps. Section \ref{sec:artin-pole} studies dominant channels, second terms, Artin poles, and convergence to Haar measure. Sections \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} and \ref{sec:central-idempotents} contain the fold-groupoid results. The paper ends with a short concluding section.

\section{Notation and Standing Hypotheses}\label{sec:preliminaries}
\subsection{Finite groups, characters, and divergences}
If \(G\) is a finite group, we write \(\widehat G\) for a complete set of irreducible complex representations of \(G\); when \(G\) is abelian, this is identified with its Pontryagin dual of characters. We write \(\mathrm{unif}_G\) for the uniform probability measure on \(G\). For a surjective homomorphism \(\pi:G_2\twoheadrightarrow G_1\) with kernel \(K\), we set
\[
K^\perp:=\{\chi\in\widehat G_2:\chi|_K\equiv 1\}\cong \widehat G_1.
\]
When \(G\) is abelian and \(f:G\to \CC\), its Fourier transform is
\[
\widehat f(\chi):=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G} f(c)\,\overline{\chi(c)}.
\]
With respect to the normalized inner product
\[
\langle f,g\rangle_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}:=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G}f(c)\,\overline{g(c)},
\]
Parseval's identity takes the form
\[
\|f\|_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}^2=\sum_{\chi\in \widehat G}\abs{\widehat f(\chi)}^2.
\]

For probability measures \(\mu,\nu\) on a finite set \(X\) with \(\mu\ll\nu\), we use
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\mu(x)\log\frac{\mu(x)}{\nu(x)},
\]
\[
\chi^2(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\frac{(\mu(x)-\nu(x))^2}{\nu(x)},
\qquad
\|\mu-\nu\|_{\mathrm{TV}}:=\frac12\sum_{x\in X}\abs{\mu(x)-\nu(x)}.
\]
When \(X\) is partitioned into fibers of a map \(\pi:X\to Y\), the relative-entropy chain rule reads
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu)=D(\pi_\ast\mu\Vert \pi_\ast\nu)+\sum_{y\in Y}(\pi_\ast\mu)(y)\,D(\mu(\cdot\mid y)\Vert \nu(\cdot\mid y)),
\]
whenever the conditional distributions are defined.

\subsection{Chebotarev input}
Fix a finite group \(G\), a temperature parameter \(u\in(0,1]\), and primitive Chebotarev counts \(B_{n,c}(u)\) indexed by \(c\in G\). We write
\[
B_n(\ind;u):=\sum_{c\in G}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
and, when \(G\) is abelian,
\[
B_n(\chi;u):=\sum_{c\in G}\chi(c)\,B_{n,c}(u)
\quad(\chi\in \widehat G),
\]
and for each irreducible channel \(\rho\) we let \(M_{K,\rho}(u)\) denote the corresponding transfer matrix. The trivial-channel Perron root is
\[
\lambda_1(u):=\spr(M_{K,\ind}(u)),
\]
the nontrivial spectral radius is
\[
\eta(u):=\max_{\rho\neq \ind}\spr(M_{K,\rho}(u)),
\]
and we set
\[
\Lambda(u):=\max\{|\lambda_2(u)|,\eta(u)\},
\]
where \(\lambda_2(u)\) denotes the second-largest eigenvalue modulus in the trivial channel.

Sections \ref{sec:entropy-chain} and \ref{sec:artin-pole} depend only on the hypotheses recorded below.

\begin{assumption}[Spectral expansion for primitive counts]\label{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp}
Assume \(\Lambda(u)<\lambda_1(u)\). Then, uniformly in \(c\in G\),
\[
B_{n,c}(u)
=
\frac{1}{|G|}\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right),
\qquad
B_n(\ind;u)
=
\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right).
\]
Moreover, for every conjugacy-invariant subset \(A\subseteq G\), if we set
\[
B_{n,A}(u):=\sum_{c\in A}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
then there exist linear functionals \(L_{A,\rho}\) on the corresponding channel spaces such that
\[
B_{n,A}(u)
=
\mathrm{unif}_G(A)\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+\frac{1}{n}\sum_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{A,\rho}\!\bigl(M_{K,\rho}(u)^n\bigr).
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Artin factorization]\label{prop:kernel-artin-factorization}
For a finite group \(G\), define
\[
L_{K,\rho}(z,u):=\det(I-zM_{K,\rho}(u))^{-1}.
\]
Then the associated zeta function satisfies
\[
\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)=\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho},
\]
and hence
\[
\frac{\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)}{L_{K,\ind}(z,u)}
=
\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho}.
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{definition}[Square-root spectral gap]\label{def:kernel-rh}
We write
\[
\textsf{GRH}_{K,G}(u):\qquad \Lambda(u)\le \lambda_1(u)^{1/2}
\]
for the square-root spectral gap condition that plays the role of a generalized Riemann-hypothesis bound in the present transfer-matrix setting.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
Sections \ref{sec:entropy-chain} and \ref{sec:artin-pole} use Assumptions \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp} and \ref{prop:kernel-artin-factorization} only through the statements written here. In particular, the results in those sections are formal consequences of the asymptotic expansion, the channel decomposition of subset counts, and the factorization identity.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Fold-groupoid input}
We next record the finite-dimensional framework used in Sections \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} and \ref{sec:central-idempotents}.

\begin{definition}[Fold groupoid algebra]\label{def:fold-equivalence-groupoid-algebra}
For each resolution \(m\), let \(\Fold_m:\Omega_m\to X_m\) be the fold map from microstates to stable macrostates, where \(\Omega_m\) is a binary microstate set of cardinality \(2^m\). The associated equivalence-relation groupoid is
\[
\mathcal R_m:=\Omega_m\times_{X_m}\Omega_m
=
\{(\omega,\omega')\in \Omega_m\times\Omega_m:\Fold_m(\omega)=\Fold_m(\omega')\},
\]
and we write
\[
A_{m,1}:=\CC[\mathcal R_m]
\]
for its complex groupoid algebra. The fiber multiplicity at \(x\in X_m\) is
\[
d_m(x):=\abs{\Fold_m^{-1}(x)},
\qquad
D_m:=\max_{x\in X_m}d_m(x),
\qquad
\mathcal M_m:=\{x\in X_m:d_m(x)=D_m\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Wedderburn decomposition]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-wedderburn}
There is a canonical \(\ast\)-isomorphism
\[
A_{m,1}\cong \bigoplus_{x\in X_m}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
For each \(x\in X_m\), we denote by \(\widetilde P_x\in Z(A_{m,1})\) the minimal central idempotent projecting onto the block \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\) determines a full equivalence-relation subgroupoid whose algebra is canonically \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\); summing over all fibers gives the direct-sum decomposition.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[\(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-sector decomposition]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-z2x2-sector-by-orientation}
For each \(x\in X_m\), let \(c_x\) and \(\iota_x\) be the scan and reversal permutations of the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Define the fingerprint
\[
\chi(x):=\bigl(\operatorname{sgn}(c_x),\operatorname{sgn}(\iota_x)\bigr)\in\{\pm1\}^2.
\]
For \((\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2\), set
\[
X_m^{\alpha,\beta}:=\{x\in X_m:\chi(x)=(\alpha,\beta)\},
\qquad
\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}:=\bigoplus_{x\in X_m^{\alpha,\beta}}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
Then
\[
A_{m,1}=\bigoplus_{(\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2}\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}
\]
is the sector decomposition determined by the scan-reversal fingerprint.
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Automorphisms of the fold groupoid algebra]\label{thm:fold-groupoid-aut-semidirect-pi0}
Let
\[
\Pi_0:=\{\sigma\in \mathrm{Sym}(X_m):d_m(\sigma(x))=d_m(x)\ \forall x\in X_m\}.
\]
Then the \(\ast\)-automorphism group of \(A_{m,1}\) is naturally isomorphic to
\[
\Aut^\ast(A_{m,1})
\cong
\Bigl(\prod_{x\in X_m}\mathrm{PU}(d_m(x))\Bigr)\rtimes \Pi_0.
\]
Equivalently, a \(\ast\)-automorphism acts by blockwise projective-unitary conjugation together with a permutation of blocks of equal size.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By finite-dimensional \(C^\ast\)-algebra structure theory, every \(\ast\)-automorphism of \(\bigoplus_x M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\) permutes simple summands and is inner on each summand. Compatibility with matrix size forces the permutation to lie in \(\Pi_0\).
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Maximal-fiber fingerprint]\label{thm:pom-maxfiber-orientation-charge-periodic}
For every resolution \(m\), there exists a fingerprint
\[
\chi_m=(\alpha_m,\beta_m)\in\{\pm1\}^2
\]
such that
\[
\chi(x)=\chi_m
\qquad\text{for all }x\in \mathcal M_m.
\]
In the odd-\(m\) stable periodic regime, one furthermore has \(\beta_m=+1\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{proposition}[Entropy loss under fiber averaging]\label{prop:fold-condexp-index-maxfiber}
Let \(E_m\) be the conditional expectation induced by \(\Fold_m\), that is, averaging uniformly on the fibers of \(\Fold_m\). For a probability distribution \(\rho\) on \(\Omega_m\), write \(\rho^\flat:=\rho\circ E_m\); equivalently, \(\rho^\flat\) has the same pushforward to \(X_m\) as \(\rho\) and is uniform on each fiber of \(\Fold_m\). Then
\[
\sup_{\rho}D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m,
\]
where \(\rho\) ranges over probability distributions on \(\Omega_m\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\rho_X:=(\Fold_m)_\ast\rho\), and for each \(x\in X_m\) let \(u_x\) denote the uniform distribution on the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Since \(\rho^\flat\) has the same macroscopic marginal as \(\rho\) and fiberwise conditionals \(u_x\), the relative-entropy chain rule gives
\[
D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}\rho_X(x)\,
D\!\bigl(\rho(\cdot\mid x)\Vert u_x\bigr).
\]
For each \(x\), the divergence to the uniform measure on a fiber of size \(d_m(x)\) is at most \(\log d_m(x)\le \log D_m\). Hence \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)\le \log D_m\) for every \(\rho\). Conversely, if \(x_\star\in X_m\) satisfies \(d_m(x_\star)=D_m\), choose \(\rho\) to be a Dirac mass on a point of \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x_\star)\). Then \(\rho^\flat\) is the uniform distribution on that same fiber, and therefore \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m\).
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Window-\texorpdfstring{$6$}{6} fiber multiplicities]\label{cor:fold-bin-m6-fiber-hist}
For \(m=6\), direct enumeration yields
\[
\begin{aligned}
d_6(x)&\in\{2,3,4\},\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=2\}&=8,\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=3\}&=4,\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=4\}&=9.
\end{aligned}
\]
and therefore
\[
\abs{X_6}=21.
\]
\end{assumption}

\end{remark}

\section{Concluding Remarks}\label{sec:discussion}
The first part of the paper shows that, under the standing spectral assumptions, passage to a quotient of finite abelian label groups is compatible with exact entropy and Fourier identities. In the same framework, a dominant nontrivial channel controls the second term in subset counts, the first nontrivial Artin pole, and the rate of convergence of primitive label distributions to Haar measure.

The second part gives a finite-dimensional analogue for fold groupoids. The fingerprint \(\chi\) is rigid under the natural automorphisms, the corresponding sectors are realized by central idempotents, and signed Watatani-index moments recover sectorwise spectral data. The window-\(6\) calculations show at the same time that these observables do not exhaust the center, and they quantify the remaining defect.
