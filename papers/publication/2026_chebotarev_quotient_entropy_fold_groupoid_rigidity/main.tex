\begin{abstract}
Working from the standard spectral expansion and Artin factorization of primitive Chebotarev counts, we show that passage to a finite abelian quotient preserves exactly the Fourier energy carried by kernel-trivial characters. Relative entropy satisfies an exact quotient/fiber chain rule, and the \(L^2\)-defect decomposes into visible and hidden character energies. Under an isolated dominant nontrivial channel, we derive an explicit second main term, the corresponding pole order in the nontrivial Artin factor, and exponential convergence of primitive label distributions to uniformity, together with witness sets that preclude square-root decay when the spectral gap is too small. On the fold-groupoid side, using the structural inputs recorded in the preliminaries, we prove \(\chi\)-rigidity for scan-reversal compatible automorphisms, construct the four central sector idempotents explicitly, identify their joint spectral measure, and derive reconstruction and resolution criteria for the resulting central data.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}
The Chebotarev density theorem explains the asymptotic distribution of Frobenius data in finite Galois extensions and is a basic organizing principle in analytic and algebraic number theory \cite{Tschebotareff1926,NeukirchSchmidtWingberg2008,IwaniecKowalski2004}. In refined counting problems one often observes only a coarse label: one passes to a quotient of the full label group, retains only selected channels, or records a simpler observable. The first aim of this paper is to identify, in exact quantitative terms, what is lost under such coarse graining.

For finite abelian quotients, the relevant classical tools are the chain rule for relative entropy and Fourier analysis on finite groups \cite{CoverThomas2006,CsiszarKorner2011,DemboZeitouni1998,Diaconis1988,Terras1999}. The point here is not to reprove those identities, but to show that in the primitive Chebotarev setting they admit a sharp arithmetic interpretation. The quotient distribution retains precisely the Fourier modes whose characters are trivial on the kernel, and the discarded part is an explicit nonnegative hidden-energy term. This yields exact identities for relative entropy and for the \(L^2\)-defect of the primitive label distribution.

The second aim is analytic. Once primitive counts are organized by the channel matrices \(M_{K,\rho}(u)\), an isolated dominant nontrivial spectral value controls both the first nontrivial pole of the Artin factor and the first nontrivial term in subset counts. Under this spectral isolation hypothesis we derive an explicit second main term, identify the exact pole order contributed by the dominant channel, and prove exponential convergence of primitive label distributions toward Haar measure. The same analysis shows that whenever the dominant nontrivial modulus exceeds the square-root threshold, one can detect the failure of square-root decay on a concrete witness subset.

The third aim is algebraic. Fold groupoids produce finite semisimple groupoid algebras whose simple blocks carry a canonical scan-reversal fingerprint in \(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\). The formulas for joint spectral projections of commuting involutions are elementary, but in the present setting they become structural because the involutions are canonically attached to the fold model. We show that scan-reversal compatible \(\ast\)-automorphisms preserve the fingerprint, that the four sector projectors are realized internally in the center, and that the central observables \(\mathrm{IndW}(E_{m,1})\), \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{scan}}\), and \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{rev}}\) control the visible part of the block spectrum \cite{Renault1980,Paterson1999,Watatani1990,BrownOzawa2008,Jones1983}.

The arguments proved in the body of the paper are intentionally separated from the model-specific inputs. On the Chebotarev side we use only the spectral expansion and Artin factorization stated in Assumptions \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp} and \ref{prop:kernel-artin-factorization}. On the fold side we use only the maximal-fiber fingerprint and the window-\(6\) fiber histogram recorded in Assumptions \ref{thm:pom-maxfiber-orientation-charge-periodic} and \ref{cor:fold-bin-m6-fiber-hist}. Once these inputs are fixed, the remaining arguments are finite-dimensional consequences of entropy identities, Fourier decomposition, and elementary \(C^\ast\)-algebra structure theory.

The main results may be summarized as follows. Theorems \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-quotient-relative-entropy-chain} and \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-visible-hidden-character-energy} identify the exact visible and hidden parts of quotient loss. Theorems \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-second-main-term-witness}, \ref{thm:kernel-artin-dominant-channel-pole-order}, and \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-statistical-convergence} turn an isolated dominant channel into second-main-term, pole-order, and convergence statements. Theorem \ref{thm:fold-groupoid-chi-rigidity} and the results of Section \ref{sec:central-idempotents} determine the corresponding central sector structure on the fold-groupoid side.

The paper is organized as follows. Section \ref{sec:preliminaries} records notation and the standing inputs. Section \ref{sec:entropy-chain} studies entropy and Fourier energy under quotient maps. Section \ref{sec:artin-pole} develops the second main term, Artin pole, and convergence consequences. Sections \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} and \ref{sec:central-idempotents} treat rigidity and central structure in the fold-groupoid algebra. Section \ref{sec:discussion} closes with a brief synthesis of the two coarse-graining mechanisms.

\section{Preliminaries}\label{sec:preliminaries}
\subsection{Finite groups, characters, and divergences}
If \(G\) is a finite group, we write \(\widehat G\) for a complete set of irreducible complex representations of \(G\); when \(G\) is abelian, this is identified with its Pontryagin dual of characters. We write \(\mathrm{unif}_G\) for the uniform probability measure on \(G\). For a surjective homomorphism \(\pi:G_2\twoheadrightarrow G_1\) with kernel \(K\), we set
\[
K^\perp:=\{\chi\in\widehat G_2:\chi|_K\equiv 1\}\cong \widehat G_1.
\]
When \(G\) is abelian and \(f:G\to \CC\), its Fourier transform is
\[
\widehat f(\chi):=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G} f(c)\,\overline{\chi(c)}.
\]
With respect to the normalized inner product
\[
\langle f,g\rangle_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}:=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{c\in G}f(c)\,\overline{g(c)},
\]
Parseval's identity takes the form
\[
\|f\|_{L^2(\mathrm{unif}_G)}^2=\sum_{\chi\in \widehat G}\abs{\widehat f(\chi)}^2.
\]

For probability measures \(\mu,\nu\) on a finite set \(X\) with \(\mu\ll\nu\), we use
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\mu(x)\log\frac{\mu(x)}{\nu(x)},
\]
\[
\chi^2(\mu\Vert \nu):=\sum_{x\in X}\frac{(\mu(x)-\nu(x))^2}{\nu(x)},
\qquad
\|\mu-\nu\|_{\mathrm{TV}}:=\frac12\sum_{x\in X}\abs{\mu(x)-\nu(x)}.
\]
When \(X\) is partitioned into fibers of a map \(\pi:X\to Y\), the relative-entropy chain rule reads
\[
D(\mu\Vert \nu)=D(\pi_\ast\mu\Vert \pi_\ast\nu)+\sum_{y\in Y}(\pi_\ast\mu)(y)\,D(\mu(\cdot\mid y)\Vert \nu(\cdot\mid y)),
\]
whenever the conditional distributions are defined.

\subsection{Standing spectral input}
Fix a finite group \(G\), a temperature parameter \(u\in(0,1]\), and primitive Chebotarev counts \(B_{n,c}(u)\) indexed by \(c\in G\). We write
\[
B_n(\ind;u):=\sum_{c\in G}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
and, when \(G\) is abelian,
\[
B_n(\chi;u):=\sum_{c\in G}\chi(c)\,B_{n,c}(u)
\quad(\chi\in \widehat G),
\]
and for each irreducible channel \(\rho\) we let \(M_{K,\rho}(u)\) denote the corresponding transfer matrix. The trivial-channel Perron root is
\[
\lambda_1(u):=\spr(M_{K,\ind}(u)),
\]
the nontrivial spectral radius is
\[
\eta(u):=\max_{\rho\neq \ind}\spr(M_{K,\rho}(u)),
\]
and we set
\[
\Lambda(u):=\max\{|\lambda_2(u)|,\eta(u)\},
\]
where \(\lambda_2(u)\) denotes the second-largest eigenvalue modulus in the trivial channel.

The results below use only the following standard spectral consequences of the transfer formalism.

\begin{assumption}[Spectral expansion for primitive Chebotarev counts]\label{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp}
Assume \(\Lambda(u)<\lambda_1(u)\). Then, uniformly in \(c\in G\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
B_{n,c}(u)
&=
\frac{1}{|G|}\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right),\\
B_n(\ind;u)
&=
\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda(u)^n}{n}\right).
\end{aligned}
\]
Moreover, for every conjugacy-invariant subset \(A\subseteq G\), if we set
\[
B_{n,A}(u):=\sum_{c\in A}B_{n,c}(u),
\]
then there exist linear functionals \(L_{A,\rho}\) on the corresponding channel spaces such that
\[
B_{n,A}(u)
=
\mathrm{unif}_G(A)\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
+\frac{1}{n}\sum_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{A,\rho}\!\bigl(M_{K,\rho}(u)^n\bigr).
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Artin factorization]\label{prop:kernel-artin-factorization}
For a finite group \(G\), define
\[
L_{K,\rho}(z,u):=\det(I-zM_{K,\rho}(u))^{-1}.
\]
Then the associated zeta function satisfies
\[
\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)=\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho},
\]
and hence
\[
\frac{\zeta_{K,G}(z,u)}{L_{K,\ind}(z,u)}
=
\prod_{\rho\in\widehat G\setminus\{\ind\}}L_{K,\rho}(z,u)^{\dim\rho}.
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{definition}[Square-root spectral gap]\label{def:kernel-rh}
We write
\[
\textsf{GRH}_{K,G}(u):\qquad \Lambda(u)\le \lambda_1(u)^{1/2}
\]
for the square-root spectral gap condition that plays the role of a generalized Riemann-hypothesis bound in the present transfer-matrix setting.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
Assumptions \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp} and \ref{prop:kernel-artin-factorization} are the only Chebotarev inputs used below. Every later argument depends solely on the explicit asymptotic expansion, the channel decomposition of subset counts, and the Artin factorization recorded here.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Fold groupoids and central observables}
We now record the finite-dimensional groupoid-algebraic framework used in Sections \ref{sec:groupoid-rigidity} and \ref{sec:central-idempotents}.

\begin{definition}[Fold equivalence groupoid algebra]\label{def:fold-equivalence-groupoid-algebra}
For each resolution \(m\), let \(\Fold_m:\Omega_m\to X_m\) be the fold map from microstates to stable macrostates, where \(\Omega_m\) is a binary microstate set of cardinality \(2^m\). The associated equivalence-relation groupoid is
\[
\mathcal R_m:=\Omega_m\times_{X_m}\Omega_m
=
\{(\omega,\omega')\in \Omega_m\times\Omega_m:\Fold_m(\omega)=\Fold_m(\omega')\},
\]
and we write
\[
A_{m,1}:=\CC[\mathcal R_m]
\]
for its complex groupoid algebra. The fiber multiplicity at \(x\in X_m\) is
\[
d_m(x):=\abs{\Fold_m^{-1}(x)},
\qquad
D_m:=\max_{x\in X_m}d_m(x),
\qquad
\mathcal M_m:=\{x\in X_m:d_m(x)=D_m\}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Wedderburn decomposition of the fold groupoid algebra]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-wedderburn}
There is a canonical \(\ast\)-isomorphism
\[
A_{m,1}\cong \bigoplus_{x\in X_m}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
For each \(x\in X_m\), we denote by \(\widetilde P_x\in Z(A_{m,1})\) the minimal central idempotent projecting onto the block \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\) determines a full equivalence-relation subgroupoid whose algebra is canonically \(M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\); summing over all fibers gives the direct-sum decomposition.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[\(\ZZ_2\times\ZZ_2\)-sector decomposition by orientation]\label{prop:fold-groupoid-z2x2-sector-by-orientation}
For each \(x\in X_m\), let \(c_x\) and \(\iota_x\) be the scan and reversal permutations of the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Define the fingerprint
\[
\chi(x):=\bigl(\operatorname{sgn}(c_x),\operatorname{sgn}(\iota_x)\bigr)\in\{\pm1\}^2.
\]
For \((\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2\), set
\[
X_m^{\alpha,\beta}:=\{x\in X_m:\chi(x)=(\alpha,\beta)\},
\qquad
\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}:=\bigoplus_{x\in X_m^{\alpha,\beta}}M_{d_m(x)}(\CC).
\]
Then
\[
A_{m,1}=\bigoplus_{(\alpha,\beta)\in\{\pm1\}^2}\CC[\mathcal R_m]^{\alpha,\beta}
\]
is the sector decomposition determined by the scan-reversal fingerprint.
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Automorphisms of the fold groupoid algebra]\label{thm:fold-groupoid-aut-semidirect-pi0}
Let
\[
\Pi_0:=\{\sigma\in \mathrm{Sym}(X_m):d_m(\sigma(x))=d_m(x)\ \forall x\in X_m\}.
\]
Then the \(\ast\)-automorphism group of \(A_{m,1}\) is naturally isomorphic to
\[
\Aut^\ast(A_{m,1})
\cong
\Bigl(\prod_{x\in X_m}\mathrm{PU}(d_m(x))\Bigr)\rtimes \Pi_0.
\]
Equivalently, a \(\ast\)-automorphism acts by blockwise projective-unitary conjugation together with a permutation of blocks of equal size.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By finite-dimensional \(C^\ast\)-algebra structure theory, every \(\ast\)-automorphism of \(\bigoplus_x M_{d_m(x)}(\CC)\) permutes simple summands and is inner on each summand. Compatibility with matrix size forces the permutation to lie in \(\Pi_0\).
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Maximal-fiber orientation charge]\label{thm:pom-maxfiber-orientation-charge-periodic}
For every resolution \(m\), there exists a fingerprint
\[
\chi_m=(\alpha_m,\beta_m)\in\{\pm1\}^2
\]
such that
\[
\chi(x)=\chi_m
\qquad\text{for all }x\in \mathcal M_m.
\]
In the odd-\(m\) stable periodic regime, one furthermore has \(\beta_m=+1\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{proposition}[Maximal entropy loss to fiber uniformization]\label{prop:fold-condexp-index-maxfiber}
Let \(E_m\) be the conditional expectation induced by \(\Fold_m\), that is, averaging uniformly on the fibers of \(\Fold_m\). For a probability distribution \(\rho\) on \(\Omega_m\), write \(\rho^\flat:=\rho\circ E_m\); equivalently, \(\rho^\flat\) has the same pushforward to \(X_m\) as \(\rho\) and is uniform on each fiber of \(\Fold_m\). Then
\[
\sup_{\rho}D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m,
\]
where \(\rho\) ranges over probability distributions on \(\Omega_m\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(\rho_X:=(\Fold_m)_\ast\rho\), and for each \(x\in X_m\) let \(u_x\) denote the uniform distribution on the fiber \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x)\). Since \(\rho^\flat\) has the same macroscopic marginal as \(\rho\) and fiberwise conditionals \(u_x\), the relative-entropy chain rule gives
\[
D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)
=
\sum_{x\in X_m}\rho_X(x)\,
D\!\bigl(\rho(\cdot\mid x)\Vert u_x\bigr).
\]
For each \(x\), the divergence to the uniform measure on a fiber of size \(d_m(x)\) is at most \(\log d_m(x)\le \log D_m\). Hence \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)\le \log D_m\) for every \(\rho\). Conversely, if \(x_\star\in X_m\) satisfies \(d_m(x_\star)=D_m\), choose \(\rho\) to be a Dirac mass on a point of \(\Fold_m^{-1}(x_\star)\). Then \(\rho^\flat\) is the uniform distribution on that same fiber, and therefore \(D(\rho\Vert \rho^\flat)=\log D_m\).
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Window-\texorpdfstring{$6$}{6} fiber histogram]\label{cor:fold-bin-m6-fiber-hist}
For \(m=6\), direct enumeration yields
\[
\begin{aligned}
d_6(x)&\in\{2,3,4\},\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=2\}&=8,\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=3\}&=4,\\
\#\{x\in X_6:d_6(x)=4\}&=9.
\end{aligned}
\]
and therefore
\[
\abs{X_6}=21.
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{remark}
Assumptions \ref{thm:pom-maxfiber-orientation-charge-periodic} and \ref{cor:fold-bin-m6-fiber-hist} are the only fold-model inputs used below. The later arguments require only the constancy of the maximal-fiber fingerprint and the stated window-\(6\) multiplicity data.
\end{remark}

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}
The Chebotarev results isolate an exact coarse-graining principle. Passing from a finite abelian label group to a quotient preserves precisely the Fourier mass carried by kernel-trivial characters, and the loss is measured simultaneously by the fiber term in the entropy chain rule and by the hidden term in the \(L^2\)-decomposition. When a dominant nontrivial channel is spectrally isolated, the same mechanism determines the first nontrivial pole of the Artin factor, the second main term in subset counts, and the sharp obstruction to square-root statistical decay.

The fold-groupoid results exhibit an analogous finite-dimensional rigidity phenomenon. The scan-reversal fingerprint is implemented by two commuting central involutions, and their joint spectral projections realize the four sectors internally. The resulting moment formulas quantify exactly how much of the center is resolved by \(\mathrm{IndW}(E_{m,1})\), \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{scan}}\), and \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{rev}}\). In both parts of the paper, the arguments separate cleanly into model-specific inputs recorded in Section \ref{sec:preliminaries} and formal consequences deduced from entropy, Fourier analysis, and finite-dimensional \(C^\ast\)-algebra structure.
