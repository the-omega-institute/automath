\begin{theorem}[The Chebotarev second main term and witness sets: explicit oscillation from a saturating mode]\label{thm:kernel-chebotarev-second-main-term-witness}
Fix a finite label group \(G\) and a temperature parameter \(u\in(0,1]\) in the setting of Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp}.
Let \(\lambda_1(u)=\rho(M_{K,\ind}(u))\) be the Perron root in the trivial channel, and for each irreducible representation \(\rho\neq \ind\) (equivalently, for each nontrivial character in the abelian case) write \(M_{K,\rho}(u)\) for the twisted channel matrix.
Assume that there exists a nontrivial channel \(\rho_\star\neq\ind\) with an eigenvalue \(\mu_\star\in\mathrm{spec}(M_{K,\rho_\star}(u))\) such that
\[
\abs{\mu_\star}
=
\max_{\rho\neq\ind}\rho\!\bigl(M_{K,\rho}(u)\bigr)
=:\Lambda(u),
\]
and that this channel has an internal spectral gap \(\eta>0\) satisfying
\[
\max\bigl\{\abs{\nu}:\ \nu\in\mathrm{spec}(M_{K,\rho_\star}(u)),\ \nu\neq \mu_\star\bigr\}\le \Lambda(u)-\eta.
\]
Then for every conjugacy-invariant subset \(A\subseteq G\) and its associated cylinder event \(U=\pi^{-1}(A)\), there exists a complex coefficient \(c_U\)---obtained by pairing the \(\rho_\star\)-Fourier component of \(1_A\) with the left and right eigenvectors of \(\mu_\star\)---such that the primitive Chebotarev count satisfies the expansion
\[
B_{n,U}(u)
=
\mu_{\mathrm{Haar}}(U)\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}
\;+\;
\Re\!\bigl(c_U\,\mu_\star^{\,n}\bigr)\frac{1}{n}
\;+\;
O\!\left(\frac{(\Lambda(u)-\eta)^n}{n}\right).
\]
If, moreover, the \(\rho_\star\)-Fourier component of \(1_A\) is nonzero, then \(c_U\neq 0\); in particular, there exists a constant \(c(A)>0\) such that
\[
\bigl|\Re(c_U\mu_\star^{\,n})\bigr|\ \ge\ c(A)\,\Lambda(u)^n
\]
for infinitely many integers \(n\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Decompose \(1_A\) into irreducible channels. Then the primitive count \(B_{n,U}(u)\) becomes a linear combination of the twisted primitive counts \(B_n(\rho;u)\), exactly as in the Fourier expansion recorded in Theorem \ref{thm:kernel-chebotarev-exp}. The trivial channel contributes the main term
\[
\mu_{\mathrm{Haar}}(U)\frac{\lambda_1(u)^n}{n}.
\]
In the channel \(\rho_\star\), the spectral-gap assumption yields the power decomposition
\[
M_{K,\rho_\star}(u)^n=\mu_\star^{\,n}P_\star+O((\Lambda(u)-\eta)^n),
\]
where \(P_\star\) is the spectral projection onto the \(\mu_\star\)-eigenspace.
Pairing the \(\rho_\star\)-component of \(1_A\) with \(P_\star\), and then passing from periodic-point counts to primitive counts by M\"obius inversion, produces the coefficient \(c_U\) and the stated error term.

If the \(\rho_\star\)-component of \(1_A\) is nonzero, then the pairing with \(P_\star\) is nontrivial, so \(c_U\neq 0\).
Since \(\mu_\star/\Lambda(u)\in\TT\), its phase either lies on a finite subgroup of the circle or is equidistributed on a dense subgroup; in either case one can choose infinitely many \(n\) for which \(\Re(c_U\mu_\star^{\,n})\) is bounded below in absolute value by a positive constant times \(\Lambda(u)^n\).
This gives the claimed oscillatory lower bound.
\end{proof}
