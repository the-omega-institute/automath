\section{Auxiliary holomorphic variation and effective truncation}
\label{app:variation}

This appendix records auxiliary finite-matrix consequences of the scalar
Perron-pole finite part: holomorphic variation and an explicit
truncation estimate. These statements are downstream of the main
finite-group Adams--M\"obius theorem package. They are not used in the
proof of Main Theorems~I and~II; they record regularity and effective
forms of the scalar normalisation after the finite-group reconstruction
has already been established. In particular, any parameter-varying
reading of the scalar cyclic-lift formulas is confined to this appendix:
the main text uses only fixed-matrix and fixed-layer recovery
statements.

\subsection{Holomorphic variation and effective truncation}

\begin{theorem}[Holomorphic variation formula]
  \label{thm:finite-part-holomorphic-variation}
  Let \(U\subset\CC\) be simply connected and let
  \(\theta\mapsto A_\theta\) be a holomorphic family of complex
  \(d\times d\) matrices. Assume that there is a holomorphic simple
  eigenvalue \(\lambda(\theta)\) such that
  \[
    |\lambda(\theta)|>1,
    \qquad
    |\nu|<|\lambda(\theta)|
    \quad
    (\nu\in\spec(A_\theta)\setminus\{\lambda(\theta)\})
  \]
  for every \(\theta\in U\). Let \(P_\theta\) be the spectral projection
  of \(A_\theta\) for \(\lambda(\theta)\), and let \(R_\theta\) be the
  reduced inverse of \(I-A_\theta/\lambda(\theta)\), characterized by
  \(R_\theta P_\theta=P_\theta R_\theta=0\) and
  \[
    R_\theta\bigl(I-A_\theta/\lambda(\theta)\bigr)
    =
    \bigl(I-A_\theta/\lambda(\theta)\bigr)R_\theta
    =
    I-P_\theta .
  \]
  After choosing the logarithm branches determined at one base point,
  set
  \[
    \mathfrak F(\theta)
    :=
    \Log C_{\lambda(\theta)}(A_\theta)
    +
    \sum_{m\ge2}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}
      \Log\det\bigl(I-\lambda(\theta)^{-m}A_\theta\bigr)^{-1}.
  \]
  For \(m\ge2\), put
  \[
    D_{m,\theta}
    :=
    \lambda(\theta)^{-m}\dot A_\theta
    -m\dot\lambda(\theta)\lambda(\theta)^{-m-1}A_\theta .
  \]
  Then \(\mathfrak F\) is holomorphic on \(U\), and
  \begin{align*}
    \frac{\dd}{\dd\theta}\mathfrak F(\theta)
    &=
    \Tr\!\left[
      R_\theta\left(
        \lambda(\theta)^{-1}\dot A_\theta
        -\dot\lambda(\theta)\lambda(\theta)^{-2}A_\theta
      \right)
    \right] \\
    &\quad+
    \sum_{m\ge2}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}
    \Tr\!\left[
      \bigl(I-\lambda(\theta)^{-m}A_\theta\bigr)^{-1}
      D_{m,\theta}
    \right],
  \end{align*}
  where dots denote \(\theta\)-derivatives. At every parameter for which
  \(A_\theta\) is primitive non-negative and \(\lambda(\theta)>1\) is its
  Perron root, \(\mathfrak F(\theta)=\FP(A_\theta)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  Put
  \[
    B_\theta:=I-A_\theta/\lambda(\theta).
  \]
  The simple eigenvalue hypothesis and the finite-dimensional Riesz
  projection formula imply that \(P_\theta\) is holomorphic locally in
  \(\theta\). Hence \(B_\theta+P_\theta\) is holomorphic and invertible:
  on \(\operatorname{im}P_\theta\) it is the identity, while on
  \(\ker P_\theta\) it is \(B_\theta\). Therefore
  \[
    C_{\lambda(\theta)}(A_\theta)
    =
    \det(B_\theta+P_\theta)^{-1}
  \]
  is a non-vanishing holomorphic function. Since \(U\) is simply
  connected, the chosen value at the base point determines a holomorphic
  branch of \(\Log C_{\lambda(\theta)}(A_\theta)\).

  On the same splitting,
  \[
    (B_\theta+P_\theta)^{-1}=R_\theta+P_\theta .
  \]
  Differentiating the logarithmic determinant gives
  \[
    \frac{\dd}{\dd\theta}\Log C_{\lambda(\theta)}(A_\theta)
    =
    -\Tr\bigl[(R_\theta+P_\theta)(\dot B_\theta+\dot P_\theta)\bigr].
  \]
  The projection identity \(P_\theta^2=P_\theta\) gives
  \(P_\theta\dot P_\theta P_\theta=0\) and
  \((I-P_\theta)\dot P_\theta(I-P_\theta)=0\). Also
  \(B_\theta P_\theta=P_\theta B_\theta=0\), so
  \(B_\theta\), \(R_\theta\), and \(P_\theta\) are block diagonal for the
  decomposition
  \(\operatorname{im}P_\theta\oplus\ker P_\theta\), whereas \(\dot P_\theta\)
  has only off-diagonal blocks. Thus the trace terms containing
  \(\dot P_\theta\) vanish. Differentiating
  \(P_\theta B_\theta P_\theta=0\) gives
  \(P_\theta\dot B_\theta P_\theta=0\), and hence
  \(\Tr(P_\theta\dot B_\theta)=0\). Consequently
  \[
    \frac{\dd}{\dd\theta}\Log C_{\lambda(\theta)}(A_\theta)
    =
    -\Tr(R_\theta\dot B_\theta)
    =
    \Tr\!\left[
      R_\theta\left(
        \lambda(\theta)^{-1}\dot A_\theta
        -\dot\lambda(\theta)\lambda(\theta)^{-2}A_\theta
      \right)
    \right].
  \]

  For \(m\ge2\), the spectral hypothesis gives
  \[
    \rho\bigl(\lambda(\theta)^{-m}A_\theta\bigr)
    \le |\lambda(\theta)|^{1-m}<1,
  \]
  so \(I-\lambda(\theta)^{-m}A_\theta\) is invertible. Its determinant is
  therefore non-zero and has a holomorphic logarithm on \(U\). On each
  compact \(K\subset U\), one has \(|\lambda(\theta)|\ge 1+\delta\) and
  the matrices \(A_\theta,\dot A_\theta\) and the scalar \(\dot\lambda\)
  are bounded. The determinant logarithms are then
  \(O((1+\delta)^{-m})\), and their derivatives are also
  \(O((1+\delta/2)^{-m})\), after increasing the implicit constant to
  handle the finitely many small \(m\). Hence the series defining
  \(\mathfrak F\) and the differentiated series converge locally
  uniformly.

  Finally, for
  \[
    B_{m,\theta}:=I-\lambda(\theta)^{-m}A_\theta
  \]
  we have
  \[
    \dot B_{m,\theta}
    =
    -\lambda(\theta)^{-m}\dot A_\theta
    +m\dot\lambda(\theta)\lambda(\theta)^{-m-1}A_\theta,
  \]
  and therefore
  \[
    \frac{\dd}{\dd\theta}\Log\det(B_{m,\theta})^{-1}
    =
    -\Tr(B_{m,\theta}^{-1}\dot B_{m,\theta})
    =
    \Tr\!\left[
      \bigl(I-\lambda(\theta)^{-m}A_\theta\bigr)^{-1}
      D_{m,\theta}
    \right].
  \]
  Combining this identity with the reduced-determinant derivative gives
  the displayed formula. At a primitive non-negative parameter, the
  determinant expression for \(\mathfrak F\) is exactly the scalar
  finite-part formula of \Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Truncation bound]
  \label{thm:logM-truncation-bound}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix with
  Perron root \(\lambda>1\), and define
  \[
    \FP_K(A)
    :=\log C(A)
      +\sum_{n\le K}p_n(A)
       \bigl(\lambda^{-n}+\log(1-\lambda^{-n})\bigr).
  \]
  Then
  \[
    \abs{\FP(A)-\FP_K(A)}
    \le
    \frac{d\,\lambda^{-(K+1)}}{(K+1)(1-\lambda^{-1})^3}.
  \]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  By \Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form},
  \[
    \abs{\FP(A)-\FP_K(A)}
    \le \sum_{n>K} \abs{p_n(A)}\psi(\lambda^{-n}).
  \]
  Since
  \[
    \psi(x)=-x-\log(1-x)\le \sum_{m\ge 2}x^m=\frac{x^2}{1-x},
  \]
  and
  \[
    \abs{p_n(A)}
    \le
    \frac{1}{n}\sum_{m\mid n}\abs{\Tr(A^{n/m})}
    \le
    \frac{d}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n\lambda^k
    \le
    \frac{d\,\lambda^n}{n(1-\lambda^{-1})},
  \]
  one obtains
  \[
    \abs{p_n(A)}\psi(\lambda^{-n})
    \le
    \frac{d}{n}\frac{\lambda^{-n}}
      {(1-\lambda^{-1})(1-\lambda^{-n})}
    \le
    \frac{d}{n}\frac{\lambda^{-n}}{(1-\lambda^{-1})^2}.
  \]
  Summing over \(n>K\) gives
  \[
    \abs{\FP(A)-\FP_K(A)}
    \le \frac{d}{(1-\lambda^{-1})^2}
      \sum_{n>K}\frac{\lambda^{-n}}{n}
    \le
      \frac{d\,\lambda^{-(K+1)}}{(K+1)(1-\lambda^{-1})^3}.
  \]
\end{proof}

\section{Boundary of parameter-varying record interpretations}
\label{app:record-boundary}

The scalar statements in \Cref{sec:finite-part} are exact recovery
results for recorded data attached to one fixed primitive matrix \(A\):
either the full cyclic-lift record \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}_{q\ge1}\) or one
fixed root-of-unity layer
\(\{F_A(\omega_q^j)\}_{0\le j\le q-1}\) with \(q\ge d\). They do not
state a local inverse theorem, even at one fixed sampling radius \(q\),
on any ambient parameter space of primitive matrices, cocycles, or
auxiliary measurement models.
Likewise, if an auxiliary model assigns to each parameter some external
response observable and then identifies that observable with one of
these scalar records, the paper contributes only the scalar record on
the right-hand side. The bridge identification itself, and any ensuing
shell or level-set statement for the auxiliary observable, is outside
the ETDS theorem hierarchy proved here.

If one nevertheless studies a holomorphic family
\(\theta\mapsto A_\theta\), the formulas of
\Cref{thm:finite-part-holomorphic-variation} produce only the induced
record map
\[
  \theta\longmapsto \bigl(\Psi_q(A_\theta)\bigr)_{q\ge1}
  \qquad\text{or}\qquad
  \theta\longmapsto
  \bigl(F_{A_\theta}(\omega_q^j)\bigr)_{0\le j\le q-1}
\]
for a fixed \(q\). Without additional hypotheses external to this
paper, that map is merely a parameterised graph of scalar records. The
present paper proves neither injectivity nor a fixed-radius local
inverse for such an ambient family. This is why the parameter-varying
viewpoint is kept in the appendix rather than in the ETDS theorem
hierarchy.

\section{Boundary of the cyclic arithmetic package}
\label{app:arithmetic}

The cyclic-lift formulas in \Cref{sec:finite-part} determine the reduced
spectral polynomial \(F_A\) by finite Fourier inversion and recover the
relative spectral radius from the full sequence \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}\). These
facts are the only root-of-unity inputs used in the article. Further
arithmetic refinements of the same scalar data, such as
Dirichlet-character residue-class decompositions, root-of-unity
rationality tests, boundary-multiplicity averages, or polylogarithmic
Dirichlet series for cyclic-lift discrepancies, concern the reduced
scalar spectrum alone. They do not interact with the Adams--M\"obius
primitive inversion for a fixed finite-group cocycle and are therefore
kept out of the ETDS theorem hierarchy.

The distinction is important for the cyclic comparison in
\Cref{sec:shadows}. Cyclic quotients of a fixed cocycle see only the
one-dimensional Peter--Weyl sector of the finite-group primitive profile.
The root-of-unity data in \Cref{sec:finite-part} instead arise from the
canonical cyclic lift, where the original cocycle has been replaced by
the constant label \(1\in\ZZ/q\ZZ\). Thus scalar arithmetic consequences
of \(F_A(\omega_q^j)\) are not additional primitive class invariants for
the fixed finite-group extension.

\section{Boundary of growing-family rigidity}
\label{app:rigidity}

The finite-part identities also have elementary consequences for growing
families of primitive matrices under extra hypotheses on
\(\Lambda(A)/\lambda\), \(d\), and \(\lambda\). Such estimates are useful
for a separate scalar finite-part project, but they are not needed for
Main Theorem~I, Main Theorem~II, quotient functoriality, the abelian
shadow, or the \(S_3\) obstruction model. The present paper therefore
uses only the fixed-matrix scalar normalisation
\(\mathcal M(A)=\gamma+\FP(A)\) and the canonical cyclic-lift comparison
proved in \Cref{sec:finite-part}.

\section[Exact rational verification for the S3 certificates]{Exact
  rational verification for the \texorpdfstring{$S_3$}{S3}
  certificates}
\label{app:s3-log-certificates}

The certified intervals in
\Cref{lem:s3-example-perron-certificates} are obtained by a finite list
of rational logarithms. The finite list of pairs \((c,q)\) is recorded
immediately after \Cref{lem:s3-example-perron-certificates}; for each
row the proof uses the
positive-series identity
\[
\begin{aligned}
  \log q&=L_3(q)+\Omega_3(q), \\
  L_3(q)&:=2\sum_{j=0}^{3}\frac{u^{2j+1}}{2j+1}, \\
  U_3(q)&:=L_3(q)+\frac{2u^9}{9(1-u^2)}, \\
  u&=\frac{q-1}{q+1}, \\
  0&<\Omega_3(q)<U_3(q)-L_3(q).
\end{aligned}
\]
Hence \(L_3(q)<\log q<U_3(q)\) for every rational \(q>1\) occurring in
\(E_7\) or \(R_{15}\), and the coefficient-weighted bounds
\(cL_3(q)\le c\log q\le cU_3(q)\) are again rational.

The next two tables print a self-contained exact rational certificate.
Every terminating decimal below is an exact rational with denominator
\(10^{15}\). In the logarithm columns the lower endpoint is obtained by
rounding \(L_3(q)\) downward and the upper endpoint by rounding
\(U_3(q)\) upward. In the weighted columns the same outward rounding is
applied after multiplying by the coefficient \(c\). Therefore every
displayed interval is rigorous.
