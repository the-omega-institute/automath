\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

The problem addressed in this paper is primitive orbit reconstruction
for finite-group extensions of mixing subshifts of finite type. Let
\((X_A^+,\sigma)\) be a mixing subshift with primitive adjacency matrix
\(A\), let \(\lambda=\rho(A)\), and let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle
into a finite group. For every irreducible representation \(\varrho\)
of \(G\), the twisted transition matrix \(A_\varrho\) records periodic
labels through its traces. These traces are periodic data. The
primitive labels are subtler: when a primitive orbit with label \(g\)
is repeated \(m\) times, its label becomes \(g^m\). Thus primitive
extraction is not ordinary M\"obius inversion in the non-abelian
setting; it must be coupled to the Adams operations
\(\psi^m\phi(g)=\phi(g^m)\) on class functions.

The centre of the paper is the Adams--M\"obius passage from twisted
determinant data to primitive Peter--Weyl channels. The finite-group
spine has two steps: Main Theorem~I linearises twisted determinants and
performs primitive Adams--M\"obius inversion; Main Theorem~II evaluates
the resulting non-trivial primitive series at \(z=\lambda^{-1}\), after
imposing the twisted spectral gap \(\eta<\lambda\), and obtains conjugacy-class Mertens
constants. The readout of these constants is the Hermitian
character-orthogonality formula, whose class coefficient is
\(\overline{\chi_\varrho(C)}\) and hence, by unitarity,
\(\chi_\varrho(C^{-1})\).

The scalar finite part of \(\zeta_A\) and the cyclic root-of-unity layer
have narrower roles. The finite part supplies only the trivial-channel
normalisation \(\mathcal M(A)\), while root-of-unity sampling belongs
only to the canonical cyclic lift, where every edge is labelled by the
generator of \(\ZZ/q\ZZ\). It is not the same as taking arbitrary cyclic
quotients of the fixed cocycle. The logical direction of the proofs is
\[
  \begin{array}{c}
    \text{twisted determinants}
    \xrightarrow{\text{trace}}
    \text{periodic Peter--Weyl channels} \\[2pt]
    \xrightarrow{\text{Adams--M\"obius}}
    \text{primitive Peter--Weyl channels} \\[2pt]
    \xrightarrow[\eta<\lambda]{z=\lambda^{-1}}
    \text{class Mertens constants}
    \xrightarrow{\text{orthogonality}}
    \text{primitive class profile at the Perron point}.
  \end{array}
\]
Here the Adams--M\"obius arrow is the primitive extraction step; the last
arrow is ordinary character orthogonality after the Perron-point values
have been constructed. The formal scope of these packages and of the
auxiliary scalar section is collected in the next subsection.

Main Theorem~I is the package
\Cref{lem:twisted-trace-identity,thm:class-function-linearisation,%
cor:class-function-adams-mobius,cor:primitive-peter-weyl-determinant,%
cor:primitive-peter-weyl-trace}.
Main Theorem~II is the package
\Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit,prop:class-mertens-deviations,%
thm:class-mertens-fourier}. The quotient and cyclic-detection results
of \Cref{sec:shadows} are structural consequences of these packages.
The scalar finite-part and root-of-unity statements are auxiliary inputs
proved later. The scalar dynamical \(\zeta\)-function
\[
  \zeta_A(z)
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\det(I-zA)^{-1}
\]
enters only through the trivial representation channel. Its rationality
and orbit-counting role are classical, going back to
Bowen--Lanford \cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta} and Manning
\cite{Manning1971Axiom}, with further development through Ruelle's
thermodynamic formalism
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic} and the
Parry--Pollicott transfer-operator programme
\cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}. The finite-part proposition below is a
classical Euler-product/M\"obius computation at this channel, while the
one-layer DFT corollary is polynomial coefficient recovery from enough
root-of-unity samples. Neither statement generates non-abelian primitive
information.

\subsection{Finite-group main theorem spine}

Let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle into a finite group, and let
\(A_\varrho\) be the twisted transition matrices. For a class function
\(\phi:G\to\CC\) write
\[
  T_{\phi,n}:=\sum_{x\in\operatorname{Fix}(\sigma^n)}
    \phi(\tau_n(x)),
  \qquad
  \mathcal L_\phi(z):=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{T_{\phi,n}}{n}z^n,
\]
and
\[
  \Pi_\phi(z):=\sum_{\gamma\in\cP}\phi(\tau(\gamma))z^{|\gamma|}.
\]
For a conjugacy class \(C\subset G\), let \(p_{n,C}\) denote the number
of primitive orbits of length \(n\) whose cocycle label lies in \(C\).
The scalar orbit-Mertens constant \(\mathcal M(A)\) is the
trivial-channel constant defined in \Cref{def:abel-finite-part}; the
finite-matrix formula behind this normalisation is the auxiliary
calculation of \Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form}.

\subsection{Scope}\label{sec:intro-scope-of-results}

\paragraph{Roadmap of results.}
The argument has three logically separate parts.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Main Theorem~I is the finite-group determinant and
  Adams--M\"obius package on the open Perron disc
  \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\).
\item Main Theorem~II begins only after imposing \(\lambda>1\) and the
  twisted spectral gap
  \[
    \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda;
  \]
  this hypothesis is used to evaluate the non-trivial primitive
  Peter--Weyl channels at \(z=\lambda^{-1}\) and hence define the class
  Mertens constants. By
  \Cref{lem:twisted-gap-skew-product-peripheral}, it is already a
  finite-state condition on the skew-product adjacency matrix
  \(\widetilde A\): it says that \(\widetilde A\) has the same
  peripheral spectrum as the primitive base block \(A\), namely the
  single simple eigenvalue \(\lambda\); if \(\widetilde A\) is
  irreducible, this is equivalent to primitivity of \(\widetilde A\).
\item The scalar results of \Cref{sec:finite-part} are auxiliary
  fixed-matrix inputs: \Cref{thm:intro-finite-part} supplies the
  trivial-channel normalisation \(\mathcal M(A)\), while
  \Cref{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction,cor:intro-one-layer-reconstruction}
  record a separate canonical cyclic-lift comparison for one fixed
  matrix. Their detailed proofs are deferred to
  \Cref{app:cyclic-lift-package}.
\end{enumerate}
All quantities \(T_{\phi,n}\), \(\mathcal L_\phi\), and \(\Pi_\phi\)
are deterministic observables of the fixed cocycle. Any identification
of these fixed-matrix records with an external detector or shell
observable would require additional hypotheses.

\begin{theorem}[Main Theorem I: Adams--M\"obius primitive
  determinant linearisation]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-group}
  \label{thm:intro-adams-mobius}
  For every irreducible representation \(\varrho\in\Irr(G)\) and every
  \(n\ge 1\),
  \[
    \Tr(A_\varrho^n)
    =
    \sum_{x\in\operatorname{Fix}(\sigma^n)}
      \chi_\varrho(\tau_n(x)).
  \]
  Hence, for every class function \(\phi:G\to\CC\) and every
  \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\),
  \[
    \mathcal L_\phi(z)
    =
    \sum_{\varrho\in\Irr(G)}
      \widehat\phi(\varrho)
      \log\det(I-zA_\varrho)^{-1},
  \]
  where \(\widehat\phi(\varrho)\) is the character coefficient of
  \(\phi\). Moreover,
  \[
    \Pi_\phi(z)
    =
    \sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}\,
      \mathcal L_{\psi^m\phi}(z^m).
  \]
  Equivalently, if
  \[
    \Pi_\varrho(z):=\Pi_{\chi_\varrho}(z)
      =\sum_{n\ge 1}\pi_{n,\varrho}(A,\tau)z^n
  \]
  and
  \(\psi^m\chi_\varrho
    =\sum_{\sigma\in\Irr(G)}a_{\varrho,\sigma}^{(m)}\chi_\sigma\),
  then
  \[
    \Pi_\varrho(z)
    =
    \sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}
      \sum_{\sigma\in\Irr(G)}
        a_{\varrho,\sigma}^{(m)}
        \log\det(I-z^mA_\sigma)^{-1}.
  \]
  Coefficient extraction gives the primitive Peter--Weyl trace formula
  \[
    \pi_{n,\varrho}(A,\tau)
    =
    \frac{1}{n}\sum_{m\mid n}\mu(m)
      \sum_{\sigma\in\Irr(G)}
        a_{\varrho,\sigma}^{(m)}
        \Tr(A_\sigma^{n/m}).
  \]
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Main Theorem II: class Mertens constants under
  the stated twisted gap]
  \label{thm:intro-class-mertens}
  Assume \(\lambda>1\) and the twisted spectral gap
  \[
    \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda,
    \qquad
    \Lambda_*:=\max\{\Lambda(A),\eta,\lambda^{1/2}\}.
  \]
  Here \(\Lambda(A)\) denotes the maximum modulus of the non-Perron
  eigenvalues of the base matrix \(A\).
  Then there is a class-uniform constant
  \(C_{\mathrm{cls}}(A,\tau;G)>0\), made explicit in
  \Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit}, such that for every conjugacy class
  \(C\subset G\),
  \[
    \abs{
      p_{n,C}
      -\frac{|C|}{|G|}\frac{\lambda^n}{n}
    }
    \le
    \frac{|C|}{|G|}
    \frac{C_{\mathrm{cls}}(A,\tau;G)\Lambda_*^n}{n},
  \]
  and
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_{n,C}}{\lambda^n}
    =
    \frac{|C|}{|G|}\log N+\mathcal M_C(A,\tau;G)+O(N^{-1}).
  \]
  For each conjugacy class \(C\subset G\), the corresponding
  class-indexed constant is
  \[
    \mathcal M_C(A,\tau;G)
    =
    \frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)
    +\frac{|C|}{|G|}
      \sum_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}
        \overline{\chi_\varrho(C)}\,\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1}).
  \]
  The deviations
  \[
    \Delta_C(A,\tau;G)
    :=\mathcal M_C(A,\tau;G)-\frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)
  \]
  have the following ordinary character-orthogonality readout:
  \[
    \Pi_\sigma(\lambda^{-1})
    =
    \sum_{C\subset G}\Delta_C(A,\tau;G)\chi_\sigma(C)
    \qquad(\sigma\neq\mathbf 1).
  \]
  These deviations also satisfy the Parseval identity
  \[
    \sum_{C\subset G}\frac{|G|}{|C|}\abs{\Delta_C(A,\tau;G)}^2
    =
    \sum_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}
      \abs{\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})}^2.
  \]
  Here the forward expansion uses the coefficient
  \(\overline{\chi_\varrho(C)}\), equivalently
  \(\chi_\varrho(C^{-1})\) by unitarity, while the inverse readout pairs
  it with \(\chi_\sigma(C)\) in the Hermitian class-function inner
  product.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Structural finite-group consequence: quotient
  functoriality and the abelian shadow]
  \label{thm:intro-quotient-shadow}
  Assume the hypotheses of Main Theorem~II, so the class deviations
  \(\Delta_C(A,\tau;G)\) are defined. Quotient maps \(q:G\twoheadrightarrow Q\)
  intertwine class-function logarithms and primitive series:
  \[
    \mathcal L_{q^\ast f}^{G}(z)=\mathcal L_f^{Q}(z),
    \qquad
    \Pi_{q^\ast f}^{G}(z)=\Pi_f^{Q}(z)
    \qquad(|z|<\lambda^{-1}).
  \]
  If
  \[
    \mathcal B_{A,\tau;G}(C):=\frac{|G|}{|C|}\Delta_C(A,\tau;G),
  \]
  then \(\mathcal B_{A,\tau;G}\) splits orthogonally into its
  one-dimensional Peter--Weyl part and its higher-dimensional
  Peter--Weyl part. The span
  of all cyclic quotient observables is exactly the one-dimensional
  sector; hence their aggregate projection is the abelian shadow and is
  orthogonal to the genuine non-abelian defect. In particular, under
  \(\eta<\lambda\), the full primitive class profile at the Perron point
  is generated by cyclic quotient shadows if and only if every
  higher-dimensional primitive channel
  \(\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})\) vanishes.
\end{corollary}

An explicit \(S_3\)-extension separates the two obstruction types:
the sign-character trace vanishes but its primitive value is non-zero by
the quadratic Adams mechanism, while the standard two-dimensional
channel gives the certified higher-dimensional defect. This is a
finite-state obstruction model, not a new periodic-orbit distribution
theorem.

The quotient/shadow consequence is proved in
\Cref{thm:quotient-functoriality,%
cor:abelian-cyclic-shadow,%
prop:canonical-cyclic-lift-root-layer,%
thm:abelian-shadow-defect,%
thm:cyclic-detection-boundary,%
thm:s3-certified-nonabelian-defect}.

\subsection{Auxiliary finite-matrix inputs}

We record only the scalar inputs needed for notation and comparison.
They are proved later in \Cref{sec:finite-part}. After removing the
Perron factor one obtains the reduced determinant
\[
  C(A):=\det\nolimits'(I-A/\lambda)^{-1}
\]
and the reduced spectral polynomial
\[
  F_A(t):=\frac{\det(I-tA/\lambda)}{1-t}
  =\prod_{j=2}^d\left(1-\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}t\right),
\]
where \(\mu_2,\dots,\mu_d\) are the non-Perron eigenvalues. The
Abel-regularised finite-part constant
\[
  \FP(A):=\lim_{r\uparrow 1}\bigl(P_A(r)+\log(1-r)\bigr),
  \qquad
  P_A(r):=\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)\lambda^{-n}r^n,
\]
measures the deviation of the primitive orbit sum from the harmonic
series.

\begin{proposition}[Auxiliary scalar finite-part computation at the
  Perron pole]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-part}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative real matrix with Perron root
  \(\lambda=\rho(A)>1\), reduced determinant \(C(A)\), and M\"obius
  coefficients \(p_n(A)\) defined by \eqref{eq:primitive-mobius}. Then
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{k\ge 2}\frac{\mu(k)}{k}
        \log\det(I-\lambda^{-k}A)^{-1}.
  \]
  Equivalently,
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)
        \bigl(\lambda^{-n}+\log(1-\lambda^{-n})\bigr).
  \]
  In particular, the orbit-Mertens constant
  \(\mathcal M(A)=\gamma+\FP(A)\) satisfies
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_n(A)}{\lambda^n}
    =\log N+\mathcal M(A)+o(1).
  \]
\end{proposition}

This is the trivial-channel scalar input singled out in
\Cref{sec:intro-scope-of-results};
\Cref{thm:intro-finite-part} is proved in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form,cor:finite-part-mertens-asymptotic}.

The second scalar component is a separate canonical cyclic-lift
comparison, related to inverse spectral questions for SFTs
\cite{LindMarcus1995}. It is used only as a fixed-matrix auxiliary
reconstruction statement, and its proof package is postponed to
\Cref{app:cyclic-lift-package}.

\begin{proposition}[Auxiliary fixed-matrix cyclic-lift recovery
  of the reduced spectrum]
  \label{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix. The input
  is the branch-lifted logarithmic cyclic-lift record
  \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}_{q\ge 1}\) from
  \Cref{def:spectral-fingerprints}, not merely the exponentiated
  determinant constants
  \(\{C_\lambda(A^{\langle q\rangle})\}_{q\ge 1}\). Then this fixed
  sequence determines the reduced spectral polynomial \(F_A\) and hence the
  relative non-Perron spectrum
  \(\{\mu_j/\lambda\}_{j=2}^d\) with algebraic multiplicity. If
  \(\lambda\) is supplied as additional data, then the unnormalised
  non-Perron spectrum \(\{\mu_j\}_{j=2}^d\) is also recovered.
  Moreover, writing
  \[
    u_q(A):=\sum_{j=2}^d\left(\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}\right)^q,
    \qquad
    \Lambda_{\mathrm{rel}}(A):=\Lambda(A)/\lambda,
  \]
  one has
  \[
    \Psi_q(A)=u_q(A)+R_q(A),
    \qquad
    \abs{R_q(A)}
    \le
    \frac{d-1}{2(1-\Lambda_{\mathrm{rel}}(A))}
    \Lambda_{\mathrm{rel}}(A)^{2q},
  \]
  and hence
  \[
    \limsup_{q\to\infty}\abs{\Psi_q(A)}^{1/q}
    =
    \Lambda(A)/\lambda.
  \]
\end{proposition}

\begin{corollary}[Elementary fixed-layer DFT corollary]
  \label{cor:intro-one-layer-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix. If
  \(q\ge d\), then for this fixed \(q\) the single root-of-unity layer
  \(\{F_A(\omega_q^j)\}_{0\le j\le q-1}\) determines every coefficient
  of the reduced spectral polynomial
  \[
    F_A(t)=\sum_{n=0}^{d-1}a_nt^n,
  \]
  by the finite Fourier formula
  \[
    a_n=\frac{1}{q}\sum_{j=0}^{q-1}
      F_A(\omega_q^j)\omega_q^{-jn}
    \qquad (0\le n\le d-1).
  \]
\end{corollary}

The proof combines M\"obius inversion on multiples with Newton's
identities, while the fixed-layer statement is ordinary finite Fourier
inversion; the details are collected in
\Cref{app:cyclic-lift-package,%
thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-spectrum-identifiability,%
thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-single-q-torsion-reconstruction}.
The terminology of cyclic, torsion, and exponential spectral
fingerprints is fixed in \Cref{def:spectral-fingerprints}.

\subsection{Relation to existing work}

The dynamical \(\zeta\)-function for SFTs goes back to
Artin--Mazur \cite{ArtinMazur1965PeriodicPoints} and Bowen--Lanford
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom}. The prime-orbit
asymptotic for shifts of finite type is classical: Parry
\cite{Parry1983PrimeOrbit} proved the analogue of the prime number
theorem for closed orbits of SFTs and their suspensions. Lalley
\cite{Lalley1987DistributionPeriodicOrbits} developed the broader
periodic-orbit distribution theory for symbolic and Axiom~A systems,
and the existence of Mertens-type asymptotics for Axiom~A flows was
established by Sharp \cite{Sharp1991Mertens}. For zeta functions
attached to broader group-action orbit data, Lind's \(\ZZ^d\)-action
zeta function is the standard reference \cite{Lind1996ZetaZdActions}.
Against this background, the present paper contributes a
determinant-level primitive reconstruction theorem for one fixed
finite-group cocycle: Adams--M\"obius inversion recovers primitive
Peter--Weyl channels from twisted determinants, and under
\(\eta<\lambda\) the same framework extracts the class-indexed
constants \(\mathcal M_C(A,\tau;G)\). The scalar finite-part identity
at the Perron pole supplies the trivial-channel normalisation and a
direct comparison with the classical Mertens constant.

The transfer-operator approach is developed systematically in
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic,%
ParryPollicott1990Zeta,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer}.
Twisted determinant and \(L\)-function constructions for dynamical
systems and finite-group extensions also appear in the twisted
Perron--Frobenius framework of Adachi--Sunada
\cite{AdachiSunada1987TwistedPF}. These references place the paper on
the determinant and twisted-zeta side of the subject rather than in the
broader transfer-operator literature.

Twisted periodic-point formulas and closed-orbit identities also appear
in Fried's work on periodic points with twisted coefficients
\cite{Fried1983PeriodicPointsTwistedCoefficients} and homological
identities for closed orbits
\cite{Fried1983HomologicalIdentitiesClosedOrbits}. These references are
closer to the present determinant-level use of twisted coefficients than
the broader transfer-operator literature and provide useful antecedents
for the twisted determinant formalism used here. Their focus, however,
is not the finite-state primitive extraction carried out below: the
present paper fixes one finite-group cocycle, tracks the repetition
effect \(g\mapsto g^m\) through Adams operations, and then applies
M\"obius cancellation at the class-function level to recover primitive
Peter--Weyl channels from twisted determinant data. For
skew-product extensions and compact-group cocycles one should also
compare Parry's compact Lie group skew-product paper
\cite{Parry1997SkewProductsCompactLie} and the
  compact-group Liv\v{s}ic theorem of Parry--Pollicott
  \cite{ParryPollicott1997LivsicCompactLie}. The leading Chebotarev-type
  asymptotics for finite extensions are classical: Parry--Pollicott treat
  Axiom~A Galois coverings \cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev}, whose
  title uses the spelling ``Chebotarov'' while proving an analogue of the
  Chebotarev density principle, and Noorani--Parry prove the
  corresponding theorem for finite homogeneous extensions of shifts
  \cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts}. Assuming the twisted gap
  \(\eta<\lambda\), the present paper identifies, for each conjugacy
  class \(C\subset G\), the secondary harmonic constant term
  \(\mathcal M_C(A,\tau;G)\) for one fixed cocycle in terms of twisted
  determinants. This is a finite-state classwise constant formula after
  the leading Chebotarev term has been separated, not a new
  leading-density theorem or a new Tauberian Mertens theorem of the kind
  proved by Sharp \cite{Sharp1991Mertens}. For homogeneous-extension
  background beyond the leading asymptotic,
  including ergodicity and weak-mixing criteria with applications to
  Markov shifts, compare Noorani
  \cite{Noorani1997HomogeneousExtensionsMarkov}. For
  dynamical analogues of Bauer's theorem and Artin reciprocity for closed
  orbits of skew products compare
  \cite{ParryPollicott2008BauerSkewProducts,%
  PollicottSharp2008ArtinReciprocitySkewProducts}. For orbit counting in
homology classes and related constrained equidistribution problems
compare
\cite{KatsudaSunada1988HomologyClosedGeodesics,%
KatsudaSunada1990ClosedOrbitsHomology,%
Sharp1993ClosedOrbitsHomologyAnosov,%
PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev}. Recent work of Coles
\cite{Coles2025VassilievWritheAxiomA} studies asymptotic averages of
knot-theoretic observables over periodic orbits of Axiom~A flows; that
is a geometric orbit-distribution problem rather than a finite-group
primitive inversion problem. For the non-abelian Liv\v{s}ic context
compare \cite{Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian,%
ParryPollicott1997LivsicCompactLie,%
BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions}. The determinant-level novelty
here is the explicit Adams--M\"obius extraction and readout of the
constant term from twisted finite-state data.

Finite group actions on SFTs provide the closest symbolic-dynamics
context. Adler--Kitchens--Marcus
\cite{AdlerKitchensMarcus1985FiniteGroupActions}, Fiebig
\cite{Fiebig1993PeriodicFiniteGroupActions}, and Silver--Williams
\cite{SilverWilliams2005InvariantFiniteGroupActions} study finite
actions, periodic data, quotient systems, and reduced-shift invariants.
Boyle--Schmieding
\cite{BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions} and
Boyle--Carlsen--Eilers \cite{BoyleCarlsenEilers2020FlowEquivalenceGSFT}
analyse finite group extensions through obstruction theory,
\(K\)-theory, and equivariant flow equivalence. Those works address
existence, classification, and periodic-data invariants. Here the cocycle
\(\tau:E_A\to G\) and its twisted matrices are fixed, and the question is
inverse-determinantal: which primitive Peter--Weyl channels and which
class-indexed constants \(\mathcal M_C(A,\tau;G)\) are forced by the
twisted determinants? The new
finite-group step is to undo repetitions \(g\mapsto g^m\) by
Adams--M\"obius inversion. The \(S_3\)-example later in the paper makes
this increment concrete by separating a one-dimensional quadratic Adams
effect from an independent higher-dimensional non-abelian defect inside
one explicit finite-state model.

Tauberian refinements and closed-orbit asymptotics are treated in
\cite{Pollicott1992AgmonTauberian} and the monograph
\cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}. For the functorial orbit-counting
perspective underlying Section~\ref{sec:shadows} one should also compare
\cite{PakapongpunWard2009FunctorialOrbit}.
For graph coverings and graph \(L\)-functions, the closest determinant
comparison is the Ihara--Bass/Hashimoto framework for finite graphs and
regular coverings. Hashimoto's determinant formulas and
graph Artin \(L\)-functions
\cite{Hashimoto1989ZetaFiniteGraphs,%
Hashimoto1990ZetaLFunctionsFiniteGraphs,%
Hashimoto1992ArtinDensityPrimeCycles}, Sunada's \(L\)-function
viewpoint and finite-graph determinant treatment
\cite{Sunada1986LFunctionsGeometry,KotaniSunada2000ZetaFiniteGraphs},
the Stark--Terras covering theory of graph Artin \(L\)-functions
\cite{StarkTerras1996FiniteGraphsCoverings,%
StarkTerras2000FiniteGraphsCoveringsII,%
StarkTerras2007FiniteGraphsCoveringsIII}, and the covering
factorisations of Mizuno--Sato
\cite{MizunoSato2000ZetaGraphCoverings} all encode prime-cycle data of
finite graph coverings by determinant or \(L\)-function formalisms
attached to representation data. Directed or weighted variants continue
this line
\cite{Bass1992Ihara,%
MizunoSato2001ZetaDigraphs,%
Horton2007IharaDigraphs,%
ChoeKwakParkSato2007Bartholdi,%
Sato2008WeightedBartholdiCoverings}. These works are directly adjacent
to the present paper at the determinant level, but their main outputs
are factorisation, covering, and prime-cycle density statements for
graph zeta or graph \(L\)-functions. The present paper instead fixes one
finite-group cocycle on an SFT and uses Adams operations together with
M\"obius inversion to recover primitive Peter--Weyl channels and the
class-indexed constants \(\mathcal M_C(A,\tau;G)\) from the twisted
determinant family itself.
For recent perspectives on dynamical zeta functions and dynamical
determinants beyond the classical symbolic-dynamics sources, compare
Baladi's modern monograph \cite{Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}, the
parameter-regularity results of J\'ez\'equel
\cite{Jezequel2019ParameterRegularity}, and Shen's work on twisted
Ruelle zeta functions on locally symmetric spaces
\cite{Shen2023ComplexValuedAnalyticTorsion}. Our results stay in the
finite-state symbolic setting but fit naturally into this determinant
programme.

The present finite-state calculation should also be distinguished from
the orbit-counting and reciprocity literature for skew products and
homology classes. Lalley's distribution results for symbolic and
Axiom~A flows \cite{Lalley1987DistributionPeriodicOrbits},
Katsuda--Sunada's homology-class orbit counting
\cite{KatsudaSunada1988HomologyClosedGeodesics,%
KatsudaSunada1990ClosedOrbitsHomology}, Parry--Pollicott's
Bauer-type theorem for skew products
\cite{ParryPollicott2008BauerSkewProducts}, and Pollicott--Sharp's
Artin reciprocity addendum
\cite{PollicottSharp2008ArtinReciprocitySkewProducts} address density,
equidistribution, or reciprocity questions for global orbit classes.
Here the cocycle is fixed and finite-state, and the target is not a new
density theorem but determinant-level primitive recovery: extracting the
primitive Peter--Weyl channels and the class-indexed constants
\(\mathcal M_C(A,\tau;G)\) from its twisted determinant data.

\subsection{Plan of the paper}

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation for primitive orbit counts,
reduced determinants, cyclic lifts, and finite-group extensions.
\Cref{sec:chebotarev} develops the Adams--M\"obius mechanism for
finite-group extensions, derives the primitive Peter--Weyl determinant
series, and establishes the class Mertens theorem under a twisted
spectral gap. \Cref{sec:shadows} proves quotient functoriality,
identifies the abelian shadow and the exact boundary of cyclic
detection inside the finite-group Peter--Weyl decomposition, and closes
with the \(S_3\)-model separating the quadratic sign-channel Adams effect
from the standard-channel non-abelian defect. \Cref{sec:finite-part}
then proves the auxiliary scalar finite-part formula, the branch-lifted
cyclic-lift reconstruction of the reduced spectral polynomial \(F_A\),
equivalently of the relative non-Perron spectrum, and the one-layer
discrete Fourier corollary used as the canonical cyclic-lift comparison
case.
The appendices record
holomorphic variation and truncation for the scalar finite part
(\Cref{app:variation}) and mark the boundary separating this article
from independent scalar arithmetic and growing-family rigidity packages
(\Cref{app:arithmetic,app:rigidity}).
