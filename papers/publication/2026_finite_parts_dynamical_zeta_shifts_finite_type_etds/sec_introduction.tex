\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

The problem addressed in this paper is primitive orbit reconstruction
for finite-group extensions of mixing subshifts of finite type. Let
\((X_A^+,\sigma)\) be a mixing subshift with primitive adjacency matrix
\(A\), let \(\lambda=\rho(A)\), and let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle
into a finite group. For every irreducible representation \(\varrho\)
of \(G\), the twisted transition matrix \(A_\varrho\) records periodic
labels through its traces. These traces are periodic data. The
primitive labels are subtler: when a primitive orbit with label \(g\)
is repeated \(m\) times, its label becomes \(g^m\). Thus primitive
extraction is not ordinary M\"obius inversion in the non-abelian
setting; it must be coupled to the Adams operations
\(\psi^m\phi(g)=\phi(g^m)\) on class functions.

The centre of the paper is the Adams--M\"obius passage from twisted
determinant data to primitive Peter--Weyl channels. The finite-group
spine has two steps: Main Theorem~I linearises twisted determinants and
performs primitive Adams--M\"obius inversion; Main Theorem~II evaluates
the resulting non-trivial primitive series at \(z=\lambda^{-1}\), under
the explicit gap \(\eta<\lambda\), and obtains conjugacy-class Mertens
constants. The character-orthogonality formula is the readout of these
constants.

The scalar finite part of \(\zeta_A\) and the cyclic root-of-unity layer
have narrower roles. The finite part supplies only the trivial-channel
normalisation \(\mathcal M(A)\). Root-of-unity sampling belongs to the
canonical cyclic lift, where every edge is labelled by the generator of
\(\ZZ/q\ZZ\); it is not the same as taking arbitrary cyclic quotients of
the fixed cocycle. The logical direction of the proofs is
\[
  \begin{array}{c}
    \text{twisted determinants}
    \xrightarrow{\text{trace}}
    \text{periodic Peter--Weyl channels} \\[2pt]
    \xrightarrow{\text{Adams--M\"obius}}
    \text{primitive Peter--Weyl channels} \\[2pt]
    \xrightarrow[\eta<\lambda]{z=\lambda^{-1}}
    \text{class Mertens constants}
    \xrightarrow{\text{orthogonality}}
    \text{primitive class profile}.
  \end{array}
\]
Here the Adams--M\"obius arrow is the primitive extraction step; the last
arrow is ordinary character orthogonality after the Perron-point values
have been constructed. The formal scope of these packages and of the
auxiliary scalar section is recorded in
\Cref{prop:intro-scope-of-results}.

Main Theorem~I is the package
\Cref{lem:twisted-trace-identity,thm:class-function-linearisation,%
cor:class-function-adams-mobius,cor:primitive-peter-weyl-determinant,%
cor:primitive-peter-weyl-trace}.
Main Theorem~II is the package
\Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit,prop:class-mertens-deviations,%
thm:class-mertens-fourier}. The quotient and cyclic-detection results
of \Cref{sec:shadows} are structural consequences of these packages.
The scalar finite-part and root-of-unity statements are auxiliary inputs
proved later. The scalar dynamical \(\zeta\)-function
\[
  \zeta_A(z)
  =\exp\!\left(\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Tr(A^n)}{n}z^n\right)
  =\det(I-zA)^{-1}
\]
enters only through the trivial representation channel. Its rationality
and orbit-counting role are classical, going back to
Bowen--Lanford \cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta} and Manning
\cite{Manning1971Axiom}, with further development through Ruelle's
thermodynamic formalism
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic} and the
Parry--Pollicott transfer-operator programme
\cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}. The finite-part proposition below is a
classical Euler-product/M\"obius computation at this channel, while the
one-layer DFT corollary is polynomial coefficient recovery from enough
root-of-unity samples. Neither statement generates non-abelian primitive
information.

\subsection{Finite-group main theorem spine}

Let \(\tau:E_A\to G\) be a cocycle into a finite group, and let
\(A_\varrho\) be the twisted transition matrices. For a class function
\(\phi:G\to\CC\) write
\[
  T_{\phi,n}:=\sum_{x\in\operatorname{Fix}(\sigma^n)}
    \phi(\tau_n(x)),
  \qquad
  \mathcal L_\phi(z):=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{T_{\phi,n}}{n}z^n,
\]
and
\[
  \Pi_\phi(z):=\sum_{\gamma\in\cP}\phi(\tau(\gamma))z^{|\gamma|}.
\]
For a conjugacy class \(C\subset G\), let \(p_{n,C}\) denote the number
of primitive orbits of length \(n\) whose cocycle label lies in \(C\).
The scalar orbit-Mertens constant \(\mathcal M(A)\) is the
trivial-channel constant defined in \Cref{def:abel-finite-part}; the
finite-matrix formula behind this normalisation is the auxiliary
calculation of \Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form}.

\begin{proposition}[Scope of the theorem packages]
  \label{prop:intro-scope-of-results}
  The argument has three logically separate parts.
  \begin{enumerate}
  \item Main Theorem~I is the finite-group determinant and
    Adams--M\"obius package on the open Perron disc
    \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\).
  \item Main Theorem~II begins only after imposing \(\lambda>1\) and
    the explicit twisted spectral gap
    \[
      \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda;
    \]
    this hypothesis is used to evaluate the non-trivial primitive
    Peter--Weyl channels at \(z=\lambda^{-1}\) and hence define the
    class Mertens constants.
  \item The scalar results of \Cref{sec:finite-part} are auxiliary
    fixed-matrix inputs: \Cref{thm:intro-finite-part} supplies the
    trivial-channel normalisation \(\mathcal M(A)\),
    \Cref{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction} recovers the reduced
    spectrum from the full canonical cyclic-lift record of one fixed
    matrix \(A\), and \Cref{cor:intro-one-layer-reconstruction} is the
    one-layer Fourier recovery for one fixed \(q\ge d\).
  \end{enumerate}
  All quantities \(T_{\phi,n}\), \(\mathcal L_\phi\), and \(\Pi_\phi\)
  are deterministic observables of the fixed cocycle; the paper does
  not introduce a separate detector-response model or a general
  geometric reconstruction theorem. Any further identification of an
  external response or shell observable with these fixed-matrix records
  would be an additional hypothesis, not a theorem proved here.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
  Item~(1) is the content of \Cref{thm:intro-finite-group};
  item~(2) is the hypothesis and Perron-point conclusion of
  \Cref{thm:intro-class-mertens}; and item~(3) restates the roles of
  \Cref{thm:intro-finite-part,thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction,%
  cor:intro-one-layer-reconstruction}. The final sentence records the
  common deterministic definition of the generating functions just
  introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Main Theorem I: Adams--M\"obius primitive
  determinant linearisation]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-group}
  \label{thm:intro-adams-mobius}
  For every irreducible representation \(\varrho\in\Irr(G)\) and every
  \(n\ge 1\),
  \[
    \Tr(A_\varrho^n)
    =
    \sum_{x\in\operatorname{Fix}(\sigma^n)}
      \chi_\varrho(\tau_n(x)).
  \]
  Hence, for every class function \(\phi:G\to\CC\) and every
  \(|z|<\lambda^{-1}\),
  \[
    \mathcal L_\phi(z)
    =
    \sum_{\varrho\in\Irr(G)}
      \widehat\phi(\varrho)
      \log\det(I-zA_\varrho)^{-1},
  \]
  where \(\widehat\phi(\varrho)\) is the character coefficient of
  \(\phi\). Moreover,
  \[
    \Pi_\phi(z)
    =
    \sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}\,
      \mathcal L_{\psi^m\phi}(z^m).
  \]
  Equivalently, if
  \[
    \Pi_\varrho(z):=\Pi_{\chi_\varrho}(z)
      =\sum_{n\ge 1}\pi_{n,\varrho}(A)z^n
  \]
  and
  \(\psi^m\chi_\varrho
    =\sum_{\sigma\in\Irr(G)}a_{\varrho,\sigma}^{(m)}\chi_\sigma\),
  then
  \[
    \Pi_\varrho(z)
    =
    \sum_{m\ge 1}\frac{\mu(m)}{m}
      \sum_{\sigma\in\Irr(G)}
        a_{\varrho,\sigma}^{(m)}
        \log\det(I-z^mA_\sigma)^{-1}.
  \]
  Coefficient extraction gives the primitive Peter--Weyl trace formula
  \[
    \pi_{n,\varrho}(A)
    =
    \frac{1}{n}\sum_{m\mid n}\mu(m)
      \sum_{\sigma\in\Irr(G)}
        a_{\varrho,\sigma}^{(m)}
        \Tr(A_\sigma^{n/m}).
  \]
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Main Theorem II: class Mertens constants under
  the twisted gap]
  \label{thm:intro-class-mertens}
  Assume \(\lambda>1\) and the explicit twisted spectral gap
  \[
    \eta:=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda,
    \qquad
    \Lambda_*:=\max\{\Lambda(A),\eta,\lambda^{1/2}\}.
  \]
  Here \(\Lambda(A)\) denotes the maximum modulus of the non-Perron
  eigenvalues of the base matrix \(A\).
  Then for every conjugacy class \(C\subset G\),
  \[
    p_{n,C}
    =
    \frac{|C|}{|G|}\frac{\lambda^n}{n}
    +O\!\left(\frac{\Lambda_*^n}{n}\right),
  \]
  and
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_{n,C}}{\lambda^n}
    =
    \frac{|C|}{|G|}\log N+\mathcal M_C(A)+O(N^{-1}).
  \]
  The constant is
  \[
    \mathcal M_C(A)
    =
    \frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)
    +\frac{|C|}{|G|}
      \sum_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}
        \chi_\varrho(C^{-1})\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1}).
  \]
  The deviations
  \[
    \Delta_C(A):=\mathcal M_C(A)-\frac{|C|}{|G|}\mathcal M(A)
  \]
  have the following ordinary character-orthogonality readout:
  \[
    \Pi_\sigma(\lambda^{-1})
    =
    \sum_{C\subset G}\Delta_C(A)\chi_\sigma(C)
    \qquad(\sigma\neq\mathbf 1).
  \]
  These deviations also satisfy the Parseval identity
  \[
    \sum_{C\subset G}\frac{|G|}{|C|}\abs{\Delta_C(A)}^2
    =
    \sum_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}
      \abs{\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})}^2.
  \]
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Structural finite-group consequence: quotient
  functoriality and the abelian shadow]
  \label{thm:intro-quotient-shadow}
  Assume the hypotheses of Main Theorem~II, so the class deviations
  \(\Delta_C(A)\) are defined. Quotient maps \(q:G\twoheadrightarrow Q\)
  intertwine class-function logarithms and primitive series:
  \[
    \mathcal L_{q^\ast f}^{G}(z)=\mathcal L_f^{Q}(z),
    \qquad
    \Pi_{q^\ast f}^{G}(z)=\Pi_f^{Q}(z)
    \qquad(|z|<\lambda^{-1}).
  \]
  If
  \[
    \mathcal B_A(C):=\frac{|G|}{|C|}\Delta_C(A),
  \]
  then \(\mathcal B_A\) splits orthogonally into its one-dimensional
  Peter--Weyl part and its higher-dimensional Peter--Weyl part. The span
  of all cyclic quotient observables is exactly the one-dimensional
  sector; hence their aggregate projection is the abelian shadow and is
  orthogonal to the genuine non-abelian defect. In particular, the full
  primitive class profile is generated by cyclic quotient shadows if and
  only if every higher-dimensional primitive channel
  \(\Pi_\varrho(\lambda^{-1})\) vanishes.
\end{corollary}

An explicit \(S_3\)-extension separates the two obstruction types:
the sign-character trace vanishes but its primitive value is non-zero by
the quadratic Adams mechanism, while the standard two-dimensional
channel gives the certified higher-dimensional defect.

The quotient/shadow consequence is proved in
\Cref{thm:quotient-functoriality,%
cor:abelian-cyclic-shadow,%
prop:canonical-cyclic-lift-root-layer,%
thm:abelian-shadow-defect,%
thm:cyclic-detection-boundary,%
thm:s3-certified-nonabelian-defect}.

\subsection{Auxiliary finite-matrix inputs}

We record only the scalar inputs needed for notation and comparison.
They are proved later in \Cref{sec:finite-part}. After removing the
Perron factor one obtains the reduced determinant
\[
  C(A):=\det\nolimits'(I-A/\lambda)^{-1}
\]
and the reduced spectral polynomial
\[
  F_A(t):=\frac{\det(I-tA/\lambda)}{1-t}
  =\prod_{j=2}^d\left(1-\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}t\right),
\]
where \(\mu_2,\dots,\mu_d\) are the non-Perron eigenvalues. The
Abel-regularised finite-part constant
\[
  \FP(A):=\lim_{r\uparrow 1}\bigl(P_A(r)+\log(1-r)\bigr),
  \qquad
  P_A(r):=\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)\lambda^{-n}r^n,
\]
measures the deviation of the primitive orbit sum from the harmonic
series.

\begin{proposition}[Auxiliary scalar finite-part computation at the
  Perron pole]
  \label{thm:intro-finite-part}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative real matrix with Perron root
  \(\lambda=\rho(A)>1\), reduced determinant \(C(A)\), and M\"obius
  coefficients \(p_n(A)\) defined by \eqref{eq:primitive-mobius}. Then
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{k\ge 2}\frac{\mu(k)}{k}
        \log\det(I-\lambda^{-k}A)^{-1}.
  \]
  Equivalently,
  \[
    \FP(A)
    =\log C(A)
      +\sum_{n\ge 1}p_n(A)
        \bigl(\lambda^{-n}+\log(1-\lambda^{-n})\bigr).
  \]
  In particular, the orbit-Mertens constant
  \(\mathcal M(A)=\gamma+\FP(A)\) satisfies
  \[
    \sum_{n\le N}\frac{p_n(A)}{\lambda^n}
    =\log N+\mathcal M(A)+o(1).
  \]
\end{proposition}

This is the trivial-channel scalar input singled out in
\Cref{prop:intro-scope-of-results};
\Cref{thm:intro-finite-part} is proved in
\Cref{thm:finite-part-primitive-closed-form,cor:finite-part-mertens-asymptotic}.

The second scalar component addresses spectral reconstruction from
root-of-unity data, a theme connected to inverse spectral problems for
SFTs \cite{LindMarcus1995}. The useful inverse-spectral part is the
fixed-matrix recovery of the reduced spectrum from the full canonical
cyclic-lift record. The single-layer statement is separated as the
elementary discrete Fourier corollary for that reset model at one fixed
value of \(q\); its role is exactly the fixed-matrix auxiliary one
recorded in \Cref{prop:intro-scope-of-results}. No local inverse theorem
for a parameter family is asserted here: the reconstruction is from one
fixed scalar record attached to one fixed matrix.

\begin{proposition}[Auxiliary fixed-matrix cyclic-lift recovery
  of the reduced spectrum with explicit remainder]
  \label{thm:intro-cyclic-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix, and set
  \[
    \Psi_q(A):=\log q+\log C_\lambda(A^{\langle q\rangle}),
  \]
  where \(A^{\langle q\rangle}=A\otimes P_q\) is the cyclic lift and
  \(C_\lambda\) denotes the reduced constant at the simple eigenvalue
  \(\lambda=\rho(A)\). Then this fixed sequence
  \(\{\Psi_q(A)\}_{q\ge 1}\) canonically determines the reduced
  spectral polynomial \(F_A\) of the same matrix \(A\), and hence the
  non-Perron spectrum of that fixed matrix with algebraic
  multiplicity. Moreover, with
  \[
    u_q(A):=\sum_{j=2}^d\left(\frac{\mu_j}{\lambda}\right)^q,
    \qquad
    \Lambda_{\mathrm{rel}}(A):=\Lambda(A)/\lambda,
  \]
  one has
  \[
    \Psi_q(A)=u_q(A)+R_q(A),
    \qquad
    \abs{R_q(A)}
    \le
    \frac{d-1}{2(1-\Lambda_{\mathrm{rel}}(A))}
    \Lambda_{\mathrm{rel}}(A)^{2q},
  \]
  and hence
  \[
    \limsup_{q\to\infty}\abs{\Psi_q(A)}^{1/q}
    =
    \Lambda(A)/\lambda.
  \]
\end{proposition}

\begin{corollary}[Elementary fixed-layer DFT corollary]
  \label{cor:intro-one-layer-reconstruction}
  Let \(A\) be a primitive non-negative \(d\times d\) matrix. If
  \(q\ge d\), then for this fixed \(q\) the single root-of-unity layer
  \(\{F_A(\omega_q^j)\}_{0\le j\le q-1}\) determines every coefficient
  of the reduced spectral polynomial
  \[
    F_A(t)=\sum_{n=0}^{d-1}a_nt^n,
  \]
  by the finite Fourier formula
  \[
    a_n=\frac{1}{q}\sum_{j=0}^{q-1}
      F_A(\omega_q^j)\omega_q^{-jn}
    \qquad (0\le n\le d-1).
  \]
\end{corollary}

The proof combines M\"obius inversion on multiples with Newton's
identities for the cyclic-lift spectrum proposition, and finite Fourier
inversion for \Cref{cor:intro-one-layer-reconstruction}; see
\Cref{thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-spectrum-identifiability,%
thm:finite-part-cyclic-lift-single-q-torsion-reconstruction,%
cor:finite-part-cyclic-lift-zeta-torsion-determines-spectrum}.
The terminology of cyclic, torsion, and exponential spectral
fingerprints is fixed in \Cref{def:spectral-fingerprints}.

\subsection{Relation to existing work}

The dynamical \(\zeta\)-function for SFTs goes back to
Artin--Mazur \cite{ArtinMazur1965PeriodicPoints} and Bowen--Lanford
\cite{BowenLanford1970Zeta,Manning1971Axiom}. The prime-orbit
asymptotic for shifts of finite type is classical: Parry
\cite{Parry1983PrimeOrbit} proved the analogue of the prime number
theorem for closed orbits of SFTs and their suspensions. The existence
of Mertens-type asymptotics for Axiom~A flows was established by Sharp
\cite{Sharp1991Mertens}. For zeta functions attached to broader
group-action orbit data, Lind's \(\ZZ^d\)-action zeta function is the
standard reference \cite{Lind1996ZetaZdActions}. The present paper does
not claim a new prime-orbit theorem. Its primary contribution is the
Adams--M\"obius reconstruction of primitive Peter--Weyl channels from
twisted determinant data. The scalar finite-part identity at the Perron
pole is included as an explicit finite-matrix input for the classwise
constants and as a checkable comparison with the classical Mertens
constant.

The transfer-operator approach is developed systematically in
\cite{Ruelle1976ZetaExpanding,Ruelle1978Thermodynamic,%
ParryPollicott1990Zeta,Baladi2000PositiveTransfer}.
Twisted determinant and \(L\)-function constructions for dynamical
systems and finite-group extensions go back at least to the twisted
Perron--Frobenius framework of Adachi--Sunada
\cite{AdachiSunada1987TwistedPF}. The leading Chebotarev asymptotics for
finite extensions are classical: Parry--Pollicott treat Axiom~A Galois
coverings \cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev}, and Noorani--Parry prove
the corresponding theorem for finite homogeneous extensions of shifts
\cite{NooraniParry1992ChebotarevShifts}; see also
\cite{PollicottSharp2007Chebotarev,Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian}. The
present paper does not reprove those leading density theorems by a new
Tauberian method. Instead it isolates the finite determinant algebra
needed after the leading term: primitive class constants and their
Peter--Weyl coordinates are recovered from twisted determinant data by
Adams--M\"obius inversion.

Finite group actions on SFTs provide the closest symbolic-dynamics
context. Adler--Kitchens--Marcus
\cite{AdlerKitchensMarcus1985FiniteGroupActions}, Fiebig
\cite{Fiebig1993PeriodicFiniteGroupActions}, and Silver--Williams
\cite{SilverWilliams2005InvariantFiniteGroupActions} study finite
actions, periodic data, quotient systems, and reduced-shift invariants.
Boyle--Schmieding
\cite{BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions} and
Boyle--Carlsen--Eilers \cite{BoyleCarlsenEilers2020FlowEquivalenceGSFT}
analyse finite group extensions through obstruction theory,
\(K\)-theory, and equivariant flow equivalence. Those works address
existence, classification, and periodic-data invariants. Here the cocycle
\(\tau:E_A\to G\) and its twisted matrices are fixed, and the question is
inverse-determinantal: which primitive Peter--Weyl channels and
classwise constants are forced by the twisted determinants? The new
finite-group step is to undo repetitions \(g\mapsto g^m\) by
Adams--M\"obius inversion.

Tauberian refinements and closed-orbit asymptotics are treated in
\cite{Pollicott1992AgmonTauberian} and the monograph
\cite{ParryPollicott1990Zeta}. For the functorial orbit-counting
perspective underlying Section~\ref{sec:shadows} one should also compare
\cite{PakapongpunWard2009FunctorialOrbit}.
For graph coverings and graph \(L\)-functions one should compare the
Ihara--Bass and Hashimoto determinant formulas, the Stark--Terras theory
of graph coverings and Artin \(L\)-functions, and directed or weighted
variants
\cite{Hashimoto1989ZetaFiniteGraphs,%
StarkTerras1996FiniteGraphsCoverings,%
StarkTerras2000FiniteGraphsCoveringsII,%
StarkTerras2007FiniteGraphsCoveringsIII,%
Bass1992Ihara,%
MizunoSato2001ZetaDigraphs,%
Horton2007IharaDigraphs,%
ChoeKwakParkSato2007Bartholdi,%
Sato2008WeightedBartholdiCoverings}.
For recent perspectives on dynamical zeta functions and dynamical
determinants beyond the classical symbolic-dynamics sources, compare
Baladi's modern monograph \cite{Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}, the
parameter-regularity results of J\'ez\'equel
\cite{Jezequel2019ParameterRegularity}, and Shen's work on twisted
Ruelle zeta functions on locally symmetric spaces
\cite{Shen2023ComplexValuedAnalyticTorsion}. Our results stay in the
finite-state symbolic setting but fit naturally into this determinant
programme.

\subsection{Plan of the paper}

\Cref{sec:preliminaries} fixes notation for primitive orbit counts,
reduced determinants, cyclic lifts, and finite-group extensions.
\Cref{sec:chebotarev} develops the Adams--M\"obius mechanism for
finite-group extensions, derives the primitive Peter--Weyl determinant
series, and establishes the class Mertens theorem under a twisted
spectral gap. \Cref{sec:shadows} proves quotient functoriality,
identifies the abelian shadow and the exact boundary of cyclic
detection inside the finite-group Peter--Weyl decomposition, and closes
with the \(S_3\)-model separating the quadratic sign-channel Adams effect
from the standard-channel non-abelian defect. \Cref{sec:finite-part}
then proves the auxiliary scalar finite-part formula, the cyclic-lift
reconstruction of the reduced spectrum, and the one-layer discrete
Fourier corollary used as the canonical cyclic-lift comparison case.
The appendices record
holomorphic variation and truncation for the scalar finite part
(\Cref{app:variation}) and mark the boundary separating this article
from independent scalar arithmetic and growing-family rigidity packages
(\Cref{app:arithmetic,app:rigidity}).
