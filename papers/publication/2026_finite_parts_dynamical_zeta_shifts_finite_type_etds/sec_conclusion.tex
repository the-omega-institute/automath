\section{Discussion and open problems}\label{sec:conclusion}

\subsection{Summary of the main contribution}

The paper has two main results, both in the finite-group part. First,
twisted determinant data in the Peter--Weyl channels are converted to
primitive orbit data by Adams--M\"obius inversion. Second, under the
stated twisted spectral gap this primitive determinant calculus yields
conjugacy-class Mertens constants at the Perron point; the accompanying
Fourier formula is the standard character-orthogonality readout of
those constants
(\Cref{cor:class-function-adams-mobius,%
thm:class-mertens-explicit,%
thm:class-mertens-fourier}). Quotient functoriality then identifies the
abelianised primitive profile, and the linear span of cyclic quotient
observables is the abelian shadow of the full primitive class profile at
the Perron point, under \(\eta<\lambda\).
The root-of-unity layer belongs to the separate canonical cyclic-lift
model, not to arbitrary cyclic quotients of the abelianised cocycle
(\Cref{cor:abelian-cyclic-shadow,%
prop:canonical-cyclic-lift-root-layer,%
thm:abelian-shadow-defect,%
thm:cyclic-detection-boundary}).

The scalar finite-part formula and cyclic-lift reconstruction enter only
through the fixed-matrix inputs listed in
\Cref{prop:intro-scope-of-results}: the trivial-channel normalisation
\(\mathcal M(A)\), recovery of the reduced spectral polynomial \(F_A\)
(equivalently the relative non-Perron spectrum) from the full
branch-lifted logarithmic cyclic-lift record, and one-layer Fourier
inversion for a fixed \(q\).

\subsection{Open problems}

We close with three problems that arise naturally from the results
above but remain outside the present scope. The first is stated as a
separate dynamical problem and begins with a non-example: by
\Cref{prop:canonical-cyclic-lift-root-layer}, the cyclic-lift comparison
used in the finite-part section has, when \(q>1\),
\[
  \max_{1\le j\le q-1}\rho(A_{\chi_j}^{\tau_q})=\lambda.
\]
Thus it is a concrete abelian failure of the non-trivial twisted gap. The
root-of-unity comparison in \Cref{sec:finite-part} therefore lies
outside the gap regime whenever \(q>1\). The problem is to find
checkable hypotheses on genuinely twisted cocycles that force all
non-trivial channels below the Perron radius.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Dynamical criteria for the twisted spectral gap.} The
  condition
  \(\eta=\max_{\varrho\neq\mathbf 1}\rho(A_\varrho)<\lambda\) is
  assumed in \Cref{thm:class-mertens-explicit}. This hypothesis is
  separate from the scalar Perron gap of the base matrix. It can fail
  even for abelian twists when a non-trivial character channel keeps
  spectral radius \(\lambda\), so it is not a consequence of
  primitivity alone. In particular, the canonical cyclic-lift comparison
  of
  \Cref{rem:abelian-toy-model-cyclic,prop:canonical-cyclic-lift-root-layer}
  has
  \(A_{\chi_j}^{\tau_q}=\omega_q^jA\) and hence
  \(\rho(A_{\chi_j}^{\tau_q})=\lambda\) for every \(j\neq0\); for
  \(q>1\) it is excluded from the twisted-gap hypothesis. This shows that
  the gap cannot be inferred from abelianity, cyclicity, or primitivity of
  the base matrix alone. In this sense the canonical abelian cyclic lift
  is a non-example that any sufficient condition must explicitly exclude;
  a proposed criterion that admitted this model for \(q>1\) would be
  false. For non-abelian extensions the analogous condition was also
  assumed without derivation in \cite{ParryPollicott1986Chebotarev}.
  Identifying computable sufficient conditions on the cocycle
  \(\tau:E_A\to G\) that guarantee \(\eta<\lambda\) for broad classes
  of finite-group extensions remains open. Noorani's analysis of
  homogeneous extensions and Markov-shift applications
  \cite{Noorani1997HomogeneousExtensionsMarkov}, the analysis of Parry and
  Liv\v{s}ic obstructions in
  \cite{BoyleSchmieding2017FiniteGroupExtensions}, together with
  the finite-action periodic-data viewpoint of
  \cite{Fiebig1993PeriodicFiniteGroupActions} and the \(G\)-SFT
  flow-equivalence framework of
  \cite{BoyleCarlsenEilers2020FlowEquivalenceGSFT}, gives a partial
  structural context. Parry's non-abelian periodic-point theorem
  \cite{Parry1999LivsicNonAbelian} gives another piece of this picture,
  and the \(S_3\)-model of \Cref{prop:s3-example-channels} (where
  \(\rho(A_\rho)=1<2=\lambda\)) provides one explicit verification.

\item \emph{Non-abelian analogues of cyclic arithmetic.} The scalar
  root-of-unity refinements separated in \Cref{app:arithmetic} are
  abelian in nature. It would be of interest to know whether any
  boundary-multiplicity or residue-class information has a natural
  higher-dimensional twisted-channel analogue.

\item \emph{Transfer-operator extensions.} The present paper works
  entirely at the level of finite matrices and algebraic determinants.
  For countable-state shifts or for the Ruelle transfer operator acting
  on spaces of H\"older-continuous functions, the finite-part constant
  and the Adams--M\"obius mechanism should have natural analogues
  involving the Fredholm determinant of the transfer operator. Relating
  \(\FP(A)\) to the spectral data of the transfer operator on an
  appropriate Banach space would connect the present results to the
  broader programme of Baladi
  \cite{Baladi2000PositiveTransfer,Baladi2018ZetaDeterminants}, the
  regularity results of J\'ez\'equel
  \cite{Jezequel2019ParameterRegularity}, and the
  Ruelle--Pollicott resonance theory.
\end{enumerate}
