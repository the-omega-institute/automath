\title[Adams--M\"obius primitive inversion]{Adams--M\"obius primitive inversion for finite-group extensions of shifts of finite type}

\author{Haobo Ma}
\address{Research Department, CHRONOAI PTE.\ LTD., \#14-02, Marina Bay Financial Centre Tower 1, 8 Marina Blvd, Singapore 018981, Singapore}
\email{haobo@chronoai.co}

\begin{abstract}
For finite-group extensions of mixing subshifts of finite type, we
develop a primitive determinant calculus in Peter--Weyl coordinates. The
theorem package is finite-group theoretic. First, twisted
determinants, which record periodic labels, are converted into primitive
Peter--Weyl series by an Adams--M\"obius inversion that accounts for the
operations \(g\mapsto g^m\) produced by repetitions of primitive
orbits. Second, under the stated twisted spectral gap, those primitive
series have well-defined Perron-point values, giving conjugacy-class
Mertens constants; Hermitian character orthogonality then reads off the
non-trivial primitive channels from the constant deviations. The
linear span of cyclic quotient observables is the one-dimensional shadow
of the full primitive class profile at the Perron point, under
\(\eta<\lambda\). The root-of-unity layer used for
scalar sampling is separate: it appears only after replacing the cocycle
by the canonical cyclic lift. The orthogonal complement is a genuine
non-abelian defect. In the \(S_3\) model the vanishing sign trace is the
quadratic one-dimensional Adams obstruction, while the standard
two-dimensional channel gives a certified non-zero non-abelian
contribution. The scalar finite part of
\(\zeta_A(z)=\det(I-zA)^{-1}\) and cyclic root-of-unity reconstruction
are auxiliary finite-matrix inputs: they provide the trivial-channel
normalisation and the canonical cyclic-lift comparison case, not a
parallel theorem chain. For one fixed matrix \(A\), the full
branch-lifted logarithmic cyclic-lift record satisfies
\(\Psi_q(A)=u_q(A)+O(\Lambda_{\mathrm{rel}}(A)^{2q})\) with an explicit
remainder bound, and the one-layer Fourier recovery is only for one
fixed \(q\ge d\).
\end{abstract}
