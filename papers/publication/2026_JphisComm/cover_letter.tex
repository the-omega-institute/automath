Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, \emph{``Two detector-defined shells from stationary Unruh--DeWitt click records in static KMS spacetimes''}, for publication in \emph{Advances in Mathematics}.

The manuscript studies the stationary binary record of a sampled two-level Unruh--DeWitt detector by combining symbolic dynamics, renewal theory, and the geometry of static KMS spacetimes. Its central point is that exact properties of the full stationary word law, rather than only the mean transition rate, define codimension-one loci and support inverse statements in static spherical families. The analysis is mathematically self-contained and concentrates on exact thresholds, explicit formulas, and precise dependence on the detector design.

We note that a shorter preliminary version was previously submitted elsewhere. The present manuscript is a substantially expanded and self-contained treatment, with new results on soft dead time, universal antibunching, shell-pair cancellation, near-horizon asymptotics, and a complete Schwarzschild worked example.

The principal results are the following.
\begin{enumerate}
\item In the ideal dead-time model, the sampled record is a Markov chain on the golden-mean shift, and exact symbolic thermality is realized at a unique detector threshold.
\item In the finite-recovery model, the sampled record is a full-support renewal process with strictly increasing hazard, so it cannot coincide with any Bernoulli law. The relevant selector is instead the memory-transition shell at exact $1$-dependence.
\item Finite recovery approaches the ideal grammar only softly: consecutive-click violations are quadratically suppressed in the sampling interval, yet the hazard remains genuinely history dependent at every positive sampling interval.
\item The finite-recovery record is universally antibunched, with strictly increasing spectral density on $[0,\pi]$ and an asymptotic Fano factor below one across the whole parameter range.
\item In the homogeneous spectral-weight regime, the shell pair obeys an exact common-amplitude cancellation law. Near a nonextremal Hartle--Hawking horizon this yields a universal leading-order ratio law for shell distances.
\item Within the same response model, the shell pair determines the redshift scale and the Schwarzschild mass parameter. Two operating modes of the same hardware then provide a local self-calibrating inverse principle on static spherical two-parameter families.
\end{enumerate}

All proofs and technical derivations are provided in a separate Supplementary Material file. The scripts generating the figures and tables are available upon request.

The work is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission. We have no competing interests to declare.
