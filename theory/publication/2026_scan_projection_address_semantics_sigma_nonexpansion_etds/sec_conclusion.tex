\section{Final remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The main point of the paper is that finite observation of an open
symbolic system detects more than an escape exponent.  For first-entry
residue events, the Bayes error profile carries three layers of
information: the escape rate, the residue-dependent quasistationary
ambiguity amplitude, and the cyclotomic lift of the open spectrum seen
through the error resolvent.  In the one-step Markov setting these
objects are completely explicit, and the doubling-hole example shows
that the resulting amplitudes can be computed in closed form.

The second organizing theme is the survivor R\'enyi pressure spectrum.
The family
\[
  s\longmapsto sP_{Y_H}(\varphi)-P_{Y_H}(s\varphi)
\]
packages the collision behaviour of surviving samples into open-system
thermodynamic formalism.  At \(s=2\) it gives the birthday scale of the
conditioned survivor law, while in the ambient system the corresponding
two-sample exponent splits into twice the escape rate plus the survivor
R\'enyi-\(2\) entropy rate.  The Poisson threshold theorem is therefore a
probabilistic consequence of a pressure-spectrum statement rather than a
separate phenomenon.

A short structural section records that recursive visible refinements
remain internal to the original factor and create no entropy.  The final
information-theoretic subsection is supplementary: if a visible quotient
collapses the collision spectrum, then any injective refinement that
restores the microscopic birthday scale must pay exactly the hidden
conditional entropy.  In that sense, the open-system pressure spectrum
and the hidden-information ledger describe the same collapse from two
different sides.

Two directions remain natural.  One is to extend the boundary-transfer
and survivor-pressure formalism beyond finite-state and standard Gibbs
classes while retaining a usable transfer-operator description.  The
other is to understand whether finer residue observables or
multidimensional holes detect larger pieces of the open resonance set
than the cyclotomic lifts considered here.
