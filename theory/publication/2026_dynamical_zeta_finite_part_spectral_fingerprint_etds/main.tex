\begin{abstract}
Let \(A\) be a primitive adjacency matrix with Perron root \(\lambda\).
We study the finite part of the dynamical \(\zeta\)-function
\(\zeta_A(z)=\det(I-zA)^{-1}\) at the Perron pole \(z=\lambda^{-1}\)
and the associated reduced determinant
\[
  C(A)=\det\nolimits'(I-A/\lambda)^{-1}.
\]
We first prove a primitive-orbit formula for the finite-part constant
\(\FP(A)\) and show that the cyclic-lift constants
\(C(A^{\langle q\rangle})\) reconstruct the reduced spectral polynomial
\[
  F_A(t)=\frac{\det(I-tA/\lambda)}{1-t},
\]
and hence the non-Perron spectrum with multiplicity. In particular,
one sufficiently large root-of-unity layer already determines \(F_A\).

For finite-group extensions we introduce class-function logarithms and
an Adams--M\"obius primitive inversion formula expressing primitive
Peter--Weyl series directly in terms of twisted determinants. Under a
twisted spectral-gap hypothesis this yields explicit conjugacy-class
Mertens constants and a non-abelian Fourier reconstruction of the
twisted primitive data at the Perron point. Quotient functoriality
then identifies cyclic quotient data as the exact abelian shadow of
the full primitive class profile, while the orthogonal complement is a
genuine non-abelian defect invisible to every cyclic observable. An
explicit \(S_3\)-example shows that vanishing periodic traces does not
force vanishing primitive bias.

Thus cyclic lifts and finite-group extensions become the abelian and
non-abelian parts of a single primitive-orbit determinant calculus at
the Perron pole.
\end{abstract}
