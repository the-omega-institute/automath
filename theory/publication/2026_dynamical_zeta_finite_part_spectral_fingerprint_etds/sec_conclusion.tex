\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

The main body of the paper establishes three structural facts. First,
the finite part at the Perron pole admits an explicit primitive-orbit
formula in terms of the reduced determinant. Second, the cyclic-lift
constants and one sufficiently large root-of-unity layer reconstruct
the reduced spectral polynomial, hence the non-Perron spectrum with
multiplicity. Third, finite-group extensions admit a primitive
determinant calculus in Peter--Weyl coordinates, and quotient
functoriality identifies cyclic data as exactly the abelian shadow of
the full primitive class profile.

The place where additional spectral input enters is now explicit. The
determinant identities and Adams--M\"obius inversion are valid in the
open Perron disc without further hypotheses. The twisted spectral-gap
condition \(\eta<\lambda\) is used precisely when the primitive
Peter--Weyl series are evaluated at the Perron point and converted into
conjugacy-class Mertens constants.

Two extension problems remain outside the present scope. One is to
clarify the sharp dynamical criteria guaranteeing the twisted spectral
gap in broader classes of finite-group extensions. The other is to
decide which of the arithmetic consequences collected in the appendices
admit intrinsic non-abelian analogues beyond the cyclic shadow.
