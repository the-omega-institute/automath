
# Revision notes for the v4 submission package

This round is a substantive expansion rather than a cosmetic polish. The main changes relative to v3 are:

1. The main text has been expanded into a fuller Research Paper structure that more clearly separates:
   - the detector-model results,
   - the static KMS shell equations,
   - the shell-pair observables,
   - and the inverse consequences.

2. Two additional record-level conclusions from the original source material have been promoted into the main text:
   - the soft-dead-time theorem, which quantifies the singular but controlled approach of finite recovery to the ideal grammar;
   - the universal antibunching theorem, which gives a closed spectral-density formula and strict sub-Poisson counting.

3. The introduction has been rewritten to sharpen the relation to recent UDW literature and to reduce overclaiming.

4. A schematic figure and a more informative summary table have been added so that the narrative feels closer to a human-written journal article and less like a compressed theorem note.

5. The geometric section now foregrounds the shell pair as the central observable, with explicit subsections for:
   - common-amplitude cancellation,
   - spectral transport,
   - near-horizon hierarchy,
   - and static spherical inversion.

6. The manuscript now includes a data availability statement, which aligns better with IOP publication requirements for accepted papers.

7. Acknowledgements remain removed, as requested.
