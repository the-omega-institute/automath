\begin{abstract}
We show that finite-window Zeckendorf fibers admit a discrete thermodynamic
formalism governed by the classical Fibonacci partition constants.  We study
the stabilization map
\[
  \Fold_m(\omega)=V_m^{-1}\bigl(N_m(\omega)\bmod F_{m+2}\bigr),
\]
where \(V_m\) identifies the stable length-\(m\) Fibonacci words with the
residue interval \(\{0,\dots,F_{m+2}-1\}\).  After an explicit affine
permutation of residues, each fiber multiplicity is exactly the Fibonacci-lag
discrete derivative
\[
  R^\dagger(n)-R^\dagger(n-F_{m+1}),
  \qquad
  R^\dagger(n):=R(n)+R(n-1),
\]
of the classical Fibonacci partition function.  Consequently the collision
moments
\[
  S_q(m):=\sum_{x\in X_m} d_m(x)^q
\]
obey the exact Fibonacci-window bounds
\[
  T_q^\dagger(F_{m+1})\le S_q(m)\le T_q^\dagger(F_{m+2}),
  \qquad
  T_q^\dagger(N):=\sum_{n=0}^{N-1} R^\dagger(n)^q.
\]
Combining this with Sanna's theorem on Fibonacci partition power sums and with
our finite-state kernel realization yields \(S_q(m)\asymp \lambda_q^m\) for
every fixed \(q\ge 1\), and for \(q\ge 2\) the Perron root of the collision
kernel is exactly Sanna's partition constant \(\lambda_q\).  It follows that
\(\lambda_q\) is an algebraic integer with a polynomial-size nonnegative matrix
model and that the pressure sequence \(P_0=\log\varphi\), \(P_q=\log\lambda_q\)
is convex.  Writing \(\Delta_q:=P_q-P_{q-1}\), we prove that under the
\(q\)-canonical law proportional to \(d_m(x)^q\), the thickness exponent
\(m^{-1}\log d_m(x)\) is exponentially concentrated in the adjacent
pressure-slope band \([\Delta_q,\Delta_{q+1}]\).  This yields two-sided
exponential estimates for the number and visible mass of fibers in every
canonical band.  We further show that diverging tilts select the zero-temperature
thickness \(\frac12\log\varphi\), upgrading the largest-fiber law
\(D_m^{1/m}\to\sqrt{\varphi}\) and the diagonal asymptotic
\[
  \log S_{q(m)}(m)=\Bigl(\frac12\log\varphi+o(1)\Bigr)m\,q(m)
\]
for every sequence \(q(m)\to\infty\).  At the visible endpoint \(q=1\), the
output law is concentrated on a deterministic thickness band controlled by
\(\Delta_1\) and \(\Delta_2\), and its full R\'enyi entropy-rate spectrum is
given by the same pressure sequence.  The finite-window fold model therefore
closes to a genuinely discrete thermodynamic system whose canonical pressures
are exactly the Fibonacci partition constants.
\end{abstract}
