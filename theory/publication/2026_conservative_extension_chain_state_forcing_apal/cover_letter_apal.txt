Please consider the enclosed manuscript,

"Homological Visibility of Gluing Obstructions in a State-Forcing Semantics,"

for publication in the Annals of Pure and Applied Logic.

The manuscript is organized around three main results in mathematical logic. First, it proves a forcing-necessity theorem: compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable in the lower information-state forcing language, so the local-object layer contributes genuine logical expressivity rather than a change of presentation.

Second, it gives a precise branched gerbe semantics for that enrichment. After fixing an abelian band on the slice site, it proves that every local object functor admits a canonical split realization prestack and formulates the obstruction theory for gluing-sensitive lifts whose stackifications recover visible value classes as global connected components and remain globally conservative at the terminal fibre. In that setting, each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe, and gluing-level absence is characterized exactly by the non-neutrality of all visible component gerbes.

Third, for finite abelian branch obstructions on finite Cech nerves, the universal coefficient theorem yields a homological evaluation map whose cokernel is the intrinsic visible quotient. This quotient detects exactly the H_2-visible part of the obstruction, while pure Ext-type residues are character-blind. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case, a formal comparison theorem recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and identifies the precise blind residual. The multi-branch exact sequence, strictification budgets, and worked finite examples are presented as consequences of this core package rather than as separate main claims.

We believe the manuscript fits the journal's scope in mathematical logic and in applications of logic to mathematics and theoretical computer science. It combines forcing semantics, sheaf-theoretic local-to-global methods, and homological algebra in a self-contained presentation that does not rely on any external unpublished framework.
