\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

We have reorganized the paper around three main results. The first is logical rather than homological: the local-object layer is genuinely necessary. Compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable in the lower information-state forcing language, so the passage to local objects adds expressive power rather than notation. The second is semantic: visible value classes are exactly the global connected components of the realization stack, and gluing-level absence is exactly the situation in which compatible local data exist but every visible branch gerbe is non-neutral. The third is homological: the intrinsic visible quotient is the cokernel of the homological evaluation map, so the visible part of a branch obstruction is precisely its $H_2$-detected part, while the blind remainder is exactly the pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-residual.

These three results also determine the external significance of the paper. The first shows that the enrichment is logically necessary rather than decorative. The second turns gluing failure into a branch-sensitive gerbe semantics instead of a binary local-to-global defect. The third provides the main external anchor: in the unique-branch support-presheaf setting, the theory recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and sharpens it by identifying the exact mechanism behind cohomological blind cases highlighted by Car\`u.

The remaining results should be read as consequences of that core package rather than as independent claims of equal weight. In particular, the multi-branch exact sequence, the branch-sensitive versus branch-uniform comparison, the strictification budgets, and the worked finite examples show how the same semantics resolves hidden information once branch data are taken seriously. Taken together, the paper turns gluing failure into a graded account of semantic visibility, hidden information, and the exact cost of recovering what the lower layers cannot see.
