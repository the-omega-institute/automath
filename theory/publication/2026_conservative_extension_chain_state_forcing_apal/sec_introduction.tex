\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Standard sheaf-theoretic semantics captures local-to-global passage: compatible local sections glue when the underlying presheaf is a sheaf, and fail to glue when it is not. The obstruction to gluing is recorded by cohomology classes, and the sheaf-theoretic analysis of contextuality \cite{AbramskyBrandenburger2011} treats the absence of a global section as the central semantic defect. Yet pointwise semantics---truth at a fully specified assignment or world---cannot express the distinction between compatible local sectionability and global sectionability at all. A formula evaluated at a single point sees either the presence or the absence of a value; it cannot see that compatible local data exist but fail to glue.

The present work sits in the forcing and sheaf-theoretic tradition in logic, building on the Kripke semantics for intuitionistic logic \cite{Kripke1965}, Beth's semantic tableaux constructions \cite{Beth1956}, Fitting's model-theoretic forcing \cite{Fitting1969}, and the topos-theoretic perspective surveyed by Goldblatt \cite{Goldblatt2006}. What distinguishes our setting is the explicit stratification of the semantics into conservative extension layers, so that each enrichment---from pointwise truth to information-state forcing, from forcing to local objects, from local objects to gluing obstructions---is accompanied by a precise invariance guarantee.

This gap motivates the present paper. We construct a four-layer conservative extension chain
\[
\mathbb{L}_0 \preceq \mathbb{L}_1 \preceq \mathbb{L}_2 \preceq \mathbb{L}_3 \preceq \mathbb{L}_4,
\]
where $\mathbb{L}_0$ is pointwise semantics, $\mathbb{L}_1$ adds information-state forcing, $\mathbb{L}_2$ adds local references and local objects, $\mathbb{L}_3$ adds structural absence and gluing-obstruction data, and $\mathbb{L}_4$ adds refinement dynamics. Each layer preserves the forcing behavior of formulas already present below (\Cref{cor:whole-chain-invariance}). The chain is the minimal framework in which gluing predicates, branch gerbes, and homological visibility can all be stated.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Research question.}
Once an admitted reference carries a finite abelian gluing obstruction, what part of that obstruction is semantically visible without enlarging the state space, and how much additional information is required to recover the hidden part?

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main results.}
The answer is delivered by three groups of results, each located at a specific boundary of the extension chain.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~A} (forcing necessity; $\mathbb{L}_1$--$\mathbb{L}_2$ boundary).
Compatible local sectionability is characterized by sheafification (\Cref{thm:sheafification-characterization}), and the predicates $\CompSec_s$, $\Sec_s$, and $\Null^{\mathrm{glue}}_s$ are not definable in the information-state forcing language without the local-object enrichment (\Cref{thm:pointwise-irreducibility}). This separation result justifies the enrichment on logical grounds: the gluing predicates that drive the obstruction theory are provably invisible at $\mathbb{L}_1$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~B} (branched gerbe semantics; $\mathbb{L}_2$--$\mathbb{L}_3$ boundary).
For a realization prestack whose stackification recovers the sheafification of isomorphism classes and is globally conservative at the terminal fibre, visible value classes are the global connected components of the realization stack (\Cref{thm:visible-value-components}). Each visible branch $v$ determines a banded component gerbe $\mathfrak{G}_v$ (\Cref{thm:component-gerbe-decomposition}), and gluing-level absence holds exactly when visible branches exist but every component gerbe is non-neutral (\Cref{thm:gerbe-null-semantics}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Theorem~C} (homological visibility; at $\mathbb{L}_3$).
For a finite abelian branch obstruction on a finite \v{C}ech nerve, the universal coefficient theorem yields a homological evaluation map $\mathrm{ev}_\omega : H_2(N(\mathcal{U}),\mathbb{Z}) \to A$ whose cokernel is the intrinsic visible quotient $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^\omega$ (\Cref{thm:intrinsic-visible-quotient}). The class-admissible characters are exactly the annihilator of the image of this map, and the blind residual---the part of the obstruction invisible to every admissible character---is a pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-type class (\Cref{thm:character-blind-obstructions}). In the unique-branch support-presheaf setting, the theory recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and refines it by identifying the precise blind mechanism (\Cref{thm:unique-branch-contextuality-comparison}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Secondary consequences.}
The multi-branch theory yields an exact comparison between branch-sensitive and branch-uniform visibility: the canonical surjection $A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\mathrm{br}}(r) \twoheadrightarrow A_{\mathrm{vis}}^{\mathrm{unif}}(r)$ has kernel $(\sum_v K_v)/(\bigcap_v K_v)$, so the hidden budget behind uniform visibility factors into a branch-resolution term and a within-branch residual (\Cref{thm:branch-comparison-exact-sequence,thm:branch-resolution-budget,cor:uniform-hidden-budget-factorization}). Worked finite examples (\Cref{ex:one-bit-hidden-obstruction,ex:rp2-character-blind,ex:s2-single-branch-maximal-collapse,ex:two-branch-sphere-separation}) separate the visible, blind, and multi-branch regimes.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Hypothesis stratification.}
The hypotheses used in these results are stratified rather than cumulative. Branch constancy enters only for the decomposition corollary (\Cref{cor:branch-decomposition}), not for the component-gerbe theorem itself. Global conservativity is used only when stack-global objects are compared back to the original local object functor. Finite nerve presentations appear only in the finite abelian visibility theorems. The logical separation result and the basic gluing semantics do not depend on the full finite obstruction package.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Plan of the paper.}
\Cref{sec:preliminaries} defines logic layers and conservative extensions and introduces the four-layer chain. \Cref{sec:information-states} defines information states, forcing, and lifted pointwise soundness. \Cref{sec:null-decomposition} develops local objects, proves the sheafification characterization and the forcing-necessity theorem (Theorem~A), and introduces realization prestacks and visible value classes. The gerbe-obstruction subsection establishes the branched gerbe semantics (Theorem~B), while the homological-visibility subsection establishes the intrinsic visible quotient and the character-blind obstruction theorem (Theorem~C), together with the multi-branch comparison sequence and the contextuality anchor. \Cref{sec:multiaxis-refinement} records refinement monotonicity results at $\mathbb{L}_4$, and the appendix contains finite-state complexity upper bounds.
