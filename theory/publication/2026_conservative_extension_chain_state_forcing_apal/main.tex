\title[Homological Visibility of Gluing Obstructions]{Homological Visibility of Gluing Obstructions in a State-Forcing Semantics}
\author{}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
We study a self-contained four-layer conservative extension from ordinary pointwise semantics to information states and local objects, with the aim of isolating the logical content of gluing failure. The paper is organized around three main results. First, compatible local sectionability and gluing-level absence are not definable in the information-state forcing language, so the local-object layer adds genuine expressive power. Second, for gluing-sensitive realization prestacks whose stackifications recover the sheafification of local isomorphism classes and are globally conservative at the terminal fibre, visible value classes are exactly the global connected components of the realization stack, each visible branch determines a banded component gerbe, and gluing-level absence occurs exactly when visible branches exist but all of those gerbes are non-neutral. Third, for finite abelian branch obstructions on finite Cech nerves, the universal coefficient theorem yields a homological evaluation map whose cokernel is the intrinsic visible quotient; this quotient captures precisely the $H_2$-visible part of the obstruction, while pure $\operatorname{Ext}$-type residues are character-blind. In the unique-branch support-presheaf case, this recovers the Abramsky--Brandenburger no-global-section picture and refines it by identifying the precise blind residual. As secondary consequences, we obtain an exact comparison between branch-sensitive and branch-uniform visibility, exact strictification budgets, and worked finite examples separating the visible, blind, and branch-resolution regimes.
\end{abstract}
