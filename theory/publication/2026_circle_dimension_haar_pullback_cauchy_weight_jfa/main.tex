\title{Cayley--Chebyshev Mode Calculus, Poisson Entropy Asymptotics, and Cardinal Reconstruction in a Strip RKHS}
\author{}
\date{}

\begin{document}

\begin{abstract}
We use the Cayley transform to identify the pullback of normalized Haar
measure on the circle with the Cauchy law on~$\RR$, and we show that
this property rigidifies the Cayley chart among normalized
orientation-preserving $C^{1}$ homeomorphisms.  The logarithmic
Jacobian yields an entropy identity in Cayley coordinates and places the
problem inside the Poisson semigroup on~$\RR$.  The functional-analytic
core is a Cayley--Chebyshev mode calculus for
the comparison kernel
\[
R_\varepsilon(y)=\frac{1+y^{2}}{1+(y-\varepsilon)^{2}},
\]
which yields a uniform mode expansion, finite Fourier formulas for all
mode functions, and a parity principle forcing the large-time relative
entropy expansion to contain only even powers of~$t^{-1}$.  Using this
calculus we obtain a sharp eighth-order asymptotic formula for the
relative entropy between a Poisson average and the translated Poisson
kernel with matching mean.  Recentering the sixth- and eighth-order
coefficients at their extremal two-point values produces a defect
ladder in which the eighth-order gap strictly amplifies the sixth-order
gap and yields a quantitative $W_2$-stability theorem for the symmetric
two-point law.  The same mode
calculus has a canonical Hilbert completion: the secant family generated
by the comparison kernel has Gram kernel
$K(a,b)=2/(4+(a-b)^{2})$, and this reproducing-kernel space is
identified with the Hardy-type Poisson image space
$\sqrt{\pi}\,e^{-|D|}L^{2}(\RR)$, furnishing a concrete Fourier
realization and a holomorphic strip model for the associated
reproducing-kernel Hilbert space.  In this strip model the Gram space
splits orthogonally into two explicit Hardy summands, and the integer
secant orbit yields a lattice-sampling theorem with explicit constants,
a Shannon-type cardinal kernel, and an exact norm identity for the
interpolant determined by its lattice data.  We also prove that the
central Poisson trace together with its central derivative determine the
centred law by Laplace uniqueness and characteristic-function
inversion, and with the mean they determine the original measure.
\end{abstract}
