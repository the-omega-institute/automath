\section{Further remarks}\label{sec:conclusion}

The obstructions collected here are deliberately elementary, but they
act at three different levels. The density dichotomy is probabilistic:
finite automata force eventual periodicity in normalised word counts.
The Zeckendorf argument is combinatorial: regular and sofic models
enforce exponential-polynomial growth. The final pair of obstructions
is analytic: Euler products with infinitely many prime factors
accumulate singularities on the unit circle, while finite determinants
become periodic after the change of variables \(z=e^{-s}\).

Taken together, these statements explain why prime languages do not sit
naturally inside finite-state symbolic dynamics. The mismatch is not
confined to one formalism; it appears simultaneously in density,
growth, and analytic continuation.
