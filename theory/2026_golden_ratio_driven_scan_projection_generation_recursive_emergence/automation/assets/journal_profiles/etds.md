# Journal Profile: ETDS

## Editorial stance

- Present the problem in the language of measurable or symbolic dynamics from the first page.
- State the principal dynamical theorem package early and separate it from secondary interpretations.
- Keep transfer-operator or coding machinery subordinate to the main dynamical payoff.

## Structure

- Favor a direct introduction, clear preliminaries, a main theorem chain, and a short conclusion.
- Avoid a large appendix unless a technical detour is genuinely peripheral.

## Citation stance

- Cite target-journal papers when they sharpen the local dynamical conversation.
- Prefer direct comparisons in entropy, pressure, zeta, coding, or factor-language terms.
