# Journal Profile: Default

## Editorial stance

- Lead with the central mathematical question and the exact theorem-level answer.
- Keep exposition compact and avoid manifesto language, inflated claims, or repeated summaries.
- Treat appendices as overflow space only; core arguments belong in the main line.

## Citation stance

- Cite prior work only where it is used for comparison, inheritance, or a genuine imported fact.
- Do not add venue-local citations decoratively.

## Revision stance

- Rewrite for precision, pacing, and local novelty.
- Prefer short, theorem-linked paragraphs over broad motivational narration.
