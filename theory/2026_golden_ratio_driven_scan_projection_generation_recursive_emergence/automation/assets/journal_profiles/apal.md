# Journal Profile: APAL

## Editorial stance

- Formulate the semantic or logical question explicitly and keep every major theorem tied to that question.
- Use exact logical vocabulary and avoid philosophical framing unless it produces formal consequences.
- Keep auxiliary constructions short unless they are indispensable to the principal theorem chain.

## Structure

- State the formal problem, the theorem chain, and the novelty signal with minimal delay.
- Relegate side examples and broader interpretation to later sections or short remarks.

## Citation stance

- Cite logical, sheaf-theoretic, or categorical predecessors only where they define the precise baseline being improved or generalized.
- Do not let related work eclipse the main theorem flow.
