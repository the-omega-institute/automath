(Q,E,C,Y,\pi,q,\mathrm{coarse},y_\ast,B_{\mathrm{occ}},|C_{y_\ast}|)

\begin{proposition}[conditional all-multiple scale audit and denominator gap ledger]\label{distill:euclid_elements-proportion_and_denominator_spectrum_families-cdim-all-multiple-gap-ledger}
\textbf{Date-time: 2026-04-23T12:21:04+08:00. Family: Euclid--Elements / proportion and denominator-spectrum families. Dependency status: conditional audit.}
Let \(Q\) be a fixed unitary-slice query and suppose that its visible or Hard-axis verifier has registered a finite normalized-ratio audit datum
\[
\mathfrak R=(I,\{(N_i,D_i)\}_{i\in I},\mathcal M),
\]
where \(I\) is finite, \(N_i,D_i\in\mathbb N_{>0}\), and \(\mathcal M\subset\mathbb N_{>0}\) is the non-empty set of admissible common scale multipliers allowed by the primitive grammar of the verifier.  For \(i,j\in I\), put
\[
\Delta_{ij}:=N_iD_j-N_jD_i .
\]
Then the following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the normalized readouts \(N_i/D_i\) and \(N_j/D_j\) pass the Euclidean all-multiple equality test: for every \(a,b\in\mathbb N_{>0}\) and every \(m\in\mathcal M\), the trichotomy sign of
  \[
  a\,(mN_i)(mD_j)-b\,(mN_j)(mD_i)
  \]
  is the trichotomy sign of \(a-b\);
  \item \(\Delta_{ij}=0\).
\end{enumerate}
Independently of equality, every already constructed comparison is transported faithfully by common scaling:
\[
\operatorname{sgn}\!\bigl(a\,(mN_i)(mD_j)-b\,(mN_j)(mD_i)\bigr)
=\operatorname{sgn}\!\bigl(aN_iD_j-bN_jD_i\bigr)
\]
for all \(a,b\in\mathbb N_{>0}\) and \(m\in\mathcal M\).  If \(\Delta_{ij}\ne0\), then no admissible common multiplier can repair the failed equality: for every \(m\in\mathcal M\), the transported denominator gap is
\[
\Delta_{ij}^{(m)}:=(mN_i)(mD_j)-(mN_j)(mD_i)=m^2\Delta_{ij},
\]
so its sign is invariant and its absolute size is only magnified.  Thus a failed normalized scale transfer is not a rounding error; it is the finite ledger item
\[
(i,j,N_i,D_i,N_j,D_j,\Delta_{ij})
\]
registered on the same support axis as the attempted comparison.  If the datum \(\mathfrak R\) has not been registered for \(Q\), the same computation proves only an external denominator-gap certificate and does not by itself imply a \(\mathrm{Hard}\)-tagged \(\mathsf{NULL}\) readout for \(Q\).
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
For any \(m\in\mathcal M\) and any \(a,b\in\mathbb N_{>0}\), direct expansion gives
\[
 a\,(mN_i)(mD_j)-b\,(mN_j)(mD_i)
 =m^2\bigl(aN_iD_j-bN_jD_i\bigr).
\]
Since \(m^2>0\), multiplication by \(m^2\) preserves the three signs \(<,=,>\).  This proves faithful transport of every already constructed comparison.

Assume first that \(\Delta_{ij}=0\).  Then \(N_iD_j=N_jD_i=:C\), with \(C>0\), and therefore
\[
 a\,(mN_i)(mD_j)-b\,(mN_j)(mD_i)=m^2C(a-b).
\]
Its sign is exactly the sign of \(a-b\), so the all-multiple equality test passes.  Conversely, if the all-multiple equality test passes, take \(a=b=1\).  For any \(m\in\mathcal M\), the required sign is the sign of \(0\), hence
\[
(mN_i)(mD_j)-(mN_j)(mD_i)=m^2\Delta_{ij}=0.
\]
Because \(m>0\), this forces \(\Delta_{ij}=0\).

When \(\Delta_{ij}\ne0\), the displayed identity with \(a=b=1\) gives \(\Delta_{ij}^{(m)}=m^2\Delta_{ij}\).  Thus common scaling cannot change the sign or cancel the obstruction.  The obstruction is finite and offline-checkable because it consists only of the two registered integer representatives and their cross-product difference.  This is precisely the Omega distinction enforced here: a legal common multiple transports an already constructed ratio comparison, whereas denominator drift produces a typed gap ledger.  The final sentence is the primitive-admissibility boundary: without registration in the verifier for \(Q\), the tuple has not crossed the type boundary required for a Hard-axis failure witness.
\end{proof}
