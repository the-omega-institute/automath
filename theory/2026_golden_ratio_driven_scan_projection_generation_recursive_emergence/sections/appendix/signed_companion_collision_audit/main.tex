\section{Signed Companion Proxies and Collision-Moment Certificates}\label{app:signed-companion-collision-audit}

This appendix isolates the linear-algebra convention behind the signed
companions reconstructed from the collision-moment recurrences.  Its purpose is
twofold.  First, it records the determinant and exterior-square coefficients
that are forced by the signed Frobenius convention.  Second, it separates those
formal proxy computations from the stronger assertion that the same matrices
are nonnegative collision kernels, or are already flow-equivalent to such
kernels.

\subsection{The signed companion convention}

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, let \(d\geq 2\), and let
\[
    c=(c_1,\ldots,c_d)\in R^d.
\]
We use the signed recurrence companion
\[
A(c)=
\begin{pmatrix}
0&1&0&\cdots&0\\
0&0&1&\cdots&0\\
\vdots&\vdots&\vdots&\ddots&\vdots\\
0&0&0&\cdots&1\\
-c_d&-c_{d-1}&-c_{d-2}&\cdots&-c_1
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Equivalently, after conjugating by the reversal permutation, this is the
first-row Frobenius companion with first row \((-c_1,\ldots,-c_d)\) and
subdiagonal entries \(1\).  This is the convention formalized in the Omega Lean
file \texttt{\detokenize{lean4/Omega/Folding/CollisionKernel.lean}}.

\begin{lemma}[Signed companion coefficients]\label{lem:signed-companion-coefficients}
The characteristic polynomial of \(A(c)\) is
\[
    \chi_{A(c)}(x)=x^d+c_1x^{d-1}+c_2x^{d-2}+\cdots+c_d.
\]
Consequently
\[
    \tr A(c)=-c_1,\qquad e_2(A(c))=c_2,
\]
where \(e_2\) denotes the second elementary symmetric polynomial in the
eigenvalues, equivalently the trace of \(\Lambda^2 A(c)\) over a splitting
field.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The displayed matrix is the usual Frobenius companion for the monic polynomial
\(x^d+c_1x^{d-1}+\cdots+c_d\), up to conjugation by the reversal permutation.
The characteristic polynomial is therefore the displayed polynomial.  Comparing
with
\[
    \chi_A(x)=x^d-e_1(A)x^{d-1}+e_2(A)x^{d-2}-\cdots
\]
gives \(e_1(A(c))=\tr A(c)=-c_1\) and \(e_2(A(c))=c_2\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Signed companion determinant]\label{thm:signed-companion-det}
For the signed companion \(A(c)\),
\[
    \det(I-A(c))=1+\sum_{i=1}^d c_i.
\]
In contrast, the ordinary recurrence companion \(C(c)\), with characteristic
polynomial \(x^d-c_1x^{d-1}-\cdots-c_d\), satisfies
\[
    \det(I-C(c))=1-\sum_{i=1}^d c_i.
\]
Thus the two determinant conventions obey the universal conversion rule
\[
    \det(I-C(c))+\det(I-A(c))=2.
\]
\leanverified{signedCompanionDet}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The signed identity follows from Lemma
\ref{lem:signed-companion-coefficients} by evaluating the characteristic
polynomial at \(x=1\):
\[
    \det(I-A(c))=\chi_{A(c)}(1)=1+\sum_{i=1}^d c_i.
\]
The ordinary recurrence companion has the same formula with the coefficients
replaced by \(-c_i\), giving \(1-\sum_i c_i\).  Adding the two expressions gives
the conversion rule.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[The \(q=6,7\) signed proxy determinants]\label{cor:signed-companion-q6-q7}
For the audited recurrence vectors
\[
\begin{aligned}
c_6&=(2,17,28,88,-26,4,-4),\\
c_7&=(2,26,74,311,-34,84,-42),
\end{aligned}
\]
the signed companion last rows are
\[
\begin{aligned}
r_6&=(4,-4,26,-88,-28,-17,-2),\\
r_7&=(42,-84,34,-311,-74,-26,-2).
\end{aligned}
\]
Their signed determinant proxies are
\[
    \det(I-A(c_6))=110,\qquad \det(I-A(c_7))=422.
\]
Hence the signed determinant excesses above the even Fibonacci baseline are
\[
    110-F_{10}=55,\qquad 422-F_{12}=278.
\]
\leanverified{signedCompanionDet6,signedCompanionDet7}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The last rows are \((-c_d,\ldots,-c_1)\).  Applying Theorem
\ref{thm:signed-companion-det} gives
\[
1+\sum_i c_{6,i}=1+2+17+28+88-26+4-4=110,
\]
and
\[
1+\sum_i c_{7,i}=1+2+26+74+311-34+84-42=422.
\]
Since \(F_{10}=55\) and \(F_{12}=144\), the two excesses are \(55\) and \(278\).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Proxy boundary]\label{rem:signed-companion-proxy-boundary}
Corollary \ref{cor:signed-companion-q6-q7} is a determinant statement about
signed companions reconstructed from the moment recurrences.  It should not be
read as a statement that \(A(c_6)\) and \(A(c_7)\) are already certified
nonnegative collision kernels.  Upgrading the signed proxies to
Bowen--Franks/SFT invariants requires an additional nonnegative presentation or
a flow-equivalence certificate.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Collision-moment coincidence audit}

Let \(c_q=(c_{q,1},\ldots,c_{q,d_q})\) denote the audited recurrence
coefficient vector for the \(q\)-collision moment sequence
\[
    S_q(m)=\sum_{i=1}^{d_q} c_{q,i}S_q(m-i).
\]
For the signed companion \(A_q:=A(c_q)\), Lemma
\ref{lem:signed-companion-coefficients} and Theorem
\ref{thm:signed-companion-det} give the two coefficient-level diagnostics
\[
    e_2(A_q)=c_{q,2},
    \qquad
    \epsilon_q:=\abs{1+\sum_i c_{q,i}}-F_{2q-2}.
\]
Here \(\epsilon_q\) is only a signed-proxy determinant excess; it is not, by
itself, an SFT invariant.

\begin{theorem}[Bounded signed-proxy Lucas certificate]\label{thm:signed-companion-lucas-certificate}
In the audited recurrence range \(2\leq q\leq 23\), the equality
\[
    e_2(A_q)=L_q
\]
holds exactly for
\[
    q=3,4,5.
\]
In the same range, the simultaneous triple coincidence
\[
    e_2(A_q)=L_q=\epsilon_q
\]
holds exactly for \(q=5\).  In particular, no \(q\in\{6,\ldots,23\}\) satisfies
either \(e_2(A_q)=L_q\) or \(\epsilon_q=L_q\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The identities \(e_2(A_q)=c_{q,2}\) and
\(\epsilon_q=\abs{1+\sum_i c_{q,i}}-F_{2q-2}\) reduce the claim to the audited
integer recurrence table.  Direct enumeration gives Table
\ref{tab:signed-companion-collision-audit-q2-23}.  The standalone
exterior-Lucas certificate has \(22\) rows, for \(q=2,\ldots,23\), and its true
rows are precisely \(q=3,4,5\).  Comparing the excess column with \(L_q\) leaves
only \(q=5\).  The computation is verified computationally (see
\texttt{\detokenize{scripts/equational_theory/}}), specifically by
\[
    \texttt{\detokenize{scripts/equational_theory/signed_companion_collision_audit.py}}
\]
and its JSON certificate
\[
    \texttt{\detokenize{scripts/equational_theory/exterior_lucas_certificate_q2_23.json}}.
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Decomposition of the \(q=5\) coincidence]\label{cor:signed-companion-q5-coincidence-split}
The \(q=5\) identity
\[
    L_5=e_2(A_5)=\epsilon_5=11
\]
is the simultaneous satisfaction of two independent coefficient equations:
\[
    c_{5,2}=L_5,
    \qquad
    1+\sum_i c_{5,i}-F_8=L_5.
\]
The first equation persists only through the audited corridor \(q=3,4,5\), and
the second has no audited solution beyond \(q=5\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
For \(c_5=(2,11,8,20,-10)\), Lemma
\ref{lem:signed-companion-coefficients} gives \(e_2(A_5)=c_{5,2}=11=L_5\).
Theorem \ref{thm:signed-companion-det} gives
\[
    1+\sum_i c_{5,i}=1+2+11+8+20-10=32,
\]
and \(F_8=21\), so \(\epsilon_5=32-21=11=L_5\).  The remaining assertions are
exactly the bounded certificate of Theorem
\ref{thm:signed-companion-lucas-certificate}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Fusion-ring shadow and obstruction}

\begin{corollary}[Fusion coherence is not certified by the signed proxy]\label{cor:signed-companion-fusion-obstruction}
The formal Omega data support a Fibonacci fusion-ring shadow:
\[
    M^2=M+I,
\]
the natural centralizer is \(\NN[M]\), and the path-independent-set model is in
bijection with the Fibonacci fusion-path basis.  They do not certify an
associator, \(F\)-symbols, braiding, or pentagon coherence for the signed
collision-moment proxies.  Moreover, any trace-preserving explanation of the
\(q=5\) equality \(e_2(A_5)=L_5\) as a persistent Fibonacci-anyon coherence
law is obstructed already at \(q=6\), since
\[
    e_2(A_6)=17\neq 18=L_6.
\]
\leanverified{goldenMeanAdjacencyNat_sq}
\leanverified{paper_pom_centralizer_semiring_fibonacci_fusion}
\leanverified{paper_pom_fiber_fibonacci_anyon_fusion_path_bijection}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
The fusion-ring and path-basis statements are the formalized semiring-level
shadow cited in the statement.  A monoidal or braided categorification would
require additional data: associators, \(F\)-symbols, braiding maps, and
pentagon or hexagon coherence.  Those data are not part of the signed companion
certificate above.  If the \(q=5\) exterior-Lucas equality were explained by a
trace-preserving law that persists with \(q\), it would force
\(e_2(A_q)=L_q\) beyond \(q=5\).  The audited coefficient \(c_{6,2}=17\), while
\(L_6=18\), contradicts that persistence at the first subsequent value.
\end{proof}
