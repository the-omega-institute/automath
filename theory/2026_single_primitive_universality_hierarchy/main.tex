\title{Beyond Expression Collapse:\\
  A Three-Level Hierarchy of Single-Primitive Universality}

\author{The Omega Institute}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\begin{abstract}
A recent result of Odrzywołek shows that every elementary function
on a scientific calculator can be reconstructed from a single binary
operator $\eml(x,y)=e^{x}-\ln y$ and the constant~$1$.
We situate this \emph{expression universality} as the bottom level
of a three-level hierarchy.
At the \emph{protocol level}, we show that integer arithmetic
itself---addition and multiplication---is not primitive but emerges
from a single scan--projection protocol with Zeckendorf fold
normalization: the unique operation is function composition~$\circ$,
read at two categorical levels.
At the \emph{certificate level}, the same fold structure yields
collision-moment sequences whose Hankel matrices are positive
semi-definite, giving a finite, auditable spectral fingerprint for
each resolution layer.
We prove the hierarchy is strict: protocol universality implies
expression universality but not conversely, and the separation
is witnessed by decidability of the canonical-form word problem---
a property that holds in the protocol setting but fails generically
for expression trees.
\end{abstract}
